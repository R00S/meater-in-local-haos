## Research notes

Sources:
- FAO/FAOSTAT: fish consumption 27 kg/capita/year (2023); helgilibrary.com/indicators/fish-consumption-per-capita/bangladesh/
- BBS Household Income and Expenditure Survey (HIES) 2022: rice 328.9 g/day, wheat 22.9 g/day, fish 67–74 g/day, meat 35–40 g/day, milk 34.1 g/day, edible oil 31 g/day; sources: tbsnews.net, bangladeshpost.net, cpd.org.bd
- Yearbook of Fisheries Statistics of Bangladesh 2023–24: hilsa 571,000 MT (12% of total), total fish 5.02 M MT; file-khulna.portal.gov.bd
- FAO State of World Fisheries and Aquaculture 2024: Bangladesh 2nd largest inland fish producer
- BBS Yearbook of Agricultural Statistics: potato ~10M MT, onion ~2–2.5M MT, total veg 8.57M MT (2024); statbase.org/data/bgd-vegetable-production/
- Spice consumption: Helgi Library 5.19 kg/capita/year (2021); IndexBox Bangladesh Spice Market Report 2026; total national spice consumption ~1.3M MT/year
- Dairy/livestock: DLS Bangladesh, Invest Bangladesh (investbangladesh.co/livestock-and-fisheries/); ~25M cattle, 1.5–1.7M buffalo, mustard seed ~0.7–0.8M MT
- Grain seasons: BRRI data; Boro 55–60%, Aman 35–40%, Aus 5–10% of rice production
- Rice varieties: Kataribhog (aromatic), BRRI Dhan28/29 (HYV boro), Swarna/BR11 (aman)
- Edible oil: Daily Star "The flavour of change" (thedailystar.net); soybean and palm dominant imports, mustard in deficit
