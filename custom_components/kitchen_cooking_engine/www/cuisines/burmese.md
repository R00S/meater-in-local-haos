description: "Burmese cuisine blends South Asian, Southeast Asian and East Asian traditions in a style that is distinctly Myanmarese. Fish — especially catfish — dominates the diet at ~45 kg per capita per year (WorldFish/FAO), providing over 60% of animal protein. The national dish mohinga is a catfish-broth rice-noodle soup seasoned with ngapi fermented fish paste, lemongrass and turmeric. Ngapi underpins almost every Burmese dish, while lahpet (pickled tea leaves) — used in the celebrated lahpet thoke salad — is entirely unique to Myanmar. Rice is consumed at ~272 kg per capita per year (FAOSTAT 2022), one of the world's highest rates. Myanmar ranks 7th globally in rice production at ~25 million tonnes (FAO 2022)."

## Research notes

Sources:
- WorldFish Center / FAO — Myanmar fish consumption ~45 kg/capita/yr; fish = 60%+ animal protein; 1,509k MT total fisheries production 2022-23 (MOALI)
- FAO / Global Diet Atlas (triagemethod.com) — total protein intake 78.4 g/person/day (2023); meat consumption 8.6 kg/capita avg FAO 2018, up to 22.5 kg in Nay Pyi Taw urban survey
- Myanmar MOALI Fishery Production 2022-23 — aquaculture 323k MT; marine fisheries 911k MT; prawn/shrimp ponds 245,043 acres; Myanmar ranked 12th global capture fisheries, 9th global aquaculture (SEAFDEC 2019)
- Helgi Library / FAOSTAT — onion 18.6 kg/capita/yr (2021); rice 272 kg/capita/yr (2022)
- FAO FAOSTAT 2022 — Myanmar 7th rice producer globally ~24.7–25 MT; 4.3% global rice exports
- Myanmar CSO Agricultural Statistics 2021-2022 — garlic ~209k MT/yr; shallot and onion major dry-zone crops
- Nationwide Edible Oil Study (ResearchGate, May Thet Htar et al.) 2022 — 14.39 kg/capita total oil; palm blend 27%, peanut 25%, sesame Magway region dominant
- USDA FAS Myanmar 2022 — peanut oilseed 1.61M MT; ~4% global peanut production; 1.3M ha oilseed area
- IndexBox 2022 — sesame oil production 142,100 MT in 2022; 100,000 MT sesame seeds exported 2022-23 FY
- Heinrich Böll Foundation 2023 — turmeric 4% world supply; ~54,000–100,000 MT/yr Myanmar production
- CEIC / bootstrapbee.com — garlic production 208,908 MT estimate (2024); Myanmar in global top producers
- IndexBox Myanmar Chili & Pepper Market 2024 — chili market $22M; significant cultivated area
- Myanmar Digital Newspaper — peanut/sesame prices and export ban resumption 2022
- SEAFDEC / Mekong Institute 2023 — fisheries sector 8% GDP; employs 3.2 million; 35% aquaculture share
- Hilsa fishery value ~$1B/yr (WorldFish); Myanmar 3rd largest hilsa producer
