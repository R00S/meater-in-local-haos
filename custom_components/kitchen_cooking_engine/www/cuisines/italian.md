## Research notes

Sources: Italy meat consumption 2023 — poultry 20.5 kg/capita (#1), pork ~20 kg/capita, beef/veal 12-13 kg/capita, lamb <3 kg/capita (Helgi/FAO/Agriculture Canada); seafood ~25 kg/capita; pasta consumption 23 kg/capita (world's highest — world's largest pasta producer); Italy's top rice producer in EU (Arborio, Carnaroli, Vialone Nano for risotto); bread 80-90 kg/year/capita; polenta important in North; 400+ Italian cheeses, ~50 DOP/IGP; olive oil production (Puglia, Tuscany, Calabria, Liguria); truffle from Alba (white) and Umbria (black); porcini wild-foraged autumn mushroom; tomato (San Marzano DOP, Pachino IGP) — Italy 2nd largest EU tomato producer; artichoke — world top producer; Italy world's largest wine producer.

Key findings: Pasta at 23 kg/capita is the defining fact of Italian food culture. Parmigiano Reggiano is "King of Cheeses", grated over pasta nationally. Prosciutto di Parma DOP and guanciale (cured pork cheek) define Rome's carbonara and amatriciana. San Marzano tomatoes are THE pizza/sauce tomato. Mozzarella di bufala DOP from Campania is globally prestigious. Italian extra-virgin olive oil is among the world's finest (Ligurian, Tuscan, Sicilian, Pugliese varieties). White truffle from Alba is the world's most expensive food ingredient per gram. Basil is THE signature Italian herb (pesto genovese).
