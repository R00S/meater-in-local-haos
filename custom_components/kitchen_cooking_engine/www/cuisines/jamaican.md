## Research notes

Sources: Jamaica Livestock Statistics 2023 (Ministry of Agriculture): poultry 137.75M kg, pork 9.19M kg, beef 7.8M kg, goat 482,272 kg; poultry is 10x all other meats combined; FAO food balance Caribbean comparison; ackee and saltfish = national dish; jerk chicken = most globally known Jamaican food; oxtail stew most beloved comfort dish; curried goat = #1 celebration protein; rice and peas (kidney beans or pigeon peas in coconut milk) = universal accompaniment; Jamaica = world's leading allspice (pimento) producer; Scotch bonnet pepper grown nationally; bammy (cassava flatbread) from indigenous Taíno tradition; browning sauce (burnt sugar caramel) unique Jamaican condiment; escovitch (spiced vinegar) = Sephardic Jewish heritage in Jamaica; hard dough bread national bread; festival (sweet fried cornmeal dough) at every jerk stand.

Key findings: Chicken at 137.75M kg (~50 kg/capita equivalent) dwarfs all other proteins. Jerk is uniquely Jamaican — the pimento wood smoke, allspice + Scotch bonnet marinade combination cannot be replicated elsewhere. Oxtail stew with butter beans (lima beans) is the comfort food of Jamaica. Ackee is a toxic fruit unless properly opened and cooked — the national dish reflects this daring. Curried goat reflects 19th century Indian indenture labor influence (Indian curry + Jamaican goat). Bammy flatbread goes back to the Arawak Taíno peoples. Jamaica's allspice (pimento) is the ONLY commercially viable allspice producer globally — Mexico and other attempts failed. Blue Mountain coffee is among the world's most expensive coffees.
