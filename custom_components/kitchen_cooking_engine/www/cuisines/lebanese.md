## Research notes

Sources: FAO Food Balance Sheets Lebanon 2022; World Bank Lebanon food security reports 2021-2022; USDA GAIN Report Lebanon 2022; key statistics: wheat ~235 kg/capita (bread dominant), chicken 20-22 kg/capita (top meat), lamb/mutton ~4-5 kg/capita, beef ~4 kg/capita, fresh fish ~5 kg/capita but culturally important; olive oil 6-8 kg/capita (one of world's highest); fresh vegetables: tomato ~45 kg/capita, cucumber ~25 kg/capita, onion ~20 kg/capita; chickpeas ~8 kg/capita; lentils ~7 kg/capita. Lebanon's mountainous terrain produces: olives, grapes (wine tradition), figs, apricots, sumac, thyme/za'atar, pomegranate, walnuts. Key dishes: hummus bi tahini, tabbouleh, fattoush, kibbeh (raw/baked/fried), kafta, shawarma, mansaf-adjacent rice with lamb, lahm bi ajeen (flatbread with meat), knafeh, baklava.
