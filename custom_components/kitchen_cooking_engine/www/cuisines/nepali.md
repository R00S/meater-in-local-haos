## Research notes

Sources: FAO Food Balance Nepal 2022; Nepal Livestock Statistics (NARC/MoALD); Nepal Fisheries Statistics; academic papers on Nepali food culture (Piers Locke, Michael Hutt); CBS Nepal National Census 2021; Nepal Agri Statistics 2022; known dietary patterns — dal-bhat-tarkari eaten twice daily by most Nepalis; buffalo meat dominant (buff) because beef is legally prohibited; India-Nepal border livestock trade statistics; Nepal Trade Statistics on food imports. Key data: Nepal ~29 million people; altitude zones (Terai, Hills, Mountains) drive very different cuisine; Terai = rice/wheat/maize; Hills = rice/wheat/potato; Mountains = potato/barley/buckwheat; buff (buffalo) ~50% of all meat consumed; chicken 2nd; fish from rivers and Terai lakes; FAO 2022: Nepal rice ~180 kg/capita (rice belt); wheat ~60 kg/capita.
