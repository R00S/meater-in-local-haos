## Research notes

Sources: FAO Food Balance Nigeria 2022; USDA GAIN Report Nigeria 2022; Nigeria National Bureau of Statistics Food Consumption Survey; World Bank Nigeria food data; academic papers on Nigerian food culture (Osseo-Asare 2005 "Food Culture in Sub-Saharan Africa"); NESREA Nigeria fisheries data; Nigeria Fish and Seafood Statistics 2022; West Africa FAO livestock statistics. Key data: Nigeria ~220 million people (Africa's most populous); diverse ethnic groups (Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo, Fulani and hundreds more); palm oil dominant cooking fat (Nigeria world's #2 palm oil producer); goat most widely eaten meat; chicken fastest growing; catfish (fresh and dried) dominant in south; suya and kilishi define north; egusi melon seeds national ingredient; dried crayfish critical seasoning; jollof rice the most internationally known Nigerian dish; stockfish (imported dried Norwegian cod) critical protein ingredient; cassava ~180 kg/capita (Nigeria world's largest producer).
