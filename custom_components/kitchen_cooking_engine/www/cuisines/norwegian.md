## Research notes

Sources used:
- Signature proteins: traditional dish surveys ("famous Norwegian dishes", "Norwegian food culture iconic ingredients"), fårikål/gravlaks/lutefisk/pinnekjøtt dish analysis
- Bulk proteins: Euromeat News 2022 (total meat 58 kg/cap); Helgi Library pork 24.9 kg/cap 2023; white meat ~30% of total → chicken ~16–18 kg/cap; beef estimated ~15 kg/cap
- Local proteins: NIBIO/SSB aquaculture data — Norway is world's largest farmed Atlantic salmon exporter; livestock: cattle, sheep, pigs, poultry
- Signature vegetables: traditional recipe analysis — rutabaga in pinnekjøtt/kålrabistappe, potato in raspeballer, cabbage in fårikål
- Bulk vegetables: SSB StatBank 13691/13692 2023 — potatoes 14.6 kg/cap fresh, carrots and onions leading field-grown volumes
- Local vegetables: SSB/IndexBox 2025 — carrots 44,070 t, onions 20,556 t, winter cabbage 13,799 t; Rogaland, Vestfold, Østfold main regions
- Signature grains: traditional dish analysis — flatbrød (barley/oats/rye), lefse (potato + wheat), grøt (oat porridge)
- Bulk grains: FAO/WorldPopulationReview wheat ~159 kg/cap/yr; SSB potatoes 30 kg/cap/yr; bread ~50 kg/cap/yr (SSB 2023)
- Local grains: SSB cereals & oil seeds — barley 690,000 t production 2025 (mainly feed/malting), oats 243,700 t 2025; Norway-grown barley
- Signature dairy: traditional culture analysis — brunost (national emblem), rømme (rømmegrøt), Jarlsberg
- Bulk dairy: SSB StatBank 2022 — milk & yoghurt 85.5 kg/cap, cheese 17.5 kg/cap, butter ~4–5 kg/cap
- Local dairy: TINE cooperative dominant; Norwegian Red (NRF) breed; Norwegian Dairy Goat; Gudbrandsdalsost from Gudbrandsdal, geitost from Sogn og Fjordane/Setesdal
- Signature spices: recipe analysis — dill (gravlaks, fish dishes), allspice (kjøttkaker, medisterpølse), juniper (game/lamb, aquavit)
- Bulk spices: SSB/Statista market analysis 2022 — salt and black pepper dominate Norwegian seasoning consumption
- Local spices: wild/cultivated survey — caraway (karve) grows wild and cultivated, used in bread and Nøkkelost; angelica (kvann) Viking-era native plant; juniper (einer) grows wild across Norway
