## Research notes

Sources: FAO FAOSTAT Food Balance Sheets Peru 2020–2022; MINAGRI (Peru Ministry of Agriculture) production statistics; INEI Peru statistical yearbooks; Oxfam/CGIAR potato origin research (3,000+ native varieties); FAO Global Fisheries Peru (world's #1 anchovy fisher by volume); PROMPERU export & production data for quinoa, cuy, alpaca; Lonely Planet Peru Food & Drink Guide; Gastón Acurio "Peru: The Cookbook"; UNESCO Declaration of Cultural Heritage — ceviche, pisco; Instituto del Bien Común livestock data; Lima Culinary Institute publications. Key facts: chicken ~44 kg/capita/year (FAOSTAT 2021, highest in South America); Peru world's #1 anchovy fisher; Peru/Bolivia origin of potato; Peru #2 quinoa producer (after Bolivia); guinea pig (cuy) ~65M raised/year; 80% of global alpaca population in Peru; aji amarillo endemic to Peru/Bolivia; huacatay (Tagetes minuta) — uniquely Peruvian-Andean black mint.
