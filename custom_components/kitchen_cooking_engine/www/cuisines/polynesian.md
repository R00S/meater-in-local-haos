## Research notes

Sources: FAO FAOSTAT Pacific Island Food Composition Tables 2016–2022; Secretariat of the Pacific Community (SPC) food security data; Bishop Museum Hawaii food culture records; University of Hawaii food science publications; Ministry of Agriculture Samoa/Tonga statistics; Koloa Rum / Hawaii Agricultural Statistics; National Tropical Botanical Garden breadfruit (Artocarpus altilis) data; Tahiti Tourisme Cuisine section; Māori food and cooking studies (Whakatupu Aotearoa); Encyclopedia Polynesia food section. Key facts: taro the sacred crop of Hawaii (kalo = elder brother of people in Hawaiian mythology); coconut crab (Birgus latro) the world's largest land arthropod, endemic to Pacific; breadfruit (Artocarpus altilis) can produce 200 fruits/year from a single tree; Tahiti vanilla (V. tahitensis) is a distinct species commanding premium prices; imu/umu/hāngi the shared earth oven tradition across all Polynesia; canned corned beef (pisupo in Samoa) now deeply embedded in Pacific diets; kalua pork the centerpiece of Hawaiian luau.
