# Beef Shank × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Beef shank is the lower portion of the front (foreshank) or hind (hindshank) leg, structurally one of the most aggressively-worked muscles in the carcass: the constant motion of walking and standing builds dense bundles of muscle fibre wrapped in thick sheets of connective tissue, with a substantial central marrow bone running through cross-cut steaks (the *osso buco* preparation). Raw shank is among the toughest cuts in the animal; braised slowly for 3–4 hours at 85–95 °C, the connective tissue dissolves into rich gelatin, the marrow renders into the sauce, and the muscle becomes pull-apart tender. The bone-in cross-cut section holds shape because the connective-tissue ring around the muscle keeps it bound during the cook. Carryover is negligible — the surrounding liquid buffers temperature swings.

## Styckesprofil

Oxlägg är den nedre delen av framlägget (foreshank) eller baklägget (hindshank), en av slaktkroppens mest bearbetade muskler: det ständiga rörelsearbetet vid gång och stående bygger täta muskelfiberbuntar inbäddade i tjocka lager bindväv, med ett markant märgben centralt i tvärsnittsbiffen (*osso buco*-snittet). Rått oxlägg är en av djurets segaste styckningsdetaljer; brässeras det långsamt i 3–4 timmar vid 85–95 °C löser sig bindväven till rik gelatin, märgen smälter ner i såsen och muskelköttet blir mjukt och trådigt. Den benskivade formen håller ihop under tillagningen tack vare bindvävsringen kring muskelköttet. Temperaturstegringen efter tillagning är försumbar — den omgivande vätskan buffrar temperatursvängningar.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Osso Buco alla Milanese (Italian / Lombard tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Knopf, 1992)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 2 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 cross-cut beef or veal hindshank steaks, 4 cm thick (about 1.6 kg total), tied around the equator with kitchen twine
- 50 g (½ cup) plain flour
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 60 g (4 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 large carrot, finely chopped
- 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 240 ml (1 cup) dry white wine (Soave or Pinot Grigio)
- 480 ml (2 cups) beef or chicken stock
- 1 tin (400 g) Italian plum tomatoes, drained and chopped
- 2 strips lemon zest
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary, 4 sprigs fresh thyme, 1 bay leaf
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- **Gremolata**: 1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley (finely chopped), zest of 1 lemon, 2 garlic cloves (finely grated)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 165 °C (325 °F). Pat shank steaks dry; season with salt and pepper. Dust in flour, tapping off excess.
2. Heat olive oil and 30 g butter in a heavy oven-proof pan over medium-high heat. Sear the shank steaks 3–4 minutes per side until deeply browned. Remove.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion, carrot, celery; cook 8 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
4. Pour in wine; reduce by half (4 minutes). Add stock, tomatoes, lemon zest and herbs.
5. Return shank steaks to the pan, standing on edge in a single layer. Liquid should reach two-thirds up the sides. Bring to a simmer.
6. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of one central steak, into the meat at its geometric centre, avoiding the marrow bone.
7. Cover and transfer to the oven. Braise 1.5–2 hours.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Hazan's test: the meat starts to recede from the bone and a fork meets no resistance.
9. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Stir gremolata together; sprinkle over each portion just before serving with risotto alla Milanese. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (gelatinous, marrow-bound, lemon-bright).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Långkokt Oxlägg (Swedish home tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hr · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 cross-cut beef shank steaks (oxlägg), 4 cm thick (about 1.6 kg)
- 30 g (2 tbsp) smör (butter)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olja
- 2 large yellow onions, halved and sliced
- 3 morötter (carrots), 2 cm chunks
- 1 large palsternacka (parsnip), 2 cm chunks
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 240 ml (1 cup) dry red wine
- 720 ml (3 cups) beef stock
- 60 ml (¼ cup) tomatpuré
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp whole kryddpeppar (allspice berries)
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shank steaks dry; season with salt and white pepper.
2. Heat butter and oil in a heavy gryta over medium-high heat. Sear steaks 4 minutes per side until deeply browned. Remove.
3. Add onions, carrots and parsnip; sauté 8 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute. Stir in tomatpuré; cook 2 minutes until darkened.
4. Pour in wine; reduce by half (4 minutes). Add stock, bay leaves, allspice and thyme.
5. Return shank steaks to the pan, standing on edge. Liquid should come to two-thirds up the sides.
6. Bring to a simmer. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one steak, tip at geometric centre, avoiding marrow.
7. Cover and transfer to the oven. Braise 3 hours.
8. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Nordström's test: the meat begins to release from the bone and a fork passes through with almost no resistance.
9. Rest 20 minutes loosely covered. Strain and reduce 240 ml of the braising liquid to a glossy sauce. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (gelatinous, allspice-perfumed).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Beef Shank Tagine with Saffron and Almonds (Levantine / North-African tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 cross-cut beef shank steaks, 4 cm thick (about 1.6 kg)
- 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cinnamon
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground turmeric
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- A generous pinch saffron threads, soaked in 60 ml warm water
- 480 ml (2 cups) beef stock
- 400 g (1 tin) chopped tomatoes
- 30 g honey
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 100 g blanched almonds, toasted
- 100 g dried apricots, halved
- **To finish**: 30 g chopped flat-leaf parsley + 30 g chopped fresh coriander, juice of 1 lemon

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Pat shank steaks dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy oven-proof pan over medium-high heat. Sear steaks 3 minutes per side until deeply browned. Remove.
3. Add onions; sauté 10 minutes until soft. Add garlic and ginger; 2 minutes. Add cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, pepper; bloom 30 seconds.
4. Stir in saffron-water, stock, tomatoes, honey and salt. Bring to a simmer.
5. Return shank steaks; nestle in the sauce. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one central steak.
6. Cover and transfer to oven. Braise 2.5 hours.
7. Add toasted almonds and apricots; continue 30 minutes uncovered to thicken slightly.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Ottolenghi's test: the meat is fork-tender and the sauce coats the back of a spoon.
9. Rest 15 minutes. Finish with herbs and lemon. Serve with couscous. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (spice-saturated, fruit-and-nut studded).

### 4. Andrea Nguyen — Bò Kho with Beef Shank (Vietnamese tradition)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (Ten Speed Press, 2019); vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg cross-cut beef shank, bone in
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) neutral oil
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely chopped
- 1 stalk lemongrass, bashed and tied in a knot
- 2 star anise pods
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 5 g (1 tsp) Chinese five-spice powder
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) tomato paste
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fish sauce
- 15 g (1 tbsp) sugar
- 1 L coconut water
- 240 ml (1 cup) light beef stock
- 3 carrots, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 1 small daikon, cut into 3 cm pieces (added in last 30 minutes)
- **To serve**: rice noodles or crusty French bread, lime wedges, Thai basil, sliced bird's-eye chillies

**Method**
1. Pat shank dry. Heat oil in a heavy pot over medium-high heat. Sear shank steaks 3–4 minutes per side. Remove.
2. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion; cook 5 minutes. Add garlic and ginger; 2 minutes. Add lemongrass, star anise, cinnamon, five-spice; toast 30 seconds. Add tomato paste; cook 2 minutes.
3. Pour in fish sauce, sugar, coconut water and stock. Bring to a simmer.
4. Return shank to pot. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one central steak.
5. Add carrots; cover; reduce to lowest heat. Simmer 2 hours.
6. Add daikon; continue 30 minutes more.
7. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Nguyen's test: meat slides off the bone with gentle pressure from a chopstick.
8. Rest 10 minutes. Discard lemongrass and whole spices. Serve with noodles, herbs, lime and chillies. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (silky, aromatic, broth-rich).
