# Beef Shank × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Beef shank cooked in an electric slow cooker is the same long-collagen-conversion preparation as a Dutch-oven braise, executed in a sealed environment that holds the cooking liquid at a steady 85–95 °C (low setting) without any monitoring. The cut's combination of dense connective tissue and a central marrow bone makes it an ideal slow-cooker subject — the long unattended cook fully converts collagen to gelatin, the marrow renders into the sauce, and the steam-tight environment keeps the muscle submerged in its own enriched liquid throughout. Searing the steaks before adding to the cooker is essential for deep flavour; the slow cooker itself contributes none of the Maillard browning that a stove-top sear or oven roast adds. Carryover is zero.

## Styckesprofil

Oxlägg tillagat i en elektrisk slowcooker är samma långsamma kollagenomvandling som en brässering i gjutjärnsgryta, men utförd i en försluten miljö som håller tillagningsvätskan på jämn 85–95 °C (låg inställning) utan bevakning. Styckningsdetaljens kombination av tät bindväv och centralt märgben gör den idealisk för slowcooker — den långa, obevakade tillagningen omvandlar kollagenet fullständigt till gelatin, märgen smälter ner i såsen och den ångförseglande miljön håller köttet nedsänkt i sin berikade vätska under hela tillagningen. Att bryna köttet i stekpanna innan det läggs i slowcookern är avgörande för djup smak; slowcookern i sig bidrar ingen av den Maillard-bryning som en stekpanna eller ugnsrostning ger. Temperaturstegringen efter tillagning är obefintlig.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Osso Buco (American Southern adaptation of Italian classic)
**Inspired by**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017); hughacheson.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 cross-cut beef hindshank steaks, 4 cm thick (about 1.6 kg)
- 50 g plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 1 large onion, small dice
- 2 carrots, small dice
- 2 stalks celery, small dice
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) tomato paste
- 240 ml (1 cup) dry white wine
- 240 ml (1 cup) low-sodium chicken stock
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 strips lemon zest
- 1 bay leaf
- **Gremolata**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley + zest of 1 lemon + 2 garlic cloves grated

**Method**
1. Pat shanks dry, season, dust in flour. Heat olive oil in a heavy pan over medium-high. Sear steaks 3 minutes per side; transfer to the slow cooker.
2. In the same pan, sauté onion, carrot and celery 8 minutes; add garlic 1 minute; add tomato paste, cook 2 minutes. Pour in wine; scrape up fond; reduce by half (4 minutes). Tip into the slow cooker.
3. Add stock, tomatoes, thyme, lemon zest and bay to the slow cooker. The shanks should be three-quarters submerged.
4. Insert the MEATER Plus probe through the side of one central shank, tip at the geometric centre of the meat, avoiding the marrow bone.
5. Cover. Cook on Low 8 hours.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Acheson's test: the meat is starting to release from the bone, a fork meets no resistance.
7. Rest 20 minutes loosely covered. Lift shanks; reduce strained liquid in a saucepan to a glossy sauce. Sprinkle gremolata over each portion. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (gelatinous, marrow-rich, lemon-bright).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Oxlägg i Slow Cooker (Swedish home tradition)
**Inspired by**: Tina Nordström — slow-cooker adaptation of *Långkokt Oxlägg* in *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 8–9 hr (low) · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 cross-cut beef shank steaks (oxlägg), 4 cm thick (about 1.6 kg)
- 30 g smör + 30 ml olja
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced
- 3 morötter (carrots), 2 cm chunks
- 1 large palsternacka (parsnip), 2 cm chunks
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 240 ml dry red wine
- 720 ml beef stock
- 60 ml tomatpuré
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp whole kryddpeppar
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 5 g salt, 2 g white pepper

**Method**
1. Pat shanks dry, season. Heat butter and oil in a heavy pan; sear shanks 4 minutes per side. Transfer to the slow cooker.
2. In the same pan, sauté onions, carrots and parsnip 8 minutes; add garlic 1 minute; stir in tomatpuré, cook 2 minutes; add wine, reduce by half. Tip into cooker.
3. Add stock, bay, kryddpeppar and thyme. Shanks should be three-quarters submerged.
4. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the side of one shank, tip at the centre.
5. Cover. Cook on Low 8–9 hours.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Nordström's test: the meat is starting to flake at the edges and the fork passes through with almost no resistance.
7. Rest 20 minutes loosely covered. Strain and reduce 240 ml of the cooking liquid to a glossy sauce. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F**.

### 3. Mark Bittman — Slow-Cooker Beef Shank with North-African Spices (American tradition)
**Inspired by**: Mark Bittman, *How to Cook Everything Fast* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2014), slow-cooker chapter; markbittman.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.6 kg cross-cut beef shank steaks, bone in
- 30 ml olive oil
- 1 large onion, small dice
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon
- 2 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 1 g (¼ tsp) cayenne
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) tomato paste
- 480 ml (2 cups) beef stock
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes
- 100 g pitted dates, halved
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- **To finish**: chopped fresh coriander, lemon wedges, 30 g toasted slivered almonds

**Method**
1. Pat shanks dry; season with salt. Heat olive oil in a heavy pan; sear shanks 3 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
2. In same pan, sauté onion 5 minutes; add garlic 1 minute; bloom spices 30 seconds; add tomato paste, cook 2 minutes; pour in stock, scrape fond. Tip into cooker.
3. Add chopped tomatoes and dates. Shanks should be three-quarters submerged.
4. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the side of one central shank.
5. Cover. Cook on Low 8 hours.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Bittman's test: meat falls off the bone with light pressure from a fork.
7. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Top with coriander, slivered almonds and lemon. Serve with couscous. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (spice-rich, sweet-savoury, deeply tender).

### 4. Andrea Nguyen — Slow-Cooker Bò Kho (Vietnamese tradition)
**Inspired by**: Andrea Nguyen, *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (Ten Speed Press, 2019) — slow-cooker variation; vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg cross-cut beef shank, bone in
- 30 ml neutral oil
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely chopped
- 1 stalk lemongrass, bashed and knotted
- 2 star anise pods
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 5 g (1 tsp) Chinese five-spice powder
- 30 ml tomato paste
- 30 ml fish sauce
- 15 g sugar
- 600 ml coconut water
- 240 ml light beef stock
- 3 carrots, cut into 3 cm chunks
- 1 small daikon, cut into 3 cm chunks (added in last 60 minutes)
- **To serve**: rice noodles or crusty French bread, lime wedges, Thai basil, sliced bird's-eye chillies

**Method**
1. Pat shank dry; sear in oil 3–4 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
2. In same pan, sauté onion 5 minutes; add garlic, ginger 2 minutes; add lemongrass, star anise, cinnamon, five-spice; toast 30 seconds; add tomato paste, cook 2 minutes; pour in fish sauce, sugar, coconut water and stock; scrape fond. Tip into cooker.
3. Add carrots. Shanks should be three-quarters submerged.
4. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the side of one central shank.
5. Cover. Cook on Low 7 hours, then add daikon; continue 1 hour more.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Nguyen's test: meat slides off the bone with gentle chopstick pressure.
7. Rest 10 minutes loosely covered. Discard lemongrass and whole spices. Serve with noodles, herbs, lime and chillies. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (silky, aromatic, broth-rich).
