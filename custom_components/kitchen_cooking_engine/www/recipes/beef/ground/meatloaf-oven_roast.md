# Meatloaf × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Meatloaf is a free-formed loaf of seasoned, bound ground meat (typically beef alone or a beef-pork-veal blend) baked in a moderate oven until cooked through. Structurally it is a colloid of ground muscle held by a panade (bread or breadcrumbs soaked in milk or stock) and bound with egg; the panade is critical because it absorbs and re-releases moisture during the bake, preventing the dry, dense loaf that pure meat-plus-egg produces. Oven temperatures of 175–190 °C give enough time for the centre to reach safe temperature without over-rendering the surface. Carryover is significant — 5–8 °C in a typical 1 kg loaf — because the loaf shape stores heat well; a 5–10 minute rest is mandatory both for carryover and for the proteins to re-absorb expressed liquid.

## Styckesprofil

Köttfärslimpa är en fritt formad limpa av kryddad, bunden malet kött (vanligtvis nötkött alternativt blandningar med fläsk eller kalv) bakad i ugn. Bindningen — ägg, ströbröd, mjölk — håller loafen sammanhållen under tillagningen och bevarar fukt. Till skillnad från hela musklar har köttfärslimpa ingen kollagenstruktur att bryta ner; målet är att värma igenom till 74 °C för livsmedelssäkerhet medan ytan glaseras eller karameliseras. Eftertillagning är måttlig (3–5 °C) beroende på limpans tjocklek och ugnstemperatur.

## Source recipes

### 1. Classic Meatloaf with Bacon Topping (inspired by America's Test Kitchen)
**Inspired by**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Cook's Illustrated Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2011); cooksillustrated.com  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 60–75 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g ground beef chuck (80/20)
- 250 g ground pork
- 100 g (1¼ cups) fresh white breadcrumbs
- 180 ml (¾ cup) whole milk
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 1 medium onion, very finely chopped, sweated in butter until soft
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Worcestershire sauce
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) soy sauce
- 5 g (1 tsp) Dijon mustard
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 8 thin slices bacon, for the top
- **Glaze**: 100 ml ketchup + 30 g brown sugar + 15 ml cider vinegar + 5 ml hot pepper sauce

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and place a wire rack inside.
2. Soak breadcrumbs in milk 5 minutes. Mash to a thick paste with a fork.
3. In a large bowl combine the panade, eggs, sweated onion, garlic, Worcestershire, soy, mustard, salt and pepper. Mix until uniform.
4. Add ground beef and pork; mix gently with hands until just combined — over-mixing produces a dense loaf.
5. Form into a loaf 23 cm long × 12 cm wide × 7 cm tall on the wire rack.
6. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre of the loaf, entering from one short end and pushing horizontally to the centre. Avoid the surface bacon zone.
7. Drape bacon strips across the top, slightly overlapping. Brush a third of the glaze over the bacon.
8. Bake 50 minutes; brush with another third of the glaze; continue baking 10–25 minutes more.
9. **Pull at 68–70 °C (155–158 °F).** ATK targets a finished centre of 71–74 °C after rest; the loaf carries 5–6 °C in the standard formulation.
10. Brush with the remaining glaze. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented with foil. Final serving temperature: **71–74 °C / 160–165 °F** (juicy, sliceable, deeply glazed).

### 2. Köttfärslimpa (inspired by Tina Nordström)
**Inspired by**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 50–60 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g blandfärs (50/50 beef-pork mince)
- 100 g (1 cup) fine breadcrumbs (ströbröd)
- 240 ml whole milk
- 1 large egg
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped, sweated in 15 g butter
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground allspice (kryddpeppar) — Swedish signature
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Worcestershire-sås
- 80 g rökt sidfläsk (smoked streaky bacon), cut in lardons (mixed in)
- Extra 6 thin slices bacon for the top

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 175 °C (350 °F). Line a sheet pan with parchment.
2. Soak breadcrumbs in milk 5 minutes; mash to paste.
3. Combine panade, egg, sweated onion, salt, pepper, allspice, Worcestershire and lardons in a bowl. Mix.
4. Add färs; mix gently until just combined.
5. Shape into a loaf about 25 × 12 × 6 cm on the parchment.
6. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre from one short end.
7. Drape the additional bacon slices across the top.
8. Bake 50–60 minutes.
9. **Pull at 68–72 °C (155–162 °F).** Nordström targets a moist, just-set Swedish *köttfärslimpa* — slightly less aggressive than American doneness because Swedish blandfärs is drier than 80/20.
10. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented. Slice 2 cm thick. Serve with potatismos, gräddsås and lingonsylt. Final serving temperature: **72–75 °C / 162–167 °F** (sliceable, savoury, allspice-perfumed).

### 3. Italian-Inspired Meatloaf with Fennel & Sun-Dried Tomato (inspired by Nigella Lawson)
**Inspired by**: Nigella Lawson, *Feast: Food to Celebrate Life* (Chatto & Windus, 2004); nigella.com/recipes  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 60 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg minced beef (10–15 % fat)
- 150 g (1½ cups) fresh ciabatta crumbs
- 240 ml (1 cup) whole milk
- 2 large eggs
- 1 small onion, finely chopped, sweated in olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 3 g (1½ tsp) fennel seeds, lightly toasted and crushed
- 60 g sun-dried tomatoes in oil, finely chopped
- 60 g grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
- 5 g (1 tsp) sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil to glaze the loaf

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 180 °C (350 °F). Line a sheet pan with parchment.
2. Soak crumbs in milk 5 minutes. Mash to paste.
3. Combine panade, eggs, sweated onion, garlic, fennel, sun-dried tomato, Parmesan, parsley, salt and pepper.
4. Add mince and mix gently to combine.
5. Shape into an oval loaf 25 × 12 × 7 cm. Brush all over with olive oil.
6. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre from one short end.
7. Bake 55–65 minutes.
8. **Pull at 70–72 °C (158–162 °F).** Nigella's loaf is fully cooked but generous-fat — the Parmesan and sun-dried tomato hold moisture, allowing a slightly higher pull without dryness.
9. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented. Slice 2 cm thick. Serve with passata sauce, mash, or simply a green salad. Final serving temperature: **74–77 °C / 165–170 °F** (savoury, herb-bright, sliceable).

### 4. Roasted Vegetable Meatloaf (inspired by Ina Garten)
**Inspired by**: Ina Garten, *Barefoot Contessa Back to Basics* (Clarkson Potter, 2008); barefootcontessa.com  
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 35 min · **Cook**: 75 min · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.1 kg ground beef chuck (80/20)
- 100 g (1 cup) panko breadcrumbs
- 240 ml (1 cup) low-sodium chicken stock
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 1 large onion, small dice
- 1 small carrot, small dice
- 1 small zucchini, small dice
- 1 small red bell pepper, small dice
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil for sautéing the vegetables
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Worcestershire sauce
- 60 ml (¼ cup) ketchup (mixed in)
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh thyme leaves
- 8 g (2 tsp) kosher salt
- 3 g (¾ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 120 ml (½ cup) ketchup, for the glaze on top

**Method**
1. Sauté onion, carrot, zucchini and pepper in olive oil over medium heat 12–15 minutes until softened and most liquid evaporated. Add garlic; 1 minute. Cool 10 minutes.
2. Soak panko in chicken stock 5 minutes; mash to paste.
3. Preheat oven to 175 °C (350 °F). Line a sheet pan with parchment.
4. Combine panade, eggs, cooled vegetables, Worcestershire, ketchup, thyme, salt and pepper.
5. Add ground beef; mix gently to just combined.
6. Form into a loaf 28 × 13 × 7 cm. Spread the additional ½ cup ketchup over the top.
7. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre from one short end.
8. Bake 70–80 minutes.
9. **Pull at 68–70 °C (155–158 °F).** Garten's loaf is wetter than ATK's because of the vegetables; she warns that pulling above 72 °C produces a chalky slice.
10. Rest 15 minutes loosely tented. Slice 2 cm thick. Final serving temperature: **72–74 °C / 162–165 °F** (juicy, vegetable-flecked, glazed).
