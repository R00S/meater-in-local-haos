# Beef Liver × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan frying liver is the classic European preparation — and the caramelised onion is inseparable from it in British, French (*foie de veau à la lyonnaise*), German (*Leber Berliner Art*), and Polish (*wątróbka z cebulą*) traditions. Sequence: soften sliced onions (8–10 min, medium heat) until golden; remove; wipe pan; increase to medium-high; add butter/oil; fry the liver slices 2–3 min per side until browned and just pink in the centre; deglaze with wine or stock; return onions.

The onion-liver interaction is important: onions going into the pan before the liver means the flavours integrate in the pan juices. MEATER probe inserted sideways through the centre of a 6–8 mm slice. Pull at 62–63 °C; carryover 1–2 °C. Serve immediately — liver continues cooking on the plate from retained heat.

## Styckesprofil

Stekning av lever i panna är den klassiska europeiska beredningen — och den karameliserade löken är inte ett komplement utan en smakbalans: leverns järnrika bitterhet möter löksötman. Lever är ett körtelvävnadsorgan med hög vatteninnehåll och lågt fettinnehåll; vid pannstek karameliseras ytan snabbt i direkt kontakt med en het panna. Tillagning ska vara snabb — 2–3 minuter per sida vid hög värme — och innertemperaturen ska nå 71 °C. Överkokad lever blir grynig och torr; underkokad lever har en järnig, blodig smak som avskräcker många. Eftertillagning är liten på grund av organets tunna geometri.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Fegato di Manzo alla Veneziana (Venetian Italian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Knopf, 1992)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 1 hr milk soak) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g beef liver, sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 2 large yellow onions, very thinly sliced
- 5 g (1 tsp) fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) red-wine vinegar, to finish
- 15 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the liver slices in milk 1 hour at room temperature. Drain; pat very dry. Discard milk.
2. Heat 30 ml olive oil and the butter in a wide heavy pan over medium-low heat. Add sliced onions; cook slowly 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply caramelised and soft (the Venetian *cipolle stufate*). Transfer to a warm dish.
3. Increase heat to high. Add the remaining oil. When shimmering, lay liver slices in a single layer.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, with the tip at the geometric centre of the slice (parallel to the pan).
5. Cook 60–90 seconds until the underside is well browned. Flip; cook another 45–60 seconds.
6. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Hazan stresses that liver should be "rosato al centro" — pink at the centre — and that overcooking by even 10 seconds turns the slice grainy.
7. Return onions to the pan; toss briefly to coat the liver. Add vinegar; let it sizzle 5 seconds. Sprinkle with parsley. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (faint pink centre, sweet caramelised onions).

### 2. Diana Henry — Devilled Calf's Liver (modern British tradition)
**Inspired by**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015) and her *Telegraph* column at telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g beef or calf's liver, sliced 5–6 mm thick
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g (½ cup) plain flour
- 5 g (1 tsp) English mustard powder
- 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 1 g (¼ tsp) cayenne pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) red-wine vinegar, to finish
- 5 g chopped fresh parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak liver slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat very dry.
2. Mix flour, mustard powder, paprika, cayenne, salt and pepper on a plate. Dust the slices, tapping off excess.
3. Heat oil and butter together in a wide heavy frying pan over high heat until the butter foam subsides.
4. Lay slices in the pan in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at the geometric centre.
5. Cook 60–90 seconds on the first side until deeply browned. Flip; cook another 60 seconds.
6. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Henry calls this "rose-pink in the centre" — the spice crust must be deeply browned but the interior still soft and bouncing back to gentle pressure.
7. Splash with vinegar; remove from pan; rest 60 seconds. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (spice-crusted, just-pink centre).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Kaleji Masala (North Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g beef or goat liver, cut into 2 cm cubes (Indian preparation uses cubes rather than slices)
- 45 ml (3 tbsp) ghee or vegetable oil
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 2 green chillies, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground turmeric
- 2 g (½ tsp) Kashmiri red chilli powder
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) plain whole-milk yoghurt
- 2 g (½ tsp) garam masala
- 5 g chopped fresh coriander, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat ghee in a karahi or heavy pan over high heat until shimmering.
2. Add onion; sauté 4 minutes until pale gold. Add garlic, ginger, green chillies; 1 minute.
3. Add coriander, cumin, turmeric, chilli powder; bloom 30 seconds.
4. Add the liver cubes. Insert the MEATER probe into the largest central cube.
5. Stir-fry 90–120 seconds — the cubes will sear and seal on the outside.
6. Add yoghurt and salt; cook another 60–90 seconds, stirring constantly.
7. **Pull at 63–66 °C (145–151 °F).** Jaffrey's *kaleji* is fully cooked but still tender — the cubes should be just firm to the touch with a pale rose centre.
8. Off the heat, sprinkle with garam masala and coriander. Final serving temperature: **65–68 °C / 149–154 °F** (spice-crusted, succulent).

### 4. Tina Nordström — Stekt Lever med Lök (Swedish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g nötlever (beef liver), sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g vetemjöl (plain flour)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper
- 50 g smör (butter)
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced thin
- 5 ml (1 tsp) Worcestershire-sås
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry-ättika or Sherry vinegar, to finish
- 5 g chopped persilja (parsley), to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the liver slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat dry.
2. In a wide heavy pan melt 25 g of butter over medium heat. Add onions; cook slowly 15 minutes until soft and golden. Transfer to a warm dish.
3. Mix flour, salt and pepper on a plate; dust the slices.
4. In the same pan, melt the remaining 25 g butter over high heat until foam subsides.
5. Lay slices in the pan in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at geometric centre.
6. Cook 60 seconds; flip; cook another 60 seconds.
7. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Nordström's *stekt lever* is "rosa men inte rå" — pink but not raw — the Swedish home standard.
8. Return onions to the pan; toss briefly. Splash with vinegar and Worcestershire. Sprinkle with parsley. Serve with potatismos and rårörda lingon. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F**.

### 5. Imma Adamu — Beef Liver and Onions (West African / Afro-Caribbean tradition)
**Inspired by**: Imma Adamu, AfricanBites; https://www.africanbites.com/beef-liver-onions/ (fetched 2026-04-28). Originally published April 2017, updated 2025. Author is West African (Nigerian).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 hr (buttermilk soak) + 15 min active · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 2 min
**Culinary tradition**: West African / Afro-Caribbean (Group C)
**Technique note**: Breaded and pan-fried in a cast-iron skillet with Creole seasoning — reflecting the African diaspora fusion where West African technique meets Creole/Caribbean seasoning. Served with caramelised onions and optional tomatoes, over rice. Soaking in buttermilk (rather than milk) is a West African/Southern American variation of the European milk-soak technique. No probe temperature stated; visual doneness: "a hint of pink left on the inside", equivalent to ~62–65°C.

**Ingredients** *(extracted from page prose; recipe card not rendered by web_fetch)*
- 450–900 g (1–2 lb) beef liver, thinly sliced
- Enough buttermilk to cover (for 2-hour soak)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Creole seasoning (paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper)
- Flour, onion powder, garlic powder, paprika — for dredging
- Oil, butter, or bacon fat for frying
- 2 medium onions, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh thyme
- Optional: 2 medium tomatoes, diced; ½ cup broth

**Method**
1. Rinse the beef liver; put in a bowl and pour over enough buttermilk to cover. Refrigerate 2 hours to tenderise and reduce bitterness.
2. Remove liver from buttermilk; drain and pat dry.
3. Season generously with salt, pepper, and Creole seasoning.
4. Mix flour, onion powder, garlic powder, and paprika in a shallow bowl. Dredge the liver slices; shake off excess.
5. Heat oil or butter in a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
6. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest liver slice.
7. Pan-fry 3 minutes per side until browned. Avoid overcooking — "the longer you cook it, the tougher it gets."
8. Remove liver and set aside. Wipe skillet.
9. Add oil or butter; sauté onions with thyme 10–15 minutes until softened. Add garlic and Creole seasoning; cook 5 more minutes.
10. Optional: add tomatoes and broth; simmer 10 minutes.
11. Return liver to the pan; cook 2–3 minutes to reheat.
12. **Pull when just pink inside** — Imma's guidance: "a hint of pink left on the inside." Equivalent to **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F**.
13. Adjust seasoning; serve with rice, roasted or mashed potatoes.
