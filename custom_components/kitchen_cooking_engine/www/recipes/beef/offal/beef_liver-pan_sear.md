# Beef Liver × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan searing liver (without onions) is the faster, more minimalist method — and the approach used in South Asian, East African, and South American traditions where bold spice marinades replace the European onion-sauce approach. Very hot, dry or lightly oiled cast-iron or stainless pan; liver slices (6–8 mm) go in to sear hard without moving for 2 minutes per side. The goal is a deep brown Maillard crust on both faces with a faint pink interior — the same visual target as the pan-fry method but achieved without the onions and deglazing step.

MEATER probe inserted sideways through the thickest point of the slice. Pull at 62–63 °C; carryover 1–2 °C. The slices are best served directly from the pan — no resting required since there is nothing for the thin slices to redistribute.

## Styckesprofil

Pannstek av lever (utan lök) är den snabbare, mer minimalistiska metoden — och den elegantare om råvaran är av hög kvalitet. Utan löksötman som motbalans kräver metoden välkontrollerad värme och tidprecision: en riktigt het panna, smör + olja, hög direkt värme, 60–70 sekunder per sida för tunna skivor (8–10 mm), 90 sekunder för tjockare (12–15 mm). Maillardytan bildas snabbt på grund av leverns höga proteininnehåll och lägre fett; varje sekund för länge leder till överkokad, grynig konsistens. Innertemperatur: 71 °C. Eftertillagning är 1–2 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fergus Henderson — Calf's Liver, Bacon and Onions (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Inspired by**: Fergus Henderson, *The Whole Beast: Nose to Tail Eating* (Macmillan, 1999/2004); stjohnrestaurant.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 thick slices (≈ 180 g each, 12 mm thick) calf's or young beef liver, membranes carefully removed
- 8 thick rashers smoked streaky bacon
- 2 large white onions, halved and sliced
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) duck fat or sunflower oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry vinegar, to deglaze
- 15 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat half the butter in a heavy pan over low-medium heat. Add onions with a pinch of salt; cook 25 minutes until soft and golden, stirring occasionally. Set aside warm.
2. In the same pan over medium-high heat, fry bacon until crisp; remove to drain on paper.
3. Pat liver slices very dry. Season with salt and pepper just before cooking.
4. Add duck fat to the bacon fat in the pan; bring to a hard sear over high heat — the fat should ripple and barely smoke.
5. Lay slices in the pan well spaced. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at its geometric centre.
6. Sear 90 seconds undisturbed. Flip; sear 60–90 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 58–61 °C (136–142 °F).** Henderson calls this "blushing pink" — the crust should be mahogany, the centre deeply rose.
8. Remove slices to warm plates. Deglaze the pan with sherry vinegar; reduce by half, swirl in the remaining 15 g butter. Top liver with onions, bacon, pan sauce and parsley. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **60–63 °C / 140–145 °F** (deeply rosé, crisp-edged).

### 2. Anthony Bourdain — Calf's Liver Persillade (French bistro tradition)
**Inspired by**: Anthony Bourdain & José de Meirelles, *Les Halles Cookbook* (Bloomsbury, 2004)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 slices calf's or young beef liver (≈ 175 g each, 10 mm thick), membranes removed
- 50 g plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) neutral oil
- 50 g unsalted butter, divided
- 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 30 g flat-leaf parsley, very finely chopped (Bourdain's *persillade* base)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) lemon juice, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat liver dry. Season; dust each slice in flour and tap off excess.
2. Heat oil and 25 g butter in a wide heavy pan over high heat until the butter foam subsides and just begins to brown.
3. Lay slices in the pan well spaced. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
4. Sear 60–90 seconds first side; flip; sear 60–90 seconds second side.
5. **Pull at 58–61 °C (136–142 °F).** Bourdain insists "*rose au milieu* — pink in the middle, not red, never grey."
6. Remove slices to warm plates. Off heat, add the remaining 25 g butter, garlic and parsley to the pan; swirl 30 seconds — the residual heat is the key, raw heat will burn the parsley.
7. Pour the persillade and butter over the slices. Squeeze lemon to taste. Rest 1 minute. Final serving temperature: **60–63 °C / 140–145 °F** (rose-pink, butter-and-herb glossed).

### 3. José Pizarro — Hígado de Ternera con Cebolla y Pimentón (Spanish tradition)
**Inspired by**: José Pizarro, *Spanish Flavours* (Kyle Books, 2012); josepizarro.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 slices calf's or young beef liver (≈ 175 g each, 10 mm thick)
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 large red onions, thinly sliced
- 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked paprika (pimentón dulce)
- 2 g (½ tsp) hot smoked paprika (pimentón picante)
- 60 ml (¼ cup) Pedro Ximénez sherry (or other sweet sherry)
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat 30 ml olive oil in a wide heavy pan over medium heat. Add onions; cook 20 minutes until soft and sweet. Sprinkle with both paprikas; off heat. Set aside warm.
2. Pat liver slices dry; season with salt and pepper.
3. In the same pan over high heat, add the remaining 30 ml olive oil. Lay slices in the pan well spaced.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
5. Sear 90 seconds first side; flip; sear 60–90 seconds second side.
6. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Pizarro's Spanish standard is "rosado pero no crudo" — pink but not raw.
7. Remove slices to warm plates. Add the sherry to the pan; reduce 30 seconds. Pour over the liver with the warm onions. Rest 1 minute. Sprinkle with parsley. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (paprika-edged, sweet-onion crowned).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Seared Calf's Liver with Browned Butter & Lingonberries (Nordic tradition)
**Inspired by**: Magnus Nilsson, *Faviken* (Phaidon, 2012) and *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 slices calf's liver (≈ 160 g each, 10 mm thick), trimmed of membrane
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 60 g cultured unsalted butter (Nilsson's preference)
- 60 g (¼ cup) raw or lightly-stirred fresh lingonberries (rårörda lingon)
- 5 ml (1 tsp) cider vinegar, to deglaze
- A small handful of micro-greens or chervil leaves, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat slices very dry. Season with salt and white pepper just before cooking.
2. Heat the rapeseed oil in a wide heavy pan over high heat until shimmering and just beginning to haze.
3. Lay slices in the pan well spaced. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at geometric centre.
4. Sear 90 seconds first side; flip; sear 60–90 seconds second side.
5. **Pull at 56–58 °C (132–136 °F).** Nilsson's Nordic standard is on the cooler end of *rosé* — he calls it "blank rosa" (gleaming rose).
6. Move slices to warm plates. Off heat, add the butter to the pan; let it brown to *beurre noisette* (60–90 seconds), then add vinegar. Spoon the brown butter over the liver and around the plate. Top each slice with a spoonful of lingonberries.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Scatter micro-greens. Final serving temperature: **59–61 °C / 138–142 °F** (cool-rose centre, brown-butter halo, sharp berry counterpoint).
