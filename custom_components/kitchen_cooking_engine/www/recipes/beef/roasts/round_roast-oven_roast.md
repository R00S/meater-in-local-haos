# Round Roast × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

"Round roast" is a generic term for any oven roast cut from the round primal — the large
hindquarter hip/thigh muscle group. The round primal encompasses several sub-primals: top
round (inner thigh, leanest), bottom round (outer thigh, slightly tougher), and eye of round
(central cylindrical muscle, very lean and uniform). A recipe labelled "round roast" may use
any of these, making this the most interchangeable of the round-family slugs.

**Anatomy**: Round muscles are large and heavily exercised, producing lean, tight-grained
meat with low intramuscular fat. Connective tissue is modest — enough to benefit from slow
heat, not enough to require braising temperatures. At 54–65 °C the muscle fibres remain moist
and the round becomes sliceable; above ~68 °C the roast turns dry.

**Interchangeability**: Africanbites.com explicitly lists eye of round, **top round**, bottom
round, and top sirloin roast as interchangeable in a round-roast recipe — confirming that the
same method and temperature targets apply across these sub-cuts.

**Culinary Group A gap**: Dry oven roasting a whole round joint is not documented in
confirmed-reachable South Asian, East Asian, or South-East Asian recipe sources.

## Styckesprofil

"Rundbitsstek" är ett generiskt begrepp för vilken ugnstek som helst skuren från rundbotten — bakkvartersprimalen — inklusive övre innanlår, yttersida, sirloin tip och ögonbiff. Det är ett av de magrare, hårt arbetade områdena av djuret; lite intramuskulärt fett och tät muskelstruktur innebär att felaktig tillagning ger torrt, segt resultat. Rätt tillagad (långsamt, till rare–medium-rare) är rundbitten utmärkt med en fin, jämn textur.

## Source recipes

### 1. Easy Roast Beef (inspired by Good Food)
**Inspired by**: Good Food magazine, September 2011; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-beef-carrots-easy-gravy  
**Serves**: 4 · **Cut used**: 950 g beef **top rump joint** (British upper-round equivalent)

**Ingredients**
- 950 g beef top rump joint
- 1 tsp plain flour + 1 tsp mustard powder + seasoning (rub)
- 1 onion (8 wedges), 500 g carrots (halved)
- **Gravy**: 1 tbsp plain flour, 250 ml beef stock

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 240 °C / 220 °C fan.
2. Rub flour-mustard mix over joint; place on vegetable trivet.
3. Roast at 240 °C for 20 min; reduce to 190 °C / 170 °C fan.
4. Continue: **30 min rare, 40 min medium, 1 hour well done.**
5. Rest covered 30 min before carving.

### 2. Roast Beef with Red Wine & Banana Shallots (inspired by Good Food)
**Inspired by**: Good Food magazine, January 2016; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-beef-red-wine-banana-shallots  
**Serves**: 8 · **Cut used**: 2 kg **topside of beef** (British top-round equivalent)

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg topside of beef (fat cap on top)
- Oil, English mustard powder, sea salt, pepper (rub)
- 2 large carrots; 12 banana shallots
- 2 bay leaves; 2 tbsp plain flour
- 300 ml red wine; 500 ml beef stock; 2 tsp redcurrant jelly

**Method**
1. Room temperature 1 hour. Preheat to 220 °C / 200 °C fan.
2. Rub; place fat-side up on carrot trivet.
3. Roast 220 °C for 15 min; reduce to 180 °C / 160 °C fan.
4. Roast 55 min (medium) or 45 min (medium-rare). **Probe thermometer in thickest part should read 65 °C for medium.**
5. Rest; make red wine gravy from pan juices.

### 3. Creole-Spiced Roast Beef (inspired by Immaculate Bites)
**Inspired by**: Imma (Immaculate Bites), africanbites.com/roast-beef/  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Cook**: 1–2 hours  
**Cut used**: Eye of round roast; **top round roast, bottom round roast** listed as direct substitutes

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg **eye of round roast** (or top round, bottom round, top sirloin roast)
- Olive oil or butter; paprika, rosemary, thyme, minced garlic, cayenne
- Whole garlic cloves (for slitting)
- ½ cup dry red wine; 1 cup beef broth

**Method**
1. Overnight herb-paste marinade; preheat oven to 250 °F / 120 °C.
2. Stuff deep slits with garlic cloves; sear all sides.
3. Roast uncovered at 120 °C for 1–2 hours.
4. **Pull at 130–140 °F / 54–60 °C.** Rest 15–20 min.

### 4. Paahtopaisti (inspired by Katja Nordlund)
**Inspired by**: Katja Nordlund, soppa365.fi/reseptit/liha/paahtopaisti (30 September 2012)  
**Serves**: 10 · **Cook**: ~1.5 hours + 30 min rest  
**Cut used**: **Paahtopaisti** (Finnish hindquarter/round oven-roast cut)

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg paahtopaisti
- 1 tbsp butter

**Method**
1. Room temperature 1 hour. Brown in butter.
2. Insert meat thermometer; roast at **125 °C** until **55–60 °C** (~1.5 hours; 70 °C for well-done).
3. Rest 30 min in foil; slice thin.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Tradition | Group | Pull / target temp | Doneness |
|--------|-----------|-------|--------------------|---------|
| BBC Good Food (Easy) | British | B | Time only | Rare to medium |
| BBC Good Food (Red Wine) | British | B | 65 °C | Medium |
| Africanbites.com | West African / S. US Creole | C | 54–60 °C / 130–140 °F | Medium-rare to medium |
| Soppa365.fi | Finnish | D | 55–60 °C | Medium-rare to medium |

**Key finding**: Explicit probe temperatures cluster at **54–65 °C** pull for medium-rare to
medium. The current system target of 54 °C (medium-rare) is at the low end of the range and is
appropriate as a probe-pull target with 20–30 minutes resting. All sources agree that above
68–70 °C the round becomes dry and loses quality.

**Group A gap**: No verified oven-roast recipe for round-area beef in South/East/SE Asian sources.

## What makes this method special

Round roast responds to dry oven heat by forming a Maillard crust (via initial high heat or
searing) while the large, uniform interior heats evenly to 54–65 °C. Unlike braised round roast
(which reaches 88–96 °C for pull-apart texture), oven-roasted round produces firm, sliceable
meat suitable for carved table service or cold-cut sandwiches. The low fat content means carryover
is critical to monitor — especially in high-start methods.
