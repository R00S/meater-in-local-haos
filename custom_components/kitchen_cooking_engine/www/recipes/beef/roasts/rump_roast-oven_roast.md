# Rump Roast × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The rump roast (North American term) is cut from the primal round — the hip area of the
hindquarter. In British butchery the closest equivalents are **topside** (inner thigh, very
lean) and **top rump** / **thick flank** (slightly higher up, a little more marbled). In
Finnish butchery this area is called **paahtopaisti** and is the standard cut for oven
roast beef. These cuts share the anatomical characteristics important for oven roasting:
low connective tissue relative to chuck or short ribs, moderate intramuscular fat, and
lean, tight-grained muscle that responds well to dry heat.

**What oven roasting does to rump**: Because the rump is lean and lacks the collagen load
of braising cuts, it does not benefit from the collagen-dissolution range (above 74 °C).
The goal is to reach 55–65 °C at the centre while maintaining moisture. Most recipes use
one of two strategies:

1. **High-start / low-finish**: Blast at 200–240 °C for 15–20 minutes to crust the
   exterior via Maillard browning, then reduce to 160–190 °C for the remainder. This
   balances surface colour with gentle interior heating.
2. **Low and slow throughout**: Roast at 120–130 °C for 1–2 hours. The surface browns
   less intensely but moisture retention is better, especially for the leaner eye-of-round
   sub-cut.

**Carryover**: A whole rump joint (1–2 kg) in a moderate oven will carry over 4–7 °C
during resting. Recipes that give only a time guide (BBC Good Food below) rely on the
cook resting the joint for 20–30 minutes before carving, so the final serving temperature
is several degrees above the pull temperature.

**Probe placement**: Insert from the end of the joint toward the geometric centre of the
lean muscle mass. For topside (a single flat-grained muscle), the probe should avoid any
fat seam or tendon running through the centre. Pull the probe back slightly if it feels
gristle under the tip.

**Culinary Group A gap**: Dry oven roasting of a whole beef rump or round joint is not a
documented tradition in confirmed-reachable South Asian, East Asian, or South-East Asian
recipe sources. The gap is noted and will remain open until a verified source is found.

## Source recipes

### 1. Good Food — Easy Roast Beef (British tradition)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, September 2011; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-beef-carrots-easy-gravy  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 50 min–1h20 (plus 30 min rest)

**Ingredients**
- 950 g beef **top rump joint**
- 1 tsp plain flour
- 1 tsp mustard powder
- Salt and pepper
- 1 onion, cut into 8 wedges
- 500 g carrots, halved lengthways

**Gravy**
- 1 tbsp plain flour
- 250 ml beef stock

**Method**
1. Heat oven to 240 °C / 220 °C fan / gas 9.
2. Mix flour and mustard powder with seasoning; rub all over the beef top rump joint.
3. Place onion wedges and carrots in a roasting tin; sit beef on top. Cook at 240 °C for 20 minutes.
4. Reduce oven to 190 °C / 170 °C fan / gas 5. Continue roasting: **30 min for rare, 40 min for medium, 1 hour for well done** (after the initial 20 min).
5. Remove beef and carrots; cover with foil. Rest 30 minutes.
6. For gravy: stir flour into tin juices over hob, cook 30 seconds, whisk in stock and bubble to thicken.

**Notes**: No probe temperature given — recipe uses time-per-weight method. "Top rump" is British butchery for the upper portion of the round (equivalent to US rump roast). Recipe tip notes: "best eaten pink as this will keep it tender."

### 2. Good Food — Roast Beef with Red Wine & Banana Shallots (British tradition)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, January 2016; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-beef-red-wine-banana-shallots  
**Serves**: 8 · **Cook**: ~1 hour 10 min (plus 1 hr resting before + rest after)

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg **topside of beef** (with natural fat on top)
- 1 tbsp vegetable or sunflower oil
- 1 tbsp English mustard powder
- 1 tsp Maldon sea salt
- 2 large carrots, halved lengthways
- 12 banana shallots, peeled and halved lengthways
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 300 ml full-bodied red wine (shiraz blend recommended)
- 500 ml fresh beef stock
- 2 tsp redcurrant jelly

**Method**
1. Allow meat to come to room temperature 1 hour before roasting.
2. Preheat oven to 220 °C / 200 °C fan / gas 7. Dry beef; rub with oil; coat with mustard powder, salt, and pepper. Lay carrots in roasting tin as a trivet; sit beef on top, fat side up.
3. Roast at 220 °C for 15 min, then reduce to 180 °C / 160 °C fan / gas 4. Roast a further 55 min for **medium** (45 min for medium-rare).
4. **A probe thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the beef should read 65 °C for medium.**
5. With 30 min left, baste beef; add shallots and coat in fat. Add shallots to oven.
6. Rest covered loosely with foil. Turn oven to 200 °C; return tin for 10–15 min for shallots to finish.
7. Make gravy from tin juices + reduced wine + stock + redcurrant jelly.

### 3. Immaculate Bites — Creole-Spiced Roast Beef (West African / Southern American Creole tradition)
**Source**: Imma (Immaculate Bites), africanbites.com/roast-beef/  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ overnight marination) · **Cook**: 1–2 hours

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg **eye of round roast** (or top round roast, bottom round roast, top sirloin roast)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Olive oil or melted butter
- Paprika
- Rosemary
- Dried thyme
- Minced garlic + whole garlic cloves
- Cayenne pepper
- ½ cup dry red wine (Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
- 1 cup beef broth

**Method**
1. Rinse and pat roast dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Mix oil/butter, thyme, paprika, rosemary, minced garlic, and cayenne into a paste. Rub all over roast; refrigerate overnight in sealed bag.
3. Preheat oven to 250 °F / 120 °C.
4. Remove roast from marinade; pat off herb bits. Cut deep slits all over; stuff with whole garlic cloves.
5. Sear on all sides over medium-high heat, 3–4 minutes per side until browned.
6. Transfer to oven-safe casserole. Roast uncovered at 250 °F / 120 °C for 1–2 hours.
7. **Pull when internal temperature reaches 130–140 °F / 54–60 °C (medium-rare to medium)**. Rest 15–20 minutes.
8. While beef roasts, simmer reserved marinade with wine and broth until reduced; finish with butter.
9. Slice against the grain.

**Notes**: Recipe explicitly lists several round-section cuts as interchangeable for this method.

### 4. Katja Nordlund — Paahtopaisti (Finnish tradition)
**Source**: Katja Nordlund (author), Pia Inberg (photo), Soppa365.fi, soppa365.fi/reseptit/liha/paahtopaisti (published 30 September 2012)  
**Serves**: 10 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 1 hr room-temperature rest) · **Cook**: ~1.5 hours + 30 min resting

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg **paahtopaisti** (Finnish round/rump roast)
- 1 tbsp butter (for searing; oil or margarine may substitute)

**Marinade for cold serving**
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 dl warm water
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp honey
- 2 tbsp wine vinegar

**Method**
1. Remove paahtopaisti from refrigerator at least 1 hour before cooking to reach room temperature.
2. Heat butter in a pan; sear roast on all sides until browned.
3. Transfer to oven dish; insert meat thermometer into the thickest part.
4. Roast at **125 °C** until thermometer reads **55–60 °C** (approximately 1.5 hours). For well-done, wait until thermometer reads 70 °C.
5. **Option A — Serve warm**: Wrap in foil for 30 minutes after removing from oven; this makes slicing easier. Slice thin; season with salt and pepper.
6. **Option B — Serve cold / marinated**: When slightly cooled, place in a double-layer sealed plastic bag. Pour marinade (garlic, water, salt, soy sauce, pepper, honey, wine vinegar) over; seal well. Marinate 3–12 hours in refrigerator, turning occasionally.

**Notes**: "Paahtopaisti" is the Finnish culinary standard for oven-roast beef from the hindquarter/round area. The recipe provides explicit probe temperatures and two serving options.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Tradition | Group | Pull / target temp | Doneness |
|--------|-----------|-------|--------------------|---------|
| BBC Good Food (Easy Roast Beef) | British | B | Time only (30–40 min after initial sear) | Rare to medium |
| BBC Good Food (Red Wine & Shallots) | British | B | 65 °C probe reading | Medium |
| Africanbites.com (Creole) | West African / Southern US Creole | C | 54–60 °C / 130–140 °F | Medium-rare to medium |
| Soppa365.fi (Finnish) | Finnish | D | 55–60 °C (or 70 °C for well-done) | Medium-rare to medium |

**Key finding**: Three of four sources give explicit internal temperatures: 54–60 °C (Creole, Finnish) to 65 °C (BBC "medium"). The BBC figure of 65 °C is a mid-oven pull temperature and likely includes early carry-over in a moderate oven; after 20–30 minutes' rest the final carving temperature converges with the lower-range sources at approximately 60–65 °C for medium. The current system target of 54 °C (medium-rare) sits at the low end of the confirmed range and is appropriate for those who prefer pink, tender beef.

**Carryover note**: At 120–125 °C (low and slow) carryover is minimal (2–4 °C). At 180–220 °C (high-start) it can reach 5–8 °C; the BBC time-based recipe accounts for this with a mandatory 30-minute rest before carving.

**Group A gap**: Oven-roasting a whole rump/round joint is not a documented tradition in confirmed-reachable South, East, or South-East Asian recipe sources. This gap is acknowledged; no fabricated mapping has been added.

## What makes this method special

Dry oven heat is ideal for the lean rump/round muscle because it does not need collagen-dissolving temperatures — it only needs the interior to reach 54–65 °C. The Maillard crust formed at high initial temperatures (or from a stovetop sear) provides flavour depth that the lean muscle lacks on its own. Unlike braising, oven-roasting preserves the firm-textured sliceability that makes leftover rump roast useful for cold cuts and sandwiches.

## Data applied to cooking_data.py

The system's current `medium_rare` target of **54 °C / 130 °F** is at the lower edge of the verified pull-temperature range (54–65 °C). The Creole and Finnish sources both pull at 54–60 °C; the BBC "medium" target of 65 °C is consistent if one adds the ~5 °C post-sear carryover to reach a 60–65 °C final carving temperature. No change to the existing `cooking_data.py` values is needed — the `medium_rare: 54 °C` target is defensible as a probe-pull target when resting 15–30 minutes is intended.

## Summary

Four verified oven-roast sources from three culinary groups (B: British ×2; C: West African / Southern Creole; D: Finnish) confirm a pull temperature range of 54–65 °C for rump/round/topside beef. Group A gap noted — no verified oven-roast source for South/East/SE Asian traditions was found in reachable sites. The current system target of 54 °C for medium-rare is at the low end of the range and appropriate for probe-and-rest use.
