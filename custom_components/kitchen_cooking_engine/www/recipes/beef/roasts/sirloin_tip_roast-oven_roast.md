# Sirloin Tip Roast × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The sirloin tip roast (also called "knuckle" in UK/Australian butchery, "pointe de culotte" in French, and "Rinderknolle" in German) is a lean, boneless roast cut from the quadriceps femoris muscle group at the front of the round, just below the sirloin. Because the quadriceps are heavily used muscles, the meat has low marbling and dense, tight muscle fibres — the structural opposite of the heavily marbled ribeye. This leanness is both its challenge and its advantage: cooked correctly to medium-rare and sliced thinly against the grain, it produces clean, beefy flavour with very little waste; overcooked even slightly, it becomes dry and difficult to chew. Oven roasting suits the sirloin tip in two ways: gentle, low-temperature roasting (200–250°F / 93–120°C) allows enzymatic tenderisation to work on the dense fibres without seizing them, while a high-heat initial blast or reverse-sear delivers the Maillard browning that a lean exterior requires for flavour. Carryover is modest — approximately 3–5°C (5–9°F) in a standard oven roast — because the lean mass has no fat layer to retain heat once removed; the carryover is larger (up to 8°C / 14°F) after a slow low-temp roast where the oven cools the surface slowly.

## Styckesprofil

Sirloin tip roast (kallas "knuckle" i UK/australisk styckning, "pointe de culotte" på franska och "Rinderknolle" på tyska) är en mager, benfri rost från quadriceps femoris på framsidan av round, strax under sirloin. Eftersom quadriceps är kraftigt arbetade muskler har köttet låg marmorering och täta, tighta fibrer – den strukturella motsatsen till den marmorerade ribeye. Magert är både utmaning och fördel: tillagad rätt till medium-rare och skivad tunt mot ådringen ger den ren, nötig smak med lite spill; överkokt ens lite blir den torr och seg. Ugnsrostning passar sirloin tip på två sätt: mild låg-temperatursrostning (93–120 °C) låter enzymatisk mörning arbeta på de täta fibrerna utan att de drar ihop sig, medan en högvärme blast eller reverse-sear ger den Maillard-skorpa som en mager yta behöver. Eftervärmen är modest – cirka 3–5 °C i en standard ugnsrost – eftersom den magra massan saknar fettlager som behåller värme; eftervärmen är större (upp till 8 °C) efter en långsam låg-temp-rost där ugnen svalnar ytan långsamt.
## Source recipes

### 1. Nagi (RecipeTin Eats) — Marinated Roast Beef
**Inspired by**: https://www.recipetineats.com/marinated-roast-beef/  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 10 min (+2–3 days marinating) · **Cook**: 55–60 min · **Rest**: 20–30 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg / 3 lb economical beef roast — explicitly listed as suitable: Eye of Round (US), Bolar Blade (AU), Oyster Blade, Top Blade Roast, Top Sirloin Roast (US) / Rump Roast (AU)
- 1 tbsp olive oil (for drizzling before roast)

*Marinade:*
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire Sauce
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 2½ tbsp balsamic vinegar
- ½ cup (125 ml) soy sauce
- ¼ cup (60 ml) olive oil
- 1 tbsp white sugar
- 2 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried rosemary

*Optional vegetables:*
- 3 potatoes, cut into 5 cm / 2″ chunks
- 4 carrots, peeled, cut into 4 cm / 1.7″ pieces, salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a large zip-lock bag. Add beef. Seal and marinate in the fridge for 2 days (minimum 24 hours, maximum 3 days).
2. Remove beef from fridge 1 hour before cooking to de-chill.
3. Preheat oven to 240°C / 450°F (both fan and regular).
4. Pour all marinade over vegetables in an ovenproof skillet; toss to coat. Remove beef from bag and lay on top of vegetables.
5. Drizzle beef with 1 tbsp olive oil. Roast at 240°C / 450°F for 20 minutes to develop a crust.
6. Reduce oven to 180°C / 350°F (160°C fan). **Insert probe into the centre of the thickest part of the roast**, avoiding thin tapered ends and fat seams. Continue roasting 35–40 minutes (smaller or thinner pieces cook faster — check from 25 minutes).
7. **Pull at 44°C (111°F)**. Note: internal temperature rises approximately 8°C / 14°F while resting.
8. Remove beef to a plate, cover loosely with foil. Rest 20–30 minutes. Final serving temperature: **52°C / 125°F (medium rare)**.
9. Return vegetables to oven to brown during rest if needed. Slice beef thinly against the grain.

### 2. Immaculate Bites (africanbites.com) — Slow-Roasted Roast Beef with Red Wine Sauce
**Inspired by**: https://www.africanbites.com/roast-beef/  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 15 min (+overnight marinating) · **Cook**: 1–2 hours · **Rest**: 15–20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.4 kg / 3 lb beef eye of round roast (or top sirloin roast — explicitly listed as equivalent)
- Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
- 2–3 tbsp olive oil (for searing)
- 2 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp dried rosemary (or 2 sprigs fresh)
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 2 tsp cayenne pepper (reduce to ½ tsp for mild)
- 4–6 garlic cloves, minced (for rub) + 4–6 whole cloves (for inserting into roast)
- 2 tbsp butter
- ½ cup (120 ml) dry red wine (Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
- ½ cup (120 ml) beef broth

**Method**
1. Rinse roast, pat dry. Season all over with salt and pepper.
2. Mix olive oil, paprika, rosemary, thyme, cayenne, and minced garlic in a small bowl. Rub all over beef. Place in a sealed bag or covered bowl and refrigerate overnight.
3. Preheat oven to 250°F / 120°C.
4. Remove roast from fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Remove herb bits from surface.
5. Use a paring knife to cut deep slits all over the roast; stuff each slit with a whole garlic clove.
6. Heat oil (or melt butter) in a skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear roast on all sides, 3–4 minutes per side, until browned.
7. Transfer roast to an oven-safe casserole dish or cast-iron pan. Add vegetables around base if desired.
8. **Insert probe into the thickest part of the roast**, avoiding slits with garlic.
9. Roast at 250°F / 120°C for 1–2 hours (25–30 minutes per pound at this temperature). Check regularly from 1 hour.
10. **Pull at 55°C (130°F)**. Author states done when internal reads 130–140°F (55–60°C) — pull at the lower end for juicier slicing.
11. Rest 15–20 minutes. Final serving temperature: **57–60°C / 135–140°F (medium)**.
12. While roast rests, heat reserved marinade in a small saucepan with red wine and beef broth; simmer until slightly thickened, finish with butter.
13. Slice against the grain; serve with sauce.
