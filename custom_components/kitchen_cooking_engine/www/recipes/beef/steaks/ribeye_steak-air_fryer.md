# Ribeye Steak × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The air fryer cooks a ribeye using rapid hot-air circulation — effectively a compact convection oven running 20–30 °C hotter than a standard oven. For a ribeye steak (2 cm thick), 190–200 °C × 10 minutes produces a result between medium-rare and medium, with a modest exterior crust. For steak-quality crust, some cooks sear on a very hot pan for 1 minute before or after the air fryer. The method is convenient and produces consistent results without the smoke of a pan sear.

Target internal temperatures (air fryer ribeye):
- Medium-rare: **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** — pull around 8–9 min at 200 °C
- Medium: **60–63 °C (140–145 °F)** — pull at 10–12 min

## Source recipes

### 1. ichkoche.at — Schopfsteak aus dem Airfryer (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/schopfsteak-aus-dem-airfryer-rezept-240872 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Austrian marinated steak in the air fryer: 5-ingredient marinade (vinegar, olive oil, paprika, garlic), 190 °C × 10 minutes. 832 user ratings.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 30 min marinate · **Cook**: 10 min

*Note: Schopfsteak (neck/collar steak) is the Austrian equivalent of ribeye/entrecôte — the same fatty, marbled cut used identically.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 190 °C × 10 minutes, turned once at 5 minutes. Adjust for thickness and desired doneness.

**Ingredients (marinade)**
- 2 ribeye/schopf steaks, 2 cm thick
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp sweet paprika powder
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Mix all marinade ingredients thoroughly.
2. Coat steaks in the marinade and leave to soak for at least 30 minutes.
3. Place marinated steaks in the air fryer basket.
4. Cook at **190 °C for 10 minutes**. Turn once at 5 minutes.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for medium-rare (account for 2–3 °C carryover in the hot basket).
6. Rest 3–5 minutes. Serve with Bratkartoffeln (pan-fried potatoes).
*Final resting temperature: 54–57 °C (129–135 °F).*

### 2. BBC Good Food — Air fryer steak (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-steak (fetched 2026-04-24). *British guide: ribeye or sirloin, 200 °C × 6 min one side + 2–3 min second side, with seasoned butter.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–9 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "50°C for rare, 60°C for medium and 70°C for well done." Guide times: 6 min side 1, 2 min (rare) / 3 min (medium) / 4 min (well done) side 2 at 200 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 2 ribeye or sirloin steaks, 200 g each
- 1 tsp rapeseed oil (applied to steaks)
- Salt and pepper
- Seasoned butter: 50 g softened butter + small garlic clove grated + 1 tsp fresh thyme

**Method**
1. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C for 3 minutes.
2. Rub steaks with rapeseed oil and season with salt and pepper.
3. Place in air fryer basket. Cook **6 minutes** on first side.
4. Flip steaks; cook **2 minutes** more for rare, **3 minutes** for medium.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F)** for rare; **57–58 °C (135–136 °F)** for medium.
6. Transfer to a plate. Top each steak with a spoonful of seasoned butter. Rest 2 minutes.
7. Serve with triple-cooked chips or dressed salad.
*Final resting temperature: 50–60 °C (122–140 °F) depending on doneness.*

### 3. GialloZafferano — Roast beef in friggitrice ad aria (Italian tradition)
**Source**: GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Roast-beef-in-friggitrice-ad-aria.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Italian air fryer beef: 200 °C × 10 min (turning halfway), then 180 °C × 20–30 min for a whole roast — scale down proportionally for a single ribeye steak.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–40 min (whole roast; steak approx. 10–15 min)

*Translation: Roast beef in the air fryer — tender and succulent with mustard-honey sauce, minimum effort for maximum result.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "La carne sarà cotta quando raggiungerà la temperatura al cuore di 48–50 °C." (The meat is cooked when it reaches a core temperature of 48–50 °C.) For a steak portion, scale down to 10–15 minutes total.

**Ingredients**
- 1 ribeye steak (or whole ribeye roast, 600–800 g)
- Rosemary sprig
- Salt
- Olive oil

*Mustard-honey sauce:*
- Juice of half a lemon
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp honey
- Pinch of salt
- 4 tbsp olive oil

**Method**
1. Tie meat if cooking as a roast (loop twine around, tuck in rosemary sprig).
2. Season with salt and rub with olive oil on all surfaces.
3. Place in air fryer basket (6-litre capacity or larger). Cook at **200 °C for 10 minutes**, turning halfway.
4. Reduce to **180 °C** and cook **20–30 minutes more** (whole roast) — or skip this step for a single 2 cm steak (already done after first 10 min phase).
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F)** for rare / **54–55 °C (129–131 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. While meat rests, whisk lemon juice, mustard, honey, salt, and olive oil into a smooth sauce.
7. Slice; plate and drizzle with mustard-honey sauce. Serve hot or at room temperature.
*Final resting temperature: 50–55 °C (122–131 °F).*
