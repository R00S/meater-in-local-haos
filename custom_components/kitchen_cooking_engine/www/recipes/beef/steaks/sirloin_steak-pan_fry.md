# Sirloin Steak × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan frying a sirloin steak uses a moderate-to-high heat with a small amount of fat (oil or butter) in the pan. Compared to pan searing, pan frying is slightly gentler — the fat acts as a heat-transfer buffer and self-bastes the meat, helping the lean sirloin retain more moisture. The steak is flipped every 1–2 minutes to build even crust development and prevent localized overcooking. Adding butter, garlic and thyme during the last 2 minutes and basting continuously is the French bistro standard (*beurre monté* technique) and compensates for the lean cut's lower intramuscular fat. Carryover is 4–5 °C from a hot pan.

## Styckesprofil

Pannstekning av entrecôtebiff använder måttlig till hög värme med en liten mängd fett (olja eller smör) i pannan. Jämfört med pannsearing är pannstekning något varsam — fettet fungerar som värmeöverföringsbuffert och bastar köttet, vilket hjälper den magra entrecôten att behålla mer fuktighet. Biffen vänds var 1–2 minut för att bygga jämn skorputveckling och förhindra lokaliserad överkokning.

Att tillsätta smör, vitlök och timjan under de sista 2 minuterna och basta kontinuerligt är den franska bistro-standarden (*beurre monté*-teknik) och kompenserar för snittets lägre intramuskulära fett. Eftertillagning är 4–5 °C från en het panna. Innertemperatur: 54–57 °C medium-rare. Dra vid 50–52 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Delia Smith — Pan-Fried Sirloin with Blue Cheese Sauce (British tradition)
**Source**: Delia Smith, *Delia's Complete Cookery Course* (BBC Books, 1989)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 250–300 g each, 2.5 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) vegetable oil
- 30 g unsalted butter
- **Blue cheese sauce**: 60 g Stilton or Gorgonzola, crumbled; 60 ml (¼ cup) double cream; 5 ml (1 tsp) Worcestershire sauce; pinch of cayenne; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Season steaks with salt and pepper. Heat a large, heavy frying pan over medium-high heat. Add oil; when hot, add steaks.
2. Cook 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre after placing in the pan.
3. In a small saucepan, melt blue cheese into cream over low heat; add Worcestershire and cayenne.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Delia's British standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with blue cheese sauce. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British home-cooking standard).

### 2. Julia Child — Steak Sauté Bercy (French classical tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Volume 1* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks (*romsteck* — French term for top sirloin), 250–300 g each
- Fine salt and white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) clarified butter
- 2 shallots, finely minced
- 80 ml (⅓ cup) dry white wine
- 80 ml (⅓ cup) beef stock
- 40 g cold unsalted butter (for sauce)
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Season steaks; heat clarified butter in a wide pan over medium-high heat.
2. Sear 3 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
3. Remove steaks to rest. Drain fat. Sauté shallots; deglaze with wine; reduce; add stock; reduce; swirl in cold butter; add parsley. This is *sauce Bercy*.
4. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Julia's French standard for sirloin: *saignant*, bright red interior.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Spoon sauce Bercy over. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*saignant*, French classical standard).

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Pan-Fried Sirloin with Rocket Salad (British contemporary tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *How to Eat* (Chatto & Windus, 1998)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 250–300 g each
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- 20 g unsalted butter
- 80 g fresh rocket (*arugula*), to serve
- Shaved Parmesan and lemon juice, to serve

**Method**
1. Season steaks. Heat a heavy pan over medium-high heat; add oil.
2. Add steaks. Cook 3 minutes per side; flip once.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Add butter; baste briefly.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nigella's standard: a warm pink interior.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve on a bed of rocket with Parmesan shavings and lemon juice. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British contemporary standard).

### 4. Nobu Matsuhisa — Pan-Fried Sirloin with Wasabi Salsa (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Nobu Matsuhisa, *Nobu Miami: The Party Cookbook* (Kodansha International, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 250–300 g each
- Fine salt and white pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- **Wasabi salsa**: 10 g (2 tsp) wasabi paste; 30 ml soy sauce; 15 ml lemon juice; 15 ml olive oil; 5 ml mirin; 30 g red onion, finely diced; 30 g tomato, finely diced

**Method**
1. Mix wasabi salsa; set aside.
2. Season steaks; heat pan over high heat; add oil.
3. Sear 3 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nobu's target for pan-fried sirloin: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Spoon wasabi salsa alongside. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Japanese standard).

### 5. Neil Perry — Pan-Fried Sirloin with Bone Marrow (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Australian sirloin steaks, 300–350 g each
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- **Bone marrow butter**: 60 g raw bone marrow, softened; 40 g softened unsalted butter; 10 g flat-leaf parsley; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix bone marrow butter; refrigerate.
2. Season steaks. Heat a heavy pan over high heat; add oil.
3. Sear 3–4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Top with bone marrow butter to melt over the resting steak. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian premium standard).
