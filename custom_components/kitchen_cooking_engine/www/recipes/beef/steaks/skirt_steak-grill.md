# Skirt Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilled skirt steak is the centrepiece of Tex-Mex and Mexican *arrachera* preparations, where the thin, coarsely-grained diaphragm muscle cooks in minutes over intense heat. Outside skirt steak (the true diaphragm) is the superior choice — it is thicker, more uniform and more intensely flavoured than inside skirt. On a grill, the thin profile (1.5–2 cm) means it cooks from cold to done in 3–4 minutes per side over maximum heat. The intense marbling in the loose grain bastes the steak as it cooks; flare-ups are common and actually contribute desirable char flavour. Skirt steak must be sliced thin across the grain before serving to be edible; the grain runs across the width of the steak, so slices should be 5 mm thin cut on a bias. Carryover is 4–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Bobby Flay — Grilled Skirt Steak Fajitas (Tex-Mex tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak
- **Marinade**: 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil; 4 garlic cloves, minced; 30 ml (2 tbsp) lime juice; 1 jalapeño, minced; 5 g ground cumin; 5 g chilli powder; 5 g smoked paprika; 10 g cilantro, chopped; fine salt
- Flour tortillas, sautéed bell peppers and onions, guacamole, pico de gallo, sour cream, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix marinade; coat skirt steak; marinate 2 hours. Pat dry before grilling.
2. Preheat grill to maximum. Clean and oil grates.
3. Place skirt steak on the hottest part of the grill.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end of the steak, angling along the length toward the centre.
5. Grill 3–4 minutes per side; do not move.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Flay's Tex-Mex standard: medium-rare. The thin profile means the probe reading may lag behind actual surface temperature.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin on a bias against the grain. Serve in warm tortillas. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Tex-Mex tradition).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Grilled *Arrachera* with Salsa de Tomatillo (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *My Mexico: A Culinary Odyssey* (Clarkson Potter, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak (*arrachera*)
- **Marinade**: 4 garlic cloves, minced; 30 ml (2 tbsp) lime juice; 5 g dried Mexican oregano (*orégano mexicano*); 5 g ground cumin; fine salt; 15 ml (1 tbsp) oil
- **Salsa de tomatillo**: 300 g *tomatillos*, husked; 2 serrano chillies; 1 garlic clove; small handful fresh coriander; salt

**Method**
1. Marinate steak 1 hour. Roast tomatillos and serranos on comal or in oven; blend salsa; season.
2. Preheat grill to maximum.
3. Grill skirt steak 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Kennedy's standard: medium-rare. *Arrachera* must be sliced thin and served immediately — it toughens on standing.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin on a bias. Serve with tomatillo salsa, tortillas. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, traditional Mexican standard).

### 3. Francis Mallmann — Grilled *Fraldinha* (Brazilian / Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g skirt steak (*fraldinha* — Brazilian term for thin skirt or flank region)
- Coarse sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal or wood embers
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Season steak with coarse salt and pepper.
2. Build a charcoal fire; allow to reach white-ash embers.
3. Grill *fraldinha* directly over embers 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Mallmann's preference for thin skirt cuts: *jugoso*.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Brazilian/Argentine standard).

### 4. Steven Raichlen — Grilled Skirt Steak (American BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak
- **Marinade**: 30 ml soy sauce; 30 ml Worcestershire sauce; 30 ml olive oil; 4 garlic cloves, minced; 5 g (1 tsp) onion powder; freshly ground black pepper; squeeze of lemon

**Method**
1. Marinate steak 1 hour; pat dry.
2. Preheat grill to maximum.
3. Grill skirt steak 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's medium-rare standard for skirt steak.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin on a bias against the grain. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 5. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled Skirt Steak with Sumac and Pomegranate (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi and Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g skirt steak
- **Marinade**: 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 10 g *sumac* (*sumac* — dried crushed berry with sharp lemony tang, Middle Eastern spice); 5 g ground allspice; fine salt and black pepper
- **Pomegranate dressing**: 30 ml pomegranate molasses; 30 ml olive oil; 15 ml lemon juice; 20 g pomegranate seeds; small handful flat-leaf parsley; salt

**Method**
1. Marinate 2 hours; wipe excess. Mix pomegranate dressing.
2. Preheat grill to maximum.
3. Grill 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Ottolenghi's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin on a bias; drizzle with pomegranate dressing. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Middle Eastern tradition).
