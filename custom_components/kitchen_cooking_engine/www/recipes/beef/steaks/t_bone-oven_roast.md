# T-Bone Steak × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting a T-bone is most effective as a reverse-sear: start at 120–135 °C to bring the whole steak to within 10 °C of the target temperature with zero gradient, then blast in a cast-iron pan or under the broiler at maximum heat for 60–90 seconds per side to form the crust. This eliminates the overcooked grey ring that conventional high-heat sear-then-oven methods leave.

The two-muscle geometry (striploin + tenderloin) is actually *helped* by the reverse sear: the slow low oven equilibrates the temperature across both muscles, minimising the differential. The insulating bone still causes the meat nearest the bone to lag 3–5 °C behind — this is desirable for anyone who prefers the tenderloin slightly rarer than the strip. MEATER probe in the striploin side, away from the bone. Carryover after the oven phase is 4–6 °C; after the final high-heat sear, 2–3 °C.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsrostning av en T-bone är mest effektiv som en reverse-sear: börja vid 120–135 °C för att föra hela biffen till inom 10 °C av måltemperaturen med noll gradient, bryna sedan i en gjutjärnspanna eller under broiler vid maximal värme i 60–90 sekunder per sida för att forma skorpan. Detta eliminerar det överkokta grå ring som konventionella högvärme-bryn-sedan-ugn-metoder lämnar.

Den tvåmuskelgeometrin (striploin + filé) *hjälps* faktiskt av reverse sear: den långsamma låga ugnen equilibrerar temperaturen över båda musklerna, vilket minimerar differentialen. Det isolerande benet orsakar fortfarande att köttet närmast benet ligger 3–5 °C efter — detta är önskvärt för alla som föredrar filén något rareare än stripen. MEATER-sond i striploin-sidan, bort från benet. Eftervärme efter ugnsfasen är 4–6 °C; efter den slutliga högvärmebryningen, 2–3 °C.
## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Reverse-Sear T-Bone (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Cook's Country TV Show Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2015)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+overnight dry brine) · **Cook**: 30–45 min low oven + 3 min sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone steak, 700–900 g, 4–5 cm thick
- Kosher salt (applied overnight)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for post-oven sear)

**Method**
1. Dry-brine T-bone overnight, refrigerated uncovered.
2. Preheat oven to 120 °C (250 °F). Place steak on a wire rack over a rimmed baking sheet.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone, into the centre of the striploin muscle.
4. Roast in the low oven until internal temperature of the striploin reaches 48–50 °C — approximately 30–45 minutes.
5. **Pull from low oven at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F).** The surface will be perfectly dry — ideal for searing.
6. Heat a large cast-iron pan to absolute maximum; add oil. Sear each side 45–60 seconds; sear the fat edge 30 seconds.
7. Final serving temperature: **53–57 °C / 127–135 °F** in the striploin (medium-rare, reverse-sear standard).

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Oven-Finished T-Bone (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4 min sear + 10–14 min oven · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700–800 g
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 40 g unsalted butter
- 3 garlic cloves, skin-on
- 3 sprigs thyme

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Season T-bone.
2. Heat a large oven-proof cast-iron pan over maximum stovetop heat; add oil.
3. Sear 2 minutes per side on the stovetop.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
5. Add butter, garlic, thyme; baste. Transfer entire pan to the oven.
6. Roast 8–12 minutes. Baste once during oven roasting.
7. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Ramsay's standard: medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, restaurant standard).

### 3. Heston Blumenthal — Ultra-Low Oven T-Bone (British Modernist tradition)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *Heston Blumenthal at Home* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2011)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1 h at 52 °C oven + 2 min sear · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700–900 g, 4–5 cm thick
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) vegetable oil (for the final sear)

**Method**
1. Season T-bone; place on a wire rack.
2. Set oven to exactly **52 °C (126 °F)** — the target internal temperature for the striploin. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
3. Cook in the 52 °C oven for 1–1.5 hours. The oven cannot overshoot the target temperature. The surface becomes extremely dry — ideal for searing.
4. Heat a cast-iron pan to maximum; add oil; sear each side 60–90 seconds.
5. **Oven temperature: 52 °C (126 °F).** Final serving temperature: **52 °C / 126 °F** at the core of the striploin (medium-rare, Blumenthal ultra-low standard).

### 4. Alice Waters — Roasted T-Bone with Herb Butter (California tradition)
**Source**: Alice Waters, *The Chez Panisse Menu Cookbook* (Random House, 1982)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 4 min sear + 10–14 min oven · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700 g
- Coarse sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra virgin olive oil
- **Herb butter**: 80 g softened butter; 20 g mixed herbs (thyme, rosemary, flat-leaf parsley); 1 garlic clove, minced; salt

**Method**
1. Mix herb butter; refrigerate. Dry-brine 1 hour; brush with oil.
2. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Heat a large oven-proof pan over high heat.
3. Sear T-bone 2 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
4. Transfer pan to oven; roast 8–12 minutes.
5. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F)** in the striploin. Waters' farm-to-table standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Top with herb butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, California tradition).

### 5. Fergus Henderson — Roasted T-Bone (British tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Complete Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4 min sear + 10–14 min oven · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700–900 g
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) beef dripping
- Mustard and pickles, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Season T-bone.
2. Heat a large oven-proof pan with dripping over maximum stovetop heat.
3. Sear 2 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
4. Transfer to oven; roast 8–12 minutes.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Henderson's standard: "must be rosy and weeping."
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with mustard and pickles. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (rare to medium-rare, British standard).
