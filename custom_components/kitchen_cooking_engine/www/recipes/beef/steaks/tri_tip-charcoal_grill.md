# Tri Tip × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Tri-tip on charcoal benefits from the classic Santa Maria barbecue tradition — the original Californian preparation. The key is a 2-zone charcoal setup: sear directly over the coals first for a crust, then finish on the indirect side to bring the interior to temperature safely. The tapered shape means the thin end will always be more done; probe the thickest section. Red oak is the traditional Santa Maria wood; hickory and mesquite are alternatives.

Target internal temperatures (charcoal grill):
- Medium-rare: **pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)**

## Styckesprofil

Tri-tip på träkol drar nytta av den klassiska Santa Maria-grilltraditionen — den ursprungliga kaliforniska beredningen. Nyckeln är en tvåzons träkolsuppställning: sear direkt över kolorna först för en krusta, avsluta sedan på den indirekta sidan för att föra insidan till rätt temperatur. Det oregelbundna triangulära snittet kräver uppmärksamhet: den tunna spetsen kokar fortare. Innertemperatur: 54–60 °C. Eftertillagning: 3–5 °C. Skär alltid tvärs mot fibern.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Ball tip i grill (charcoal adaptation) (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ball-tip/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Two-zone charcoal: indirect heat 175 °C (or lower), 40 minutes per kg, core 56 °C. Rest 15 minutes.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 40–50 min charcoal indirect + 5 min direct sear

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Hold grillen nede på 175 grader." (Hold the grill at 175 °C.) Core target: 56 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg tri-tip
- Olive oil, garlic, rosemary marinade (4 hr or overnight — see tri_tip-oven_roast)
- Rapeseed oil for grill grate

**Method**
1. Marinate tri-tip as per oven recipe. Score fat cap.
2. Prepare two-zone charcoal fire: mound coals on one half; leave other half empty. Target indirect side ambient temperature: **175 °C**.
3. Grease grill grate with oil.
4. Place tri-tip on indirect zone, fat-side up. Close lid.
5. Cook approximately **40 min for 1 kg**. MEATER monitors from inside the meat.
6. In the final 5 minutes, move to direct zone for a quick crust on each side (2–3 min).
7. **Pull at 56 °C (133 °F)**.
8. Rest 15 minutes. Slice first in half at the centre seam, then slice each half against its grain.
*Final resting temperature: 58–60 °C (136–140 °F).*

### 2. Immaculate Bites (africanbites.com) — charcoal grill adaptation (Californian / West African tradition)
**Inspired by**: Imma Adamu, africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/juicy-smoked-tri-tip/ and https://www.africanbites.com/oven-tri-tip-roast/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *The africanbites smoked tri-tip method applied to a charcoal grill: hot coals on one side, meat on the other; add flavoured wood chips for smoke.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 30–40 min charcoal indirect + 5 min sear

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Place tri-tip on the other side of the grill farthest from the coals." Core target 135–140 °F (57–60 °C) for medium.

**Ingredients**
- 1 tri-tip
- Red wine/garlic marinade (as per oven recipe) — 1 hr minimum
- Hickory or mesquite wood chips (soaked 30 min)

**Method**
1. Prepare and marinate tri-tip. Score fat cap. Let come to room temperature.
2. Mound hot coals on one side. Place soaked wood chips on the coals. Add a small tin of water beside the coals to catch drips.
3. Place tri-tip on the **side farthest from the coals**. Close lid.
4. Cook 30–40 minutes with lid closed, checking every 10–15 minutes. Flip when using charcoal if one side browns faster.
5. Once near temperature, move to direct heat zone for 2–3 minutes per side to create crust.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 57 °C (135 °F)** for medium-rare.
7. Rest 10–15 minutes. Slice against the grain.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Das perfekte Steak mit Steakkruste (adapted for charcoal grill) (German tradition)
**Inspired by**: Lumacone, Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3258661484569054/Das-perfekte-Steak-mit-Steakkruste.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *The constant-turning pan method translated to charcoal: direct zone as the "pan", frequent turning for even Maillard crust.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 6–10 min over direct charcoal

**Temperature guidance (from recipe adapted)**: "Crust transitions from light to dark brown = your stop point." De-chill 2+ hours before grilling. Butter rest 2–4 minutes in foil.

**Ingredients**
- 2 tri-tip steaks (sliced 2 cm thick), or 1 small whole tri-tip
- Neutral oil (rubbed on meat)
- Salt and pepper (after)
- Knob of butter (for resting)
- Rosemary, onion (for improvised grill sauce)

**Method**
1. De-chill tri-tip steaks 2–2.5 hours at room temperature. Oil lightly.
2. Get direct charcoal zone very hot.
3. Place steaks on direct zone. Turn every **5–10 seconds** — continuous turning, not waiting for each side to cook. A crust develops progressively.
4. After 3rd–4th turn the crust is visible; continue until it goes from light to deep brown.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)**.
6. Transfer to foil; add butter knob; season with salt and pepper. Rest **3–4 minutes** while the butter melts over the hot meat.
7. Serve with pan sauce made from onion, garlic, rosemary in a grill-side pan.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*
