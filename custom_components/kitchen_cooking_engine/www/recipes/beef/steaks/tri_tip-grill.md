# Tri Tip × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The tri-tip on the grill benefits from a two-zone setup (direct for crust, indirect for interior). Because it is an irregularly shaped triangular cut (thicker in the centre, tapered at the ends), the thin end will overcook before the thick part reaches temperature if cooked entirely over direct heat. A low indirect zone (175 °C) for 20–30 minutes followed by a quick crust sear gives the best result. The same marinade used for oven roasting works excellently on the grill — the charred edges add another flavour dimension.

Target internal temperatures (grill):
- Medium-rare: **pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)**, rest 10–15 min to **57–60 °C (135–140 °F)**

## Styckesprofil

Tri-tip på grillen drar nytta av tvåzonsuppställning (direkt för krusta, indirekt för insida). Eftersom det är ett oregelbundet triangelformat snitt (tjockare i mitten, avsmalnande i ändarna) kokar den tunna änden fortare. Hantera formen genom att lägga den tunna spetsen mot den svalare kanten av indirekt zon. Innertemperatur: 54–60 °C. Eftertillagning: 3–5 °C. Skär alltid tvärs mot fibern för möra skivor.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Ball tip i grill (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ball-tip/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Danish gas grill indirect method: hold grill at 175 °C, 40 min for 1 kg, pull at 56 °C. Rest 15 minutes before carving.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 4 hr marinade · **Cook**: 40 min indirect grill

*Translation: Ball tip (tri-tip) on the grill — indirect heat, 175 °C or lower, until core temperature reaches 56 °C. Rest 15 minutes.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Hold helst grillen nede på 175 grader, og endda gerne med en endnu lavere temperatur. Tag stegen af når den når en kernetemperatur på 56 grader." (Hold the grill at 175 °C or lower. Remove when core temperature reaches 56 °C.)

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg ball tip / tri-tip
- 5 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp salt
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp dried rosemary
- Freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Prepare and marinate tri-tip as per oven method (4 hours or overnight in olive oil, garlic, rosemary, salt, pepper marinade). See tri_tip-oven_roast for marinade details.
2. Set up 2-zone grill: one side lit (direct), one side off (indirect). Target ambient temperature on indirect side: **175 °C**.
3. Place tri-tip on indirect zone, fat-side up. Close lid.
4. Cook for approximately **40 minutes** for a 1 kg piece — check with MEATER probe.
5. **Pull at 56 °C (133 °F)**. The triangular shape means the tapered end will be more done — probe the thickest part.
6. For a crust, move to direct heat for 2–3 minutes per side in the final 5 minutes.
7. Rest on a chopping board **15 minutes**. Slice across the grain, changing direction at the centre.
*Final resting temperature: 58–60 °C (136–140 °F).*

### 2. Immaculate Bites (africanbites.com) — Oven Tri Tip Roast (marinade for grill) (Californian / West African tradition)
**Inspired by**: Imma Adamu, africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/oven-tri-tip-roast/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *The red wine and garlic marinade is equally excellent on the grill — sear first on direct heat to replicate the stovetop step, then finish on indirect heat.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinade · **Cook**: 25–30 min grill

**Temperature guidance (from recipe adapted for grill)**: "The internal temperature is 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare and 145°F (63°C) for medium." Adapted: sear on direct heat first, finish on indirect to target temperature.

**Ingredients**
- 1 tri-tip, 700 g–1 kg
- Red wine and garlic marinade (see tri_tip-oven_roast for full marinade ingredients)
- 1 tbsp olive oil

**Method**
1. Marinate tri-tip as per the oven recipe at least 1 hour (overnight better). Score the fat cap.
2. Set up 2-zone grill (direct + indirect). Preheat direct side to high.
3. Sear tri-tip fat-side down over direct heat **4–5 minutes** until dark brown. Flip; sear 2 minutes.
4. Move to indirect zone. Close lid; cook 20–25 minutes until core reaches 57 °C.
5. Insert MEATER probe into thickest part. **Pull at 57 °C (135 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Rest 5–10 minutes. Slice in half then slice each half against the grain.
7. Reduce reserved marinade in a pan for a sauce; serve alongside.
*Final resting temperature: 58–62 °C (136–144 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Das perfekte Steak mit Steakkruste (German tradition — constant-turning method)
**Inspired by**: Lumacone, Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3258661484569054/Das-perfekte-Steak-mit-Steakkruste.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *For tri-tip steaks (sliced from the roast): constant-turning grill method for the best crust with maximum röstaromas. 90 ratings, 4.8/5 stars.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 2–2.5 hr de-chill · **Cook**: 6–8 min + 4 min rest

*Translation: The perfect steak with steak crust. "I have long searched for the perfect method for a beautiful crispy crust with a lot of röstaromas." Key technique: constant turning.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Crust colour (light to dark brown) indicates doneness. "The meat firms up steadily — decide when your perfect doneness is reached." At 6 minutes constant-turning → medium.

**Ingredients**
- 2 tri-tip steaks (1.5–2 cm slices from the roast), or 1 whole small tri-tip
- Neutral oil (e.g. rapeseed)
- Salt and pepper (added after searing)
- Butter (knob, added when crust is done)
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 1 onion or shallot (for pan sauce)

**Method**
1. Remove steak from refrigerator **2–2.5 hours ahead**. Allow to fully de-chill to room temperature — cold meat causes contraction on the hot grill and loses juice.
2. Lightly oil the steak.
3. Heat grill (or heavy pan on grill/stove) to very hot. Place steak.
4. Wait **5 seconds**, then turn. Keep turning every 5–10 seconds — continuous flipping is the method. A beautiful crust forms from the 3rd or 4th turn.
5. Continue turning as the crust progressively deepens from pale to dark brown. The meat firms with each turn. Stop when crust transitions from light to dark brown.
6. Turn off heat. Add a knob of butter; season with salt and pepper. Toss steak briefly in the butter.
7. Wrap in foil; rest **2–4 minutes**.
8. Insert MEATER probe before resting. **Core should read 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** for medium.
9. For a pan sauce: return pan to medium heat; add sliced onion, garlic, rosemary. Sauté until soft. Pour a spoonful over the plated steak.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*
