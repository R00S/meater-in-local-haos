# Tri Tip × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The tri-tip (also known as triangle steak, Santa Maria steak, bottom sirloin, and in German-speaking countries as Bürgermeisterstück or ball tip) is a triangular cut from the bottom sirloin. It is lean with moderate marbling, making it ideal for oven roasting with a marinade to add juiciness. Typical weight: 700 g–1.5 kg. It must be sliced against the grain (the grain changes direction in the centre) for tenderness. Temperature target: 54–57 °C (129–135 °F) for medium-rare.

Target internal temperatures (oven roast):
- Medium-rare: **pull at 44–50 °C (111–122 °F)**, rest 20–30 min to reach **52–57 °C (125–135 °F)**
- Medium: **pull at 55–57 °C (131–135 °F)**, rest to **63 °C (145 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Immaculate Bites (africanbites.com) — Oven Tri Tip Roast (Californian / West African tradition)
**Source**: Imma Adamu, africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/oven-tri-tip-roast/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"California's pride: the Tri Tip. A unique beef cut that is part roast and part steak — 100% delicious whether grilled or roasted in the oven."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinate (overnight better) · **Cook**: 20 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Remove when internal temperature reaches 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare and 145°F (63°C) for medium. Let rest 5–10 minutes."

**Ingredients (marinade)**
- 1 tri-tip, 700 g–1 kg (fat layer scored but left on)
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh thyme (or dried)
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp dried basil
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3 tbsp red wine
- 1 tsp onion powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 tbsp olive oil (for pan-searing)

**Method**
1. Trim excess fat from tri-tip, leaving a thin layer. Score the fat layer in a criss-cross pattern with a knife — cutting through the fat but not the meat.
2. Mix garlic, thyme, paprika, basil, olive oil, red wine, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Rub marinade all over the meat. Refrigerate minimum 1 hour (overnight for best flavour).
3. Preheat oven to **425 °F / 220 °C**.
4. In a large cast-iron skillet, heat 1 tbsp oil on high. Sear tri-tip fat-side down for **4–5 minutes** until deeply browned. Flip; sear 2 more minutes.
5. Transfer skillet to preheated oven. Roast **15–20 minutes** depending on weight. Use MEATER probe.
6. Insert probe into the thickest part. **Pull from oven at 135 °F / 57 °C** for medium-rare.
7. Rest on a plate tented with foil for 5–10 minutes.
8. Slice first in half then slice each half against the grain. The grain direction changes in the centre of the tri-tip — adjust accordingly.
9. While tri-tip rests, reduce reserved marinade in a saucepan to a sauce. Drizzle over sliced meat.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 2. Madens Verden — Ball tip oksesteg (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ball-tip/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Ball tip (Danish name for tri-tip / bottom sirloin) with olive oil, garlic, and rosemary marinade roasted at 160 °C fan — "mør, rosa og saftig" (tender, pink and juicy).*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 4 hr marinade · **Cook**: 40–45 min

*Translation: Ball tip (tri-tip) — "Take it out when it has a core temperature of 56 °C — then it is tender, pink and juicy without being the least bit raw."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Brug et stegetermometer, og tag stegen ud når den har en kernetemperatur på 56 grader." (Use a meat thermometer; remove when it has a core temperature of 56 °C.)

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg ball tip / tri-tip
- 5 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp salt
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp dried rosemary
- Freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Trim sinew and any dirty bits from the meat. Leave the fat cap on top; score in a diamond pattern.
2. Mix olive oil, salt, finely chopped garlic, rosemary, and pepper. Massage marinade all over the meat.
3. Place in a freezer bag. Refrigerate at least **4 hours** (overnight for best results).
4. Preheat oven to **160 °C fan** (conventional or varmluft). Put a small roasting dish or ovenproof dish in to preheat with a little water in the base.
5. Place marinated tri-tip in the dish. Roast for approximately **40–45 minutes**, depending on how hot your oven runs.
6. Insert MEATER probe from the thickest end. **Pull at 56 °C (133 °F)**.
7. Rest on a chopping board for **15 minutes** before slicing — essential for juice redistribution.
8. Serve with Hasselback potatoes and spidskål salad. The roasting juices can be thickened with a little cornflour for gravy.
*Final resting temperature: 58–60 °C (136–140 °F).*

### 3. RecipeTin Eats — Marinated Roast Beef (Australian tradition, adapted for tri-tip)
**Source**: Nagi Maehashi, RecipeTin Eats; https://www.recipetineats.com/marinated-roast-beef/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Marinade with garlic, soy, balsamic, Dijon, and Worcestershire, marinated 2–3 days; starts high at 240 °C, finishes at 180 °C. Pull at 44 °C; rises to 52 °C after 20–30 min rest.*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 2–3 days marinade + 1 hr de-chill · **Cook**: 55–60 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Insert meat thermometer in the centre; registers 44 °C / 111 °F for medium-rare. Rest 20–30 min; internal temp rises to 52 °C / 125 °F — perfect medium-rare."

**Doneness chart (from recipe)**:
| Doneness | Pull from oven | After 20–30 min rest |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Medium-rare | 44 °C (111 °F) | 52 °C (125 °F) |
| Medium | 50 °C (122 °F) | 58 °C (136 °F) |
| Well done | 60 °C (140 °F) | 68 °C (154 °F) |

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg tri-tip (or any economical roast beef cut)
- 1 tbsp olive oil (for roasting)
- 3 potatoes + 4 carrots, chunked (optional vegetable base)

*Marinade:*
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 2.5 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- ½ cup (125 ml) soy sauce
- ¼ cup (60 ml) olive oil
- 1 tbsp white sugar
- 2 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried rosemary

**Method**
1. Mix all marinade ingredients in a large zip-lock bag. Add beef; marinate **2–3 days** in refrigerator (minimum 24 hours).
2. Remove beef from fridge **1 hour before** cooking.
3. Toss vegetables in the marinade and place in an ovenproof skillet. Set beef on top.
4. **High-temp phase**: Preheat oven to 240 °C (460 °F). Drizzle beef with oil; roast **20 minutes**.
5. **Lower-temp phase**: Turn oven down to 180 °C (350 °F) / 160 °C fan. Roast a further **35–40 minutes**.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull from oven at 44 °C (111 °F)** for medium-rare.
7. Transfer beef to a plate. Cover loosely with foil. Rest **20–30 minutes** — temperature rises to 52 °C (125 °F).
8. Return vegetables to oven to brown while beef rests.
9. Slice very thinly against the grain. Serve with roasted vegetables and optional gravy (deglaze skillet with beef stock).
*Final resting temperature: 52 °C (125 °F).*
