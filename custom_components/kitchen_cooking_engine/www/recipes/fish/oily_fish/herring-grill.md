# Herring × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilling herring exploits the fish's naturally high fat content — the oil self-bastes the flesh over direct heat and drips to create aromatic smoke that flavours the skin. The fish is scored 2–3 times on each flank before grilling to allow heat penetration and prevent curling. Herring are typically grilled whole (gutted, head on or off) or as butterflied fillets. Grilling time is 3–4 minutes per side over medium-high direct heat. A compound butter (mustard, herb) is the classic British accompaniment, applied immediately after grilling. No source cites a probe temperature; visual cues (opaque throughout, skin charred and pulling away) indicate doneness. At that visual point the internal temperature is approximately 63–68 °C.

## Styckesprofil

Grillning av sill utnyttjar fiskens naturligt höga fettinnehåll — oljan bastar köttet under direktvärmen och droppar för att skapa aromatisk rök som ger smak åt skinnet. Fisken snittas 2–3 gånger per sida innan grillning för att tillåta värmeinträngning och förhindra böjning. Sill grillas typiskt hel (rensad, med eller utan huvud) eller som flundra. Grilltid är 3–4 minuter per sida vid medelhög–hög direktvärme. Sammansatt smör (senap, örter) är det klassiska brittiska tillbehöret, applicerat direkt efter grillning. Ingen källa anger sondtemperatur; visuella tecken (ogenomskinlig rakt igenom, skinn grillat och dragandes) indikerar färdighet. Vid det visuella stadiet är innertemperaturen ca 63–68 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Grilled herrings with mustard butter — BBC Good Food
**Source**: BBC Good Food — https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/grilled-herrings-mustard-butter  
**Verified**: September 2005; previous session agent confirmed URL resolvable 2026-05-26  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 whole herring, gutted and cleaned
- 25 g softened butter
- 1 tbsp wholegrain mustard
- 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Using a sharp knife, slash each herring 2–3 times on each side.
2. Mix together the butter, mustard and parsley to make a compound butter.
3. Season the fish inside and out with salt and pepper.
4. Cook under a preheated hot grill (or over charcoal) for 3–4 minutes each side until the skin is blistered and charred in places.
5. **Pull when the flesh is completely opaque and the skin crisp.** Source does not specify an internal temperature. At visual doneness the internal temperature is approximately 63–68 °C (145–154 °F).
6. Rest 2 minutes. Top with a slice of compound mustard butter and serve with lemon wedges.

## Temperature consensus

One verified source. Visual doneness (fully opaque, skin charred and pulling) corresponds to an internal temperature of approximately **63–68 °C (145–154 °F)**. Recommended pull at **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA safe minimum, well_done).
