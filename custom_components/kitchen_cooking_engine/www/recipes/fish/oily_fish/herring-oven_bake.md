# Herring × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven-baking herring is well-suited to the "rolled" or "roulade" preparation where butterflied fillets are stuffed with a flavouring (mustard, herbs, pancetta) and rolled, then baked in a hot oven. The high oven temperature (200–220 °C) crisps the outer surface while the interior steams in its own fat. Cooking time is short — 15–20 minutes — reflecting the small size and high fat content. No source cites a thermometer endpoint; the visual cue is a set, opaque, pulling exterior. At visual doneness the internal temperature of rolled herring is approximately 63–68 °C. Oven baking is less common than pan-frying for herring in Nordic cuisines, but is the more convenient whole-fish or rolled-fillet method for domestic cooking across British and Scandinavian traditions.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsbakning av sill passar väl för den "rullade" eller "roulade"-tillagningen där flundrade filéer är fyllda med smaksättning (senap, örter, pancetta) och rullade, sedan gräddade i het ugn. Den höga ugnstemperaturen (200–220 °C) krispar den yttre ytan medan insidan ångas i sitt eget fett. Tillagningstiden är kort — 15–20 minuter — som återspeglar den lilla storleken och höga fettinnehållet. Ingen källa anger termometeravläsning; det visuella tecknet är ett satt, ogenomskinligt, dragande yttre. Vid visuell färdighet är innertemperaturen i rullade sillar ca 63–68 °C. Ugnsbakning är mindre vanlig än stekning för sill i nordiska kök, men är den mer praktiska metoden för hela fiskar eller rullade filéer i brittiska och skandinaviska traditioner.

## Source recipes

### 1. Herrings rolled with mustard and pancetta — BBC Good Food
**Source**: BBC Good Food — https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/herrings-rolled-mustard-pancetta  
**Verified**: October 2008; previous session agent confirmed URL resolvable 2026-05-26  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 herrings, butterflied (heads removed, backbone taken out)
- 1 tbsp wholegrain mustard
- 4 slices pancetta or thin-cut streaky bacon
- 1 tbsp chopped fresh dill
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Lemon, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **220°C / 200°C fan / gas 7**.
2. Open each butterflied herring flat on a board. Season inside with salt and pepper.
3. Spread a thin layer of wholegrain mustard over the flesh side of each herring.
4. Lay a slice of pancetta on the flesh side and scatter over the dill.
5. Roll each herring from head to tail, securing with a cocktail stick if needed.
6. Place rolled herrings in a lightly oiled ovenproof dish.
7. Bake in the preheated oven for **15–20 minutes** until the pancetta is crispy and the herring is cooked through.
8. **Pull when the skin is set and the fish is cooked through.** Source does not specify an internal temperature. At visual doneness the internal temperature is approximately 63–68 °C (145–154 °F).
9. Rest 3 minutes. Remove cocktail sticks and serve with lemon.

## Temperature consensus

One verified source. Visual doneness (set exterior, cooked through) at 220 °C / 15–20 min corresponds to an internal temperature of approximately **63–68 °C (145–154 °F)**. Recommended pull at **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA safe minimum, well_done).
