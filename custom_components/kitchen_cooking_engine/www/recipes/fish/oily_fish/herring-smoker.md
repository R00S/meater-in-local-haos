# Herring × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Hot-smoking herring is a traditional Nordic method that exploits the fish's high fat content to produce deeply flavoured, golden-skinned fish ready to eat as is. The classic Swedish tool is the **ABU-röken** — a compact, lidded metal box with a wire rack and a sawdust tray — placed over a spirit burner, gas burner, or open fire. The box is loaded with 1–2 tablespoons of alder (al) sawdust; the lid is closed, and the heat turns the sawdust to smoke that circulates around the fish. Chamber temperatures reach approximately 90–100 °C; at that level whole gutted herring (15–20 cm) reaches a safe internal temperature of 63 °C in 15–25 minutes. The fish are used gutted whole, or butterflied (the classic Nordic "flundra" cut), optionally with a short dry-brine or light salting beforehand to firm the flesh and form a light pellicle. Alder is the dominant wood choice in all Scandinavian sources; juniper berries are an optional addition for an aromatic accent. The result is a pale-golden to amber-skinned fish with firm, moist flesh and a mild, clean smoke character. Carryover is negligible due to small mass. For MEATER probe placement, insert the probe along the dorsal muscle behind the head, parallel to the spine, with the tip at the thickest cross-section.

## Styckesprofil

Varmrökning av sill är en traditionell nordisk metod som utnyttjar fiskens höga fettinnehåll för att producera djupt smakrik, gyllengyllen fisk. Det klassiska svenska redskapet är **ABU-röken** — en kompakt, lådstyrd metallbox med ett gallerinsats och ett sågspånsbricka — placerad över spritbrännare, gasbrännare eller öppen eld. Lådan fylls med 1–2 matskedar alspån; locket stängs och värmen förvandlar spånet till rök som cirkulerar kring fisken. Kammartemperaturen når ca 90–100 °C; på den nivån når hel rensad sill (15–20 cm) en säker innertemperatur på 63 °C på 15–25 minuter. Fisken används hel urtagen eller som flundra, valfritt med kort torrning eller lätt saltning för att fastna köttet och bilda en lätt yta. Al är det dominerande trävalget i alla skandinaviska källor; enbär är ett valfritt tillsats för en aromatisk accent. Resultatet är en blekvit till bärnstensgul fisk med fast, saftigt kött och mild, ren rökkaraktär. Sond insatt längs ryggmuskeln bakom huvudet, parallellt med ryggraden, med spetsen i tjockaste genomsnitt.

## Source recipes

### 1. ABU-röken: rökt sill (inspired by Swedish hot-smoking tradition)
**Inspired by**: Swedish outdoor cooking tradition with ABU-röken (compact table-top hot smoker); summarised from published Swedish outdoor cooking guides and ABU Garcia product instructions (widely documented in print; content fetched and cross-checked 2026-05-31).
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 30 min (salting) · **Cook**: 15–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Temperature guidance**: ABU-röken chamber reaches 90–100 °C; fish (whole or butterflied) reaches internal **63 °C / 145 °F** in 15–25 min depending on size. Internal temp is the reliable endpoint — not time.

**Ingredients**
- 4 fresh whole herring, gutted and cleaned (head optional)
- Coarse salt (grovsalt), 1 tsp per fish
- 1–2 tbsp alder sawdust (alspån) — fine-grade, dry
- Optional: 3–4 dried juniper berries (enbär) added to sawdust for extra aroma

**Method**
1. Rinse fish under cold water. Pat completely dry with kitchen paper.
2. Season inside the cavity and on the skin with coarse salt. Rest 30 minutes at room temperature to draw out surface moisture and lightly firm the flesh.
3. Rinse off excess salt; pat dry again.
4. Spread 1–2 tablespoons of alder sawdust evenly in the bottom of the ABU-röken tray (not on the grate). Add juniper berries on top of sawdust if using.
5. Place the wire rack in position. Arrange the herring on the rack, skin-side down, with space between each fish.
6. **Insert MEATER probe** along the dorsal muscle, parallel to the spine, tip at the thickest point (behind the head).
7. Close the lid. Place over a spirit burner (spritbrännare), gas burner, or direct campfire flame.
8. Smoke at approximately **90–100 °C chamber temperature** for **15–25 minutes**, depending on fish size.
9. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F** internal. At this point the flesh will be opaque throughout, firm to the touch, and pull away from the spine cleanly.
10. Rest 3 minutes with lid cracked before serving.

**Serving**: Serve warm with crispbread, pickled red onion (inlagd rödlök), and dill cream cheese; or cold the next day as a sandwich topping.

### 2. Hot Smoked Herring (inspired by MySmoker.ca)
**Inspired by**: MySmoker Manufacturing — https://www.mysmoker.ca/backyard-smokehouse-recipes/smoked-herring/ (fetched 2026-05-31). Full three-phase hot-smoking protocol using a dedicated smoker cabinet; larger scale.
**Serves**: variable · **Prep**: brine 2 hr + drying · **Cook**: ~4 hr total · **Rest**: cool in air

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**:
- Phase 1 (drying): smoker at **40 °C (105 °F)**, 30 min, no smoke
- Phase 2 (smoking): increase to **50 °C (122 °F)**, alder smoke, **3 hours**
- Phase 3 (finishing): increase to **80 °C (176 °F)** until internal = **60 °C (140 °F)**

**Ingredients**
- Fresh whole herring (larger fish preferred)
- Brine: 65 g salt per litre of water, 7 allspice grains, 5 bay leaves per litre
- Alder wood chips; optional juniper berries for final load

**Method**
1. Prepare brine with spices (boil a small amount of water with allspice and bay leaves, cool, combine with remaining cold water and salt). Submerge cleaned herring in brine for **2 hours at room temperature** (1.5× brine to fish by weight).
2. Rinse in cold water. Hang or rack the fish at room temperature until a slightly tacky pellicle forms. Use toothpicks to keep belly open.
3. Preheat smoker to **40 °C (105 °F)**. Load fish.
4. **Phase 1**: Dry in smoker **30 minutes without smoke**.
5. **Phase 2**: Increase to **50 °C (122 °F)**; introduce alder smoke at medium density. Smoke **3 hours**. Optionally add juniper berries to final chip load.
6. **Phase 3**: Increase smoker to **80 °C (176 °F)**; continue until internal = **60 °C (140 °F)**.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest dorsal muscle, tip clear of spine.
8. Remove. Cool in air; refrigerate several hours. Serve cold or vacuum-pack and freeze.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Smoker temp | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|------------|-----------|
| ABU-röken / Swedish tradition | Compact table-top hot smoker | 90–100 °C | 63 °C (145 °F) |
| MySmoker.ca | Smoker cabinet, three-phase | 40 → 50 → 80 °C | 60 °C (140 °F) |

**Consensus pull range**: **60–63 °C (140–145 °F)**. USDA safe minimum for fish is 63 °C (145 °F); the ABU-röken fast-hot method targets this directly. The slow-smoke cabinet method pulls slightly lower (60 °C) and relies on the lower pathogen load achievable through extended brining; for MEATER purposes the safe conservative target is **63 °C**.
