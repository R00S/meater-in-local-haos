# Mackerel × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven-baking mackerel works especially well for two very different world traditions: the Japanese salt-grilling preparation (saba shioyaki) done under an oven broil or at high oven heat, and the Moroccan-spiced (chermoula) whole or fillet oven bake. Both traditions bake at 180–220 °C for 15–25 minutes depending on fillet thickness. In saba shioyaki the fish is salted 15–30 minutes before cooking to draw out moisture and firm the flesh. In the chermoula preparation a herb-spice marinade (parsley, coriander, cumin, paprika, lemon) is rubbed over the fish and it is baked in an open dish. No source cites a thermometer endpoint; all use visual cues (opaque throughout, flaking at the thickest point, skin blistered). At visual doneness the internal temperature is approximately 63–70 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Saba Shioyaki (Japanese salt-grilled mackerel) — Just One Cookbook
**Source**: Just One Cookbook (Namiko Hirasawa Chen) — https://www.justonecookbook.com/saba-shioyaki/  
**Verified**: 2026-05-26 by previous session agent  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min (inc. salting rest) · **Cook**: 15–20 min at 200 °C · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 saba (mackerel) fillets, skin on (about 150–200 g each)
- 1 tsp salt (kosher or sea salt)
- 1 tbsp sake
- Grated daikon radish (*daikon oroshi*), to serve
- Soy sauce, to serve
- Lemon wedge, to serve

**Method**
1. Pat mackerel fillets dry. Sprinkle both sides generously with salt. Let rest 15–20 minutes — the salt will draw moisture to the surface.
2. Pat dry again — removing the drawn-out moisture is key to crinkled, blistered skin.
3. Brush the flesh side lightly with sake.
4. Preheat oven to **200 °C (400 °F)**, grill/broil setting or high heat.
5. Place fillets skin-side up on a wire rack over a foil-lined tray.
6. Bake (or broil) for **15–20 minutes** until the skin is blistered and the flesh is fully opaque.
7. **Pull when skin is golden-blistered and the flesh is completely opaque throughout.** Source does not specify an internal temperature. At visual doneness the internal temperature is approximately 63–70 °C (145–158 °F).
8. Serve immediately with grated daikon, a drop of soy sauce, and a wedge of lemon.

### 2. Mackerel baked with chermoula — BBC Good Food
**Source**: BBC Good Food — https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/baked-mackerel-chermoula  
**Verified**: 2026-05-26 by previous session agent  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min at 180 °C · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 mackerel fillets (skin on) or 2 whole mackerel, scored
- **Chermoula marinade**: 1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley; 1 small bunch coriander; 3 garlic cloves; 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp paprika; pinch of chilli flakes; juice of 1 lemon; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt
- Lemon wedges, to serve
- Couscous or flatbread, to serve

**Method**
1. Blend all chermoula ingredients in a food processor to a rough paste. Season.
2. Rub the chermoula paste all over the mackerel fillets (or inside and outside the scored whole fish).
3. Preheat oven to **180 °C / 160 °C fan / gas 4**.
4. Place mackerel in a single layer in an ovenproof dish. Spoon any remaining chermoula over the top.
5. Bake for **20–25 minutes** until the fish is cooked through and the chermoula is beginning to char at the edges.
6. **Pull when flesh is fully opaque and flakes easily at the thickest point.** Source does not specify an internal temperature. At visual doneness the internal temperature is approximately 63–68 °C (145–154 °F).
7. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with couscous or flatbread and lemon wedges.

## Temperature consensus

Two verified sources from Culinary Groups A (Japan) and C (Moroccan-spiced North African / British adaptation). Both converge on full doneness — fully opaque, flaking — at 180–200 °C for 15–25 minutes. Recommended pull at **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA safe minimum, well_done).

| Source | Target | Method notes |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Just One Cookbook (JP) | visual (~63–70 °C) | saba shioyaki, salted, sake, 200 °C 15–20 min |
| BBC Good Food (chermoula) | visual (~63–68 °C) | chermoula marinade, 180 °C 20–25 min |
