# Salmon Fillet × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Hot-smoked salmon fillet is one of the most widely practised uses of the compact table-top hot smoker (Swedish: **röklåda** or **ABU-röken**; Finnish: **savustuslaatikko** / **savustuspönttö**; Danish: **røgekasse**; Norwegian: **røykeboks**; North American: **Camerons Stovetop Smoker**; Japanese: **いぶし鍋 ibushi nabe**) — all of these are the same concept: a lidded metal box, 1–2 tablespoons of sawdust in the base tray, a wire rack above, placed over a spirit burner, gas burner, campfire, or kitchen hob. The ABU-röken is the best-known Scandinavian brand; clones are sold under Primus, Fladen, and Outdoorchef names. In non-Swedish contexts, the Camerons Stovetop Smoker (USA) is the direct equivalent; Scandinavian savustuspönttö / røgekasse are identical in concept.

Salmon fillet for hot-smoking is typically skin-on, boneless (laxfilé, urbenad), portion-sized (150–250 g) or as a half-side (500–1000 g). The high fat content (10–15 g/100 g for farmed Atlantic salmon) makes it self-basting during smoking — the rendered fat carries smoke compounds deep into the flesh and keeps it moist. In the röklåda at 80–100 °C chamber temperature, a 200 g portion fillet reaches internal 63 °C in approximately 15–25 minutes; a 500–700 g side fillet takes 25–40 minutes. A whole laxfilé (1+ kg) can require 40–60 minutes.

The Swedish tradition uses alder (al), juniper (enbär), and occasionally birch; the Finnish tradition adds birch leaves (koivunlehtiä) and a sugar cube to the sawdust for a sweeter smoke and a caramel note; the North American tradition uses alder or oak. Probe placement: insert the probe horizontally into the thickest section of the fillet from the side (lateral insertion), keeping the tip well away from the skin and the centre of the flesh at the thickest cross-section.

Temperature note: Swedish varmrökt lax is traditionally served at 50–57 °C internal (moist, yielding texture, sometimes pink inside) — a style documented on Köket.se and preferred by many Scandinavian home cooks. At 63 °C (USDA minimum for fish) the flesh is fully set, opaque, and flakes cleanly. Both endpoints are common in practice; the MEATER system targets USDA safe (63 °C) for the smoker method.

## Styckesprofil

Varmrökt laxfilé är ett av de mest utövade användningarna av den kompakta bordsröklådan — **ABU-röken** är det välkändaste svenska märket; synonymer är röklåda (generiskt), savustuslaatikko/savustuspönttö (finska), røgekasse (danska), røykeboks (norska) och Camerons Stovetop Smoker (nordamerikansk). Enheten är identisk i koncept: ett slutet metallhölje, 1–2 matskedar alspån i botten, ett gallerinsats ovanför, placerat över spritbrännare, gasbrännare, eld eller hällspis. Laxfilé för varmrökning är vanligtvis med skinn, urbenad (laxfilé, urbenad), portionsbitar (150–250 g) eller som en halvsida (500–1000 g). Det höga fettinnehållet (10–15 g/100 g för odlad atlantlax) gör den självbastande under rökning. I röklådan vid 80–100 °C kammartemperatur når en 200 g portionsfilé innertemperatur 63 °C på ca 15–25 minuter; en halvsida på 500–700 g tar 25–40 minuter. Sond insatt horisontellt in i tjockaste tvärsnittets mitt från sidan, med spetsen fri från skinn och ben.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hemrökt lax i röklåda — Swedish ABU-röken tradition (inspired by Köket.se)
**Inspired by**: Köket.se — https://www.koket.se/hemrokt-lax (fetched 2026-05-31). Swedish röklåda method for whole gutted salmon or side fillet; salt-juniper-bay cure. Method adapted here for portion fillets.
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 30 min + 2–3 h cure · **Cook**: 25–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Röklåda chamber ~80 °C. Whole salmon 2–3 kg smoked 60–90 minutes. For portion fillets (150–250 g), scale proportionally: approximately **20–30 minutes** until internal **63 °C / 145 °F**. Source does not state internal temp; visual cue is firm, opaque flesh.

**Ingredients**
- 600–800 g skin-on laxfilé, urbenad (boneless), cut into 150–200 g portions
- 3 tbsp grovt havssalt (coarse sea salt)
- 1 tbsp strösocker (white sugar)
- 8–10 krossade enbär (crushed juniper berries)
- 2 malda lagerblad (ground bay leaves)
- 1 tsp grovmalda pepparkorn (coarsely ground black pepper)
- 2 tbsp alspån (alder sawdust), dry and fine-grade

**Method**
1. Combine salt, sugar, juniper, bay, and pepper in a small bowl. Rub the mixture evenly over the flesh side of each fillet portion. Rest at room temperature 30 minutes, or in the refrigerator up to 3 hours.
2. Rinse off excess cure under cold water. Pat dry with kitchen paper. The surface should be slightly tacky — this pellicle helps smoke adhere.
3. Spread 2 tablespoons of alspån evenly in the röklåda tray.
4. Place the wire rack. Lay fillet portions skin-side down on the rack, leaving space between pieces.
5. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest portion from the side, tip at the centre of the flesh.
6. Close the lid. Place over a gas burner or campfire. Heat gradually until smoke begins rising (approximately 3–5 minutes), then maintain steady heat.
7. Smoke at approximately **80–100 °C chamber temperature** for **20–30 minutes** for portion fillets (200 g), or 30–40 minutes for a half-side.
8. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F** for USDA-safe well-done; or at **54 °C / 130 °F** for the softer, Scandinavian varmrökt style (still moist and slightly translucent at the centre).
9. Rest 5 minutes with the lid cracked before serving.

**Serving**: Serve with nypotatis (new potatoes), lemon wedge, dill cream, and knäckebröd.

### 2. Varmrökt lax i stekpanna — Swedish foil-smoke method (inspired by Köket.se)
**Inspired by**: Köket.se / Peter Larsson — https://www.koket.se/peter_larsson/_/lax/varmrokt_lax_i_stekpanna (fetched 2026-05-31). Indoor foil-in-pan stovetop smoking. Requires a large frying pan and aluminium foil in place of a dedicated röklåda.
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: ~15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Terminate when internal temperature reaches **50 °C / 122 °F** — the source's explicit pull temperature. This is the soft, restaurant-style varmrökt texture. At 50 °C the flesh is moist, slightly translucent at the very centre, and pulls apart in large silky flakes.

**Ingredients**
- 1 piece of laxfilé, urbenad (boneless salmon fillet)
- Salt to taste
- 2 dl träflis / spån from deciduous wood (alder or beech chips)
- Aluminium foil (heavy-duty, 2 large sheets)
- A large frying pan with a lid or tight-fitting cover
- Probe thermometer with cable (sladdsonder)

**Method**
1. Cut a sheet of aluminium foil to fit under the fillet; salt the fillet and rest ~10 minutes.
2. Spread a large sheet of heavy-duty foil in the bottom of a large frying pan. Pour the wood chips on top of the foil.
3. Lay the salmon on its foil sheet, place it directly on the wood chips. Insert the probe thermometer.
4. Lay a second large piece of foil over the top and crimp firmly all around to seal the pan.
5. Set the hob to **maximum heat**.
6. **Pull at internal 50 °C / 122 °F**. Open carefully under the kitchen hood.
7. Serve immediately or at room temperature.

### 3. Koivunlehdillä savustettu lohi — Finnish birch-leaf savustuslaatikko (inspired by Soppa365)
**Inspired by**: Soppa365 / Anikó Lehtinen — https://www.soppa365.fi/reseptit/kala-lisukkeet-salaatit-juhli-ja-nauti-kastikkeet-tahnat-ja-marinadit/koivunlehdilla (fetched 2026-05-31). Finnish savustuslaatikko (compact table-top smoking box), birch leaf smoke, lager-lemon marinade. *Kirjolohi* (rainbow trout) and *lohi* (salmon) are interchangeable here.
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 10 min + 2 h marinade · **Cook**: 10–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: The recipe gives **10–15 minutes** smoking time; no internal temperature stated. At 10–15 minutes in a hot savustuslaatikko over a burner, a 800–1000 g fillet reaches approximately 63 °C internal at the thickest point.

**Ingredients**
- 1 boneless salmon fillet, 800–1000 g (kirjolohi/laxfilé)
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- ½ dl light lager beer
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tbsp coarse sea salt
- 2 tsp pink peppercorns (rosépippar)
- Savustuspuruja (alder sawdust chips)
- 1 sugar cube (sokeripala)
- A generous handful of fresh birch leaves (koivunlehtiä)

**Method**
1. Combine lemon zest, lemon juice, lager, honey, salt, and pink pepper. Rub the marinade over the fillet surface. Marinate in the refrigerator for 2 hours.
2. Load sawdust chips and the sugar cube into the base of the savustuslaatikko. Heat with the lid cracked until initial acrid smoke escapes, then close. This step burns off the bitter first-phase smoke.
3. Open briefly, add the fresh birch leaves on top of the sawdust. Place the fillet on the rack (in a shallow baking dish or directly on the rack).
4. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest part of the fillet.
5. Smoke for **10–15 minutes** at savustuslaatikko chamber temperature (~90–100 °C).
6. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. If the fillet is very thick, allow up to 20 minutes.
7. Serve with a sharp crayfish sauce and avocado salad.

### 4. Savustettu lohi — Finnish campfire savustuspönttö (inspired by Kotikokki.net)
**Inspired by**: Kotikokki.net — https://www.kotikokki.net/reseptit/nayta/18610/Savustettu%20lohi/ (fetched 2026-05-31). Finnish campfire savustuspönttö (larger tube-smoker over open fire); method and timings apply equally to the compact savustuslaatikko on a gas burner.
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30–45 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoke for **30–45 min from when first smoke appears**; no internal temperature given. At 30–45 min in a savustuspönttö at campfire temperature (90–110 °C), a whole laxfilé of 800–1000 g reaches internal 63–68 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 1 laxfilé (lohifilee), 800–1000 g
- 2–3 tsp merisuola (coarse sea salt)
- 1–2 tsp valkopippuri (white pepper)
- 1 pussi savustuspuruja (bag of alder chips)
- 3–4 sokeripalaa (sugar cubes)
- 5–7 katajanmarjoja tai katajanoksia (juniper berries or short juniper sprigs, optional)

**Method**
1. Rub salt and white pepper evenly over the flesh side of the fillet. Wrap loosely in baking paper.
2. Line the base of the savustuspönttö with foil. Spread a 1–2 cm even layer of alder chips on the foil. Add sugar cubes and juniper berries (if using).
3. Place the fillet on the rack, rack into the savustuspönttö, close the lid.
4. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest section of the fillet.
5. Set the savustuspönttö over an open fire (or gas burner) and maintain a steady, moderate flame.
6. Smoke for **30–45 minutes** from when the first wisps of smoke emerge.
7. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. Flesh should be fully opaque and pull apart into large flakes.
8. Serve warm with boiled potatoes or with lemon slices as a smoked salmon salad.

### 5. Smoked Salmon Fillet — North American Camerons Stovetop Smoker (inspired by Camerons Products)
**Inspired by**: Camerons Products — https://cameronsproducts.com/salmon/ (fetched 2026-05-31). The Camerons Stovetop Smoker is the North American direct equivalent of the ABU-röken: a stainless lidded box with sawdust tray, wire rack, and drip tray, placed over a kitchen burner or campfire. Recommended woodchip: alder or oak.
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 17–22 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoke **17–20 minutes** over medium stovetop heat; turn burner off and **let stand covered 5 minutes** before opening. No internal temperature stated; at 17–20 minutes on a Camerons over medium heat, a 450 g (1 lb) fillet reaches approximately **63–66 °C / 145–151 °F** internal.

**Ingredients**
- 450 g (1 lb) skin-on salmon fillet
- Fresh lemon, halved
- Black pepper, freshly ground
- Dried dill
- 1 tbsp alder or oak wood chips (or up to 1½ tbsp for stronger smoke)

**Method**
1. Place salmon skin-side down on the Camerons rack. Squeeze fresh lemon over the flesh. Season with a pinch of pepper and dill to taste.
2. Load 1 tablespoon of alder or oak chips into the base of the Camerons smoker. Slot in drip tray and rack.
3. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest part of the fillet from the side.
4. Close the Camerons lid. Place over **medium heat** on a kitchen burner.
5. Smoke for **17–20 minutes**.
6. Turn off burner. **Let stand covered 5 minutes** (carryover finishes cooking; the lid holds residual smoke).
7. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. Open lid away from face; the smoke will be concentrated.
8. Serve immediately, or let cool and use in salads, sandwiches, or pasta.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Device | Chamber temp | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Köket.se / röklåda cure | SE | Röklåda | ~80 °C | 63 °C (USDA) / 54 °C (chef style) |
| Köket.se / pan foil | SE | Foil-in-pan | high hob heat | **50 °C** (source explicit) |
| Soppa365 / birch leaf | FI | Savustuslaatikko | ~90–100 °C | ~63 °C at 10–15 min |
| Kotikokki.net / campfire | FI | Savustuspönttö | campfire ~90–110 °C | ~63–68 °C at 30–45 min |
| Camerons Products | NA | Camerons Stovetop Smoker | medium burner | ~63–66 °C at 17–20 min |
| Minmadopskrift.dk | DK | Røgeovn | 60–70 °C | n/a (time-based) |

**Consensus pull range**: **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA minimum for fish, fully opaque, large flakes). Swedish style optionally pulled at **50–57 °C** for a softer, moister varmrökt texture. Hot smoking is a full-doneness method — no medium-rare option in the smoker cooking path.
