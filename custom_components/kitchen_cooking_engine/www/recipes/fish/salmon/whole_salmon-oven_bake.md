# Whole Salmon × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven-baking an intact whole salmon cooks by convective heat around the fish while cavity steam and rendered fat moderate drying. Because the shoulder/loin section is much thicker than tail meat, whole-fish oven work is a gradient problem: the thick zone sets last while tail can overcook first. Foil or parcel baking reduces moisture loss and is common in banquet-style whole-salmon service. Compared with open high-heat roasting, low or moderate oven methods produce less aggressive carryover and easier carving from bone after resting.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsbakning av hel lax bygger på konvektionsvärme runt fisken samtidigt som ånga i bukhålan och fett från skinnet dämpar uttorkning. Den tjocka skulder/ryggdelen blir klar senare än stjärtköttet, så tillagningen är en tydlig temperaturgradient. Folie/parceltillagning används ofta för hel lax vid servering till många eftersom fukten hålls kvar och urbening efter vila blir enklare. Jämfört med öppen, hård ugnsrostning ger dessa metoder mjukare eftervärme.

## Source recipes

### 1. Foil-baked Whole Fresh Salmon (Delia Smith)
**Inspired by**: Delia Online — https://www.deliaonline.com/recipes/collections/large-servings/foil-baked-whole-fresh-salmon-with-green-herb-mayonnaise  
**Serves**: large-serving format · **Prep**: sauce + fish prep · **Cook**: 2.5 h · **Rest**: cool fully before service

**Ingredients (fish section)**
- Whole fresh salmon (whole fish)
- Butter
- Onion slices
- Bay leaves
- Tarragon sprigs
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **130°C / 250°F**.
2. Place whole salmon on a generously buttered large double sheet of foil.
3. Fill cavity with onion, bay, tarragon, seasoning, and part of butter; smear remaining butter on top.
4. Seal into a loose but tight foil parcel and bake centrally for **2.5 hours**.
5. Cool fully before opening and serving; separate fillets from bone for service.
6. Source uses time/doneness cues, not a thermometer endpoint.

### 2. Whole Roasted Salmon Stuffed with Lemon and Herbs (Jamie Oliver)
**Inspired by**: Jamie Oliver — https://www.jamieoliver.com/recipes/fish/whole-roasted-salmon-stuffed-with-lemon-and-herbs/  
**Serves**: whole-fish centrepiece · **Prep**: fish + tray setup · **Cook**: 45 min total · **Rest**: serve after doneness check

**Ingredients (core fish setup)**
- 1 whole salmon (~2.5 kg), scaled and gutted
- Fresh dill, parsley, tarragon
- Lemon zest and lemon slices
- Olive oil, salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven high, then later reduce to **180°C / 350°F / gas 4**.
2. Score fish with angled slashes and pack lemon-herb mix into cuts.
3. Stuff cavity with lemon slices and remaining herbs.
4. Bake at high heat 15 min, then lower to 180°C and continue 30 min.
5. Check doneness by skewer in thickest part behind head; if skewer feels hot after 10 seconds, fish is done.
6. Source uses tactile doneness cue, not a numeric pull-temperature.

### 3. Baked Whole Salmon (Taste of Home)
**Inspired by**: Taste of Home — https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/baked-whole-salmon/  
**Serves**: large whole-fish service · **Prep**: sauce + foil setup · **Cook**: 60–75 min at 375°F · **Rest**: brief hold while sauce is finished

**Ingredients (core fish setup)**
- Whole salmon (head/tail removable if desired)
- Herb sauce base (prepared first in saucepan)
- Salt
- Foil-lined, greased baking sheet

**Method**
1. Prepare herb sauce by simmering specified aromatics.
2. Place whole salmon on double-thickness greased foil; salt cavity.
3. Pour herb sauce over fish and seal foil tightly.
4. Bake at **375°F** for **60–75 minutes** until fish flakes easily.
5. Hold fish warm while finishing sauce from reserved cooking juices.
6. Source uses flake-doneness cue, not a numeric pull-temperature.

### 4. Baked Whole Salmon (Savor the Best)
**Inspired by**: Savor the Best — https://savorthebest.com/how-to-bake-a-whole-salmon/  
**Serves**: whole fish (3–5 lb) · **Prep**: optional brine + stuffing · **Cook**: 20–30 min at 425°F · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients (core fish setup)**
- Whole salmon (3–5 lb)
- Olive oil and seasoning salt
- Onion, fennel, garlic, lemon, fresh herbs for cavity

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **425°F**.
2. Pat fish dry, oil exterior and cavity, season, and stuff with aromatics/herbs.
3. Bake on lined tray for **20–30 minutes** depending on thickness.
4. **Pull at 122–130°F** (site guidance for tender result; notes FDA 145°F as safety reference).
5. Rest 5 minutes before skin/spine separation for service.
