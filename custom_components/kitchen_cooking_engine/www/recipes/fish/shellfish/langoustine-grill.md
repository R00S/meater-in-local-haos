# Langoustine × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilling is the most theatrical and flavour-forward preparation for langoustine. The shell acts as a natural flavour vessel: direct heat caramelises the natural sugars in the shell and transmits a light, smoky char to the flesh. The simplest approach is to split the langoustine in half lengthwise along the back, place it shell-side down over direct heat, and pile compound butter onto the exposed flesh — the butter melts into the tail cavity and bastes the meat as it cooks. The alternative is to grill whole-shell on, which seals in juices but requires the diner to shell at table.

Small size means rapid heat penetration: 3–5 minutes shell-side down over strong heat is sufficient. The flesh turns from translucent grey-white to opaque white; the shell transitions from pale orange to bright vivid orange. Visual doneness cues: flesh is opaque and just firm to the press, with a pearlescent rather than chalky surface. The MEATER probe should be inserted from the open head end of the tail, angling through the shell opening into the centre of the tail muscle at its thickest point. **Pull at 52–54 °C**; carryover of 2 °C brings the serving temperature to 54–56 °C (tender, sweet, moist). Above 58 °C the flesh tightens sharply. USDA minimum is 63 °C (145 °F).

## Styckesprofil

Grillning är den mest dramatiska och smakrika beredningen för havskräfta. Skalet fungerar som ett naturligt smakkärl: direkt värme karamelliserar de naturliga sockerarterna i skalet och ger köttet en lätt, rökig char. Det enklaste angreppssättet är att dela kräftan i halvor längs ryggen, lägga den med skalsidan nedåt över direkt värme, och lägga kompoundat smör på det exponerade köttet — smöret smälter ned i svanshålan och bastar köttet medan det tillagas. Alternativet är att grilla hela kräftor med skalet på, vilket bevarar saften men kräver att gästen skalar vid bordet.

Den lilla storleken innebär snabb värmegenomträngning: 3–5 minuter med skalsidan nedåt över stark värme räcker. Köttet övergår från genomskinlig gråvit till opak vit; skalet går från blekrosa till klarorange. Visuell doneness: köttet är opakt och precis fast vid trycket, med en pärlemorsyta snarare än en kalkliknande yta. MEATER-sonden placeras från den öppna huvud-änden av svansen in i mitten av svansmuskeln. **Dra vid 52–54 °C**; eftertillagning på 2 °C ger serveringstemperaturen 54–56 °C (tender/mör, söt, saftig). Ovanför 58 °C stramar köttet kraftigt. USDA-minimum är 63 °C (145 °F).

## Source recipes

### 1. Griddled Langoustines with Hazelnut Butter (inspired by Good Food Team)
**Inspired by**: Good Food team, BBC Good Food, https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/griddled-langoustines-hazelnut-butter  
**Archived**: https://web.archive.org/web/20210410113935/https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/griddled-langoustines-hazelnut-butter  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 1–2 min · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 10 Scottish langoustines, heads removed, shelled, tails left on
- 1 tsp hazelnuts, toasted
- 25 g butter
- A few parsley leaves, finely chopped
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 10 wooden skewers

**Method**
1. Thread the langoustines carefully onto the skewers and chill for up to a day.
2. For the hazelnut butter: finely grate or chop half the hazelnuts (grating gives the nuttiest flavour), then roughly chop the rest. Tip into a small saucepan with the butter, parsley and a little seasoning, then set aside.
3. When ready to serve, melt the hazelnut butter in the pan.
4. Heat a griddle pan or barbecue, then brush the langoustines with the oil.
5. Griddle each skewer for 1–2 minutes, turning, until pink and just cooked.
6. Pile on a platter, pour half the hazelnut butter over the langoustines, then serve the rest in a small bowl for dipping.

**Doneness cue**: "pink and just cooked" — opaque throughout, still moist.

### 2. Grillade Havskräftor med Vitlöks- och Citronsmör (inspired by Lisa Lemke)
**Inspired by**: Lisa Lemke, Köket.se, https://www.koket.se/lisa-lemke/forratt/fisk-och-skaldjur/grillade-havskraftor-med-vitloks--och-citronsmor  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 3–5 min · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 12 raw langoustines (råa havskräftor)
- 3–4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 150 g butter, room temperature
- 1 lemon, zested
- ½ dl parsley, finely chopped
- 1 tsp lemon juice, freshly squeezed
- Flaky salt
- Black pepper, coarsely ground

**Method**
1. Make the compound butter: grate garlic finely, then mix with butter, parsley, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt and pepper to taste.
2. Split the langoustines in half lengthwise with a sharp knife and place the halves shell-side down on a cutting board.
3. Distribute the compound butter over the langoustine flesh.
4. Grill the langoustine halves shell-side down over fine embers for approximately 3–5 minutes. Serve immediately.

**Doneness cue**: shell-side down until butter is melted and bubbling and flesh is opaque.

### 3. Grillede Jomfruhummere (inspired by Karen Seneca)
**Inspired by**: Karen Seneca, Madens Verden, https://madensverden.dk/grillede-jomfruhummere/  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 40 min (incl. 30 min marinade) · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 12 langoustine tails (halerne)
- 1 tsp chili flakes
- 2 tbsp lime juice
- 1 handful parsley, finely chopped
- Coriander, finely chopped, to taste
- 1 dl oil (100 ml)
- 1 garlic clove, finely pressed
- Salt and freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a mini-chopper or blender and blend together.
2. Remove the heads from the langoustines. Clip the back shell or use a sharp knife; remove the intestinal tract (black vein) and claws so you are left with just the tails.
3. Place the tails in the marinade for 30 minutes. Reserve 50 ml of marinade for basting.
4. Place the langoustine tails in a foil tray with the flesh facing up and brush with the remaining marinade.
5. Place on the grill grate over direct heat and grill for 4–5 minutes. They are done when the flesh is firm.
6. Baste with reserved marinade continuously while grilling.

**Doneness cue**: "faste i kødet" — firm in the flesh.

### 4. Langoustines et Gambas Grillées (inspired by Chef Simon)
**Inspired by**: Chef Simon, ChefSimon.com, https://chefsimon.com/gourmets/chef-simon/recettes/langoustines-et-gambas-grillees  
**Serves**: variable · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- Langoustines, whole (uncooked)
- Oil
- Thyme
- Lemon juice
- Optional: spices of choice (curry, colombo, turmeric, or similar aromatics)

**Method**
1. Remove the intestinal tract from the langoustines (*châtrer*) and cut off the antennae.
2. Pass the langoustines through a simple instant marinade: oil, thyme, lemon juice, and any optional spices.
3. Grill the langoustines whole and uncooked — Chef Simon explicitly instructs against grilling already-poached crustaceans, as the reaction of intense heat on the raw shell produces an incomparable flavour (*cardinalisation*).
4. Once cooked, shell the langoustines and pass them briefly back over the grill just before serving to perfect the harmony of flavours.

**Notes**: This is a technique-focused recipe from a professional French chef. Chef Simon emphasises cooking raw crustaceans in their shells, then shelling after cooking — the reverse of many home recipes.

**Doneness cue**: shells are fully bright red-orange and flesh is opaque throughout.

### 5. Brochettes de Langoustines à la Plancha et Beurre d'Herbes (inspired by Kaderick)
**Inspired by**: Kaderick, Kaderickenkuizinn.com (referenced via ChefSimon.com), https://kaderickenkuizinn.com/2013/09/langoustines-plancha-beurre-herbes/  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: ~9 min (3+3+3) · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 langoustines per person (whole)
- Thyme, rosemary, savory, and rocket (roquette)
- Butter
- Himalayan pink salt (or regular flaky salt)
- Wooden skewers

**Method — Herb butter**
1. Place thyme, rosemary and salt in a mortar or small food processor. Crush together with a pestle.
2. Add savory and a handful of rocket; crush again.
3. Add butter in pieces and combine. Keep refrigerated until needed.

**Method — Langoustine brochettes**
1. Soak wooden skewers in water to prevent burning.
2. Remove the heads and claws from the langoustines.
3. Thread each langoustine body onto a skewer through the centre.
4. Heat the plancha to normal heat and wipe with an oil-dampened cloth.
5. Place the langoustine bodies on the plancha for 3 minutes.
6. Turn, add the brochettes and cook 3 minutes, then turn once more for 3 minutes.

**Doneness cue**: "les langoustines sont cuites quand la carapace est devenue bien orangée et que la chair est encore nacrée" — done when the shell is fully orange and the flesh is still pearlescent/nacreous.

### 6. Dublin Bay Prawns with Garlic Butter (inspired by Irish Food Hub)
**Inspired by**: Irish Food Hub, https://irishfoodhub.com/dublin-bay-prawns-with-garlic-butter/  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 20–24 fresh Dublin Bay prawns (langoustines), peeled and deveined
- 115 g (½ cup) unsalted butter
- 4–5 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Lemon wedges for serving

**Method**
1. Make the garlic butter: melt butter over low heat in a small saucepan. Add minced garlic and cook for 2 minutes until fragrant but not browned. Stir in parsley, salt and pepper. Infuse 1–2 minutes then remove from heat.
2. Preheat grill or barbecue to medium-high. Optionally thread the prawns onto skewers.
3. Place the Dublin Bay prawns directly on the grill grates. Cook approximately 2–3 minutes per side, or until pink and opaque. (Cooking time varies with size.)
4. Remove from grill and place on a serving platter.
5. Drizzle prepared garlic butter over the cooked prawns, ensuring each is well coated.
6. Serve immediately with extra parsley and lemon wedges.

**Doneness cue**: "pink and opaque" — both colour change and texture change confirm doneness.
