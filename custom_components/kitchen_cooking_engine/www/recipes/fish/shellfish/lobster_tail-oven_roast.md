# Lobster Tail × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting lobster tail uses indirect heat and is more gentle than grilling — no risk of grill-stick, better control, and easier for home cooks. The tail is butterflied (or cut in half lengthwise) and placed flesh-up in a roasting dish. Oven at 200–220 °C (400–425 °F); total cook time 12–15 min for a standard 170–200 g tail. Basting with butter, garlic, and herbs is essential — the flesh has no fat of its own and will dry out quickly without constant basting.

MEATER probe inserted from the tail end horizontally into the thickest cross-section of flesh. Pull at 56–58 °C; carryover 2–3 °C in the oven roast environment (less aggressive carryover than grill). Above 63 °C: rubbery and dry.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsstekning av hummersvans använder indirekt värme och är varsam jämfört med grillning — ingen risk för att fastna i gallret, bättre kontroll och lättare för hemmakockar. Svansen fjärillskärs (eller skärs på hälften på längden) och placeras köttssidan uppåt i en ugnsform. Ugn vid 200–220 °C; total tillagningstid 12–15 minuter för en standard 170–200 g svans.

Pensling med smör, vitlök och örter är avgörande — köttet har inget eget fett och torkar ut snabbt utan konstant pensling. MEATER-sonden stickas in från svansspetsens ände horisontellt in i den tjockaste tvärsnittsprofilen av köttet. Dra vid 56–58 °C; eftertillagning 2–3 °C i ugnsstekningsmiljön (mindre aggressiv eftertillagning än grill). Ovanför 63 °C: gummigt och torrt. Innertemperatur: 58–62 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Oven-Roasted Lobster Tails with Beurre Monté (American haute cuisine) (inspired by Thomas Keller)
**Inspired by**: Thomas Keller, *The French Laundry Cookbook* (Artisan, 1999) (the oven-roast variant of his butter-poached technique); thomaskeller.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cold-water lobster tails (about 180 g each), shells intact
- 120 g unsalted European-style butter
- 30 ml dry white wine (Chardonnay)
- 4 sprigs fresh tarragon
- 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 g chopped fresh chervil and tarragon, to finish
- Lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Use kitchen shears to cut lengthwise through the top of each shell from base to tail; pry the shell open and lift the meat up so it sits on top of the shell, still attached at the tail end.
2. Make beurre monté: combine 60 ml water and white wine in a small pan, bring to a simmer, then whisk in cold butter cube by cube until emulsified. Add tarragon sprigs, garlic and lemon zest; keep warm.
3. Season the lifted meat with salt and pepper. Place tails on a small baking tray. Brush generously with beurre monté.
4. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest tail.
5. Roast 8–9 minutes, brushing with beurre monté at the 4-minute mark.
6. **Pull at 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).** Keller's standard for oven-roasted lobster — the meat has just turned opaque ivory through its full cross-section, still juicy and slightly translucent at the geometric centre.
7. Rest 2 minutes, brushing once more with beurre monté. Strew with chervil and tarragon. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (just-set, butter-lacquered, fragrant).

### 2. Ugnsbakade Hummerstjärtar med Dillsmör (inspired by Tina Nordström)
**Inspired by**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se — hummer chapter  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cold-water lobster tails (about 180 g each), shells intact
- 120 g cultured butter (smör)
- 30 ml torrt vitt vin
- 30 g chopped fresh dill
- 30 g chopped fresh chervil
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- Lemon wedges and a little extra dill, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Butterfly tails as above (cut top of shell, lift meat onto shell).
2. Melt butter in a small pan over low heat. Off heat, stir in white wine, dill, chervil, garlic and lemon zest.
3. Season the lifted meat with salt and white pepper. Place tails on a baking tray. Brush generously with the dill butter.
4. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest tail.
5. Roast 8–9 minutes, brushing again at the 4-minute mark.
6. **Pull at 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).** Nordström's standard for ugnsbakad hummer — opaque ivory through, juicy, slightly translucent at the geometric centre.
7. Rest 2 minutes, brushing once more. Top with extra dill. Serve with lemon wedges and gott bröd. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (dill-perfumed, butter-lacquered).

### 3. Oven-Roasted Lobster Tails with Garlic & Parsley (inspired by Rick Stein)
**Inspired by**: Rick Stein, *Rick Stein's Seafood* (BBC Books, 2001); rickstein.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cold-water lobster tails (about 180 g each), shells intact
- 120 g salted butter
- 6 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
- 15 ml extra-virgin olive oil
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) sweet paprika

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F). Butterfly tails as above.
2. Soften butter; mash in garlic, parsley, olive oil, lemon zest and juice, salt, pepper and paprika.
3. Place tails on a baking tray. Spread the garlic-parsley butter generously over the lifted meat, pressing to adhere.
4. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest tail.
5. Roast 8–9 minutes (Stein notes that the high heat is essential for the butter to melt over the meat and bubble).
6. **Pull at 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).** Stein: opaque white, juicy, the butter pooled in the shell.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Spoon the butter from the tray over each tail. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (garlic-bright, parsley-fresh, butter-rich).

### 4. Roasted Lobster Tails with Vanilla Beurre Blanc (French haute cuisine) (inspired by Eric Ripert)
**Inspired by**: Eric Ripert, *Avec Eric* (Wiley, 2010) (and recurring at Le Bernardin tasting menus); le-bernardin.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cold-water lobster tails (about 180 g each), shells intact
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter, softened, for the meat
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- **Vanilla beurre blanc**: 60 ml dry white wine + 30 ml white wine vinegar + 1 finely chopped shallot + 1 vanilla pod (split, seeds scraped) + 30 ml double cream + 120 g cold cubed unsalted butter
- 30 g chopped fresh chives, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Butterfly tails as above.
2. Make beurre blanc: in a small pan combine wine, vinegar, shallot, vanilla pod and seeds. Reduce to 30 ml (5 minutes). Add cream; reduce by half (2 minutes). Off heat, whisk in cold butter cube by cube. Strain; keep warm.
3. Brush the lifted meat with softened butter; season with salt and white pepper. Place tails on a baking tray.
4. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest tail.
5. Roast 8–9 minutes.
6. **Pull at 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).** Ripert's haute-cuisine standard: opaque ivory, juicy, just-set.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Plate each tail; spoon vanilla beurre blanc generously around. Strew with chives. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (vanilla-perfumed, butter-glossed).
