# Tuna Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilling tuna steak over a gas or charcoal grill produces a slightly different result than pan searing: the open grate allows drip smoke to contribute flavour, and the dry radiant heat creates char marks on the surface without the oil-bath environment of a pan. The grate must be very clean, very hot, and well-oiled before the tuna goes on — tuna sticks aggressively to a dirty or cool grate and will tear apart.

Typical approach: grate at 250–270 °C, 1–2 minutes per side for rare (38–45 °C centre). For a 3 cm steak, 90 seconds per side over the hottest zone. MEATER probe from the side, aimed at the geometric centre. Pull at 38–42 °C for rare (cold-raw centre) or 50–55 °C for medium-rare (lightly warmed centre). Always rest 2–3 min and slice across the grain for presentation. Carryover: 3–5 °C from a hot grate.

## Styckesprofil

Grillning av tonfiskbiff över gas- eller träkolsgrill ger ett något annorlunda resultat än pannstekning: det öppna gallret låter droppröken bidra med smak, och den torra strålningsvärmen skapar kolmärken på ytan utan oljebadsmiljön i en panna. Gallret måste vara mycket rent, mycket hett och välolja innan tonfisken läggs på — tonfisk fastnar aggressivt i ett smutsigt eller svalt galler och slits sönder.

Typisk ansats: galler vid 250–270 °C, 1–2 minuter per sida för rare (38–45 °C centrum). För en 3 cm biff, 90 sekunder per sida över den hetaste zonen. MEATER-sond från sidan, riktad mot den geometriska mitten. Dra vid 38–42 °C för rare (kallrå mitt) eller 50–55 °C för medium-rare (lätt uppvärmd mitt). Vila alltid 2–3 minuter och skär tvärs mot fibern för presentation. Eftertillagning: 3–5 °C från ett hett galler.

## Source recipes

### 1. Grilled Yellowfin Tuna Steaks (inspired by Steven Raichlen)
**Inspired by**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min marinade) · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 yellowfin (*ahi*) tuna steaks, 200–250 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- 60 ml (¼ cup) soy sauce
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fresh lemon juice
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) sesame oil
- 10 g (2 tsp) freshly grated fresh ginger
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- Lemon wedges and sliced green onions, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix soy sauce, lemon juice, olive oil, sesame oil, ginger, garlic and pepper into a marinade. Submerge tuna steaks; marinate 30 minutes at room temperature. Do not marinate longer — citric and sodium acids begin to chemically denature the flesh after an hour.
2. Remove steaks from marinade; pat dry with paper towels.
3. Preheat grill to high; brush grates generously with oil. Raichlen emphasises a "screaming hot" grill for fish — the grates must be blazing to prevent sticking.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one tuna steak into the centre of the flesh.
5. Place steaks on the grill at a 45-degree angle. Grill 2–3 minutes without moving for clean grill marks.
6. Rotate 90 degrees; grill 1 minute more for crosshatch marks.
7. Flip; grill 2–3 minutes on the second side.
8. **Pull at 44–46 °C (111–115 °F).** Raichlen's target is medium-rare — warm red-pink throughout. He notes "tuna is a fish you want to undercook, not overcook."
9. Rest 1 minute. Serve with lemon and spring onions. Final serving temperature: **46–48 °C / 115–118 °F** (medium-rare, warm red-pink interior).

### 2. Grilled Tuna with Avocado and Wasabi (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 tuna steaks (yellowfin or bluefin), 200 g each, 2.5 cm thick
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 ripe avocado, sliced
- 5 g (1 tsp) wasabi paste (*wasabi* — Japanese green horseradish paste)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) good-quality soy sauce
- 5 ml (1 tsp) sesame oil
- Micro cress or watercress, to serve

**Method**
1. Brush tuna steaks with olive oil; season with salt and pepper.
2. Mix wasabi paste with soy sauce and sesame oil to make a dipping sauce. Set aside.
3. Preheat grill (gas or charcoal) to very high. Brush grates with oil.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Grill tuna 2 minutes per side for a 2.5 cm steak — Ottolenghi's preference is for a very rare centre with bold char marks.
6. **Pull at 38–42 °C (100–108 °F).** Ottolenghi's published preference is a barely-cooked, almost raw-centred tuna — he writes "serve very rare, the centre as close to raw as your nerve allows."
7. Rest 1 minute. Slice into 1 cm slices; fan alongside avocado slices. Drizzle with wasabi-soy. Final serving temperature: **40–44 °C / 104–111 °F** (very rare, raw red centre).

### 3. Tonno alla Griglia (inspired by Marcella Hazan)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 fresh tuna steaks (bluefin or albacore, preferably Sicilian-style cuts), 180–200 g each, 2 cm thick
- 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fresh lemon juice
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, lemon juice, garlic and parsley; season with salt and pepper. Marinate tuna steaks 1 hour at room temperature.
2. Remove from marinade; pat surface dry to prevent steam at the grill surface.
3. Preheat charcoal or gas grill to high; brush grates with oil.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre of the flesh.
5. Grill tuna approximately 4–5 minutes per side — Hazan's Italian tradition calls for tuna that is fully cooked through, with no raw centre; she writes that "in Italy, tuna is not eaten raw, and grilled tuna should be cooked like a good pork chop."
6. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).** Hazan's standard: the flesh should be pale and opaque throughout, firm but still moist. The Italian-Sicilian tradition cooks grilled tuna fully through.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Drizzle with remaining marinade. Final serving temperature: **60–62 °C / 140–144 °F** (well-done, opaque, firm, Italian standard).

### 4. Thon Grillé à la Tunisienne (inspired by Paula Wolfert)
**Inspired by**: Paula Wolfert, *The Cooking of the Eastern Mediterranean* (HarperCollins, 1994)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+3 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 fresh tuna steaks, 180–200 g each, 2–3 cm thick
- **Harissa marinade**: 15 g (1 tbsp) *harissa* paste (*harissa* — Tunisian fermented chilli and spice paste); 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 15 ml (1 tbsp) fresh lemon juice; 3 garlic cloves, minced; 5 g (1 tsp) ground caraway; 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin; fine salt
- 1 preserved lemon, flesh discarded, rind finely sliced, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix harissa, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, caraway and cumin into a marinade. Coat tuna steaks generously. Marinate 3 hours refrigerated.
2. Remove from fridge 30 minutes before grilling. Pat surface dry.
3. Preheat grill to medium-high.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Grill 4–5 minutes per side. The harissa will char and caramelise on the surface.
6. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).** Wolfert's North African tradition follows the Tunisian preference for fully cooked fish; tuna grilled *à la tunisienne* is served opaque throughout, its pungent spice marinade the focus rather than the raw-centre texture.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with preserved lemon rind scattered over the top. Final serving temperature: **60–62 °C / 140–144 °F** (well-done, Tunisian standard).

### 5. Swordfish or Tuna Steaks with Gremolata (inspired by Rick Stein)
**Inspired by**: Rick Stein, *Rick Stein's Mediterranean Escapes* (BBC Books, 2007)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 fresh tuna (or swordfish) steaks, 200–250 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **Gremolata**: finely grated zest of 2 lemons; 30 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped; 2 garlic cloves, minced

**Method**
1. Mix gremolata ingredients; set aside.
2. Brush tuna steaks with olive oil; season generously.
3. Preheat grill to high; clean and oil grates well.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre of the flesh.
5. Grill 2–3 minutes per side for medium (pink centre), or 3–4 minutes per side for medium-well.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Stein's Mediterranean book targets medium for tuna grilled this way — he writes that tuna should still be "nicely pink in the middle."
7. Rest 1 minute. Scatter gremolata generously over the top. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium, pink warm centre).
