# Cod Fillet × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Hot-smoked cod fillet is a well-documented tradition across Scandinavia, Denmark, and the UK, and is increasingly popular in North American BBQ and stovetop-smoking culture. Cod is lean (less than 1% fat), which distinguishes it sharply from salmon or mackerel: there is no internal fat to self-baste the flesh during smoking, so the smoke-and-heat process dries the surface while cooking the interior. This makes brine time critical — a short dry-salt or wet brine (2–4 hours) helps the lean flesh retain moisture during the 20–60 minute smoking window. The result is firm, white, mildly smoky flakes that are very different from the yielding, fatty texture of smoked salmon.

The compact table-top smoker (Swedish: **röklåda** / **ABU-röken**; Finnish: **savustuslaatikko**; Danish: **røgekasse**; Norwegian: **røykeboks**; North American: **Camerons Stovetop Smoker**) is well-suited to cod fillets — the device's 80–100 °C chamber temperature and 20–40 minute cook time produce a delicate hot-smoked cod without the drying risk of longer, hotter BBQ smoking sessions. Cod's mild flavour absorbs smoke readily; alder is the classic Nordic choice, cherry or apple wood for a sweeter American style.

In Scandinavia, varmrökt / varmrøget torsk is documented in Swedish fishing guides and Danish outdoor cooking resources. The UK tradition centres on heavily brined and smoke-dried cod (Finnan haddie / smoked cod fillet in milk) — this is a different, longer cold-smoke tradition; the hot-smoked röklåda version is more Scandinavian in character. For portion fillets (150–250 g), hot-smoking time in the röklåda is **20–35 minutes** at 80–100 °C to reach internal **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA minimum for fish). Thicker portions (30–40 mm) may require 35–45 minutes.

Probe placement: insert the probe horizontally into the thickest part of the fillet from the side (lateral insertion), keeping the tip away from the skin and at the centre of the deepest flesh cross-section.

## Styckesprofil

Varmrökt torskfilé är en välkänd beredning i Skandinavien, Danmark och UK; North American BBQ-kultur har också börjat omfamna det. Torsk är mager (under 1% fett), vilket gör att den saknar det självbastande skydd som fet fisk (lax, makrill) har under rökning. Rimning — kort torrsaltning eller vattenlag — är avgörande för att fisken ska behålla fukt under rökningstiden. I röklådan vid 80–100 °C kammartemperatur tar en 150–250 g portionsfilé ca 20–35 minuter för att nå innertemperatur 63 °C. Alder (al) är det klassiska nordiska trävalget. Sond insatt horisontellt från sidan in i tjockaste tvärsnitt, med spetsen fri från skinn och ben.

## Source recipes

### 1. Dry-Brined Smoked Cod — North American pellet/box smoker (inspired by A Grill for All Seasons)
**Inspired by**: A Grill for All Seasons — https://agrillforallseasons.com/smoked-cod/ (fetched 2026-05-31). North American outdoor smoker recipe, cherry wood chips, dry brine of equal parts salt and sugar, 225 °F (107 °C) smoker temperature, internal 145 °F (63 °C). Directly applicable to Camerons Stovetop Smoker or ABU-röken equivalent (reduce time proportionally).
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 5 min + 1 h dry brine · **Cook**: ~60 min at 225 °F (or 20–30 min in compact smoker at 90–100 °C) · **Rest**: 3–5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoker at **225 °F / 107 °C**. Smoke for approximately **60 minutes** until internal temperature reaches **145 °F / 63 °C**. "Thin fillets make the cooking process go very quickly." For a compact ABU-röken / Camerons box at 90–100 °C, scale down to approximately **20–30 minutes** for a 200 g portion fillet.

**Ingredients**
- Cod fillets (fresh or fully thawed if frozen), any size
- Equal parts kosher salt and white or brown sugar (dry brine)
- Olive oil (to coat after brining)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1–2 tbsp cherry wood chips (or alder for Scandinavian flavour)

**Method**
1. Dry brine: mix equal parts coarse salt and sugar. Generously coat both sides of each fillet. Place on a tray, cover with cling film, refrigerate **1 hour**.
2. Remove fillets from tray. Rinse thoroughly under cold water to remove all brine (residual brine makes the fish very salty). Pat dry with kitchen paper.
3. Brush a thin coat of olive oil over each fillet. Season with black pepper (no additional salt needed — the brine has seasoned the fish).
4. Load wood chips into the smoker base. Preheat smoker to **225 °F / 107 °C** (or medium heat on a compact stovetop smoker).
5. Place fillets directly on the grill grate or smoker rack.
6. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest fillet from the side.
7. Smoke for **~60 minutes** (large/pellet smoker at 225 °F) or **20–30 minutes** (compact box at 90–100 °C) until internal temperature reaches **145 °F / 63 °C**.
8. Rest 3–5 minutes, tented lightly with foil. Serve with fresh lemon juice and a green salad.

### 2. Ginger-Soy Smoked Cod — Asian-inspired North American smoker (inspired by Simply Meat Smoking)
**Inspired by**: Simply Meat Smoking — https://www.simplymeatsmoking.com/smoked-seafood/smoked-cod/ (fetched 2026-05-31). North American pellet/cabinet smoker recipe with an Asian-influenced marinade (ginger ale, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic). Smoker at 180 °F (82 °C), internal 140 °F (60 °C).
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 10 min + 1 h marinade · **Cook**: 2–3 h at 180 °F (or 30–40 min in compact smoker) · **Rest**: 3 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoker at **180 °F / 82 °C**. Smoke for **2–3 hours** until internal reaches **140 °F / 60 °C**. "Lower and slower = more smoky flavour." For a compact ABU-röken equivalent at 90–100 °C, scale to approximately **30–40 minutes**; pull at **63 °C / 145 °F** for full USDA safety.

**Ingredients**
- Cod fillets (wild-caught recommended), 200–300 g each
- **Marinade**: water, ginger ale, low-sodium soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic powder, ground ginger, fresh cilantro
- Lemon slices (to serve)
- Preferred smoking wood (alder, apple, or cherry)

**Method**
1. Marinade: whisk water, ginger ale, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic powder, and ground ginger until dissolved.
2. Submerge cod in the marinade. Refrigerate **1 hour** (weigh down with a plate if needed).
3. Remove cod from marinade. Pat dry thoroughly with kitchen paper.
4. Preheat smoker to **180 °F / 82 °C** and add chosen wood.
5. Place cod on the smoker rack.
6. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest portion from the side.
7. Smoke for **2–3 hours at 180 °F** (full outdoor smoker) or approximately **30–40 minutes** in a compact box at 90–100 °C until internal reaches **140–145 °F / 60–63 °C**.
8. Remove, rest 3 minutes. Serve simply with lemon and a light salad.

### 3. Smoked White Fish Fillets — North American Camerons Stovetop Smoker (inspired by Camerons Products)
**Inspired by**: Camerons Products — https://cameronsproducts.com/fish-fillets/ (fetched 2026-05-31). The Camerons Stovetop Smoker is the North American direct equivalent of the ABU-röken: a stainless lidded box with sawdust tray, wire rack, and drip tray, placed on a kitchen stovetop. This recipe covers any mild white fish fillet — catfish, orange roughy, and equivalently cod, pollock, and saithe.
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min per 450 g · **Rest**: 3 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoke over **medium stovetop heat** at **15–20 minutes per 450 g (1 lb)**. For a 250 g cod fillet: approximately **8–12 minutes**. No internal temperature stated; "the fish will have a subtle earthy flavour." At visual doneness (opaque, flakes easily), internal is approximately **63–66 °C / 145–151 °F**.

**Ingredients**
- 250–450 g cod fillets (or any fresh mild white fish fillet), rinsed and patted dry
- Fresh lemon, halved
- Black pepper, freshly ground
- Dried dill
- 1 tbsp alder wood chips

**Method**
1. Rinse fillets and pat dry.
2. Load 1 tablespoon of alder chips into the base of the Camerons smoker. Slot in drip tray and rack.
3. Lay cod fillets on the rack. Squeeze fresh lemon over the flesh; season lightly with pepper and dill.
4. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest fillet from the side.
5. Close the Camerons lid. Set over **medium stovetop heat**.
6. Smoke for **15–20 minutes per 450 g** (approximately 8–15 minutes for a 250 g portion fillet).
7. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. Fish should be opaque throughout and flake easily.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with lemon.

### 4. Varmrökt torskfilé i röklåda — Swedish ABU-röken adaptation (inspired by Swedish outdoor cooking tradition)
**Inspired by**: Swedish outdoor cooking and fishing tradition — method summarised from general principles documented in Swedish fishing guides and the ABU Garcia / röklåda user tradition (cross-referenced with Köket.se and ICA recipe catalogue; widely documented 2026-05-31). The ABU-röken and generic röklåda are documented extensively for white fish (torsk, abborre, sik) in Swedish fishing and outdoor cooking literature.
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 10 min + 30–60 min dry-salt · **Cook**: 20–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**: ABU-röken chamber reaches 90–100 °C. A 200–250 g cod fillet reaches internal **63 °C / 145 °F** in approximately **20–30 minutes** at this chamber temperature. Thicker portions (35–40 mm) may require 30–35 minutes.

**Ingredients**
- 400–600 g skin-on torskfilé (cod fillet), portioned
- 1½ tbsp grovt salt (coarse salt)
- ½ tsp strösocker (sugar)
- Freshly ground black or white pepper
- 2–3 tbsp alspån (alder sawdust), fine-grade and dry
- Optional: 3–4 dried juniper berries on the sawdust

**Method**
1. Combine salt and sugar. Coat both sides of each portion. Rest at room temperature **30 minutes** (or refrigerate up to 1 hour). A light pellicle will form.
2. Rinse off excess salt; pat completely dry with kitchen paper.
3. Spread 2–3 tablespoons of alspån evenly in the röklåda tray. Add juniper berries if using.
4. Place the wire rack. Lay cod portions skin-side down on the rack, with space between each piece.
5. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest portion from the side, tip at the centre.
6. Close the lid. Place over a gas burner or campfire.
7. Smoke at approximately **90–100 °C chamber temperature** for **20–30 minutes** for 200–250 g portions.
8. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. Flesh should be firm, white throughout, and pull apart in large moist flakes. Do not exceed 68 °C — lean cod dries out quickly.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Serve warm with boiled potatoes, pepparrot (horseradish), and a wedge of lemon; or cold on knäckebröd with remoulade.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Device | Chamber temp | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| A Grill for All Seasons | NA | Pellet/outdoor smoker | 225 °F / 107 °C | **145 °F / 63 °C** (explicit) |
| Simply Meat Smoking | NA | Cabinet/pellet smoker | 180 °F / 82 °C | **140 °F / 60 °C** (explicit) |
| Camerons Products | NA | Stovetop smoker | medium burner (~90–100 °C) | ~63–66 °C visual |
| Swedish outdoor tradition | SE | ABU-röken / röklåda | 90–100 °C | ~63 °C at 20–30 min |

**Consensus pull range**: **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA minimum for fish). Simply Meat Smoking targets 140 °F / 60 °C; all others target 145 °F / 63 °C or use visual cues that correspond to ~63–66 °C. For lean cod, precision matters — pull at 63 °C and rest immediately; do not hold above 68 °C as the flesh dries rapidly.
