# Flounder × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Flounder is a lean, delicate flatfish with sweet, mild white flesh and a thin, flat profile that makes it exceptionally well suited to steaming. Unlike thicker round fish, flounder can be steamed whole, as bone-in portions, or as fillets in just 3–15 minutes depending on the equipment — the flat shape means heat penetrates to the centre from every surface simultaneously. Steaming preserves flounder's delicate moisture that would be lost in a pan or oven, and the clean flavour absorbs aromatics beautifully: ginger, scallion, lemongrass and citrus are natural pairings. This method is common in Chinese, Vietnamese, and Southeast Asian kitchens, and increasingly in European cooking.

## Styckesprofil

Flundra är en mager, delikat plattfisk med söt, mild vit fiskkött och en tunn, plan form som gör den exceptionellt väl lämpad för ångkokning. Till skillnad från tjockare rundfisk kan flundra tillagas hel, som benbehållande portioner eller som fileter på bara 3–15 minuter beroende på utrustning — den platta formen innebär att värmen tränger in till mitten från varje yta simultant. Ångkokning bevarar flundrans delikata fuktighet som annars skulle gå förlorad i panna eller ugn, och den rena smaken absorberar aromatika vackert: ingefära, salladslök, citrongräs och citrus är naturliga kombinationer. Denna metod är vanlig i kinesiska, vietnamesiska och sydostasiatiska kök, och alltmer i europeisk matlagning.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chinese Steamed Whole Fish — The Woks of Life
**Attribution**: https://thewoksoflife.com/steamed-whole-fish/
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: none (serve immediately)

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole flat fish (1–2 lb; flounder, plaice, grey sole, striped bass), scaled, gutted, fins trimmed
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce or seasoned soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- ¼ tsp salt
- ¼ tsp ground white pepper
- 3 tbsp water
- 3 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable or peanut)
- 15 g fresh ginger, peeled and cut into thin matchsticks (divided)
- 2 scallions, white and green parts separated, cut into thin strips

**Method**
1. Scale, gut and clean the flounder. Cut off tough fins with kitchen shears; leave head and tail intact for presentation.
2. Rinse thoroughly. **Do not add any salt or seasoning before steaming.**
3. Place the fish flat on a heat-proof plate.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest part of the back, angling toward the centre, before placing in the steamer.
5. Set up a steaming arrangement with enough boiling water for 9 minutes of vigorous steam.
6. Place the plate in the steamer and steam on high for 9 minutes.
7. Check: flesh should be completely opaque down to the bone; the bone may be slightly translucent.
8. Pour off all accumulated liquid from the plate.
9. Lay half the ginger, the scallion greens, and a little cilantro over the fish.
10. Whisk together soy sauce, sugar, salt, white pepper and water; heat 2 tbsp oil with remaining ginger until sizzling; combine and bring to a simmer.
11. Add remaining oil and scallion whites, bring back to a sizzle, then pour the entire hot mixture over the fish.
12. Serve immediately with steamed white rice.
13. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** Nine minutes of high steam brings the thin flounder flesh fully to temperature; probe confirms the centre.
14. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 2. Pla Kapong Neung Manao (Thai Steamed Fish with Lime) — 196 Flavors
**Attribution**: https://www.196flavors.com/thailand-pla-kapong-neung-manao/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 15 min · **Rest**: none

**Ingredients**
- 1–2 whole fish (barramundi, sea bass, flounder or similar flat fish; ~1.5–2 kg total), scaled and gutted
- 4 stalks lemongrass, bruised and cut into 3 cm sections (for cavity stuffing)
- 1 cup fish stock
- 2 tbsp palm sugar, chopped
- Juice of 3 limes
- 4 tbsp fish sauce
- 8 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 2 Thai red chillies, finely chopped
- ½ bunch fresh cilantro, chopped

**Method**
1. Make 3 diagonal scoring cuts on each side of the fish.
2. Stuff the lemongrass sections into the belly cavity.
3. Insert the probe through the scored cuts toward the thickest part of the back.
4. Steam in a bamboo steamer or double-boiler over vigorous boiling water for approximately 15 minutes.
5. While steaming, bring fish stock to a boil; add palm sugar and stir until dissolved. Remove from heat.
6. Allow to cool slightly; add lime juice, fish sauce, chopped garlic, chillies and cilantro.
7. Transfer cooked fish to a large serving platter; pour sauce over, piling garlic and chillies on top.
8. Serve with steamed white rice.
9. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** Scoring and lemongrass stuffing help heat penetrate the flat fish quickly; 15 minutes steaming ensures safe internal temperature throughout.
10. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 3. Microwave Steamed Flounder with Ginger Sizzle — Viet World Kitchen
**Attribution**: https://www.vietworldkitchen.com/blog/2022/02/microwave-steamed-fish-with-ginger-sizzle.html
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 3–5 min · **Rest**: none (serve immediately)

*Note: This recipe uses bone-in, skin-on flounder explicitly (purchased from H-Mart). The microwave steaming technique mimics Chinese steamer results and is described by the author as a Vietnamese-Chinese tradition.*

**Ingredients**
- ~450–550 g bone-in, skin-on flounder (steaks, bone-in sections or whole small fish)
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine (or dry sherry)
- 3 tbsp water
- Fine sea salt, to taste
- 1 chubby 4 cm knob ginger, peeled and cut into fine strips
- 2–3 medium scallions, whites cut into 5 cm pieces, greens cut into thin strips
- Handful of fresh shiitake mushrooms, thinly sliced
- 3 tbsp neutral oil (canola or peanut)
- 1–2 tbsp soy sauce

**Method**
1. Cut 2–3 shallow slits (max 6 mm) on each side if the fish is uneven in thickness. Rub with salt, rub into slits, then rinse and pat dry.
2. Spread scallion whites in a shallow microwave-safe bowl; lay the flounder on top.
3. Stuff slits with scallion whites; pour over the Shaoxing rice wine; lay mushrooms on top; add the water.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest part from the side.
5. Cover loosely with plastic wrap. Microwave on high for 3 minutes; check doneness by pressing with a knife tip. Continue in 30-second increments if needed.
6. Scatter scallion greens over cooked fish.
7. Add oil to ginger strips in a small saucepan; heat over medium, shaking pan, until ginger is lightly crisp and golden (~2 minutes).
8. Pour the sizzling ginger oil over the scallion greens; drizzle soy sauce over the fish.
9. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The probe confirms the bone-in flounder centre has reached safe temperature through the fast microwave steam.
10. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 4. Scholle auf Zucchini-Paprika-Bett aus dem Dampfgarer — Chefkoch.de
**Attribution**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3360121499636996/Scholle-auf-Zucchini-Paprika-Bett-aus-dem-Dampfgarer.html
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: none

*Note: The recipe specifies Scholle (plaice) fillets, a closely related European flatfish interchangeable with flounder in Dampfgarer applications.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 medium flatfish fillets (Scholle/plaice or flounder, filletiert)
- 1 medium courgette, washed and sliced into rounds
- 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and cut into strips
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Method**
1. Season the flatfish fillets lightly with salt and pepper; lay flat on the steam oven tray.
2. Slice courgette into rounds; cut pepper into strips. Arrange around and beneath the fillets on the tray; season vegetables lightly.
3. Insert the probe into the thickest fillet from the side.
4. Steam on the highest steam setting for 25–30 minutes.
5. Plate by first laying the vegetable bed on each plate, then placing the steamed fillets on top.
6. Serve with boiled potatoes in sauce or a side salad.
7. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The 25–30-minute high steam in a Dampfgarer is appropriate for a steam oven's gentler approach; the probe confirms the delicate fillets have reached minimum safe temperature.
8. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

All four sources converge on 60°C pull. The wide range of steam times (3–30 min) reflects differences in equipment and fish thickness, not differences in target temperature. The probe removes this ambiguity completely.

## What makes this method special

Steaming is arguably the optimal method for flounder: the fish's extreme thinness means it would overcook instantly in a pan or hot oven, but in a steamer the gentle, even heat yields perfectly moist flesh every time. The method spans cultures — from Chinese New Year ceremony fish to Vietnamese daily home cooking to German steam-oven meal prep — all arriving at the same pull temperature, all using aromatic garnish to complement the neutral flesh. The MEATER probe is especially valuable here because steam-cooked fish has no colour change to indicate doneness on the outside.
