# Flounder — Cut Overview

## Cut profile

European flounder (*Platichthys flesus*, Swedish: flundra, German: Flunder, Finnish: kampela, Estonian: lest) is a flatfish of the family Pleuronectidae, widely distributed in the North Sea, Baltic Sea, and Atlantic coasts of Europe. It is one of the most important inshore fish caught by recreational and artisanal fishermen in Scandinavia, the Baltic states, and the British Isles. Fat content is low (1–2 g per 100 g) and the flesh is white, mild, and slightly sweet. The fish is typically cooked whole (gutted, scaled) with both sides intact, as the thin, flat body shape means the flesh is equally accessible from either side; it can also be filleted into 4 thin fillets. Because of its flat shape, flounder cooks very quickly — 3–4 minutes per side in a pan or on a grill is usually sufficient. It is frequently pan-fried whole in butter with a light flour coating (*Flunder Müllerin Art* in German cooking), grilled over charcoal, or oven-baked. Hot smoking whole flounder in a stovetop box smoker (*abu-röken*, *røgekasse*, *savustuslaatikko*) is a traditional Scandinavian and Baltic preparation — the flat shape makes it ideal for box-smoking since the fish lies flat and smokes evenly. Flounder is also sold smoked commercially in Germany, Denmark, and Finland. Probe placement: insert from the edge into the thickest part of the top fillet, clear of the central spine.

## Styckesprofil

Flundra (*Platichthys flesus*; tyska: Flunder; finska: kampela) är en plattfisk utbredd i Nordsjön, Östersjön och Atlantkusten. Fetthalten är låg (1–2 g/100 g) med vitt, milt, lätt sött kött. Fisken tillagas typiskt hel (rensad, fjällad) med båda sidor intakta. Den platta formen gör den ideal för röklådan (abu-röken) — fisken lägger sig plant och röks jämnt. Stekning hel i smör med mjölpanering (*Müllerin*-metoden) är klassisk i tysk och skandinavisk matlagning. Sond placeras från kanten in i tjockaste del av överfilén, fri från mittenbenet.
