# Gilt-Head Bream — Cut Overview

## Cut profile

Gilt-head bream (*Sparus aurata*, Swedish: guldspirad / havsruda, Spanish/Italian: dorada / orata, French: daurade royale, German: Goldbrasse) is a premium Mediterranean sea fish of the family Sparidae, widely farmed in Greece, Turkey, Spain, and Italy and sold throughout European supermarkets and fish counters year-round. It is named for the distinctive gold stripe between its eyes. The flesh is white to off-white, firm, moist, with a delicate sweet flavour and a fat content of 3–5 g per 100 g. It is sold whole (300–600 g) or as fillets; cooking whole preserves moisture best. Pan-searing skin-on fillets in olive oil is the classic Italian preparation (*orata in padella*). Oven-baking whole with Mediterranean herbs, olive oil, lemon, and salt-crust is iconic in Greek and Italian cuisines (*orata al forno*, *dorada a la sal*). Grilling whole over charcoal is traditional across the Mediterranean. It is also widely pan-fried and baked in Scandinavian home cooking where it is sold as "dorada" or "guldspirad" at fish counters. The skin is edible and crisps well. Probe placement: insert into the thickest part of the fillet or, for whole fish, behind the head into the shoulder muscle.

## Styckesprofil

Köttet är vitt, fast, fuktigt och delikat sött med fetthalt 3–5 g/100 g. Säljs hel (300–600 g) eller som filé; tillagning hel bevarar fuktigheten bäst. Pannstekning skinnsida ned i olivolja är klassisk italiensk metod. Ugnsbakning hel med örter och olivolja (*orata al forno*) och saltskorstsbakning (*dorada a la sal*) är ikoniska mediterrana beredningar. Grillning hel över kol är tradition runt Medelhavet. Sond placeras i tjockaste delen av filén eller, för hel fisk, bakom huvudet in i axelmuskeln.
