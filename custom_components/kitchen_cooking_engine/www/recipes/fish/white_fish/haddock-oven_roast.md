# Haddock Fillet × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting haddock is a gentle dry-heat method that protects this thin, lean fillet better than aggressive pan heat when the fish is cooked in a sauced tray, crumb crust, or vegetable bed. The fillet is usually 1–2 cm thick and quickly shifts from translucent to opaque; that transition window is short and overcooking causes immediate moisture loss and small dry flakes. Probe placement should be lateral from the thick edge so the tip lands at the geometric center of the thickest part.

## Source recipes

### 1. Oven-Baked Crispy Panko-Crusted Haddock — Hamiltons Fish (UK)
**Inspired by**: https://hamiltonsfish.co.uk/blogs/recipes/oven-baked-crispy-panko-crusted-haddock  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Method notes**
1. Preheat oven to 200°C (fan 180°C), crumb and tray-prepare fillets.
2. Insert probe laterally into the thickest fillet before baking.
3. Bake until crust is golden and fish flakes; this aligns with a full-cook finish.
4. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** and rest briefly to finish.

### 2. Illes leichtes gebackenes Schellfischfilet ohne Panade — Chefkoch.de (Germany)
**Inspired by**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1572071264752239/Illes-leichtes-gebackenes-Schellfischfilet-ohne-Panade.html  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: about 30 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Method notes**
1. Fish pieces are layered in an oiled baking dish with tomato and a yogurt-egg topping.
2. Bake at 200°C top/bottom heat until lightly browned; recipe notes fish should remain slightly glassy in core.
3. Insert probe from the side into the largest piece because casserole toppings hide the center.
4. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F** to preserve moisture in the center before carryover.

### 3. New Nordic Haddock on Shredded Vegetables with Almond Gremolata — Panning The Globe (Scandinavian-style)
**Inspired by**: https://www.panningtheglobe.com/new-nordic-haddock-shredded-vegetables-almond-gremolata/  
**Serves**: 5 · **Prep**: 40 min · **Cook**: 15 min veg + 8–12 min fish · **Rest**: 2 min

**Method notes**
1. Vegetables are pre-roasted, then haddock is added and finished in the same tray.
2. Recipe explicitly notes to remove when just cooked and slightly underdone because carryover continues.
3. Probe from the thick edge into the fillet center to avoid reading tray heat.
4. **Pull at 59–60°C / 138–140°F** for a just-set, moist result.

Observed pull range is **59–61°C (138–142°F)**. Across casserole, crumbed, and tray-bake variants, the consistent finish is just-set opaque flesh with a short rest, which lands near the lower end of the cut's medium-to-well fish band.

## What makes this method special

Oven roasting is especially useful for haddock because it allows moisture buffering from toppings, oil, or vegetables while still giving controlled doneness in a thin fillet. Unlike pan-frying, the oven format supports one-pan vegetable pairings and crusted applications with less breakage risk when turning.
