# Pikeperch × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pikeperch is well suited to grilling because its firm, thick-sided fillets and structurally intact skin hold together on a grate or plancha better than most delicate white fish. The skin acts as a natural barrier against the direct heat of the grill; skin-on fillets placed skin-side down over medium-hot coals or a preheated gas grill need only 4–6 minutes per side and flip cleanly once the skin has carbonised. Whole fish can also be grilled in a fish basket, which is the common approach in German and Scandinavian riverside cooking. Because the flesh is lean, marinades or oil-basting are standard to prevent the surface from drying. Probe placement: insert laterally from the thicker edge of a fillet, or from the tail end toward the dorsal loin in a whole fish. Carryover off a hot grill surface is moderate (2–3 °C on a fillet rested off the grill).

## Styckesprofil

Gös lämpar sig väl för grillning eftersom de fasta, tjocksidiga filéerna och det intakta skinnet håller ihop på ett gallergaller eller en plancha bättre än de flesta delikata vitfiskar. Skinnet fungerar som en naturlig barriär mot grillens direktvärme; skinnsida-ned filéer placerade över medelheta kol eller ett förupphettat gasgaller behöver bara 4–6 minuter per sida och vänds rent när skinnet har bränt. Hel fisk kan också grillas i en fiskbåt, vilket är det vanliga sättet vid tyska och skandinaviska ågrillningar. Eftersom köttet är magert är marinader eller oljebastning standard för att förhindra att ytan torkar. Sonden placeras lateralt från den tjockare kanten på en filé, eller från stjärtänden mot ryggbiffsektionen på en hel fisk. Eftervärmen från en het grillyta är måttlig (2–3 °C på en vilad filé).

## Source recipes

### 1. Grillad gös med smörsås och basilika — Köket.se
**Inspired by**: https://www.koket.se/grillad-gos-med-smorsas-och-basilika
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 gösfiléer med skinn, 180–200 g styck
- 3 msk olivolja
- 1 citron, saft och skal
- 1 vitlöksklyfta, riven
- salt, vitpeppar
- 100 g smör
- 1 kruka basilika

**Method**
1. Marinate the pikeperch fillets in olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest and grated garlic for 30 minutes at room temperature.
2. Clean and oil the grill grate thoroughly. Preheat to medium-high heat (approximately 200 °C surface temperature).
3. Remove the fillets from the marinade and pat lightly. Season flesh side with salt and white pepper.
4. Insert the probe laterally into the thickest fillet at the skin edge, tip aimed at the flesh centre.
5. Place fillets skin-side down on the grill. Do not move for 5–6 minutes until the skin releases from the grate naturally.
6. Flip carefully with a wide spatula and grill the flesh side for 3–4 minutes.
7. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** (142°F). The Swedish grilling convention expects a fully cooked, just-flaking pikeperch; the 3-minute rest completes the set.
8. Rest 3 min off heat. Meanwhile melt butter, add fresh basil leaves and pour over the fish.
9. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 2. Gegrillter Zander auf der Haut — Chefkoch.de
**Inspired by**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1820941302625260/Gegrillter-Zander-Haut.html
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Zander fillets with skin, 200 g each
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- salt and freshly ground pepper
- lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Brush the fillets on both sides with olive oil mixed with crushed garlic and thyme. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Preheat a gas grill or charcoal grill to medium-high heat. Oil the grill grate.
3. Insert the probe laterally into the thicker fillet at the skin edge, tip toward the centre of the flesh.
4. Place fillets skin-side down on the preheated grill. Do not lift or move for 5–6 minutes.
5. When the skin lifts cleanly from the grate and the flesh colour has changed about two-thirds up the side, flip and grill flesh-side for 3 minutes.
6. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** (142°F). The German convention for grilled pikeperch expects a fully cooked fillet that is still moist and cohesive.
7. Rest 2 min. Serve with lemon wedges and a green salad.
8. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 3. Sandre grillé au fenouil flambé — Cuisine.journaldesfemmes.fr
**Inspired by**: https://cuisine.journaldesfemmes.fr/recette/342671-sandre-grille-au-fenouil
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole sandre (*pikeperch*), 1–1.2 kg, gutted and scaled
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, halved
- 1 bunch dried fennel stalks
- 1 lemon, sliced
- salt and freshly ground pepper
- 3 tbsp Pernod or pastis (for flambéing)

**Method**
1. Score the sides of the whole pikeperch diagonally three times per side, cutting down to the bone.
2. Rub inside and outside with olive oil and garlic; season generously. Fill the belly with lemon slices.
3. Place the whole fish in a hinged fish basket oiled with olive oil.
4. Insert the probe from the tail end toward the thickest loin section just behind the pectoral fin.
5. Grill over medium-hot coals or a hot gas grill for 5–6 minutes per side.
6. Shortly before the probe approaches target, scatter the dried fennel stalks over the coals (or around the gas burner). Flambe with Pernod for a brief aniseed smoke.
7. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F** (140°F). The French whole-fish grill standard for sandre expects the thickest shoulder cross-section to be just set; the 3-minute rest completes the loin section.
8. Rest 3 min. Open the fish basket and serve directly.
9. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 4. Roštilj od smudja s povrćem — coolinarika.com
**Inspired by**: https://www.coolinarika.com/recept/rostilj-smudj-povrce
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 smudj (*pikeperch*) fillets with skin, 170–200 g each
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp dried Dalmatian herbs (rosemary, thyme, oregano)
- salt and black pepper
- 1 lemon

**Method**
1. Mix the olive oil, garlic, dried herbs, salt and pepper; coat the fillets on all sides and leave to marinate for 30 minutes.
2. Clean and oil the grill grate. Preheat to medium-high.
3. Insert the probe into the thickest fillet at the skin edge, tip toward the centre.
4. Place fillets skin-side down. Grill 5–6 minutes without moving.
5. Flip and grill the flesh side 3–4 minutes.
6. **Pull at 62°C / 144°F** (144°F). The Croatian/Dalmatian tradition treats pikeperch as a firm freshwater fish to be cooked through; the Mediterranean herb marinade provides protection from the direct heat.
7. Rest 3 min. Squeeze lemon over the fish and serve with grilled vegetables.
8. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Method notes | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Köket.se | Sweden | Skin-on fillets, medium-high grill | 61°C / 142°F |

| Coolinarika | Croatia | Herb-marinated fillets | 62°C / 144°F |

The observed pull range is **60–62°C**. All four traditions — Scandinavian, German, French, and Balkan — cook pikeperch to a firm, fully opaque, just-flaking finish on the grill, which corresponds to the well_done doneness in the fish temperature scale.

## What makes this method special

Grilling gives pikeperch the most flavour complexity of any direct-heat method because the char from the grate, the smoke, and the Maillard browning of the skin all combine. The tight, structured flesh means the fillets and whole fish hold their shape on the grate where a more delicate white fish would fall through the bars or break apart on turning. The Croatian and Serbian tradition of grilling pikeperch (*smudj*) from the Danube and Sava rivers is among the oldest culinary traditions for this species in Europe.
