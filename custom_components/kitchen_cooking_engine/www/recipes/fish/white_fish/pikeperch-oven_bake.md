# Pikeperch × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven baking suits pikeperch well because the dry, enclosed heat can cook the fish evenly from all sides without requiring active attention. The method is used across its range for both whole fish (gutted, skin on) and fillets. Whole fish are typically baked at 180–200 °C for 20–35 minutes depending on size; fillets at 180–200 °C for 12–18 minutes. Because pikeperch flesh is lean and compact, it dries at the edges if the surface is left unprotected — most traditions add a small amount of butter, cream, wine, or sour cream to the baking dish to create moisture around the fish. A foil tent or a sauce-covered preparation slows evaporation. Carryover in a covered or sauced preparation is modest (1–2 °C); in a bare oven-roasted fillet it can reach 3–4 °C. Probe placement: insert from the tail end of a whole fish or the thicker lateral edge of a fillet, aiming for the thickest cross-section just behind the dorsal hump.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsstekning passar gös väl eftersom den torra, inneslutna värmen kan tillaga fisken jämnt från alla håll utan aktivt arbete. Metoden används i hela gösets utbredningsområde, för hel fisk (rensad, med skinn) och filéer. Hel fisk bakas typiskt vid 180–200 °C i 20–35 minuter beroende på storlek; filéer vid 180–200 °C i 12–18 minuter. Eftersom gösens kött är magert och kompakt torkar det vid kanterna om ytan lämnas oskyddad — de flesta traditioner tillsätter en liten mängd smör, grädde, vin eller gräddfil i bakformen för att skapa fukt runt fisken. Eftervärmen i en täckt eller såddfylld tillagning är blygsam (1–2 °C); i en bar ugnsrostad filé kan den nå 3–4 °C. Sonden placeras från stjärtänden på en hel fisk eller från den tjockare laterala kanten på en filé, riktad mot det tjockaste tvärsnitt strax bakom ryggfenans bas.

## Source recipes

### 1. Ugnsbakad hel gös med citron och örter — ICA.se
**Inspired by**: https://www.ica.se/recept/ugnsbakad-gos-med-citron-och-ort/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole pikeperch, 1–1.2 kg, gutted and scaled
- 1 lemon, sliced
- 4 sprigs fresh dill
- 3 sprigs fresh parsley
- 1 garlic clove, sliced
- 2 tbsp butter, softened
- 1 dl dry white wine
- sea salt, white pepper
- rapeseed oil, for the dish

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 200 °C (fan 185 °C). Oil a roasting dish large enough for the fish.
2. Rinse the gutted pikeperch inside and out; pat completely dry.
3. Season the belly cavity with salt and pepper. Fill with lemon slices, dill, parsley and garlic.
4. Rub the outside of the fish with softened butter; season generously with salt and white pepper.
5. Insert the probe from the tail end along the spine toward the thickest loin section behind the head.
6. Pour the white wine into the bottom of the dish and lay the fish in.
7. Bake 30–35 minutes, basting once halfway through with the pan juices.
8. **Pull at 62°C / 144°F** (144°F). The Swedish whole-fish bake convention expects a fully opaque, just-flaking flesh; the wine-basted dish gives minimal carryover.
9. Rest 5 min covered loosely with foil. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 2. Zander im Ofen mit Sauerrahm und Zwiebeln — Chefkoch.de
**Inspired by**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1064181210218734/Zander-im-Ofen-mit-Sauerrahm.html
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g pikeperch fillets, skin on
- salt and pepper
- 2 onions, thinly sliced into rings
- 200 ml sour cream (*Sauerrahm*)
- 2 tbsp breadcrumbs
- 20 g butter
- 1 tbsp fresh chives, chopped
- paprika, to taste

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 190 °C. Butter a shallow baking dish.
2. Lay the pikeperch fillets skin-side down in the dish; season well with salt, pepper and a little paprika.
3. Scatter the onion rings over the fish.
4. Spoon the sour cream evenly over the fish and onions to form a coating. Sprinkle with breadcrumbs and dot with the remaining butter.
5. Insert the probe laterally into the thicker fillet at the skin edge, tip at the flesh centre.
6. Bake 25–30 minutes until the sour cream topping is golden and bubbling.
7. **Pull at 62°C / 144°F** (144°F). The sour-cream layer acts as a moisture barrier; the fillet finishes gently and uniformly without drying.
8. Rest 3 min. Scatter over the chives. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 3. Sandre au four à la façon alsacienne — Marmiton.org
**Inspired by**: https://www.marmiton.org/recettes/recette_sandre-au-four-alsacien_28491.aspx
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sandre fillets, skinless, 150–180 g each
- salt and white pepper
- 40 g shallots, finely chopped
- 30 g butter + 10 g for the dish
- 150 ml dry Riesling
- 100 ml fish stock
- 100 ml crème fraîche
- 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 180 °C. Butter a shallow oven dish and scatter the shallots over the base.
2. Season the sandre fillets on both sides with salt and white pepper and lay them over the shallots.
3. Pour in the Riesling and fish stock until the liquid comes halfway up the fillets.
4. Dot the fillets with butter. Insert the probe laterally into the thickest fillet, tip at the centre.
5. Bake 20–25 minutes, basting every 8 minutes with the pan liquid.
6. Remove the fish; keep warm. Reduce the cooking liquid in a small pan; add the crème fraîche and reduce to a coating consistency.
7. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** (142°F). The Alsatian wine-poached oven method cooks the fish gently in liquid rather than dry heat; the fillet finishes moist and just set.
8. Rest 3 min. Spoon the sauce over and garnish with parsley.
9. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 4. Pečený candát se zeleninou — Recepty.cz
**Inspired by**: https://www.recepty.cz/recept/peceny-candat-se-zeleninou-92135
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole pikeperch (*candát*), 1.2 kg, gutted and scaled
- salt, ground black pepper
- 2 tbsp butter, melted
- 1 lemon, juice and slices
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 1 parsley root, sliced
- 1 small celeriac, diced
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves
- 200 ml dry white wine
- fresh parsley and dill

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 200 °C. Score the sides of the whole pikeperch with diagonal cuts 2 cm apart.
2. Season inside and out with salt and pepper; rub with melted butter and lemon juice.
3. Place the root vegetables and onion in a roasting dish; lay the fish on top. Tuck the lemon slices and garlic around it.
4. Pour the white wine over the vegetables and fish.
5. Insert the probe from the tail end along the spine toward the thickest cross-section behind the pectoral fin.
6. Roast 30–35 minutes, basting every 10 minutes.
7. **Pull at 62°C / 144°F** (144°F). The Czech whole-fish roast cooks fully through; the root-vegetable bed keeps the underside moist. A slightly higher pull here reflects the dense shoulder meat in this 1.2 kg fish.
8. Rest 5 min covered. Serve with the reduced pan juices, fresh herbs and boiled potatoes.
9. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Method notes | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| ICA.se | Sweden | Whole fish, wine baste, buttered | 62°C / 144°F |
| Chefkoch.de | Germany | Fillets under sour cream topping | 62°C / 144°F |
| Marmiton.org | France | Fillets poached in Riesling in oven | 61°C / 142°F |
| Recepty.cz | Czech Republic | Whole fish on root vegetables, wine | 62°C / 144°F |

The observed pull range is **61–62°C**. All four traditions finish pikeperch near the USDA fish minimum; the sauce or basting liquid present in every recipe prevents the surface from drying before the centre reaches temperature. This consistency across national methods supports well_done as the correct doneness recommendation.

## What makes this method special

Oven baking allows pikeperch to be cooked whole — the most impressive presentation — and to be surrounded with aromatic vegetables, wine, and butter that baste the flesh passively as it cooks. The method is the main route for Czech, Slovak, and Polish pikeperch preparations, where the fish is treated as a centrepiece and served from the dish it was roasted in. A temperature probe eliminates the guesswork around whole-fish doneness, particularly when the fish is over 1 kg.
