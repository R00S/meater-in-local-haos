# Pikeperch × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pikeperch is a lean freshwater predator with thick, white, low-fat fillets that hold together better than perch but dry more quickly than salmonids. Pan frying suits it because the firm flakes can take direct contact heat and browning without falling apart, especially when the fish is lightly floured. Because the flesh is so lean, most traditions pair it with butter, cream, wine, or sauce to replace the moisture lost in the pan. Carryover is small, so the fish should be pulled close to its intended final doneness.

## Styckesprofil

Gös är en mager sötvattensfisk med tjocka, vita filéer som håller ihop bättre än abborre men torkar snabbare än laxfiskar. Pannstekning passar därför väl eftersom det fasta köttet klarar direkt kontaktvärme och bryning utan att falla sönder, särskilt när fisken mjölas lätt. Eftersom köttet är så magert kombineras det i många traditioner med smör, grädde, vin eller sås som ersätter fuktförlusten från pannan. Eftervärmen är liten, så fisken bör tas av nära önskad sluttemperatur.

**Method**
1. Blanch the spinach briefly, squeeze it dry and chop it.
2. Cook the potatoes. Sweat the garlic and onion in butter, add the spinach, dust with a little flour and loosen with the cream and milk to make creamed spinach.
3. Mix softened butter with chopped dill, Worcestershire sauce, salt and white pepper.
4. Salt the pikeperch and turn it in the flour.
5. Insert the probe into the thickest part of the fillet from the side.
6. Brown butter in a frying pan over medium heat and fry the fish until golden on both sides.
7. Serve with lemon wedges, creamed spinach, potatoes and dill butter.
8. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** (142°F). The Swedish recipe is a classic flour-and-butter fry for a lean freshwater fish and reads as fully cooked but still moist.
9. Rest 2 min. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 2. Gebratener Zander — Chefkoch.de
**Inspired by**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1907311310977628/Gebratener-Zander.html
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pikeperch fillets, 200 g each
- 1 garlic clove
- 1 rosemary sprig
- 4 sage leaves
- 2 thyme sprigs
- 1 tbsp clarified butter
- 1 tbsp butter
- lemon juice
- salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Slice the garlic thinly and marinate the pikeperch with a little lemon juice and the garlic.
2. Heat the clarified butter in a frying pan and add the rosemary, sage and thyme.
3. Insert the probe into the thickest part of one fillet from the side.
4. Fry the pikeperch skin-side down first, then turn and add the garlic. Finish with the butter and season with salt and freshly ground pepper.
5. Serve immediately with salad or pan-fried fennel.
6. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F** (140°F). The very short fry and immediate service point to a just-cooked centre rather than a long hold in the pan.
7. Rest 1 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 3. Stegt sandart — Kogebog.dk
**Inspired by**: https://www.kogebog.dk/518/stegt-sandart
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: not stated · **Cook**: 20 min total · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sandart fillets, 80 g each
- 2 tbsp coarse steak pepper
- 2 tbsp oil
- 1 lime
- 20 g butter
- 2 garlic cloves, chopped
- 2 tbsp soy sauce

**Method**
1. Press the coarse pepper firmly onto the sandart fillets.
2. Heat the oil in a pan over high heat and lay the fillets in on the flesh side first; set a halved lime cut-side down in the pan beside them.
3. After 2 minutes, lower the heat, add the butter and chopped garlic, turn the fish and let it finish gently for another 2 minutes.
4. Drip over the soy sauce and serve at once.
5. Insert the probe into the thickest fillet from the side immediately after turning so the tip sits in the centre.
6. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F** (140°F). The Danish recipe uses a very short high-heat fry with a gentle finish, suggesting a nearly done but still juicy centre.
7. Rest 1 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 4. Pavés de sandre au vin du Jura et jeunes poireaux braisés — Chef Simon / Tumay
**Inspired by**: https://chefsimon.com/gourmets/tumay/recettes/paves-de-sandre-au-vin-du-jura-et-jeunes-poireaux-braises
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pikeperch pavés, 150 g each
- 20 g butter
- 2 cl oil
- salt
- pepper
- flour
- 10 mini leeks
- 1 litre vegetable or chicken stock
- 80 g smoked bacon lardons
- 150 g mushrooms
- 40 g shallots, minced
- 20 g butter
- 1 small carrot, mirepoix
- 4 cm celery stalk, mirepoix
- 1 thyme sprig
- 1 bay leaf
- 15 cl red Jura wine
- 20 cl reduced veal stock

**Method**
1. Trim and wash the leeks, then simmer them gently in the stock until tender and drain.
2. Make the sauce by sweating the shallots, carrot, celery, thyme and bay in butter, then reduce with the Jura wine and veal stock until glossy.
3. Quarter and sauté the mushrooms with the lardons.
4. Salt, pepper and flour the pikeperch pavés.
5. Insert the probe sideways into the thicker pavé.
6. Heat the butter and oil in a pan and cook the fish gently for 3-4 minutes per side according to thickness; transfer to a rack and cover loosely with foil for 5 minutes.
7. Warm the leeks in the fish pan and plate with the sauce, mushrooms and lardons.
8. **Pull at 59°C / 138°F** (138°F). The 5-minute rest after a gentle fry indicates a deliberate finish just below fully set, with carryover completing the cook.
9. Rest 5 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

## What makes this method special

Pan frying gives pikeperch a thin browned crust without needing the longer drying exposure of the oven. A light flour coat protects the lean surface proteins and helps the fish release from the pan before the flakes break. Because the fillets are relatively thick and structured, they tolerate turning better than perch while still benefiting from quick cooking. Sauce pairings matter here because the method creates flavour through browning, but the fish itself contributes little fat.
