# Pikeperch × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pikeperch is a classic box-smoker fish in Scandinavia and Germany, where whole fish or large fillets are hot-smoked in a stovetop or garden smokebox (*abu-röken*, *Tischräucherofen*). The lean flesh has a surprisingly affinity for light smoke — alder and beech are the traditional fuels — and the resulting texture is firm, dry at the surface, and clean-flavoured. Because pikeperch lacks the high fat of eel or mackerel, the smoking phase must be managed carefully to avoid drying the flesh before the centre is cooked through; many recipes call for a short pre-brine or dry salt to help the surface form a pellicle. Whole fish of 400–700 g typically smoke for 20–40 minutes in a tabletop box at 80–110 °C box temperature; large fillets smoke faster. Unlike cold-smoking, hot smoking drives the internal temperature of the flesh up to safe levels within the session. Carryover after smoking is small (1–2 °C), and the fish may cool slightly during an optional post-smoke rest. Probe placement: insert from the tail end toward the thickest loin section in a whole fish, or laterally from the thicker edge in large fillets.

## Styckesprofil

Gös är en klassisk röklådefisk i Skandinavien och Tyskland, där hel fisk eller stora filéer varmröks i en bordsröklåda (*abu-röken*, *Tischräucherofen*). Det magra köttet har en förvånansvärt fin affinitet med lätt rök — al och bok är de traditionella bränslena — och den resulterande texturen är fast, torr vid ytan och renflavig. Eftersom gös saknar den höga fetthalten hos ål eller makrill måste rökfasen hanteras noggrant för att undvika att köttet torkas ut innan kärnan är genomkokt; många recept kräver en kort försaltning eller torrsaltning för att hjälpa ytan att bilda en pellicle. Hela fiskar på 400–700 g röks typiskt i 20–40 minuter i en bordsröklåda vid 80–110 °C lådtemperatur; stora filéer röks snabbare. Till skillnad från kallrökning driver varmrökning köttet inre temperatur upp till säkra nivåer under sessionen. Eftervärmen är liten (1–2 °C) och fisken kan svalna något under en valfri vila efter rökning. Sonden placeras från stjärtänden mot den tjockaste ryggbiffsektionen i en hel fisk, eller lateralt från den tjockare kanten i stora filéer.

## Source recipes

### 1. ABU-rökt gös — Fiskeklubben.se / traditionell metod
**Inspired by**: https://www.fiskeklubben.se/article/abu-roka-gos
**Serves**: not stated · **Prep**: 30 min + 2 h salting · **Cook**: 25–35 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2–3 whole pikeperch, 400–600 g each, gutted, skin on, heads on
- coarse sea salt, for dry-salting
- alder wood chips (Swedish: alspån)

**Method**
1. Rinse and gut the pikeperch; leave the heads on. Pat dry inside and out.
2. Rub with coarse sea salt inside the belly cavity and lightly on the outside. Leave to salt for 1–2 hours in the refrigerator on a rack so moisture can drain.
3. Rinse the salt off and pat dry. Leave uncovered in the refrigerator or at room temperature for 30 minutes to form a pellicle.
4. Place a thin layer of alder chips in the bottom of the smoker box; do not pack tightly.
5. Insert the probe from the tail end of the largest fish toward the thick loin section, keeping the tip clear of the spine.
6. Place the fish on the smoker rack, not touching. Close the lid and heat on the stovetop over medium-high heat until smoke appears (about 3–4 minutes), then reduce to medium heat.
7. Smoke 25–35 minutes depending on fish size (25 min for 400 g, 35 min for 600 g).
8. **Pull at 64°C / 147°F** (147°F). The Swedish box-smoke convention for pikeperch expects a fully cooked, dry-surfaced fish that lifts from the rack cleanly.
9. Rest 10 min with the lid slightly ajar. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 2. Geräucherter Zander in der Tischräucherofen — Anglerboard.de
**Inspired by**: https://www.anglerboard.de/geraucherten-zander-in-tischraucherofen/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 3 h brining · **Cook**: 30 min · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole pikeperch, 700–800 g, gutted and scaled
- 1 litre cold water
- 30 g table salt (3% brine)
- 1 tsp black peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 slice lemon
- beech wood shavings (*Buchenspäne*)

**Method**
1. Mix the water, salt, peppercorns, bay leaf and lemon to make a light brine. Submerge the gutted pikeperch and brine for 2–3 hours in the refrigerator.
2. Remove the fish; rinse and pat thoroughly dry. Air-dry on a rack for 30 minutes.
3. Place one thin layer of beech wood shavings (or a mix of beech and oak) in the smoker tray.
4. Insert the probe from the tail end along the spine toward the thick shoulder section.
5. Position the fish on the rack; close the smoker lid. Place on the gas burner over medium-high heat.
6. Once smoke is visible, reduce to medium heat. Smoke for 28–32 minutes depending on fish thickness.
7. **Pull at 65°C / 149°F** (149°F). The German recipe uses beech smoke at slightly higher box temperatures than the Swedish method; the larger fish requires the extra margin to confirm the shoulder is fully cooked.
8. Open the lid and rest 15 min. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 3. Rookzander (gerookte snoekbaars) — VisRoker.nl
**Inspired by**: https://www.visroker.nl/rookzander-snoekbaars/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 4 h brining · **Cook**: 35–45 min · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whole pikeperch (*snoekbaars*), 500–600 g each, gutted
- 1 litre cold water
- 60 g coarse sea salt (6% brine)
- 1 tsp sugar
- alder wood chips

**Method**
1. Prepare a 6% brine with salt and a little sugar dissolved in cold water. Submerge the pikeperch and brine for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
2. Remove the fish; rinse and pat dry. Allow to dry-cure on a rack in a cool place for 1–2 hours until a shiny pellicle forms.
3. Prepare the smoker with alder chips. Preheat the smoker to 70–80 °C box temperature.
4. Insert the probe from the tail end toward the thick mid-loin section.
5. Place the fish in the smoker. Smoke at 70–80 °C for the first 20 minutes (drying phase); then raise to 100–110 °C for 15–25 minutes (smoking phase).
6. **Pull at 65°C / 149°F** (149°F). The Dutch two-phase method — low drying heat then higher smoking heat — brings the fish to a fully rendered, firm finish above the safe minimum.
7. Rest 20 min outside the smoker before serving warm or chilling for serving cold.
8. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 4. Savustettu kuha — Kalajamies.fi
**Inspired by**: https://www.kalajamies.fi/reseptit/savustettu-kuha-roykkerokladan/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 h salting · **Cook**: 30–40 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whole kuha (*pikeperch*), 500 g each, gutted and gilled
- coarse sea salt
- alder or bird-cherry wood chips (*leppä* or *tuomi*)

**Method**
1. Score the sides of the gutted kuha with two diagonal cuts per side.
2. Rub inside and outside with coarse sea salt. Rest on a rack for 1 hour; do not rinse.
3. Pat gently to remove surface brine droplets. Leave to dry briefly.
4. Fill the smoker tray with alder or bird-cherry wood chips.
5. Insert the probe from the tail end toward the thickest loin section.
6. Place kuha on the rack; close the lid. Heat on the stovetop over medium-high until smoke forms (3–4 min); lower to medium.
7. Smoke 30–40 minutes.
8. **Pull at 64°C / 147°F** (147°F). The Finnish röklåda tradition expects a fully cooked fish that separates cleanly from the backbone; bird-cherry (*tuomi*) wood gives a distinctly Finnish aromatic character.
9. Rest 10 min. Serve hot or cold, with rye bread and butter.
10. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Method notes | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Fiskeklubben.se | Sweden | Tabletop alder-smoke box, dry-salted | 64°C / 147°F |
| Anglerboard.de | Germany | Beech-smoke box, brined | 65°C / 149°F |
| VisRoker.nl | Netherlands | Two-phase alder smoke, brined | 65°C / 149°F |
| Kalajamies.fi | Finland | Tabletop alder/bird-cherry box | 64°C / 147°F |

The observed pull range is **64–65°C**. All four smoking traditions finish pikeperch slightly above the USDA minimum to ensure the dense shoulder meat of whole fish is completely set and the surface has dried and flavoured. The consistency across Swedish, German, Dutch and Finnish sources confirms this as the correct target for hot-smoked pikeperch.

## What makes this method special

Smoking transforms pikeperch from a mild-flavoured table fish into a delicacy with a complex, wood-scented profile. The lean flesh absorbs smoke aromas more cleanly than fatty fish, where excess fat can carry bitter compounds. The Swedish box-smoker tradition (*abu-röken*) made whole smoked pikeperch a weekend ritual in Scandinavian outdoor cooking. Unlike eel or mackerel, pikeperch holds its white, flaky texture after smoking and can be served warm or chilled equally well.
