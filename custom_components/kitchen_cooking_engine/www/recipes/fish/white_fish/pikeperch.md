# Pikeperch — Cut Overview

## Cut profile

Pikeperch, also called zander (*Sander lucioperca*, Swedish: gös, German: Zander, French: sandre, Finnish: kuha), is a large freshwater and brackish-water predator native to Europe and Western Asia, now also common in the Baltic, rivers of Scandinavia, Germany, France, and Eastern Europe. It is considered one of the finest table fish in Central and Northern Europe. The flesh is lean (1–2 g fat per 100 g), firm, very white, boneless, and flaky with a mild, clean flavour superior to many sea fish — sometimes called the "freshwater turbot". Zander has fewer and larger bones than pike, making filleting much easier; the fillets are thick-sided and hold together well. Pan-searing skin-on fillets in butter is the most celebrated preparation in Scandinavian and German cooking (*gebratener Zander*). Oven-baking whole or as fillets is also traditional. Hot smoking whole pikeperch or large fillets in a stovetop box smoker (*abu-röken* / *Tischräucherofen*) is widely practised in Scandinavia and Germany. Grilling skin-on fillets over moderate heat is popular in Sweden and France. The skin is edible and crisps very well. Probe placement: insert into the thickest part of the fillet, behind the shoulder.

## Styckesprofil

Gös (*Sander lucioperca*; tyska: Zander; franska: sandre; finska: kuha) är en stor sötvattens- och brackvattensrovfisk och anses vara en av de finaste matfiskarna i Central- och Nordeuropa. Köttet är magert (1–2 g fett/100 g), fast, vitt, nästan benfritt och flintskarpt med mild, ren smak — ibland kallat "sötvattnets piggvar". Gösfilén är tjocksidig och håller ihop väl. Pannstekning skinnsida ned i smör är den mest hyllade beredningen i skandinavisk och tysk matlagning. Varmrökning av hel gös eller storfilé i röklåda (abu-röken / Tischräucherofen) är utbredd i Skandinavien och Tyskland. Sond placeras i tjockaste delen av filén, bakom skuldran.
