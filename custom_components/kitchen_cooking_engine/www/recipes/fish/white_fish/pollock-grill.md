# Pollock × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pollock (saithe) is a lean, firm North Atlantic gadoid with white-to-pale flesh and a slightly more pronounced flavour than cod or haddock. The firm texture makes it one of the few lean white fish that tolerates open-grill heat reasonably well — provided the fish is coated, marinated, wrapped in fatty material (bacon, cured ham), or topped with a protective crust to prevent the surface from drying before the interior is cooked through. Grilling pollock is well established in British, German, Swedish and Norwegian kitchens: it stands up to high direct heat, picks up char flavour from the grill marks, and pairs well with robust accompaniments like cured meats, mustard, garden herbs, and acidic vegetable sauces.

## Styckesprofil

Sej är en mager, fast nordatlantisk torskfisk med vit till ljus fiskkött och något kraftigare smak än torsk eller kolja. Den fasta texturen gör sej till en av de få magra vitfiskarna som klarar öppen grillvärme relativt väl — förutsatt att fisken täcks, marineras, lindas in i fettrikt material (bacon, rökt fläsk) eller toppas med ett skyddande krustskal för att förhindra att ytan torkar ut innan innertemperaturen nåtts. Grillad sej är väletablerat i brittiska, tyska, svenska och norska kök: det klarar hög direkt värme, tar upp kolgrillarömerna och passar utmärkt med kraftiga tillbehör som fläskkött, senap, örter och syrliga grönsakssåser.

## Source recipes

### 1. Grilled Pollock with Wild Garlic Crust — Great British Chefs
**Attribution**: https://www.greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/grilled-pollock-recipe-wild-garlic-crust
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: ~25 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 pollock fillet, pin-boned, skinned, cut into 4 even portions
- Wild garlic crust (wild garlic blended with butter)
- Tomato fondue (plum tomatoes, onion, garlic, olive oil, tomato juice)
- Purple sprouting broccoli, 16 stems
- Polenta gnocchi (baked potato, Parmesan, pasta flour, egg yolks)
- Double cream, 300 ml; Noilly Prat vermouth, 50 ml (sauce)
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Make the wild garlic butter crust between sheets of parchment and chill.
2. Make the tomato fondue: sweat onion and garlic, add plum tomato flesh, add tomato juice and reduce to a thick sauce. Cool.
3. Preheat a chargrill pan over high heat.
4. Season the pollock lightly with salt. Top each portion with a spoonful of tomato fondue; press the cut crust on top.
5. Insert the probe into the thickest portion from the side before placing under the grill.
6. Place the crusted pollock under a hot grill until the crust is just coloured; finish in the oven at 180°C / 356°F for 2–3 minutes.
7. Chargrill the blanched broccoli until charred on both sides. Shallow-fry the gnocchi until golden.
8. Serve immediately with gnocchi, broccoli and the Noilly Prat cream sauce.
9. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The combined grill + brief oven finish brings the lean pollock to just cooked; the crust insulates the top so the interior reaches doneness slightly after the surface colour forms.
10. Rest 2 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 2. Gegrillte Seelachsfilets — Chefkoch.de
**Attribution**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1143921220769169/Gegrillte-Seelachsfilets.html
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Marinate**: 2 h · **Cook**: 12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 pollock fillets (frozen, thawed)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 130 ml dry white wine
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 garlic clove, pressed
- chopped flat-leaf parsley, to taste
- 1 tsp black peppercorns
- salt
- 1 tbsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Lay the pollock fillets in a shallow dish. Whisk together olive oil, white wine, bay leaf, pressed garlic, parsley, peppercorns, salt and lemon juice to make a marinade.
2. Pour the marinade over the fillets and let them thaw in it for approximately 2 hours (or marinate fresh fillets for 30 min).
3. Remove fillets from marinade and pat dry.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest fillet from the side before placing on the grill.
5. Grill over high heat for a total of 10–12 minutes, turning once halfway through. Brush with the reserved marinade several times during grilling.
6. Serve with rice and a side salad.
7. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The wine-herb marinade keeps the lean surface moist; 10–12 minutes on a hot grill brings the fish to fully opaque.
8. Rest 2 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 3. Grillad sejrygg med sidfläsk — Köket.se
**Attribution**: https://www.koket.se/mitt-kok---sverigeresan/tommy-myllymaki/grillad-sejrygg-med-sidflask
**Serves**: not stated (serves ~4) · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: ~20–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g pollock back (sejrygg)
- 400 g cured side pork (sidfläsk), in thin slices
- 3 tbsp picked thyme leaves
- 25 g butter
- 2 garlic cloves, sliced
- 100 g haricots verts
- 100 g sugar snap peas
- 100 g dried white beans, cooked
- 1 tbsp strong mustard
- 1 banana shallot, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 3 tbsp cooking oil
- 3 tbsp cream
- salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Season the pollock with a little salt and the thyme leaves. Wrap the pollock back in the side pork slices.
2. Slice the garlic and warm it gently in the butter until fragrant.
3. Insert the probe into the centre of the wrapped pollock from the end.
4. Grill the fish over medium heat, basting with the garlic butter throughout grilling, until the core temperature reaches 37°C / 99°F. (Note: the recipe's target of 37°C is for a "warm pink" centre that the chef intended; for food-safety compliance, continue to 60°C / 140°F.)
5. Blanch the haricots verts and sugar snaps briefly in lightly salted water; combine with the white beans.
6. Whisk the mustard, shallot, rapeseed oil, cooking oil and cream into a dressing. Season. Toss with the beans.
7. Serve the grilled pollock over the bean-mustard salad.
8. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** Wrapped in fatty side pork the lean pollock cooks gently from the outside; the bacon fat bastes the flesh throughout and prevents drying.
9. Rest 3 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 4. Tropisk sei på grillwok — Aperitif.no
**Attribution**: https://www.aperitif.no/oppskrifter/oppskrift/tropisk-sei-pa-grillwok,62623
**Serves**: not stated · **Prep/Marinate**: 4–6 h · **Cook**: 3 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- Pollock fillets (sei)
- Marinade: oil, acid (lime/vinegar), garlic, chilli, herbs (recipe-specific blend)
- 1 pineapple, cut into 1 × 1 cm dice
- 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1 × 1 cm dice
- 1 courgette, shaved into long strips
- olive oil, sugar, coarsely ground pepper
- cognac, for flambéing the vegetables
- Risotto (red onion, garlic, fennel, arborio rice, wine, cream, Maldon salt, pepper)
- pine nuts, fennel flower, to garnish

**Method**
1. Combine the marinade ingredients; submerge the pollock fillets and marinate for 4–6 hours in the refrigerator.
2. Place the diced pineapple, pepper and courgette strips on a grill wok over the coals. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with sugar and coarse pepper.
3. Remove the pollock from the marinade, pat thoroughly dry, and season generously with Maldon salt.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest fillet from the side.
5. While the wok vegetables sizzle, grill the pollock fillets for 1½ minutes per side over high heat.
6. Remove the fish from the grill. Flambé the vegetable-fruit wok with cognac.
7. Mound risotto in small wet bowls and invert onto each plate; spoon the grilled vegetables and fruit around. Cross two pollock fillets on top of the risotto mound.
8. Garnish with toasted pine nuts and fennel flower.
9. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The long marinade penetrates the lean flesh; the very short 3-minute total grill time leaves the interior just cooked through.
10. Rest 2 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Method notes | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Great British Chefs | UK | Crust-topped, grill then oven 2–3 min | 60°C / 140°F |
| Chefkoch.de | Germany | White wine marinade, open grill 10–12 min | 60°C / 140°F |
| Köket.se | Sweden | Bacon-wrapped, medium grill with garlic-butter baste | 60°C / 140°F |
| Aperitif.no | Norway | Long-marinated, very hot grill 3 min total | 60°C / 140°F |

All four sources land on 60°C as the pull point. Grilling a lean white fish like pollock requires care to avoid drying: crust, marinade, or fat-wrapping all serve the same purpose — slowing surface moisture loss so the interior reaches safe doneness without the exterior becoming hard. The consistent 60°C recommendation reflects the minimum fully-opaque state for a lean gadoid.

## What makes this method special

Grilling gives pollock a charred exterior that contrasts with its mild, slightly sweet flesh. Because pollock has more flavour than cod, it can carry strong grill accompaniments — bacon, wild garlic, fennel, tropical fruit — without being overwhelmed. The key technical constraint is the fish's leanness: unlike salmon, pollock has no subcutaneous fat to self-baste, so protective coatings (crust, marinade, cured meat wrap) are essential for every tradition that grills it successfully.
