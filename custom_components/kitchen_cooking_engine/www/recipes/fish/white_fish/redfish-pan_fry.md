# Redfish × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Redfish (*Sebastes norvegicus* / *Sebastes mentella*, Swedish: uer / rödfisk, German: Rotbarsch, Danish: rødfisk, Norwegian: uer) is a deep-water rockfish from the North Atlantic with pink-to-white moderately firm flesh, mild flavour, and a fat content of 3–5 g per 100 g — slightly richer than cod but still a lean white fish. Fillets are typically 10–20 mm thick and cook very quickly. Pan-frying is the classic everyday preparation across German, Austrian, Scandinavian, and Australian home cooking. The three most common approaches are: (1) breaded/crumbed — the German *Uromas Art* tradition where fillets are marinated in lemon, dried, then coated in flour, egg, and breadcrumbs before frying in butter-and-oil; (2) skin-on pan-fry — the Scandinavian approach where fillets are fried skin-side down first in oil, then basted in butter until the flesh turns opaque from below; (3) lightly flour-dusted crispy fry — the Australian/general technique using a heavy pan with oil, no egg or crumb, relying on high-even heat and a thick pan for a uniformly golden crust. All three approaches share the same internal target: 60°C at the thickest cross-section. Probe placement: insert into the thickest cross-section of the fillet, from the thicker end, running parallel to the skin.

## Styckesprofil

Uer (*Sebastes norvegicus* / *S. mentella*; tyska: Rotbarsch; danska: rødfisk; norska: uer) är en djuphavsfisk med rosa till vitt, måttligt fast och milt smaksatt kött (3–5 g fett/100 g). Filén är tunn (10–20 mm) och tillagas snabbt. Pannstekning är klassisk vardagsberedning i tyska, österrikiska, skandinaviska och australiska hem. Tre vanliga tekniker: (1) panerad — tyska *Uromas Art*-traditionen med marinad i citron, torkning och panering i mjöl-ägg-ströbröd; (2) hudstekt — skandinaviskt, steks hudsidan ned i olja, sedan penslas med smör tills köttet blivit ogenomskinligt underifrån; (3) lätt mjölad krispig stekning — australisk teknik i het panna utan ägg eller ströbröd. Alla tre tekniker delar samma inre mål: 60°C vid tjockaste tvärsnittet. Sond placeras från tjockaste ändan, parallellt med huden.

## Source recipes

### 1. Gebratenes Rotbarschfilet nach Uromas Art — Chefkoch.de
**Attribution**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3744901569014609/Gebratenes-Rotbarschfilet-nach-Uromas-Art.html
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 30 min rest · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: none (serve immediately)

**Ingredients**
- 4 redfish fillets, approx. 250 g each
- Fresh lemon juice
- Sea salt
- Plain flour (Mehl)
- Fine breadcrumbs (Paniermehl)
- 1 egg, size M
- 30 g butter
- 2 tbsp rapeseed oil

**Method**
1. Drizzle the redfish fillets with lemon juice; leave for approximately 30 minutes, then pat dry with kitchen paper and rub with sea salt.
2. Set out three shallow bowls: flour, beaten egg, and breadcrumbs. Coat the fillets first in flour, then draw through the egg, and finally roll in breadcrumbs.
3. Heat butter and rapeseed oil together in a pan over medium heat.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest cross-section of a fillet from the thick end, running parallel to the skin.
5. Fry the coated fillets for 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown.
6. Serve immediately.
7. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** Four to five minutes per side in a butter-oil mixture brings thin redfish fillets through safely. The breadcrumb coating retains heat, so pulling at 60°C avoids the dry, rubbery result of overcooking.
8. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 2. Gebratenes Rotbarschfilet — ichkoche.at
**Attribution**: https://www.ichkoche.at/gebratenes-rotbarschfilet-rezept-186735
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 30 min rest · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: none

**Ingredients**
- 750 g redfish fillets (Rotbarschfilets)
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- Salt and pepper
- 1 egg
- 2 tbsp cold water
- 1 tbsp plain flour
- 75 g fine breadcrumbs (Semmelmehl)
- 80 g butter (Leichtbutter)

**Method**
1. Rinse the redfish fillets under cold running water; pat dry and cut into portion pieces.
2. Drizzle with lemon juice; leave to stand for approximately 30 minutes, then pat dry again and season with salt and pepper.
3. Beat the egg with the cold water. Dip the fillets first in flour, then in the beaten egg, and finally turn in breadcrumbs.
4. Heat butter in a pan over medium heat.
5. Insert the probe into the thickest cross-section of a fillet from the thicker edge.
6. Fry the crumbed fillets for 10–12 minutes in total, turning once, until golden brown on both sides.
7. Serve with potato salad as a side.
8. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The Austrian version uses a thick crumb coating with slightly longer frying time (10–12 min total); 60°C pull confirms the interior is done without the double crumb layer hiding the true state of the flesh.
9. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 3. Skindstegt Rødfisk — Madensverden.dk
**Attribution**: https://madensverden.dk/skindstegt-roedfisk/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–7 min · **Rest**: none (serve immediately)

**Ingredients**
- 600 g redfish fillets (rødfisk), ideally skin-on
- 1 tbsp rapeseed oil (rapsolie)
- 2 tbsp butter (smør)
- Salt
- Freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Check that the redfish is perfectly fresh — it should smell of clean sea water. Purchase fillets if possible to simplify preparation; verify they are completely boneless and pat gently dry with kitchen paper.
2. Sprinkle both sides with a little salt. Score the skin with a sharp knife (1–2 cuts per fillet) to prevent curling.
3. Heat 1 tbsp rapeseed oil in a non-stick frying pan over high heat.
4. Lay the fillets skin-side down in the pan. Insert the probe from the thicker end, running parallel to the skin.
5. Fry without moving for 3 minutes until the skin is golden and crisp.
6. Reduce heat slightly; add 2 tbsp butter.
7. Continue frying for a further 3–4 minutes, spooning butter over the flesh periodically, until the top surface of the fillet begins to turn white and opaque from below.
8. Turn the fillets and cook for only 10 seconds on the flesh side to close the surface.
9. Remove from the pan immediately and serve skin-side up so the crispy skin stays crisp.
10. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** The Danish skin-fried technique is designed to cook through from below, confirmed by the white opaque zone rising from the skin. The probe tells you exactly when the centre hits 60°C — removing any guesswork from the 10-second flip method.
11. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 4. Crispy Pan Fried Fish — RecipeTin Eats
**Attribution**: https://www.recipetineats.com/crispy-pan-fried-fish/
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: none

**Ingredients**
- 4 fish fillets, 130–200 g each (any firm white fish; redfish / ocean perch excellent)
- Salt and pepper
- 2–3 tbsp plain flour
- 2–3 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable or light olive)
- Lemon wedges to serve

**Method**
1. Pat the fish fillets dry with paper towels — this is the single most important step for crispiness.
2. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. Place flour on a plate; lightly coat the fillets, then shake off all excess flour. A very thin, even coating is the key — too thick becomes gummy, too thin doesn't crisp.
4. Heat the pan dry first over medium-high heat until you can see faint wisps of smoke rising.
5. Add a thin layer of oil; swirl to coat the base, then immediately place the fillets in the pan.
6. Insert the probe into the thickest cross-section from the thicker end, parallel to the skin.
7. Leave the fillets completely undisturbed for 2–3 minutes. Do not move or press them. The fish will release naturally from the pan once the base is properly golden and crispy.
8. Flip carefully once; cook the second side for 1–2 minutes.
9. Remove from pan; serve immediately with lemon wedges.
10. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F.** At medium-high heat in a heavy pan, thin redfish fillets reach 60°C in approximately 2–3 minutes per side. The probe prevents the common mistake of pressing or moving the fish to check doneness, which destroys the crust.
11. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Method notes | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Chefkoch.de | Germany | Breaded (flour–egg–crumb), butter + oil, 4–5 min per side | 60°C / 140°F |
| ichkoche.at | Austria | Double-breaded (flour–egg–crumb), butter, 10–12 min total | 60°C / 140°F |
| Madensverden.dk | Denmark | Skin-on, oil then butter-baste, 3 min skin + 3–4 min baste + 10 sec flip | 60°C / 140°F |
| RecipeTin Eats | Australia | Flour-dusted only, heavy pan, 2–3 min per side | 60°C / 140°F |

All four sources converge on 60°C pull. The different breading/coating techniques across German, Austrian, Danish, and Australian traditions show distinctly different surface textures and flavour profiles, but all reach the same internal target. Time variation (4–12 min total) reflects coating thickness and starting temperature rather than any difference in doneness target.

## What makes this method special

Pan-frying is the dominant everyday cooking method for redfish across Northern Europe and beyond, because the fish's moderate fat content (3–5 g/100 g) and firm-but-not-tough texture stand up well to high-heat frying without drying out as fast as leaner fish. The flavour is mild enough that it takes background flavours (butter nuttiness, breadcrumb crunch, lemon brightness) very readily, making it versatile across cooking traditions. The three-style variation — breaded German/Austrian, Scandinavian skin-fry, and Australian flour-only — illustrates how different national traditions have developed distinct textural results from the same underlying technique. The MEATER probe is particularly useful for breaded fish because the coating hides the true state of the flesh; experienced cooks still overcook breaded redfish regularly by judging only the crumb colour.

Pan-frying is the unambiguous primary cooking method for redfish in its home waters of the North Atlantic. The German/Austrian breaded tradition, the Danish skin-fry, and the Australian crispy-fry all target the same internal temperature of 60°C and all finish in under 12 minutes.
