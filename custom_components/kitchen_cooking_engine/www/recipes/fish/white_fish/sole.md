# Sole — Cut Overview

## Cut profile

European sole (*Solea solea*, Swedish: sjötunga / tunga, German: Seezunge, French: sole commune, Dutch: tong) is a flatfish of the family Soleidae, found in the Eastern Atlantic and Mediterranean. It is also known as Dover sole (the UK designation for *Solea solea*) and is one of the most celebrated and expensive fish in European fine dining. The flesh is very white, extremely delicate, mildly sweet, with a low fat content (1–2 g per 100 g) and a fine, slightly firmer texture than flounder or plaice. Sole is sold whole (200–500 g) or as very thin fillets; the dark upper skin and white lower skin are traditionally removed before cooking whole fish. Pan-frying whole in clarified butter and lemon (*sole meunière* / *Seezunge Müllerin Art*) is the most famous preparation — a cornerstone of French and classical European cuisine. Grilling whole on a very hot grill with butter and herbs is traditional in Normandy and Belgium. Oven-baking with butter and white wine (*sole bonne femme*) is another classic French method. Unlike many flatfish, sole is not commonly smoked due to its extreme delicacy and expense. Probe placement: insert from the thickest edge into the upper fillet, clear of the central spine.

## Styckesprofil

Sjötunga (*Solea solea*; tyska: Seezunge; franska: sole; holländska: tong) är en flatfisk i familjen Soleidae och en av de mest hyllade och dyra matfiskarna i europeisk finmatlagning ("Dover sole"). Köttet är kritvitt, ytterst delikat, milt sött och magert (1–2 g/100 g). Pannstekning hel i klarat smör med citron (*sole meunière*) är den mest kända beredningen — en hörnsten i det franska köket. Grillning hel på het grill med smör och örter är tradition i Normandiet och Belgien. Ugnsbakad med smör och vitt vin (*sole bonne femme*) är ytterligare en klassisk fransk metod. Sond placeras från tjockaste kanten in i överfilén, fri från mittenbenet.
