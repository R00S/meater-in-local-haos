# Trout × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Hot-smoked trout (*rökt forell* / *räucherforelle* / *røget ørred* / *savustettu forelli*) is one of the oldest and most widely practised uses of the compact table-top hot smoker across Northern Europe. In Scandinavia, the **ABU-röken** / **röklåda** (Swedish), **savustuslaatikko** / **savustuspönttö** (Finnish), **røgekasse** (Danish), and **røykeboks** (Norwegian) are all the same device: a lidded metal box with a sawdust tray, a wire rack above, placed over a spirit burner, gas burner, campfire, or kitchen hob. In Germany, the **Tischräucherofen** (table smoker) is the direct equivalent. In North America, the **Camerons Stovetop Smoker** fulfils the same role. All these devices operate at 80–100 °C chamber temperature and produce hot-smoked fish in 15–45 minutes.

Rökt forell is a strong Nordic tradition — arguably more classically Scandinavian than even smoked salmon — documented since the nineteenth century in Swedish, Finnish, and German freshwater fishing and outdoor cooking literature. Rainbow trout (*Oncorhynchus mykiss*, Swedish: *regnbågsforell*, Finnish: *kirjolohi*) and brown trout (*Salmo trutta*, Swedish/Norwegian: *forell/ørret*, German: *Bachforelle*) are both excellent; Arctic char (*röding*, Finnish: *nieriä*) is closely related and can be substituted throughout. Brown trout from rivers and rainbow trout from lakes typically weigh 200–500 g whole (gutted), which fits the röklåda perfectly — one or two fish per batch.

Whole gutted trout is the classic format. The fish may be brined (12% salt brine for 12 h in the German tradition, or a quick 30-minute dry salt in the Scandinavian tradition), or simply salted. The fish is done when the dorsal fin pulls free effortlessly and the flesh along the backbone is fully opaque — corresponding to internal **63 °C / 145 °F** (USDA minimum for fish). The ABU-röken / Tischräucherofen reaches this temperature in approximately 15–25 minutes for a 200–300 g fish; larger fish (400–500 g) take 25–40 minutes. German tradition extends brine time to 12 hours for more thorough cure and even moisture distribution.

For MEATER probe placement in a whole trout: insert the probe along the dorsal muscle, parallel to the spine, with the tip at the thickest cross-section (behind the shoulder, just forward of the dorsal fin). The probe must clear the backbone.

## Styckesprofil

Varmrökt forell är en av de starkaste och mest utövade traditionerna med den kompakta bordsröklådan — känd som **ABU-röken** / **röklåda** på svenska, **savustuslaatikko** / **savustuspönttö** på finska, **røgekasse** på danska, **røykeboks** på norska och **Tischräucherofen** på tyska. Alla dessa är samma redskap: ett slutet metallhölje med alspånsbricka, gallerinsats och spritbrännare eller gasbrännare. Rökt forell — regnbågsforell (*Oncorhynchus mykiss*) eller brun öring (*Salmo trutta*) — är en klassisk skandinavisk, finsk och tysk tradition. Hel urtagen fisk (200–500 g) passar perfekt i röklådan. Fisken är klar när ryggfenan lätt kan dras ut och ryggmuskelköttet är helt ogenomskinligt — innetemperatur ca **63 °C**. Sond insatt längs ryggmuskeln, parallellt med ryggraden, med spetsen i tjockaste tvärsnitt bakom skuldran.

## Source recipes

### 1. Heiß geräucherte Forellen aus dem Tischräucherofen — German table smoker (inspired by Chefkoch.de)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de — https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3450651514232447/Heiss-geraeucherte-Forellen-aus-dem-Tischraeucherofen.html (fetched 2026-05-31). German Tischräucherofen (table smoker — direct equivalent of ABU-röken). 6 whole trout, long brine for even cure, juniper aromatics.
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min + 12 h brine · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Räuchervorgang (smoking phase) begins when **chamber temperature reaches at least 60 °C** and lasts **15–20 minutes**; chamber temperature **must not exceed 90 °C** (throttle burners or briefly lift lid if needed). Doneness check: **dorsal fin pulls free effortlessly**. At visual doneness, internal temperature is approximately **63–68 °C / 145–154 °F**.

**Ingredients**
- 6 whole trout (Forellen), gutted and well-cleaned, gills removed
- **Brine**: 2 litres water per kg gutted fish + 120 g salt per kg fish
- 2 tsp Senfkörner (mustard seeds)
- 1 tsp Salbei (sage)
- 3 Lorbeerblätter (bay leaves)
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 10 Wacholderbeeren (juniper berries) for the smoker
- Räuchermehl (fine smoking sawdust — beech or alder)

**Method**
1. Prepare the brine: dissolve salt in water (use boiling water, then cool). Add mustard seeds, sage, bay leaves, and black pepper.
2. Submerge gutted trout completely in the cooled brine. Refrigerate for **12 hours** (overnight). This gives even, deep cure and is especially suited to fish that was previously frozen.
3. Remove fish from brine. Rinse under cold water. Pat dry and let air-dry on a rack until the skin feels slightly papery and leathery — the pellicle that promotes smoke adhesion.
4. Line the smoker base with two sheets of foil (for easy cleaning). Wrap the drip tray in foil. Fill the sawdust recess with **2–3 tablespoons of Räuchermehl** and add approximately **10 juniper berries**.
5. Load the racks with fish, ensuring fish do not touch each other.
6. **Insert MEATER probe** along the dorsal muscle of the largest fish, parallel to the spine, tip at the thickest cross-section.
7. Light the spirit burners (Spiritusbrenner) according to the smoker's instructions.
8. Smoke at **60–90 °C chamber temperature** for **15–20 minutes**. If chamber temperature approaches 90 °C, briefly lift the lid or partially throttle the burners.
9. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F** internal. At that point the dorsal fin pulls free easily and all flesh along the backbone is completely opaque.
10. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

**Serving**: Serve warm — räucherforelle are typically eaten immediately, still warm, with fresh bread, butter, and horseradish or a squeeze of lemon.

### 2. Savustettu lohi ja forelli — Finnish savustuspönttö over campfire (inspired by Kotikokki.net)
**Inspired by**: Kotikokki.net — https://www.kotikokki.net/reseptit/nayta/18610/Savustettu%20lohi/ (fetched 2026-05-31). Finnish campfire savustuspönttö (savustuslaatikko); recipe covers salmon and trout (*lohi ja forelli*) interchangeably. Alder chips, sugar, juniper.
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–45 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoke for **30–45 minutes** from when the first wisps of smoke appear, keeping fire moderate and steady. No internal temperature stated; at 30–45 min over a maintained campfire in a savustuspönttö, a 300–500 g whole trout reaches internal **63–68 °C**.

**Ingredients**
- 2–3 whole trout (forell/forelli), 300–500 g each, gutted and cleaned
- 2–3 tsp merisuola (coarse sea salt) per fish
- 1–2 tsp valkopippuri (white pepper)
- 1 pussi savustuspuruja (alder sawdust chips)
- 3–4 sokeripalaa (sugar cubes)
- 5–7 katajanmarjoja tai katajanoksia (juniper berries or short juniper sprigs, optional)

**Method**
1. Rub each fish inside the cavity and over the skin with coarse salt and white pepper. Wrap loosely in baking paper and rest 15–30 minutes.
2. Line the base of the savustuspönttö with foil. Spread a 1–2 cm even layer of alder chips. Add sugar cubes and juniper (if using).
3. Arrange trout on the rack with space between them. Close the lid.
4. **Insert MEATER probe** along the dorsal muscle of the largest fish, parallel to the spine, tip at the thickest cross-section.
5. Place the savustuspönttö over an open fire. Maintain a steady moderate flame; add small logs periodically to keep heat constant.
6. Smoke for **30–45 minutes** after first smoke appears.
7. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. Dorsal fin should pull free effortlessly; flesh along backbone is fully opaque.
8. Serve warm with boiled potatoes and lemon.

### 3. Hurtig røgning af ørred — Danish quick røgeovn method (inspired by Minmadopskrift.dk)
**Inspired by**: Minmadopskrift.dk — https://minmadopskrift.dk/fisk-og-skaldyr/hurtig-roegning-af-laks-og-oerred.html/ (fetched 2026-05-31). Danish røgeovn (smoking oven / røgekasse). Explicitly covers *ørred* (trout) alongside laks. Honey-mustard-seed glaze, dry-salt, short smoking at 60–70 °C.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min + 2 h dry-salt · **Cook**: ~1 h (full røgeovn) or 20–30 min (compact box) · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Preheat røgeovn to 100 °C for 10 minutes, then reduce to **60–70 °C**. Apply honey, mustard seeds, and spices. Smoke approximately **1 hour at 60–70 °C** (full-size oven); for a compact ABU-röken equivalent, scale to **20–30 minutes at 80–90 °C chamber**. The source cautions against exceeding 80–100 °C as it dries the fish. No internal temperature given; at 60–70 °C for 1 hour a 300–500 g fillet reaches approximately 63 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 2–4 whole trout or trout fillets (ørred or ørredfileter), skin-on
- 1 tsp salt + a little black pepper (for dry-salt)
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tbsp sennepsfrø (mustard seeds)
- 1 tbsp paprika or preferred spice mix
- Røgsmuld or flis (smoking chips/dust — beech or alder)

**Method**
1. Dry-brine: pat fish dry and dust with equal parts salt and sugar over all surfaces. Refrigerate 2 hours. The fish will release liquid — keep it in a deep dish.
2. Remove fish, rinse under cold water, and dry thoroughly with kitchen paper. Leave on a rack to air-dry 30–60 minutes until surface is dry and slightly tacky.
3. Preheat the røgeovn to 100 °C × 10 minutes to burn off bitterness. Then reduce to 60–70 °C.
4. Brush the fish with honey. Season with mustard seeds and spices.
5. Place fish on the topmost rack in the røgeovn. Apply røgsmuld/flis. Maintain 60–70 °C (ABU-röken equivalent: 80–90 °C × 20–30 min).
6. **Insert MEATER probe** along the dorsal muscle.
7. Smoke for approximately **1 hour at 60–70 °C** (or 20–30 min in a compact smoker box at 80–90 °C).
8. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F** internal. Dorsal fin pulls free; flesh is opaque throughout.

### 4. Smoked Fish Fillets — North American Camerons Stovetop Smoker (inspired by Camerons Products)
**Inspired by**: Camerons Products — https://cameronsproducts.com/fish-fillets/ (fetched 2026-05-31). The Camerons Stovetop Smoker is the North American direct equivalent of the ABU-röken: a stainless lidded box with sawdust tray, wire rack, and drip tray, placed on a kitchen stovetop. This recipe covers any mild white fish fillet, including trout.
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min per pound · **Rest**: 3 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoke over **medium heat** for **15–20 minutes per 450 g (1 lb)**. For a 300–400 g trout fillet, this corresponds to approximately **10–18 minutes**. No internal temperature stated; at visual doneness (opaque, flakes easily, subtle earthy smoke aroma) the internal is approximately **63–66 °C / 145–151 °F**.

**Ingredients**
- 300–400 g trout fillets (or any mild white fish fillet), rinsed and patted dry
- Fresh lemon, halved
- Black pepper, freshly ground
- Dried dill
- 1 tbsp alder wood chips

**Method**
1. Rinse and pat the trout fillets dry.
2. Load 1 tablespoon of alder chips into the base of the Camerons smoker. Slot in drip tray and rack.
3. Lay fillets on the rack. Squeeze fresh lemon over the flesh; season with pepper and dill.
4. **Insert MEATER probe** horizontally into the thickest fillet from the side.
5. Close the Camerons lid. Place over **medium stovetop heat**.
6. Smoke for **15–20 minutes per 450 g**.
7. **Pull at 63 °C / 145 °F**. "The fish will have a subtle earthy flavour" — quote from source.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with lemon.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Device | Chamber temp | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Chefkoch.de / Tischräucherofen | DE | Table smoker | 60–90 °C | 63–68 °C (145–154 °F) visual |
| Kotikokki.net / savustuspönttö | FI | Campfire box | campfire ~90–110 °C | ~63–68 °C at 30–45 min |
| Minmadopskrift.dk / røgeovn | DK | Røgeovn | 60–70 °C | ~63 °C at 1 h |
| Camerons Products | NA | Stovetop smoker | medium burner | ~63–66 °C at 15–20 min/450 g |

**Consensus pull range**: **63–68 °C (145–154 °F)** for fully cooked hot-smoked trout. All sources use visual doneness (dorsal fin pulls free; flesh opaque throughout) as the primary cue; MEATER probe at 63 °C replicates this reliably. Hot smoking is a full-doneness method — no rare option.
