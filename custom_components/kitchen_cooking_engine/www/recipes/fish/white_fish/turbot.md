# Turbot — Cut Overview

## Cut profile

Turbot (*Scophthalmus maximus*, Swedish: piggvar, German: Steinbutt, French: turbot, Dutch: tarbot, Norwegian: piggvar) is a large, diamond-shaped flatfish of the family Scophthalmidae and one of the most prized and expensive food fish in Europe. It is found in the North Sea, Baltic Sea, and North Atlantic and is both wild-caught and farmed (mainly in Spain, France, and Norway). The flesh is brilliant white, very firm, lean (1–2 g fat per 100 g), and richly flavoured — considered by many chefs to be the finest European flatfish. Turbot is sold whole (2–8 kg) or as thick portioned fillets; the thick, gelatinous skin and bones are rich in collagen and add flavour when the fish is cooked on the bone. Pan-searing thick turbot fillets or steaks in butter until golden is the classic restaurant preparation (*turbot meunière*). Oven-roasting whole or portioned on the bone in butter is traditional in French and British fine dining. Grilling over charcoal is popular in Spain (*rodaballo a la parrilla*). Sous vide at 52–54 °C produces luxurious, silky results. Probe placement: insert from the thickest edge of the fillet or steak into the centre, avoiding the central bone on whole fish.

## Styckesprofil

Piggvar (*Scophthalmus maximus*; tyska: Steinbutt; franska: turbot; norska: piggvar) är en stor, diamantformad plattfisk och ett av Europas dyraste och mest uppskattade matfiskar. Köttet är kritvitt, mycket fast, magert (1–2 g fett/100 g) och rikt smaksatt — anses av många kockar vara den finaste europeiska plattfisken. Pannstekning av tjock filé eller biff i smör (*turbot meunière*) är klassisk restaurangberedning. Ugnsstekt hel eller portionerad på benet med smör är tradition i fransk och brittisk finmatlagning. Sous vide vid 52–54 °C ger lyxigt silkig textur. Sond placeras från tjockaste kanten av filén in till mitten, undviker mittenbenet på hel fisk.
