# Whitefish × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Whitefish is a lean to moderately lean salmonid with delicate flakes and a mild, sweet flavour. Oven baking suits it because the flesh is fragile enough to benefit from even, surrounding heat, especially when baked whole or protected by sauce, pesto, or wine. Compared with pan frying, the oven is gentler and reduces the risk of the thin outer layers drying out before the centre is done. Carryover is small on fillets and moderate on whole fish, especially when baked in a hot oven.

## Styckesprofil

Sik är en mager till måttligt mager laxfisk med fina flagor och mild, söt smak. Ugnsbakning passar därför väl eftersom köttet är tillräckligt ömtåligt för att vinna på jämn omgivande värme, särskilt när fisken bakas hel eller skyddas av sås, pesto eller vin. Jämfört med pannstekning är ugnen skonsammare och minskar risken att de tunna ytterlagren torkar innan mitten är klar. Eftervärmen är liten i filéer men måttlig i hel fisk, särskilt vid varm ugn.

## Source recipes

### 1. Pestouunisiika — Soppa365.fi
**Inspired by**: https://www.soppa365.fi/reseptit/kala-arjen-nopeat/pestouunisiika
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole whitefish, about 800 g
- 1 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 lemon
- 2 tbsp breadcrumbs
- 1 pot basil
- 1/2 pack rocket
- 1 dl peeled almonds
- 1 dl parmesan pieces
- 1 1/2 dl olive oil
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat the oven to 200°C.
2. Scale the whitefish, remove the gills and cut a few slashes in the back. Rub salt inside the fish and over the back.
3. Halve the lemon; slice one half and reserve the other for serving.
4. Blend the basil, rocket, almonds, parmesan, olive oil, salt and pepper into a pesto.
5. Stuff the fish with a couple of spoonfuls of pesto and the lemon slices; scatter the breadcrumbs over the top.
6. Insert the probe behind the head into the thickest part of the flesh.
7. Bake for about 30 minutes.
8. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** (142°F). A whole 800 g whitefish baked at 200°C for 30 minutes is intended to finish flaky and fully cooked with a little carryover still to come.
9. Rest 3 min. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 2. Helstekt sik — Köket.se
**Inspired by**: https://www.koket.se/helstekt-sik
**Serves**: not stated · **Prep**: not stated · **Cook**: 25-30 min + browning · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whitefish, about 2 kg
- salt
- 1 egg yolk, beaten
- 0,5 krm cayenne pepper
- 2 tbsp breadcrumbs
- 1 dl dry white wine
- 2 dl dry white wine
- 1 dl fish stock
- 2 dl whipping cream
- tarragon
- 500 g broccoli florets
- 2 dl grated cheese
- sliced lemon

**Method**
1. Heat the oven to 275°C.
2. Salt the fish inside and out, lay it in a greased roasting pan, brush with beaten egg yolk, dust with cayenne and sift over the breadcrumbs.
3. Roast until the surface takes on colour.
4. Lower the oven to 225°C, pour over the wine and continue roasting for 25-30 minutes.
5. Insert the probe behind the shoulder into the thickest flesh before the second stage begins.
6. Remove the fish, make the sauce from the pan juices with more wine, fish stock, cream and tarragon, and serve with broccoli gratin and potatoes.
7. **Pull at 61°C / 142°F** (142°F). The hot-to-moderate two-stage roast is designed for a large whole fish that will carry over into a fully flaky finish.
8. Rest 3 min. Final serving temperature: **63°C / 145°F** (well_done).

### 3. Felchenfilet nach Konstanzer Art — ichkoche.at
**Inspired by**: https://www.ichkoche.at/felchenfilet-nach-konstanzer-art-rezept-115802
**Serves**: 1 · **Prep**: not stated · **Cook**: 10-15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 400 g felchen fillets
- 1 onion
- 1 can mushrooms
- salt
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- parsley
- 125 ml dry white wine
- 1 egg yolk
- 125 ml cream
- 1 tbsp butter

**Method**
1. Put a little butter into an ovenproof dish and sweat the finely chopped onion lightly.
2. Lay in the felchen fillets and top with the mushrooms.
3. Season with salt, lemon juice and white wine.
4. Cover with aluminium foil.
5. Insert the probe into the thickest fillet before covering.
6. Steam-bake at 200°C for about 10-15 minutes.
7. Use the fish juices to make a pale sauce bound with the egg yolk and cream, then pour it over the baked fillets.
8. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F** (140°F). The short covered bake in wine is a moist fillet treatment that finishes just before the fully safe zone, then rises slightly during service.
9. Rest 2 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

### 4. Baked Whitefish Recipe — Lake Michigan Angler
**Inspired by**: https://lakemichiganangler.com/pages/baked-whitefish
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: not stated · **Cook**: 15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whitefish fillets, about 2 lb total
- 6 tbsp butter
- 1 tsp parsley flakes

**Method**
1. Remove the skin from the whitefish fillets, wash them in cold water and dry them with paper towels.
2. Coat the bottom of the baking pan with 1 tablespoon of the butter.
3. Place the fillets skin-side down in the dish and dot the tops with thin pads of the remaining butter.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest part of one fillet from the side.
5. Bake at 375°F / 190°C for 15 minutes or until the fillets flake.
6. Sprinkle with parsley flakes before serving.
7. **Pull at 60°C / 140°F** (140°F). The source cue is “until the fillets flake,” which for whitefish corresponds to a low-60s finish after a brief rest.
8. Rest 2 min. Final serving temperature: **62°C / 144°F** (well_done).

The observed pull range is **60-61°C**. Whole-fish Scandinavian sources and fillet-based Central European/North American sources all converge closely, with just enough margin for carryover to bring the fish to a safe, flaky finish. That makes `well_done` the best common coding choice, even though the result should still be moist and delicate rather than overcooked.

## What makes this method special

Oven baking is especially useful for whitefish because it protects the fish’s delicate flakes from aggressive turning and pan contact. Sauce, pesto, butter or wine create a humid micro-environment that slows moisture loss in this relatively lean salmonid. The method also scales well from small fillets to large whole fish, unlike pan frying which becomes awkward with larger specimens. In short, the oven is the most forgiving way to cook whitefish while preserving its mild sweetness.
