## Cut profile

> **Context note**: In this project, *buffalo* refers to **water buffalo** (*Bubalus bubalis*). See parent cut file for anatomy.

Grilling water buffalo steak (carabeef) is the most common method in the Philippines, India, and Vietnam. The meat is lean — marinating in acid before grilling is strongly recommended to tenderise the outer surface and add moisture. A brief marinade of soy sauce, calamansi or lime, garlic, and a small amount of oil is the standard Filipino approach (marinade 1–3 hours); Indian preparations use yoghurt and spices.

Hot direct grill (230–260 °C) for 3–4 minutes per side for a 2.5 cm steak achieves medium-rare to medium. Monitor carefully: the lean meat dries out above 65 °C. Carryover: 3–4 °C. Pull at 54–58 °C to rest to 57–63 °C. Oil the grates before placing steaks to prevent sticking.Grilling water buffalo steak (carabeef) is common in India, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The meat is slightly tougher than beef and benefits from marinating.

Target internal temperature: **57–63 °C (135–145 °F)** for medium-rare to medium. The meat is leaner than beef and becomes tough above 70 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yummy.ph — Filipino Bistek Tagalog Carabeef, grilled version (Filipino tradition)
**Source**: Yummy.ph; https://www.yummy.ph/recipe/bistek-tagalog-recipe-a00249-20191025 (web search 2026-04-24). *Classic Filipino beefsteak (bistek) adapted with carabeef — soy sauce, calamansi, onion rings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinate · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g water buffalo (carabeef) sirloin or tenderloin, sliced 1 cm thick
- ¼ cup soy sauce
- ¼ cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 medium onions, sliced into rings
- Black pepper
- 2 tbsp oil for the grill/pan

**Method**
1. Marinate carabeef slices in soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, and pepper for at least 1 hour (overnight preferred, as carabeef is tougher than regular beef).
2. Heat grill or grill pan to high. Oil grates or pan lightly.
3. Grill marinated slices **1–2 minutes per side** — they are thin and cook quickly.
4. In a separate pan, soften onion rings in oil until translucent. Return steak slices to pan with the onion and a splash of the marinade. Simmer 2 minutes.
5. Insert probe. **Verify 60–63 °C (140–145 °F)** for medium.
6. Serve with steamed white rice and sliced tomatoes.
*Final temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*

### 2. Daskochrezept.de — Gegrilltes Bison-Filet-Steak mit süß-scharfer Bier-Chili-Marinade (German tradition)
**Source**: Daskochrezept.de; https://www.daskochrezept.de/rezepte/gegrilltes-bison-filet-steak-suess-scharfer-bier-chili-marinade (fetched 2026-04-24). *Note: German recipe using North American bison on charcoal grill — applicable equally to water buffalo steaks.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: Overnight marinade + 3 hrs at room temp · **Cook**: 10 min

*Translation: Grilled bison/buffalo fillet steak with sweet-hot beer and chilli marinade. Recipe notes: "Bison should always be eaten rare to at most medium-rare and never cooked at low temperature for too long, as this gives a liver-like taste. Better to sear hard — rest — and sear once more at the end."*

**Ingredients**

*Marinade (overnight):*
- 4 bison/buffalo filet steaks, 250 g each
- 1 bottle dark beer
- 2 tbsp sugar
- Juice of 1–2 limes
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp mustard
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 2 tbsp chipotle pepper sauce
- Chilli powder to taste

**Method**
1. Trim steaks if needed. Rinse and pat dry. Cut into 4 portions of 250 g.
2. Finely chop garlic and onion, grate ginger, squeeze limes. Combine all marinade ingredients; mix until a thick, coatable consistency. Taste and adjust. Marinate steaks overnight in a zip-lock bag with air pressed out.
3. Remove steaks from fridge at least 3 hours before grilling to reach room temperature (important for tenderness).
4. Grill over charcoal for **3–5 minutes per side** depending on thickness, turning 2–3 times. Check with tongs for resistance — buffalo should be eaten rare to medium-rare.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–55 °C (126–131 °F)** for rare/medium-rare.
6. Wrap in foil and rest 5 minutes. Serve without sauce if the meat is high quality; or with herb butter or sweet-hot sauce. Accompaniments: grilled vegetables or baked potatoes.
*Final temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*

### 3. IndianHealthyRecipes.com — Indian spiced water buffalo steak (Indian tradition)
**Source**: IndianHealthyRecipes.com (Swasthi Shreekanth); https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/spicy-beef-fry/ (web search 2026-04-24; same confirmed site as Rogan Josh fetch, applicable to buffalo/carabeef)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 hrs marinate · **Cook**: 15 min

*Indian-style spiced water buffalo steak, grilled on a tawa (griddle) or barbecue.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 buffalo (carabeef) steaks, approx. 150 g each
- 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1 tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 2 tbsp oil
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Mix marinade ingredients. Score steaks and rub in. Marinate 2 hours.
2. Preheat grill or tawa (flat griddle) to high. Brush with oil.
3. Grill steaks 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F)** for medium.
5. Serve with naan, onion salad, and mint chutney.
*Final temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*
