## Cut profile

Grilling goat chops over direct heat is the traditional preparation across West Africa, the Caribbean, and the Middle East. The lean meat and modest fat content mean grill time must be controlled carefully — goat chops dry out faster than lamb chops at the same temperature. Marinading (24 h in citrus, garlic, and spices) tenderises the lean muscle and adds moisture retention.

Direct medium-high heat: 3–4 minutes per side for 2.5 cm chops. MEATER probe inserted from the bone end into the thickest part of the eye muscle (*longissimus dorsi*), avoiding the bone. Target: 68–72 °C (medium-well) — the conventional serving range for goat chops. Carryover: 3–4 °C from a hot grate. Let rest 3 min before serving.

Target internal temperature: **70–74 °C (158–165 °F)**.

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Pulled Goat Kebabs with Coriander Dressing (British/Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/pulled-goat-kebabs-coriander-dressing (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min + overnight · **Cook**: 3 hrs

*A slow-cooked and then grilled goat preparation — shoulder is slow-roasted, then shredded and grilled/charred on kebab sticks.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg goat shoulder or chops
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp each: cumin, coriander, smoked paprika
- 1 tsp each: turmeric, cinnamon, chilli flakes
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 lemon, juice
- Salt and black pepper

*Coriander dressing:*
- Large bunch fresh coriander
- 2 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 4 tbsp olive oil

**Method**
1. Mix spices, oil, garlic, and lemon juice into a paste. Rub all over goat. Marinate overnight.
2. Slow-roast at 150 °C covered for 2.5–3 hours until falling off the bone.
3. Shred meat onto metal skewers. Grill or char over high heat 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert probe. **Verify 72–74 °C (162–165 °F)** before serving.
5. Blend coriander dressing ingredients until smooth.
6. Serve in flatbreads with dressing and pickled red onion.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 2. Caribbean Pot — Jamaican Jerk Goat Chops (Caribbean tradition)
**Source**: Caribbeanpot.com (Trinidad & Tobago home cooking); https://www.caribbeanpot.com/ (fetched 2026-04-24; jerk goat is flagship recipe)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hrs marinate · **Cook**: 25 min

*Jamaican jerk marinade: scotch bonnet, allspice, thyme, garlic.*

**Ingredients**
- 8 goat rib or loin chops
- 3 tbsp jerk seasoning (shop-bought or: 2 scotch bonnets, 3 garlic cloves, 1 tbsp allspice, 2 tsp thyme, 1 tsp cinnamon, 2 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp oil, blended)
- Salt

**Method**
1. Coat chops thoroughly with jerk seasoning. Marinate overnight in the fridge.
2. Bring to room temperature 30 minutes before cooking.
3. Preheat charcoal grill to medium-high. Grill chops 8–10 minutes per side.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 72–74 °C (162–165 °F)**.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with festival dumplings or rice and peas.
*Final temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*

### 3. African Bites — Grilled Goat (West African tradition, adapted from lamb chops recipe)
**Source**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/grilled-lamb-chops/ (fetched 2026-04-24 — applies equally to goat)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinate · **Cook**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 8 goat chops
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix marinade. Coat chops and marinate 1 hour.
2. Hot grill. Cook 5–8 minutes per side.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 72 °C (162 °F)** for medium-well.
4. Serve with jollof rice or fried plantain.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*
