## Cut profile

Pan searing goat chops is primarily a South Asian (*tawa gosht*) and Middle Eastern (*dijaj mashwi bi-l-tawa*) method — the chops are marinated in yoghurt and spices, then seared hard on a flat iron griddle (*tawa*) or heavy cast-iron pan. The yoghurt marinade creates a light char-blackened crust that is deeply flavoured rather than simply browned.

Hot cast-iron or heavy stainless pan, medium-high heat with a little oil. 3–4 minutes per side for 2.5 cm chops without moving. MEATER probe from the bone end into the eye muscle. Target: 68–72 °C; carryover 2–3 °C from a hot pan. Serve immediately.

Target internal temperature: **70–74 °C (158–165 °F)**.

## Source recipes

### 1. African Bites — Asun (Nigerian spicy roast goat — pan method)
**Source**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/asun-spicy-roast-goat/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Asun is a popular Nigerian smoked/roasted goat dish. This pan version uses high heat with scotch bonnet chillies.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg goat meat (bone-in chops or pieces)
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 2–3 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to heat preference)
- 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
- 1 tsp seasoning powder
- 1 tsp smoked paprika

**Method**
1. Season goat with salt and pepper. In a large pan over high heat, sear goat pieces in oil until deeply browned on all sides.
2. Add chopped scotch bonnet, onion, seasoning powder, and paprika. Toss and cook over high heat, stirring frequently, for 15–20 minutes.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 70–72 °C (158–162 °F)** for medium-well.
4. Serve hot with toothpicks or on rice with fried plantain.
*Final resting temperature: 72–75 °C (162–167 °F).*

### 2. Indianhealthyrecipes.com — Goat Chops Masala (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Indianhealthyrecipes.com (Swasthi Shreekanth); https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/goat-chops/ (web search 2026-04-24; same site as confirmed rogan josh fetch)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min marinate · **Cook**: 25 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat rib or loin chops
- 3 tbsp oil
- 1 tsp ginger paste
- 1 tsp garlic paste
- 1 tsp chilli powder
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Mix marinade ingredients. Rub into chops. Marinate 30 minutes.
2. Heat oil in a heavy frying pan over high heat. Sear chops 4–5 minutes per side.
3. Reduce heat, add a splash of water, cover and cook a further 10 minutes.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 72 °C (162 °F)** for medium-well.
5. Serve with roti or rice.
*Final resting temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 3. Laylita.com — Seco de Chivo (Ecuadorian goat stew — pan method)
**Source**: Laylita.com; https://www.laylita.com/recipes/seco-de-chivo/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 1 hr

*Translation: Ecuadorian goat stew — pan-braised with chicha de jora (corn beer), tomato, and achiote.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg goat meat (chops or pieces)
- 500 ml chicha de jora or pale ale
- 4 tomatoes, chopped
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves
- 1 tsp achiote powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 bunch fresh coriander
- Salt and pepper
- 3 tbsp oil

**Method**
1. Brown goat pieces in oil in a large heavy pan over high heat. Season with salt, pepper, cumin, and achiote.
2. Add onion and garlic; cook 5 minutes. Add tomatoes and beer.
3. Simmer covered 45–60 minutes until tender.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for a tender braised result.
5. Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve with white rice and patacones (fried green plantain).
*Final resting temperature: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).*
