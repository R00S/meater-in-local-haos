# Goat Shank × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting a goat shank (dry or semi-dry, uncovered or lightly tented) is used primarily in Middle Eastern, Mediterranean, and South Asian traditions where the shank is roasted whole at moderate temperature until the exterior is deeply coloured and the interior is tender. This is a hybrid method: the shank typically starts uncovered to brown, then liquid is added and it continues covered — making it closer to a braise. The distinction from the braised version is (1) starting dry and uncovered for colour development and (2) using less liquid than a full braise.

Oven at 180 °C (350 °F); initial uncovered roast 20–30 min for browning; add liquid, cover tightly or tent, continue 1.5–2 hours until the meat reaches 88–93 °C and sustains that for 45 min. MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest muscle, parallel to the bone.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Oven-Roasted Goat Shanks with Pomegranate (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012) — covered-roast variant; ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil
- 2 large onions, sliced
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g fresh ginger, sliced
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin + 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander + 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon
- 100 ml pomegranate molasses
- 200 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 2 cinnamon sticks
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley + 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season generously.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy roasting tin over medium-high heat. Sear shanks on all sides until deeply browned, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Add onions, garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander and cinnamon to the tin; toss with the rendered oil.
4. Whisk pomegranate molasses with chicken stock; pour into the tin. Add bay and cinnamon sticks.
5. Return shanks; cover tightly with foil. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Roast 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes; uncover for the final 30 minutes to brown.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides into the muscle without resistance.
8. Rest 15 minutes uncovered in the pan juices. Strew with parsley and pomegranate seeds. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (deeply spiced, jewel-bright, fork-tender).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Ugnsbakade Getlägg med Rödvin (Swedish/Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009) — covered-roast variant; köket.se  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml rapsolja
- 2 large gula lökar, sliced
- 1 large morot + 1 palsternacka, diced; 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 200 ml torrt rödvin + 200 ml kalvfond
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 4 enbär crushed; 2 g (½ tsp) ground kryddpeppar
- 4 sprigs fresh timjan + 4 sprigs fresh rosmarin
- 30 g chopped fresh persilja, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season.
2. Heat rapsolja in a heavy roasting tin over medium-high heat. Sear shanks all over, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Add onions, carrot, parsnip and garlic to the tin; toss with the oil.
4. Pour in red wine and stock. Add bay, juniper, allspice, thyme and rosemary.
5. Return shanks; cover tightly with foil. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Roast 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes; uncover for the final 30 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides through — Nordström's standard for ugnsbakade getlägg.
8. Rest 15 minutes in pan juices. Strew with parsley. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (juniper-perfumed, fork-tender).

### 3. Diana Henry — Slow-Roasted Goat Shanks with Anchovy & Rosemary (British/Mediterranean tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *Roast Figs, Sugar Snow* (Mitchell Beazley, 2005) — slow-roast game-shank technique adapted to goat; telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 large onions, sliced; 1 large carrot, diced; 1 celery stick, diced
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 4 anchovy fillets, finely chopped (Henry's umami booster)
- 200 ml dry red wine + 200 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 4 sprigs fresh rosemary; 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- Zest of 1 orange (in long strips)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy roasting tin over medium-high heat. Sear shanks all over, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Add onions, carrot, celery, garlic and anchovies to the tin; toss with the oil until anchovies dissolve.
4. Pour in red wine and stock. Add bay, rosemary, thyme and orange zest.
5. Return shanks; cover tightly with foil. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Roast 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes; uncover for the final 30 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides through — Henry's standard.
8. Rest 15 minutes. Discard rosemary stems and orange peel. Serve with creamy polenta. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (umami-deep, orange-bright, fork-tender).

### 4. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Slow-Roast Goat Shanks (British River Cottage tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *The River Cottage Meat Book* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2004) — goat-shank slow-roast technique; rivercottage.net  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml rapeseed oil
- 2 large onions, sliced; 1 large carrot, diced; 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) tomato purée
- 200 ml dry cider + 200 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 4 sprigs fresh thyme; 4 sage leaves
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season.
2. Heat rapeseed oil in a heavy roasting tin over medium-high heat. Sear shanks all over, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Add onions, carrot and garlic to the tin; toss with the oil. Stir in tomato purée; cook 1 minute.
4. Pour in cider and stock. Add bay, thyme and sage.
5. Return shanks; cover tightly with foil. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Roast 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes; uncover for the final 30 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides through — Fearnley-Whittingstall's standard.
8. Rest 15 minutes. Strew with parsley. Serve with mash. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (cider-mellow, herb-fragrant, fork-tender).
