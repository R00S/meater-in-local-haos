## Cut profile

Goat shoulder contains more connective tissue than the leg and rewards longer, slower cooking. It is the traditional cut for Jamaican curry goat, Indian roganjosh-style preparations, Caribbean slow-roasts, and Moroccan mechoui. The shoulder is cooked until pull-apart tender at 85–90 °C internal, or to a still-sliceable 75–78 °C.

Target internal temperature: **82–88 °C (180–190 °F)** for pull-apart.

## Styckesprofil

Getaxel innehåller mer bindväv än benet och belönar längre, långsammare tillagning. Det är det traditionella snittet för jamaicansk *curry goat*, indiska *roganjosh*-beredningar, karibiska slow-roasts och marockansk *mechoui*. Axeln tillagas tills den är gaffelömt vid 85–90 °C internt.

Innertemperatur: **82–88 °C (180–190 °F)** för pull-apart. Eftertillagning: försumbar. Sond i den tjockaste muskelmassadelen, borta från skulderbladsben. Marinering 24 timmar i kryddad yoghurt eller citrusbaserad lag mörnar köttet och tillsätter fuktighetsbevaring under lång tillagning.

## Source recipes

### 1. African Bites — Jamaican Curry Goat (Caribbean tradition)
**Inspired by**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/jamaican-curry-goat/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min + overnight marinade · **Cook**: 2–2.5 hrs

*Jamaican curry goat is one of the most beloved dishes of the Caribbean diaspora.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg goat shoulder (bone-in), cut into large chunks
- 3 tbsp Jamaican curry powder
- 1 tsp allspice (ground pimento)
- 1 scotch bonnet pepper, chopped (seeds removed for less heat)
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp fresh thyme
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 2 cups (480 ml) water or stock
- 2 potatoes, quartered
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Rub goat with curry powder, allspice, scotch bonnet, onion, garlic, and thyme. Marinate overnight or at least 4 hours.
2. Heat oil in a large heavy pot. Sear goat in batches until browned.
3. Return all goat to pot. Add water. Bring to boil, reduce to low simmer.
4. Cover and cook 1.5–2 hours. Add potatoes for the last 30 minutes.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** for fall-apart tender.
6. Serve with rice and peas (kidney beans and coconut rice) or roti.
*Final temperature: 85–90 °C (185–194 °F).*

### 2. IndianHealthyRecipes.com — Rogan Josh (Kashmir/Indian goat shoulder)
**Inspired by**: Indianhealthyrecipes.com; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/rogan-josh/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min

*Rogan Josh is a flagship Kashmiri dish, traditionally made with goat shoulder on the bone.*

**Ingredients** *(From actual fetched page)*
- 500 g goat (or mutton/lamb) shoulder pieces (bone-in)
- 4 tbsp oil or ghee
- 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder (for colour and mild heat)
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp garam masala
- ½ tsp fennel powder (saunf)
- 2 whole cardamoms
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 cinnamon stick
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil/ghee in a heavy pot. Add whole spices (cardamom, bay, cinnamon), cook 30 seconds. Add sliced onions, fry golden.
2. Add garlic and ginger; cook 2 minutes. Add ground spices and Kashmiri chilli. Fry 1 minute.
3. Add goat pieces; sear on all sides over high heat 5 minutes.
4. Add water (about 200 ml), bring to a boil. Reduce to low, cover, and simmer 1–1.5 hours.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** for tender, falling-off-bone meat.
6. Garnish with coriander. Serve with steamed rice or naan.
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*

### 3. Nefisyemektarifleri.com — Fırında Kuzu Omuz (Turkish goat/lamb shoulder)
**Inspired by**: Nefisyemektarifleri.com; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-kuzu-omuz/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hrs

*Translation: Oven-roasted goat (or lamb) shoulder, Turkish style.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg goat or lamb shoulder, bone-in
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp paprika
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 onions, quartered
- 200 ml water

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Rub shoulder with oil, salt, pepper, oregano, paprika, and garlic.
2. Place in roasting pan with onions and water. Cover tightly with foil.
3. Roast 2.5 hours covered. Uncover, roast 30 more minutes at 200 °C to brown.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 83–85 °C (181–185 °F)**.
5. Rest 10 minutes. Serve with Turkish rice pilaf and yoghurt.
*Final temperature: 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).*
