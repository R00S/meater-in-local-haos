# Goat Shoulder × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Slow-cooker goat shoulder (Swedish *getskuldra* / *getbog*) takes the same braising principles but uses a 6–8 hour low-temperature cycle in a sealed countertop crock-pot at ~88–93 °C. The lower-and-slower environment is gentler than oven-braising and tolerates inattention, but the lid traps all the steam, so the surface never browns; chefs always sear the shoulder first in a separate pan. Internal target is the same collagen-melt band: 88–93 °C, sustained for 4–5 hours. Probe horizontally through the thickest part of the muscle, away from bone.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Slow-Cooker Goat Shoulder with Pomegranate (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012) — slow-cooker adaptation; ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 8 hours on LOW · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil for searing
- 3 large onions, finely chopped
- 8 garlic cloves, finely chopped; 60 g fresh ginger, grated
- 10 g (2 tsp) cumin + 10 g (2 tsp) coriander + 5 g (1 tsp) cinnamon + 5 g (1 tsp) allspice + 5 g (1 tsp) smoked paprika
- 200 ml pomegranate molasses
- 400 ml chicken stock (less than oven-braise — slow cooker doesn't reduce)
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 2 cinnamon sticks
- 100 g toasted walnuts; 30 g chopped fresh parsley + 60 g pomegranate seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat shoulder dry; season generously. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder all over until deeply browned, 4 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
2. In the same pan, sweat onions until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic and ginger; cook 1 minute. Stir in spices; cook 30 seconds. Whisk in pomegranate molasses and chicken stock; bring to a boil.
3. Pour mixture over shoulder in slow cooker. Add bay and cinnamon sticks.
4. Cover. Set to LOW for 8 hours. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the muscle through the slow-cooker lid port (or accept the lid will need to be lifted briefly at hour 6 to verify).
5. After 8 hours, **pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when the meat pulls apart with a fork without resistance.
6. Rest 20 minutes uncovered. Pull meat into chunks. Skim fat from sauce. Strew with walnuts, parsley and pomegranate seeds. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (deeply spiced, walnut-rich, fork-tender).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Långkokt Getskuldra i Crock-Pot (Swedish/Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009) — slow-cooker adaptation; köket.se  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 8 hours on LOW · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml rapsolja for searing
- 3 large gula lökar, finely chopped
- 2 large morötter + 2 palsternacka, diced; 8 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) tomatpuré
- 400 ml torrt rödvin + 400 ml kalvfond (less than oven-braise)
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 6 enbär crushed; 5 g (1 tsp) ground kryddpeppar
- 6 sprigs fresh timjan + 6 sprigs fresh rosmarin
- 30 g chopped fresh persilja, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat shoulder dry; season. Heat rapsolja in a large pan over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder all over, 4 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
2. In the same pan, sweat onions, carrots and parsnips until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic and tomato purée; cook 1 minute. Add red wine; reduce by half (10 minutes). Add stock; bring to a boil.
3. Pour mixture over shoulder. Add bay, juniper, allspice, thyme and rosemary.
4. Cover. Set to LOW for 8 hours. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into thickest part of muscle through lid port.
5. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when fork pulls the meat apart — Nordström's standard.
6. Rest 20 minutes. Pull meat into chunks. Skim fat. Strew with parsley. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (juniper-perfumed, fork-tender).

- 2 large carrots, diced; 8 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) tomato purée

4. Cover. Set to LOW for 8 hours. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into thickest part of muscle through lid port.
5. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when fork pulls the meat apart — Fearnley-Whittingstall's standard.
6. Rest 20 minutes. Pull meat. Skim fat. Strew with parsley. Serve with mash. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (cider-mellow, sage-perfumed, fork-tender).

### 4. Maricel Presilla — Slow-Cooker Cabrito en Salsa Verde (Latin American tradition)
**Source**: Maricel E. Presilla, *Gran Cocina Latina: The Food of Latin America* (W. W. Norton, 2012) — slow-cooker adaptation  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 8 hours on LOW · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil for searing
- 3 large onions, finely chopped; 10 garlic cloves, chopped
- 30 g fresh oregano leaves; 10 g (2 tsp) ground cumin
- **Salsa verde**: 500 g tomatillos (husked, blackened) + 4 jalapeños + 1 bunch coriander + 30 ml lime juice — blended
- 400 ml chicken stock; 4 fresh bay leaves
- 30 g chopped fresh coriander, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat shoulder dry; season. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder all over, 4 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
2. In the same pan, sweat onions until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic, oregano and cumin; cook 1 minute. Stir in salsa verde; cook 5 minutes. Add stock; bring to a boil.
3. Pour mixture over shoulder. Add bay.
4. Cover. Set to LOW for 8 hours. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into thickest part of muscle through lid port.
5. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when fork pulls the meat apart — Presilla's standard.
6. Rest 20 minutes. Pull meat. Skim fat. Strew with coriander. Serve with arroz blanco and warm tortillas. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (tomatillo-bright, jalapeño-warm, fork-tender).
