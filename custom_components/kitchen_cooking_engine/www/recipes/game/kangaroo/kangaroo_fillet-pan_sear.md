# Kangaroo Fillet × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The kangaroo fillet (backstrap) is ideal for pan searing: its thin, lean profile heats through rapidly at very high heat, developing a dark, caramelised Maillard crust in 2–3 minutes per side while retaining a warm red interior. Because there is virtually no fat cap, the searing must occur in a well-oiled, very hot pan (cast iron preferred) — adding oil to an inadequately hot pan results in poaching rather than searing. The backstrap is even thinner than most kangaroo rump cuts, so timing precision is more critical: 2 minutes per side at maximum heat is sufficient for medium-rare in fillets under 3 cm thick; 3 minutes per side risks medium-well. A probe thermometer inserted lengthwise (parallel to the fillet, towards the centre) is the most accurate approach for fillets this thin, but experienced cooks use the firm-to-touch method (rare: no resistance; medium-rare: slight spring back). Rest time of 5 minutes is mandatory — skipping rest leads to immediate moisture loss. Carryover is typically 2–4°C / 4–7°F for the thin fillet profile.

## Styckesprofil

Känguru-filé (ryggsträng) lämpar sig idealiskt för pannstekning: dess tunna, magra profil värms upp snabbt vid mycket hög värme och utvecklar en mörk, karameliserad Maillard-skorpa på 2–3 minuter per sida med ett varmt rött inre kvar.

Ryggsträngen är ännu tunnare än känguru-rumps, varför timingprecision är mer kritisk: 2 minuter per sida vid maximal värme är tillräcklig för medium-rare i filéer under 3 cm tjocka. Sond insatt längsvis (parallellt med filén, mot mitten) är det mest exakta tillvägagångssättet. Vilotid 5 minuter obligatorisk. Innertemperatur: **52–55 °C** (126–131 °F). Eftertillagning: 2–4 °C.

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Kängurufilet mit Pflaumensauce, geschmorten Kirschtomaten und Potatoes Wedges
**Inspired by**: https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/307911111108186/Kaengurufilet-mit-Pflaumensauce-geschmorten-Kirschtomaten-und-Potatoes-Wedges.html  
**Tradition**: German home cooking — **Culinary Group D** (Slavic/Baltic/European)  
**Rating**: 4.3/5 stars (12 reviews)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 40 min (incl. 8–10 min oven finish) · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**

*Kangaroo:*
- 700 g kangaroo fillet (*Känguru Filet*)
- Cracked black pepper, to season

*Plum sauce:*
- 1 onion, roughly diced
- 200 g prunes (*Trockenpflaumen*), roughly diced
- 1 tbsp tomato paste (*Tomatenmark*)
- 50 ml red wine
- 200 ml plum wine (*Pflaumenwein*)
- 300 ml broth (*Brühe*)
- 2 tbsp sweet chili sauce
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1 sprig thyme
- 1 sprig rosemary
- Soup powder (to taste)
- Oil for frying

*Braised cherry tomatoes:*
- 500 g cherry tomatoes
- 50 ml dark balsamic vinegar
- 3 tbsp sugar
- Salt and pepper

*Accompaniment:*
- 1 packet frozen potato wedges (prepared per packet instructions)

**Method**
1. Trim the kangaroo fillet of sinew. Set aside.
2. Dice the onion and prunes. Sauté with the fillet trimmings in a little oil for 3–4 minutes. Add tomato paste, continue roasting for 2 minutes. Deglaze with red wine; reduce completely. Add plum wine; reduce again. Add sugar, rosemary, thyme, sweet chili sauce, and broth. Reduce heat; simmer 30 minutes. Strain through a fine sieve; adjust thickness with soup powder if needed.
3. In a separate pan, boil down balsamic vinegar with sugar, salt, and pepper. Add cherry tomatoes; reduce to creamy consistency. Do not overcook or the balsamic will turn bitter.
4. Preheat oven to 190°C / 375°F (Ober-/Unterhitze).
5. Heat oil in an ovenproof pan over high heat. Season fillet with salt and cracked pepper. **Sear on both sides until browned** (2–3 minutes per side).
6. Transfer ovenproof pan to the preheated oven for 8–10 minutes.
7. Remove from oven and rest for 5 minutes.
8. Slice fillet and serve with plum sauce, cherry tomatoes, and potato wedges.

**Temperature note**: No explicit probe temperature given; recipe relies on timing. Based on 8–10 min oven finish at 190°C, the fillet reaches approximately **55–58°C / 131–136°F** (medium) at the centre. For medium-rare, reduce oven time to 6–7 minutes and **pull at ≈54°C / 130°F**.

### 2. Kangaroo Wings (Australia) — Cabernet Sauvignon Marinated Kangaroo Backstrap with Basil Pesto
**Inspired by**: https://kangaroowings.au/recipes/cabernet-sauvignon-marinated-kangaroo-backstrap-with-basil-pesto/  
**Tradition**: Australian contemporary home cooking — **Culinary Group B** (North American/Western European/Australasian)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 12 hr (overnight marinade) + 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**

*Kangaroo:*
- 400 g kangaroo backstrap (fillet), portioned as desired

*Marinade:*
- 750 ml Cabernet Sauvignon (full-bodied Australian red)
- 1 sprig thyme
- 10 g star anise
- 10 g blackberries
- 2 cinnamon sticks

*Pesto:*
- 2 bunches (approx. 4 cups) fresh basil
- ½ bunch parsley
- 25 g pine kernels, toasted
- 1 cup grated Parmesan
- 3 cloves garlic
- ½ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 carrot, chopped
- Small amount pine nut oil (optional)

*To serve:*
- 1 tbsp clarified butter

**Method**
1. Portion the backstrap as desired. Combine in a large bowl with the Cabernet Sauvignon, thyme, star anise, blackberries, and cinnamon sticks. Marinate overnight in the refrigerator.
2. Next day, make the pesto: pulse all pesto ingredients in a food processor until smooth. Set aside.
3. Remove backstrap from marinade; pat dry thoroughly.
4. Heat a heavy-based pan (cast iron preferred) or preheat charcoal grill to high. Sear backstrap over high heat **3–4 minutes per side** until medium-rare (or as desired).
5. Remove; rest for a few minutes.
6. Brush with clarified butter; return briefly to the hot pan to finish and baste.
7. Rest a further 2–3 minutes; slice thinly across the grain.
8. Serve with pesto, creamy mashed potatoes, and crisp green salad; finish with a few drops of clarified butter.

**Temperature note**: No explicit pull temperature given; recipe uses visual/timing cues. Based on standard Australian practice for kangaroo backstrap (see kangaroo_steak-pan_sear.md), medium-rare is **pull at 52–54°C (126–130°F)**, serving at **55–58°C (131–136°F)** after rest.
