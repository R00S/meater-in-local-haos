## Cut profile

A whole mutton leg (bone-in, 2–3 kg) is a festive cut in the Middle East, Pakistan, Afghanistan, UK, and parts of Europe. Its strong, mature flavour benefits from marinating and long, slow oven roasting. Most traditions roast at 160–180 °C for 2.5–4 hours, covered for the majority of the time to prevent drying.

Target internal temperature: **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for fully cooked, still juicy. Some Pakistani and Indian recipes cook to 85 °C for fall-apart texture.

## Styckesprofil

Ett helt fårben (med ben, 2–3 kg) är ett festsnitt i Mellanöstern, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Storbritannien och delar av Europa. Den starka, mogna smaken gynnas av marinering och lång, långsam ugnsstekning. De flesta traditioner rostar vid 160–180 °C i 2,5–4 timmar, täckt för majoriteten av tiden för att förhindra uttorkning.

Innertemperatur: **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** för fullt genomstekt, fortfarande saftigt. Vissa pakistanska och indiska recept tillagar till 85 °C för fall-apart-textur. Eftertillagning: 3–5 °C. Sond i den tjockaste köttdelen borta från lårbenet. Vila 15–20 minuter täckt före skärning.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hammel aus dem Ofen (inspired by Chefkoch.de)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1804451291813862/Hammel-aus-dem-Ofen.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Rated 4.5/5 — "Super tender and incredibly tasty"*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr · **Cook**: 3.5–4 hrs

*Translation: Mutton from the oven. Author's description: "shoulder cut in gulash-sized pieces, roasted in wine — super tender."*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg mutton shoulder (can use leg), cut into large pieces
- 4 medium onions, quartered
- Handful of fresh thyme
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 100 ml olive oil
- ¾ bottle (approx. 550 ml) medium-sweet red wine
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Cut mutton into large gulash pieces. Heat 50 ml olive oil in a large pot over high heat and brown the meat all over.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C.
3. Add quartered onions, crushed garlic, and thyme to the pot. Reduce heat and cook until the juices have run out (approx. 30 minutes).
4. Transfer everything to an ovenproof dish. Season with salt and pepper. Add the remaining olive oil and pour the wine over everything. Cover tightly with foil, pierce foil 3–4 times with a sharp knife.
5. Cook in the oven at 200 °C for 3–4 hours.
6. Insert probe. **Pull at 80 °C (176 °F)** for fall-apart tender meat.
7. Serve with roast vegetables, crusty bread, or couscous.
*Final resting temperature: 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).*

### 2. Raan Musallam (inspired by IndianHealthyRecipes.com)
**Inspired by**: IndianHealthyRecipes.com; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/raan-musallam/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 2 hrs marinate · **Cook**: 3 hrs

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole mutton leg (approx. 2 kg, bone-in)
- 1 cup thick yoghurt
- 3 tbsp oil
- 1 tbsp ginger paste
- 1 tbsp garlic paste
- 2 tsp red chilli powder
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp cumin powder
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Deeply score the leg all over. Mix marinade ingredients and rub into the meat and into the cuts. Marinate at least 2 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Place on a rack over a roasting tray with 200 ml water below.
3. Roast covered with foil 1.5 hours. Remove foil, reduce to 170 °C, roast further 1–1.5 hours.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 80 °C (176 °F)** for fully tender.
5. Rest 15 minutes. Serve with biryani or naan and mint chutney.
*Final resting temperature: 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).*

### 3. Lammelår i ovnen (inspired by Madensverden.dk)
**Inspired by**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/lammekølle/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 2 h 30 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 mutton leg (1.5–2 kg bone-in)
- 4 garlic cloves, slivered
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp pepper
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 200 ml lamb stock or water

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Insert garlic slivers into cuts in the meat. Rub with butter, salt, pepper, rosemary.
2. Brown uncovered 20 minutes. Add stock to tray.
3. Reduce to 160 °C. Roast 1.5–2 hours covered until probe reads **75–78 °C (167–172 °F)**.
4. Uncover, return to 200 °C for 15 minutes to crisp. Rest 15 minutes.
*Final resting temperature: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).*
