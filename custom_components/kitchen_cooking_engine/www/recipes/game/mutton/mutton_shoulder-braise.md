## Cut profile

Mutton shoulder is a heavily worked, intensely flavoured cut from an older animal (12 months or more). The extra age means deeper flavour but also denser connective tissue, requiring longer braising times than lamb — typically 2.5–3.5 hours on the stovetop or 3–4 hours in the oven. When braised low and slow, the shoulder yields incredibly rich, yielding meat that is the centrepiece of Indian korma, Turkish tandır, and Italian stuffed shoulder (spalla) traditions. Mutton benefits from full-bodied braising liquids — wine, yoghurt, spiced stocks — that can stand up to its assertive flavour. The MEATER probe is inserted through the thickest muscle of the shoulder, well away from the shoulder blade bone.

Target internal temperature: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for fall-off-the-bone; mutton shoulder may need 90–95 °C due to the older animal's denser tissue.

## Styckesprofil

Fåraxel är ett hårt arbetat, intensivt smakfull snitt från ett äldre djur (12+ månader). Den extra åldern innebär djupare smak men också tätare bindväv, vilket kräver längre braiseringstider än lamm — typiskt 2,5–3,5 timmar på spisen eller 3–4 timmar i ugnen.

Bräserat lågt och långsamt ger axeln otroligt rikt kött centralt i indisk korma, turkisk *tandır* och italiensk *spalla*. Fårkött gynnas av kraftfulla braiseringsvätskor — vin, yoghurt, kryddade fonder. MEATER-sonden stickas in genom den tjockaste muskeln i axeln, väl borta från skulderbladsben. Innertemperatur: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)**. Kan behöva 90–95 °C för äldre djur.

## Source recipes

### 1. Indian Healthy Recipes — Mutton Korma / Gosht Korma (Indian / Mughlai tradition)
**Source**: Indian Healthy Recipes (Swasthi Shreekanth); https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/mutton-korma-recipe/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Mutton Korma — Mughlai braised mutton in rich yoghurt and spice gravy."*
**Serves**: 4–5 · **Prep**: 20 min + 30 min marinate · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min

**Ingredients**

*Marinade:*
- 500 g bone-in mutton shoulder pieces
- 1 cup (240 ml) full-fat yoghurt
- 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1 tsp salt

*For the braise:*
- 2 tbsp ghee or neutral oil
- 2 small cinnamon sticks
- 3–4 green cardamom pods
- 1 black cardamom pod
- 4–5 cloves
- ½ tsp black peppercorns
- 2–3 green chilies, slit
- 1 cup (160 g) deep-fried or well-browned onions (birista)
- ½ tsp black pepper, ground

**Method**
1. Mix yoghurt with all marinade spices. Coat mutton pieces thoroughly and marinate for at least 30 minutes (overnight if possible).
2. Heat ghee in a heavy pot over medium heat. Add whole spices — cinnamon, cardamoms, cloves, and peppercorns. Fry 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add browned/fried onions (birista) and stir well. Add the marinated mutton with all the yoghurt marinade.
4. Stir well to combine. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to very low.
5. Cover and braise on the lowest heat for **1 hour to 1 hour 30 minutes**, stirring every 20–30 minutes. Add a small splash of water if the mixture dries out before the meat is tender.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest shoulder piece. **Pull at 87–90 °C (189–194 °F)** when meat is very tender. Mutton may require up to 95 °C due to denser tissue.
7. Stir in a little ghee at the end. Garnish with green chilies.
8. **Rest 5–10 min** before serving with naan, roti, or basmati rice.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 2. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Fırında Kuzu Kol Tandır (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-kuzu-kol-tandir-11409971/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Fırında Kuzu Kol Tandır — Turkish oven tandır shoulder, adapted here as a covered braise for mutton."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr (stovetop braise)

*Braise adaptation: the original recipe wraps the shoulder in foil and bakes at 220 °C for 2.5 hours. For a stovetop braise, the same ingredients work beautifully in a covered Dutch oven over low heat for 3 hours with added liquid.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 mutton shoulder, bone-in (~1.5 kg)
- 6–8 garlic cloves, whole
- 1 tsp coarse salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp mixed spice (Turkish baharat)
- Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 300 ml water or lamb/mutton stock
- 2 onions, quartered

**Method**
1. Pierce the shoulder all over with a sharp knife. Push a garlic clove into each incision.
2. Rub shoulder all over with salt, pepper, mixed spice, and olive oil.
3. Press rosemary and thyme sprigs against the meat.
4. Heat a heavy Dutch oven over high heat with a little oil. Sear shoulder on all sides until well browned, about 4–5 minutes per side.
5. Add onions to the pot. Pour in stock/water. Bring to a boil, reduce to very low.
6. Cover tightly and braise for **3 hours**, turning shoulder once at the halfway point and adding more water if needed.
7. Insert MEATER probe before braising begins. **Pull at 87–90 °C (189–194 °F)**.
8. **Rest 15–20 min** loosely tented. Serve with rice pilav and roasted vegetables.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 3. Giallo Zafferano — Spalla d'agnello farcita alla toscana (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Ricette GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Spalla-d-agnello-farcita-alla-toscana.html (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Spalla d'agnello farcita alla toscana — Tuscan stuffed lamb/mutton shoulder, braised in wine and broth."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 45 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 deboned mutton or lamb shoulder (~1.2 kg)
- 200 g pork sausage meat (salsiccia)
- 1 carrot + 1 celery stalk + 1 onion (for filling and broth)
- 2 garlic cloves; fresh sage, rosemary sprigs
- 2 tbsp breadcrumbs (mollica)
- 1 egg
- 200 ml dry red wine
- 500 ml meat broth
- 60 ml extra-virgin olive oil
- Salt, black pepper
- Kitchen string

**Method**
1. Bone out the shoulder and flatten it with a meat mallet. Season with salt, pepper, and part of the chopped garlic, sage, and rosemary.
2. Prepare filling: sauté finely diced carrot, celery, onion in 30 g olive oil until softened. Cool. Mix with crumbled sausage meat, breadcrumbs, egg, and remaining herbs. Season.
3. Spread filling over the shoulder. Roll tightly; tie with kitchen string.
4. In a casserole, heat remaining olive oil with a garlic clove and rosemary sprig. Brown the rolled shoulder on all sides.
5. Deglaze with red wine; allow to sizzle briefly. Add coarsely chopped carrot, celery, and onion. Pour in broth.
6. Cover and braise over medium-low heat for approximately **1 hour**, adding more broth if needed.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the roll. **Pull at 87–90 °C (189–194 °F)**.
8. Remove string. Blend braising juices and vegetables for a rich sauce.
9. **Rest 10 min**. Slice and serve with sauce.
*Final temperature: 90–92 °C (194–198 °F).*

### 4. Chefkoch.de — Geschmorter Lammhals (shoulder adaptation) (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2358101374724165/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Geschmorter Lammhals — German braised lamb neck; technique applied here to mutton shoulder."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 hr 30 min (oven 200 °C fan 180 °C)

*Mutton adaptation: the same German red-wine braise technique used for lamb neck applies directly to mutton shoulder, with cooking time extended by 30 minutes to account for the older, tougher meat.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.2–1.5 kg mutton shoulder, bone-in or boned and tied
- 6 large onions, coarsely chopped
- 5 carrots, roughly chopped
- 10 bay leaves
- Fresh thyme sprigs
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 200 ml dry red wine
- 200 ml beef or mutton stock
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (fan 180 °C).
2. Heat olive oil in a large ovenproof casserole. Brown shoulder pieces on all sides, 4–5 minutes per side.
3. Remove meat. Sauté onions, carrots, and garlic until golden, about 5 minutes.
4. Return shoulder to pot on top of vegetables. Add bay leaves and thyme.
5. Pour in wine and stock. Season. Bring to a simmer.
6. Cover tightly and transfer to oven. Braise for **2 hours 30 minutes**.
7. Insert MEATER probe before going into oven. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F)**.
8. **Rest 15 min**. Serve with Semmelknödel (bread dumplings) or roasted potatoes.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA lamb/mutton minimum | 63 °C (145 °F) | Not relevant for braised shoulder |
| Approaching tender (lamb) | 85–88 °C (185–190 °F) | Lamb collagen converted |
| Fall-off-the-bone (mutton) | 88–92 °C (190–198 °F) | Older animal needs higher final temp |
| Indian korma extended | 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) | Very long low-heat cook |
