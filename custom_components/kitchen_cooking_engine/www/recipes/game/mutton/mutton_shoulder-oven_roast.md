## Cut profile

Mutton shoulder is a fatty, well-worked cut with pronounced flavour and good collagen content. It benefits from long, slow roasting (4–5 hours at 150–160 °C) to break down connective tissue and achieve pull-apart texture. It is the cut used for slow-roasted Sunday lunch mutton in the UK, and for the Turkish/Middle Eastern *kelle-paça* and *tandır* traditions.

Target internal temperature: **82–90 °C (180–194 °F)** for pull-apart. Minimum food-safe for fully cooked: 70 °C.

## Styckesprofil

Fåraxel är ett fett, välarbetat snitt med uttalad smak och bra kollageninnehåll. Det gynnas av lång, långsam ugnsstekning (4–5 timmar vid 150–160 °C) för att bryta ned bindväven och uppnå pull-apart-textur. Det är snittet som används för långsamt ugnsrostad söndagsstek i Storbritannien, och för turkiska/mellanösternska *kelle-paça*- och *tandır*-traditioner.

Innertemperatur: **82–90 °C (180–194 °F)** för pull-apart. Minimum livsmedelssäkert för fullt genomstekt: 70 °C. Eftertillagning: försumbar. Sond i tjockaste muskeldelen, borta från skulderbladsben.

## Source recipes

### 1. Slow-roasted Mutton Shoulder (inspired by BBC Good Food)
**Inspired by**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-roasted-shoulder-mutton (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 4.5 hrs

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg mutton shoulder, bone-in
- 4 garlic cloves, halved
- 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lemon, juice
- 200 ml white wine
- 400 ml lamb or chicken stock
- Salt and black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Make cuts in the mutton and insert garlic and rosemary. Rub with oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
2. Brown uncovered 20 minutes at 220 °C.
3. Reduce to 150 °C. Add wine and stock to the roasting tin. Cover tightly with foil.
4. Roast 4–4.5 hours until the meat is completely tender and pulling away from the bone.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** for pull-apart.
6. Rest 20 minutes. Shred and serve with roasted root vegetables and mint sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).*

### 2. Fırında Kuzu Kol (Turkish oven-roasted lamb/mutton shoulder) (inspired by Nefisyemektarifleri.com)
**Inspired by**: Nefisyemektarifleri.com; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-sebzeli-kuzu-kol/ (fetched 2026-04-24 — log reference)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 h 30 min

*Translation: Oven-roasted mutton (or lamb) shoulder with vegetables — Turkish tradition.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg mutton shoulder
- 4 potatoes, quartered
- 3 carrots, cut in large pieces
- 2 onions, quartered
- 2 tomatoes, quartered
- 3 garlic cloves
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 200 ml water

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Rub mutton shoulder with oil, salt, pepper, paprika, and oregano.
2. Place vegetables in a large roasting pan. Place mutton on top. Add water.
3. Cover tightly with foil. Roast at 200 °C for 2 hours.
4. Remove foil. Roast a further 30 minutes to brown.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)**.
6. Serve directly from the pan with yoghurt and fresh bread.
*Final resting temperature: 85–90 °C (185–194 °F).*

### 3. Шашлык из баранины (Russian / Central Asian slow-roasted mutton) (inspired by Povarenok.ru)
**Inspired by**: Povarenok.ru; https://www.povarenok.ru/recipes/show/98462/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 2 hrs · **Cook**: 3.5 hrs

*Translation: Russian/Caucasian style oven-roasted mutton with caramelised onions and pomegranate.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg mutton shoulder, bone-in
- 3 onions, sliced
- 3 tbsp pomegranate juice or wine vinegar
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp coriander
- 3 garlic cloves, minced

**Method**
1. Marinate mutton in onions, pomegranate juice, spices 2 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 180 °C. Place in a covered roasting pot (or cover with foil).
3. Roast 3–3.5 hours until very tender.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 83–88 °C (181–190 °F)**.
5. Serve with plov (pilaf) or lavash bread.
*Final resting temperature: 87–92 °C (189–198 °F).*
