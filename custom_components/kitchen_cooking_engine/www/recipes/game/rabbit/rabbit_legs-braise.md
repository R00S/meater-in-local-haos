## Cut profile

Rabbit (*Oryctolagus cuniculus*) legs (front and hind) are the most commonly braised cut. The hind legs carry more meat and are the prime braising cut; the front legs are often included for flavour. Rabbit is lean, low in fat, and prone to drying if overcooked — braising in wine, stock, or cream keeps the meat moist while allowing the connective tissue to break down.

The French tradition (lapin aux pruneaux, lapin à la moutarde) is the most internationally recognised; German, Italian, and British traditions also have strong recipes. Braising time: 45–75 minutes. The meat should be fork-tender and pulling away from the bone.

Target internal temperature: **74–77 °C (165–175 °F)** — rabbit, like chicken, should be cooked through. Fork-tender braised rabbit reads 74–80 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Geschmorte Kaninchenkeulen (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1565741264063511/Geschmorte-Kaninchenkeulen.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Rated 4.7/5 from 17 reviews.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hrs overnight marinade · **Cook**: 45 min

*Translation: Braised rabbit legs — overnight mustard marinade, then simmered with smoked bacon and onions.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 rabbit legs (Kaninchenkeulen)
- 125 g smoked bacon, roughly diced
- 2 onions, roughly chopped
- Salt and pepper
- Mustard (grainy or Dijon), enough to coat the legs
- Oil for frying
- Sauce thickener (Saucenbinder) or cornflour, to taste

**Method**
1. Brush rabbit legs generously on all sides with mustard. Season with salt and pepper. Leave to marinate overnight in the fridge (or at least 2 hours).
2. Heat oil in a heavy pot over high heat. Brown the legs well on all sides. Remove from pot.
3. Add bacon and onions to the same pot and cook until lightly coloured.
4. Return the legs to the pot. Place bacon and onions on top. Add a splash of water (enough to keep moisture). Cover tightly.
5. Braise on low heat for 45 minutes, turning halfway. Add a little water if it dries out.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the largest leg. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)** — the meat should be tender and pulling from the bone.
7. If desired, thicken the braising juices with sauce thickener and season to taste.
8. Serve with Spätzle or potato noodles.
*Final resting temperature: 76–78 °C (169–172 °F).*

### 2. BBC Good Food — Rabbit au Vin (British/French tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/rabbit-au-vin (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min

*Coq au vin technique applied to rabbit — lardons, wine, mushrooms.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole rabbit, jointed into 6 pieces (or 4–6 rabbit legs)
- 150 g smoked lardons or streaky bacon
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 200 g chestnut mushrooms, halved
- 500 ml red wine (Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
- 200 ml chicken stock
- 1 tbsp tomato purée
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt and black pepper
- 2 tbsp flour
- Oil for frying
- Fresh parsley to garnish

**Method**
1. Season rabbit pieces with salt and pepper and dust with flour.
2. Heat oil in a large heavy casserole. Brown rabbit pieces in batches. Remove.
3. In the same pot, fry lardons until crispy. Add onion and garlic, cook 5 minutes.
4. Add mushrooms and cook 3 minutes. Return rabbit to the pot.
5. Pour in wine, stock, and tomato purée. Add thyme and bay leaves.
6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook 1 hour–1 h 15 min.
7. Insert probe into the thickest leg. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)** and meat falling from bone.
8. Garnish with parsley. Serve with crusty bread or mashed potato.
*Final resting temperature: 76–80 °C (169–176 °F).*

### 3. Giallozafferano.it — Coniglio alla cacciatora (Italian hunter-style braised rabbit)
**Source**: Giallozafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Coniglio-alla-cacciatora.html (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 10 min

*Classic Italian cacciatore: tomatoes, capers, olives, rosemary.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 rabbit, jointed (or 4 hind legs)
- 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 150 ml white wine
- 400 g canned peeled tomatoes
- 2 tbsp capers
- 100 g black olives
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Brown rabbit pieces in olive oil with garlic and rosemary until golden.
2. Deglaze with white wine. Cook off alcohol.
3. Add tomatoes, capers, olives. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Cover and simmer 50–60 minutes, until rabbit is tender and probe reads **74 °C (165 °F)**.
*Final resting temperature: 76–80 °C (169–176 °F).*
