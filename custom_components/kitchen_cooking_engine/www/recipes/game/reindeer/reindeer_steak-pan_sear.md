## Cut profile

Reindeer (*Rangifer tarandus*) is the traditional game meat of Sápmi (Lapland), northern Finland, Norway, and Sweden. The fillet (renfilé) and backstrap (sadel) are the premium cuts for pan searing. Reindeer meat is very lean, deep red, and has a delicate sweet-earthy flavour milder than moose. Because of its leanness it must be treated like venison: high heat, short cooking time, pulled well below well-done. Medium-rare (57 °C) is the Nordic standard.

Swedish temperature guide (köket.se):
- Renfilé: rare 52 °C, medium 57 °C, well done 65 °C
- Ren stekdetaljer: 55–62–68 °C

## Styckesprofil

Ren (*Rangifer tarandus*) är det traditionella viltköttet i Sápmi (Lappland), norra Finland, Norge och Sverige. Filén (renfilé) och sadelryggen är de bästa snitten för pannstekning. Renkött är mycket magert, djupt rött med en delikat sötjordsmak mildare än älg.

Pannstekas i smör vid hög värme — 3–4 minuter per sida — sedan kort vila. Dra 3–5 °C under mål för att kompensera eftertillagning. Innertemperatur: **52–57 °C (126–135 °F)** för medium-rare. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Sond insatt parallellt med stekens yta in till mitten. Välgenomstekt avråds starkt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Renfilé med smörig blåbärssås (inspired by Köket.se / TV4 Halv åtta hos mig)
**Inspired by**: Köket.se; https://www.koket.se/renfile-med-smorig-blabarssas (fetched 2026-04-24). *Recipe by Nazuk Turdieva on TV4's Halv åtta hos mig (Half Past Seven at Mine). "A luxury dish with exciting northern flavours."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 45 min

*Translation: Reindeer fillet with buttery blueberry sauce, potato-artichoke purée, pickled funnel chanterelles, and charred pearl onions.*

**Ingredients**

*Reindeer fillet:*
- 600 g reindeer fillet (renfilé)
- 50 g butter
- Oil (neutral), for frying
- Fresh thyme, a few sprigs
- Salt and black pepper

*Buttery blueberry sauce:*
- 300 ml Swedish blueberries (frozen OK)
- 100 ml caster sugar
- 50 ml balsamic vinegar
- 100 ml red wine
- 200 ml meat stock (chicken, game, or beef)
- 50 g unsalted butter
- Salt to taste

*Potato-Jerusalem artichoke purée:*
- 200 g almond potatoes (or similar)
- 300 g Jerusalem artichoke
- 30 g butter
- 100 ml whipping cream
- 50 g extra butter
- ½ lemon, zest
- Salt

**Method**
1. **Blueberry sauce**: Combine all sauce ingredients except butter in a saucepan. Bring to the boil and simmer, stirring, until reduced by ⅔. Strain, pressing berries through the sieve. Whisk in butter piece by piece. Season with salt. Warm before serving.
2. **Purée**: Boil potato and artichoke until tender. Drain and whisk with remaining purée ingredients. Season.
3. **Reindeer fillet**: Clean fillet of membranes and tendons. Season generously with salt and pepper 30 minutes before cooking.
4. Preheat oven to **125 °C**. Heat a neutral-oil frying pan over high heat. Brown fillet with thyme and butter until golden on all sides, basting continuously with the butter.
5. Transfer fillet to an ovenproof dish. Insert the MEATER probe. Finish in the oven until core temperature reaches **52 °C (126 °F)** for rare/medium-rare. Rest 10 minutes.
6. Plate artfully with all components.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*

### 2. Reninnanlår à la Norrland (inspired by Köket.se / TV4)
**Inspired by**: Köket.se; https://www.koket.se/petter_tidestrom/huvudratter/vilt/reninnanlar_a_la_norrland (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1.5–2 hrs

*Translation: Reindeer inside-round (innanlår) Norrland style — seared in pan then finished in 100 °C oven.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 piece reindeer meat (saddle, inside round, or topside)
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 garlic clove, grated
- 1 tsp chilli pepper
- 4 tsp salt
- 3 tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp butter + a little oil

*Sauce:*
- 100 g celeriac, diced
- 2 shallots
- Fresh parsley
- 50 ml red wine
- 300 ml single cream
- 1 beef stock cube
- 1 tbsp messmör (whey butter) or cream cheese
- 100 ml frozen lingonberries
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Clean meat of tendons and membranes. Use a knife tip to make small holes; grate garlic and rub into holes. Chop thyme and press into meat. Season with salt, pepper, and chilli.
2. Brown the meat in butter and oil in a hot frying pan until a golden crust forms on all sides.
3. Insert probe. Roast in the oven at **100 °C** until internal temperature reaches **60 °C (140 °F)**. Pour pan juices over the meat periodically.
4. Rest 10 minutes. Serve with the cream-lingonberry sauce and vegetable pytt.
*Final resting temperature: 62–64 °C (144–147 °F).*

### 3. Paistettua poronlihaa (inspired by Kotikokki.net)
**Inspired by**: Kotikokki.net Finnish home cooking site; https://www.kotikokki.net/reseptit/haku/?hakusana=poronlihapihvi (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6 min

*Finnish tradition: brief frying in butter with juniper berries.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 reindeer steaks (poronlihapihvit), approx. 150 g each
- 2 tbsp butter
- 4 juniper berries, crushed
- Salt and pepper
- Lingonberry jam for serving

**Method**
1. Season reindeer steaks with salt and pepper. Let come to room temperature.
2. Heat butter in a heavy pan until foaming. Add crushed juniper berries.
3. Sear steaks 2 minutes per side over high heat.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–55 °C (126–131 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with mashed potato and lingonberry jam.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*
