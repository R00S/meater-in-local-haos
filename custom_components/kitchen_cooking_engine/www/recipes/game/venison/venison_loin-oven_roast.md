## Cut profile

Venison loin oven roast (also called sear-and-roast, or pan-to-oven) is the technique for whole loins or larger saddle sections where pan-searing alone does not cook through the meat evenly. The standard technique is: season → sear all sides in a hot oven-proof pan until golden-brown → transfer to a preheated oven at 180–200 °C → roast 6–10 minutes depending on thickness and desired doneness → rest on a rack 5–8 minutes off the heat.

Venison loin is very lean with minimal connective tissue; carryover after resting is typically 2–3 °C. Target internal temperature is 52–57 °C (medium-rare). The cut becomes dry and tough above 60 °C (medium-well). Oven temperature is higher than for beef (180–200 °C vs 120–160 °C for larger beef roasts) because venison loin is a small, lean muscle and benefits from fast, high-heat cooking.

Target internal temperature: **52–54 °C** for rare · **54–57 °C** for medium-rare (recommended) · **60–63 °C** for medium

## Source recipes

### 1. Roast Loin of Venison, Wild Mushroom Pie and Parsnip Purée (inspired by Adam Gray, Great British Chefs)
**Inspired by**: Adam Gray, Great British Chefs; https://www.greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/roast-venison-recipe (fetched 2026-04-28). First published 2015.
**Serves**: 4 · **Time**: 2 hours (including sauce and garnishes)
**Culinary tradition**: British (Group B)

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 180 °C / Gas Mark 4
- Method: sear in frying pan until golden brown, then move to oven
- Time in oven: 8–10 minutes for medium-rare, or longer for medium to well-done
- Rest: 5–8 minutes on a wire rack

**Ingredients** (venison portion only; recipe also includes wild mushroom pie and parsnip purée)
- 4 venison steaks (from loin / saddle), approximately 150 g each
- 100 ml rapeseed oil

**Method** (venison steps only)
1. Heat the oven to 180 °C / Gas Mark 4.
2. Seal the venison steaks in a hot frying pan with a little rapeseed oil until golden brown all over.
3. Move the steaks to the oven for **8–10 minutes for medium-rare**, or longer for medium to well done.
4. Remove from the oven and rest on a wire rack for 5–8 minutes.
5. Return the cooked venison steaks to the oven for 2 minutes to reheat just before plating.
6. Slice each steak into four even pieces and serve with mushroom pie and parsnip purée.

**Notes**: No explicit internal temperature probe stated; timing-based guidance implies approximately 54–57 °C (medium-rare) for 150 g steaks at 180 °C.

### 2. Roast Saddle of Red Deer with Rajasthani Spice Paste (Loin Steaks) (inspired by Vivek Singh, Great British Chefs)
**Inspired by**: Vivek Singh, Great British Chefs; https://www.greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/indian-venison-recipe (fetched 2026-04-28). Award-winning recipe; best British curry 2006.
**Serves**: 4 · **Time**: 1 hour 30 minutes
**Culinary tradition**: South Asian / Rajasthani (Group A)

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 200 °C / Gas Mark 6
- Method: marinade (30 min) → sear 2–3 minutes until browned → transfer pan to oven and roast 6–8 minutes (longer for more done) → finish with spice paste glaze under grill or oven 1–2 min
- No explicit probe temperature stated; chef leaves exact doneness to preference ("or longer depending on how you like your meat cooked")

**Ingredients** (venison portion)
- 4 venison steaks, each weighing 150 g, cut from 1 kg of saddle
- Marinade: ½ tsp salt, ½ tsp red chilli powder, 1 tbsp corn/vegetable oil
- Glaze: 1 tbsp Rajasthani spice paste + 1 tbsp Greek yoghurt

**Rajasthani Spice Paste** (key flavourings)
- Mustard oil, ghee, large onion, garlic, cloves, green cardamom, coriander seeds, black peppercorns, fennel seeds, coriander stalks

**Method** (venison steps)
1. Combine salt, red chilli powder and oil; rub over steaks. Marinate 30 minutes.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C / Gas Mark 6.
3. Sear marinated steaks in an oven-proof frying pan 2–3 minutes until browned evenly all over.
4. Transfer pan to the oven and roast for a further **6–8 minutes**, or longer depending on doneness preference.
5. Remove from oven. Spread Rajasthani spice paste mixed with Greek yoghurt over the steaks.
6. Return to oven (or under a hot grill) for 1–2 minutes until coating browns.
7. Serve with pickling-spice sauce and crispy deep-fried mushrooms.

**Notes**: Rajasthani preparation with game is a historic tradition from northern India (Rajasthan was a major hunting ground pre-ban). The spice coating protects the lean meat and adds moisture. No probe temperature stated; timing suggests approximately 54–57 °C after the oven stage for 150 g steaks at 200 °C.
