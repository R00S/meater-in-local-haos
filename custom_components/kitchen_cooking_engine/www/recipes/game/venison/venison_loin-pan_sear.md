## Cut profile

Venison loin (also: backstrap, saddle, or haunch steak — depending on country) from roe deer (*Capreolus capreolus*) or red deer (*Cervus elaphus*) is the premium pan-searing cut. The meat is very lean, deep red-brown, and has a rich, gamey flavour prized throughout European cooking. Pan-searing 2–3 minutes per side over high heat is the standard technique; the meat is universally pulled at medium-rare to medium to avoid dryness.

Köket.se temperature guide (Sweden):
- Rådjur/hjort ytterfilé & filé: rare 52 °C, medium 57 °C, well done 65 °C
- Stekdetaljer: 55/62/68 °C

## Styckesprofil

Viltrygghbiff (ryggsträng, sadel eller haunche-stek) från rådjur (*Capreolus capreolus*) eller kronhjort (*Cervus elaphus*) är det bästa pannstekssnittet. Köttet är mycket magert, djupt rödbrunt med en rik, viltartig smak upphöjd i hela europeisk matlagning.

Pannstekning i 2–3 cm tjocklek: 3–4 minuter per sida i smör eller klarat smör vid hög värme. Sond insatt från sidan in till mitten. Innertemperatur: **57–63 °C (135–145 °F)** för medium-rare till medium. Välgenomstekt avråds starkt. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Viltköttets magerhet kräver att man drar 3–4 °C under mål.

## Source recipes

### 1. Pan-Fried Venison with Blackberry Sauce (inspired by BBC Good Food)
**Inspired by**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/pan-fried-venison-blackberry-sauce (fetched 2026-04-24). *Recipe from Good Food magazine, November 2007.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 thick venison steaks, or 4 medallions
- 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- 150 ml beef stock (2 tsp Knorr concentrate or similar)
- 2 tbsp redcurrant jelly
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- 85 g fresh or frozen blackberries

**Method**
1. Heat the oil in a frying pan. Cook the venison for **5 minutes**, then turn and cook **3–5 more minutes**, depending on thickness (5–6 min per side for well-done). Lift meat from pan and set aside to rest.
2. Add balsamic vinegar to the pan, pour in stock, redcurrant jelly, and garlic. Stir over high heat to blend, then add blackberries. Cook until blackberries soften.
3. Insert MEATER probe. For medium-rare, **verify 57 °C (135 °F)** after resting.
4. Serve with celeriac mash (boil celeriac with 3 potatoes, mash with butter) and broccoli.

*Timing note: 5 min first side + 3 min second side → approx. 57–60 °C (135–140 °F). Adjust for thickness.*
*Final resting temperature: 59–62 °C (138–144 °F).*

### 2. Venison Steak with Port Sauce (inspired by BBC Good Food)
**Inspired by**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/venison-steak-port-sauce (fetched 2026-04-24). *Recipe from Good Food magazine, October 2010.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 750 g small potatoes, halved or quartered
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 venison steaks
- 1 tbsp cracked black pepper
- Chopped parsley to serve (optional)

*Sauce:*
- Zest of 1 orange, removed in strips, plus its juice
- 6 tbsp redcurrant jelly
- 4 tbsp port
- 1 cinnamon stick

**Method**
1. Make the gravy: simmer orange zest, orange juice, redcurrant jelly, port, and cinnamon stick together until jelly melts completely. Keep warm.
2. Steam or simmer potatoes until just tender (about 8 minutes). Drain, add a few drops of oil.
3. Press cracked black pepper and a little salt onto both sides of each steak.
4. Heat remaining oil in a pan until shimmering. Cook steaks **2 at a time: 2 minutes per side for rare, 3 minutes for medium, 4 minutes for well-done**.
5. Return all steaks to the pan, pour over sauce, warm 1 minute. Garnish with parsley.
6. Insert probe: **57 °C (135 °F)** for medium, **52 °C (126 °F)** for rare.
*Final resting temperature: 59–62 °C (138–144 °F).*

### 3. Pan-Roasted Loin of Venison with Redcurrant Sauce (inspired by Great British Chefs)
**Inspired by**: Great British Chefs; https://www.greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/pan-roasted-loin-venison-recipe (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 750 g loin of venison
- 2 tsp beef dripping or vegetable oil
- Sea salt
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 2 tsp redcurrant jelly
- 70 ml red wine

**Method**
1. Season venison with sea salt on both sides. Heat dripping in a heavy-based pan over medium heat.
2. Brown venison on each side for 2 minutes. Transfer pan to oven preheated to 200 °C and roast 5–7 minutes.
3. Remove venison, rest covered with foil 8–10 minutes.
4. Return pan to medium heat. Remove rosemary. Add redcurrant jelly; stir to dissolve. Add wine, deglaze, reduce by half. Mix in resting juices.
5. Insert probe: **57 °C (135 °F)** for medium, **52 °C (126 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Carve into slices and serve with sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 59–62 °C (138–144 °F).*

### 4. Reh- oder Hirschsteaks mit verschiedenen Marinaden (inspired by Chefkoch.de)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1520541257290915/Reh-oder-Hirschsteaks-mit-verschiedenen-Marinaden.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hrs · **Cook**: 15 min

*Translation: Roe deer or red deer steaks with various marinades — three marinade options for pan searing.*

**Ingredients** *(Herb-oil marinade):*
- 4 venison loin steaks, approx. 180 g each
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 1 sprig thyme
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix marinade. Coat steaks and marinate overnight in the fridge.
2. Remove from fridge 30 minutes before cooking.
3. Heat a cast-iron pan over high heat. Sear steaks 3 minutes first side without moving.
4. Flip, cook 3 more minutes.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for rare/medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with potato gratin and red wine sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).*
