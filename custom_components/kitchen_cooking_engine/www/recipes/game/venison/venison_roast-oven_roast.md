## Cut profile

Venison haunch or saddle roast is a classic British, German, French, and Scandinavian festive dish. The typical method: dry-age if possible, then sear all over in a hot pan and finish in the oven (180–200 °C for medium/well, or low 120–140 °C for precision doneness with a probe). The saddle in particular is a show-piece roast in Michelin-star British cooking.

Target internal temperature: **55–60 °C (131–140 °F)** for a pink, moist result. Some German recipes use the "Niedergarmethode" (low-temperature method) at 80–90 °C to hit exactly 58 °C core.

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Stuffed Saddle of Venison with Prunes and Brioche (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/stuffed-saddle-venison-prunes-brioche (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 40 min · **Cook**: 45 min

*An elegant dinner-party dish: venison saddle stuffed with prune, herb, and brioche filling.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 saddle of venison, approx. 1.5 kg, bones removed (ask your butcher), sinew trimmed
- 100 g pitted prunes, roughly chopped
- 50 g brioche or white bread crumbs
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- Salt and black pepper
- 200 ml red wine (for sauce)
- 200 ml game stock

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Mix prunes, breadcrumbs, thyme, rosemary, olive oil, and garlic into a stuffing. Season.
2. Open out the saddle, lay stuffing along the centre. Roll up tightly and tie with kitchen twine at 3 cm intervals.
3. Season outside with salt and pepper. Sear in an oven-safe pan over high heat 2 minutes per side until browned all over.
4. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part. Roast in oven 35–45 minutes until probe reads **58 °C (136 °F)** for a pink centre.
5. Rest 10 minutes. Remove twine, slice thickly.
6. **Sauce**: Pour off fat from roasting pan. Deglaze with red wine, add stock, reduce by half.
7. Serve with roasted root vegetables and sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Rehsteak rosa gebraten (German tradition — pink venison)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3954921606145264/Rehsteak-rosa-gebraten.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Translation: Pink-roasted venison steak (saddle) — seared and finished at 80 °C.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 venison loin steaks or 1 piece of saddle (approx. 500 g)
- Oil for searing
- Salt and pepper
- 2 tbsp butter
- Thyme and rosemary sprigs

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **80 °C** with an ovenproof dish inside.
2. Season venison with salt and pepper. Heat oil in pan over very high heat. Sear venison hard on all sides, 2 minutes per side.
3. Transfer to warm oven dish. Add herbs. Insert probe.
4. Roast at 80 °C for approx. 20–30 min until core reads **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** (the slow oven gives perfect even pink throughout).
5. Rest 5 minutes. The meat stays evenly pink with no grey band.
6. Serve with potato gratin and red cabbage or Rotkohl.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 3. Tasteline.com — Rådjursstek i ugn (Swedish roe deer roast)
**Source**: Tasteline.com; https://www.tasteline.com/ingrediens/alg/ (fetched 2026-04-24 — general Scandinavian game guidance; also see koket.se)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min + overnight marinade · **Cook**: 1.5 hrs

*Swedish oven-roasted roe deer (equivalent technique for red deer/venison).*

**Ingredients**
- 1–1.2 kg roe deer haunch or saddle roast
- 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
- 2 tbsp butter
- 4 juniper berries, crushed
- 1 tsp coarse salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 sprig thyme
- 150 ml game or beef stock

**Method**
1. Season roast with salt, pepper, and juniper. Sear in oil/butter all sides in a hot oven-safe pan.
2. Insert probe. Add stock. Transfer to 160 °C oven.
3. Roast until probe reads **57 °C (135 °F)** for medium (approx. 60–80 min for 1 kg).
4. Rest 10 minutes. Serve with lingonberry sauce and roasted root vegetables.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*
