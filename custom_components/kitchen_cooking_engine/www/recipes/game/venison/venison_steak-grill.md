## Cut profile

Venison backstrap, loin or haunch steaks are excellent on the grill. The lean, dense muscle benefits from high-heat direct grilling — it sears quickly, developing a smoky, caramelised crust while the centre stays pink and juicy. Because there is little intramuscular fat, venison goes from perfect to overcooked in moments; the grill lid should remain open to avoid trapped steam speeding up cooking.

The backstrap (outer loin) is the preferred cut for grilling: it is uniform in thickness, tender, and holds together on the grate. Haunch steaks (from the hindleg) benefit from a simple marinade to tenderise muscle fibres. Medallions cut from the loin are harder to grill successfully as they curl; a whole piece grills better and is then sliced.

Temperature precision is essential. Pull at **54–57°C (130–135°F)** for medium-rare; carry-over will bring it to 57–60°C after a 5-minute rest. Never take venison above 65°C (149°F) — it rapidly becomes grey, dry and livery. A MEATER probe inserted into the thickest part before grilling allows wireless monitoring without opening the grill.

## Styckesprofil

Viltbiff, loin eller haunche-steak är utmärkt på grillen. Den magra, täta muskeln gynnas av direkt hög värmegrillning — den searas snabbt och utvecklar en rökig, karameliserad yta medan mitten förblir rosa och saftig.

Grillen måste förvärmas till max (230–260 °C). Köttet bör oljeborsttas och gärna marineras 1–2 timmar i rödvin, olja och vitlök. Tillagningstid: 3–4 minuter per sida. Innertemperatur: **57–63 °C (135–145 °F)** för medium-rare till medium. Välgenomstekt avråds starkt. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Dra vid 53–58 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Venison Steak with Port Sauce (inspired by BBC Good Food)
**Inspired by**: Good Food Magazine, October 2010; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/venison-steak-port-sauce (fetched 2025-07-13)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 750 g small potatoes, halved or quartered
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 venison steaks (approx. 175 g each)
- 1 tbsp cracked black pepper
- Chopped parsley, to serve

*Port sauce:*
- Zest of 1 orange, removed in strips, plus its juice
- 6 tbsp redcurrant jelly
- 4 tbsp port
- 1 cinnamon stick

**Method**
1. Make the port sauce: simmer orange zest, orange juice, redcurrant jelly, port and cinnamon stick together in a small saucepan over medium heat until the jelly melts and the sauce is glossy. Keep warm.
2. Steam or simmer potatoes until just tender, about 8 minutes. Drain and toss with a few drops of olive oil.
3. Lay the venison steaks on a board. Sprinkle each side with cracked black pepper and a little salt, pressing the pepper into the meat.
4. Heat remaining oil in a heavy grill pan or cast iron skillet over very high heat until shimmering. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest steak.
5. Cook steaks 2 at a time. Grill 2 minutes per side for rare, 3 minutes for medium.
6. **Pull at 54°C (130°F)** for rare, **57°C (135°F)** for medium-rare. Return all steaks to the pan and pour sauce over; warm for 1 minute.
7. Sprinkle with parsley. Serve with potatoes and peas.
*Final serving temperature: 57–60°C (135–140°F).*

### 2. Grilled Venison Backstrap (inspired by Honest Food)
**Inspired by**: Hank Shaw, Honest Food; https://honest-food.net/grilled-venison-loin/ (fetched 2025-07-13)
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 60 min (rest) · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 450–900 g venison backstrap/loin, in one piece (at least 25 cm long)
- Olive oil, to coat
- Coarse salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- Optional: porcini powder for rubbing
- Optional sauce: green or red chimichurri, or maple-bourbon gravy

**Method**
1. Coat the venison backstrap thoroughly in olive oil and salt generously on all surfaces. Set aside at room temperature for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
2. Prepare a hot grill (charcoal or gas). Clean the grates thoroughly.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the end of the backstrap, centred in the thickest part.
4. Place the venison on the hot grill **with the lid open**. Grill without moving for 5–7 minutes until strong grill marks form on the first side.
5. Flip and grill the second side for 5–7 minutes. If the loin needs more time, rotate to the remaining sides and cook 2–3 minutes more per side.
6. **Pull at 54°C (130°F)** for medium-rare (larger elk/moose loins: pull at 52°C/125°F for same result after carry-over). Do not use a lid — trapped heat overcooks venison rapidly.
7. Rest on a board for 5–10 minutes. Apply spice rub if using (after grilling keeps the rub flavour fresh). Slice against the grain.
8. Serve with chimichurri, potato salad or corn, and a squeeze of lemon.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60°C (135–140°F).*

### 3. Reh- oder Hirschsteaks mit verschiedenen Marinaden (inspired by Chefkoch)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1520541257290915/Reh-oder-Hirschsteaks-mit-verschiedenen-Marinaden.html (fetched 2025-07-13)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min (+ 24 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg venison steaks (Reh/Hirsch), cut from the haunch about 2–3 cm thick

*Chilli-honey marinade (Option A):*
- Several red chillies, chopped · 10 tbsp pumpkin seed oil · 10 tbsp honey · Salt and pepper · Handful parsley, chopped · 4 garlic cloves, chopped

*Wild herb marinade (Option B):*
- 10 tbsp olive oil · Several bay leaves · 6 juniper berries · Fresh rosemary and thyme · Salt and pepper · 4 garlic cloves, chopped

*Beer-mustard marinade (Option C):*
- 330 ml dark beer · 10 tbsp olive oil · 2 tbsp Dijon mustard · 1 small onion, finely chopped · Bay leaves · 6 juniper berries · 4 garlic cloves · Salt and pepper · Fresh basil

**Method**
1. Cut the venison haunch into steaks approximately 2–3 cm thick.
2. Prepare your chosen marinade and immerse the steaks fully, turning to coat. Cover and refrigerate overnight (up to 24 hours).
3. Remove steaks from marinade 30 minutes before grilling. Pat lightly dry.
4. Preheat grill to high heat, clean grates and oil lightly.
5. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest steak.
6. Grill the steaks on high direct heat. They do not need to be fully cooked through — leaving them slightly pink keeps them juicy.
7. **Pull at 56°C (133°F)** for medium-rare. Rest 5 minutes.
8. Serve with potato gratin, green salad, or grilled vegetables.
*Note: Wildschwein (wild boar) also suits this treatment but should be cooked through (see wild boar files).*
*Final resting temperature: 59–62°C (138–144°F).*

### 4. Hjortfilé med grönpepparsås (inspired by Bara en kaka till)
**Inspired by**: Helena, Bara en kaka till; https://baraenkakatill.se/hjortfile-gronpepparsas-potatistorn/ (fetched 2025-07-13)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 75 min

**Ingredients**
- ca. 600 g ytterfilé av dovhjort (fallow deer outer fillet/backstrap)
- Butter or oil for browning
- Salt and pepper · Flaky salt to serve

*Potato towers:*
- 8 medium potatoes · 75 g butter · ½ tsp salt · Pepper · ½ tsp dried thyme · 150 ml finely grated Parmesan

*Green peppercorn sauce:*
- 2 shallots · 50 g butter · 2 tbsp whole green peppercorns in brine · 300 ml cream
- 1 tbsp beef stock concentrate · ½ tbsp soy sauce · 50 ml sherry · 1 tbsp brown Maizena starch · Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Bring the venison fillet to room temperature. Preheat oven to 200°C.
2. Make potato towers: slice potatoes very thinly on a mandoline. Melt butter, stir in salt, pepper, thyme and grated Parmesan. Toss potato slices in butter mixture and layer upright into muffin tin. Bake in centre of oven 45 minutes until tender. Cover with foil if browning too quickly. Reduce oven to 125°C.
3. Trim the venison fillet, season with salt and pepper. Sear quickly all over in butter or oil in a hot pan.
4. Transfer to an ovenproof dish. Insert the MEATER probe into the widest part.
5. Roast at 125°C until the probe reads the target temperature.
6. **Pull at 55–57°C (131–135°F)** for medium-rare. Wrap in baking parchment and rest 15 minutes.
7. While resting, make green peppercorn sauce: fry shallots in butter. Add peppercorns, cream, stock, soy and sherry. Simmer and thicken with brown Maizena. Season.
8. Return oven to 200°C to reheat potato towers (cover with foil).
9. Slice the venison and serve with potato towers, green peppercorn sauce and flaky salt.
*Final resting temperature: 58–61°C (136–142°F).*

### 5. Plankebiff av elg eller hjort (inspired by Matprat)
**Inspired by**: Matprat.no; https://www.matprat.no/oppskrifter/kos/plankebiff-av-elg/ (fetched 2025-07-13)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 2 hr soak for plank) · **Cook**: 30 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g ytrefilet av elg or hjort (outer fillet of moose or deer), cut into steaks
- 1 tsp salt · 1 tsp pepper · 2 tbsp margarine for browning

*Side:*
- 4 baking potatoes, wrapped in foil
- 100 g herb butter (recipe separate)

**Method**
1. Soak cedar or oak plank in water for at least 2 hours.
2. Wrap potatoes in foil and bake at 230°C for approximately 1 hour until cooked through.
3. Season the elk or deer steaks with salt and pepper.
4. Brown steaks on both sides in margarine in a hot frying pan until well-coloured.
5. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest steak.
6. Transfer steaks to the soaked wooden plank and place in the oven at 200°C for 8 minutes.
7. **Pull at 57°C (135°F)** for medium. Rest 8 minutes on the plank — the recipe notes this gives a medium result.
8. Top each steak with a knob of herb butter. Serve with the baked potato and a dollop of soured cream and salad.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63°C (140–145°F).*

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Pull temperature | Method |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| BBC Good Food (UK) | 54–57°C (130–135°F) | Hot pan/grill, 2–3 min/side |
| Honest Food / Hank Shaw (USA) | 54°C (130°F) | Open grill, no lid |
| Chefkoch (Germany) | 56°C (133°F) | Overnight marinade, direct grill |
| Bara en kaka till (Sweden) | 55–57°C (131–135°F) | Pan sear + low oven |
| Matprat (Norway) | 57°C (135°F) → 60°C rested | Pan sear + oven on plank |

**Consensus pull**: 54–57°C (130–135°F) for medium-rare across all traditions.

**Representative coding value**: 55°C (131°F)
