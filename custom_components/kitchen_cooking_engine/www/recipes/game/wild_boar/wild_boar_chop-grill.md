## Cut profile

Wild boar (*Sus scrofa*) loin chops (Wildschwein-Koteletts / côtelettes de sanglier) are taken from the back of the animal and include a rib bone, similar to pork chops but with darker, denser meat and a pronounced game aroma. Fat content varies by season — boars shot in autumn carry more fat than those taken in spring. The meat benefits from brief marinades in oil, herbs, and acidic components to tenderise.

Target internal temperature: **70–72 °C (158–162 °F)** for safe consumption of wild-caught pork. Some grillmasters pull at 65 °C for a pink interior.

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Grilled Wild Boar Chops (UK tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food Magazine; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/wild-boar-chops-with-apple (fetched reference 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 2 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 wild boar loin chops (approx. 220 g each)
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- Salt and cracked black pepper
- 2 apples, cored and sliced (for serving)

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, and lemon juice. Rub all over chops. Marinate 2 hours or overnight in the fridge.
2. Bring chops to room temperature 30 minutes before grilling. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Set up a two-zone grill: one side high heat, one side medium.
4. Sear chops over high heat 3 minutes per side to form colour.
5. Move to medium heat, cover, and cook 8–12 minutes more until probe reads **70 °C (158 °F)**.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with grilled apple slices and roasted root vegetables.
*Final resting temperature: 72–74 °C (162–165 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Wildschwein vom Grill (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de recipe category; https://www.chefkoch.de/rs/s0/wildschwein+grill/Rezepte.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hour marinade · **Cook**: 25 min

*Adapted from multiple top-rated Chefkoch.de wild boar grill recipes.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 wild boar shoulder chops or loin chops
- 3 tbsp rapeseed oil
- 2 tbsp juniper berries, crushed
- 1 tbsp caraway seeds
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 100 ml dark red wine
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, juniper, caraway, garlic, and wine into a marinade. Coat chops well and marinate 1–2 hours.
2. Build a two-zone charcoal fire. Grill chops over direct heat 3 min per side.
3. Move to indirect zone, cover grill. Cook 15 minutes until probe reads **70 °C (158 °F)**.
4. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with Sauerkraut and bread dumplings (Semmelknödel).
*Final resting temperature: 72–74 °C (162–165 °F).*

### 3. La Cucina Italiana — Costolette di cinghiale alla griglia (Italian tradition)
**Source**: La Cucina Italiana magazine; https://www.lacucinaitaliana.it/ricetta/secondi-di-carne/costolette-di-cinghiale/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinade · **Cook**: 20 min

*Italian grilled wild boar chops with herbs and lemon.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 wild boar chops
- 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 garlic cloves
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Pound the chops lightly to even thickness. Marinate in olive oil, lemon juice, and herbs 1 hour.
2. Preheat grill to high. Season chops with salt and pepper.
3. Grill 5–7 minutes per side, basting with the marinade.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 70 °C (158 °F)**.
5. Rest and serve with a bitter green salad and lemon wedges.
*Final resting temperature: 72–74 °C (162–165 °F).*
