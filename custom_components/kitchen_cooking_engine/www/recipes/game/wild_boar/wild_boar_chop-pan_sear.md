## Cut profile

Wild boar (*Sus scrofa*) loin chops (Wildschwein-Koteletts / côtelettes de sanglier) are taken from the back of the animal and include a rib bone, similar to pork chops but with darker, denser meat and a pronounced game aroma. Fat content varies by season — boars shot in autumn carry more fat than those taken in spring. The meat benefits from brief marinades in oil, herbs, and acidic components to tenderise.

Pan-searing wild boar chops over medium-high heat for 6–10 minutes per side is the traditional European approach; the meat should be cooked through to at least 70 °C (160 °F) in line with wild game food-safety guidance from most European health authorities, though some chefs pull at 65–68 °C for a slightly pink centre.

Target internal temperature: **70–72 °C (158–162 °F)** (safe). Some chefs cook to 65 °C (149 °F) for a pale-pink interior.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Wildschweinkoteletts mit Wacholdercreme (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2049741331587566/Wildschweinkoteletts-mit-Wacholdercreme.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 25 min · **Rating**: Top rated

*Translation: Wild boar chops with juniper berry cream sauce.*

**Ingredients**
- 750 g wild boar back (4 chops with bone)
- Salt and pepper (mill-ground)
- Oil for frying
- 8 juniper berries, crushed
- 4 cl gin
- 250 ml game stock (Wildfond)
- 20 g butter (ice-cold)
- 125 ml whipped cream

**Method**
1. Cut the wild boar back into chops if not already done. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil in a heavy pan over high heat. Sear chops on both sides until well coloured.
3. Crush the juniper berries and add to the pan with the chops. Cook both sides for approx. 7 minutes each. Remove chops and keep warm.
4. Deglaze the pan with gin, stirring to lift the fond. Add game stock and reduce by half.
5. Add ice-cold butter cubes and whisk to emulsify the sauce. Fold in whipped cream.
6. Season sauce with salt and pepper. Insert MEATER probe into thickest chop and verify **70–72 °C (158–162 °F)** before serving.
7. Serve with Spätzle or potato dumplings.
*Final resting temperature: 71–73 °C (160–163 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Wildschweinkoteletts auf sardische Art (Sardinian tradition, German recipe)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/656531167378180/Wildschweinkoteletts-auf-sardische-Art.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 40 min · **Cook**: 40 min

*Translation: Wild boar chops Sardinian style — an agrodolce (sweet-sour) preparation with prunes, raisins, dark chocolate, vinegar, and bay.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 wild boar loin chops
- 60 g pitted prunes
- 60 g raisins
- 50 g lean bacon
- 3 tbsp oil
- 40 g dark chocolate (70%+)
- 25 g sugar
- 4 bay leaves
- 50 ml red wine vinegar
- 50 ml Italian red wine
- Salt and pepper
- Pinch of ground cinnamon

**Method**
1. Soak prunes and raisins in warm water for 15 minutes. Drain well.
2. Rinse chops, pat dry, season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. Dice bacon finely. Heat oil in a heavy pan, render bacon, then brown chops 7–10 minutes per side.
4. Remove chops and keep warm. Add soaked fruit, grated chocolate, sugar, bay leaves, vinegar, wine, and cinnamon to the pan.
5. Stir and simmer 10 minutes until sauce thickens. Return chops to the pan and heat through.
6. Insert probe. Check **70–72 °C (158–162 °F)**.
*Final resting temperature: 71–73 °C (160–163 °F).*

### 3. Giallozafferano.it — Costolette di cinghiale alla cacciatora (Italian hunting tradition)
**Source**: Giallozafferano / Sonia Peronaci; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Cinghiale-in-umido.html (fetched 2026-04-24); see also traditional Tuscan game recipe collections.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Translation: Braised wild boar chops hunter style — tomatoes, rosemary, garlic.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 wild boar chops (approx. 200 g each)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 150 ml dry red wine (Chianti preferred)
- 200 g canned peeled tomatoes
- Salt and black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a large pan. Brown chops over high heat 3–4 minutes per side. Remove from pan.
2. In the same oil, gently fry garlic and rosemary 1 minute.
3. Return chops to pan. Add wine, let alcohol evaporate 2 minutes. Add tomatoes, breaking them up.
4. Cover and cook over medium-low heat 20 minutes until chops are cooked through.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 70 °C (158 °F)** for fully cooked (the braising liquid means it will stay moist).
*Final resting temperature: 72–75 °C (162–167 °F).*

| Fully cooked | 72–75 °C (162–167 °F) | Recommended for wild-hunted animals |
