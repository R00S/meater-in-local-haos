## Cut profile

Wild boar shoulder is a large, heavily worked muscle containing significant connective tissue. It benefits from long, slow roasting (3–4 hours) at moderate temperature (160–200 °C) in a braising liquid to achieve pull-apart tenderness. The German tradition uses wine, game stock, juniper, and root vegetables; the Italian and French traditions add tomato and herbs.

Target internal temperature: **80–90 °C (176–194 °F)** for fall-apart texture. Food-safety minimum is 70 °C (158 °F) for safe serving, but the collagen conversion to gelatin that makes the shoulder delicious requires 80+ °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Wildschweinschulter ganz einfach (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3444381513162705/Wildschweinschulter-ganz-einfach.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 3 · **Prep**: 35 min · **Cook**: 3 h 20 min

*Translation: Wild boar shoulder — very simple. A classic German roasting method.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg wild boar shoulder with bone
- 250 ml dry red wine (Merlot)
- 400 ml game stock (Wildfond)
- 1 bunch soup vegetables (carrot, celery root, leek, parsley)
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 juniper berries
- 2 small bay leaves
- 2 onions, quartered
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 250 °C.
2. Rub shoulder all over with oil and salt. Mix soup vegetables with tomato paste and place in a roasting dish; set shoulder on top. Roast at 250 °C for 20 minutes to develop colour and caramelise the vegetables.
3. Reduce oven to 160 °C. Add wine, game stock, juniper berries, bay leaves, and onions to the dish.
4. Cover with foil and braise for 2.5–3 hours, turning once halfway, until probe reads **85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** and meat is pull-apart tender.
5. Remove meat and rest 15 minutes. Strain braising juices, reduce to sauce consistency.
6. Serve with potato dumplings and red cabbage.
*Final resting temperature: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).*

### 2. Giallozafferano.it — Cinghiale in umido (Italian slow-roast tradition)
**Source**: Giallozafferano / Sonia Peronaci; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Cinghiale-in-umido.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 24 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 3 hrs

*Translation: Braised wild boar in the traditional Tuscan style — overnight marinade in wine and herbs, then long-braised in tomato and olives.*

**Ingredients**
*(Marinade, overnight):*
- 1.5 kg wild boar shoulder, cut into large pieces
- 1 bottle dry red wine (Chianti or Montepulciano)
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 2 celery stalks, sliced
- 1 onion, quartered
- 4 bay leaves
- 10 peppercorns
- 4 juniper berries
- 2 sprigs rosemary

*(For cooking):*
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 100 g pancetta or bacon
- 400 g canned peeled tomatoes
- 20 black olives (Taggiasca preferred)
- Salt

**Method**
1. Marinate boar pieces in wine and aromatics overnight (12–24 hours) in the fridge. Drain and pat dry.
2. Brown meat in batches in olive oil over high heat. Remove.
3. Render pancetta in the same pot. Return meat. Add strained marinade wine and tomatoes.
4. Simmer covered at low heat for 2.5–3 hours until probe reads **82–88 °C (180–190 °F)** and meat is very tender.
5. Add olives in the final 20 minutes.
*Final resting temperature: 85–92 °C (185–198 °F).*

### 3. Madensverden.dk — Langtidsstegt vildsvinsskulder (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/vildsvinsskulder-opskrift/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 4 hrs

*Translation: Long-roasted wild boar shoulder, Danish style.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg wild boar shoulder
- 4 garlic cloves, slivered
- 1 tbsp coarse salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 sprigs rosemary
- 200 ml red wine
- 200 ml water

**Method**
1. Score the skin/fat cap and insert garlic slivers into the cuts. Rub with salt, pepper, and rosemary.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Brown the shoulder uncovered for 20 minutes.
3. Reduce to 140 °C. Add wine and water to the roasting dish. Cover with foil.
4. Roast 3–3.5 hours until probe reads **85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
5. Remove foil and return to 200 °C for 15 minutes to crisp the surface. Rest 15 minutes.
*Final resting temperature: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).*
