## Cut profile

Lamb cutlets (thin, Frenched rib chops) are the most refined lamb cut — a single rib bone with the meat formed into a small medallion. They are a bistro classic in France and the UK, a restaurant staple in Australia and New Zealand, and are popular as party food throughout the Middle East and South Asia. Cooking time is very brief — 2–3 minutes per side — making them ideal for a pan with butter basting.

Target: **52–57 °C (126–135 °F)** for medium-rare. They overcook rapidly given their thin cross-section.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madensverden.dk — Lammekotelet (Danish pan-fried lamb cutlets)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/lammekotelet/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb cutlets (Frenched rib chops)
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 garlic clove, smashed
- 1 sprig rosemary
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Season cutlets with salt and pepper. Heat a heavy pan over very high heat.
2. Add butter. When foaming subsides, sear cutlets standing on their fat cap 1 minute, then flat 2 minutes per side.
3. Add garlic and rosemary. Baste with foaming butter 1 minute.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with herb yoghurt and salad.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*

### 2. BBC Good Food — Lamb Cutlets with Herbed Breadcrumbs (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/lamb-cutlets-herbed-breadcrumbs (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 min

*Crusted lamb cutlets — breadcrumb-herb crust applied and pan-fried.*

**Ingredients**
- 8 lamb cutlets
- 100 g fresh breadcrumbs
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix breadcrumbs, parsley, thyme, and lemon zest. Season.
2. Brush cutlets with mustard on both sides. Press into breadcrumb mixture to coat.
3. Heat oil in pan over medium-high heat. Fry 2–3 minutes per side until golden.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** for medium-rare to medium.
5. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with mint sauce and roasted potatoes.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 3. Giallozafferano.it — Costolette di agnello impanate (Italian breaded lamb cutlets)
**Source**: Giallozafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Costolette-di-agnello-impanate.html (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 10 min

*Classic Italian Milanese-style lamb cutlets — egg, breadcrumbs, fried in oil.*

**Ingredients**
- 8 lamb cutlets, gently pounded flat
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 100 g fine breadcrumbs
- 50 g Parmesan, finely grated
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper
- Olive oil for frying

**Method**
1. Mix breadcrumbs with Parmesan and parsley. Season.
2. Dip cutlets in egg, then in breadcrumb mixture, pressing to adhere.
3. Heat olive oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Fry cutlets 3–4 minutes per side until golden.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F)** for medium.
5. Drain on paper. Serve with lemon wedges and a dressed salad.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*
