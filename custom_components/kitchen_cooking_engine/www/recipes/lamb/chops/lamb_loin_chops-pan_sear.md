## Cut profile

Lamb loin chops are the T-bone of lamb — cut from the saddle, containing both the loin and the small section of tenderloin. They are larger and meatier than rib chops, with a characteristic T-shaped bone. In the UK they are sold as "lamb chump chops" (slightly different cut, from the rump). They are pan-seared 4–5 minutes per side to medium.

Target: **57–62 °C (135–144 °F)** for medium (slightly pink). Africanbites.com: 135–140°F (57–60°C).

## Styckesprofil

Lammryggstek (lumbarsnitt, T-ben) är lammets T-ben — innehåller både loin och en liten del av filén. De är större och köttigare än ryggkotletter. I UK säljs de som "lamb chump chops" (något annorlunda snitt, från rumpen). 

Het gjutjärns- eller tung panna, 4–5 minuter per sida, sedan vila. Sond in i den tjockaste loin-muskeldelen borta från benet. Innertemperatur: **57–63 °C (135–145 °F)** för medium-rare till medium. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Basta med rosmarin-vitlökssmör under de sista minuterna.

## Source recipes

### 1. Grilled Lamb Loin Chops (inspired by Africanbites.com)
**Inspired by**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/grilled-lamb-chops/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Applies to loin chop pan sear — recipe note: "For loin chops, anywhere from 135–140°F (57–60°C) is ideal."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr · **Cook**: 12 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb loin chops, approx. 220 g each
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary
- ½ tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes
- Salt and cracked black pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, garlic, rosemary, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Coat chops and marinate 1 hour.
2. Heat a heavy pan over high heat. Sear chops 4–5 minutes per side.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 55–57 °C (131–135 °F)** for medium-rare.
4. Rest 10 minutes. Serve with peppercorn sauce or mint sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 2. Lammekoteletter med rosmarin (Danish pan-seared loin chops) (inspired by Madensverden.dk)
**Inspired by**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/grillede-lammekoteletter/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Adapted for pan method — same marinade.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 6 hrs · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 lamb loin chops, 250 g each
- ½ dl rapeseed oil
- 2 tbsp rosemary
- 3 garlic cloves, pressed
- 1 tsp salt, 30 g butter

**Method**
1. Marinate 6 hours. Bring to room temperature.
2. Heavy pan over high heat. Sear 4–5 minutes per side. Add butter in last 2 minutes; baste.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F)** for medium.
4. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with potato salad and grilled vegetables.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*

### 3. Pan Seared Lamb Chops (inspired by RecipeTinEats.com)
**Inspired by**: RecipeTinEats.com (Nagi Maehashi); https://www.recipetineats.com/pan-seared-lamb-chops/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8 min

*Australian home-cooking approach — simple seasoning, butter basting.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb loin chops (or forequarter chops)
- Salt and pepper
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 30 g butter
- 2 garlic cloves, smashed
- Sprigs of rosemary

**Method**
1. Season chops generously. Heat a heavy pan to smoking hot with oil.
2. Sear 4–5 minutes first side. Flip. Add butter, garlic, and rosemary.
3. Baste continuously with foaming butter 3–4 minutes.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F)**.
5. Rest 3 minutes. Serve with mint sauce and roasted sweet potato.
*Final resting temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*
