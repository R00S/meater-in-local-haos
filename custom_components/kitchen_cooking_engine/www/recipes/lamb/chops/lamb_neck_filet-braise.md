# Lamb Neck Filet × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Lamb neck fillet braised in aromatic liquid becomes remarkably tender. The neck muscles, densely packed with connective tissue and worked continuously in life, require 2–3 hours of gentle moist-heat cooking to convert the collagen to gelatin and render the fibres soft. The resulting braise is intensely flavoured — the gelatin from the collagen enriches the braising liquid into a glossy, body-coating sauce. The fillet takes flavouring particularly well: wine, herbs, aromatics, and stock all penetrate the cylindrical muscle during the long braise. In South African and British traditions, the neck is braised in red wine with carrots, celery, and tomato paste. In Turkish and Middle Eastern traditions, the neck is braised with spices (cinnamon, allspice, cumin) and fresh tomatoes, often over couscous.

## Source recipes

### 1. Simply Delicious (Alida Ryder, Cape Town, South Africa) — Slow Braised Lamb Neck
**Source**: https://simply-delicious-food.com/slow-braised-lamb-neck/  
**Tradition**: South African / French-inspired cooking — **Culinary Group B** *(recipe author: Alida Ryder, South African food blogger based in Cape Town; the recipe is labelled "Cuisine: French" in the recipe card but is a South African home cook's interpretation)*  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Total**: 3 hr 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 lb (1 kg) lamb neck on the bone, sliced
- 2 tsp salt
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 medium carrots, peeled and finely chopped
- 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, grated/minced
- 8 thyme sprigs
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 cup (240 ml) red wine (Cabernet Sauvignon or full-bodied red)
- 3 cups (720 ml) beef stock (or lamb stock if available)

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 320°F / 160°C.
2. Pat the lamb dry with paper towels. Season generously with salt on both sides.
3. Brown in a hot, deep pan set over medium-high heat. Remove and set aside.
4. In the same pan, sauté the vegetables, garlic, and thyme until they begin to brown.
5. Stir in the tomato paste; deglaze the pan with the red wine.
6. Pour in the stock.
7. Add the lamb necks back in; season with salt and pepper. Cover and place in the preheated oven to braise for 2–2.5 hours, or until the lamb is fork tender. Check hourly; top up stock if needed — the lamb should stay covered.
8. Optional: remove the lid and increase heat to 400°F / 200°C for 10–15 minutes until the sauce has reduced slightly and the lamb is caramelised.
9. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with side dishes.

**Temperature note**: No explicit internal temperature given; recipe relies on timing and fork-tenderness. After 2–2.5 hours braising at 160°C, internal temperature will be well above 85°C / 185°F.
