## Cut profile

Grilling is the only traditional method for kofta — it is a strictly fire-based preparation across all the traditions that created it. High, direct heat (charcoal preferred; gas acceptable) chars the exterior in 3–4 minutes per side, rendering the fat and creating the distinctive smoky-char crust that defines kofta's flavour. The fat renders so quickly on the grill that the kofta self-bastes continuously.

The skewer placement means the MEATER probe must be inserted lengthwise through one end of the kofta, through the skewer channel, into the centre. Alternatively, probe the thickest kofta without the skewer in place before serving. Target: 71 °C (160 °F). Carryover: 3–4 °C. Pull at 66–67 °C to rest to 71 °C.

## Styckesprofil

Grillning är den enda traditionella metoden för köfte — det är en strikt eldsbaserad beredning i alla traditioner som skapade den. Hög direkt värme (kol föredraget) förkolnar utsidan på 3–4 minuter per sida, smälter ned fettet och skapar den karakteristiska rökhaltiga kolskorpan.

Det enda säkra sättet att verifiera genomkokning är med sond. Innertemperatur: **72–75 °C (162–167 °F)** för malet lamm. Eftertillagning: 2–3 °C. Sond insatt längsvis från änden in till mitten av köfte-spettet. Vila 3–5 minuter täckt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Authentic Turkish Köfte (inspired by TurkishFoodTravel.com)
**Inspired by**: TurkishFoodTravel.com; https://www.turkishfoodtravel.com/2025/02/26/turkish-kofte-meatballs/ (web search 2026-04-24). *"Turkish Meatballs/Köfte: How to Make Authentic Turkish Köfte"*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 hrs + overnight rest · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg ground lamb (or 70% beef / 30% lamb mixture)
- 150 g breadcrumbs (fine, from stale bread)
- 1 large onion, grated (juice squeezed out to prevent wetness)
- 1 tsp baking soda + 1 tbsp lemon juice (activated)
- 1–1.5 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp paprika (optional)
- 1 tsp cumin (optional)

**Method**
1. Process stale bread into fine breadcrumbs. Mix baking soda with lemon juice; let foam.
2. Combine ground lamb, breadcrumbs, activated baking soda/lemon, salt, pepper, paprika, and cumin in a large bowl. Knead 4–5 minutes until fully incorporated. Throw the mix against the bowl a few times to bind.
3. Cover and refrigerate overnight or at least 2 hours for flavours to develop.
4. Before shaping, fold in the grated, squeezed onion.
5. Take portions, roll into thick finger shapes or ovals. Flatten slightly for better grill contact.
6. Grill over hot charcoal or gas 4–5 minutes per side until browned through.
7. Insert probe into the thickest kofta. **Verify 74 °C (165 °F)**.
8. Serve with flatbread, grilled peppers, tomatoes, and yoghurt.
*Final temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*

### 2. Adana Kebabı (Turkish kofta on skewer) (inspired by Nefisyemektarifleri.com)
**Inspired by**: Nefisyemektarifleri.com; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/adana-kebabi/ (web search 2026-04-24). *Adana kebab — Turkey's most famous grilled kofta, from Adana province.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr · **Cook**: 20 min

*Translation: Adana Kebab — ground lamb with tail fat (kuyruk yağı), red pepper, and paprika, shaped on wide flat metal skewers and grilled over charcoal.*

**Ingredients**
- 500 g ground lamb shoulder (coarsely minced)
- 100 g lamb tail fat (kuyruk yağı), very finely chopped; or 50 g suet
- 2 tsp red pepper paste (aci biber salçasi)
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp cumin
- 1 medium onion, grated (squeezed dry)

**Method**
1. Mix all ingredients by hand, kneading 5 minutes until very smooth and cohesive. The fat helps the kofta bind and stay juicy.
2. Refrigerate 1 hour to firm up.
3. Thread firmly onto wide, flat metal skewers, pressing into a long sausage shape.
4. Grill over hot charcoal, turning frequently, 4–6 minutes per side.
5. Insert probe. **Verify 74 °C (165 °F)**.
6. Serve with flatbread, grilled tomatoes and pepper, and sliced onion with sumac.
*Final temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*

### 3. Seekh Kebab (inspired by IndianHealthyRecipes.com)
**Inspired by**: IndianHealthyRecipes.com (Swasthi Shreekanth); https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/seekh-kebab-recipe/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 15 min

*Seekh kebab — similar to Turkish kofta but spiced with South Asian aromatics: garam masala, chaat masala, ginger.*

**Ingredients**
- 500 g ground lamb
- 1 tsp ginger paste
- 1 tsp garlic paste
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- ½ tsp chaat masala
- ½ tsp red chilli powder
- 2 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh mint, chopped
- 1 tbsp roasted chickpea flour (besan, for binding)
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Mix all ingredients thoroughly. Knead 3–4 minutes. Chill 30 minutes.
2. Thread firmly onto pre-soaked wooden or metal skewers.
3. Grill or cook in a tandoor/oven at 220 °C for 12–15 minutes, turning twice.
4. Insert probe into thickest kebab. **Verify 74 °C (165 °F)**.
5. Serve with mint chutney, sliced onion with lemon, and naan.
*Final temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*
