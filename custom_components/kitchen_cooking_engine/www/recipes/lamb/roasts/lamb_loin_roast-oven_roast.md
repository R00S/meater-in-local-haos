# Lamb Loin Roast × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Lamb loin roast is the boneless, cylindrical muscle removed from the saddle (the section between the last rib and the hip). It is the lamb equivalent of a beef tenderloin in terms of tenderness — lean, fine-grained, low in connective tissue, and quick-cooking. Because it lacks the fat deposits and collagen of tougher cuts, it cannot be slow-cooked or braised without becoming dry. It must be roasted quickly at high heat (or moderate heat with a prior sear) to medium-rare or medium, then rested before slicing. Carryover cooking is modest (3–5°C) due to the low fat content. Sold boneless and often rolled and tied; sometimes sold bone-in as part of a saddle.

**Research note:** The whole boneless lamb loin roast is primarily a British, French, and Australasian preparation. It is not a standard whole-cut preparation in South/East/SE Asian or African/Middle Eastern culinary traditions — those traditions typically cut the loin into chops, use it in curries/stews, or prepare the whole saddle differently. Genuine recipe sources for Group A and Group C could not be found for this cut and method after targeted searches across South Asian, East Asian, SE Asian, Moroccan, Lebanese, and Turkish recipe sites.

## Source recipes

### 1. Paul Heathcote / Great British Chefs — Best-end of Lamb 'Provençale herbs'
**Source**: https://www.greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/lamb-loin-recipe-provencale-herbs  
**Author**: Paul Heathcote (two Michelin stars) · **Published**: 2015  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 lamb loin, on the bone with fat removed
- 1 egg white
- 1 garlic clove
- 1 sprig fresh thyme
- 1 sprig rosemary
- 30 g fresh parsley
- 2 slices white bread
- Olive oil, to bind
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Blitz garlic, thyme, rosemary, parsley, and white bread in a food processor to a fine, crumbly green mixture. Slowly add olive oil until the mixture is slightly runny.
2. Season the lamb loin with sea salt and freshly milled pepper. Dip in egg white, roll in the herb crumbs and press firmly so the crust adheres.
3. Place lamb on a lightly greased baking tray. Insert probe into the thickest part of the loin, avoiding the bone.
4. Roast at **150–160°C** for **10–12 minutes until pink**.
5. Remove from oven. Rest under foil for 10 minutes. Serve immediately.

**Pull temperature**: Not stated explicitly. Descriptor is "until pink" — consistent with medium-rare at approximately **54–57°C / 130–135°F**.

### 2. Gourmet Traveller (Emma Knowles & Vanessa Austin) — Roast Rolled Lamb Loin with Anchovies and Rosemary
**Source**: https://www.gourmettraveller.com.au/recipe/mains/roast-rolled-lamb-loin-with-anchovies-and-rosemary-12579/  
**Cuisine**: Modern Australian · **Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min + 30 min marinate · **Cook**: 50 min – 1 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 anchovy fillets, coarsely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, coarsely chopped + 1 extra head, halved
- 1 tbsp finely chopped rosemary + 2 extra sprigs
- Finely grated rind of 1 lemon + juice of ½ lemon
- 1 tbsp olive oil + extra for drizzling
- 1 boneless lamb loin, approx. 1.2 kg, fat trimmed
- 2 small fennel bulbs
- 3 Désirée potatoes, scrubbed and cut into wedges
- 1 onion, cut into wedges
- 150 ml chicken stock
- 100 ml dry white wine
- 1½ tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tsp white wine vinegar

**Method**
1. Pound anchovies, garlic, rosemary, and lemon rind with a mortar and pestle to a rough paste. Season with black pepper, stir in olive oil.
2. Place lamb loin flat on a work surface, spread with anchovy paste, roll into a cylinder with rosemary sprigs on top. Secure with kitchen string. Stand at room temperature 30 minutes to marinate.
3. Preheat oven to **200°C**. Cut 1 fennel bulb into wedges. Combine fennel, potatoes, onion, and extra garlic in a roasting pan. Season. Place lamb on top of vegetables. Drizzle with olive oil. Add stock, wine, and half the lemon juice to the pan.
4. Insert probe lengthwise into the centre of the rolled loin.
5. Roast until cooked to your liking: **50 minutes to 1 hour for medium-rare**.
6. **Pull when probe reads 54–58°C / 130–136°F** for medium-rare. Recipe states: "internal temperature reads 54°C–58°C on a meat thermometer."
7. Cover with foil and rest 15 minutes. Discard string.
8. Meanwhile, shave remaining fennel on a mandolin, crisp in iced water 10–15 minutes. Dress with oil, vinegar, and remaining lemon juice. Serve with sliced lamb and pan vegetables.

**Final serving temperature**: **57–63°C / 135–145°F** (after 15 min rest from 54–58°C pull).

### 3. ICA — Lammstek med rosmarin (Rosemary Lamb Roast)
**Source**: https://www.ica.se/recept/lammstek-med-rosmarin-727828/  
**Cuisine**: Swedish · **Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1 hr · **Rest**: 10 min

**Note**: ICA labels this *lammstek* (lamb roast) — the recipe uses a 1 kg *urbenad lammstek* (boneless lamb roast), consistent with a boneless loin roast or part of the saddle, tied with kitchen string.

**Ingredients**
- ~1 kg boneless lamb roast (*urbenad lammstek*)
- 4 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 4 sprigs sage
- 3 sprigs rosemary
- 2 tsp flaky sea salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
- Salt and olive oil to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **150°C**.
2. Pick and roughly chop sage and rosemary. Mix with garlic, 2 tsp flaky salt, and ½ tsp pepper.
3. Remove any netting from the lamb. Open the roast flat, massage the herb-garlic mixture all over the inside. Fold closed and tie with butcher's twine.
4. Season the outside with salt and pepper all over. Sear in rapeseed oil in a frying pan until golden all over.
5. Place on an oven rack with a drip tray underneath. Insert probe into the geometric centre of the roast.
6. Roast in the middle of the oven approximately 1 hour.
7. **Pull at 63°C / 145°F**. Recipe states: "tills innertemperaturen är 63°C" (until internal temperature is 63°C). This corresponds to medium (USDA safe threshold).
8. Cover with foil and rest 10 minutes. Remove twine, slice, and serve drizzled with olive oil and flaky sea salt.

**Final serving temperature**: ~65–68°C / 149–155°F (after 10 min rest from 63°C pull). Corresponds to medium to medium-well.
