## Cut profile

Lamb neck (Lammhals / collo di agnello / kuzu gerdan / lammehals) is one of the most underappreciated braising cuts. It is richly marbled, heavily worked, and packed with connective tissue and deep flavour. Cut into cross-sections or braised as a whole neck, it softens magnificently over 1.5–2.5 hours of moist heat, yielding deeply tender, self-basting meat in a rich, unctuous braising liquid. The neck is used in braises throughout Turkish, Italian, German, Greek, and Scandinavian traditions. A MEATER probe is inserted into the thickest cross-section of neck meat, away from the vertebrae, to track internal temperature.

Target internal temperature: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for fall-off-the-bone oven tenderness.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Fırında Kuzu Gerdan (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-sahane-lezzeti-ile-kuzu-gerdan-tarifi/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Fırında Şahane Lezzeti ile Kuzu Gerdan — Turkish oven-roasted lamb neck with garlic and herb vegetables."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 15 min covered + 20 min uncovered at 250 °C

**Ingredients**
- 10–12 lamb neck pieces (kuzu gerdan), bone-in
- 50 g butter
- 5 tbsp olive oil
- 1 whole head of garlic, cloves separated
- 20 small onions, peeled
- 20 small potatoes, peeled
- 15 cherry tomatoes
- 1.5 tbsp sweet red pepper paste (tatlı biber salçası)
- 2 tsp dried mountain thyme (kekik)
- 200 ml hot water
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Heat butter and olive oil in a large ovenproof pot or casserole over high heat. Sear the neck pieces on all sides until well browned, about 3–4 minutes per side.
2. Add garlic, onions, and potatoes around the meat; sauté briefly until lightly coloured.
3. Scatter cherry tomatoes over everything. Mix red pepper paste with hot water; pour over the meat and vegetables. Season with thyme and salt.
4. Cover the pot tightly and transfer to the oven at 250 °C (480 °F). Roast covered for **1 hour 15 minutes**.
5. Remove lid; continue roasting a further 15–20 minutes to brown the top.
6. Insert MEATER probe before going into the oven. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat is fall-off-the-bone tender.
7. **Rest 10 min** before serving with flatbread and yoghurt.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

### 2. Argiro.gr — Αρνάκι στο ταψί με λαχανικά και βότανα (Greek tradition)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/arnaki-sto-tapsi-me-laxanika-kai-votana/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Αρνάκι στο ταψί με λαχανικά και βότανα — Greek lamb tray-bake with vegetables and herbs."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2 hr covered + 25 min uncovered at 170–200 °C

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg lamb neck pieces or bone-in shoulder
- 2 onions, roughly chopped
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 4 medium potatoes, halved
- 50 g butter
- 200 ml dry white wine
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Fresh thyme, oregano, rosemary
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Rub lamb pieces with mustard, salt, pepper, and herbs.
2. Heat butter and olive oil in a large roasting dish on the hob. Brown lamb pieces well on all sides, about 4 minutes per side.
3. Arrange potatoes, onions, garlic, and peppers in the tray around the lamb.
4. Pour wine over everything. Season vegetables.
5. Cover tightly with foil or a lid and roast at 170 °C (340 °F) for **2 hours**.
6. Remove cover; increase to 200 °C (390 °F) and roast a further 25 minutes until golden.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest piece. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
8. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with crusty bread.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

### 3. Blog GialloZafferano — Collo di agnello stufato nel coccio (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Blog GialloZafferano (Tentazioni Dolci e Salate); https://blog.giallozafferano.it/tentazionidolciesalate/ricetta-collo-di-agnello-stufato-nel-coccio/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Collo di agnello stufato nel coccio — Italian lamb neck braised in a clay pot in the oven."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1 hr at 200 °C

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck pieces
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, sliced
- 150 ml rosé wine
- Fresh rosemary and sage
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 carrot, diced
- 1 celery stalk, diced
- 3–4 cloves
- 4–5 juniper berries
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 400 g crushed plum tomatoes
- 1 dried chili
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Brown lamb neck pieces on all sides in olive oil in a clay pot or casserole over medium-high heat.
2. Add onion, garlic, carrot, celery, cloves, juniper berries, bay leaf, rosemary, sage, and chili.
3. Pour in wine; allow to sizzle. Add crushed tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Bring to the boil on the hob. Cover and transfer to oven at 200 °C (390 °F). Braise **1 hour**.
5. Insert MEATER probe before going into the oven. **Pull at 85–87 °C (185–189 °F)** when meat is completely tender.
6. **Rest 10 min**. Serve over polenta or with rustic bread.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

### 4. Chefkoch.de — Geschmorter Lammhals (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2358101374724165/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Geschmorter Lammhals — German oven-braised lamb neck in red wine."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 hr at 200 °C (fan 180 °C)

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg lamb neck, cut into thick slices
- 6 large onions, coarsely chopped
- 5 carrots, roughly chopped
- 10 bay leaves
- Fresh thyme sprigs
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 200 ml dry red wine
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (fan 180 °C).
2. Heat olive oil in a large ovenproof casserole. Sear neck slices on all sides until deeply browned.
3. Remove meat. Sauté onions, carrots, and garlic in the same pot until beginning to colour.
4. Return lamb. Tuck bay leaves and thyme among the pieces. Pour in red wine.
5. Cover tightly. Transfer to oven and braise for **2 hours**.
6. Insert MEATER probe before going into the oven. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with Semmelknödel or roasted potatoes.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA lamb minimum | 63 °C (145 °F) | Not relevant for oven-braised neck |
| Approaching tender | 80–82 °C (176–180 °F) | Connective tissue still converting |
| Fall-off-the-bone | 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) | Target for all oven-roasted neck traditions |
