## Cut profile

Lamb neck is an excellent slow cooker cut. Its dense marbling, connective tissue, and vertebral bone all benefit from 7–8 hours of LOW moist heat — the neck becomes gelatinous and richly flavoured, surrendering all toughness by the time the long cook ends. Traditional clay-pot and covered-pot braises from Turkey, Italy, and Greece are natural slow-cooker analogues, requiring little adaptation beyond time conversion. The meat comes off the bone in large, tender flakes. The MEATER probe is inserted through the slow cooker lid seal or placed before the lid goes on, deep into the thickest neck piece, away from the vertebrae.

Target internal temperature: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for full fall-off-the-bone tenderness.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Fırında Kuzu Gerdan (slow cooker adaptation) (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-sahane-lezzeti-ile-kuzu-gerdan-tarifi/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Fırında Kuzu Gerdan — Turkish oven-braised lamb neck, adapted here for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7–8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe is an oven-covered braise at 250 °C for 1 hr 15 min. The slow cooker produces an identical result at LOW for 7–8 hours.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck pieces (kuzu gerdan), bone-in
- 50 g butter
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 1 head of garlic, cloves separated
- 10 small onions, peeled
- 10 small potatoes, peeled
- 8 cherry tomatoes
- 1.5 tbsp sweet red pepper paste
- 1 tsp dried thyme (kekik)
- 150 ml hot water
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Heat butter and olive oil in a large frying pan over high heat. Sear neck pieces on all sides until well browned, 3–4 minutes per side.
2. Transfer to slow cooker. Add garlic, onions, and potatoes around the meat.
3. Scatter cherry tomatoes on top. Mix red pepper paste with hot water; pour over everything. Season with thyme and salt.
4. Cook on LOW for **7–8 hours** until meat is tender and falling from the bone.
5. Insert MEATER probe before starting. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
6. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with flatbread or rice pilav.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Lammhaxen aus dem Slowcooker (neck adaptation) (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/4089241641380759/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Lammhaxen aus dem Slowcooker — German slow-cooker lamb, adapted here for lamb neck pieces."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 5–6 hr (LOW)

*Note: The original recipe uses lamb shanks. Lamb neck pieces cook identically at the same times.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg lamb neck, cut into thick pieces
- 3 carrots, roughly chopped
- 5 garlic cloves, halved
- 1 large onion, roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 400 ml lamb or veal stock
- 400 ml dry red wine
- 100 ml water
- Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Season neck pieces with salt and pepper. Brown in oil over high heat, 3–4 minutes per side.
2. Sauté carrots, onion, and garlic in the same pan until beginning to colour.
3. Add tomato paste; stir 1 minute. Deglaze with 200 ml of the wine.
4. Transfer vegetables and liquid to slow cooker. Add neck pieces. Pour in remaining wine, stock, and water. Tuck in rosemary and thyme.
5. Cook on LOW for **5–6 hours**.
6. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
7. Strain and reduce cooking liquid to a sauce. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with mashed potatoes.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

### 3. Blog GialloZafferano — Collo di agnello stufato nel coccio (slow cooker adaptation) (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Blog GialloZafferano (Tentazioni Dolci e Salate); https://blog.giallozafferano.it/tentazionidolciesalate/ricetta-collo-di-agnello-stufato-nel-coccio/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Collo di agnello stufato nel coccio — Italian clay-pot lamb neck stew, adapted for slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original braises in a sealed clay pot (coccio) in the oven at 200 °C for 1 hour. Slow cooker on LOW for 7 hours produces equivalent results.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck pieces (collo di agnello)
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, sliced
- 150 ml rosé wine
- Fresh rosemary and sage
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 carrot, diced
- 1 celery stalk, diced
- 3–4 cloves (chiodi di garofano)
- 4 juniper berries
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 400 g crushed plum tomatoes
- 1 dried chili pepper
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Brown lamb neck pieces on all sides in olive oil over high heat.
2. Transfer to slow cooker. Add all vegetables, herbs, spices, tomatoes, and wine.
3. Stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Cook on LOW for **7 hours**.
5. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–87 °C (185–189 °F)**.
6. **Rest 10 min**. Serve over creamy polenta.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

### 4. Argiro.gr — Αρνάκι στο ταψί (slow cooker adaptation) (Greek tradition)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/arnaki-sto-tapsi-me-laxanika-kai-votana/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Αρνάκι στο ταψί με λαχανικά και βότανα — Greek lamb tray-bake, adapted for slow cooker with neck pieces."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7–8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: original is a covered oven bake at 170 °C for 2 hours. Slow cooker LOW 7–8 hours replicates this easily.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck pieces
- 2 onions, roughly chopped
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 3 medium potatoes, halved
- 50 g butter
- 150 ml dry white wine
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Fresh thyme and oregano
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Rub neck pieces with mustard, salt, pepper, and herbs. Brown on all sides in butter and olive oil.
2. Place vegetables in slow cooker. Lay neck pieces on top. Pour wine over.
3. Cook on LOW for **7–8 hours**.
4. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
5. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with crusty bread.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

| Fall-off-the-bone | 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) | Target for all slow-cooker neck traditions |
