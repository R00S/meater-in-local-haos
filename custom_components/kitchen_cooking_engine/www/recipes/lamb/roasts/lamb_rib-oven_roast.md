# Lamb Rib × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

⚠️ **STATUS: COLLECTION IN PROGRESS** — 2 of 4–6 required source recipes collected (Group B + Group C). Needs Group A (e.g. Japanese/Korean spiced rack of lamb) and Group D (e.g. Scandinavian juniper-crusted rack of lamb) before Temperature consensus and Data applied sections can be written.

## Cut profile

Lamb rib covers two distinct but related cuts: (1) **rack of lamb** — the French-trimmed rib section (bones 6–13), sold as a full rack of 6–8 bones or as individual rib chops (lollipops); and (2) **lamb spare ribs / breast ribs** — the lower, fattier rib section. The temperature targets in this cut file (medium-rare at 54 °C, rare at 49 °C) are appropriate for rack-style rib cuts cooked like a steak, not for the lower spare rib which requires long collagen-breakdown cooking.

**Rack of lamb anatomy**: the rack is a curved slab of six to eight rib bones with the eye of the rib (longissimus dorsi) and a cap of fat on the outer face. When French-trimmed (frenched), the rib bones are cleaned of meat and fat for presentation. The eye is a tender muscle — little connective tissue — so short high-heat roasting is the primary method. Each full rack (750–900 g) serves 2–3 people; individual chops (80–120 g) cook faster.

**Temperature target**: medium-rare at 52–57 °C / 125–135 °F (target 54 °C / 130 °F). Dominic Chapman (GBC): "medium-rare finish" for a whole rack at 180 °C in 10 minutes after pan-searing. This cut is analogous to a prime beef rib-eye in temperature sensitivity — fast carryover, immediate rest required.

**Probe placement**: for a whole rack, insert the probe from one end, running the tip through the centre of the eye-of-rib, parallel to the rib bones. For individual chops, insert from the side of the chop, tip at the centre of the eye, keeping the probe clear of the bone. Do not insert through the fat cap as the fat layer will give a misleadingly low reading.

## Styckesprofil

Lammrev täcker två distinkta men relaterade snitt: (1) **rack of lamb** — den frenserade rev-sektionen (ben 6–13), såld som ett komplett rack på 6–8 ben eller som individuella revkotletter (lollipops); och (2) **lammspjäll / bröstrev** — den nedre, fetare rev-sektionen. Dessa snitt har dramatiskt olika tillagningstider.

Rack: 200–220 °C, 15–25 minuter, innertemperatur **57–63 °C** för medium-rare. Bröstrev: 150 °C, 2–3 timmar, innertemperatur **88–95 °C**. Sond i tjockaste loin-muskeln borta från benet (rack); eller i tjockaste köttmassan borta från revbenslben (bröstrev).

## Source recipes

### 1. Dominic Chapman — Roast Rack of Lamb with Anchovy Butter (British tradition, Culinary Group B)
**Source**: Dominic Chapman, greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/rack-of-lamb-recipe  
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 4 hr (incl. marinating) + 20 min · **Cook**: 15 min total · **Rest**: 5–7 min

**Ingredients** (rack of lamb component)
- 1 rack of lamb, 8 ribs (approximately 750–900 g)
- 50 ml olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 1 tsp thyme, chopped
- 1 tsp rosemary, chopped
- salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Combine olive oil, garlic and herbs; rub over the rack. Marinate in the fridge for a minimum of 4 hours.
2. Remove from fridge 1 hour before cooking. Preheat the oven to 180 °C / gas mark 4.
3. Place a heavy ovenproof pan over medium-high heat. Lay the rack fat-side down; cook 2–4 minutes until the fat cap is crispy and golden brown.
4. Turn to colour evenly on all sides.
5. **Insert MEATER probe from one end of the rack into the eye-of-rib, parallel to the rib bones, tip at the centre of the eye.**
6. If using an ovenproof pan, place directly into the oven; otherwise transfer to a baking tray.
7. Roast at 180 °C for **10 minutes** to achieve a medium-rare finish.
8. **Pull at 52–55 °C (125–131 °F).** Chapman's criterion: "medium-rare finish" for an 8-rib rack at 180 °C in 10 minutes. Carryover in a full rack (large thermal mass) is 2–3 °C.
9. Remove from the oven; spoon over the anchovy butter; rest 5–7 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (rosy, juicy, lightly herb-crusted).

### 2. The Mediterranean Dish — Spiced Lamb Ribs with Pomegranate-Rosemary Glaze (Levant tradition, Culinary Group C)
**Source**: The Mediterranean Dish (Suzy Karadsheh), themediterraneandish.com/lamb-ribs/ (published 2026)  
**Note**: This recipe uses lamb spare ribs / breast ribs (lower rib section) — the fatty cut that benefits from low-and-slow foil-covered cooking. Internal temperature is not stated explicitly; "quite tender, fall-off-the-bone" for lamb ribs corresponds to ≥ 77 °C / 170 °F. This source covers a different preparation from the steak-style rack above and should be considered separately when assessing temperature strategy.  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ optional 24 hr dry brine) · **Cook**: 1 hr 45 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1½–2 lb (680–900 g) lamb spare ribs, sliced into individual ribs
- 1½ tsp kosher salt
- 1½ tsp ras el hanout (or baharat)
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 1 garlic clove
- 1 tsp minced fresh rosemary
- 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp honey
- ½ tsp salt (for glaze)
- Fresh mint and pomegranate arils, to finish

**Method**
1. Season ribs with salt and ras el hanout. Optional: dry brine 24–48 hours uncovered in the fridge.
2. Preheat oven to 350 °F / 175 °C.
3. Set ribs directly on a foil-lined sheet pan. Lay a second sheet of foil on top; crimp and fold edges to seal the packet.
4. **Insert MEATER probe from the thick end of the largest rib, tip at the geometric centre of the meat closest to the bone.**
5. Bake sealed for approximately **1½ hours** until "quite tender."
6. **Pull at 77–82 °C (170–180 °F)** when the meat begins to pull from the bone (Karadsheh: "quite tender"). This is the typical collagen-yielding temperature for lamb breast ribs.
7. Increase heat to 400 °F / 200 °C. Open the packet; pour off juices. Brush ribs with pomegranate-rosemary glaze (combine olive oil, garlic, rosemary, pomegranate molasses, mustard, honey, salt in a small saucepan over medium-low). Return uncovered for 10 minutes; flip; brush remaining glaze; 5–10 minutes more until lacquered and sticky.
8. Scatter fresh mint and pomegranate arils. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (falling-tender, pomegranate-lacquered, Levantine presentation).
