## Cut profile

Lamb rump (also called lamb rump steak or rump roast) is the rear-end, triangular muscle mass — the biceps femoris and associated gluteal muscles — sitting just above the hind leg. Each portion weighs 150–280 g and has a generous cap of fat running across one side that self-bastes the meat during roasting. The rump is moderately worked, making it more flavourful than the loin but still tender enough for quick-roast medium-rare cookery. It is firmer than fillet and benefits from pan-searing before oven-finishing to develop the fat cap and crust.

**Anatomy and structure**: the rump is a compact, tapering muscle with grain running lengthways. The fat cap sits on one broad face and should be scored before cooking to help it render. The meat has modest intramuscular fat — more than loin, less than shoulder — giving a good balance of flavour and tenderness. There are no bones.

**Temperature target**: lamb rump is most often cooked to medium-rare at 52–57 °C / 125–135 °F (target 54 °C / 130 °F). Culinary tradition explicitly targets this range. Richard Corrigan (GBC) gives the most specific published guidance: "55–60 °C when tested with a meat thermometer" for a medium-rare finish. Going beyond 66 °C / 150 °F (well-done) results in a tougher, drier texture.

**Probe placement**: insert from the tapered end of the rump portion, pushing the tip to the geometric centre of the thickest part. The probe should run parallel to the length of the muscle grain, not into the fat cap. If multiple rumps are in the pan, probe the thickest piece.

## Styckesprofil

Lamprumpa (även kallad lamprumpstek) är den bakre, triangulära muskelmassan — *biceps femoris* och tillhörande glutealsmuskler — sittande precis ovanför bakbenet. Varje portion väger 150–280 g och har ett generöst fettlock längs en sida som självbastar köttet under rostning.

Sear fettlocket nedåt i het torr panna tills rendat och gyllene, sedan sear alla sidor. Avsluta i 190–200 °C ugn. Innertemperatur: **57–63 °C (135–145 °F)** för medium-rare till medium. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Dra vid 53–58 °C. Sond insatt horisontellt in till mitten. Vila 5–8 minuter.

## Source recipes

### 1. Lamb Rump with Salsify and Wild Mushrooms (inspired by Richard Corrigan)
**Inspired by**: Richard Corrigan, greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/lamb-rump-salsify-recipe  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 30 min (including sides) · **Rest**: 6–8 min

**Ingredients** (lamb component)
- 4 lamb rumps, 180 g each
- salt, to taste
- 1 knob of unsalted butter
- vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 220 °C / gas mark 8.
2. Season the lamb rumps all over with salt.
3. Heat a dash of vegetable oil in an ovenproof pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the lamb skin-side (fat cap) down; allow the fat to render until golden — approximately 3–4 minutes.
4. Turn and colour evenly on all sides.
5. Add a good knob of butter; allow to foam and baste the lamb.
6. **Insert MEATER probe from the tapered end into the thickest rump, tip at geometric centre, parallel to grain.**
7. Place the pan into the oven and cook for **6–8 minutes** for a medium-rare finish.
8. **Pull at 55–60 °C (131–140 °F).** Corrigan's explicit temperature: "55–60˚C when tested with a meat thermometer."
9. Remove from the oven; rest 6–8 minutes. Final serving temperature: **57–62 °C / 135–144 °F** (rosy throughout, fat cap rendered, rich lamb flavour).

### 2. Rump of Lamb with Baby Gem Lettuce (inspired by Tom Kitchin)
**Inspired by**: Tom Kitchin (The Kitchin, Edinburgh), greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/lamb-rump-recipe  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 15 min (lamb component) · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients** (lamb component)
- 4 lamb rumps, approximately 250 g each
- salt and pepper, to taste
- vegetable oil, for searing

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 200 °C / gas mark 6.
2. Season the lamb rumps all over with salt and pepper.
3. Heat a dash of oil in a heavy ovenproof pan over high heat. Sear the rumps, browning all over — approximately 2–3 minutes total.
4. **Insert MEATER probe from the tapered end into the thickest rump, tip at geometric centre.**
5. Transfer the pan to the oven and cook for **4–6 minutes** to achieve a medium-rare finish.
6. **Pull at 52–55 °C (125–131 °F).** Kitchin's criterion: "medium-rare finish" for 250 g rumps at 200 °C in 4–6 minutes corresponds to this temperature range given the pan residual heat and carryover.
7. Remove and leave to rest for 10 minutes before plating. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (deep pink, juicy, Michelin-quality presentation).

### 3. Herb-Crusted Lamb Rump (inspired by Pierre Koffmann)
**Inspired by**: Pierre Koffmann, greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/lamb-butternut-squash-recipe  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 40 min · **Cook**: 20 min (lamb component) · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients** (lamb component)
- 6 lamb rumps, each 250 g
- 50 ml olive oil
- salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 tsp thyme, 1 tsp rosemary, 1 tsp oregano, 1 tsp parsley, 1 small garlic clove (blitzed to herb crust)
- 80 g breadcrumbs
- Dijon mustard (for basting)

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven to 230 °C / gas mark 8.
2. Season the lamb. Heat olive oil in a roasting pan over high heat; seal the rumps until lightly golden all over.
3. **Insert MEATER probe from the tapered end of the largest rump, tip at geometric centre.**
4. Transfer to the oven and roast for **4–5 minutes**.
5. Remove; baste in juices; spread Dijon mustard over the meaty side and press the herb-breadcrumb crust on top.
6. **Pull at 52–55 °C (125–131 °F).** Koffmann's "medium-rare" for 250 g rumps at 230 °C in 4–5 minutes (high-heat method, faster penetration, crust applied post-roast).
7. Return to oven for 1–2 minutes to set the crust. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (herb-crusted, rosy, classically French).
