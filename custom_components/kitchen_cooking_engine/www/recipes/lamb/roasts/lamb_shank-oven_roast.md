## Cut profile

Lamb shank (the lower leg, hind or fore) is one of the most prized braising cuts in the world. The dense connective tissue, bone marrow, and rich muscle fibres transform through 2–3 hours of moist heat into fall-off-the-bone, gelatinous, deeply flavoured meat. It is the flagship dish of Mediterranean cooking (Italy, Greece, Turkey, France), the Middle East (Persian mahiche), and Australia. The target is **pull-apart tender** at 82–88 °C internal — not medium-pink as with steaks. A MEATER probe is inserted into the thickest part of the shank meat (above the bone) to verify doneness.

Target internal temperature: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for fall-off-the-bone tenderness across all traditions.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Fırında Kuzu İncik (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-kuzu-incik-5375085/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Fırında Kuzu İncik — Turkish oven-baked lamb shanks, tandır style."*
**Serves**: 8–10 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: ~3 hr

**Ingredients**
- 8–10 lamb shanks (kuzu incik)
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground allspice
- 1 tsp salt
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 10 small whole onions (for the final stage)

**Method**
1. Sear the lamb shanks in a hot dry pan or with a little oil on all sides until browned, about 3–4 minutes per side.
2. Prepare the marinade: mix minced garlic, bay leaves, thyme, rosemary, black pepper, cumin, allspice, salt, and olive oil into a paste.
3. Coat each seared shank thoroughly with the marinade.
4. Wrap the shanks tightly in parchment paper first, then in aluminium foil. Insert the MEATER probe through the foil/parchment into the thickest part of the meat.
5. Roast at 230 °C (450 °F) for 1 hour.
6. Reduce to 200 °C (390 °F) and roast a further 1 hour.
7. Reduce to 180 °C (355 °F) and roast a further 30 minutes.
8. Add the small onions to the tray, increase to 200 °C, and roast uncovered a final 30–45 minutes until the shanks colour and the onions caramelise.
9. **Pull at 87–90 °C (189–194 °F)** when meat is deeply tender.
10. **Rest 10–15 min**. Serve with bulgur pilav or rice.
*Final temperature: 90–92 °C (194–198 °F).*

### 2. Argiro.gr — Αρνίσιο Κότσι στη Γάστρα (Greek tradition)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/arnisio-kotsi-sti-gastra/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Αρνίσιο Κότσι στη Γάστρα — lamb shank in a sealed clay pot (gastra), a signature Greek method."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 hr 30 min at 180 °C

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks (αρνίσια κότσια)
- ¼ cup dry white wine
- ½ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 onion, quartered
- 2 garlic cloves, sliced
- Fresh thyme sprigs
- 6–8 medium potatoes, peeled and halved
- Salt, black pepper

*To seal the pot:*
- Flour and water (mixed into a stiff dough to seal the gastra lid)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 180 °C (355 °F).
2. Place the lamb shanks in a gastra (clay pot) or a large lidded casserole. Add onion, garlic, and thyme.
3. Arrange potatoes around the shanks. Season generously with salt and pepper.
4. Pour olive oil and wine over everything.
5. Mix flour and a little water into a stiff dough. Use this to seal the lid tightly onto the pot so no steam can escape.
6. Insert MEATER probe through the seal into the thickest shank before sealing completely.
7. Bake at 180 °C for **2 hours 30 minutes**, undisturbed (do not open).
8. Break the dough seal at the table for a dramatic presentation. **Pull at 87–90 °C (189–194 °F)**.
9. **Rest 10 min** before serving with the oven-softened potatoes.
*Final temperature: 90–92 °C (194–198 °F).*

### 3. Giallo Zafferano — Agnello al forno con patate (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Ricette GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Agnello-al-forno-con-patate.html (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Agnello al forno con patate — classic Italian oven lamb with potatoes."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min + 30 min soak · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min at 200 °C

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg bone-in lamb shanks
- 1 kg baby potatoes
- 200 g white onions, thickly sliced
- 1 head garlic, cloves separated (unpeeled)
- 200 ml water + 2 tbsp white wine vinegar (to soak)
- Fresh thyme, rosemary, sage, 3 bay leaves
- 6 juniper berries, crushed
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Soak lamb in water and vinegar for 30 minutes to mellow the gamey notes. Rinse and pat dry.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F).
3. Season lamb with salt, pepper, and chopped herbs. Heat olive oil in a roasting tray; brown shanks on all sides, about 4–5 minutes per side.
4. Add potatoes, onions, garlic, and juniper berries around the shanks. Drizzle with more olive oil. Season the vegetables too.
5. Roast uncovered for **90 minutes**, rotating the shanks and basting every 20 minutes.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest shank. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat is very tender.
7. **Rest 10 min** loosely tented. Serve directly from the tray.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

### 4. Madensverden.dk — Lammeskank i rødvin (oven version) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk (Holger Rørby Madsen); https://madensverden.dk/lammeskank-roedvin-langtidsstegt/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Lammeskank i rødvin — red wine lamb shank, cooked low in the oven as a covered braise."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 2 hr 30 min (oven at 160 °C covered)

*Oven variation: the stovetop version simmers for 2.5 hours; equivalently, the pot can be placed in a 160 °C oven, covered, for the same duration.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 1 bottle dry red wine
- 200 ml veal stock
- 2 shallots, quartered
- 8 garlic cloves
- Fresh thyme and rosemary
- Juice of ½ lemon
- Butter for searing, salt, pepper
- 400 ml cooking juices (for sauce)
- 100 ml cream, 1 beurre manié, 1 tbsp gastrik

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 160 °C. Season and brown shanks in butter on all sides over high heat, 3–4 minutes per side.
2. Add shallots and garlic to the pot; cook 2 minutes. Pour over wine and stock. Squeeze lemon juice over. Add herbs.
3. Cover tightly and transfer to oven. Cook for **2 hours 30 minutes**.
4. Insert MEATER probe before going into oven. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat is completely tender.
5. Remove shanks. Strain and reduce juices; thicken with beurre manié; add gastrik and cream; season.
6. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with boiled or mashed potato.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA lamb minimum | 63 °C (145 °F) | Far too early for oven-braised shanks |
| Tender, still on bone | 80–82 °C (176–180 °F) | Approaching doneness |
| Fall-off-the-bone | 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) | Target for all oven traditions |
| Tandır (very long cook) | 90–92 °C (194–198 °F) | Turkish/Greek sealed-pot method |
