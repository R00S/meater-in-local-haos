## Cut profile

Lamb shank is one of the most naturally suited cuts for the slow cooker. The dense, collagen-rich lower leg requires 6–8 hours on LOW to fully dissolve connective tissue into rich gelatin. The long, moist environment of a slow cooker replicates the effect of a traditional braising pot or clay oven — the method used in Turkish, Greek, Italian, and North African cooking for centuries. The shank is often browned first for depth of flavour, then placed in the slow cooker with wine, stock, and aromatics. The MEATER probe is inserted into the thickest muscle of the shank, above the knuckle end and away from the bone.

Target internal temperature: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for full fall-off-the-bone tenderness.

## Styckesprofil

Lammlägg är ett naturligt lämpat slow cooker-snitt. Den täta, kollagenrika underbenet kräver 6–8 timmar på LÅG för att fullständigt lösa upp bindväven till rik gelatin. Den långa fuktiga miljön i slow cooker replicerar effekten av en traditionell bräseringskruka eller lerugn — lägre temperatur, längre tid, likvärdiga resultat.

Bryn alltid lägget i en het panna med olja och smör innan det läggs i slow cooker för att skapa arom. Innertemperatur: **85–92 °C (185–198 °F)** i slow cooker. Eftertillagning: noll. Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från benet.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Düdüklü Tencerede Kuzu İncik (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/duduklu-tencerede-kuzu-incik/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Düdüklü Tencerede Kuzu İncik — lamb shank in the pressure cooker (adapted for slow cooker)."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 7–8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe uses a pressure cooker for 50 minutes. Adapted to slow cooker LOW for 7–8 hours.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks (kuzu incik)
- 4 medium potatoes, halved
- 1 large onion, sliced into rings
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
- Salt to taste
- 3 bay leaves
- 200 ml water or lamb broth

**Method**
1. Season the lamb shanks with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes.
2. Heat olive oil and butter in a large pan over high heat. Brown shanks on all sides until deeply coloured, 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Transfer browned shanks to the slow cooker. Add sliced onion, garlic, and potatoes.
4. Add bay leaves. Pour over water or broth.
5. Cook on LOW for **7–8 hours** until meat is falling off the bone.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the largest shank. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat separates freely from the bone.
7. **Rest 10 min** before serving with flatbread or rice.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Lammhaxen aus dem Slowcooker (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/4089241641380759/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Lammhaxen aus dem Slowcooker — lamb shanks from the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 5–6 hr (LOW), 3.5 L pot minimum

**Ingredients**
- 2 lamb shanks
- 3 carrots, roughly chopped
- 5 garlic cloves, halved
- 1 large onion, roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 400 ml lamb stock
- 400 ml dry red wine
- 100 ml water
- Fresh rosemary sprigs
- Fresh thyme sprigs
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Pat shanks dry. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil in a large pan over high heat. Brown shanks on all sides, about 4–5 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
3. In the same pan, sauté carrots, onion, and garlic until beginning to colour, 5 minutes.
4. Add tomato paste; stir 1 minute. Deglaze with 200 ml of the red wine; scrape up browned bits.
5. Pour sautéed vegetables and deglazing wine into the slow cooker. Add remaining red wine, stock, and water. Tuck in rosemary and thyme.
6. Cook on LOW for **5–6 hours** until meat is completely tender.
7. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat falls freely from the bone.
8. **Rest 10 min**. Strain and reduce cooking liquid to a sauce. Serve with mashed potatoes.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

### 3. Argiro.gr — Αρνάκι στο ταψί με λαχανικά και βότανα (slow cooker adaptation) (Greek tradition)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/arnaki-sto-tapsi-me-laxanika-kai-votana/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Αρνάκι στο ταψί — Greek lamb tray-bake with vegetables and herbs, adapted for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 6–8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe bakes at 170 °C for 2 hours covered, then 200 °C for 25 minutes uncovered. Adapted here to the slow cooker.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg lamb shanks or bone-in shoulder pieces
- 2 onions, roughly chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 4 medium potatoes, halved
- 50 g butter
- 200 ml dry white wine
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Fresh thyme, oregano, rosemary
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Rub lamb with mustard, salt, pepper, and herbs.
2. Heat butter and olive oil in a large frying pan. Brown lamb on all sides over high heat, about 4 minutes per side.
3. Place potatoes, onions, garlic, and peppers on the base of the slow cooker. Place browned lamb on top.
4. Pour wine over everything.
5. Cook on LOW for **6–8 hours** until completely tender.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest shank. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with crusty bread to mop up the juices.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

### 4. Giallo Zafferano — Agnello al forno con patate (slow cooker adaptation) (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Ricette GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Agnello-al-forno-con-patate.html (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Agnello al forno con patate — Italian oven lamb with potatoes, adapted here for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min + 30 min soak · **Cook**: 7–8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original bakes at 200 °C for 90 minutes. For the slow cooker, the herb-marinated lamb goes in on LOW for 7–8 hours.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg bone-in lamb shanks
- 1 kg baby potatoes
- 200 g white onions, sliced
- 1 head of garlic, cloves separated (unpeeled)
- 200 ml water + 2 tbsp white wine vinegar (to soak lamb)
- Fresh thyme, rosemary, sage, bay leaves
- 6 juniper berries, lightly crushed
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Soak lamb in water and vinegar for 30 minutes to reduce the strong gamey smell. Rinse and pat dry.
2. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs. Brown on all sides in olive oil over high heat.
3. Place onions, garlic, and potatoes in the slow cooker. Layer browned lamb over the vegetables.
4. Tuck in the herbs and juniper berries. Add a splash of water if needed.
5. Cook on LOW for **7–8 hours** until lamb is completely tender.
6. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with bread and a green salad.
*Final temperature: 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).*

### 5. Madensverden.dk — Lammeskank i rødvin, langtidsstegt (slow cooker adaptation) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk (Holger Rørby Madsen); https://madensverden.dk/lammeskank-roedvin-langtidsstegt/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Lammeskank i rødvin — lamb shank in red wine, long-simmered. Adapted for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 7 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original simmers on the stovetop for 2.5 hours. For slow cooker, cook on LOW 6–7 hours.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 1 bottle (750 ml) dry red wine
- 200 ml veal stock (kalvefond)
- 2 shallots, quartered
- 8 garlic cloves, smashed
- Large handful fresh thyme
- Large handful fresh rosemary
- Juice of ½ lemon
- Salt, black pepper
- Butter for searing

**Method**
1. Season shanks with salt and pepper. Brown in butter over high heat, 3–4 minutes per side.
2. Transfer to slow cooker. Add shallots, garlic, thyme, rosemary, and lemon juice.
3. Pour over wine and veal stock.
4. Cook on LOW for **6–7 hours**.
5. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat is completely falling off the bone.
6. Carefully remove shanks. Strain cooking juices; reduce in a saucepan with cream and beurre manié for a sauce.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with boiled potatoes or mashed potato.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*
