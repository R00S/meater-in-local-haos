# Pork Chop × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

A pork chop is a cross-sectional cut of the pork loin taken from the back of the pig between the shoulder and the leg. Depending on position along the loin, it may be a rib chop (one rib bone, mostly loin muscle), loin chop (between two vertebrae, sometimes with tenderloin attached), or sirloin chop (hip end, irregular shape). Bone-in chops have more flavour and better moisture retention. The lean loin muscle dries rapidly above 68°C; modern food-safety guidelines (USDA 2011, Livsmedelsverket) allow a pull temperature of 63°C (145°F) with a 3-minute rest. Carryover is 3–5°C.

Target internal temperatures (grill):
- Juicy/pink: **62–65 °C (144–149 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Svinekoteletter på grill (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/svinekoteletter-paa-grill/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Danish grill chops: 250 g bone-in chops, salted 30 min ahead, 5–6 min each side, baste with BBQ sauce every 1–2 min. Rest 5–10 min.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min salting · **Cook**: 10–12 min grill

*Translation: "How do you best grill your pork chops on a kettle or gas grill? Actually not so difficult."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Grilltiden afhænger selvfølgelig af størrelsen." (Cooking time depends on size.) 5–6 minutes per side with frequent turning. Core 62–65 °C. Rest minimum 5 minutes.

**Ingredients**
- 4 pork chops, 250 g each (bone-in preferred)
- BBQ sauce (bought or homemade)
- Flaked salt (applied 30 min before)
- Oil (to grease grill grate)

**Method**
1. Pat chops dry with kitchen paper.
2. Season with flaked salt generously; rest in fridge **30 minutes**.
3. Preheat grill (gas or charcoal) with direct zone hot.
4. Grease grill grate with oil.
5. Place chops over direct heat. Grill **5–6 minutes** per side, turning every 1–2 minutes.
6. Each time you turn, brush with BBQ sauce.
7. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 63–65 °C (145–149 °F)**.
8. Rest **5–10 minutes**. Do not cover (covering causes steaming).
9. Serve with potato salad or brasede kartofler.
*Final resting temperature: 65–68 °C (149–154 °F).*

### 2. ichkoche.at — Grillkotelett (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/grillkotelett-rezept-234382 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Austrian overnight herb-and-oil marinade (thyme, oregano, estragon, garlic), grill 3–4 min per side. Serve on pre-warmed plates.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: overnight marinade · **Cook**: 6–8 min grill

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 3–4 minutes per side, turn midway; "until nicely coloured and cooked through."

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork chops, 2 cm thick
- Enough olive oil to cover a bowl base
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried tarragon (Estragon)
- Small pinch dried hyssop or extra rosemary
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- Salt and pepper (to taste)

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, dried herbs, garlic, salt, and pepper in a deep bowl.
2. Place chops in the marinade; turn to coat. Cover; refrigerate **overnight** (minimum 2 hours).
3. Preheat grill to high.
4. Place chops on grill grate. Cook **3–4 minutes per side**, turning once halfway.
5. Remove when nicely browned and cooked through.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F)**.
7. Serve on pre-warmed plates with Folienkartoffeln (foil potatoes) and salad.
*Final temperature: 65–68 °C (149–154 °F).*

### 3. Directo al Paladar — Chuleta de cerdo a la plancha (adapted for grill) (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: Directo al Paladar; https://www.directoalpaladar.com/recetas-de-carnes-y-aves/como-hacer-mejores-chuletas-cerdo-a-plancha-jugosas-churruscaditas (fetched 2026-04-24). *The same atemperar + hot surface method applied to the grill: 15 min de-chill, medium-high grill, 1.5–2 min per side for thin chops; 5–6 min per side for thicker bone-in chops.*
**Serves**: 1–2 · **Prep**: 15 min atempering · **Cook**: 3–12 min (depending on thickness)

**Temperature guidance (from recipe adapted)**: "If the chop is cold, or the grill not hot enough, it will steam not sear." Respect temperature of meat and grill surface. Core: 63–65 °C. Salt with flakes after.

**Ingredients**
- 1–2 pork chops (aguja/neck preferred)
- A few drops of olive oil (rubbed on chop)
- Flaked salt (applied after)
- Freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Remove chops from fridge **15 minutes** before grilling. Rub with olive oil.
2. Heat grill to medium-high (direct zone).
3. Place chops. Cook undisturbed:
   - Thin chops (1–1.5 cm): **1.5 min per side**
   - Standard (2 cm): **3–4 min per side**
   - Thick bone-in (3 cm+): **5–6 min per side** with occasional turning
4. Remove. Season with flaked salt and black pepper.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F)**.
6. Rest 2–3 minutes uncovered. Serve.
*Final temperature: 65–68 °C (149–154 °F).*
