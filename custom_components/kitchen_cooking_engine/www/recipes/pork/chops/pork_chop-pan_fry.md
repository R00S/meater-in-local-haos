# Pork Chop × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan-fried pork chop is a classic weeknight dish across Europe. The key is a properly hot pan (butter foaming but not burning), adequate thickness (at least 150–200 g, 2 cm), and salting in advance. Bone-in chops (with rib or shank) retain more flavour and are more forgiving. The chop is fully cooked when it reaches 63–65 °C internally — a light pink blush is safe and desirable. Thinner chops (under 1.5 cm) overcook quickly; thicker ones (3+ cm) benefit from finishing in the oven.

Target internal temperatures (pan fry):
- Juicy/slightly pink: **63–65 °C (145–149 °F)** (safe and recommended)
- Well done: **70–72 °C (158–162 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Pandestegning af koteletter (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/pandestegning-af-koteletter/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Classic Danish pan-fried pork chop in butter with garlic, rosemary, and thyme. "Salt koteletterne 20 minutter før stegning."*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min salting · **Cook**: 8 min + 5 min rest

*Translation: Pan frying a pork chop — "a pork chop tastes best when perfectly fried and tender."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Core temperature 62–65 °C for juicy results. "Koteletter skal have en kernetemperatur på 62–65 grader for at være møre og saftige — og må gerne være let rosa indeni." Total frying time: 4 minutes each side (2 min on high + 2 min on reduced heat).

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork chops, approx. 175 g each
- Generous knob of butter
- 2 garlic cloves (whole, lightly crushed)
- Sprigs of thyme and rosemary
- Salt (applied 20 min ahead) and freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Salt the chops generously on both sides **20 minutes** before frying. This improves tenderness.
2. Heat a pan to high. Add a generous knob of butter; let it foam and settle.
3. Place chops in the pan; press lightly. Fry **2 minutes** without moving.
4. Turn; fry **2 more minutes** on the other side.
5. Reduce heat; add garlic, rosemary, and thyme around the chops.
6. Continuously spoon the fat and herb butter over the chops. Fry **2 more minutes per side** (4 min total per side). Season with pepper.
7. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 62–63 °C (144–145 °F)**.
8. Rest 5 minutes uncovered. Serve with pan sauce, potatoes, and salad.
*Final resting temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*

### 2. ichkoche.at — Schweinskotelett (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/schweinskotelett-rezept-236782 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Austrian pork chop basic recipe: hot pan without fat first, then oil, 2 minutes each side, then butter and herb baste. 269 ratings.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 4–5 min

*Translation: Pork chop — "This recipe is a basic recipe that can of course be extended as you like."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 2 minutes each side at high heat; add butter, herbs, and garlic between the two sides. Pull from pan when golden-brown and aromatic.

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork chops (thick-cut)
- Neutral oil (rapeseed)
- 1–2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 rosemary sprig
- 3–4 thyme sprigs
- 1 tbsp butter
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Heat a pan **without fat** to very hot.
2. Add a little oil; place chops in the pan.
3. Fry without moving for **2 minutes** until a nice golden-brown develops.
4. Turn chops. Fry second side **2 minutes** without moving.
5. Between the 2nd and final sides: add garlic, herbs, and butter. Spoon the melted butter over the chops.
6. Remove chops from the pan; season with salt and pepper. Serve immediately.
7. Insert MEATER probe. **Core should read 63–65 °C (145–149 °F)**.
*Final temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*

### 3. Directo al Paladar — Chuleta de cerdo a la plancha (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: Directo al Paladar; https://www.directoalpaladar.com/recetas-de-carnes-y-aves/como-hacer-mejores-chuletas-cerdo-a-plancha-jugosas-churruscaditas (fetched 2026-04-24). *Spanish method: atemperar 15 min, medium-high heat, 1.5 min per side, salt with flakes after. 51 votes. Recommends aguja (neck/collar) chops for more fat and flavour.*
**Serves**: 1–2 · **Prep**: 15 min atempering · **Cook**: 3–4 min

*Translation: How to make the best pork chops on the griddle — juicy but golden-brown. Key: temperature of both the meat and the pan.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 1.5 minutes per side on medium-high heat. "If the meat is cold or the pan is not hot enough, the chop will steam rather than sear." Rest 1 minute before cutting.

**Ingredients**
- 1–2 pork chops (aguja/neck preferred), 2 cm thick
- A few drops of olive oil (rubbed on the chop)
- Flaked salt (added after cooking)
- Freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Remove chops from fridge **15 minutes** before cooking.
2. Rub chops with a few drops of olive oil — just enough to grease them lightly.
3. Heat a pan or griddle pan on medium-high until well heated.
4. Place chops; cook **1.5 minutes per side**. Do not move during cooking.
5. Remove from heat. Season with flaked salt and black pepper.
6. Rest **1 minute** before cutting.
7. Insert MEATER probe. **Core should read 63–65 °C (145–149 °F)**.
8. Serve with potato fries, salad, or sauerkraut.
*Final temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*
