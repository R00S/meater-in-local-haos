# Pork Shoulder Steak × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork shoulder steak (nakkesteak, Schweinenackensteak / Schopfsteak) is from the neck/collar area — more marbled than loin steak, more forgiving, and ideal for pan frying. The fat renders during cooking, basting the meat from the inside. 3–4 minutes per side on medium-high heat is the standard, and it can withstand a slightly higher final temperature (68–70 °C) without drying out. The Austrian Schopfsteak is an iconic example of this cut.

Target internal temperatures (pan fry):
- Juicy: **65–68 °C (149–154 °F)**
- Well done: **70–72 °C (158–162 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Langtidsstegt nakkesteg som bøffer (adapted for pan-fried shoulder steak) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/langtidsstegt-nakkesteg-boeffer/ (referenced 2026-04-24). *Danish slow-cooked collar steak method — brown first in butter, then slow finish. For a quick pan-only version: high heat 3–4 min per side with butter baste; add stock for pan sauce.*
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Cook**: 8–12 min

**Temperature guidance**: Core 65–68 °C for juicy; 70–72 °C for well done. Neck collar has enough fat to stay juicy even at higher temperatures.

**Ingredients**
- 2–3 pork shoulder/collar steaks (nakkesteaks), 200 g each
- Butter
- Garlic, thyme
- Salt and pepper
- Optional: 100 ml beef stock for sauce

**Method**
1. Pat steaks dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat a heavy pan to high; add butter.
3. Sear steaks **3–4 min per side** on high, turning once. Baste with butter.
4. Reduce heat; add garlic and thyme; **2 more min per side** basting continuously.
5. Remove; deglaze with beef stock if making sauce.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 65–68 °C (149–154 °F)** for juicy.
7. Rest 5 min. Serve with fried potatoes and salad.
*Final temperature: 65–70 °C (149–158 °F).*

### 2. ichkoche.at — Schopfsteak (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/schopfsteak-aus-dem-airfryer-rezept-240872 (referenced — Schopfsteak being the Austrian name for shoulder/neck steak). *Vienna's classic: neck steak with garlic, vinegar, paprika marinade. Sear in hot oil or fat 3–4 min per side.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 6–8 min

**Temperature guidance**: 3–4 min per side at medium-high. Core 65–70 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 2 Schopfsteaks (pork neck steaks)
- 1 tbsp lard, ghee, or oil
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- Salt and pepper
- Optional: paprika powder, caraway seeds

**Method**
1. Season steaks with paprika, garlic, salt, and pepper.
2. Heat pan with fat to high.
3. Sear **3–4 minutes per side** without moving.
4. Reduce heat; continue **2 more min per side** if thick (3 cm+).
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 65–68 °C (149–154 °F)**.
6. Rest 3 min. Serve with Bratkartoffeln and sauerkraut.
*Final temperature: 68–70 °C (154–158 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Nackensteak Schwein Pfanne (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rs/s0/nackensteak+schwein+pfanne/Rezepte.html (referenced 2026-04-24). *German classic pan necksteak: season, sear in cast iron 3–4 min per side, add onion, garlic, herbs for pan sauce.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 8–12 min

**Temperature guidance**: 3–4 min first side; then 5–8 more min at reduced heat with occasional turning. Core 65–70 °C (internal fats render).

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork neck steaks (Nackensteaks), 200 g each
- Oil or lard
- 1 onion, sliced into rings
- 1–2 garlic cloves
- Fresh herbs (optional)
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat cast-iron pan with oil to high.
3. Sear **3–4 minutes** on first side at high heat.
4. Turn; add onion rings and garlic. Reduce heat.
5. Cook **5–8 more minutes**, turning occasionally. Onion caramelises.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 65–68 °C (149–154 °F)** for juicy.
7. Serve with Bratkartoffeln, Pommes frites, or salad.
*Final temperature: 68–70 °C (154–158 °F).*
