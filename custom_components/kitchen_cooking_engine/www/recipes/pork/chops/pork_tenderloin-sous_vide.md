# Pork Tenderloin × Sous Vide — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork tenderloin is an ideal candidate for sous vide: lean meat has no fat buffer so even slight overcooking in a conventional pan is noticeable, whereas a 58 °C bath delivers uniform, perfectly pink results wall-to-wall. Bath time for a whole tenderloin (300–400 g): 1–1.5 hours. After the bath, dry completely and sear 1–2 minutes total for a crust. The Danish method uses BBQ sauce as a finish layer during searing.

Target sous vide bath temperatures (pork tenderloin):
- Pink/juicy: **58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** × 1–1.5 hr
- More done: **63–65 °C (145–149 °F)** × 1–1.5 hr

## Styckesprofil

Fläskfilé är en idealisk kandidat för sous vide: det magra köttet har ingen fettbuffert, varför även lätt överkokning i en konventionell panna märks, medan ett 58 °C bad ger jämna, perfekt rosa resultat från kant till kant.

Sous vide-parametrar: **58–60 °C** i 1–2 timmar (hel filé); sear sedan snabbt i skrikhet panna 1 minut per sida för yta. Innertemperatur: **58–60 °C** med pasteurisering via tid och temperatur. USDA-standard: **63 °C**, 3 minuters vila. Eftertillagning: försumbar (sous vide). Sond insatt längsvis.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Svinemørbrad sous vide (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/svinemoerbrad-sous-vide/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Classic Danish sous vide method: 58 °C × 1.5 hours, then grill or pan finish with BBQ sauce. "A fantastic way to make tenderloin."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 1.5 hr bath + 5 min sear

*Translation: "Set your sous vide to 58 °C. Set the time to 1 hour 30 minutes. This is the perfect way to cook pork tenderloin sous vide."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Tænd din sous vide og indstil temperaturen til 58 grader. Tiden skal indstilles til 1 time og 30 minutter." (58 °C × 1 hr 30 min.) Then brown at high heat with BBQ sauce.

**Ingredients**
- 1 pork tenderloin (silverskin removed)
- Salt and freshly ground pepper
- 2 rosemary sprigs
- 2 thyme sprigs
- 1 portion BBQ sauce (for finishing)

**Method**
1. Remove silverskin carefully. Season tenderloin with salt and pepper.
2. Place rosemary and thyme sprigs alongside the tenderloin. Vacuum-seal.
3. Set sous vide to **58 °C**. Submerge bag; cook **1 hour 30 minutes**.
4. Make BBQ sauce while it cooks.
5. Remove from bag. Pat completely dry — moisture prevents crust.
6. Heat grill or pan to screaming hot. Brown tenderloin quickly on all sides, brushing with BBQ sauce with each turn.
7. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** — it is already cooked, just needs colour.
8. Rest briefly. Slice diagonally; serve with grilled potatoes.
*Final temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*

### 2. ichkoche.at — Schweinefilet sous vide (Austrian tradition, adapted)
**Inspired by**: ichkoche.at / based on Schweinefilet im Speckmantel recipe; https://www.ichkoche.at/schweinefilet-im-speckmantel-rezept-13540 (fetched 2026-04-24). *The Austrian method adapted to sous vide: vacuum the tenderloin with shallots, butter, thyme; 58–60 °C × 1.5 hours; then brown in pan 1 min per side; deglaze with beef stock for sauce.*
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Cook**: 1.5 hr bath + 5 min finish

**Temperature guidance**: 58–60 °C × 1.5 hours, pan-seared finish. Core after sear: 60–63 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 1 pork tenderloin
- 2 shallots, quartered
- 30 g butter
- 2 thyme sprigs
- Salt and pepper
- 100 ml beef stock (for sauce)
- 20 g cold butter (for sauce)

**Method**
1. Season tenderloin with salt and pepper. Place in vacuum bag with shallots, butter, and thyme. Seal.
2. Set sous vide to **60 °C**. Submerge; cook **1.5 hours**.
3. Reserve all bag juices through a sieve.
4. Pat tenderloin dry. Heat pan to screaming hot with a little oil. Sear **1 minute per side** on all sides.
5. Remove; deglaze pan with bag juices + beef stock; reduce.
6. Stir cold butter into the sauce to finish.
7. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F)**.
8. Slice and serve with the shallot sauce and Bratkartoffeln.
*Final temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*

### 3. GialloZafferano blog — Filetto di maiale sous vide (Italian tradition)
**Inspired by**: GialloZafferano Blog; https://blog.giallozafferano.it/ilmattinohaluovoinbocca/la-bistecca-perfetta-sousvide-cottura-sottovuoto/ (referenced 2026-04-24). *Italian sous vide adaptation for pork fillet: 60 °C × 1.5–2 hr, then cast-iron sear 60 seconds per side. No resting needed.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 1.5–2 hr bath + 2 min sear

**Temperature guidance**: "Set temperature to 60 °C" for a rosy, juicy pork tenderloin. 1.5–2 hours. Sear 60 seconds per side in screaming hot cast iron with seed oil.

**Ingredients**
- 1 pork tenderloin
- Coarse salt and black pepper (generous)
- Neutral oil (for searing)
- Optional: rosemary, garlic in bag

**Method**
1. Season tenderloin generously with coarse salt and pepper. Add herbs if using. Vacuum-seal.
2. Set roner to **60 °C**. Submerge; cook **1.5–2 hours**.
3. Remove; pat very dry.
4. Heat cast-iron pan until screaming hot. Add thin layer of seed oil.
5. Sear **60 seconds per side** on all 4 sides.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F)**.
7. No resting needed — juices redistributed in the bath. Slice immediately.
*Final temperature: 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).*
