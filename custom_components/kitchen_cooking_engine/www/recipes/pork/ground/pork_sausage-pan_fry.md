# Pork Sausage × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork sausage is a processed meat product made from finely minced or coarsely ground pork, seasoned with salt, pepper, herbs and spices, and encased in natural pig intestine or synthetic collagen casing. Fat content is typically 20–30%, essential for juiciness and flavour development. Fresh sausages require cooking to a minimum internal temperature of 71°C (160°F); pre-cooked or smoked sausages require only reheating to serving temperature (60–65°C). The casing is the critical monitoring challenge: if it ruptures early, moisture and fat escape and the sausage dries out. Probing from one end into the centre avoids puncturing the casing mid-body. Carryover is 2–3°C.

Target internal temperatures (pan fry):
- Fully cooked/safe: **71–75 °C (160–167 °F)**
- ⚠️ Pork sausage must reach 71 °C minimum

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Sprøde pølser på pande (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/saadan-laver-du-sproede-poelser-som-paa-tankstationen/ (referenced 2026-04-24). *"Crispy sausages on the pan like from the petrol station." Method: foil lining, very low heat × 1 hour, turn a few times. The low heat prevents splitting.*
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Cook**: 45–60 min at low heat

*Translation: "Place a piece of foil on a pan, heat to low, place sausages on top, cook approximately 1 hour at very low heat, turning a few times. This way the sausages become very crispy — just like at the petrol station."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Very low heat; total time ~45–60 min; sausages should not exceed 80 °C internally to avoid bursting. Core must reach 71 °C.

**Method**
1. Place a sheet of foil on a non-stick or stainless pan.
2. Heat to **very low heat**.
3. Place sausages on the foil. Turn every 10–15 minutes.
4. Cook **45–60 minutes** at very low heat. Skin becomes evenly crispy all over.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 72–75 °C (162–167 °F)**.
6. Serve in bread with mustard, ketchup, or remoulade.
*Final temperature: 73–75 °C (163–167 °F).*

### 2. ichkoche.at — Bratwurst in der Pfanne (Austrian tradition, adapted from pork pan methods)
**Source**: ichkoche.at (general pork in Pfanne methodology, referenced 2026-04-24). *Medium heat, roll in pan with butter; add a splash of water and cover midway to steam-cook the interior; uncover for final 3–4 minutes to crisp the skin.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 15 min

**Temperature guidance**: Medium heat; cover with lid for 8 minutes, then uncover for 5 more minutes. Core: 71–75 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 4 pork sausages (Bratwürste)
- 1 tbsp butter or lard
- Splash of water

**Method**
1. Heat pan to medium. Add butter.
2. Place sausages. Brown all around gently — **3–4 minutes total**, rolling frequently.
3. Add a small splash of water; cover with lid. Cook **8 more minutes** on medium-low.
4. Remove lid. Cook **3–4 more minutes** at medium heat, rolling for even browning.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 71–73 °C (160–163 °F)**.
6. Serve with Bratkartoffeln and Sauerkraut.
*Final temperature: 73–75 °C (163–167 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Bratwurst in der Pfanne (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rs/s0/bratwurst+pfanne/Rezepte.html (referenced 2026-04-24). *German standard: medium-high heat, small amount of oil, brown all sides, then reduce and cook through. Total 12–15 min.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 12–15 min

**Temperature guidance**: Medium-high initially; reduce after browning. Core 71–75 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 4 Bratwürste
- 1 tsp neutral oil

**Method**
1. Heat pan to medium-high. Add oil.
2. Place sausages. Brown on one side **3 min**, then roll to the next side every 2–3 min.
3. After all sides browned (~8 min), reduce to medium-low. Cook **5 more minutes**, turning occasionally.
4. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 71–73 °C (160–163 °F)**.
5. Serve in a Brötchen with mustard and onions.
*Final temperature: 73–75 °C (163–167 °F).*
