# Pork Cheek × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Braised pork cheeks are the canonical preparation for the cut — small, fist-sized lozenges of dense jaw muscle that are too collagen-rich to enjoy quickly cooked, but transform under 2–3 hours of gentle wet heat into the most plush, gelatinous bites in the carcass. The cheeks are typically left whole, browned hard, then submerged in red wine, sherry, cider or stock and simmered at 85–92 °C until the connective tissue dissolves and the muscles can be cut with a spoon. The classic Spanish *carrillera al vino tinto* and Italian *guancia brasata* are both built on this technique. Carryover is irrelevant in a braise — the meat sits at simmer temperature until removed.

## Source recipes

### 1. José Pizarro — Carrillera de Cerdo al Vino Tinto (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: José Pizarro, *Spanish Flavours* (Kyle Books, 2012); josepizarro.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks (about 12), trimmed of silver-skin
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 2 large carrots, finely chopped
- 1 leek, white and pale-green only, sliced
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) tomato paste
- 720 ml (3 cups) full-bodied red wine (Rioja or Garnacha)
- 480 ml (2 cups) chicken or veal stock
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 2 bay leaves
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- A pinch of saffron threads
- **To finish**: 30 g chopped flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy oven-proof gratin or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks in batches 2 minutes per side until deeply browned. Remove.
3. Add onion, carrot and leek; sauté 10 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste; cook 2 minutes.
4. Pour in wine; reduce by half (8 minutes). Add stock, cinnamon, bay, thyme, saffron.
5. Return cheeks to the pan; nestle so they are three-quarters submerged. Bring to a simmer.
6. Insert the MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
7. Cover and transfer to the oven. Braise 2–2.5 hours.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Pizarro's test: the cheeks can be cut with a spoon.
9. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Lift cheeks; reduce strained liquid in a saucepan to a glossy sauce. Sprinkle with parsley. Serve with mashed potatoes. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (wine-deep, gelatinous, spoon-tender).

### 2. Marcella Hazan — Guancia di Maiale Brasata al Marsala (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks, trimmed
- 50 g plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 medium carrot, finely chopped
- 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
- 4 sage leaves
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 240 ml (1 cup) dry Marsala
- 480 ml (2 cups) chicken stock
- 1 tin (400 g) plum tomatoes, drained and crushed
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season; dust in flour.
2. Heat olive oil and butter in a heavy oven-proof Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks in batches 2 minutes per side; remove.
3. Add onion, carrot, celery; sauté 8 minutes. Add sage and rosemary; 30 seconds.
4. Pour in Marsala; reduce by half (4 minutes). Add stock and tomatoes.
5. Return cheeks to the pan; nestle three-quarters submerged. Bring to a simmer.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
7. Cover; transfer to oven. Braise 2–2.5 hours.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Hazan's test: the cheek can be cut with a spoon and a fork meets no resistance.
9. Rest 15 minutes. Lift; reduce strained liquid to a glossy sauce. Serve with polenta. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (Marsala-sweet, herbal, spoon-tender).

### 3. Diana Henry — Pork Cheeks Braised in Cider with Apples (British tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015), cheek section; telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks, trimmed
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 2 large onions, sliced
- 2 leeks, white and pale-green only, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 720 ml (3 cups) dry cider
- 480 ml (2 cups) chicken stock
- 30 g Dijon mustard
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh thyme leaves
- 2 bay leaves
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 2 large Bramley or Cox apples, peeled and cut into 8 wedges (added in last 30 minutes)
- 100 ml (½ cup) double cream, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season.
2. Heat oil and butter in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks in batches 2 minutes per side; remove.
3. Add onions and leeks; sauté 10 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
4. Pour in cider; reduce by half (8 minutes). Add stock, mustard, thyme and bay.
5. Return cheeks; nestle three-quarters submerged. Bring to a simmer.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
7. Cover; transfer to oven. Braise 2 hours, then add apple wedges; continue 30 minutes.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Henry's test: the cheek can be cut with a spoon and the apples are just translucent.
9. Lift cheeks. Reduce strained liquid; off heat swirl in cream. Spoon over cheeks. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (cider-sweet, cream-rounded, apple-flecked).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Långbräserade Griskindar i Mörkt Öl (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks (griskind), trimmed
- 30 g cultured unsalted butter
- 30 ml cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced
- 1 large carrot, finely chopped
- 1 small celeriac, peeled and finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 500 ml mörkt öl (dark Nordic ale, e.g. porter or stout)
- 240 ml beef or veal stock
- 30 ml soya sauce
- 30 g brown sugar
- 1 tbsp whole kryddpeppar (allspice berries)
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 4 bay leaves
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- **To finish**: 30 g cold cultured butter, 15 g chopped fresh dill

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season.
2. Heat butter and oil in a heavy oven-proof gryta over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks in batches 2 minutes per side; remove.
3. Add onions, carrot and celeriac; sauté 10 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
4. Pour in dark beer; reduce by one-third (5 minutes). Add stock, soya, brown sugar, allspice, thyme and bay.
5. Return cheeks; nestle three-quarters submerged. Bring to a simmer.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
7. Cover; transfer to oven. Braise 2.5–3 hours.
8. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Nilsson's test: the cheek can be cut with a spoon and the sauce coats a spoon back.
9. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Lift cheeks; reduce strained liquid; off heat swirl in cold butter. Spoon sauce over the cheeks; sprinkle with dill. Serve with kokt potatis. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (beer-deep, allspice-perfumed, glossy).
