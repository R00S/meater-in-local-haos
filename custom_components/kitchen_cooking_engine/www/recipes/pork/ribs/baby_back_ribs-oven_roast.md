# Baby Back Ribs × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Baby back ribs oven-roasted are typically cooked low-and-slow wrapped in foil at 150 °C for 1–2 hours (depending on whether they were pre-boiled), then unwrapped and finished at higher heat with BBQ sauce for a glazed, caramelised crust. The foil phase traps steam and renders the collagen; the open phase drives Maillard browning. Pre-boiling (40–50 min) reduces the oven time but can wash out flavour; the no-boil foil method retains more flavour. Target internal: 88–95 °C for collagen-rendered, fork-tender ribs.

Target internal temperatures (oven roast):
- Tender, glazed: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)**
- ⚠️ Below 80 °C ribs are food-safe but still chewy and tough

## Styckesprofil

Innertemperatur: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)** för fall-från-benet. Eftertillagning: försumbar. Sond längs revbenets riktning in till tjockaste köttmassan borta från benet. Foliepackning: sluten i 1,5 timmar, sedan öppen i 20–30 min vid 220 °C med BBQ-glasering.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Verdens bedste spareribs i ovn (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/verdens-bedste-spareribs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"World's best spareribs — no pre-boiling needed." Foil-wrapped at low heat; then oven-grill finish. The recipe explicitly notes it can be done entirely in the oven.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 1 hr foil + 8–10 min grill element

*Translation: "The trick is to wrap the ribs in foil, where they get to become tender while retaining all the flavour in the packets."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 150 °C oven × 1 hour foil-wrapped; then oven grill element for 8–10 minutes with BBQ sauce. "Keep temperature below 150 °C during foil phase — ribs risk splitting if they go over 80 °C too quickly."

**Ingredients**
- 4 rækker baby back ribs (4 racks)
- **Dry rub**: 1 tbsp paprika, 1 tbsp garlic powder, 1 tbsp dried thyme, 1 tbsp salt, 1 tsp dried chilli, freshly ground pepper
- A little apple juice (for spraying / drizzling)
- 1 portion BBQ marinade (homemade or shop-bought)

**Method**
1. Remove the membrane from the bone side of each rack.
2. Cut each rack into 2–3 even sections.
3. Mix dry rub ingredients. Season all sections generously on both sides.
4. Lay each section on a double layer of foil. Spray or drizzle with a little apple juice. Seal tightly (take care — rib bones are sharp).
5. Place foil packets on a baking tray. Bake in oven at **150 °C × 1 hour**. Turn packets occasionally.
6. Remove from oven; rest 10 minutes in foil.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest section from the side, tip at centre.
8. Unwrap; brush all surfaces with BBQ marinade. Return to oven under the grill element.
9. Grill **8–10 minutes**, brushing each turn. Watch for scorching.
10. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).**
11. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tender, caramelised).

### 2. Madens Verden — Spareribs i grill eller ovn med hjemmelavet marinade (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/spareribs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Pre-boil method: boil 45–50 min, then oven at 220 °C × 20–25 min. Brush with marinade at halfway. 4.8/5, 40 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45 min boil + 25 min oven

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven at 220 °C fan (varmluft), 20–25 min; brush with grillmarinade (BBQ marinade) at 15-min mark. Rack in the centre of oven.

**Ingredients**
- 2 000 g kamben (baby back ribs)
- 3 laurbærblade (bay leaves)
- 1 rødløg (red onion, coarsely chopped)
- 20 sorte peberkorn (black peppercorns)
- 2 tsp salt (for boiling water)
- 1 portion grillmarinade (homemade or shop-bought BBQ marinade)

**Method**
1. Cut racks to fit in a large pot; cover completely with water; add salt, bay leaves, red onion and peppercorns.
2. Simmer at gentle heat **45–50 minutes**. Skim foam in first 5 minutes. Remove; place in a roasting tray.
3. Preheat oven to **220 °C fan (varmluft)**. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
4. Lay ribs on the tray; place in oven centre rack.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest section, tip at centre away from bone.
6. Roast **15 minutes**; then brush all surfaces with marinade on both sides.
7. Continue roasting **5–10 more minutes** until caramelised and glazed.
8. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** Optional: 2 min under direct grill element for extra char.
9. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tender, deeply glazed).

### 3. ichkoche.at — Spare Ribs (Austrian tradition)
**Inspired by**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/spare-ribs-rezept-16908 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Overnight garlic-oil marinade, oven 200 °C × 15 min then 150 °C × 45 min with BBQ sauce basting. 40 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: overnight marinade · **Cook**: 1 hr

*Translation: "Spare ribs — The Grill classic."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Start at 200 °C × 15 min (to set the exterior), then reduce to 150 °C × 45 min, basting several times. "Place a deep tray filled with water on the lower shelf — this prevents drying and catches the fat."

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg Ripperln (baby back or spare ribs)
- 6 garlic cloves, peeled, roughly chopped
- 125 ml olive oil
- Salt and pepper (for overnight marinade)
- **Sauce**: tomato ketchup, vinegar, honey, chilli, mustard, paprika (all to taste)

**Method**
1. Coat ribs in garlic-olive oil marinade; cover and refrigerate overnight.
2. Make sauce: combine ketchup, vinegar, honey, chilli, mustard, paprika in a small pan; heat gently briefly.
3. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Place a deep tray filled with water on the lowest shelf to keep moisture in and catch fat drippings. Oil the grill rack.
4. Lift ribs from marinade; drain; brush with BBQ sauce. Lay on the oiled rack on the middle shelf.
5. Roast at **200 °C × 15 minutes**, then reduce to **150 °C**.
6. Insert MEATER probe from the side, tip at geometric centre of a thick section.
7. Continue roasting at **150 °C × 45 minutes**; turn ribs several times, basting with sauce each turn.
8. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** Ribs should be tender, caramelised, aromatic.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (glazed, fall-off-bone).

### 4. Chefkoch — Spareribs zum Grillen oder für den Backofen (German tradition)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1474841252484744/Spareribs-zum-Grillen-oder-fuer-den-Backofen.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Long low-and-slow foil method at 150 °C × 2.5–3 hrs. Marinade cooked first in a pot. 4.9/5, 420 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 3 hr 30 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 150 °C Ober-/Unterhitze × 2.5–3 hrs foil-wrapped; rest 10 min in foil; then grill/broil for finishing.

**Ingredients**
- 2.5 kg spare ribs
- **Marinade**: 1 cup tomato ketchup (200–250 ml), ½ cup honey, ½ cup apple or orange juice, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 1.5 tsp ground ginger, 2 onions (diced), 3 garlic cloves, 3 dashes Tabasco, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 5 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tsp medium mustard, ½ tsp sweet paprika powder, 2 tsp black pepper, salt, 5 tbsp red wine or apple vinegar

**Method**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a saucepan; stir; bring to a boil and simmer **15 minutes** until slightly reduced. Cool.
2. Wash and dry ribs. Remove thin membrane from the bone side by sliding a spoon tip under it at one end and peeling off.
3. Cut ribs into 3–4 bone sections.
4. Lay each section on double foil; brush generously with marinade on both sides. Wrap tightly in foil. Place on a baking tray, bone side down (so fat travels through the meat).
5. Bake at **150 °C (top/bottom heat) × 2.5–3 hours**.
6. Remove from oven. Rest **10 minutes** in foil.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest section, tip at centre.
8. Unwrap; place on grill rack or broiler. If marinade remains, brush again.
9. Broil/grill until nicely browned and caramelised.
10. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
11. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (fall-off-bone, deeply caramelised).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Madens Verden — no-boil (DK) | 150 °C foil × 1 hr + grill | 88–93 °C |
| Madens Verden — pre-boil (DK) | 220 °C × 20–25 min | 88–93 °C |
| ichkoche.at (AT) | 200 °C × 15 min then 150 °C × 45 min | 88–93 °C |
| Chefkoch.de (DE) | 150 °C foil × 2.5–3 hr + broil | 90–95 °C |

**Consensus pull range**: 88–95 °C (190–203 °F). The low-and-slow foil method (150 °C × 1–3 hr) is the dominant approach across all traditions; higher-temp finish is used for glazing only.
