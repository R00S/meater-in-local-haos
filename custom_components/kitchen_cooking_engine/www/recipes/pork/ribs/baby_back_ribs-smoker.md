# Baby Back Ribs × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Baby back ribs from the smoker are the gold standard of low-and-slow barbecue. The ribs (Schälrippchen / Rippchen vom Loin) are cooked bone-side down in a chamber at 110–120 °C with hardwood smoke (apple, cherry, hickory, beech) for 3–4 hours, optionally wrapped in foil with juice for a steam phase, then unwrapped and glazed. The 3-2-1 method (3 hr smoke → 2 hr steam → 1 hr glaze) and the 2-2-1 method (same but compressed) produce reliably tender, fall-off-bone results. The prolonged low heat renders collagen into gelatin and the smoke provides the characteristic bark. Silver skin removal is mandatory — it prevents smoke penetration and turns rubbery. Target internal: 88–95 °C; at 95 °C the membrane probe easily slides between the bones.

Target internal temperatures (smoker):
- Fall-off-bone tender: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)**
- ⚠️ Below 80 °C ribs are food-safe but collagen is incompletely rendered

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch — Baby Back Ribs (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2528931396356052/Baby-Back-Ribs.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Wood-smoking chips and a smoker required." 4.6/5, 11 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 3 hr rest) · **Cook**: 3 hr 30 min – 4 hr · **Total active**: ~10 min + tending smoker

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoker at 110–120 °C; water tray 3/4 full; smoked wood chips on coals; total 3.5–4 hours. Tip: replace water with apple juice in the water tray.

**Ingredients**
- 4 racks baby back ribs (Spare Ribs / Baby Back Loin)
- 2 tbsp rapeseed oil (Rapsöl)
- **Dry rub**: 2 tbsp sea salt, 2 tbsp raw/brown sugar, 2 tbsp paprika powder, 2 tbsp chilli powder, 2 tbsp onion powder, 2 tbsp black pepper, garlic powder to taste
- BBQ sauce for finishing
- Soaked wood smoking chips (beech / Buche recommended)

**Method**
1. Mix dry rub. Remove silver skin from bone side of each rack.
2. Rub rapeseed oil over ribs; then coat both sides with the dry rub, massaging in well.
3. Refrigerate ribs 3 hours. Remove from fridge 30 min before smoking.
4. Prepare smoker: fill water tray 3/4 full (water or apple juice). Light charcoal; get smoker to **110–120 °C**.
5. Add soaked smoking chips to the coals. Place ribs bone-side down on the grate.
6. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest section, tip at centre away from bone.
7. Smoke at **110–120 °C × 3.5–4 hours**, maintaining temperature and adding chips as needed.
8. In the last 20–30 minutes, brush all surfaces with BBQ sauce; let the glaze set.
9. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Meat should pull back visibly from the bone ends.
10. Rest 10 minutes. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tender, smoky, caramelised).

### 2. Chefkoch — Spareribs 3-2-1 vom Grill oder aus dem Backofen (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3356071498993486/Spareribs-3-2-1-vom-Grill-oder-aus-dem-Backofen.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Classic 3-2-1 method explained in full: 3 hr smoke, 2 hr steam, 1 hr glaze. "Magic Dust" BBQ rub. 4.8/5, 22 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr (+ 12 hr overnight) · **Cook**: 6 hr · **Total**: 19 hr

*Method: the 3-2-1 explanation — "3 hours indirect/oven at 110–120 °C; 2 hours steaming at 140–150 °C; 1 hour glazing at 110–120 °C."*

**Ingredients**
- 6 racks spare ribs (St. Louis cut preferred)
- **Magic Dust dry rub**: 2 tbsp sea salt, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp mustard powder, 2 tbsp chilli powder, 1 tbsp cumin, 1 tsp black pepper, 2 tbsp garlic powder, 1 tsp cayenne, 4 tbsp sweet paprika
- **BBQ glaze**: 70 g ketchup, 70 g BBQ sauce, hot chilli sauce to taste, 35 g honey, 35 g brown sugar, 1 tbsp vinegar, 1 tbsp mustard, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 tsp garlic powder, 0.5 tsp each salt/pepper/cayenne, 30 ml apple juice
- **Steaming liquid**: 200 ml apple juice (or other juice)

**Method**
1. Remove silver skin from bone side of each rack. Massage rub into both sides. Wrap individually in cling film; refrigerate overnight (12 hr minimum).
2. Bring ribs to room temperature ~1 hr before cooking.
3. **Phase 1 — Smoke (3 hr at 110–120 °C)**: Place ribs in a stainless steel rack in a pan; put in grill (indirect) or oven (fan). Maintain 110–120 °C × 3 hours.
4. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest rib section, tip at centre.
5. **Phase 2 — Steam (2 hr at 140–150 °C)**: Pour 200 ml apple juice into the pan; seal tightly with foil (or invert a second pan over the first). Increase heat to 140–150 °C × 2 hours.
6. Meanwhile, combine all glaze ingredients in a saucepan; stir, bring to boil, simmer until thickened. Cool.
7. **Phase 3 — Glaze (1 hr at 110–120 °C)**: Remove foil; reduce heat back to 110–120 °C. Brush generously with glaze. Glaze for 1 hour, basting every 15–20 min.
8. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
9. Rest 10 min. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (fall-off-bone, smoky, deeply glazed).

### 3. ichkoche.at — Perfekte Spareribs (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/perfekte-spareribs-rezept-11835 (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Perfect spareribs so tender they fall off the bone. Our secret: a combination of slow cooking and an irresistible glaze." 895 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 60–90 min indirect heat

*Translation: "Perfect spareribs — tender inside and crispy outside — need some patience. Depending on the thickness, grill at consistent 140–160 °C for at least 1 hour, better 90 minutes with indirect heat."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Indirect heat, 140–160 °C; 60–90 minutes minimum. "When the meat has contracted so that the bone ends are clearly visible and the meat easily detaches from the bone — the spareribs are ready."

**Ingredients**
- 2 racks spare ribs / Rippchen
- Marinade: oil, tomato passata, honey or maple syrup, brown sugar, chilli (powder or fresh or Tabasco), soy sauce or Worcestershire sauce, acid (apple juice, pineapple juice, orange juice, beer or cola) — quantities to taste; or substitute dry rub

**Method**
1. Ask the butcher to remove the silver skin, or do it yourself: score lightly along the bone side, then pull off firmly.
2. Mix marinade ingredients (or prepare dry rub). Massage well into both sides of each rack. Cover and marinate several hours or overnight.
3. Set up grill or smoker for indirect heat. Target 140–160 °C.
4. Place ribs bone-side down in the indirect zone. Close lid.
5. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest section, tip at centre.
6. Cook at **140–160 °C × 60–90 minutes** (indirect heat). Optionally baste with marinade during cooking.
7. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F)** — when bones are visibly exposed and meat separates easily.
8. Tip: serve with finger bowls — "you eat spareribs with your fingers."
9. Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (tender, aromatic).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Temp | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|------|-----------|
| Chefkoch Baby Back Ribs (DE) | Smoker 3.5–4 hr | 110–120 °C | 90–95 °C |
| Chefkoch 3-2-1 (DE) | Smoker 3+2+1 hr | 110–150 °C | 90–95 °C |
| ichkoche.at (AT) | Indirect heat | 140–160 °C | 88–93 °C |

**Consensus pull range**: 88–95 °C (190–203 °F). True smoking at 110–120 °C takes 3.5–6 hours; higher-temp indirect cooking at 140–160 °C can be done in 60–90 minutes but produces less smoke ring. Both converge on the same pull temperature.
