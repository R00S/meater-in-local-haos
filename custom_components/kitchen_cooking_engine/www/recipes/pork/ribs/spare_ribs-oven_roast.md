# Spare Ribs × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Spare ribs (revelsben/kamben in Danish; Spareribs in German/Austrian) are the lower belly ribs, larger and flatter than baby back ribs with more fat and connective tissue. They benefit particularly from low-and-slow oven cooking: either pre-boiled then finished high, or foil-wrapped at low heat then glazed. The higher fat content makes them more forgiving than baby backs. Target internal: 88–95 °C, the same range as baby back ribs — collagen renders at these temperatures giving the characteristic tender, pull-off-bone texture and glossy glaze.

Target internal temperatures (oven roast):
- Fall-off-bone tender: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)**
- ⚠️ Below 80 °C spare ribs are food-safe but still tough

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Spareribs i grill eller ovn (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/spareribs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Pre-boil + high oven method. 4.8/5, 40 ratings. "Classic Danish spare ribs."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45 min boil + 20–25 min oven

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Boil 45–50 min at gentle heat; oven 220 °C fan × 20–25 min; brush with grillmarinade at 15 min mark.

**Ingredients**
- 2 000 g kamben (spare ribs)
- 3 laurbærblade (bay leaves)
- 1 rødløg (red onion, coarsely chopped)
- 20 sorte peberkorn (black peppercorns)
- 2 tsp salt (for boiling water)
- 1 portion grillmarinade (homemade BBQ marinade or shop-bought)

**Method**
1. Cut racks to fit a large pot; cover completely with water.
2. Add salt, bay leaves, red onion and peppercorns. Skim foam in first 5 min; simmer **45–50 minutes** at gentle heat.
3. Remove ribs; drain; place on a baking tray lined with baking paper.
4. Preheat oven to **220 °C fan (varmluft)**.
5. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest section, tip at centre away from bone.
6. Roast **15 minutes**; then brush all surfaces with grillmarinade.
7. Continue **5–10 minutes** until caramelised and glazed.
8. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** Optional: 2 min under grill element for extra char.
9. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tender, deeply glazed).

### 2. Madens Verden — Verdens bedste spareribs (no-boil foil method, Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/verdens-bedste-spareribs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"World's best spare ribs — no pre-boiling." Foil-wrapped at 150 °C × 1 hour, then glazed. 4.8/5, 4 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 1 hr foil + 8–10 min grill

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Foil-wrapped at 150 °C × 1 hour; then oven grill element 8–10 min. "It's important to keep the temperature down — the oven should preferably not go above 150 °C during the foil phase."

**Ingredients**
- 4 rækker spareribs (4 racks)
- **Dry rub**: 1 tbsp paprika, 1 tbsp garlic powder, 1 tbsp dried thyme, 1 tbsp salt, 1 tsp dried chilli, freshly ground pepper
- A little apple juice (for spraying / drizzling inside the foil)
- 1 portion BBQ marinade

**Method**
1. Remove membrane from bone side of each rack. Cut each into 2–3 sections.
2. Mix dry rub; season all sections generously on both sides.
3. Lay each section on a double layer of foil; drizzle with apple juice; seal tightly.
4. Place foil packets on a baking tray. Bake at **150 °C × 1 hour**. Turn packets occasionally.
5. Remove from oven; rest 10 min in foil.
6. Insert MEATER probe from the side, tip at centre.
7. Unwrap; brush with BBQ marinade. Return under oven grill element.
8. Grill **8–10 minutes**, brushing each turn. Watch for scorching.
9. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).**
10. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tender, caramelised).

### 3. ichkoche.at — Spare Ribs (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/spare-ribs-rezept-16908 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Overnight garlic marinade; oven 200 °C × 15 min then 150 °C × 45 min with repeated basting. "The grill classic." 40 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: overnight marinade · **Cook**: 1 hr oven

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Start 200 °C × 15 min (sets exterior), then reduce to 150 °C × 45 min with basting. Place a water-filled deep tray on the lower shelf to prevent drying and catch drippings.

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg Ripperln / spare ribs
- 6 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 125 ml olive oil
- Salt and pepper
- **Sauce**: tomato ketchup, vinegar, honey, chilli, mustard, paprika (to taste)

**Method**
1. Marinate ribs in garlic and olive oil overnight, covered, in the fridge.
2. Make sauce: combine ketchup, vinegar, honey, chilli, mustard, paprika; heat gently and stir.
3. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Put a deep tray of water on the lowest shelf. Oil the middle grill rack.
4. Lift ribs from marinade; drain; brush with BBQ sauce. Lay on oiled rack.
5. Roast at **200 °C × 15 minutes**.
6. Reduce to **150 °C**. Insert MEATER probe from the side, tip at centre.
7. Continue at **150 °C × 45 minutes**; turn ribs several times, basting with sauce each time.
8. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).**
9. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (glazed, fall-off-bone).

### 4. Chefkoch — Spareribs zum Grillen oder für den Backofen (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1474841252484744/Spareribs-zum-Grillen-oder-fuer-den-Backofen.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Marinade cooked first in a pot; foil-wrapped at 150 °C × 2.5–3 hrs. 4.9/5, 420 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 3 hr 30 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 150 °C Ober-/Unterhitze (top/bottom heat) × 2.5–3 hrs foil-wrapped; rest 10 min; then grill finish.

**Ingredients**
- 2.5 kg spare ribs
- **Marinade**: 1 cup (200–250 ml) tomato ketchup, ½ cup honey, ½ cup apple or orange juice, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 1.5 tsp ground ginger, 2 onions diced, 3 garlic cloves, 3 dashes Tabasco, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 5 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tsp mustard, ½ tsp sweet paprika, 2 tsp black pepper, salt, 5 tbsp red wine or apple vinegar

**Method**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a saucepan; bring to boil; simmer 15 min; cool.
2. Remove silver skin from bone side of each rack. Cut into 3–4 bone sections.
3. Brush sections with marinade on both sides; wrap tightly in double foil; place bone-side down on a tray.
4. Bake at **150 °C (top/bottom heat) × 2.5–3 hours**.
5. Remove; rest **10 min** in foil. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest section, tip at centre.
6. Unwrap; place on grill rack or broiler. Brush with remaining marinade if available.
7. Broil/grill until caramelised.
8. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
9. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (fall-off-bone, deeply caramelised).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Madens Verden — pre-boil (DK) | 220 °C × 20–25 min | 88–93 °C |
| Madens Verden — no-boil (DK) | 150 °C foil × 1 hr + grill | 88–93 °C |
| ichkoche.at (AT) | 200 °C × 15 min + 150 °C × 45 min | 88–93 °C |
| Chefkoch.de (DE) | 150 °C foil × 2.5–3 hr + broil | 90–95 °C |

**Consensus pull range**: 88–95 °C (190–203 °F). Foil-wrapped low-and-slow (150 °C) is the dominant approach across all four traditions; high-heat or broil finish is used only for glazing.
