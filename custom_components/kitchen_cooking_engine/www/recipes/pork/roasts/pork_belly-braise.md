# Pork Belly × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork belly (svinekam/flæskesteg in Danish; Schweinebauch in German/Austrian) is the quintessential Nordic and German Sunday roast. Key to success is the crackling (svær / Kruste): the rind must be deeply scored, generously salted, and positioned flat in the oven. Danish flæskesteg is cooked at high temperature (225°C) the whole way; German Krustenbraten often starts with browning, then medium oven; a simpler approach boils the belly first then glazes in a very hot oven. Target internal for pork belly: **65–75 °C** — unlike the leaner loin, belly has so much fat that it stays juicy and safe at 65 °C.

Target internal temperatures (braise):
- Tender, sliceable: **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)**
- Very tender, fall-apart: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)**

## Styckesprofil

Fläskbuk (*svinekam*/*flæskesteg* danska; *Schweinebauch* tyska) är den klassiska nordiska och tyska festrostningen. Svålen (*svær* / *Kruste*) är nyckeln: ritsas djupt med en vass kniv, saltas, och placeras plant i ugnen för jämn värmepenetration.

Ugnsrostning med svål: 180–200 °C, 1,5–2 timmar, avsluta under grillelementet för knäppande-kris svål. Innertemperatur: **80–88 °C (176–190 °F)** för genomstekt, saftig. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan utan svål, borta från revbenslben.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch — Chashu (Japanese-style braised pork belly)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/4088041641052756/Chashu.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Japanese braised pork belly for ramen. "Soft and meltingly tender." 2022 recipe.*
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 3 hr oven + 12 hr chill

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven 120°C top/bottom heat × 3 hours (turning every 30 min); cool and refrigerate overnight; slice and sear briefly in a pan before serving. Core is not measured — 3 hrs at 120°C brings the rolled belly to approximately 80–85°C internally.

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg Schweinebauch (boneless pork belly, with or without rind)
- 250 ml soy sauce
- 75 g sugar
- 80 ml mirin
- 80 ml sake
- 4 cm fresh ginger (sliced)
- 3 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 1 tbsp rapeseed oil
- Water (to half-cover)
- Optional: eggs for marinade

**Method**
1. Roll the pork belly tightly (rind on outside) and tie firmly with kitchen string.
2. In a Dutch oven or flameproof casserole, sear the roll on all sides in rapeseed oil over high heat.
3. Add soy sauce, sake, mirin, sugar, sliced ginger and crushed garlic cloves. Top up with water so the meat is at least half submerged.
4. Preheat oven to **120°C top/bottom heat**.
5. Place covered pot in oven. Braise at **120°C × 3 hours**, turning every 30 minutes.
6. Insert MEATER probe through the lid seal or cover with foil if lid doesn't allow probe.
7. **Pull at 80–85°C (176–185°F)** — meat should be very firm but yielding when probed.
8. Allow to cool in the broth; refrigerate overnight (broth sets to jelly; fat solidifies on surface).
9. Next day: scrape off fat (reserve 1 spoonful for flavour); lift meat from jellied broth; remove string.
10. Slice into 1 cm rounds; sear briefly in a hot pan each side to caramelise.
11. Serve over ramen; marinade eggs (soft-boiled) in the remaining broth overnight as a bonus.
12. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** (seared slices — soft, fragrant, glazed).

### 2. ichkoche.at — Gjuwetsch — Geschmorter Schweinebauch (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/gjuwetsch-geschmorter-schweinebauch-rezept-214749 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Bulgarian-inspired Austrian braised pork belly stew in an earthenware pot. 11 ratings.*
**Serves**: 6 · **Cook**: 1.5 hr at 180°C + 30 min at 200°C

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Earthenware pot (Tontopf) at 180°C × 1.5 hrs, then 200°C × 30 min after adding canned tomatoes.

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg Schweinebauch (pork belly), cut into goulash-sized cubes
- 500 g onions, 1 eggplant (Melanzani), 1 zucchini
- 500 g broad green beans (Fisolen), 1 kg potatoes
- 1 red + 1 green bell pepper, 2 tins canned tomatoes
- 1 tbsp oregano, 1 tbsp hot paprika powder
- 250 ml red wine; oil, salt, pepper; fresh parsley for serving

**Method**
1. Cut pork belly into goulash-sized cubes; sear in a little oil until browned. Transfer to earthenware pot (or heavy casserole).
2. In the same pan, brown onions; add to pot.
3. Brown eggplant, then zucchini; add to pot.
4. Roughly chop potatoes, green beans and bell peppers; mix with all remaining spices and herbs. Add red wine. Combine everything.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest meat cube, tip at centre.
6. Place earthenware pot in oven at **180°C × 1.5 hours**, stirring occasionally; add water if needed.
7. Add canned tomatoes; continue at **200°C × 30 minutes** until everything is tender.
8. **Pull at 80–88°C (176–190°F).**
9. Serve with fresh parsley. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (meltingly tender, aromatic stew).

### 3. BBC Good Food — Slow-roast Five-spice Pork Belly & Gravy (British/Chinese fusion)
**Source**: Barney Desmazery, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-roast-five-spice-pork-belly-gravy (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Take a simple dinner shortcut for aromatic pork with Chinese five spice." 4.5/5, 7 ratings.*
**Serves**: 5–6 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ optional overnight marinate) · **Cook**: 2.5–3 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven 150°C/130°C fan × 2 hrs (low braising phase); then 230°C/210°C fan × 30–35 min (skin-crackle phase).

**Ingredients**
- 1–1.2 kg boneless pork belly, lightly scored all over
- 1 tbsp Chinese five-spice powder, 3 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp honey
- 100 ml Shaohsing rice wine or dry sherry, 2 tbsp malt vinegar
- Thumb-sized piece ginger (finely grated), 4 garlic cloves (finely grated)
- ½ red chilli (optional), 1 tsp sunflower oil
- 200 ml chicken stock, 2 tsp cornflour; spring onions, coriander (to serve)

**Method**
1. Mix five-spice, soy, honey, rice wine, malt vinegar, ginger, garlic and chilli into a marinade.
2. If time allows: marinate pork skin-side up overnight (only meaty side in marinade; salt skin lightly).
3. Preheat oven to **150°C/130°C fan (Gas 2)**. Pour marinade into a snug roasting tin; sit pork skin-side up on top.
4. Insert MEATER probe from the side, tip at centre.
5. Roast at **150°C × 2 hours**.
6. Lift pork; pour fat into a bowl; strain juices into a small pan. Wipe tin.
7. Turn oven up to **230°C/210°C fan (Gas 8)**. Return pork skin-side up; brush with oil.
8. Roast **30–35 min**, basting with reserved fat, until skin is blistered and crackled. Finish under grill if needed.
9. **Pull at 78–85°C (172–185°F).**
10. Make gravy: add stock to strained juices; bring to boil; thicken with cornflour slurry.
11. Final serving temperature: **78–85 °C / 172–185 °F** (tender interior, crackled skin, fragrant five-spice gravy).
