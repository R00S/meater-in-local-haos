# Pork Belly × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork belly (svinekam/flæskesteg in Danish; Schweinebauch in German/Austrian) is the quintessential Nordic and German Sunday roast. Key to success is the crackling (svær / Kruste): the rind must be deeply scored, generously salted, and positioned flat in the oven. Danish flæskesteg is cooked at high temperature (225°C) the whole way; German Krustenbraten often starts with browning, then medium oven; a simpler approach boils the belly first then glazes in a very hot oven. Target internal for pork belly: **65–75 °C** — unlike the leaner loin, belly has so much fat that it stays juicy and safe at 65 °C.

Target internal temperatures (oven roast):
- Danish flæskesteg: **65 °C (149 °F)** — juicy, just-cooked through
- German Krustenbraten: **72–75 °C (162–167 °F)** — firmer but crackling-focused
- Boil + glaze methods: **already cooked; glaze at high temp**

## Styckesprofil

Fläskbuk (*svinekam* / *Schweinebauch*) med svål är en quintessentiell nordisk och tysk söndagsstek. Svålen ritsas djupt, saltas generöst. Ugnsrostning: 180–200 °C, 1,5–2 timmar, med buljong och grönsaker i botten av formen.

Avsluta under grillelementet för knäppande svål. Innertemperatur: **80–88 °C (176–190 °F)** för genomstekt, saftig fläskbuk. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från revbensben och svål. Vila täckt 10–15 minuter.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Perfekt Flæskesteg i Ovn (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/perfekt-flaeskesteg-i-ovn/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Easy and reliable recipe for a perfect flæskesteg in the oven." 4.8/5, 548 ratings.*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 15 min · **Total**: 1.5 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven 225°C fan throughout; takes about 1 hr 15 min for 1.5 kg; insert thermometer after 45 min; done when internal = **65°C**. *"It is the core temperature that determines when your flæskesteg is done — not the time!"*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg svinekam (boneless pork belly roast/loin, with rind)
- Flake salt
- **For the drippings (sky)**: 5 dl water, 1 bouillon cube, 1 onion (roughly chopped), 1 carrot (diced), 1 parsley root (diced), 1–2 tsp dried thyme

**Method**
1. Check the rind has been scored all the way through to the meat (but not into it) by the butcher — if not, score yourself with a sharp knife.
2. Wet fingers and push salt between ALL the rind cuts. Wet fingers help the salt bind. Salt the underside and sides generously + add thyme.
3. Place on a rack inside a roasting pan. The rind **must lie completely flat** — use a vegetable wedge underneath if needed.
4. Add water + bouillon + chopped vegetables to the pan. Add 1–2 tsp thyme.
5. Preheat oven to **225°C fan (varmluft)**. Roast for **45 minutes**.
6. Remove from oven; strain drippings into a bowl (keep for sauce).
7. Insert MEATER probe into the centre of the roast.
8. Return to oven; continue approximately **30 minutes** until internal = **65°C (149°F)**.
9. If rind isn't crispy, run briefly under the grill element — watch constantly.
10. **Pull at 65°C (149°F).** Rest 10 min. Final serving temperature: **65–70 °C / 149–158 °F** (perfectly juicy, crackling crispy).

### 2. Chefkoch — Krustenbraten (German tradition)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1583701265817692/Krustenbraten.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Pork shoulder or belly with exceptionally crispy crackling." Uses wheat beer and vegetables. 4.8/5, multiple ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 45 min · **Cook**: 2 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 180°C fan × 2 hrs; baste with beer and water throughout. Final grill to pop crackling. Core target approximately 75°C.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg Schweineschulter (shoulder) or Schweinebauch, with Schwarte (rind)
- 1 medium carrot, 1 leek, 1 small onion, 1 garlic clove
- 1 bottle wheat beer (Weizenbier)
- 1 roasting stock cube (Bratensaftwürfel)
- 2 tbsp sour cream (Sauerrahm)
- Caraway (Kümmel), salt, black pepper
- Chicken spice salt (optional), Butterschmalz for browning, cornstarch for sauce

**Method**
1. Score rind into small squares. Salt the rind strongly. Season remaining sides with caraway, pepper and salt.
2. Preheat oven to **180°C fan**.
3. Brown Butterschmalz in a roasting pan; sear all sides except the rind. Add vegetables around the meat. Deglaze with 1/3 of the beer.
4. Add roasting stock cube and enough water so liquid reaches halfway up the roast.
5. Roast crackling-side up at **180°C × 2 hours**, basting and topping up liquid as needed. Watch crackling doesn't scorch.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part.
7. After 2 hours, lift roast out; strain sauce into a pot; return roast to pan on vegetables.
8. Run under grill to finish crackling if needed.
9. **Pull at 72–75°C (162–167°F).** Thicken sauce with cornstarch + sour cream.
10. Final serving temperature: **72–75°C / 162–167°F** (crispy Kruste, moist interior).

### 3. Chefkoch — Schweinebauch aus dem Ofen, knusprig (German tradition, beer glaze)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/605041160289876/Schweinebauch-aus-dem-Ofen-knusprig.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Pre-boil method + honey-beer glaze. 4.8/5, 2 830 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 40 min boil + 40 min oven

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Boil 40 min; oven at **220°C (non-fan, Gas Mark 3–4) × 40 min**, basting regularly with glaze.

**Ingredients**
- 800 g Schweinebraten (Bauch — pork belly)
- 1 onion, 5 cloves, 3 bay leaves
- 2 tbsp liquid honey
- 330 ml Pils beer
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp each: chopped thyme, chopped rosemary
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Method**
1. Rinse pork belly; place in a pot of cold water with onion (spiked with cloves) and bay leaves. Bring to boil; simmer at medium heat **40 minutes**.
2. Remove belly; cool slightly. Score rind in a diamond pattern with a sharp knife.
3. Combine honey, beer, soy sauce, thyme, rosemary; bring to a boil (makes the glaze).
4. Preheat oven to **220°C (no fan / Gas 3–4)**. Place pork belly rind-side up on a baking tray.
5. Insert MEATER probe from the side into the thickest section.
6. Roast at **220°C × 40 minutes**, brushing frequently with the beer glaze.
7. Rest 5 minutes.
8. **Pull at 72–75°C (162–167°F)** — it is mostly cooked from boiling; the oven phase finishes it and glazes.
9. Tip: also excellent cold the next day.
10. Final serving temperature: **72–75°C / 162–167°F** (crispy rind, tender and glazed).

### 4. Chefkoch — Schweinebauch mit Brezel-Brätfüllung (German stuffed belly)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3698481558437938/Schweinebauch-mit-Brezel-Braetfuellung.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Stuffed with Bavarian pretzel-sausage filling; 150°C fan × 2 hrs; classic Sunday roast.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 45 min · **Cook**: 2 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 150°C fan × 2 hrs. Optional: honey-beer glaze in last 30 min; optional raise temperature for crispier exterior.

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg Schweinebauch (pork belly slab)
- **Filling**: 2 lye pretzels (Laugenbrezel), 250 g Brät (sausage meat), 1 garlic clove, ½ bunch flat-leaf parsley, 1 onion, 1 tsp caraway (Kümmel), salt water for brushing, salt and pepper
- Optional glaze: 1 tbsp honey + 2 tbsp beer

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **150°C fan**.
2. Cut Brezel into small cubes; mix with Brät, garlic, parsley, onion and caraway; season well with salt and pepper.
3. Butterfly the pork belly along its length (cut a pocket) so it opens flat. Shape the pretzel-filling into a sausage; lay on the opened belly.
4. Roll the belly around the filling; wrap in a pork net (Schweinenetz) or tie tightly with kitchen string. Brush with salt water.
5. Place on a rack on a baking tray (drip tray below).
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the meat, tip at centre.
7. Roast at **150°C × 2 hours**.
8. Optional: brush with honey-beer glaze for last 30 minutes.
9. Optional: raise heat or switch to upper heat (Oberhitze) for crispier exterior in last 10–15 min.
10. **Pull at 70–75°C (158–167°F).** Slice and serve with Spätzle, Schupfnudeln or croquettes, vegetables and brown sauce.
11. Final serving temperature: **70–75°C / 158–167°F** (succulent, hearty filling, crispy exterior).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Madens Verden (DK) | 225°C fan × 1 hr 15 | **65°C** |
| Chefkoch Krustenbraten (DE) | 180°C × 2 hr | 72–75°C |
| Chefkoch Schweinebauch beer (DE) | Boil + 220°C × 40 min | 72–75°C |
| Chefkoch stuffed (DE) | 150°C × 2 hr | 70–75°C |

**Notes on temperature difference**: The Danish approach targets 65°C (recommended by Holger Rørby Madsen) for the juiciest result; at 65°C pork is food-safe. German sources suggest 72–75°C for a firmer texture and fully rendered rind crackling. For a MEATER probe use case:
- Set target 65°C for Danish-style juicy roast
- Set target 72–75°C for German-style Krustenbraten
