# Pork Belly × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork belly (svinekam/flæskesteg in Danish; Schweinebauch in German/Austrian) is the quintessential Nordic and German Sunday roast. Key to success is the crackling (svær / Kruste): the rind must be deeply scored, generously salted, and positioned flat in the oven. Danish flæskesteg is cooked at high temperature (225°C) the whole way; German Krustenbraten often starts with browning, then medium oven; a simpler approach boils the belly first then glazes in a very hot oven. Target internal for pork belly: **65–75 °C** — unlike the leaner loin, belly has so much fat that it stays juicy and safe at 65 °C.

Target internal temperatures (pan fry):
- Cooked through, still juicy: **72–75 °C (162–167 °F)**
- Traditional stegt flæsk (crispy and well-rendered): **80°C+ / 176°F+** (visual indicator: deeply golden and crispy)

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Stegt Flæsk med Persillesovs (Danish national dish)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/stegt-flaesk-persillesovs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Stegt flæsk med persillesovs — Denmark's national dish." 4.7/5, 96 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 40 min · **Total**: 1 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: No probe temperature given — done by visual crispiness. "Steg flæsket færdig indtil det er sprødt" (fry until crispy). 4–6 minutes per side at medium heat. Hold warm in oven at 60°C.

**Ingredients**
- 20 slices flæsk (pork belly slices, about 4 per person)
- Salt
- **Parsley sauce (persillesovs)**: 2 tbsp butter, 2 tbsp flour, 5 dl milk, salt, pepper, nutmeg, 1 handful fresh parsley

**Method**
1. Pat pork slices dry with kitchen paper. Lightly beat flat (with the heel of your hand or a rolling pin) — do not smash them flat.
2. Sprinkle both sides lightly with salt; rest 15 minutes.
3. Heat a dry pan over a good heat (medium-high, no oil needed — the fat renders from the pork).
4. Brown slices on both sides. If very lean, add a little butter.
5. Reduce to medium heat; fry until fully crispy — typically **4–6 minutes per side**. Pour off excess fat as it renders (this helps the slices get crispier).
6. Hold warm in oven at 60°C until all slices are cooked.
7. **Internal temperature**: aim for 72–75°C (162–167°F) but visual crispiness is the practical doneness indicator.
8. **Parsley sauce**: melt butter; whisk in flour without colouring; gradually whisk in milk until desired consistency; season with salt, pepper, nutmeg; stir in finely chopped parsley.
9. Serve with boiled potatoes, parsley sauce and extra parsley.
10. Final serving temperature: **72–80 °C / 162–176 °F** (deeply golden, crispy rendered pork).

### 2. ichkoche.at — Gebratene Bauchfleischstreifen (Austrian tradition — pan-fried strips)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/gebratene-bauchfleischstreifen-rezept-219500 (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Fried pork belly strips from the Heuriger tradition." 333 ratings, 15–30 min.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 15–30 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Pan fry on medium-to-high heat with a little oil, turning frequently; done when golden-brown and crispy. No probe temperature stated.

**Ingredients**
- Pork belly (Bauchfleisch), sliced into strips
- Salt, pepper, (optional: paprika, caraway, garlic)
- A little oil for the pan

**Method**
1. Season pork belly strips with salt, pepper and (optionally) paprika and caraway.
2. Heat a pan with a little oil over medium-high heat.
3. Add belly strips; fry, turning frequently so fat renders out gradually.
4. Continue until strips are a beautiful golden-brown and crispy on all sides (usually 8–12 min total depending on thickness).
5. **Internal temperature**: 72–75°C (162–167°F) minimum, though visual crispiness is the practical guide.
6. Remove and rest on kitchen paper briefly. Serve immediately — crackling softens quickly.
7. Tip: do not overcrowd the pan; fry in batches if needed (crowding causes steaming instead of frying).
8. Final serving temperature: **72–75 °C / 162–167 °F** (crispy, golden-brown strips).

### 3. ichkoche.at — Gebratenes Bauchfleisch (Austrian tradition — oven-to-pan)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/gebratenes-bauchfleisch-rezept-216728 (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Roasted pork belly: first oven, then pan-finished." 215 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 1 hr oven + 2 min pan

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven 180°C × 60–90 min first; then slice 2 cm thick; pan fry in hot oil 2 minutes per side.

**Ingredients**
- Pork belly (Bauchfleisch), piece
- Salt, pepper, ground caraway (Kümmel), crushed garlic cloves
- Oil for frying

**Method**
1. Rub the pork belly all over with salt, pepper, ground caraway and crushed garlic.
2. Roast in oven at **180°C × 60–90 minutes** (until cooked through and tender).
3. Remove from oven; allow to cool slightly so it can be sliced.
4. Slice into approximately **2 cm thick slices**.
5. Heat oil in a pan until very hot; fry slices **~2 minutes per side** until crispy exterior forms.
6. **Pull at 72–75°C (162–167°F)** — after the oven phase, the belly is already safely cooked; the pan phase creates the crust.
7. Serve immediately with potatoes or bread, various sauces and salad.
8. Final serving temperature: **72–75 °C / 162–167 °F** (juicy interior from oven, crispy exterior from pan).
