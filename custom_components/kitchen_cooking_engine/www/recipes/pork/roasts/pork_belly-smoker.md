# Pork Belly × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork belly (svinekam/flæskesteg in Danish; Schweinebauch in German/Austrian) is the quintessential Nordic and German Sunday roast. Key to success is the crackling (svær / Kruste): the rind must be deeply scored, generously salted, and positioned flat in the oven. Danish flæskesteg is cooked at high temperature (225°C) the whole way; German Krustenbraten often starts with browning, then medium oven; a simpler approach boils the belly first then glazes in a very hot oven. Target internal for pork belly: **65–75 °C** — unlike the leaner loin, belly has so much fat that it stays juicy and safe at 65 °C.

Target internal temperatures (smoker method):
- **Safe minimum**: **65 °C (149 °F)** — USDA safe but fat is still firm
- **Phase 1 pull for wrap**: **74–79 °C (165–175 °F)** — bark set, good color; wrap in foil here
- **Sliceable finish**: **93 °C (200 °F)** — fat fully rendered, meat tender, slices cleanly
- **Pull-apart / burnt-ends finish**: **93–97 °C (200–207 °F)** — falling-apart texture

## Source recipes

### 1. Smoking-Meat.com — Smoked Pork Belly (whole slab)
**Source**: Jeff Phillips, Smoking-Meat.com; https://www.smoking-meat.com/smoked-pork-belly (fetched 2026-04-24). *"As you may know, smoked pork belly is the stuff that bacon is made of." Whole 4–6 kg slab, two-phase cook.*
**Serves**: 8–12 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: ~6 hr · **Rest**: 15–60 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**:
- Smoker set to **225 °F (107 °C)**, indirect heat, water pan filled
- **Phase 1**: Cook uncovered, fat-cap up, ~4–4.5 hr until internal **160–168 °F (71–75 °C)** or desired bark/colour
- **Phase 2**: Wrap tightly in foil (or place in foil pan covered with foil), return to smoker ~1.5 hr until internal **195–200 °F (90–93 °C)**
- **Sliceable target**: **198 °F (92 °C)** — tender, slices without falling apart
- **Pull-apart target**: **207 °F (97 °C)** — best pulled-pork texture
- Rest at least 15 min (up to 1 hour) before slicing ¼ inch thick
- Recommended wood: **Cherry and/or Apple** (sweet fruit wood)

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole pork belly (~4–6 kg / 9–13 lb), tough skin/rind removed
- Yellow mustard or BBQ sauce as binder (optional)
- Jeff's Texas-style rub (or any BBQ rub) — generous coating on fat and meat side
- Jeff's original rub — second half if doing two flavours

**Method**
1. Remove rind if present (ask butcher, or use a sharp knife between skin and fat cap).
2. With fat cap up, score a crosshatch pattern ¼ inch deep and 1 inch apart to increase surface area for rub.
3. Optionally brush a thin layer of yellow mustard or BBQ sauce as binder.
4. Apply rub generously to all surfaces. Rest 15 minutes, flip, repeat on meaty side.
5. Preheat smoker to **225 °F (107 °C)** with indirect heat and a filled water pan.
6. Place pork belly **fat-side up** on grate. Insert MEATER probe into thickest meat section.
7. **Phase 1**: Smoke uncovered ~4–4.5 hr until probe reads **165–170 °F (74–77 °C)** and bark is deep brown.
8. **Phase 2**: Wrap in foil or place in covered foil pan. Return to smoker 1–1.5 hr until internal = **195–200 °F (90–93 °C)**.
9. Remove, crack foil open slightly. Rest 15–60 min.
10. Slice ¼ inch thick and serve.

### 2. Smoking-Meat.com — Smoked Pork Belly Burnt Ends
**Source**: Jeff Phillips, Smoking-Meat.com; https://www.smoking-meat.com/smoked-pork-belly-burnt-ends (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Here's my own personal take on smoked pork belly burnt ends." 4.5/5 from 12 votes.*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: ~4 hr · **Total**: ~4.5 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**:
- Smoker set to **225 °F (107 °C)**, indirect heat
- **Phase 1**: Smoke cubes uncovered until internal **165 °F (74 °C)** and bark is well formed — typically 2–3 hr
- **Phase 2**: Transfer cubes to foil pan, coat with honey + BBQ sauce (or honey + hot pepper jelly), cover with foil, return to smoker **60–90 min** until internal **200 °F (93 °C)**
- Recommended wood: **Apple, Maple or Cherry** (or a mixture)

**Ingredients**
- 5 lb (2.3 kg) pork belly, rind removed
- 1 cup BBQ rub (Jeff's original or any favourite)
- ½ cup honey
- ¾ cup BBQ sauce (or ¼ cup hot pepper jelly as alternative)

**Method**
1. Freeze pork belly 30–45 min to firm the fat, then slice into 1.5-inch strips, then cube to 1.5 × 1.5 inch cubes.
2. Coat cubes in rub (shake in a zip bag with rub). Arrange on a cooling rack or grill pan.
3. Preheat smoker to **225 °F (107 °C)**, indirect heat.
4. **Phase 1**: Place racks in smoker. Smoke until cubes reach **165 °F (74 °C)** (2–3 hr) and have a well-formed bark.
5. **Phase 2**: Transfer cubes to a foil pan. Drizzle with honey and BBQ sauce per 2 lbs of meat (¼–⅓ cup honey + ¼–½ cup sauce). Stir to coat. Cover pan with foil.
6. Return to smoker **60–90 min** until internal = **200 °F (93 °C)**.
7. Remove foil, cool a few minutes, serve with toothpicks as finger food.

### 3. Smart Fritid / Agneta — Varmrökt sidfläsk (Hot-smoked pork belly, Swedish style)
**Source**: Agneta, Smart Fritid; https://www.smartfritid.se/varmrokt-sidflask (fetched 2026-04-24). Swedish-language recipe using a hot-smoking cabinet (e.g. Borniak or Bradley Smoker).
**Serves**: variable · **Prep**: brine 2.5 days + overnight dry · **Cook**: up to 7 h · **Rest**: 2 days (maturing in fridge)

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**:
- Smoker cabinet set to **80 °C (176 °F)** when belly is loaded, rind/skin-side up
- Smoke until internal temperature reaches **70 °C (158 °F)** — takes up to 7 hours
- After smoking: mature in refrigerator for **2 days** before eating (flavour development)

**Ingredients**
- Pork belly (whole slab), either pre-brined (rimmat fläsk, available in Swedish supermarkets) or fresh
- If brining fresh belly: 7.5% brine — 925 ml water + 75 ml non-iodised salt per litre of brine
- Wood chips as preferred (Scandinavian smoking; alder and beech are typical)

**Method**
1. **Option A (pre-brined)**: Use ready-brined (rimmat) belly from the supermarket — no additional preparation needed before smoking.
2. **Option B (fresh belly)**: Inject belly with the 7.5% brine using a syringe; also submerge in brine for **2.5 days (60 hours)** in the refrigerator.
3. Remove belly from brine, pat dry, and leave uncovered in the refrigerator **overnight** to form a dry surface (pellicle).
4. Preheat smoking cabinet to **80 °C (176 °F)**.
5. Place belly **skin/rind-side up** on the smoker rack.
6. Smoke until internal temperature = **70 °C (158 °F)** — allow up to 7 hours.
7. Remove; allow to cool, then refrigerate for **at least 2 days** to mature and develop full flavour.
8. Serve thinly sliced as a cold cut (smörgåspålägg) or use in cooking (e.g. Cowboy Pie with beans).

### 4. Karlstein.nu / Thomas Karlstein — Burnt Ends av fläsksida (Swedish pork belly burnt ends)
**Source**: Thomas Karlstein, karlstein.nu; https://karlstein.nu/burnt-ends-av-flasksida/ (fetched 2026-04-24). Swedish-language recipe, written in collaboration with Bradley Smoker. Published 6 November 2020.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: ~6 h · **Total**: ~6 h 20 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**:
- Smoker set to **115 °C (239 °F)**
- **Phase 1**: Smoke cubes uncovered for **3 hours** until bark forms (no specific internal temp stated — visual: dark, slightly reddish "bark")
- **Phase 2**: Transfer to foil pan, coat with BBQ sauce, cover with foil, return to smoker **1.5–2 h**
- **Phase 3**: Remove foil, let go another **15 minutes** to set glaze
- Total cook: approximately 6 hours

**Ingredients**
- 2–3 kg pork belly (fresh / naturell — not brined/rimmat)
- ~2 dl (200 ml) dry rub (see recipe below)
- ~3 dl (300 ml) BBQ sauce (favourite brand, or homemade)

**Rub (basic recipe — makes a large batch)**
- 2 dl paprika powder, 0.75 dl brown sugar, 0.75 dl salt, 0.25 dl freshly ground black pepper, 3 tbsp cayenne, 2 tbsp dried thyme, 2 tbsp mustard powder, 2 tbsp cumin (lightly crushed), 1 tbsp dried sage

**BBQ sauce (basic recipe)**
- 3 dl mild chilli sauce (Heinz type), 3 dl ketchup, 3 tbsp Dijon mustard, 4 tsp liquid honey, 4 tbsp brown sugar, 4 tbsp liquid smoke, 1 solo garlic clove (or 6 regular cloves), 4 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp cayenne, 4 tbsp soy sauce, salt and black pepper to taste

**Method**
1. Remove rind and most of the top fat layer (not all) from the pork belly.
2. Cut belly into large cubes approximately **3 × 3 cm**.
3. Coat cubes thoroughly in dry rub (toss in a bag with rub).
4. Preheat smoker to **115 °C (239 °F)**.
5. **Phase 1**: Place cubed belly on smoker racks. Smoke uncovered for **3 hours** until a dark bark forms with a slight reddish tinge.
6. **Phase 2**: Transfer cubes to a foil pan. Brush/toss generously in BBQ sauce. Cover pan tightly with aluminium foil. Return to smoker for **1.5–2 hours**.
7. **Phase 3**: Remove foil lid. Continue smoking for **15 minutes** to set the glaze.
8. Serve straight from the smoker as finger food, or pile into fresh bread with toppings (salad, cheddar, onion, jalapeño, mayo, extra BBQ sauce).

|--------|---------|------------|-------------|----------------|------|

| Karlstein.nu (burnt ends) | SE | 115 °C (239 °F) | ~3 h (visual bark) | ~1.5–2 h foiled + 15 min unfoiled | Unspecified |

**Recommended MEATER settings**:
- Set phase 1 alert at **74 °C (165 °F)** → wrap/pan and glaze
- Final pull at **93 °C (200 °F)** for sliceable or burnt-end texture
- For pull-apart texture, let it go to **97 °C (207 °F)**
- For Scandinavian-style hot-smoke (80 °C cabinet): pull at **70 °C (158 °F)** internal
