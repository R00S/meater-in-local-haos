## Cut profile

Pork belly is especially well-suited to sous vide. The abundant intramuscular fat and connective tissue render and convert to gelatin at low temperatures (60–75 °C), producing a silky, butter-soft texture that is impossible to achieve by any faster method. Cooking times range from 12 to 72 hours depending on desired texture; 24 h at 70–75 °C is a widely cited sweet-spot. After the bath the belly is usually seared or crisped (skin-side up under a broiler, or in a very hot pan) to create crackling or a mahogany crust.

Target internal temperatures (sous vide):
- **INKBIRD app default**: **68 °C / 154 °F × 24 h** — moist, silky, slight chew
- **Author's preference**: **75 °C / 167 °F × 24 h** — slightly firmer, fat fully rendered, easier to crisp afterwards

## Styckesprofil

Fläskbuk lämpar sig idealiskt för sous vide. Det rikliga intramuskulära fettet och bindväven smälter ned och omvandlas till gelatin vid låga temperaturer (60–75 °C), vilket ger en silkig, smörsoft textur som är omöjlig att uppnå med snabbare metoder.

Sous vide-parametrar: **72–80 °C** i 24–48 timmar; sear sedan snabbt i skrikhet panna med svålen nedåt för kris. Innertemperatur: **72–80 °C** beroende på önskad textur — lägre för mjukare, gelatinöst resultat. Eftertillagning: försumbar (sous vide). Sond insatt längsvis in till mitten av buken.

## Source recipes

### 1. INKBIRD Blog / tastybits.de — Asian-style Pork Belly Sous Vide (75 °C × 24 h)
**Source**: Jannik Degner, tastybits.de; republished on INKBIRD blog — https://www.inkbird.com/blogs/recipes/schweinebauch-sous-vide-gegart-asian-style (fetched 2026-04-24). German-language recipe. Original at tastybits.de.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 24 h sous vide + 15 min broiler · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**:
- Sous vide bath: **75 °C (167 °F) for 24 hours** (author's choice; INKBIRD app recommends 68 °C / 154 °F × 24 h)
- After bath: pat dry, brush with rice vinegar, then crisp skin under **250 °C (482 °F) broiler** for ~15 minutes
- Rest at least 5 minutes before cubing and serving

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg pork belly (Duroc or free-range), rind on
- **Marinade / bag liquid**: 100 ml light soy sauce, 60 g hoisin sauce, 1 tbsp rice vinegar, 1 tbsp honey, 1 squeeze lime juice
- 1 thumb-sized piece fresh ginger (finely chopped), 2 garlic cloves (finely chopped), 1 spring onion (finely chopped), 1 red chilli (finely chopped), freshly ground black pepper
- Extra: 1 tbsp rice vinegar (for brushing after bath), a little fine sea salt

**Method**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients (soy, hoisin, rice vinegar, honey, lime, ginger, garlic, spring onion, chilli, pepper) in a bowl or blender.
2. Rinse and dry the pork belly. Place in a vacuum bag together with the marinade; vacuum-seal.
3. Set sous vide circulator to **75 °C (167 °F)**. Submerge bag (weigh it down if it floats). Cook for **24 hours**.
4. Remove belly from bag; reserve the bag liquid for sauce. Pat the belly very dry.
5. Brush skin with ~1 tbsp rice vinegar and sprinkle a little fine sea salt over the skin.
6. Place skin-side up on a rack in a preheated **250 °C (482 °F)** oven on broil/grill setting. Crisp for ~15 minutes until rind is evenly puffed and golden. Use crumpled foil under the meat to keep the skin level.
7. Rest 5 minutes, then slice into cubes and serve.
8. **Sauce**: Reduce the reserved bag liquid in a small saucepan until thick and syrupy. Use as base or dip.
