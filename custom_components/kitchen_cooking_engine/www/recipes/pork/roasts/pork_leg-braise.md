## Cut profile

Pork leg and hock (Schweinshaxe / stinco di maiale / kuzu incik) is a collagen-rich cut with thick connective tissue, bone marrow, and heavily worked muscle. Braising unlocks the cut's full potential: 2–3 hours of moist heat at low temperature converts collagen to gelatin, creating sticky, unctuous, fall-off-the-bone meat surrounded by a richly reduced sauce. Popular braised in beer across Bavaria, Central Europe, and the British Isles; in soy and spices across East Asia; and in wine with herbs across Mediterranean Europe. The MEATER probe is placed deep into the thickest muscle mass of the leg or hock, away from the bone, to track internal temperature accurately.

Target internal temperature: **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for sliceable, tender; **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for pull-apart.

## Styckesprofil

Fläsklåg och lägg (*Schweinshaxe* / *stinco di maiale* / *kuzu incik*) är ett kollagenrikt snitt med tjock bindväv, benmärg och hårt arbetad muskulatur. Bräsering frigör snittets fulla potential: 2–3 timmars fuktig värme förvandlar seghet till pull-apart-mörhet.

Bryn i panna, bräsera sedan täckt i ugn vid 160–170 °C med fond, vin, lök och aromater i 2–3 timmar. Innertemperatur: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** för pull-apart. Eftertillagning: försumbar. Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från benet.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Geschmorte Schweinshaxenscheiben (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/113281047567960/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Geschmorte Schweinshaxenscheiben — braised pork hock slices in dark beer."*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g pork hock, cut into thick slices
- 2 onions, sliced into rings
- 125 ml dark Bavarian beer (Dunkles Bier)
- Salt, black pepper, ground cumin
- Oil for searing

**Method**
1. Season hock slices generously on both sides with salt, pepper, and cumin.
2. Heat oil in a heavy-based pot over high heat. Sear hock slices on both sides until deeply browned, about 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add sliced onion rings around and over the meat.
4. Deglaze with the dark beer, scraping up all browned bits from the base.
5. Cover tightly and braise over low heat for 35 minutes.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest slice before braising begins. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** when meat is tender and pulling away from bone.
7. **Rest 10 min** before serving with potato dumplings (Knödel) and sauerkraut.
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*

### 2. The Woks of Life — Chinese Red Braised Pork Hock / Ti Pang (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: The Woks of Life (Bill, Judy, Sarah, and Kaitlin Leung); https://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-braised-ti-pang-pork/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Ti Pang (蹄膀) — red-braised pork hock, the definitive Shanghainese family dish."*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr

**Ingredients**
- 1.4 kg (3 lb) pork front hock, bone-in

*Spice pouch:*
- 2 star anise
- 3 cloves
- 1 small cinnamon stick
- 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns
- 1 tsp fennel seeds
- 1 black cardamom pod
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 piece dried orange peel

*Braising liquid:*
- 5 cm (2 in) fresh ginger, sliced
- 3 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 40 g (3 tbsp) rock sugar
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 3 scallions, knotted
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce
- 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
- Water to cover

**Method**
1. Blanch the hock in boiling water for 5 minutes to remove impurities. Rinse and pat dry.
2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wok or Dutch oven over high heat. Sear the hock on all sides until golden, about 10 minutes total.
3. Transfer to a large pot. Add spice pouch, ginger, scallions, garlic, Shaoxing wine, rock sugar, soy sauces, and enough water to just cover the hock.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and braise for **3 hours**, flipping the hock every 30 minutes and spooning braising liquid over it.
5. Remove hock when the bone pulls out cleanly and meat barely holds its shape.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the hock (away from bone) before braising. **Pull at 87–90 °C (189–194 °F)** when completely fall-off-the-bone.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Strain and reduce braising liquid to a glaze. Serve over white rice.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 3. Giallo Zafferano — Stinco di maiale al forno con patate (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Ricette GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Stinco-di-maiale-al-forno-con-patate.html (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Stinco di maiale al forno — classic Italian braised-then-roasted pork shank with potatoes."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 h 45 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g pork shank (stinco di maiale)
- 500 g potatoes, halved
- 4 garlic cloves
- Fresh rosemary and thyme
- 50 ml dry red wine
- 200 ml water
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Season the shank with salt, pepper, rosemary, and thyme.
2. Heat olive oil in a wide casserole over medium-high. Brown the shank well on all sides, about 5 minutes per side.
3. Add garlic cloves, pour in the red wine and water. Bring to a simmer.
4. Cover and braise on medium-low heat for 30 minutes, turning once.
5. Add potatoes around the shank. Transfer to oven, uncovered, at 200 °C (390 °F).
6. Cook 1 hour 15 minutes, basting with pan juices every 20 minutes, until skin is golden.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the shank. **Pull at 75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for sliceable; **85 °C (185 °F)** for fully tender. (Giallo Zafferano explicitly states 75–85 °C as target.)
8. **Rest 10 min** loosely tented with foil before serving.
*Final temperature: 80–85 °C (176–185 °F).*

### 4. Valdemarsro.dk — Braiseret nakkefilet (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Valdemarsro.dk (Marie Holm); https://www.valdemarsro.dk/braiseret-svinenakke/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Braiseret nakkefilet — Danish braised pork with apple and dark ale."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 hr

*Note: This recipe uses pork neck (nakke) but the braising method is identical for leg and hock cuts.*

**Ingredients**
- 1–1.5 kg pork neck or hock
- 3 carrots, roughly chopped
- 1 leek, chopped
- 1 large onion, quartered
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 250 ml (1 cup) apple juice (æblejuice)
- 250 ml (1 cup) dark ale or stout
- Fresh thyme, bay leaves
- Salt, pepper
- Oil or butter for searing

**Method**
1. Pat pork dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil or butter in a heavy casserole over high heat. Brown the meat on all sides until a deep mahogany crust forms, about 4–5 minutes per side.
3. Remove meat. In the same pot, sauté carrots, leek, onion, and garlic until lightly coloured, about 5 minutes.
4. Return meat to pot. Pour over apple juice and ale. Add thyme and bay leaves.
5. Cover and braise on very low heat (or oven at 150 °C) for 1.5–2 hours, turning once or twice.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part before braising. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** when fork-tender.
7. Remove pork and allow to **rest 15 min**. Serve with mashed potatoes, the braising liquid reduced to sauce, and pickled red cabbage.
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA pork minimum | 63 °C (145 °F) | Not relevant for braising — collagen not yet converted |
| Sliceable tender | 75–80 °C (167–176 °F) | Collagen beginning to convert; still holds shape |
| Pull-apart tender | 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) | Optimal for all braised hock/leg dishes |
| Chinese red-braise | 87–95 °C (189–203 °F) | Very high temp for maximum gelatin and texture |
