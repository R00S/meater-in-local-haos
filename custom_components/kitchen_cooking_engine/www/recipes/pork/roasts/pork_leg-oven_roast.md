## Cut profile

Pork leg and hock (Schweinshaxe / stinco di maiale / kuzu incik) is a collagen-rich cut with thick connective tissue, bone marrow, and heavily worked muscle. Braising unlocks the cut's full potential: 2–3 hours of moist heat at low temperature converts collagen to gelatin, creating sticky, unctuous, fall-off-the-bone meat surrounded by a richly reduced sauce. Popular braised in beer across Bavaria, Central Europe, and the British Isles; in soy and spices across East Asia; and in wine with herbs across Mediterranean Europe. The MEATER probe is placed deep into the thickest muscle mass of the leg or hock, away from the bone, to track internal temperature accurately.

Target internal temperature: **75–85 °C (167–185 °F)** as explicitly stated by Giallo Zafferano. Pull-apart fall-off-the-bone is achieved closer to 88–90 °C.

## Styckesprofil

Fläsklåg och lägg (*Schweinshaxe* / *stinco di maiale* / *kuzu incik*) är ett kollagenrikt snitt med tjock bindväv, benmärg och hårt arbetad muskulatur. Ugnsrostningsmetod: börja vid hög temperatur (220 °C) för att brynna ytan, sänk sedan till 160 °C för 2–3 timmars långsam tillagning.

Klassiska beredningar: bayrisk *Schweinshaxe* (krisp svål), italiensk *stinco di maiale* (med vin och rosmarintomater). Innertemperatur: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** för pull-apart. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från benet.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Knusprige Schweinshaxe aus dem Ofen (German tradition)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3947241604678014/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Knusprige Schweinshaxe aus dem Ofen — crispy oven pork hock."*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 3 hr (90 min simmering + oven)

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork hocks (~1 kg each)
- 1.5 tsp salt
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp dried marjoram
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 0.5 tsp caraway seeds
- 1 onion, halved
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 500 ml beef or pork broth
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 250 ml dark beer

**Method**
1. Place hocks in a large pot with onion, garlic, and broth. Bring to the boil, then simmer for **90 minutes** to pre-tenderise.
2. Preheat oven to 170 °C (fan 150 °C). Remove hocks from pot; score the rind in a diamond pattern.
3. Mix salt, pepper, marjoram, rosemary, and caraway. Rub the spice mixture all over the hocks.
4. Dissolve tomato paste in the reserved broth. Place hocks in a roasting tray. Pour broth mixture around them.
5. Roast at 170 °C for 70 minutes, basting with pan juices every 20 minutes.
6. Increase temperature to 185 °C (fan 165 °C) and roast a further 20 minutes to blister and crisp the rind. Add the dark beer to the tray in the last 15 minutes.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest muscle. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** when the meat is very tender and skin deeply golden and crisp.
8. **Rest 10–15 min** loosely tented before serving with potato dumplings and sauerkraut.
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*

### 2. Giallo Zafferano — Stinco di maiale al forno con patate (Italian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Ricette GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Stinco-di-maiale-al-forno-con-patate.html (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Stinco di maiale al forno con patate — classic Italian oven pork shank with potatoes."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 h 45 min at 200 °C

**Ingredients**
- 800 g pork shank (stinco di maiale)
- 500 g baby potatoes, halved
- 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs
- 50 ml dry red wine
- 200 ml water
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Season shank with salt, pepper, and herbs.
2. Heat a splash of olive oil in a large ovenproof casserole over medium-high heat. Brown shank on all sides, about 5 minutes per side.
3. Arrange potatoes and garlic around the shank. Pour wine and water into the base.
4. Transfer, uncovered, to the oven. Roast for approximately 1 hour 45 minutes, basting with the pan juices every 20 minutes.
5. Add more water to the pan if it dries out.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the shank. **Pull at 75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for sliceable; **85 °C (185 °F)** for fall-off-the-bone. *(Giallo Zafferano explicitly states the target range as 75–85 °C.)*
7. **Rest 10 min** before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs.
*Final temperature: 80–85 °C (176–185 °F).*

### 3. Ichkoche.at — Knusprig gebratene Schweinsstelze (Austrian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/knusprig-gebratene-schweinsstelze-rezept-226650 (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Knusprig gebratene Schweinsstelze — Austrian crispy roasted pork hock."*
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 hr 15 min + 15 min finish

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork hocks (Stelzen)
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 whole garlic head, halved
- 1 onion, halved (skin on)
- 1 tbsp whole caraway seeds
- Pinch of freshly grated nutmeg
- Black pepper from a mill
- 1 sprig each marjoram and rosemary
- 200 ml pale lager beer
- 250 ml beef broth (Rindsuppe)
- 250 g butter
- A little oil

**Method**
1. Parboil hocks in a large pot of salted water for **15 minutes**. Remove and pat dry.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Score the rind in a diamond pattern. Rub hocks with oil and minced garlic. Press caraway seeds gently onto the skin.
3. Place the halved garlic head, onion, and herbs in a roasting tray. Set hocks on top. Place butter at the top of each hock — it will melt and baste the meat during roasting.
4. Roast at 200 °C for **1 hour**, repeatedly basting with the rendered juices, broth, and beer.
5. Reduce oven to 160 °C and roast a further **60 minutes**, continuing to baste every 20 minutes.
6. For extra-crispy crackling: mix iced water with a generous pinch of salt; brush the rind repeatedly and roast a final 15 minutes.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** when meat is very tender.
8. **Rest briefly**. Press braising juices through a sieve, season with salt, nutmeg, caraway, and pepper. Serve sauce separately. Accompany with bread dumplings (Knödel) and warm coleslaw (Krautsalat).
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*

### 4. Madensverden.dk — Ovnbagt svineskank med rodfrugter (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madensverden.dk (Holger Rørby Madsen); https://madensverden.dk/ovnbagt-svineskank-med-rodfrugter/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Ovnbagt svineskank med rodfrugter — slow oven-baked pork shank with root vegetables."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr at 120 °C

**Ingredients**
- 1 large pork hock (svineskank), ~1.2 kg
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tsp coarse sea salt
- 2 carrots, roughly chopped
- 1 parsley root (or parsnip), chopped
- ½ celeriac, cubed
- 200 ml double cream (piskefløde)
- Black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 120 °C. Score the rind of the hock in a close diamond pattern. Rub all over with thyme and coarse salt.
2. Place root vegetables in a deep roasting dish to form a trivet. Rest hock on top, rind facing up.
3. Pour cream over the root vegetables (not over the rind).
4. Cover tightly with a lid or double layer of foil. Roast **4 hours** covered.
5. Remove lid or foil. Continue roasting uncovered a further **4 hours**, until the rind is golden and crackling.
6. Insert MEATER probe before placing in oven. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** when meat is very tender and pulls away from the bone.
7. **Rest 15 min**. Blend or mash root vegetables with cream cooking juices for the purée. Serve with pickled vegetables or lingonberry jam.
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA pork minimum | 63 °C (145 °F) | Not relevant for oven hock — too early |
| Sliceable, tender | 75–80 °C (167–176 °F) | Italian target; holds shape |
| Fall-off-the-bone | 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) | German/Austrian/Danish target |
| Giallo Zafferano range | 75–85 °C (167–185 °F) | Explicitly stated on the source page |
