## Cut profile

Pork leg and hock (Schweinshaxe / Stelze) is an ideal slow-cooker cut. The dense connective tissue and bone marrow require long, low moist heat — exactly what a slow cooker delivers. The hock goes into the pot with aromatics, liquid, and seasoning, and over 6–8 hours on LOW the meat slowly detaches from the bone, the skin softens, and the braising liquid concentrates. A brief blast of oven heat at the end restores the traditional crisp skin if desired. The MEATER probe is placed in the thickest meat mass, away from the bone, throughout the long cook.

Target internal temperature: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** pull-apart. The slow cooker environment will hold the meat at this range safely for extended periods.

## Styckesprofil

Fläsklåg och lägg (*Schweinshaxe* / *Stelze*) är ett idealiskt slow cooker-snitt. Den täta bindväven och benmärgen kräver lång, låg fuktig värme — exakt vad slow cooker levererar.

Slow cooker-metod: LÅG i 8–10 timmar med aromatiska grönsaker, fond och öl (för bayrisk stil) eller vin och örter (för italien-stil). Innertemperatur: **85–92 °C (185–198 °F)**. Eftertillagning: noll (slow cooker). Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från benet.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Zwiebelschweinshaxen im Crockpot (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3292381488829058/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Zwiebelschweinshaxen im Crockpot — onion pork hock in the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7 hr (slow cooker) + 15 min (oven grill)

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork hocks, ~1 kg total
- 1 kg onions, thickly sliced
- 2 cups (500 ml) water
- 3 bay leaves
- 3 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 2 tbsp oil
- 1 tbsp cornstarch (optional, to thicken sauce)
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Score the skin of the hocks in a diamond pattern. Season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil in a large frying pan and brown hocks on all sides over high heat, about 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Layer half the onions on the bottom of the slow cooker insert. Place browned hocks on top. Scatter remaining onions and garlic around and over the hocks. Add bay leaves.
4. Mix water with vinegar and pour over everything.
5. Cook on HIGH for 1 hour, then switch to LOW for 6 hours (or LOW only for 8 hours total).
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the hock. **Pull at 85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** when meat is separating from the bone.
7. Transfer hocks to a foil-lined baking tray. Grill at 220 °C for 10–15 minutes until skin is crisp and blistered.
8. **Rest 10 min**. Strain and reduce slow-cooker juices with dissolved cornstarch for a gravy. Serve with potato salad or Sauerkraut.
*Final temperature after oven grill: 87–92 °C (189–198 °F).*

### 2. The Woks of Life — Chinese Red Braised Ti Pang (slow cooker adaptation) (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: The Woks of Life (Bill, Judy, Sarah, and Kaitlin Leung); https://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-braised-ti-pang-pork/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Ti Pang (蹄膀) — slow-cooker adaptation of the classic Shanghainese red-braised pork hock."*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7–8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe simmers for 3 hours on the stovetop; for slow cooker, reduce liquid slightly and cook on LOW 7–8 hours.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.4 kg (3 lb) pork front hock, bone-in

*Spice pouch:*
- 2 star anise, 3 cloves, 1 small cinnamon stick
- 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, 1 tsp fennel seeds
- 1 black cardamom pod, 3 bay leaves, dried orange peel

*Braising liquid:*
- 5 cm (2 in) ginger, sliced; 3 scallions, knotted
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 3 tbsp Shaoxing wine
- 40 g rock sugar
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce, 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 300 ml water (reduced from stovetop version)

**Method**
1. Blanch hock in boiling water 5 minutes. Rinse, pat dry.
2. Sear all sides in oil in a wok over high heat until deeply golden, 10 minutes.
3. Transfer hock to slow cooker. Add all aromatics and braising liquid; tuck spice pouch in beside.
4. Cook on LOW for **7–8 hours**, turning hock once at the halfway point if possible.
5. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F)** when meat is completely soft.
6. Remove hock. Strain braising liquid into a saucepan; reduce by half over high heat to form a glaze.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Pour glaze over hock. Serve over white rice or noodles.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 3. Madensverden.dk — Ovnbagt svineskank med rodfrugter (slow cooker adaptation) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk (Holger Rørby Madsen); https://madensverden.dk/ovnbagt-svineskank-med-rodfrugter/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Ovnbagt svineskank — oven-baked pork shank with root vegetables; adapted here for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe bakes at 120 °C for 8 hours (4 hr covered + 4 hr uncovered). For the slow cooker, cook on LOW 8 hours, then crisp skin briefly under the oven grill.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 large pork hock (svineskank), ~1.2 kg
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tsp coarse salt
- 2 carrots, roughly chopped
- 1 parsley root (or parsnip), chopped
- ½ celeriac, cubed
- 200 ml cream (piskefløde)
- Black pepper

**Method**
1. Score the rind of the hock in a diamond pattern. Rub all over with thyme and salt.
2. Place root vegetables on the bottom of the slow cooker to form a rack. Rest hock on top.
3. Pour cream over the vegetables (not over the hock skin).
4. Cook on LOW for **8 hours**.
5. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** when meat is tender.
6. Transfer hock to a hot grill or 240 °C oven for 10–15 minutes to crisp the skin.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with root-vegetable cream purée and pickled vegetables.
*Final temperature: 87–90 °C (189–194 °F).*

### 4. Giallo Zafferano — Stinco di maiale al forno (slow cooker adaptation) (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Ricette GialloZafferano; https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Stinco-di-maiale-al-forno-con-patate.html (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Stinco di maiale al forno con patate — adapted for the slow cooker from the classic Italian oven recipe."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 7 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original bakes at 200 °C uncovered for 1 hr 45 min. For slow cooker, brown the shank first, then cook LOW 6–7 hours. Giallo Zafferano explicitly states 75–85 °C as the internal target.*

**Ingredients**
- 800 g pork shank (stinco di maiale)
- 500 g baby potatoes
- 4 garlic cloves
- Fresh rosemary and thyme
- 50 ml dry red wine
- 200 ml water or broth
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Season shank with salt, pepper, rosemary, and garlic.
2. Sear on all sides in olive oil over high heat, about 5 minutes per side.
3. Place potatoes in slow cooker, rest shank on top. Add garlic, herbs, wine, and water.
4. Cook on LOW for **6–7 hours** until meat is very tender.
5. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for sliceable; **85 °C (185 °F)** for pull-apart. (Giallo Zafferano target: 75–85 °C.)
6. Transfer shank to a hot oven (220 °C) for 10 minutes for colour if desired.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with potatoes and pan juices.
*Final temperature: 80–85 °C (176–185 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA pork minimum | 63 °C (145 °F) | Not relevant for slow-cooked hock |
| Sliceable tender | 75–80 °C (167–176 °F) | Collagen partially converted |
| Pull-apart tender | 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) | Optimal slow-cooker target |
| Chinese red-braise | 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) | Extended cook for maximum gelatin |
