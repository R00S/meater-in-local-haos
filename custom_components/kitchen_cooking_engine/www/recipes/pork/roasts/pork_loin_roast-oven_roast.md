# Pork Loin Roast × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork loin roast (svinekam in Danish; Schweinerücken / Kotelettstrang in German/Austrian) with rind (svær / Schwarte) is the quintessential Scandinavian and German Sunday dinner. The loin is leaner than the belly so it needs more careful temperature management — pull too early and it's under-safe; pull too late and it dries out. The Danish approach targets **65°C** for a juicy, slightly rosy result; German recipes often go to 72–75°C for a firmer texture. For a MEATER probe, 65°C is an excellent target with a high-heat crackling finish.

Target internal temperatures (oven roast):
- Juicy, slightly rosy (Danish-style): **60–65 °C (140–149 °F)**
- Traditional firm, fully white (German-style): **70–75 °C (158–167 °F)**

## Styckesprofil

Fläskloin-stek (*svinekam* danska; *Schweinerücken* / *Kotelettstrang* tyska) med svål är den quintessentiella skandinaviska och tyska söndagsmiddagen. Loinen är magrare än magen, varför den behöver noggrannare temperaturkontroll för att förbli saftig.

Standardteknik: djup ritsning av svålen, salt i ritsorna, ugn 180–200 °C i 1–1,5 timmar, avsluta under grillelementet för knäppande svål. Innertemperatur: **68–72 °C (154–162 °F)** för genomstekt, saftig loin. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C. Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från benet och svålen. Vila 15 minuter täckt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Valdemarsro — Flæskesteg med sprød svær (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Valdemarsro.dk; https://www.valdemarsro.dk/flaeskesteg/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Classic Danish pork loin roast with crispy crackling. "Cold oven, slow start — the key to success." Total 3 hrs.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 2 hr 45 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe + site)**: Cold oven start at 125°C fan × 2 hrs; then 200°C × 30 min; then 225°C × 15–20 min for crackling. Pull at **60–65°C** for juicy, slightly pink result.

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg svinekam (pork loin, with rind, scored)
- 8 bay leaves (optional)
- 4 stalks fresh thyme (optional)
- Salt, freshly ground pepper
- 2 dl water

**Method**
1. Check the rind is scored all the way through (just to the meat, not into it). Score with a sharp knife if needed.
2. Grind salt finely in a mortar; mix with pepper. Rub thoroughly into the rind and down into every single cut. This makes a big difference for crackling.
3. Set loin on a rack over a roasting pan. Place bay leaves and thyme under the loin on the rack (elevates it from the liquid).
4. Make sure the rind lies completely flat — use a peeled potato as a wedge if one end is lower.
5. Add water to the roasting pan (should not touch the loin).
6. Place roasting pan in a **cold oven**. Switch oven to **125°C fan**; roast **2 hours**.
7. Raise to **200°C**; roast a further **30 minutes**.
8. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part, tip at centre.
9. Raise to **225°C**; roast until rind is crispy, approximately **15–20 minutes**. If needed, finish briefly under grill.
10. **Pull at 60–65°C (140–149°F)** — juicy, slightly rosy. Pull at 70°C for firmer, fully white result.
11. Rest 15 min before carving.
12. Final serving temperature: **60–65 °C / 140–149 °F** (moist, juicy meat, crackled crispy rind).

### 2. Madens Verden — Perfekt Flæskesteg i Ovn (Danish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/perfekt-flaeskesteg-i-ovn/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Easy and reliable recipe for a perfect flæskesteg in the oven." 4.8/5, 548 ratings.*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 15 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven 225°C fan throughout; done when internal = **65°C**. *"It is the core temperature that determines when your flæskesteg is done — not the time!"*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg svinekam (boneless pork loin roast, with rind)
- Flake salt
- **Drip pan**: 5 dl water, 1 bouillon cube, 1 onion, 1 carrot, 1 parsley root, 1–2 tsp dried thyme

**Method**
1. Check and deepen rind cuts if needed; salt thoroughly between every cut.
2. Place on a rack in a roasting pan; add water + bouillon + chopped vegetables below.
3. Preheat oven to **225°C fan**; roast **45 minutes**.
4. Remove; strain drippings; insert MEATER probe into the centre.
5. Return to oven; continue approximately **30 minutes** until internal = **65°C (149°F)**.
6. Pull at **65°C**. Rest 10 min.
7. Final serving temperature: **65 °C / 149 °F** (juicy, crackling crispy).

### 3. Chefkoch — Schweinerücken im Bratschlauch (German tradition)
**Inspired by**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1201241227183862/Schweineruecken-im-Bratschlauch.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Pork loin in a roasting bag — guarantees the meat stays juicy. "For those who want to be on the safe side."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: ~2 hr (oven 160°C → 60°C core)

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven 160°C until internal = **60°C** (about 2 hrs for a 1.5 kg piece); rest in bag 10 min; then brown in hot pan.

**Ingredients**
- 1–1.5 kg Schweinerücken (pork loin roast, boneless)
- 1 bag roasting sleeve (Bratschlauch)
- Spices: garlic, salt, pepper, herbs of your choice (rosemary, thyme, sage)

**Method**
1. Rub loin all over with garlic, salt, pepper and herbs. Slide into roasting sleeve; seal both ends; make 3 small holes for steam venting.
2. Preheat oven to **160°C fan**.
3. Place bagged loin on a rack or roasting dish.
4. Insert MEATER probe through the bag seal or a small foil gap at the side, tip at centre.
5. Roast at **160°C until internal = 60°C (140°F)** — approximately 2 hours.
6. Remove from oven; rest in closed bag 10 minutes (temperature rises to ~63–65°C).
7. Open bag; quickly sear all sides in a hot pan with oil if crackling is desired.
8. Final serving temperature: **60–65 °C / 140–149 °F** (juicy, evenly cooked).
