# Pork Shoulder × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork shoulder / neck (nakkefilet / nakkekam / svinekølle in Danish; Schweinenacken / Schweineschulter in German/Austrian) is one of the most forgiving cuts for the oven — its fat marbling prevents drying out and it develops deep flavour at virtually any oven temperature. The Danish approach covers with foil at 200°C for 105 minutes (firm but juicy); the Norwegian approach targets 75°C core at 175°C oven; the German approach uses the Krustenbraten method at 180°C × 2 hrs. Unlike the leaner loin, shoulder can be taken higher without losing juiciness.

Target internal temperatures (oven roast):
- Juicy, sliceable: **72–75 °C (162–167 °F)**
- Very tender (approaching pulled): **80–85 °C (176–185 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Nakkekam i Ovn, Langtidsstegt i Bradepande (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/nakkekam-ovn-langtidsstegt/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Long-slow roasted pork neck in a covered roasting pan — the most tender result." *
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 105 min covered at 200°C

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven at **200°C fan × 1 hour 45 min (105 min) covered with foil**. "Derefter har du den møreste nakkekam" (then you have the most tender nakkekam). Internal temperature not explicitly stated; this method produces approximately 80–82°C at 200°C for 105 min.

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole nakkekam (pork neck, boneless), cut into 3 × 4–5 cm thick steaks, then halved = 6 pieces
- Knob of butter + splash of oil (for browning)
- **Marinade**: 1 dl olive oil, 1 tbsp acacia honey, 1 tsp (smoked) paprika, 2 tsp dried rosemary, 1 tsp onion powder
- 6 garlic cloves (half crushed, half finely chopped for marinade), 2 red onions
- 2.5 dl water, 3 tbsp concentrated chicken stock (or 1 bouillon cube)
- Salt, freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Cut nakkekam into 3 thick steaks (~4–5 cm thick); halve each steak = 6 pieces.
2. Melt a generous knob of butter + splash of oil in a very hot pan; brown meat pieces thoroughly on both sides — work in 2 batches of 3 (so they brown, not steam). Season with salt as they brown.
3. Place browned pieces in a roasting dish.
4. Add half the garlic (crushed) and red onion wedges around the meat. Pour water + chicken stock over.
5. Mix olive oil + honey + paprika + rosemary + onion powder + finely chopped remaining garlic = marinade; brush all over the meat pieces.
6. Season with black pepper. Cover dish very tightly with foil along all edges.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest piece, tip at centre.
8. Oven at **200°C fan × 1 hr 45 min (105 min)**. Do not open foil during cooking.
9. **Pull at 80–85°C (176–185°F)** — "den møreste nakkekam" (the most tender nakkekam).
10. Rest 10 min before serving. Sauce: use the cooking juices directly from the dish.
11. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (very tender, juicy, richly flavoured).

### 2. Madens Verden — Nakkesteg i Ovn med Hvidløg og Kartofler i Fad (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/nakkesteg-ovn-hvidloeg-kartofler-fad/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Nakkesteg in oven with garlic and potatoes — simple and delicious all-in-one."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 90 min at 175°C

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven **175°C fan × approximately 90 minutes until core = 75°C**. *"til nakkestegen når en kernetemperatur på 75 grader"*

**Ingredients**
- 1 nakkesteg (pork neck roast, whole)
- 4 tbsp olive oil, 4 garlic cloves (pressed)
- 500 g small potatoes, 6–8 shallots, 3 carrots (in bite-sized pieces)
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes, 3 dl veal stock (or regular bouillon)
- Dried thyme, rosemary; salt, freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil + pressed garlic = garlic oil.
2. Score the top of the nakkesteg in a diamond pattern; rub all over with garlic oil.
3. Wash potatoes; place in a deep roasting dish with carrots and halved shallots.
4. Pour canned tomatoes + stock over; season with rosemary, thyme, salt and pepper.
5. Lay nakkesteg on top of vegetables; season with salt and pepper.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part, tip at centre.
7. Roast in preheated oven at **175°C fan × approximately 90 minutes** until core = **75°C (167°F)**.
8. **Pull at 75°C (167°F)** — juicy and fully cooked through.
9. Rest 10 min. Serve directly from the dish; sauce from the dish.
10. Final serving temperature: **75 °C / 167 °F** (juicy, well-flavoured, one-pan meal).

### 3. Chefkoch — Krustenbraten (German tradition — shoulder variant)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1583701265817692/Krustenbraten.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Pork shoulder or belly with exceptionally crispy crackling and wheat beer." 4.8/5, popular recipe.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 2 hr at 180°C

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 180°C fan × 2 hrs; baste with beer and water. Core approximately 72–75°C.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg Schweineschulter (pork shoulder) or Schweinebauch (belly), with rind
- 1 carrot, 1 leek, 1 small onion, 1 garlic clove
- 1 bottle wheat beer (Weizenbier)
- 1 roasting stock cube
- 2 tbsp sour cream (Sauerrahm)
- Caraway (Kümmel), salt, black pepper; Butterschmalz for browning; cornstarch for thickening

**Method**
1. Score rind into small squares. Season rind generously with salt; season other sides with caraway, pepper and salt.
2. Preheat oven to **180°C fan**.
3. Brown Butterschmalz in a roasting pan; sear all sides except the rind. Add vegetables around meat. Deglaze with 1/3 of the beer.
4. Add stock cube + enough water so liquid reaches halfway up the roast. Roast rind-side up at **180°C × 2 hours**, basting and topping up liquid.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part, tip at centre (away from bone if any).
6. After 2 hours, lift roast out; strain sauce into a pot; return roast to pan on vegetables.
7. If rind isn't crispy enough, run under grill briefly.
8. **Pull at 72–75°C (162–167°F).** Thicken sauce with cornstarch + sour cream.
9. Final serving temperature: **72–75 °C / 162–167 °F** (crispy Kruste, moist interior).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Madens Verden Nakkekam (DK) | 200°C fan × 105 min (foil-covered) | ~80–85°C |
| Madens Verden Nakkesteg (DK) | 175°C fan × 90 min | **75°C** |
| Chefkoch Krustenbraten (DE) | 180°C × 2 hr | 72–75°C |

**Consensus pull range**: **72–82 °C (162–180 °F)** — the shoulder's fat marbling means there is wide safe latitude. 75°C is the sweet spot for juicy, fully-cooked shoulder.
