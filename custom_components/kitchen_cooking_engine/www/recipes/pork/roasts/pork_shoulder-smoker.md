# Pork Shoulder × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork shoulder / neck (Schweinenacken / Schweineschulter in German/Austrian; nakkefilet / svinekam in Danish/Norwegian) is the most popular cut for smoked pulled pork. Its high fat content and generous marbling makes it extremely forgiving over the 10–18 hours of low-and-slow smoking required to convert collagen to gelatin. The plateau phase (Stall) at 65–82°C is normal and does not mean something is wrong. The smoke ring (Rauchring) develops in the first 2–3 hours. Target internal: **90–95 °C** for pull-apart texture. Boston Butt (bone-in shoulder) = more flavour and longer cook; Schweinenacken (boneless neck) = faster and more even.

Target internal temperatures (smoker):
- Sliceable (not pulled): **82–88 °C (180–190 °F)**
- Classic pulled pork: **90–95 °C (194–203 °F)**

## Styckesprofil

Fläskbog / nacke (*Schweinenacken* / *Schweineschulter* tyska; *nakkefilet* / *svinekam* danska/norska) är det populäraste snittet för rökt pulled pork. Högt fettinnehåll och generös marmorering gör det utmärkt för 10–14 timmars rök vid 110–120 °C.

Innertemperatur: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)** för pull-apart. Eftertillagning: försumbar (lång fuktig rök). Sond in i tjockaste köttmassan, borta från skulderbladsben. Slå in i folie vid 74 °C (Texas Crutch) för att passera "stall" och minska total tillagningstid.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch — Pulled Pork aus dem Smoker (German BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3247691483287698/Pulled-Pork-aus-dem-Smoker.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Dry rub + apple juice mop; smoker 120–130°C × 7–10 hrs; rest 1 hr.*
**Serves**: 5 · **Prep**: 1 hr (+ 25 hr marinate/rest) · **Cook**: 7–10 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoker at **120–130°C**. Check core at 3 hrs, then hourly. Plateau phase at 65–70°C is normal; optional wrap in foil. Second plateau may occur at 75–82°C. **Pull at 90–95°C**; rest 1 hour.

**Ingredients**
- 2.5 kg Schweinenacken (pork neck) or Schulter (shoulder)
- 1 cup (scoop) dry rub (see Chefkoch profile for "Der perfekte Pulled Pork Dry Rub" or "Butt Glitter")
- **Mop liquid**: 100 ml apple juice, 50 ml apple cider vinegar, 10 ml Worcestershire sauce, 10 g white sugar

**Method**
1. Apply dry rub generously; wrap tightly in cling film (or vacuum seal). Refrigerate 24–25 hrs.
2. Remove from fridge 2–3 hours before smoking.
3. Prepare smoker for **120–130°C** (indirect heat, wood chunks — apple or hickory recommended).
4. Place neck on smoker grate. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part, tip at centre, away from fat pockets or bone.
5. Check internal temperature at 3 hrs; then hourly.
6. **Plateau phase**: at 65–70°C the temperature may stall or drop for an hour or more — this is normal (collagen dissolving). Optional: wrap in foil to push through plateau faster.
7. Second plateau may occur at 75–82°C.
8. **Pull at 90–95°C (194–203°F).** Remove; rest 1 hour in foil.
9. Unwrap; pull with two forks (all fibres separate easily at 93°C+).
10. Classic serve: pulled pork burger on brioche/sandwich roll with coleslaw and Carolina mustard sauce.
11. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (smoky, meltingly tender, rich).

### 2. Chefkoch — Pulled Pork aus dem Smoker (Chefkoch Video recipe, 8 kg)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2528741396351623/Pulled-Pork-aus-dem-Smoker.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Full-size 8 kg Boston Butt (Schweinenacken); 100–110°C × 16–18 hrs → 90°C; smoker with smoked chips on demand.*
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ overnight) · **Cook**: 16–18 hr + 3.5 hr rest

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Smoker at **100–110°C × 16–18 hours** until core = **approximately 90°C**; rest 30 min; pull; add BBQ sauce.

**Ingredients**
- 8 kg Schweinenacken (Boston Butt)
- Sunflower oil (to bind rub)
- **Dry rub**: 2 tbsp each salt, paprika, onion granules, garlic granules, rosemary, raw cane sugar, coarse black pepper; 1 tbsp each thyme, mustard powder, chilli powder
- 500 ml BBQ sauce; dark beer (Malzbier) to taste
- Bread rolls or sandwiches, coleslaw for serving

**Method**
1. Rub meat with sunflower oil all over. Mix dry rub ingredients; press into meat on all sides. Refrigerate at least 2–3 hrs (overnight preferred).
2. Start smoker; bring to **100–110°C** stable temperature. Add wood chips for smoke as desired.
3. Place neck on grate. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part.
4. Cook at **100–110°C × 16–18 hours** until internal ≈ **90°C**. Add smoking chips as needed.
5. Remove carefully with tongs and spatula. Rest 30 min.
6. Pull with a fork until all fibres are separated.
7. Mix pulled meat with BBQ sauce and Malzbier (dark beer) for a sweet, caramel note. Knead well.
8. Grill bread rolls; serve with pulled pork and coleslaw.
9. **Pull at ~90°C (194°F).** Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (smoky, tender).

### 3. Madens Verden — Pulled Pork i Smoker (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/pulled-pork/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Pulled pork in smoker" — smoker section from the same Madens Verden pulled pork article. 4.7/5, 40 ratings.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe TOC)**: The article includes "Pulled pork på grill" and "Pulled pork i smoker" sections alongside the main oven method, all targeting 90–95°C. The smoker section follows the same rub and pull-temperature approach as the oven method.

**Ingredients** (same as oven method)
- 1.5 kg nakkefilet (pork neck/shoulder, boneless)
- **Rub**: 1 tbsp salt, 1 tbsp BBQ seasoning, 1 tbsp paprika, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp mild chilli

**Method (smoker)**
1. Mix rub; apply all over. Marinate overnight in fridge (minimum 12 hrs).
2. Bring meat to room temperature 1–2 hrs before smoking.
3. Prepare smoker for **110–120°C** with wood chips (hickory, apple or cherry).
4. Place meat in smoker. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part.
5. Smoke at **110–120°C** until internal = **90–95°C** — approximately 8–10 hrs.
6. **Pull at 90–95°C (194–203°F).** Rest 30 min.
7. Pull with two forks; serve with BBQ sauce and sides.
8. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (smoky, tender).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Smoker temp | Pull temp |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Chefkoch smoker 2.5 kg (DE) | 120–130°C | 90–95°C |
| Chefkoch smoker 8 kg (DE) | 100–110°C | ~90°C |
| Madens Verden (DK) | 110–120°C | 90–95°C |

**Consensus pull range**: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F). All sources agree; lower smoker temp (100–110°C) takes 16–18 hrs for an 8 kg piece; higher smoker temp (120–130°C) takes 7–10 hrs for 2.5 kg. MEATER probe makes the plateau phase manageable.
