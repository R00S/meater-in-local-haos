# Chicken Breast (Bone-in) × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

⚠️ **STATUS: COLLECTION IN PROGRESS** — 2 of 4–6 required source recipes collected (Groups B and C). Needs Group A (e.g. Japanese, Chinese, Korean oven chicken) and Group D (Scandinavian, Polish, Russian) sources before the Temperature consensus and Data applied sections can be written.

## Cut profile

Oven roasting bone-in, skin-on chicken breast uses dry convective heat to cook the breast from the outside in while the skin crisps and bastes the meat. Unlike braising or poaching, oven roasting develops a flavoured, browned crust on the skin through Maillard reaction and fat rendering while the interior rises to the pull temperature of 74 °C / 165 °F — the USDA minimum and culinary standard for safe, juicy breast meat. The bone slows heat penetration relative to boneless breast, requiring an additional 10–15 minutes of cook time; this also means the skin has more time to colour and crisp before the interior overheats. Oven temperatures for bone-in breast typically range from 190–220 °C / 375–425 °F, with cook times of 30–45 minutes depending on size. The MEATER probe must be inserted from the thick end of the breast, horizontal to the bone and at least 1 cm away from it, to the geometric centre of the pectoralis major.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsrostning av benbibehållet, skinnklätt kycklingbröst (*split breast*) använder torr konvektiv värme för att tillaga brösten utifrån medan skinnet krispar och bastar köttet. Den benbibehållna varianten ger mer smakfull, saftigare bröst än benfri version — benet isolerar och saktar temperaturstegringen, vilket minskar risken för överkokning.

Ugn 200–220 °C, 35–45 minuter. Innertemperatur: **74 °C (165 °F)** USDA-minimum. Eftertillagning: 2–3 °C. Sond längsmed bröstets tjockaste del, borta från benet. Vila 5–10 minuter täckt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Baked Chicken Thighs / Bone-in Breast adaptation (inspired by Suzy Karadsheh)
**Inspired by**: Suzy Karadsheh, themediterraneandish.com/baked-chicken-thighs/  
**Serves**: 8 (thighs) / 4 (bone-in breasts) · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min (bone-in breast) · **Rest**: 5–10 min

**Note**: This recipe is primarily for bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs at 425 °F. The author explicitly provides a bone-in breast adaptation with adjusted temperature and time — cited verbatim below.

**Ingredients** (as written for bone-in breast substitution)
- 4–8 bone-in, skin-on chicken breasts (split breasts), trimmed of excess fat
- Extra virgin olive oil (for brushing)
- Kosher salt
- Spice rub: cumin, paprika, dried oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper (the Mediterranean spice blend from the base recipe)
- 3 medium yellow onions, sliced (for the bed)
- 1 tomato, sliced
- ¼ cup water (added to the corners of the baking dish)

**Method** (bone-in breast version per Karadsheh's stated adaptation)
1. Preheat oven to **375 °F / 190 °C**. Brush the bottom of a 9×13-inch baking dish with olive oil. Arrange three quarters of the sliced onions on the bottom.
2. Pat chicken breasts dry. Mix the Mediterranean spice rub and coat the breasts thoroughly on all sides. Arrange skin-side up on the onion bed. Tuck remaining onion and tomato slices into the spaces between the pieces. Pour ¼ cup water into the corners of the baking dish, avoiding the chicken.
3. **Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of the breast horizontally into the centre of the thickest part of the pectoralis major, keeping the tip at least 1 cm away from the breast bone and parallel to the bone.**
4. Bake for **30 to 35 minutes**, or until cooked through. "Watch closely as chicken breasts cook more quickly than thighs."
5. Optional: Place on top rack, broil on high for 1–3 minutes to brown the skin; watch closely to avoid burning.
6. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F).** Karadsheh states: "the internal temperature of chicken breasts should register at 165 °F at the thickest part." Let rest 5–10 minutes.
7. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Final serving temperature: **74–77 °C / 165–170 °F** (juicy breast, browned skin, Mediterranean herbs, tomato-onion base).

### 2. Oven Baked Chicken Breast (inspired by Imma Adamu)
**Inspired by**: Imma Adamu, africanbites.com/oven-baked-chicken/  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 16–18 min (boneless) · **Rest**: 5–10 min

**Note**: This recipe uses boneless, skinless chicken breast. It is included because it documents the target internal temperature (165–170 °F / 74–77 °C) explicitly, with an instant-read thermometer, which confirms the pull temperature for bone-in breast is identical — only the cook time differs (add 10–15 min for bone-in). Imma Adamu is a Nigerian-American food blogger who presents this recipe as a West African-American interpretation using accessible technique.

**Ingredients**
- 4 chicken breasts (boneless, skinless; the same temperature target applies to bone-in)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp Italian herb blend
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- ¼ tsp white pepper
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **425–450 °F / 220–232 °C**.
2. Pound chicken breasts with a mallet through plastic wrap to even thickness (⅓–½ inch). *Note: skip this step for bone-in breasts.*
3. Combine Italian herbs, onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, white pepper, and cayenne; mix well. Rub olive oil then spice mixture over all sides of the breasts.
4. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet in a single layer.
5. **Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the breast.** (For bone-in: enter from the thick end, parallel to the bone, at least 1 cm from the bone.)
6. Bake for **16–18 minutes** (boneless) or approximately 30–40 minutes (bone-in at same temperature). "An instant-read thermometer stuck in the thickest part of the breast will read **165–170 °F (74–77 °C)**."
7. "For safety, it's best to use a thermometer."
8. **Pull at 74–77 °C (165–170 °F).** Let rest 5–10 minutes so juice reabsorbs into the meat.
9. Final serving temperature: **74–77 °C / 165–170 °F** (juicy, caramelised outer layer, African-American spice blend).
