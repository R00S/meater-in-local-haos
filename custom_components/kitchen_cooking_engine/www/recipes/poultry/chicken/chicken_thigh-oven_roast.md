## Cut profile

Oven-roasted chicken thighs are one of the most reliable weeknight cuts — dark-meat fat keeps them moist even when slightly overcooked, and the oven environment renders skin beautifully crisp. Both bone-in and boneless versions work well; bone-in thighs take longer but develop deeper flavour. The USDA minimum of 165 °F (74 °C) is safe, but pulling at 175 °F (79 °C) produces the best texture as collagen breaks down into gelatin.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** minimum — **175 °F (79 °C)** preferred for dark meat

## Source recipes

### 1. Natasha's Kitchen — BBQ Oven-Baked Chicken Thighs
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/juicy-barbecued-chicken-thighs-recipe/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 10 min + 6 hr marinate · **Cook**: 35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 400 °F (204 °C), then increase to 450 °F (232 °C) for glaze
- Internal target: juices run clear; dark meat cooked through — 175 °F (79 °C) recommended
- Time: 30 min at 400 °F + 5 min at 450 °F = 35 min total

**Ingredients**
- 3 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 1 tsp sea salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1 tbsp mayonnaise (in marinade)
- 1 medium onion, sliced
- Your favourite BBQ sauce (for glazing)

**Method**
1. Mix salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and mayonnaise together in a large bowl.
2. Add chicken thighs and sliced onion. Toss to coat completely.
3. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator 6 hours minimum; overnight delivers best results.
4. Preheat oven to 400 °F (204 °C).
5. Remove thighs from marinade, discard onion and used marinade.
6. Place thighs flat, smooth-side up, in a single layer in a rimmed baking dish or roasting pan.
7. Bake at 400 °F for 30 minutes.
8. Increase oven temperature to 450 °F (232 °C).
9. Brush thighs generously with BBQ sauce.
10. Return to oven and bake 5 more minutes until BBQ glaze is set and caramelised.
11. Check internal temperature in the thickest part: target 175 °F (79 °C) for best texture.
12. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

### 2. Skinnytaste — Baked Chicken Thighs with Vegetables
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/baked-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: ~55–60 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 425 °F (218 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) minimum
- Time: approximately 55–60 min for bone-in skin-on thighs roasted on a bed of vegetables

**Ingredients**
- 4 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
- 2 cups Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
- 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and cubed into 1-inch pieces
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 425 °F (218 °C).
2. Toss Brussels sprouts and sweet potato cubes with 1 tbsp olive oil, half the seasoning, salt, and pepper on a large rimmed baking sheet.
3. Spread vegetables in a single layer.
4. Pat chicken thighs dry with paper towels.
5. Rub thighs with remaining olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper.
6. Nestle thighs skin-side up on top of the vegetables — the vegetables act as a roasting rack while also soaking up rendered chicken fat.
7. Roast at 425 °F for 50–60 minutes until skin is deep golden brown and crispy.
8. Check internal temp in the thickest part of the thigh (avoid bone): at least 165 °F (74 °C); ideally 175 °F (79 °C).
9. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

### 3. Argiro Barbarigou — Μπουτάκια Κοτόπουλο στο Φούρνο (Greek Yogurt-Crusted Oven Chicken Thighs)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou (one of Greece's most recognized television chefs), Argiro.gr; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/mpoutakia-kotopoulo-sto-fourno/ (fetched 2026-04-24, language: Greek — ingredients and method translated below).
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min + 4–24 hr marinating · **Cook**: 45 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven: 180 °C (356 °F) static heat (top and bottom), conventional oven
- Internal target: **75 °C (167 °F)** — Greek/EU food-safety minimum for poultry
- Time: 45 minutes; thighs should be nicely golden and well-browned

**Ingredients** *(translated from Greek)*
- 12 bone-in skin-on chicken thigh pieces (drumettes / small thighs)
- 200 g Greek yogurt (full-fat)
- 2 cups (180 g) dried breadcrumbs
- 1 cup (90 g) grated Parmesan (or Kefalotiri/Pecorino)
- 1 tbsp sweet paprika
- 2 pinches freshly grated nutmeg
- Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter, cut into small knobs

**Method** *(translated from Greek)*
1. Place chicken thighs in a baking dish; pour yogurt over them and turn to coat all surfaces thoroughly.
2. Cover with cling film; refrigerate at least 4 hours (overnight for best results).
3. In a wide bowl, combine breadcrumbs, Parmesan, paprika, nutmeg, salt, and pepper; mix well.
4. Remove chicken thighs from yogurt one at a time; press each piece into the breadcrumb mixture on all sides, ensuring a thorough, even coating.
5. Arrange coated thighs in a single layer on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
6. Place a small knob of butter on top of each thigh.
7. If preparing the day before, cover and refrigerate until ready to bake; remove from fridge 30 minutes before baking to come to room temperature.
8. Bake in a well-preheated oven at 180 °C (356 °F) for 45 minutes until golden brown and cooked through to 75 °C (167 °F) internal.

## Practical tips

### Skin crispiness in the oven
- Drying the skin thoroughly before roasting is the single most important step for crispy skin.
- Dry brine: salt the thighs uncovered on a rack in the refrigerator 1–24 hours before roasting — the salt draws out surface moisture and then reabsorbs, seasoning the meat deeply while keeping the surface dry.
- Do not cover the pan during roasting — steam created by a lid or foil tent will soften the skin.

### Bone-in vs. boneless in the oven
- Bone-in: typically 400–425 °F (204–218 °C) for 35–50 min; the bone slows down cooking near the joint.
- Boneless: 400 °F for 20–25 min; cooks faster but the lack of bone means the probe position is simpler.

### Temperature probe placement (oven roast)
- Insert probe from the side of the thigh into the thickest part, avoiding the bone.
- For bone-in thighs, the area immediately next to the bone (which is the last part to cook) gives the most conservative reading.
- Target 175 °F (79 °C) at the thickest part for best texture.

### Pan drippings
- Dark-meat thighs produce excellent pan drippings rich in collagen and flavour.
- After resting the chicken, deglaze the pan with 1/2 cup chicken stock, scraping up the fond.
- Simmer 3–5 min, strain, and serve as a simple pan jus over the thighs.

### Seasoning variations for oven-roasted thighs
| Style | Approach |
|-------|---------|
| Classic | Garlic powder, smoked paprika, Italian seasoning, salt, pepper |
| Honey-mustard glaze | Dijon + honey + apple cider vinegar — brush last 10 min |
| Lemon-herb | Lemon zest, thyme, rosemary, garlic, olive oil |
| Shawarma | Cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, garlic, lemon |
| Balsamic | Balsamic reduction glaze — brush last 8–10 min at high heat |

### MEATER probe guidance for thighs
- Insert probe into the thickest part of the thigh, avoiding the bone.
- Bone-in: push probe from the side, angle it away from the central bone.
- Boneless: insert from the end along the length of the thigh.
- Target alert: **170 °F (77 °C)** — 5-min rest brings to 175 °F.

### Storage and leftovers
- Oven-roasted thighs keep refrigerated up to 4 days.
- Reheat in a 375 °F oven for 12–15 min on a wire rack to restore skin crispiness.
- Debone and shred cold leftover thighs for tacos, soup, or fried rice.

> **Summary**: Roast at 400–425 °F; bone-in 50–60 min; boneless 20–35 min.
> Pull at 170 °F (77 °C); rest 5 min → 175 °F (79 °C). Optional glaze at high temp last 5 min.

**Quick reference**: 400–425 °F; bone-in 50–60 min; boneless 25–35 min. Target 175 °F (79 °C) for best dark-meat texture.
