## Cut profile

Bone-in, skin-on chicken thigh retains the femur bone and intact skin. The bone
insulates the meat at the joint end, slowing penetration of oven heat and adding
roughly 20–30% to cook time compared to boneless thighs of the same weight. The skin,
exposed to high dry oven heat, renders its subcutaneous fat and Maillard-browns to a
crisp, flavourful crust — the primary reason cooks prefer this format for oven roasting.
Dark-meat muscle fibres (soleus, biceps femoris and related upper-leg muscles) have a
higher fat content (6–10% vs 1–2% for breast) and more connective tissue, making them
forgiving across a wide internal temperature range.

USDA minimum for all poultry is 74°C (165°F). Collagen in the connective tissue begins
converting to gelatin above 70°C; extended cooking at 74–82°C yields progressively more
relaxed, silky texture as the connective tissue renders. For oven roast at high heat
(180–240°C), most mainstream sources target 74–75°C as the practical pull temperature,
relying on skin crispiness and visual doneness as secondary cues. Carryover from oven
roast is typically 2–3°C, bringing the thigh to ~77°C at rest.

**Probe placement**: Insert the probe horizontally from the outer exposed muscle edge,
keeping the tip in the geometric centre of the thickest part of the thigh, parallel to
the femur and at least 1 cm from the bone surface. The bone heats slower than meat and
reads false-low if the probe tip contacts it.

### 1. Crispy Herb Baked Chicken with Gravy (inspired by RecipeTin Eats)

**Inspired by**: Nagi Maehashi, RecipeTin Eats;
https://www.recipetineats.com/crispy-baked-chicken-with-gravy/ (fetched 2026-05-06).
*Australian food writer. Bone-in skin-on thighs and drumsticks roasted at very high
heat for a "shortcut roast chicken" approach. Recipe was developed for crowd catering
(Christmas lunch for 40 guests).*

**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ optional overnight fridge dry) · **Cook**: 35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven: 240°C / 465°F (220°C fan)
- Internal target: **75°C / 165°F** (or juices run clear when pierced)
- Time: 35 minutes

**Ingredients**
- 1.8 kg bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (and/or drumsticks)
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil (not olive oil — smoke point too low for 240°C)

*Herb rub:*
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried sage (or ½ tsp ground sage)
- ¾ tsp dried marjoram
- 1 tsp salt (coarse / kosher)
- ½ tsp black pepper

*Simple gravy (optional):*
- 3 tbsp plain flour
- 375 ml (1½ cups) low-sodium chicken stock
- ¾ tsp dark soy sauce or gravy browning liquid
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Method**
1. (Optional) Pat chicken skin dry with paper towels. Refrigerate overnight uncovered
   on a rack to dry the skin surface — this maximises crispiness.
2. Preheat oven to 240°C / 465°F (220°C fan). Line a large rimmed baking tray with
   foil, then parchment paper; place a wire rack on the tray.
3. Combine all herb rub ingredients in a small bowl.
4. Pat chicken skin dry again with paper towels.
5. Drizzle oil over chicken and rub all over, getting into all crevices. Sprinkle with
   herb rub; use hands to coat thoroughly, including under the skin.
6. Arrange thigh pieces skin-side up on the rack; drumsticks skin-side up.
7. Roast for 35 minutes until internal temperature in the thickest part (probe parallel
   to bone, not touching) reads **75°C / 165°F**, or juices run clear when pierced.
8. (Optional) If skin is not brown enough after 35 min, switch to broil/grill on high
   for 1–3 minutes — watch closely, it browns very fast.
9. Tent loosely with foil for 5 minutes before serving.
10. *Gravy*: Scrape all pan juices and fat off the paper into a saucepan. Over medium
    heat, whisk in flour until combined. Pour in stock gradually, whisking continuously.
    Add soy sauce and pepper; stir 3–5 minutes until syrupy. Adjust salt.

**Pull at 75°C (165°F)** · Final serving temperature: **~77°C / ~171°F** (after 5 min rest)

### 2. Roasted Lemon Chicken Thighs with Potatoes (inspired by The Woks of Life)

**Inspired by**: Bill, Judy, Sarah & Kaitlin Leung, The Woks of Life;
https://thewoksoflife.com/roasted-lemon-chicken-thighs-potatoes/ (fetched 2026-05-06).
*Chinese-American family food blog. A simple 7-ingredient weeknight recipe that begins
on the stovetop for skin-side searing before finishing in the oven. Lemon slices and
garlic roast alongside the chicken and potatoes.*

**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–37 min (5–7 min stovetop + 30 min oven) · **Rest**: 3 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Stovetop sear: medium-high heat
- Oven: 425°F / 218°C
- Internal target: "cooked through and potatoes are tender" — source does not specify
  an internal temperature; no probe insertion step given. US USDA standard 165°F (74°C)
  for poultry applies.
- Time in oven: 30 minutes

**Ingredients**
- 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- ¼ cup (60 ml) olive oil
- 1½ lb (680 g) Eastern or Yukon Gold potatoes, scrubbed, cut into 1-inch chunks
- 1 lemon, sliced
- 10 cloves garlic, peeled
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro or flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C). Season chicken thighs generously on both sides with
   salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a roasting pan set over two burners on medium-high heat.
3. Sear chicken skin-side down in the hot oil for 5–7 minutes until the skin is golden.
4. Flip chicken. Add potato chunks, lemon slices, and peeled garlic cloves to the pan;
   toss everything in the oil so potatoes and garlic are well coated.
5. Transfer the roasting pan to the oven and cook for 30 minutes, or until the chicken
   is cooked through and the potatoes are tender.
   *(Source does not state a probe temperature or specify probe placement. Insert probe
   from the side into the thickest part, parallel to bone, away from bone, to verify
   74°C / 165°F minimum.)*
6. Remove from oven; stir in chopped herbs and serve immediately.

**Pull at ~74°C (165°F) minimum (USDA)** · Source uses visual/time cue; no stated final temperature.

### 3. Baked Chicken Thighs with Spiced Tomato Rub (inspired by The Mediterranean Dish)

**Inspired by**: Suzy Karadsheh (Egyptian-American food writer), The Mediterranean Dish;
https://www.themediterraneandish.com/baked-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2026-05-06).
*A spiced tomato paste–olive oil–cumin–paprika wet rub applied without marinating time.
Layered over sliced onions and a fresh tomato in a baking dish, finished with a brief
broil for colour. Egyptian/Mediterranean tradition.*

**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 40 min oven + 1–3 min broil · **Rest**: 5–10 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven: 425°F / 218°C
- Internal target: **165°F / 74°C** ("juices run clear … should register at 165°F at
  the thickest part away from the bone using an instant-read thermometer")
- Time: approximately 40 minutes at 425°F + 1–3 min broil

**Ingredients**
- 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs, trimmed of excess fat
- Kosher salt
- Extra-virgin olive oil (for the baking dish)
- 3 medium yellow onions, halved and thinly sliced
- 1 vine-ripe tomato, halved and sliced
- ¼ cup (60 ml) water
- 5 tbsp tomato paste
- ⅓ cup (80 ml) extra-virgin olive oil (for the wet rub)
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp smoked paprika (or sweet paprika)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground black pepper
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to garnish

**Method**
1. Arrange one rack in the centre of the oven and another ~15 cm (6 inches) from the
   broiler element. Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C).
2. Sprinkle chicken thighs with kosher salt on both sides and underneath the skin. Set
   aside while making the rub.
3. In a small bowl or measuring cup, whisk together tomato paste, olive oil, lemon
   juice, minced garlic, oregano, smoked paprika, cumin, and black pepper until smooth.
4. Coat chicken thoroughly on all surfaces, including underneath the skin.
5. Brush the base of a 23 × 33 cm (9 × 13 inch) baking dish with olive oil. Arrange
   ¾ of the sliced onions on the bottom. Place chicken on top, skin-side up. Tuck
   remaining onions and sliced tomato into the spaces around the chicken. Pour water
   into the corners, taking care not to disturb the rub.
6. Insert probe into the thickest part of one thigh, keeping the tip parallel to the
   bone and at least 1 cm from the bone surface.
7. Bake until probe reads **165°F (74°C)** — approximately 40 minutes.
8. Move dish to the top rack; switch oven to broil on high. Broil 1–3 minutes until
   the top of the chicken browns lightly — watch closely to avoid burning.
9. Rest 5–10 minutes. Garnish with chopped parsley. Serve with couscous, warm
   flatbread, or rice.

**Pull at 165°F (74°C)** · Final serving temperature: **165–168°F / 74–76°C**

### 4. Ugnsstekta kycklinglår (Oven-Roasted Chicken Thighs) (inspired by Köket.se)

**Inspired by**: Köket.se (Swedish TV4 food site);
https://www.koket.se/jessica_frej/varmratter/kyckling_och_fagel/ungstekta_kycklinglar
(fetched 2026-05-06; language: Swedish — ingredients and method translated below).
*A simple bone-in thigh preparation from a Swedish TV cooking segment: lemon, rosemary,
garlic, high-heat oven, served alongside a romaine salad with a homemade aioli-style
dressing. Swedish home cooking norm: visual doneness, no thermometer guidance given.*

**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 20–30 min · **Rest**: 3–5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven: 180°C (356°F) — standard Swedish household oven setting
- Internal target: **not stated in source** — visual cue "done after 20–30 min"
- ⚠️ Note: 20–30 min at 180°C for full-sized bone-in thighs is at the short end of
  typical ranges; actual time will depend on thigh size. Swedish Livsmedelsverket
  recommends 70°C minimum for poultry (lower than USDA's 74°C).

**Ingredients** *(translated from Swedish)*
- 8 kycklinglår — bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 citron — lemon (squeezed over the chicken)
- 50 g färsk rosmarin — fresh rosemary
- 1 vitlök — head of garlic (3 cloves chopped for chicken, 2 grated for dressing)

*For the romaine salad:*
- 2 romansallad — romaine lettuce hearts
- 1 gurka — cucumber
- 2 tomater — tomatoes
- 1 knippe rädisa — bunch of radishes

*For the vitlöksdressing (garlic dressing):*
- 1 ägg — egg yolk (for emulsification)
- 1 msk senap — 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 3 dl olivolja — 300 ml olive oil
- Sherryvinäger — sherry vinegar, to taste
- Salt, to taste

**Method** *(translated from Swedish)*
1. Set oven to 180°C (356°F).
2. Place chicken thighs in an ovenproof dish in a single layer, skin-side up.
3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
4. Squeeze the lemon over the thighs.
5. Coarsely chop the fresh rosemary and 3 garlic cloves; scatter over the thighs.
6. Bake in oven approximately 20–30 minutes.
   *(Source does not state a probe temperature. Insert probe from the side, parallel to
   bone, into thickest part; verify at least 70°C / 158°F by Swedish standard or 74°C /
   165°F by USDA standard before serving.)*
7. While chicken bakes, prepare the salad: halve and slice romaine hearts, radishes,
   cucumber, and tomatoes; combine in a bowl.
8. Make the dressing: whisk egg yolk, sherry vinegar, mustard, salt, and 2 grated
   garlic cloves until combined; very slowly drizzle in olive oil while whisking
   continuously until emulsified to a light, pourable consistency. Adjust seasoning.
9. Drizzle dressing over salad. Serve chicken and salad with bread.

**Pull at minimum 70°C (158°F) Swedish standard / 74°C (165°F) USDA** · Source uses
visual/time cue; no stated final temperature.

### 5. Rosemary Roast Chicken Thighs, New Potatoes, Asparagus & Garlic (inspired by BBC Good Food)

**Inspired by**: Barney Desmazery, BBC Good Food magazine (May 2013);
archived at https://web.archive.org/web/20241010111210/https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/rosemary-roast-chicken-thighs-new-potatoes-asparagus-garlic
(fetched 2026-05-06). *A one-tray lighter roast. Potatoes, asparagus, and garlic are
pre-roasted foil-covered at 200°C to begin softening, then the chicken thighs go in
uncovered for the final 25–30 minutes. No internal temperature given; UK/British
tradition uses visual doneness.*

**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 40–45 min total (15 min foil + 25–30 min uncovered) · **Rest**: 3–5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven: 200°C / 180°C fan / Gas 6 (392°F / 356°F fan)
- Internal target: **not stated** — "crisp and cooked through" (UK Food Standards
  Agency recommendation for poultry: 75°C / 167°F)
- Time: 15 min foil-covered (vegetables only) then 25–30 min uncovered with chicken

**Ingredients**
- 750 g new potatoes (preferably Jersey Royals), halved
- 2 large bunches asparagus, woody ends snapped off
- 1 whole garlic bulb, cloves separated but unpeeled
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lemon, halved
- Small handful fresh rosemary sprigs
- 8 chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on implied by recipe method and cook time)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat oven to 200°C / 180°C fan / Gas 6. Put the potatoes, asparagus, and separated
   garlic cloves into a large roasting dish (approx. 30 × 20 cm). Add olive oil and
   lots of seasoning. Squeeze all the juice from both lemon halves over the top; cut
   squeezed lemon into chunks and add them to the dish. Toss everything together well.
   Cover the dish tightly with foil and roast for 15 minutes.
2. Remove the foil and mix the rosemary sprigs through the vegetables. Season the
   chicken thighs on both sides. Arrange evenly in the dish over the vegetables,
   skin-side up.
3. Roast uncovered for 25–30 minutes until the potatoes are tender and the chicken is
   crisp and cooked through.
   *(Source does not state a probe temperature. Insert probe from the side into thickest
   part, parallel to bone, to verify 75°C / 167°F by UK FSA guidance.)*
4. Serve the dish in the middle of the table for everyone to share.

**Pull at ~75°C (167°F) by UK FSA guidance** · Source uses visual cue; no stated
final temperature.

## Temperature consensus

| Tradition | Preferred doneness | Pull / internal target |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Australian (RecipeTin Eats) | "75°C / 165°F" | 75°C / 165°F (explicit) |
| Chinese-American (The Woks of Life) | "cooked through" | Not stated (USDA 74°C implied) |
| Egyptian-American (The Mediterranean Dish) | "165°F" | 74°C / 165°F (explicit) |
| Swedish (Köket.se) | Visual doneness, 20–30 min | Not stated (70–74°C range implied) |
| British (BBC Good Food) | "crisp and cooked through" | Not stated (UK FSA 75°C implied) |

The observed range from sources that give an explicit temperature is 74–75°C (165–167°F).
Sources that give only visual or time cues (Woks of Life, Köket.se, BBC Good Food) are
consistent with this range in practice: the Swedish source's 180°C / 20–30 min is at
the shorter end for large bone-in thighs and may target 70–74°C; the British and
American visual-cue sources are consistent with 74–75°C by USDA or UK FSA convention.
There is no tradition represented here that targets the higher dark-meat collagen range
of 79°C (175°F) for oven roast at high heat — all sources use high oven temperatures
(180–240°C) that yield a crisp skin and a juicy 74–75°C interior as the endpoint.
The `safe` doneness band (74–77°C target) covers all five traditions for this specific
method. The `dark_meat_optimal` band (79°C / 175°F) applies more naturally to slower
or lower-temperature methods (braise, low-and-slow) where the collagen has more time
to render.

## What makes this method special

High-heat oven roasting of bone-in chicken thighs achieves two simultaneous goals:
fat-render-and-Maillard browning of the skin at the surface, and safe internal cooking
of the dark muscle tissue around the bone. The skin acts as a natural basting layer —
as the subcutaneous fat renders and migrates outward it bastes the surface, while also
providing a flavour barrier that keeps the flesh below moist. Because the bone conducts
heat inward from the cavity only slowly (bone thermal conductivity is ~0.4 W/m·K vs.
~0.5 W/m·K for meat), the meat nearest the joint is always the last to reach target
temperature; this is why probe placement away from the bone is critical and why
pull-at-165°F (74°C) is universally applied here rather than the safer lean-meat margins
used for breast. The higher fat and collagen content of dark meat means the thigh
tolerates the oven's radiant heat load from above without drying the interior, giving
the cook wide margin for timing error. Oven temperature determines skin outcome:
200–240°C produces crisped, rendered skin in 35–45 minutes; 180°C produces a slower,
less shattering skin but a more forgiving time window.

## Summary

Bone-in chicken thigh is ideally suited to high-heat oven roasting: the fat-rich dark
muscle resists drying at the 74–75°C pull temperatures all five sources target, and the
skin renders to a crisp, flavourful crust that boneless formats cannot replicate. The
bone adds approximately 20–30% to cook time but does not change the target temperature —
probe placement parallel to the femur and away from the bone surface is the critical
practical difference versus the boneless cut. For oven roast specifically, `safe`
(74–75°C) is the practitioner consensus, not `dark_meat_optimal` (79°C); the additional
collagen-render benefit at 79°C is more relevant to braise or lower-temperature methods
with longer dwell time.
