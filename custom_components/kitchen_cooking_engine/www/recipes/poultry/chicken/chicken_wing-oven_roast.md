## Cut profile

Oven roasting chicken wings at high heat (200–230 °C / 390–445 °F) is the most accessible method for home cooks wanting crispy results without dedicated equipment. The oven works by surrounding the wing in hot air; the key to achieving deep-fried-level crispiness is a combination of three factors: (1) drying the skin thoroughly before cooking, (2) coating in baking powder (alkaline — accelerates Maillard browning and desiccates skin), and (3) using a wire rack over a sheet pan so hot air circulates underneath the wings, preventing the bottom from steaming.

Unlike the air fryer, a conventional oven has a larger chamber and slower air circulation, so drying time is longer (50–60 min vs 16–22 min for air fryer). However, the oven can accommodate larger batches without quality loss.

Wings come out with golden-brown, crispy skin and fully cooked dark meat. The thin wing bones and high skin-to-meat ratio mean the interior reaches 165 °F (74 °C) well before the skin has had time to over-brown at 220 °C, making oven wings exceptionally forgiving. Carryover is 1–2 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Taste of Home — Baked Chicken Wings
**Source**: Nancy Mock, reviewed by Lindsay D. Mattison; Taste of Home; https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/baked-chicken-wings/ (fetched 2026-04-27).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 50–60 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: **425 °F (220 °C)**
- Internal target: not explicitly stated; recipe criterion is "golden brown and juices run clear" (~165 °F / 74 °C per USDA poultry standard)
- Time: 50–60 min, turning every 20 minutes

**Ingredients**
- 4 lbs chicken wing pieces (wingettes and drumettes), separated
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp black pepper
- Pinch cayenne pepper
- 2 tbsp butter, melted
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- **1 tbsp baking powder** (key — draws moisture from skin)

*Dipping sauce:*
- ½ cup sour cream
- 2 tbsp buttermilk
- 1–2 tbsp Sriracha
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 425 °F (220 °C). Line a 15×10×1-inch baking pan with foil or parchment. Pour butter and oil over foil; brush to coat evenly.
2. Separate whole wings into drumettes and wingettes; discard tips.
3. Pat wings completely dry with paper towels.
4. In a small bowl, combine salt, onion powder, garlic powder, pepper, and cayenne. Sprinkle over both sides of wings, patting to adhere.
5. Place baking powder in a shallow dish. Add wing pieces a few at a time and toss to coat; shake off excess.
6. Arrange wings in a single layer on the prepared pan — leave space between each piece.
7. Bake at 425 °F (220 °C) for **50–60 minutes**, turning every 20 minutes, until golden brown and juices run clear.
8. Meanwhile, whisk together dipping sauce ingredients in a small bowl. Refrigerate until serving.
9. Serve wings hot with dipping sauce.

**Notes**:
- Baking powder draws moisture out of the skin during cooking, leaving behind dry skin that browns and crisps easily.
- Space between wings is critical — crowded wings steam instead of crisping.
- Recipe explicitly discourages using a lower oven temperature: "chicken wings are technically white meat, which doesn't need low-and-slow temperatures to become tender. Blasting the wing in a high-temperature oven is the best way to ensure crispy skin and juicy meat."
