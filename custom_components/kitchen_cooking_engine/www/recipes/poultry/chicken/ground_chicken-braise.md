## Cut profile

Ground chicken braised in liquid is a moist-heat method that keeps the lean meat from drying out and develops a flavourful sauce simultaneously. Because ground poultry is mixed throughout with bacteria (unlike whole muscle), every part of the meat must reach safe temperature. Braising is forgiving — the surrounding liquid prevents the meat from going above 212 °F (100 °C), but interior temp still easily exceeds 165 °F (74 °C) with adequate cooking time.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — USDA minimum for ground poultry; visual: no pink inside

## Source recipes

### 1. Natasha's Kitchen — Chicken Meatballs in Cream Sauce (Tefteli)
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/chicken-meatballs/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 30 min total · **Rest**: 0 min (serve in sauce)

**Temperature guidance**:
- Sauté phase: medium heat, 4 min first side + 3 min covered after flip
- Braise phase: simmer in cream sauce ~10 min
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C), no pink

**Ingredients (meatballs)**
- 1–1.25 lb ground chicken (thigh meat preferred for moisture and flavour)
- 1 cup cooked white rice, cooled completely
- 1 large egg
- 1 small onion, grated on the large holes of a box grater
- 1 garlic clove, pressed through a garlic press
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (for dredging before browning)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (for browning)

**Ingredients (braise / cream sauce)**
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 2 cups chicken broth (low-sodium)
- 1/4 cup sour cream
- 1/2 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine ground chicken, cooled cooked rice, egg, grated onion, pressed garlic, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
2. Mix gently with a fork until just combined — overworking makes the meatballs dense.
3. With wet hands (prevents sticking), roll mixture into 1–1.25 inch balls. Flatten slightly for even cooking.
4. Pour flour onto a shallow plate. Dredge each meatball in flour, shaking off excess.
5. Heat oil in a large wide skillet over medium heat.
6. Add meatballs in a single layer without crowding (work in batches if needed).
7. Cook undisturbed 4 minutes on the first side until lightly golden.
8. Flip gently with tongs. Cover pan with a lid. Cook 3 more minutes.
9. Transfer meatballs to a plate. Reserve pan drippings.
10. **Make the braise sauce**: Melt butter in the same skillet over medium heat.
11. Whisk in flour and cook 1 minute, stirring, to make a roux.
12. Gradually pour in chicken broth, whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
13. Stir in sour cream, paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring to a gentle simmer.
14. Return meatballs to the sauce. Stir gently to coat.
15. Simmer on low heat 10 minutes, turning meatballs once, until sauce thickens and meatballs are cooked through.
16. Check internal temperature: target 165 °F (74 °C), no pink inside.
17. Serve over mashed potatoes, egg noodles, or rice.

### 2. Skinnytaste — Ground Poultry Braised Skillet (adapted)
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/ground-turkey/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 0 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Sauté phase: high heat 5 min for browning
- Braise/simmer phase: low heat 12–14 min covered
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C); no pink

**Ingredients**
- 1 lb ground chicken
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 small onion, diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 1 cup diced vegetables (bell pepper, zucchini, or whatever is on hand)
- 1 tbsp neutral oil

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
2. Add ground chicken. Cook without stirring 2–3 minutes to develop a brown crust on the bottom.
3. Break up into large chunks with a wooden spoon. Season with salt and cumin.
4. Continue cooking on high, stirring occasionally, until no longer pink — about 5 minutes total.
5. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion and garlic. Cook 2 minutes until softened.
6. Add diced vegetables and stir to combine.
7. Pour in chicken broth. Bring to a simmer.
8. Reduce heat to low, cover pan tightly.
9. Braise 12–14 minutes until liquid is mostly absorbed and chicken is fully cooked through.
10. Check internal temp: 165 °F (74 °C).
11. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve over rice or in tacos.

### 3. Swasthi Shreekanth — Chicken Keema (Indian Spiced Ground Chicken Curry)
**Source**: Swasthi Shreekanth, IndianHealthyRecipes.com; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/chicken-keema/ (fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 3–4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Pan heat: medium-high for initial sauté; reduce to medium for simmering
- Internal target: **165 °F (74 °C)** — all ground poultry fully cooked with no pink
- Time: ~20 min total; keema (minced meat) cooks faster than whole cuts due to small particle size

**Ingredients**
- 500 g ground chicken (keema)
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
- 1½ tsp oil or ghee
- 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1–2 green chillies, slit
- ¾ tsp garam masala
- ½ tsp ground coriander (dhania powder)
- ½ tsp chilli powder (or to taste)
- ¼ tsp turmeric
- ¾ tsp salt (or to taste)
- 2 tbsp fresh coriander (cilantro), chopped

**Method**
1. Heat oil or ghee in a skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat.
2. Add onion; sauté 5–6 minutes until golden brown (this builds the base flavour).
3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chillies; cook 1–2 minutes until raw smell disappears.
4. Add tomatoes, salt, chilli powder, ground coriander, and turmeric; cook over medium heat until tomatoes become mushy and the oil separates from the masala, ~5 minutes.
5. Add ground chicken; break up any lumps thoroughly with a wooden spoon.
6. Stir well to combine chicken with masala; cook over medium heat 8–10 minutes, stirring regularly, until no pink remains — internal temperature in the largest clump should read **165 °F (74 °C)**.
7. Reduce heat; add garam masala and fresh coriander. Stir and cook a further 1–2 minutes.
8. Taste and adjust salt. Serve with naan, paratha, roti, or steamed basmati rice.
9. *For a curry version*: add 120 ml coconut milk or cashew cream in step 7 and simmer 3 minutes.

## Practical tips

### Why braise for ground chicken?
- Ground chicken is very lean (~93% lean when using breast meat). Direct high-heat cooking with no liquid quickly dries it out.
- Braising surrounds the protein in flavourful liquid, keeping it moist throughout while building a sauce simultaneously.
- The rice binder used in Natasha's Kitchen meatballs is particularly effective because rice absorbs and retains water, continuously releasing moisture into the meat during cooking.

### Temperature safety for ground poultry
- Unlike whole-muscle chicken where only the surface contacts cutting boards (and thus carries bacteria), ground chicken has bacteria distributed throughout.
- Every internal particle must reach 165 °F (74 °C).
- In a braise, the surrounding liquid is typically 185–200 °F (85–93 °C), so the interior of meatballs reaches temperature rapidly once submerged — usually within 8–12 minutes of simmering.

### Liquid ratios
- Too little liquid causes the bottom layer to scorch before the interior of the meatballs is cooked.
- Too much liquid dilutes the sauce and may make the exterior of formed pieces soggy.
- Ideal: meatballs should be half-submerged in liquid while braising. Spoon or ladle liquid over tops as it reduces.

### Make-ahead potential
- Braised ground chicken dishes reheat excellently — the liquid prevents the meat from drying out during reheating.
- Store in the braising liquid and gently reheat covered on low heat or in a 325 °F oven for 15–20 minutes.

### Variations on braised ground chicken
| Style | Liquid | Aromatics |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Cream sauce (Tefteli) | Chicken broth + sour cream | Paprika, dill |
| Tomato | Crushed tomatoes + broth | Basil, oregano, garlic |
| Asian | Soy, ginger, rice wine | Star anise, scallion |
| Coconut curry | Coconut milk + broth | Curry paste, lemongrass |
