## Cut profile

Ground chicken (tavuk kıyması / poulet haché / Hühnerhackfleisch) is a lean, versatile protein that is grilled as köfte (elongated meatballs), patties, or flat burgers. Because ground chicken is lighter and drier than beef or lamb, it benefits from added moisture — egg, milk, yoghurt, or applesauce — and binding agents such as breadcrumbs, flour, or soaked bread. Flavour-boosters such as grated onion, garlic, and spice blends are essential. Ground chicken must be cooked to a safe internal temperature throughout; unlike whole muscle cuts, no pink is acceptable. The MEATER probe is inserted horizontally into the thickest part of the patty or köfte from the side, positioned centrally, away from the grill surface.

Target internal temperature: **74 °C (165 °F)** — the USDA minimum for all ground poultry; confirmed by multiple sources.

## Styckesprofil

Malet kyckling (*tavuk kıyması* / *poulet haché* / *Hühnerhackfleisch*) grillas som köfte (avlånga köttbullar), biffar eller platta burgare. Eftersom malet kyckling är ljust och magert krävs smaktillsats (lök, örter, kryddor) och fuktbindare (ägg, brödsmulor) för att hålla formen och förhindra uttorkning.

Grilla vid medel-hög direkt värme, 3–4 minuter per sida för köfte/biffar. Innertemperatur: **74 °C (165 °F)** USDA. Eftertillagning: 2–3 °C. Sond längsmed köfte-spettet in till mitten. Vila 3–5 minuter täckt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Izgara Tavuk Köftesi (Turkish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/izgara-tavuk-koftesi/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Izgara Tavuk Köftesi — Turkish grilled chicken meatballs (köfte)."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 15 min + 1 hr rest · **Cook**: 12–15 min on grill

**Ingredients**
- 2 chicken breasts, roughly cubed
- 1 small onion, quartered
- 3 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp breadcrumbs (galeta unu)
- 1 tbsp plain flour
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp milk
- 1 tsp ground red pepper (toz kırmızı biber)
- 1 tsp black pepper (karabiber)
- ½ tsp ground cumin (kimyon)
- 1 tsp salt

**Method**
1. Place chicken breast pieces, onion, and garlic in a food processor. Pulse to a uniform fine mince — do not over-process to a paste; small bits of texture are desirable.
2. Transfer mince to a large bowl. Add egg, milk, breadcrumbs, flour, red pepper, black pepper, cumin, and salt.
3. Mix firmly with your hands until fully combined and the mixture is cohesive.
4. Cover and refrigerate for **1 hour** to firm the mixture and allow flavours to develop.
5. Preheat grill to medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
6. Divide mixture into 12–16 portions. With lightly oiled hands, shape each portion into an elongated köfte around a flat skewer, or into a thick oval patty.
7. Grill over medium-high heat for 5–6 minutes per side, turning only once.
8. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest köfte from the end before placing on grill. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)** — the USDA minimum for ground poultry.
9. **Rest 3–5 min** before serving with flatbread, sliced tomato, onion, and sumac.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 2. Tori Avey — Mediterranean Grilled Chicken Burgers (Israeli / Levantine tradition)
**Inspired by**: Tori Avey; https://toriavey.com/mediterranean-turkey-burgers/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Mediterranean Turkey Burgers — can be made with ground turkey or ground chicken; Levantine-spiced grilled patties."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–15 min on grill

**Ingredients**
- 450 g (1 lb) ground chicken (or turkey)
- ¼ cup fresh basil, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp yellow onion, minced
- 1 egg
- ¾ tsp smoked paprika
- ¼ tsp ground cumin
- ¼ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp kosher salt
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- 1½ tbsp unsweetened applesauce (keeps patties moist)
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

**Method**
1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl. Mix gently with hands until just combined — do not overmix.
2. Divide into 4 equal portions and form into patties approximately 2 cm (¾ in) thick.
3. Preheat grill to medium heat. Oil the grate well.
4. Grill patties for 10–15 minutes total, flipping every 3–4 minutes.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest patty from the side before placing on grill. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**. *(Tori Avey explicitly states: "cook to an internal temperature of 165 °F / 74 °C".)*
6. **Rest 3–5 min**. Serve in pita or a burger bun with tzatziki, tomato, and fresh herbs.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 3. African Bites — Jamaican Chicken Patties (Jamaican / West African tradition)
**Inspired by**: Immaculate Bites (African Bites); https://www.africanbites.com/jamaican-chicken-patties/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Jamaican Chicken Patties — Scotch bonnet-spiced chicken filling, used here as a spiced grilled patty without the pastry shell."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min on grill

*Note: The original recipe is a baked pastry (flaky turnover). The spiced chicken filling is used here as the basis for a grilled patty by adding binding agents.*

**Ingredients** *(filling adapted for grilling)*
- 500 g ground chicken
- 3 spring onions (scallions), finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- ½ tsp allspice
- ½ tsp curry powder
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- ¼ tsp white pepper
- 1 Scotch bonnet (or habanero), minced
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 50 g dried breadcrumbs (for binding)
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp oil
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. In a frying pan, heat oil over medium heat. Cook spring onions and garlic until softened, 2–3 minutes.
2. Add chicken mince; cook through with all spices — paprika, allspice, curry, thyme, white pepper, Scotch bonnet. Stir to coat.
3. Remove from heat and allow to cool completely.
4. Once cooled, mix in breadcrumbs and egg to bind. Form into flat oval patties about 2 cm thick.
5. Grill over medium heat for 5–6 minutes per side.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest patty from the side. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
7. **Rest 3–5 min** before serving with rice and peas, plantain, or in a roll.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 4. TASTE — Chicken Patties with Preserved Lemon (South African tradition)
**Inspired by**: TASTE magazine South Africa; https://taste.co.za/recipes/chicken-patties/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Chicken Patties with Preserved Lemon and Green Tahini — South African-Mediterranean fusion patties."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min on grill

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg chicken mince
- 3 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
- 3 tbsp fresh coriander (cilantro), chopped
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp chicken seasoning (or mixed spice)
- ½ tsp fennel seeds, lightly crushed
- ½ tsp cumin seeds, lightly crushed
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 100 g feta, crumbled
- 2 tbsp preserved lemon rind, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- Salt, black pepper

*To serve:*
- Full-cream yoghurt or green tahini dip

**Method**
1. Combine chicken mince with dill, coriander, paprika, garlic, chicken seasoning, fennel seeds, cumin seeds, and ground coriander.
2. Gently fold in crumbled feta and preserved lemon rind. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Drizzle in olive oil and brown sugar; mix until just combined.
4. Shape into 8–10 patties, each about 1.5 cm thick. Refrigerate 15 minutes to firm up.
5. Preheat grill to medium-high; oil the grate. Grill patties 5–6 minutes per side until cooked through.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest patty from the side. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
7. **Rest 3–5 min**. Serve with yoghurt, green tahini sauce, and flatbread.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA minimum — ground poultry | 74 °C (165 °F) | Confirmed by Tori Avey and USDA |
| Safe and moist | 74–76 °C (165–169 °F) | Optimal for all grilled chicken patties/köfte |
| Overcooked / dry | > 79 °C (175 °F) | Lean ground chicken dries out quickly past this point |
