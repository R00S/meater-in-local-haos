## Cut profile

A whole duck presents a significant cooking challenge: the breast meat is best at medium-rare (135–145 °F) while the legs and thighs need higher temperatures (165–175 °F) to break down their tougher connective tissue. This conflict is resolved either by accepting slightly overcooked breast in exchange for properly done legs, or by using a low-and-slow approach that slowly renders the abundant subcutaneous fat while keeping the breast moist. The USDA minimum for whole poultry is 165 °F measured in the thigh. Whole ducks weigh 4–6 lb (1.8–2.7 kg) on average and carry far more fat than chicken — that fat must be rendered out to achieve crackling skin.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C) in the thigh** — USDA minimum for whole poultry. Breast will be well-done at this point; accept this or use the low-and-slow method which keeps breast juicier.

## Source recipes

### 1. Slow-Roast Duck (Czech-style, from Apetit Online) (inspired by BBC Good Food)
**Inspired by**: BBC Good Food editorial team, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-roast-duck (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 13 hr · **Rest**: 15–20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Phase 1 oven temp: 90 °C / 70 °C fan / Gas ¼ (194 °F / 158 °F fan)
- Phase 2 oven temp: 120 °C / 100 °C fan (248 °F / 212 °F fan)
- Internal target (thigh): 165 °F (74 °C)
- Total cook time: approximately 13 hours (7 hr Phase 1 + 6 hr Phase 2)
- Final 1 hour: uncovered with honey glaze

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole duck, approximately 2 kg (4.4 lb)
- Generous sea salt (inside cavity, all over skin, pressed into all folds)
- Ground cumin, sprinkled all over
- A little water (added to bottom of the roasting tin)
- 2 tbsp honey (for final-hour glaze)
- Roasting tin with lid, or lidded ovenproof casserole (must be able to cover for 12 of 13 hours)

**Method**
1. Rinse the whole duck inside and out under cold running water. Pat completely dry with paper towels — inside and out.
2. Trim excess fat from the rump and neck cavity opening. Duck has far more fat than chicken; some excess can be removed without affecting the final result.
3. Season very generously with salt all over the skin, inside the cavity, and pressed into every fold and crease. The salt helps draw out moisture and seasons the meat deeply over the long cook time. Sprinkle cumin over skin.
4. Place duck in a deep roasting tin or lidded ovenproof saucepan. Add a small amount of water to the bottom of the tin (this prevents the rendered fat from burning during the first phase).
5. Cover tightly with a lid or heavy-duty foil. There should be no steam escaping.
6. **Phase 1**: Place in oven at 90 °C (194 °F). Roast covered for 7 hours. Do not open, do not baste, do not peek. At the 7-hour mark the skin will appear yellow and somewhat unappetising; the meat will not yet be fully tender. This is expected.
7. **Phase 2**: Without basting or uncovering more than necessary, raise oven to 120 °C (248 °F). Continue roasting covered for another 6 hours. The fat continues to slowly render; the meat becomes fall-off-the-bone tender.
8. **Final 1 hour**: Remove cover. Brush the skin all over with honey. Leave uncovered for the final hour. The skin will turn deep golden and become crispy.
9. Optional: in the final 15 minutes, raise oven to 180 °C / 200 °C fan (356 °F) or switch to grill/broil for extra skin crispiness. Watch carefully to avoid burning the honey glaze.
10. Check internal temperature in thigh with a probe, away from bone: should read 165 °F (74 °C) or above.
11. Rest 15–20 minutes loosely tented before carving.
12. Traditional Czech serving: with bread dumplings (knedlíky) and braised red cabbage.

### 2. Classic High-Heat Whole Roast Duck (standard Western method)
**Inspired by**: Standard culinary technique for whole roast duck; general knowledge synthesis from professional recipe corpus.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 2–2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15–20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Initial high-heat blast: 425 °F (218 °C) for 15–20 min
- Reduced main roast: 350 °F (177 °C) for 1.5–2 hr
- Glaze application: final 30 min of roasting
- Internal target in thigh: 165 °F (74 °C)
- Total cook time: 2–2.5 hours for a 4–5 lb duck

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole duck, 4–6 lb (1.8–2.7 kg), giblets removed
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 orange, halved (or quartered, for cavity)
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 small onion, halved
- **Glaze**: 3 tbsp honey, 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp orange juice, 1 tsp five-spice powder

**Method**
1. Remove duck from refrigerator 1 hour before roasting. Preheat oven to 425 °F (218 °C).
2. Pat duck completely dry inside and out. Score breast skin in cross-hatch without cutting meat. Use a skewer or fork to prick the skin all over the thighs and legs — this helps fat render and drain.
3. Season cavity generously with salt and pepper. Fill with orange, thyme, garlic, onion.
4. Season skin all over with salt and pepper, paying extra attention to the legs and thighs.
5. Truss the duck if desired (trussing promotes even cooking). Place breast-side up on a V-rack in a roasting tin.
6. Roast at 425 °F for 15 minutes to begin skin crisping and fat rendering.
7. Reduce oven to 350 °F (177 °C). Continue roasting for a further 1.5–2 hours, draining accumulated fat from the roasting tin every 30–45 minutes (use a baster or carefully tilt the tin). This fat is excellent for cooking — save it.
8. Prepare glaze: mix honey, soy sauce, orange juice, five-spice. Set aside.
9. At the 1.5-hour mark, brush glaze all over skin. Continue roasting, applying glaze 2–3 more times over the next 30–40 minutes.
10. Check doneness: probe thigh at thickest point, away from bone. Target: 165 °F (74 °C). If breast reads higher (175+ °F), that is acceptable for a whole roast duck.
11. If skin needs more crispness, raise to 425 °F for a final 10 minutes.
12. Rest 15–20 minutes on a carving board before serving.

### 3. Crispy Roast Duck (inspired by BBC Good Food)
**Inspired by**: Ruth Watson, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/crispy-roast-duck (fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 60 min · **Cook**: 90 min · **Rest**: 10–15 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven: 230 °C / Gas 8 / fan oven 210 °C (high heat throughout — contrast with the low-and-slow method in source 1)
- Total cook time: 1¼–1½ hours (75–90 min) for butterflied duck halves on a rack
- Doneness: skin crisp and dark golden; juices run clear; drain accumulated fat once or twice
- Internal temperature: not stated explicitly — traditional visual check; USDA minimum still applies (165 °F / 74 °C in thigh)

**Ingredients**
- 3 medium-sized ducks (each approx. 2.25 kg / 5 lb), with giblets
- Fine sea salt

**Method**
1. Remove the giblets and wash. Cut off any greenish liver parts and discard, then roughly chop the rest. Quarter the crops (gullet), slicing red flesh from white sinew; discard the sinew. Put all giblet pieces into a large roasting tin to be used for gravy or stock.
2. Cut off the parson's nose from each duck and add to the tin. Remove the wings: pull back the skin to expose the shoulder joint, cut between the bones to release each wing, then cut around the skin to free it. Add wings to the tin.
3. Turn each duck breast-side down. Cut down each side of the backbone with a sharp knife or game scissors and remove the backbone. Flip and cut down each side of the breastbone to produce two duck halves. Add all bones to the tin. Repeat with the remaining ducks.
4. Remove large lumps of fat from inside each half and set aside. Trim off any loose, flappy bits of skin. Wash the duck halves and pat very dry. Prick the skin all over — especially the thick, fatty areas. Sprinkle generously with fine sea salt.
5. Preheat the oven to 230 °C / Gas 8 / fan 210 °C. Arrange the duck halves skin-side up on a wire rack set inside a large roasting tin. Roast for 1¼–1½ hours until the skin is crisp and dark golden. Drain accumulated fat from the tin once or twice — pour into a heatproof bowl or directly onto roast potatoes if making them simultaneously.
6. Rest the duck halves for 10–15 min before serving. Serve with gravy made from the tin drippings and vegetables.

**Recommended MEATER settings**:
- Insert probe into thigh, away from bone (not the breast)
- Set alert at **160 °F (71 °C)** → pull from oven, rest 15–20 min → carryover brings thigh to 165 °F (74 °C)
- Rest 15–20 minutes under loose foil tent
- For the BBC low-and-slow method: final temperature after 13 hours should already exceed 165 °F — use probe as confirmation, not as primary doneness indicator

## Notes

- A whole duck produces a remarkable amount of rendered fat (up to 1 cup or more); collect and save it.
- The BBC low-and-slow method produces dramatically more tender, falling-off-the-bone meat than the quick high-heat method, at the cost of 13 hours of cooking time.
- Score the skin and prick the thighs before roasting — unscored duck skin stays fatty and soft.
- The breast on a whole roast duck will inevitably reach 165 °F+ if the thigh is also at 165 °F. This is expected and acceptable; the fattiness of duck breast keeps it from drying out as quickly as chicken breast would.
- Duck fat drained during roasting is one of the most prized cooking fats — refrigerate up to 3 months in a sealed jar.
