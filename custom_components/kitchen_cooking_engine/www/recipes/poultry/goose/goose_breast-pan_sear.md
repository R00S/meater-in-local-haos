## Cut profile

Goose breast shares the same cold-pan fat-rendering technique as duck breast. The skin-side goes into a cold dry skillet and the thick fat cap is rendered slowly over medium-low heat before the breast is flipped and finished. Goose breasts are larger than duck (often 10–14 oz each), so rendering time is slightly longer. The result — thin, crackling skin over juicy, pink meat — is the hallmark of properly executed goose breast pan sear. Medium to medium-rare (145–155 °F) is the standard in European cuisine; USDA requires 165 °F.

Target internal temperature: **145–155 °F (63–68 °C)** — medium; pull at 138–140 °F and rest. USDA well-done: 165 °F (74 °C).

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Slow-Roast Duck (fat-rendering technique adapted for goose breast pan sear)
**Inspired by**: BBC Good Food editorial team, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-roast-duck (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 18–25 min · **Rest**: 6–8 min

> **Note**: BBC source is whole slow-roast duck. The cold-pan, low-and-slow fat rendering principle
> — central to the BBC recipe — applies directly and is the key technique for goose breast pan sear.
> Goose has even more fat than duck; patience during rendering is even more critical.

**Temperature guidance**:
- Pan heat: medium-low for rendering (14–18 min skin-side)
- Flesh side: medium heat, 3–5 min
- Internal target medium: 138–140 °F pull → rest to 145–150 °F
- Internal target well-done: 160 °F pull → rest to 165 °F

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on, boneless goose breasts (10–14 oz / 280–400 g each)
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- ½ tsp ground cumin or four-spice
- 1 tsp honey (final glaze, optional)
- 1 tbsp cold butter (for basting)
- 2 sprigs thyme

**Method**
1. Remove goose breasts from refrigerator 30 minutes ahead. Pat completely dry.
2. Score skin in tight diagonal cross-hatch ¼ inch deep; do not cut meat.
3. Season skin and flesh generously with salt, pepper, and cumin.
4. Place skin-side down in cold, heavy-bottomed pan (cast iron preferred). Do not preheat.
5. Set to medium-low. Allow fat to begin rendering over first 3–4 minutes without moving.
6. Render 14–18 minutes, draining excess fat every 5 minutes into a heatproof container. Skin must be uniformly golden and thin.
7. Flip to flesh side; add butter and thyme; baste 3–4 minutes.
8. Optional glaze: brush honey on skin side in last minute of cooking.
9. Pull at internal temperature of 138–140 °F (medium). Rest 6–8 minutes. Final temp: ~148 °F.
10. Slice across the grain and serve immediately, skin-side up.

### 2. Classic Culinary Technique — Professional Pan-Seared Goose Breast
**Inspired by**: Standard culinary technique for goose breast, documented in European professional kitchens; general knowledge synthesis.
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ optional 2–24 hr dry brine) · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 6–8 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Stovetop only; optional brief oven finish 400 °F (204 °C) 3–5 min
- Internal target: 138–140 °F pull → 145–150 °F rested (medium)
- Well-done: 160 °F pull → 165 °F rested

**Ingredients**
- 2 goose breasts, skin-on, boneless
- Kosher salt, black pepper
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme, 1 sprig rosemary
- 1 garlic clove, smashed
- 80 ml (⅓ cup) red wine or cherry juice (pan sauce)
- 120 ml (½ cup) game or chicken stock (pan sauce)
- 1 tsp cold butter (to finish sauce)

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 2–24 hours: salt lightly, place uncovered on rack in refrigerator. Produces drier skin, deeper seasoning.
2. Score skin in 1-inch cross-hatch ¼ inch deep. Season with pepper.
3. Cold pan, skin-side down, medium-low. Do not add fat.
4. Render 14–18 minutes, pouring off fat every 5 minutes. Press gently; skin should feel dry, not pliable, when done.
5. Flip; add butter, thyme, rosemary, garlic. Baste flesh side 3–4 minutes.
6. For even doneness on thick breasts: optional transfer to 400 °F oven 3–5 min.
7. Pull at 138–140 °F (medium). Rest 6–8 minutes on wire rack.
8. Pan sauce: drain all but 1 tbsp fat; sauté shallot 1 min; deglaze with wine; add stock; reduce by half; mount with cold butter.
9. Slice ¼-inch thick against grain; serve skin-side up.

### 3. Vasilevg — Грудка гуся на гриле (Russian Pan-Grilled Goose Breast)
**Inspired by**: Home cook "vasilevg", Povarenok.ru (Russia's largest recipe-sharing community); https://www.povarenok.ru/recipes/show/168647/ (fetched 2026-04-24, language: Russian — ingredients and method translated below).
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 35 min (incl. marinating) · **Cook**: 6 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Pan heat: grill pan (ribbed cast-iron) preheated well over medium-high heat
- Internal target: fully cooked through — no pink at centre; **80 °C (176 °F)** for safety with goose
- Time: 3 minutes per side for 5 mm slices; adjust for thicker cuts

**Ingredients** *(translated from Russian)*
- 1 goose breast (skin on or removed according to preference), ~300–400 g
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

**Method** *(translated from Russian)*
1. Slice goose breast crosswise (against the grain) into slices approximately 5 mm thick.
2. Season slices with salt and freshly ground black pepper; drizzle with olive oil and turn to coat.
3. Leave to marinate at room temperature for 30 minutes.
4. Heat a ribbed grill pan (or heavy cast-iron skillet) over medium-high heat until very hot.
5. Lay slices flat on the grill pan without overlapping; cook 3 minutes on the first side without moving (for grill marks and a crust).
6. Flip; cook a further 3 minutes until cooked through and juices run clear.
7. Internal temperature should reach **80 °C (176 °F)** in the centre of each slice.
8. Serve immediately with fresh herbs or a simple salad.

*Note*: Goose breast is denser and leaner than duck breast and benefits from thin-slicing for quick pan-searing. Thicker portions (bone-in) require longer oven finishing — see oven-roast file.

## Notes

- Goose breasts are larger and thicker than duck — allow longer rendering time (14–18 min vs 12–15 min for duck).
- Cold-pan start is mandatory: same reasoning as duck breast — prevents burnt skin over under-rendered fat.
- Goose fat rendered off is exceptional for cooking and can be stored refrigerated up to 3 months.
- Pairs well with cherry, lingonberry, or red currant sauces; also classic with braised red cabbage.
