## Cut profile

Ground turkey (hindi kıyması / Putenhackfleisch / tacchino macinato) is a lean alternative to beef in grilled patties and köfte. Turkey mince dries out more easily than beef or even chicken, so moisture-binding additions — grated onion, soaked bread, applesauce, or egg — are essential. Turkey's mild, slightly sweet flavour takes well to bold spice blends: Mediterranean (paprika, cumin, garlic), Turkish (thyme, cumin, kırmızı biber), and German (salt, pepper, Röstzwiebeln). Ground turkey must reach a safe internal temperature throughout without becoming dry. The MEATER probe is inserted horizontally into the thickest patty or köfte from the side, positioned centrally.

Target internal temperature: **74 °C (165 °F)** — the USDA minimum for all ground poultry; explicitly confirmed by multiple sources.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Hindi Köftesi (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/hindi-koftesi-tarifi/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Hindi Köftesi — Turkish grilled turkey meatballs (köfte)."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min on grill

**Ingredients**
- 500 g turkey thigh mince (hindi but kıyması)
- 100 g chicken breast mince (tavuk göğüs kıyması)
- 2 slices stale white bread (crusts removed)
- 1 medium onion, finely grated
- 1 tsp black pepper (karabiber)
- ½ tsp ground cumin (kimyon)
- 1 tsp dried thyme (kekik)
- 2 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 2 tsp salt

**Method**
1. Soak bread slices in cold water for 2–3 minutes. Squeeze out as much water as possible; tear into small pieces.
2. In a large bowl, combine turkey mince, chicken mince, soaked bread, and grated onion.
3. Add black pepper, cumin, thyme, parsley, and salt. Knead firmly for 3–4 minutes until the mixture is smooth, cohesive, and slightly sticky.
4. Cover and refrigerate 30 minutes to firm up.
5. Divide into 12 equal portions. With lightly oiled or dampened hands, shape into elongated oval köfte about 2 cm thick.
6. Preheat grill to medium-high heat and oil the grate.
7. Grill for 5–6 minutes per side until cooked through and showing grill marks.
8. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest köfte from one end. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
9. **Rest 3–5 min** before serving with flatbread, sliced raw onion, tomato, and yoghurt.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 2. Tori Avey — Mediterranean Grilled Turkey Burgers (Israeli / Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Tori Avey; https://toriavey.com/mediterranean-turkey-burgers/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Mediterranean Turkey Burgers — Levantine-spiced grilled turkey patties with basil and smoked paprika."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–15 min on grill

**Ingredients**
- 450 g (1 lb) ground turkey
- ¼ cup fresh basil leaves, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp yellow onion, minced
- 1 egg
- ¾ tsp smoked paprika
- ¼ tsp ground cumin
- ¼ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp kosher salt
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- 1½ tbsp unsweetened applesauce (for moisture)
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

**Method**
1. Combine all ingredients in a large bowl. Mix gently with your hands until just combined — do not overmix.
2. Divide into 4 equal portions and shape into patties approximately 2 cm (¾ in) thick.
3. Preheat grill to medium heat. Oil the grate thoroughly.
4. Place patties on grill. Cook for 10–15 minutes total, turning every 3–4 minutes.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest patty from the side before placing on grill. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**. *(Tori Avey explicitly states: "cook to an internal temperature of 165 °F / 74 °C".)*
6. **Rest 3–5 min**. Serve in pita or a bun with tzatziki, tomato, cucumber, and fresh herbs.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Puten-Hamburger mit Röstzwiebeln (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3064261458952311/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Puten-Hamburger mit Röstzwiebeln — German turkey burger with crispy onions."*
**Serves**: 2 (4 patties) · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 7 min

**Ingredients**
- 400 g ground turkey (Putenhackfleisch)
- 1 small onion, finely diced (optional, to knead into mince)
- 1 pinch salt
- 1 pinch black pepper
- A little olive oil
- 4 hamburger buns
- 4 slices cheese
- Crispy fried onions (Röstzwiebeln), ketchup, remoulade, salad, tomato, gherkin

**Method**
1. Toast split hamburger buns in the oven at 180 °C until lightly golden and crisp on the cut surface.
2. Knead diced onion into the turkey mince if desired. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Form the mince into a thick roll; divide into 4 equal portions; press each into a round patty.
4. Heat a little olive oil in a grill pan or preheat the outdoor grill. Season patties lightly again with salt and pepper.
5. Cook first side for **3 minutes** over medium heat; flip and cook the second side for **4 minutes**.
6. In the last minute, place a slice of cheese on each patty and allow to melt.
7. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest patty from the side. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)** — the minimum safe temperature for ground poultry.
8. **Rest 3 min**. Assemble burger with remoulade, ketchup, salad, tomato, gherkin, melted cheese, and crispy onions. Secure with a wooden skewer.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

### 4. TASTE — Chicken Patties adapted for Turkey (South African tradition)
**Source**: TASTE magazine South Africa; https://taste.co.za/recipes/chicken-patties/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Chicken Patties with Preserved Lemon — TASTE recipe, adapted here using turkey mince in place of chicken."*
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min on grill

*Turkey adaptation: ground turkey can be substituted 1:1 for chicken mince. Turkey benefits from the preserved lemon and feta additions which add moisture and flavour to the leaner meat.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg ground turkey mince
- 3 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
- 3 tbsp fresh coriander (cilantro), chopped
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp chicken seasoning (or mixed spice)
- ½ tsp fennel seeds, lightly crushed
- ½ tsp cumin seeds, lightly crushed
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 100 g feta, crumbled
- 2 tbsp preserved lemon rind, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine turkey mince with all herbs, spices, and seasonings in a large bowl.
2. Gently fold in crumbled feta and preserved lemon. Add olive oil and brown sugar; mix until combined.
3. Shape into 8–10 flat patties, about 1.5 cm thick. Refrigerate 15 minutes to firm up.
4. Preheat grill to medium-high; oil grate well.
5. Grill patties 5–6 minutes per side until cooked through and nicely marked.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest patty from the side. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
7. **Rest 3–5 min**. Serve with yoghurt dip and flatbread.
*Final temperature: 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA minimum — ground poultry | 74 °C (165 °F) | Confirmed by Tori Avey and USDA; no exceptions |
| Safe and moist | 74–76 °C (165–169 °F) | Optimal for grilled turkey patties/köfte |
| Overcooked / dry | > 79 °C (175 °F) | Very lean turkey dries out rapidly above this |
