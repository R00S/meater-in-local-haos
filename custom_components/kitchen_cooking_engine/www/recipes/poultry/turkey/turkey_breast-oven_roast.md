## Cut profile

Turkey breast can be purchased bone-in (half breast on the rib rack, 1.5–2.5 kg / 3–5.5 lb) or boneless (rolled and tied, 1–2 kg / 2–4.5 lb). Both have dramatically less fat than dark turkey meat and will dry out rapidly if taken past 165 °F (74 °C); the sweet spot for juicy, food-safe turkey breast is **160–165 °F (71–74 °C)** at the thickest point (the USDA minimum is 165 °F; pulling at 160 °F and resting 10–15 minutes under foil achieves safe temperatures via carryover). Bone-in breasts conduct heat more slowly near the bone and need accurate probe placement to the geometric centre of the thickest part. Boneless rolled breasts cook more evenly. At 190–200 °C oven temperature, a 1.5 kg bone-in breast takes approximately 60–75 minutes.

## Styckesprofil

Kalkonbröst kan köpas med ben (halvbröst på revbensstål, 1,5–2,5 kg) eller benfritt (rullat och bundet, 1–2 kg). Båda har dramatiskt lägre fett än mörkt kalkonkött och torkar ut lätt. Ugnsstekning: 160–180 °C i 1,5–2,5 timmar beroende på storlek. Innertemperatur: 74 °C. Eftertillagning: 4–6 °C. Basteras regelbundet.

## Source recipes

### 1. Spatchcocked Turkey Breast (inspired by Kenji López-Alt)
**Inspired by**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab* (W. W. Norton, 2015); seriouseats.com/the-best-roast-turkey-breast-recipe  
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min + optional overnight brine · **Cook**: 60–80 min · **Rest**: 10–15 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp Phase 1 (blast): 230 °C (450 °F) × 15 min for skin crisping
- Oven temp Phase 2: 165 °C (325 °F) × 45–60 min to target internal
- Internal target: 160 °F (71 °C) at centre; carryover brings to 165 °F (74 °C) during rest

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in half turkey breast, 1.5–2 kg (3–4.5 lb), skin-on
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil or melted butter
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt
- 3 g (¾ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) garlic powder
- 3 g (¾ tsp) sweet paprika
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves finely chopped
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves stripped

**Method**
1. If brining: soak breast in 1 litre of 5 % brine (50 g salt per litre of water) for 4–8 hours, refrigerated. Pat completely dry before roasting.
2. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F). Place the breast skin-side-up on a roasting rack set in a rimmed baking sheet.
3. Mix olive oil with salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, rosemary and thyme. Lift the skin and rub half the mixture directly on the flesh; rub the remainder over the skin.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the breast from the side, parallel to the rib bones, tip at the geometric centre, not touching bone.
5. **Phase 1**: Roast at 230 °C for 15 minutes to brown the skin.
6. **Phase 2**: Reduce oven to 165 °C (325 °F). Roast until probe reads 160 °F (71 °C) — approximately 45–60 minutes for 1.5 kg.
7. Remove from oven, tent loosely with foil, rest 10–15 minutes. Internal temperature will reach 165 °F (74 °C) during rest.
8. Carve across the grain and serve.

### 2. Herb-Roasted Turkey Breast (inspired by Ina Garten)
**Inspired by**: Ina Garten, *Barefoot Contessa Foolproof* (Clarkson Potter, 2012); food52.com  
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1.5–2 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 190 °C (375 °F) throughout
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) — Garten uses the full USDA minimum without a lower pull-and-rest strategy

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in turkey breast, 2.5–3 kg (5.5–6.5 lb), skin-on
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter, softened
- 2 large garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp fresh sage, minced (or 1 tsp dried)
- 2 tsp fresh rosemary, minced (or 1 tsp dried)
- 1 tsp fresh thyme, minced (or ½ tsp dried)
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt
- 3 g (¾ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 1 lemon, halved
- 1 medium onion, thickly sliced (for the base)
- 500 ml chicken stock (for the roasting pan)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Place the onion slices in a roasting pan; pour stock around them.
2. Mix butter with garlic, sage, rosemary, thyme, salt and pepper to a smooth herb butter.
3. Pat the turkey breast completely dry. Lift skin and spread half the butter directly on the breast meat. Rub the remaining butter over the skin. Squeeze lemon juice over the top; tuck the spent lemon halves under the breast.
4. Set the breast skin-side-up in the pan. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the breast from the side, tip at centre, not touching bone.
5. Roast at 190 °C, basting every 30 minutes with pan juices.
6. **Pull when probe reads 165 °F (74 °C)**. For a 2.5 kg breast, this takes approximately 1.5–2 hours.
7. Rest 15 minutes before carving. Use the pan drippings to make a simple pan gravy.

### 3. Slow-Roasted Turkey Breast with Preserved Lemon and Olives (British/Moroccan fusion) (inspired by Diana Henry)
**Inspired by**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015); telegraph.co.uk  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min + overnight marinating · **Cook**: 1.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 180 °C / 160 °C fan (356 °F / 320 °F fan)
- Internal target: 160–165 °F (71–74 °C) — Henry pulls at 160 °F and rests 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 boneless turkey breast, 1.5 kg (3.3 lb), rolled and tied, skin-on or skinless
- 1 preserved lemon, skin only, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground cumin
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground coriander
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground cinnamon
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 60 g green or Castelvetrano olives, pitted, roughly chopped
- 30 ml dry white wine
- Small handful fresh coriander (cilantro), to garnish

**Method**
1. **Night before**: mix preserved lemon, garlic, olive oil, cumin, coriander, cinnamon and salt into a paste. Rub all over the turkey breast, under the skin if present. Refrigerate overnight in a resealable bag.
2. Remove from refrigerator 45 minutes before roasting. Preheat oven to 180 °C (356 °F).
3. Place turkey breast in a snug roasting dish. Scatter the olives around the meat. Pour in the white wine.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the roll from the end, with the tip at the geometric centre.
5. Roast at 180 °C for 1.5 hours, basting once or twice with pan juices.
6. **Pull at 160 °F (71 °C)**. Remove from oven and rest 15 minutes loosely tented with foil. Carryover brings the centre to 165 °F (74 °C).
7. Slice thickly and serve with the pan juices poured over. Scatter fresh coriander on top.

## Temperature summary

| Source | Oven temp | Weight | Total time | Target internal |
|--------|-----------|--------|------------|-----------------|
| Kenji López-Alt | 230 °C → 165 °C (450 °F → 325 °F) | 1.5–2 kg bone-in | ~60–80 min | 160 °F pull → 165 °F rested |
| Ina Garten | 190 °C (375 °F) | 2.5–3 kg bone-in | ~1.5–2 hr | 165 °F (74 °C) |
| Diana Henry | 180 °C (356 °F) | 1.5 kg boneless | ~1.5 hr | 160 °F pull → 165 °F rested |
| USDA minimum | Any | Any | Any | 165 °F (74 °C) |

**Recommended MEATER settings**:
- Insert probe into the thickest part of the breast from the side, parallel to the bone (bone-in) or from the end (boneless roll), tip at geometric centre
- Set alert at **160 °F (71 °C)** → remove, tent with foil, rest 10–15 min → centre reaches 165 °F (74 °C) via carryover
- Avoid touching the bone with the probe tip — bone heats faster than meat and gives falsely high readings

## Notes

- Turkey breast is significantly leaner than chicken breast (especially boneless, skinless): it dries out more quickly at higher temperatures. Never roast above 200 °C for extended periods.
- For a boneless rolled breast, check that the string/twine is intact before roasting — loose rolls cook unevenly.
- If the skin is browning too fast, tent loosely with foil (shiny side up) during the last 30 minutes.
