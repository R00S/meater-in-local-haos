## Source recipes

### 1. Smoked Turkey Legs (inspired by Immaculate Bites)
**Inspired by**: Imma (Immaculate Bites); africanbites.com/smoked-turkey-legs/ — original 2021, updated 2025  
**Serves**: varies · **Cut used**: Turkey legs (drumsticks), size varies

**Ingredients**
- Turkey legs
- Salt and pepper
- Choice of seasoning rub (not specified — user's preference)

**Method**
1. Season legs with salt and pepper; rub with choice of seasoning.
2. Preheat smoker to 225–275 °F (110–135 °C).
3. **Smoke the turkey legs, basting approximately every 1 hour, until internal temperature of the thickest part reaches at least 165 °F (75 °C).**
4. Remove from smoker; rest covered with foil for 10 min.

**Time table by smoker temperature**:

| Smoker temp | Estimated time |
|-------------|----------------|
| 225 °F / 110 °C | 3½–4 hours |
| 250 °F / 120 °C | 3–4 hours |
| 275 °F / 135 °C | 2½–3 hours |
| 300 °F / 150 °C | 2–3 hours |
| 325 °F / 165 °C | 1½–2 hours |

**Probe temperature**: **165 °F (75 °C)** minimum; "many prefer 175 °F (80 °C)."

**Notes from author**: "Turkey comes out a whole lot juicier when slow-cooked in a smoker. The legs and thighs are more forgiving because they're higher fat, so they stay juicy. They also take longer to cook, so the smoky flavour really has time to penetrate."
