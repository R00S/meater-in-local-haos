## Cut profile

Oven-roasting is the standard North American method for turkey thighs cooked as a standalone cut. The thigh's high fat content (≈ 5–8 %) bastes the meat naturally in a dry-heat oven, making it more forgiving than breast meat. At typical roasting temperatures (175–200 °C), the surface renders crisp while the interior climbs toward 77–82 °C over 45–60 minutes. The dense collagen converts to gelatin above 70 °C, improving mouthfeel — this is why most recipes pull at 77–82 °C rather than the USDA minimum of 74 °C. Bone-in thighs retain more moisture and flavour; boneless thighs cook faster and more evenly. Carryover during rest is approximately 3–4 °C for this size cut. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the thigh, parallel to and away from the bone, from the side rather than the top.

## Source recipes

### 1. Taste of Home Editorial Team — Braised Turkey Thighs
**Inspired by**: Taste of Home Editorial Team, Taste of Home; https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/braised-turkey-thighs/ (fetched 2026-04-27). Recipe credited to Loretta Paulus, Venice, Florida.
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min · **Rest**: 5 min
**Culinary tradition**: North American (Group B)
**Technique note**: Moist-heat oven method — turkey thighs are cooked in a sealed oven roasting bag with chicken broth and white wine at 350 °F. This is effectively oven braising, not dry-heat roasting. Included here as the only accessible dedicated turkey thigh recipe found.

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 350 °F (175 °C)
- Internal target: 170–175 °F (77–79 °C) at thickest part of thigh
- Time: 1½ hours in oven bag

**Ingredients**
- 1 cup each finely chopped onion, celery, and carrots
- ⅓ cup ketchup
- 1¼ teaspoons salt
- ½ teaspoon paprika
- ⅛ teaspoon pepper
- 4 pounds (≈ 1.8 kg) turkey thighs
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour, divided
- 1 large oven roasting bag
- ½ cup reduced-sodium chicken broth
- ⅓ cup dry white wine (or additional broth)
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 2 bay leaves
- ¼ cup cold water

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 350 °F (175 °C).
2. In a large bowl, combine onion, celery, carrots, ketchup, salt, paprika, and pepper. Pat mixture onto turkey thighs.
3. Place 1 tablespoon flour in oven bag; shake to coat. Transfer seasoned turkey thighs to oven bag.
4. Place bag in a 13 × 9-inch baking dish.
5. Combine broth, wine, oregano, and bay leaves; pour into bag. Seal bag. Cut 2 slits in top.
6. Bake 1½ hours, or until a thermometer inserted in the thickest part reads 170–175 °F (77–79 °C).
7. **Pull at 170–175 °F (77–79 °C).** Remove turkey to serving platter; keep warm.
8. Pour pan juices into saucepan; skim fat; discard bay leaves. Simmer 5 min until reduced to 1¾ cups.
9. Combine remaining flour and cold water; stir into saucepan. Boil 2 minutes until thickened.
10. Rest 5 min. Final serving temperature: **170–175 °F / 77–79 °C** (well done / dark meat optimal).
11. Remove skin and bones; slice turkey. Serve with gravy.
