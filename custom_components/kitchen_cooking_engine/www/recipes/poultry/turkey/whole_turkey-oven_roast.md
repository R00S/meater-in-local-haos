## Cut profile

A whole turkey presents the same fundamental challenge as a whole chicken but at larger scale and with more extreme differences between breast and leg/thigh: the breast meat is lean and prone to drying above 160–165 °F (71–74 °C), while the legs and thighs are dark, collagen-rich and benefit from 165–175 °F (74–79 °C) to become properly tender. A 4.5 kg / 10 lb turkey feeds 6–8 people; a 7 kg / 15 lb turkey feeds 10–12. The USDA minimum for whole turkey is **165 °F (74 °C)** measured in the thigh at its thickest point, well away from bone. Probe placement in the thigh (not the breast) is the authoritative food-safety measurement. Modern approaches — brining, spatchcocking, basting — all aim to let the thigh reach 165 °F without pushing the breast past 165 °F.

Typical roasting time at 190 °C (375 °F): ~13–15 minutes per 450 g (1 lb) unstuffed. A 4.5 kg (10 lb) bird takes approximately 2.5–3 hours; a 7 kg (15 lb) bird takes approximately 3.5–4 hours.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — The Best Roast Turkey (American tradition, dry-brined and spatchcocked)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab* (W. W. Norton, 2015); seriouseats.com/the-best-roast-turkey  
**Serves**: 8–10 · **Prep**: 30 min + 1–3 days dry-brine · **Cook**: 80–90 min (spatchcocked) · **Rest**: 30 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 230 °C / 210 °C fan (450 °F / 410 °F fan) throughout (high-heat works for spatchcocked because the bird is thin and flat)
- Thigh target: 165 °F (74 °C); breast should not exceed 160–165 °F
- Carryover after rest: 3–5 °C in thigh

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole turkey, 5.5–6.5 kg (12–14 lb), neck and giblets removed
- 20 g (4 tsp) kosher salt for dry-brine (1 tsp per 2.5 kg / 5 lb)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil or melted butter
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) garlic powder
- 3 g (½ tsp) sweet paprika
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves stripped
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves finely chopped

**Method**
1. **1–3 days before**: Spatchcock the turkey (remove backbone with poultry shears; press flat so the keel bone cracks). Rub salt all over skin and meat surfaces. Place uncovered on a wire rack set in a rimmed baking sheet; refrigerate 1–3 days.
2. Remove from refrigerator 1 hour before roasting. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F).
3. Mix olive oil with black pepper, garlic powder, paprika, thyme and rosemary. Lift the skin and rub half directly on the breast and thigh meat; spread the rest over the skin.
4. Place the turkey skin-side-up on a roasting rack set in a large rimmed roasting pan. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of one thigh, well away from bone.
5. Roast at 230 °C for 80–90 minutes. The high heat and flat shape ensure relatively even cooking.
6. **Pull when thigh probe reads 165 °F (74 °C) and breast reads 155–160 °F (68–71 °C).**
7. Rest at least 30 minutes before carving. Breast will continue to 160–165 °F during rest; thigh will rise to ~170 °F — well within the safe and tender zone for dark meat.
8. Total cook time for a spatchcocked 5.5–6.5 kg turkey: 80–90 minutes at 230 °C.

### 2. Ina Garten — Herb-Roasted Traditional Turkey (American home tradition)
**Source**: Ina Garten, *Barefoot Contessa at Home* (Clarkson Potter, 2006); food52.com/recipes/ina-garten-turkey  
**Serves**: 10–12 · **Prep**: 30 min + overnight brine · **Cook**: 3–3.5 hr · **Rest**: 30 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp Phase 1: 200 °C (400 °F) × 30 min
- Oven temp Phase 2: 180 °C (350 °F) × remainder
- Internal target (thigh): 165 °F (74 °C) — Garten uses the full USDA target

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole turkey, 7–8 kg (15–17 lb), neck and giblets removed
- **Brine**: 1 litre warm water, 100 g salt, 50 g sugar, 2 bay leaves, 1 tsp black peppercorns, zest of 1 orange; plus 3 litres cold water to fill
- 120 g (½ cup) unsalted butter, softened
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, minced; 1 tbsp fresh thyme, minced; 1 tbsp fresh sage, minced
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 medium onions, quartered (cavity stuffing)
- 1 orange, quartered (cavity stuffing)
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme, rosemary and sage (cavity stuffing)
- 2 cups chicken stock (for the roasting pan)

**Method**
1. **Night before**: Combine brine ingredients; cool to room temperature. Submerge turkey in brine for 12–16 hours, refrigerated. (Or dry-brine: rub 25 g salt all over and refrigerate uncovered 12–24 hr.)
2. Remove turkey from brine; rinse and pat completely dry. Bring to room temperature 1–1.5 hours before roasting.
3. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F).
4. Mix butter with garlic, rosemary, thyme, sage, salt and pepper. Lift skin over breast and thighs; rub half the butter directly on the flesh. Rub the rest over the skin.
5. Fill cavity loosely with onions, orange and fresh herbs. Truss the legs. Place breast-side-up on a rack in a large roasting pan. Pour stock into the pan.
6. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of one thigh, well away from bone.
7. **Phase 1**: Roast at 200 °C for 30 minutes.
8. **Phase 2**: Reduce to 180 °C. Baste every 30–45 minutes with pan juices. Tent with foil if skin is browning too quickly (usually after 1.5 hours).
9. **Pull at 165 °F (74 °C)** in the thigh. Total time for 7–8 kg: approximately 3–3.5 hours.
10. Rest 30 minutes loosely tented before carving. Use the pan drippings to make gravy.

### 3. Diana Henry — Classic Slow-Roast Turkey with Apple and Calvados Butter (British tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015); telegraph.co.uk  
**Serves**: 8–10 · **Prep**: 30 min + overnight dry-brine · **Cook**: 3–4 hr · **Rest**: 30–40 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 170 °C / 150 °C fan (338 °F / 302 °F fan) × 3–3.5 hr, then blast 220 °C / 200 °C fan (428 °F / 392 °F fan) × 20–25 min for skin
- Internal target (thigh): 165 °F (74 °C); breast not above 165 °F

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole turkey, 5.5–6 kg (12–13 lb)
- 25 g coarse sea salt for dry-brine
- 120 g (½ cup) unsalted butter, softened
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Calvados or apple brandy
- 1 small eating apple, grated
- 10 g (2 tsp) fresh thyme, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) dried sage
- Salt and pepper
- 2 onions, halved (for the pan)
- 500 ml dry cider (for the pan)

**Method**
1. **Night before**: rub turkey all over with sea salt; refrigerate uncovered overnight.
2. Beat butter with Calvados, grated apple, thyme, sage, salt and pepper to a smooth compound.
3. Preheat oven to 170 °C (338 °F). Pat turkey dry; spread butter under and over the skin. Place breast-side-up on a rack in a roasting pan; add onions and cider to the pan.
4. Insert MEATER probe deep into thigh, away from bone.
5. Roast at 170 °C for 3–3.5 hours for a 5.5–6 kg bird, basting every hour.
6. When thigh reaches 155 °F (68 °C), raise oven to 220 °C for the final 20–25 minutes for a golden, crisp skin.
7. **Pull when thigh reads 165 °F (74 °C).** Rest 30–40 minutes under loose foil.

## Temperature summary

| Source | Method | Oven temp | Weight | Total time | Target (thigh) |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|------------|----------------|
| Kenji López-Alt (spatchcocked) | Spatchcocked high-heat | 230 °C (450 °F) | 5.5–6.5 kg | 80–90 min | 165 °F (74 °C) |
| Ina Garten (traditional) | Whole-bird classic | 200 °C → 180 °C | 7–8 kg | 3–3.5 hr | 165 °F (74 °C) |
| Diana Henry (slow-roast) | Low-and-slow + blast | 170 °C → 220 °C | 5.5–6 kg | 3–3.5 hr | 165 °F (74 °C) |
| USDA minimum | Any | Any | Any | Any | 165 °F (74 °C) |

**Recommended MEATER settings**:
- **Always** insert probe into the **thigh** (not the breast) — thigh is the food-safety reference point for whole turkey
- Place probe into thickest part of the thigh, well away from the femur/thigh bone
- Set alert at **160 °F (71 °C)** → rest 30 min → thigh will reach 165+ °F (74 °C) via carryover
- For spatchcocked birds: 160 °F alert still appropriate; rest 20–30 min

## Notes

- For large turkeys (7+ kg) it is common for the probe to show 165 °F in the thigh while the breast joint at the inner wing is still under 160 °F. Check both locations.
- Spatchcocking (removing the backbone; pressing flat) reduces roasting time by 30–40 % and results in more even cooking; it is the most reliable method for a juicy breast + fully cooked legs.
- Stuffing the turkey cavity adds 30–45 minutes to roasting time and requires the cavity stuffing to also reach 165 °F — use a second probe if stuffing the bird.
- Basting doesn't significantly moisten the meat (water doesn't penetrate through the skin) but does help brown the skin and is useful for building flavour in the pan drippings.
