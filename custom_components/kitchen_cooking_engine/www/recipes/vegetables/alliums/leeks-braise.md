# Leeks × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Leeks (*Allium ampeloprasum* var. *porrum*) braised are one of the most refined of all vegetable preparations. The long white shafts soften over 20–40 minutes of gentle simmering in stock, wine, or butter-enriched liquid, their sharp raw flavour mellowing to a delicate sweetness. The technique — covering with a cartouche (buttered paper) or lid and cooking barely at simmer — is classical French *étuvée*, used also in British and Flemish cuisines. Leeks are cut in half lengthwise, or left whole and halved after cooking. The internal temperature of a fully braised leek is 82–88 °C (180–190 °F), where the cell walls have fully softened and the shaft is yielding to a paring knife but not collapsed. A MEATER probe is inserted along the axis of the leek into the thickest part of the white base, as far from the cut ends as possible to get a representative reading.

## Source recipes

### 1. Julia Child — Poireaux Braisés au Beurre (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *The French Chef Cookbook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1968); and the braised-vegetable methodology elaborated in *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–35 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g leeks (about 4–5 large), white and pale-green parts only, trimmed and halved lengthwise; washed thoroughly between layers
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 125 ml (½ cup) dry white wine
- 125 ml (½ cup) light chicken stock or vegetable stock
- 1 bouquet garni (1 bay leaf, 1 thyme sprig, 5 parsley stems, tied)
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to finish
- Optional: 60 g crème fraîche or double cream, to stir in at the end

**Method**
1. Lay the halved leeks cut-side-down in a single layer in a wide, heavy sauté pan or gratin dish. Add butter, salt, wine, stock and bouquet garni.
2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Press a piece of buttered parchment (cartouche) directly onto the leeks, then cover with a lid.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek, through the cut end, with the tip in the geometric centre of the white base.
4. Braise over very low heat for 25–30 minutes, or until a paring knife meets no resistance when inserted into the thickest white stem. Child specifies "completely tender, not just yielding — press the leek: it should give like soft butter."
5. Remove the lid and parchment. If the liquid is abundant, increase heat briefly to reduce it to a syrupy glaze (2–3 minutes). Discard bouquet garni. Stir in crème fraîche if using.
6. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).** Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (silky, butter-glazed, wine-fragrant — serve as a starter or side to roasted chicken or fish).

### 2. Claudia Roden — Braised Leeks with Tomato and Coriander (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g leeks, white and pale-green parts, cut into 4 cm pieces
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 400 g ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped (or 1 × 400 g can)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- ½ tsp ground ginger
- ½ tsp sugar
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- Small bunch fresh coriander, roughly chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, lidded pan over medium heat. Add garlic; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
2. Add the leek pieces; sauté, turning, for 5 minutes until beginning to colour.
3. Add tomatoes, cumin, paprika, ginger, sugar and salt; stir well. Bring to a simmer.
4. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek piece, with the tip in the centre of the white base.
5. Cover and braise over low heat for 20–25 minutes until the leeks are completely tender and the tomato sauce has thickened and coats the leeks.
6. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Roden's North African style aims for leeks that are completely soft, almost collapsing, enrobed in a spiced tomato sauce. Scatter fresh coriander. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (warm-spiced, tomato-rich, herbal).

### 3. Deborah Madison — Braised Leeks with Mustard and Cream (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g leeks (4–5 medium), white and pale green, halved lengthwise and rinsed
- 20 g (1½ tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tsp olive oil
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 175 ml (¾ cup) dry white wine
- 175 ml (¾ cup) vegetable stock
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 60 ml (¼ cup) heavy cream
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- Freshly ground white pepper

**Method**
1. Heat butter and olive oil in a wide, lidded pan over medium heat. Add leeks cut-side-down in a single layer; cook 4–5 minutes until lightly browned on the cut face.
2. Turn leeks cut-side-up. Add wine, stock, thyme and salt. Bring to a simmer.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek, through the root end, with the tip in the centre of the white base.
4. Cover and braise over low heat 18–22 minutes until the leeks are completely tender throughout.
5. Remove the leeks to a warm platter. Increase heat and reduce the braising liquid by half (3–4 min). Whisk in mustard and cream; reduce to a light sauce consistency (2–3 min). Season with salt and white pepper.
6. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).** Madison targets leeks that are "completely tender — slide a small paring knife through without any resistance." Pour the mustard cream sauce over. Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (creamy, mustardy, delicately sweet).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Braised Leeks with Saffron and Preserved Lemon (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g leeks, white and pale-green, cut into 5 cm lengths
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp saffron threads, soaked in 2 tbsp boiling water for 10 min
- 200 ml (¾ cup) dry white wine
- 200 ml (¾ cup) vegetable stock
- 1 preserved lemon, skin only, finely diced (discard flesh)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 30 g dried cranberries or raisins
- 30 g flaked almonds, toasted, to finish
- 15 g fresh dill or flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, lidded pan over medium heat. Add leek pieces, tossing to coat; cook 5 minutes until lightly coloured.
2. Add saffron water, wine, stock, preserved lemon, sugar, salt and cranberries. Bring to a simmer.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek piece, with the tip in the centre of the white base.
4. Cover and braise over low heat 20–25 minutes until the leeks are completely tender and the sauce is aromatic and slightly thickened.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** The leeks should yield easily to a paring knife and have absorbed the saffron-gold colour throughout.
6. Scatter almonds and fresh herbs. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (golden, saffron-fragrant, sweet-sour from preserved lemon).
