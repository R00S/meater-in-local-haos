# Leeks × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Leeks (*Allium ampeloprasum* var. *porrum*) sautéed in a pan deliver the most versatile everyday leek preparation: sliced into rounds or batons, they are cooked in butter or oil over medium heat until their harsh rawness transforms into a sweet, mildly pungent softness. Unlike the long braise, the sauté takes 8–15 minutes and aims for a tender-but-intact result with some residual structure. Internal temperature at the ideal sauté doneness — tender, translucent, slightly caramelised at the edges — is 80–86 °C (176–187 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted horizontally into the thickest round or baton near the centre of the pan.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Negi no Itame (Japanese leek sauté)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g negi (Japanese leek-onions) or large-stem leeks, white and light green parts only, cut into 4 cm diagonal pieces
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (usukuchi-shoyu)
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat sesame oil in a wide, heavy frying pan over medium-high heat until shimmering.
2. Add the negi pieces in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through one of the thicker pieces near the centre, tip at its geometric centre.
3. Sauté undisturbed 3–4 minutes until the cut faces are golden-brown.
4. Flip each piece. Add soy sauce, mirin and sugar; toss to coat. Cook 2–3 more minutes, tossing frequently, until the glaze caramelises and the negi are tender throughout.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Harumi targets negi that are caramelised at the faces but still slightly firm at the core — contrast of textures is the point. Drizzle finishing sesame oil; scatter sesame seeds. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (sweet-savoury, sesame-fragrant, slightly charred).

### 2. Marcella Hazan — Porri Saltati al Burro (Italian sautéed leeks)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g leeks (3–4 medium), white and pale-green, cut into 5 mm rounds
- 40 g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 g Parmigiano-Reggiano, freshly grated, to finish
- Optional: a squeeze of lemon juice

**Method**
1. Wash the sliced leeks thoroughly in a bowl of cold water; lift out without shaking and spread on paper towels to drain.
2. Melt butter with the olive oil in a wide, heavy sauté pan over medium heat. When the butter foam subsides, add the leeks; season with salt and white pepper.
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the thickest section of leek rounds near the centre of the pan.
4. Sauté over medium heat, stirring and tossing every 2 minutes, for 10–12 minutes until the leeks are completely soft, translucent, and lightly caramelised in spots.
5. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Hazan's Italian style — very soft, butter-glazed, slightly sweet — the leeks should "have almost disappeared into a heap of silky, pale-golden softness." Scatter Parmesan; add a drop of lemon juice if desired. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (butter-soft, cheese-flecked, refined).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Sautéed Leeks with Cumin and Mustard Seeds (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g leeks, white and pale-green, cut into 1 cm rounds, washed
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole mustard seeds
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 10–12 fresh curry leaves (or 6–8 dried)
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder (or mild paprika)
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 1 tbsp desiccated coconut, to finish (optional, South Indian touch)
- 15 g fresh coriander, chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add mustard seeds; when they pop (10–15 s), add cumin seeds and curry leaves; sizzle 5 seconds.
2. Add leek rounds, turmeric and chilli powder; toss to coat with the spiced oil.
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest leek round near the centre of the pan.
4. Sauté over medium heat, stirring every 2 minutes, for 8–10 minutes until the leeks are tender, slightly caramelised, and fragrant with the spice tempering.
5. Add salt; toss. Scatter coconut if using; garnish with fresh coriander.
6. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Jaffrey's style is fully cooked but still with some texture — not mushy. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (warm-spiced, fragrant, south-meets-north Indian).

### 4. Julia Child — Poireaux à la Crème (French leeks with cream)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g leeks, white and pale-green, sliced into 5 mm rounds
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry white wine or dry vermouth
- 125 ml (½ cup) double cream or crème fraîche
- Freshly ground white pepper
- 1 tsp fresh lemon juice
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Sauté the leek rounds in butter over medium heat for 8–10 minutes until completely soft and beginning to colour lightly.
2. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest leek mass near the centre.
3. Add wine; increase heat and cook 2 minutes until wine nearly evaporates.
4. Add cream; simmer 3–4 minutes until reduced to a sauce that coats the leeks.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Child calls this the classic *blanquette*-of-leeks style — completely tender leeks in a light cream sauce. Season with salt, white pepper and lemon juice; scatter parsley. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (creamy, elegant, French).
