# Roasted Onion × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole or halved onions (*Allium cepa*) oven-roasted concentrate the natural sugars, soften the fibrous layers, and caramelise the cut surfaces without the live-flame char of the grill. At 190–220 °C, the outer skin acts as insulation: the interior reaches 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) by the time the skin is papery-dry and the interior is fully collapsed and jam-like. Halved onions placed cut-side-down on an oiled pan caramelise intensely on the cut face while the outer layers steam from the inside. A MEATER probe is inserted through the side between skin layers into the centre of the onion half or whole.

## Styckesprofil

Hela eller halverade lökar (*Allium cepa*) ugnsrostade koncentrerar de naturliga sockerarterna, mjukar de fibrosa lagren och karameliserar snittytan. Vid 180–200 °C i 40–60 minuter halverade, eller 60–80 minuter hela. Klar när genomgående mjuk och djupt gyllenbrun.

## Source recipes

### 1. Najmieh Batmanglij — Piyaz Kebab (Persian oven-roasted onion)
**Source**: Najmieh Batmanglij, *Food of Life: Ancient Persian and Modern Iranian Cooking and Ceremonies* (Mage Publishers, 2011)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 45–55 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large yellow onions (≈ 250 g each), unpeeled, root intact
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil or clarified butter (roghan)
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- ½ tsp fine salt
- Flatbread (lavash) and fresh herbs (tarragon, basil), to serve
- Plain yoghurt, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Rub each onion all over with oil, turmeric and salt.
2. Wrap each onion individually in aluminium foil; seal tightly. Place on a baking tray.
3. Insert the MEATER probe by piercing through the foil from the side into the onion interior, tip in the geometric centre. Re-seal foil around the probe.
4. Roast 45–55 minutes until completely soft throughout — squeeze gently through the foil; there should be zero resistance.
5. **Pull at 90–96 °C (194–205 °F).** Batmanglij's whole roasted onions are served as a traditional Iranian side — peeled at the table, the sweet, turmeric-scented flesh is eaten with lavash, fresh herbs and cold yoghurt. Final serving temperature: **room temperature to warm** (sweet, turmeric-golden, Persian tradition).

### 2. José Andrés — Cebollas Enteras al Horno (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 50–60 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large white or yellow onions (≈ 250 g each), unpeeled
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Flor de sal (flaked sea salt)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tbsp aged sherry vinegar (vinagre de Jerez), to drizzle after
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Trim the root end flat (so the onions stand) but leave the skin on and the root just trimmed — skin keeps moisture in.
2. Rub each onion with olive oil; place in a snug roasting tin.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of one onion between skin and flesh into the centre.
4. Roast 50–60 minutes until the outer skin is deeply brown and dry and the interior is completely soft.
5. **Pull at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F).** Andrés describes the doneness as "almost collapsing — the skin is the only thing keeping them together."
6. Score a cross through the top; push sides open; drizzle with sherry vinegar and olive oil, season with salt and pepper. Scatter parsley. Final serving temperature: **warm** (sweet, caramelised, sherry-bright).

### 3. Diana Kennedy — Cebollas al Horno (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium white onions (≈ 200 g each), unpeeled, trimmed at root
- 2 tbsp corn oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 2 fresh serrano chillies (roasted alongside — just place on the tray)
- Lime juice, to serve
- Dried chilli powder and crumbled queso fresco, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Rub each onion with corn oil; season. Place in a lightly oiled baking dish.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the skin side into the centre of one onion.
3. Roast 40–50 minutes, turning once at 25 minutes, until the outer skin is charred and dry and the onion is completely soft.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Place a serrano chilli on the tray for the last 15 minutes of roasting.
5. Remove from oven; cut each onion in half; season with salt and lime juice; dust with chilli powder; crumble queso fresco over. Kennedy serves these alongside grilled meats or as a salsa base (roughly chopped). Final serving temperature: **warm** (charred-sweet, chilli-bright).

### 4. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Whole Roasted Onions with Blue Cheese and Walnuts (British tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *River Cottage Veg Every Day!* (Bloomsbury, 2011)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 50–60 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large white or red onions (≈ 250 g each), unpeeled
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 80 g Stilton or other strong blue cheese, crumbled
- 30 g toasted walnuts, roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground pepper
- Crusty bread, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Trim the root end flat; leave skin on.
2. Rub each onion with olive oil; place on a foil-lined baking tray. Insert the MEATER probe through the skin into the side of one onion, tip in the centre.
3. Roast 50–60 minutes until completely soft. Remove from oven; allow to cool 10 minutes.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Score a cross through the top of each onion; push the sides apart to open a cavity. Fill generously with blue cheese, walnuts and thyme. Return to the oven for 5–8 minutes until the cheese begins to melt.
5. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (sweet, savoury-rich, walnut-crunchy — a complete British vegetarian main).
