# Roasted Onion × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Onions (*Allium cepa*) sautéed as a roasted-style preparation — thick wedges, large rounds, or quartered halves sautéed at medium-high heat in a pan — occupy the territory between caramelised onion (slow-cooked strips) and grilled whole onion. The goal is pieces that brown deeply and develop a caramelised crust while the interior softens and sweetens. This preparation is distinct from the slow caramelised-onion technique (which uses thin slices and low heat) and from grilling (which uses live fire). Internal temperature for fully sautéed onion wedges or rounds with a good crust: 86–92 °C (187–198 °F). MEATER probe inserted horizontally into the thickest wedge near the centre of the pan.

## Styckesprofil

Lökar (*Allium cepa*) sauterade som en ugnsrostningsstil — tjocka klyftor, stora rundlar eller kvartsade halvor sauterade vid medel-hög värme — befinner sig i territoriet mellan karameliserad lök och ugnsrostad lök. 10–15 minuter vid hög värme ger gyllenbrun yta och halvmjuk insida.

## Source recipes

### 1. Cōng Bào Ròu (葱爆肉 — Chinese sautéed scallion/onion, veg adaptation) (inspired by Fuchsia Dunlop)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g large spring onions or large bulb onions, cut into 3 cm sections (white and green parts)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil (peanut or sunflower)
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (老抽) for colour
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish

**Method**
1. Separate the onion pieces; pat completely dry.
2. Heat a wok or wide, heavy frying pan over maximum heat until smoking. Add oil; heat until it shimmers.
3. Add the onion pieces. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into one of the thicker pieces near the centre of the wok.
4. Stir-fry without stirring for 1 minute, then toss every 30 seconds for 3–4 more minutes until the pieces are charred and blistered at the edges and beginning to soften.
5. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry 20 seconds. Add soy sauces, Shaoxing wine and sugar; toss vigorously for 1 minute until the sauce caramelises and glosses the onion.
6. **Pull at 80–86 °C (176–187 °F).** Dunlop targets a tender-crisp result — soft throughout but with some charred-crisp spots at the edges. Drizzle sesame oil; toss once. Final serving temperature: **80–86 °C / 176–186 °F** (charred, sweet-savoury, soy-glossed, serve immediately).

### 2. Tarkari Pyaz (Indian sautéed whole small onions) (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g small pearl onions or shallots (or medium onions quartered into thick wedges), peeled
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 5–6 curry leaves (fresh or frozen)
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 tsp caster sugar (to encourage browning)
- ½ tsp garam masala, to finish
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil or ghee in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds; sizzle 10 seconds. Add curry leaves; sizzle 5 seconds.
2. Add the whole pearl onions or wedges; sprinkle with turmeric, coriander, chilli and salt. Toss to coat.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into one of the thicker onions or wedges near the centre of the pan.
4. Sauté over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, 12–15 minutes until deeply golden-brown on multiple faces and completely tender throughout.
5. Add sugar; toss and cook 2 minutes until caramelised. Add garam masala; toss.
6. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Jaffrey's style is deeply golden-brown whole onions, soft throughout with crisp caramelised edges. Garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (spiced, caramelised, fragrant).

### 3. Cebollas Soasadas a la Plancha (inspired by Francis Mallmann)
**Inspired by**: Francis Mallmann with Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large white onions (≈ 800 g), peeled and halved through the equator
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Coarse sea salt
- 2 tbsp red-wine vinegar
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Heat a large, heavy cast-iron plancha or griddle over medium-high heat until very hot.
2. Brush the cut faces of the onion halves with olive oil. Place cut-side-down on the hot plancha.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of one half into the centre.
4. Cook undisturbed over medium-high heat 8–10 minutes until the cut face is deeply charred with nearly black caramelisation spots.
5. Turn each half; drizzle with remaining olive oil; season with coarse salt. Cook skin-side-down for another 6–8 minutes until the onions are completely soft throughout.
6. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Mallmann's plancha onions are a classic Argentine asado garnish — charred face, sweet interior.
7. Drizzle with red-wine vinegar; scatter parsley. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (charred, sweet, tangy).

### 4. Sautéed Onion Halves with Za'atar (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large red onions (≈ 200 g each), peeled and quartered into thick wedges
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 1 tsp za'atar
- ½ tsp sumac
- 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses, to drizzle
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish
- 15 g fresh mint, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high heat until shimmering.
2. Add the onion wedges in a single layer; season with salt.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into one of the thicker wedges near the centre of the pan.
4. Sauté 6–7 minutes undisturbed until one face is deeply golden. Turn each wedge; sauté 6–7 more minutes on the second face.
5. Dust with za'atar and sumac; toss; cook 2 more minutes until the spices are fragrant.
6. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Ottolenghi's red-onion style: caramelised on two faces, soft inside, with a spice crust.
7. Transfer to a platter; drizzle with pomegranate molasses; scatter pomegranate seeds and mint. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (sweet, spiced, jewel-bright).
