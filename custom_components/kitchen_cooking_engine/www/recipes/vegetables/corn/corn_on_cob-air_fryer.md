# Corn On Cob × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Corn on the cob (*Zea mays*) in the air fryer achieves results similar to oven-roasting but in 12–16 minutes rather than 25–30, with the circulating hot air crisping the outer kernel rows quickly while the inner cob heats through. The corn must fit in the air fryer basket — a standard cob is too long and should be cut in half. The air fryer is particularly effective at caramelising the outer kernel rows to give a satisfying char without live fire. Pull temperature: 86–92 °C (187–198 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted through the kernel rows into the cob core at the mid-point of the half-ear. Husked halved corn ears are the standard preparation.

## Source recipes

### 1. Maangchi — Korean Air-Fried Corn (구운 옥수수 에어프라이어)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); air fryer adaptation of grilled corn technique.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ears of corn, husked, each cut in half (4 pieces)
- *Korean butter baste:* 30 g softened unsalted butter; 1 tbsp gochujang; 1 tsp honey; 1 garlic clove grated; ½ tsp sesame oil; pinch fine salt
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- Spring onion, finely sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Mix the Korean butter baste. Brush the corn halves generously on all sides.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob core of one of the thicker half-ears.
3. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 12 minutes**, turning once at 7 minutes.
4. Brush again with more baste; air fry at 190 °C for 2–4 more minutes until the kernels are golden-caramelised.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (spicy-sweet, gochujang-caramelised).

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Air-Fried Yakimorokoshi (Japanese Soy-Glazed Corn)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ears of corn, husked, cut in half
- *Soy glaze:* 3 tbsp soy sauce; 2 tbsp mirin; 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 10 g cold butter, to finish

**Method**
1. Whisk glaze; brush corn halves on all sides. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob core.
2. Air fry at **190 °C (374 °F) for 10 minutes**, turning once. Brush with more glaze; air fry 2–4 more minutes until golden and caramelised.
3. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Rub cold butter over while hot. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (sweet, soy-caramelised, buttery).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Air-Fried Bhutta with Masala (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); air fryer adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ears of corn, husked, cut in half
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- *Masala mix:* 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; ½ tsp ground cumin; ½ tsp amchur; ½ tsp fine salt; ½ tsp garam masala
- 1 lime, to finish

**Method**
1. Mix masala with oil into a paste; rub generously over corn halves.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob core.
3. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 14–16 minutes**, turning once at 8 minutes.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Squeeze lime over; serve immediately. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (spiced, golden, tangy).

---

### 4. José Andrés — Air-Fried Corn with Compound Butter (Spanish / American tradition)
**Source**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019); technique adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ears of corn, husked, cut in half
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Fine sea salt
- *Compound butter:* 60 g softened butter; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1 tbsp flat-leaf parsley (chopped); ½ tsp lemon zest

**Method**
1. Brush corn halves with olive oil; season lightly with salt.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob core of one half.
3. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 12–14 minutes**, turning once.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Rub compound butter over hot corn. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (golden, paprika-bright, butter-rich).
