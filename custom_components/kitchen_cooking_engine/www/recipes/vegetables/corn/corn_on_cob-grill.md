# Corn On Cob × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Corn on the cob (*Zea mays*) grilled develops a deeply caramelised, smoky exterior when the husks are removed and the bare cob contacts the grill grates. The natural sugars in the kernels (sucrose, which converts to fructose and glucose over time after harvest) caramelise and char where they contact the hot grates, producing the blistered, char-marked kernels characteristic of Mexican elote, Japanese yakimorokoshi, and Indian bhutta. The cob can be grilled in its husk (which steams the kernels) or stripped for direct contact charring. Internal kernel temperature at the ideal doneness — fully cooked, tender and slightly caramelised — is 82–90 °C (180–194 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted through the rows of kernels into the dense cob at the mid-point of the ear, with the tip in the centre of the cob. This monitors the temperature at the kernel-cob interface.

## Source recipes

### 1. Diana Kennedy — Elote Asado con Chile y Crema (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks and silks removed
- 1 tbsp corn oil, to brush
- *Elote toppings (Kennedy's traditional preparation):*
  - 120 ml (½ cup) Mexican crema or soured cream
  - 60 g cotija cheese (or crumbled feta as substitute), finely grated
  - 1–2 tsp dried chilli flakes or ancho chilli powder (polvo de chile)
  - 1 lime, quartered
  - Fine sea salt

**Method**
1. Light a charcoal fire. Allow to burn to white-hot coals. Brush the bare corn ears lightly with oil.
2. Place the ears directly on the grill grate over high direct heat.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernel rows at the mid-point of one ear, with the tip in the centre of the cob.
4. Grill 15–20 minutes, turning a quarter turn every 3–4 minutes, until kernels are golden-brown to deep amber on the outer rows, with some kernels charred in spots. Kennedy states: "The corn should have patches of deep brown — not fully black, but showing the characteristic *tostado* colour of properly grilled corn."
5. **Pull at 85–90 °C (185–194 °F).** The kernels should be tender with an intense caramelised sweetness.
6. Brush the hot corn with crema; roll in grated cotija; dust with chilli powder; squeeze lime over; season with salt. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (charred-sweet, creamy, chilli-bright, the iconic Mexican street corn).

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Yakimorokoshi (焼きとうもろこし — Japanese Soy-Glazed Corn)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks and silks removed
- *Glaze:* 3 tbsp soy sauce; 2 tbsp mirin; 1 tbsp caster sugar; 1 tsp neutral oil
- 10 g unsalted butter, to finish
- Optional: small amount of Kewpie mayonnaise, to serve (the Tokyo street-food style)

**Method**
1. Combine soy sauce, mirin and sugar in a small saucepan over low heat; stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is glossy (2 min). Add oil; reserve.
2. Preheat a gas or charcoal grill to medium-high.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernel rows at the mid-point of one ear, tip in the cob centre.
4. Grill the corn 10–12 minutes over medium-high direct heat, turning a quarter turn every 3 minutes, until lightly golden and beginning to blister.
5. Brush all surfaces generously with the soy-mirin glaze. Grill 3–4 more minutes, turning and brushing repeatedly, until the glaze caramelises and chars slightly at the edges.
6. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Rub a knob of cold butter over each cob while hot; it will melt into the glaze. Serve with Kewpie mayonnaise if desired.
7. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (sweet, smoky, soy-caramelised — the classic Japanese summer festival corn).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Bhutta (Indian Spiced Grilled Corn)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010) and *Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks removed, silks removed
- 1 tbsp sunflower oil
- *Spice rub:* ½ tsp fine sea salt; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; ½ tsp ground cumin; ½ tsp dried mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice
- 1 lime or lemon half, for rubbing (the traditional street-food method: half a lime is cut and used as a brush after dipping in the spice mix)

**Method**
1. Mix salt, chilli powder, cumin and amchur in a small bowl. Brush the corn lightly with oil.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernel rows at the mid-point of one ear, tip in the cob centre.
3. Grill over moderate charcoal or on a gas grill at medium-high direct heat, turning every 3–4 minutes, 15–18 minutes total until the kernels are golden and charred in spots.
4. Jaffrey's authentic street-food technique: cut a lime (or lemon) in half; dip the cut face into the spice mixture; rub this spice-coated lime half all over the hot corn — the lime juice and spices coat every kernel simultaneously.
5. **Pull at 82–88 °C (180–190 °F).** The corn should be hot, tender, spice-coated, and tangy from the lime. Final serving temperature: **82–88 °C / 180–190 °F** (spicy, smoky, citrus-bright — the iconic Indian monsoon street food).

### 4. Maangchi — Guun Oksusu (구운 옥수수 — Korean Grilled Corn)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); grilled corn technique from the Korean BBQ chapter.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks and silks removed
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- *Korean butter baste:* 30 g unsalted butter, softened; 1 tbsp gochujang; 1 tsp honey; 1 garlic clove, grated; ½ tsp sesame oil; pinch of fine salt
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- Spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Mix the Korean butter baste ingredients into a paste.
2. Brush the corn with sesame oil. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernel rows at the mid-point of one ear.
3. Grill over medium-high direct heat 10–12 minutes, turning every 3 minutes, until lightly golden and blistering in spots.
4. Brush the hot corn generously with the gochujang butter baste. Grill 3–4 more minutes, turning and brushing until the baste caramelises.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (spicy-sweet, gochujang-buttery, Korean character).
