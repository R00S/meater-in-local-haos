# Corn on the Cob × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sweet corn on the cob (*Zea mays saccharata*, husked or husk-on) is a dense rack of starch-rich kernels each enclosed in a delicate pericarp. The kernels are 70–75 % water and contain heat-labile sugars that begin converting to starch the moment the cob is harvested. Steaming preserves more of those sugars than boiling because the kernels are not leached; it is also the gentlest method, holding the cob at 100 °C (vapour temperature) without any agitation. The kernel pericarp turns from cloudy-white to translucent-glossy when fully heated through; this visual cue corresponds to an internal kernel temperature of roughly 82–90 °C. Carryover is essentially zero — the cobs are removed straight onto the plate and served immediately.

## Source recipes

### 1. Deborah Madison — Steamed Sweet Corn (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears very fresh sweet corn, husks and silk removed
- Water for the steamer pot (approximately 5 cm depth)
- 40 g unsalted butter, softened, to serve
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt, to serve
- Freshly ground black pepper, to serve
- 1 lime, cut in wedges, to serve (optional)

**Method**
1. Bring 5 cm of water to a vigorous boil in a large pot fitted with a steamer basket or rack.
2. Stand the cobs upright in the basket if possible (Madison's preference, to avoid stacking) — otherwise stack in a single crossed layer.
3. Insert the MEATER Plus probe into the centre of one of the cobs along its long axis, sliding it deep into the pith between rows of kernels so the tip rests at the geometric centre of the cob. Probe should not touch the steamer basket.
4. Cover and steam over high heat 6–8 minutes for very fresh corn (Madison stresses *very fresh* — older corn needs the longer time).
5. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Madison notes the kernels should turn glossy-translucent and burst with sweet liquor when pierced with a thumbnail.
6. Remove from steamer with tongs; serve immediately with butter, salt, pepper and optional lime. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (sweet, juicy, bright kernel snap).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Elotes al Vapor (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Cuisines of Mexico* (Harper & Row, 1972; expanded edition *The Essential Cuisines of Mexico*, Clarkson Potter, 2000)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–14 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears fresh corn, husks left on (silk pulled out from the top, husks loosely re-folded around the cob)
- 2 sprigs fresh epazote (or substitute fresh thyme)
- Water for the steamer (5 cm)
- 80 g (⅓ cup) Mexican crema or sour cream, to serve
- 60 g (½ cup) crumbled queso fresco or cotija, to serve
- 5 g (2 tsp) chilli powder (Tajín or pure ground chile de árbol)
- 1 lime, cut in wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Bring 5 cm of water to a vigorous boil in a large stockpot. Place a steamer rack in the bottom and lay the epazote on the rack.
2. Stand the husk-on cobs vertically on the rack — Kennedy notes that retaining the husk during steaming preserves a grassier corn flavour.
3. Insert the MEATER Plus probe between two rows of kernels into the centre of one cob, parallel to the cob axis, with the tip in the geometric centre.
4. Cover tightly and steam over high heat 10–14 minutes (longer because the husk insulates).
5. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).** Kennedy targets a slightly higher finish than the husk-off American style — the kernels through the husk need a touch more time to set their sugars.
6. Lift cobs out, peel back husks (use tongs, they are very hot), and serve immediately. Smear with crema, sprinkle with cheese, dust with chilli, and squeeze lime over each cob. Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (tender, savoury, husk-perfumed).

### 3. Sarit Packer & Itamar Srulovich — Steamed Corn with Za'atar Butter (Israeli/Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Sarit Packer & Itamar Srulovich, *Honey & Co: The Baking Book* technique extended in their *Honey & Co at Home* (Saltyard, 2018), corn chapter; honeyandco.co.uk  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 7–9 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears sweet corn, husks and silk removed, snapped in half
- Water for the steamer (5 cm)
- 60 g unsalted butter, softened
- 10 g (4 tsp) za'atar
- 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 1 lemon, cut in wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Beat butter with za'atar, garlic and a pinch of salt to make a soft compound. Set aside at room temperature.
2. Bring 5 cm of water to a vigorous boil in a large pot with a steamer basket. Stack the half-cobs in a single layer.
3. Insert the MEATER Plus probe into the cut end of one of the half-cobs nearest the centre, sliding it parallel to the cob axis to the geometric centre.
4. Cover and steam 7–9 minutes over high heat.
5. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** The cobs should look glossy and the kernels translucent with no chalky-white centres.
6. Lift onto a warm platter and immediately smear with the za'atar butter so it melts into the kernels. Squeeze lemon to taste. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (lemony, herbal, juicy).

### 4. Tetsuya Wakuda — Steamed Corn with Yuzu Butter (Japanese-Australian tradition)
**Source**: Tetsuya Wakuda, *Tetsuya: Recipes from Australia's Most Acclaimed Chef* (Ten Speed Press, 2007), corn chapter  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears sweet white or bicolour corn, husks and silk removed
- Water for the steamer (5 cm) with 2 thick slices of fresh ginger added to the water
- 60 g unsalted butter, softened
- 5 ml (1 tsp) yuzu juice (or substitute ½ tsp lemon + ½ tsp mandarin juice)
- 2 g (1 tsp) finely grated yuzu zest (or lemon zest)
- 10 ml (2 tsp) light Japanese soy sauce
- 2 g flaked sea salt, to finish
- 1 g shichimi togarashi, to finish (optional)

**Method**
1. Beat the butter with yuzu juice, zest and soy until smooth and pale. Set aside.
2. Bring 5 cm of water with the ginger slices to a vigorous boil. Place a steamer rack on top.
3. Lay the cobs in a single layer on the rack.
4. Insert the MEATER Plus probe into one of the central cobs along its axis, tip at geometric centre.
5. Cover and steam over high heat 6–8 minutes.
6. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Tetsuya's slightly lower finish preserves the more delicate flavour of white sweet corn varieties — the kernels should be just glossy and pop at the bite.
7. Lift onto warm plates. Smear immediately with yuzu butter; sprinkle with sea salt and a pinch of shichimi if using. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (citrus-perfumed, sweet, delicate).
