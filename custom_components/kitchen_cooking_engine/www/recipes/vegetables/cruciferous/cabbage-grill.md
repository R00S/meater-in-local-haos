# Cabbage × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cabbage (*Brassica oleracea* var. *capitata*) on the grill is most dramatic as thick wedges or halves placed directly on high-heat grates, developing charred outer leaves and a creamy-tender core. The outer leaves act as insulation and protectors: they carbonise at the surface while the inner leaves steam. The technique is particularly popular in Korean BBQ (ssam-style grilled cabbage leaves), Indian street-food style (whole cabbage on coal), Japanese *yakiniku* preparations, and in the new American vegetable-forward grilling style. Pull temperature: 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) at the core of a grilled wedge — the outermost leaves will be charred, the inner ones silky-tender. MEATER probe is inserted through the outer leaves into the thickest part of the core.

## Styckesprofil

Kål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *capitata*) på grillen är mest dramatisk som tjocka klyftor eller halvor placerade direkt på heta grillgallret, och utvecklar förkolnade ytterblad och en krämig insida. Grilla med olja i 5–6 minuter per sida vid direkt hög värme.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yangbaechu Gui (Korean Grilled Cabbage — BBQ style) (inspired by Maangchi)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); https://www.maangchi.com (Korean BBQ vegetable preparations).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 small green cabbage (≈ 700 g), cut into 4 thick wedges, core intact (the core holds the leaves together on the grill)
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- *Gochujang dipping sauce:* 2 tbsp gochujang; 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tsp honey; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tsp rice vinegar; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Brush the cut faces and outer surfaces of each cabbage wedge with sesame oil.
2. Preheat a charcoal or gas grill to high direct heat.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the outer leaves into the thickest part of the core.
4. Place wedges cut-face-down on the hot grates. Grill undisturbed 4–5 minutes until deeply charred on the cut face. Turn to the second cut face; grill 3–4 minutes more. Turn outer leaf–side-down for final 2–3 minutes.
5. **Pull at 85–90 °C (185–194 °F).** Maangchi targets charred outer leaves and soft, silky inner leaves with a sweet-smoky flavour.
6. Mix the gochujang dipping sauce. Serve wedges whole — eat by pulling individual leaves and dipping in the sauce. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (charred, smoky, spicy-sweet dipping sauce).

### 2. Repollo a las Brasas (inspired by Francis Mallmann)
**Inspired by**: Francis Mallmann with Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 small green cabbage (≈ 700 g), quartered through the core
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Coarse sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) red-wine vinegar
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Optional: 1 tsp crushed dried chilli flakes

**Method**
1. Build a wood fire to moderate-heat embers. Brush each cabbage quarter on all faces with olive oil; season generously with coarse salt and pepper.
2. Place quarters directly on a hot plancha or cast-iron griddle over the coals. Insert the MEATER probe through the outer leaves into the core.
3. Grill 6–8 minutes on the first cut face (undisturbed) until deeply charred. Turn to the second cut face; grill 5–6 more minutes. Turn outer-leaf side down; grill 3–4 minutes.
4. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Mallmann describes the result as "charred-black on the outside and perfectly tender within — a vegetable that has been properly transformed by fire."
5. Drizzle with vinegar; scatter parsley and chilli flakes. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (charred, sweet, tangy).

### 3. Grilled Cabbage with Indian Spices (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 small green cabbage (≈ 700 g), cut into 4 wedges, core intact
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp fine salt
- Juice of 1 lime
- 15 g fresh coriander, chopped

**Method**
1. Mix oil with cumin, turmeric, chilli powder, garam masala and salt into a spice paste. Brush over all surfaces of the cabbage wedges.
2. Preheat grill to high. Insert MEATER probe through outer leaves into core.
3. Grill 4–5 min per face (two cut faces + outer leaf side) as in source 1 above.
4. **Pull at 85–90 °C (185–194 °F).** Squeeze lime over; scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (charred, spiced, tangy-bright).

---

### 4. Grilled Cabbage Wedges with Blue Cheese Dressing (inspired by Deborah Madison)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 small green cabbage (≈ 700 g), cut into 4 wedges
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground pepper
- *Blue cheese dressing:* 80 g crumbled Roquefort or Gorgonzola; 60 g soured cream; 1 tbsp white-wine vinegar; 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Brush cabbage wedges with olive oil; season.
2. Grill cut-face-down on a high-heat gas or charcoal grill 4–5 min per face. Insert MEATER probe through outer leaves into core.
3. **Pull at 85–90 °C (185–194 °F).** Whisk dressing and spoon over hot wedges. Final serving temperature: **85–90 °C / 185–194 °F** (charred, sweet, creamy-rich blue cheese dressing).
