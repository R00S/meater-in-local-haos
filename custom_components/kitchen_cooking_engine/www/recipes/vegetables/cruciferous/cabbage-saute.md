# Cabbage × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cabbage (*Brassica oleracea* var. *capitata*) sautéed is one of the fastest and most flavourful preparations: shredded or thinly sliced cabbage in a hot, oiled pan wilts and sweetens within 5–8 minutes, going from raw-crunchy to tender-crisp. The technique spans Chinese stir-fries (高麗菜炒蛋 or cumin spiced), Thai pad cabbage (pad kalam plee), Indian bandh gobhi ki sabzi, German Schmorkohl, and East European *kapusta smażona*. Internal temperature at tender-crisp (ideal for most stir-fries): 78–82 °C (172–180 °F); at fully soft: 84–88 °C (183–190 °F). The MEATER probe is inserted into the densest pile of shredded cabbage in the pan.

## Styckesprofil

Kål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *capitata*) sauterad är en av de snabbaste och mest smakrika beredningarna: strimlad eller tunt skivad kål i en het, olja-smord panna vissnar och sötar sig under 8–12 minuter. Klar när mjuk och lätt gyllenbrun.

## Source recipes

### 1. Pailin Chongchitnant — Pad Kalam Plee (Thai Stir-Fried Cabbage)
**Inspired by**: Pailin Chongchitnant, *Hot Thai Kitchen* (Appetite by Random House, 2016); https://hot-thai-kitchen.com (stir-fried cabbage technique).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g green cabbage, tough outer leaves removed, thinly shredded (3–4 mm)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce (นํ้ามันหอย)
- 1 tbsp fish sauce (nám pla)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp white pepper
- Optional: 1–2 long red chillies (prik cheefa), diagonally sliced

**Method**
1. Pat cabbage completely dry. Heat wok or wide pan over maximum heat until smoking. Add oil; heat until it shimmers.
2. Add garlic; stir-fry 15 seconds until fragrant (not browned). Add cabbage all at once.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the densest part of the cabbage pile near the centre of the wok.
4. Stir-fry over maximum heat, tossing constantly, for 3–4 minutes until the cabbage is beginning to wilt but still has good texture.
5. Add oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar and white pepper; toss vigorously 1 more minute. Add chillies if using.
6. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Pailin targets tender-crisp cabbage — slightly wilted but with bite, in a savoury-sweet glossy sauce. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (tender-crisp, savoury-umami, serve immediately with rice).

### 2. Fuchsia Dunlop — Gān Biān Bǎitái (干煸白菜 — Chinese Dry-Fried Cabbage with Chilli)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g Chinese cabbage or green cabbage, cut into irregular chunks (Dunlop uses 4 cm pieces)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil (peanut or sunflower)
- 3 dried red chillies (线辣椒), snipped into 2–3 cm pieces (seeds shaken out)
- 1 tbsp Sichuan doubanjiang (辣豆瓣酱, optional) or 1 tsp dried chilli flakes
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp black rice vinegar (Chinkiang)
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil

**Method**
1. Pat cabbage pieces very dry. Heat wok over very high heat until smoking.
2. Add oil; when it shimmers, add chillies and Sichuan spice (if using); stir-fry 15 seconds. Add cabbage.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the densest piece near the centre.
4. Stir-fry over high heat 4–5 minutes, tossing frequently, until the cabbage is slightly wilted and beginning to char at the edges. Add garlic; cook 30 seconds. Add soy sauce, sugar and vinegar; toss vigorously 30 seconds.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Dunlop's *gan bian* technique allows some edges to char while retaining some bite. Drizzle sesame oil. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (charred-edged, spicy, balanced sweet-sour).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Bandh Gobhi aur Zeera (Indian Cumin-Spiced Cabbage)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g green cabbage, finely shredded
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- ¼ tsp whole brown or black mustard seeds
- 10–12 fresh curry leaves
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 tbsp desiccated coconut, to finish (optional, South Indian touch)
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high. Add mustard seeds; let them pop (10 s). Add cumin and curry leaves; sizzle 5 s.
2. Add cabbage, turmeric, chilli and salt; toss. Insert MEATER probe into the densest section.
3. Sauté over medium-high heat 8–10 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes, until tender.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Scatter coconut if using; garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, fragrant, tender).

---

### 4. Nigel Slater — Schmorkohl / Sautéed Sweetheart Cabbage with Caraway and Cream (German / British tradition)
**Inspired by**: Nigel Slater, *Tender Vol. I* (4th Estate, 2009); sweetheart cabbage with cream and caraway as a British-German interpretation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g sweetheart (pointed) cabbage or Savoy cabbage, thinly shredded
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tsp caraway seeds
- 125 ml (½ cup) double cream
- 1 tbsp white-wine vinegar
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground white pepper
- 15 g fresh dill or flat-leaf parsley, to scatter

**Method**
1. Melt butter in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add caraway seeds; sizzle 20 seconds. Add cabbage; toss.
2. Insert MEATER probe into the densest shredded pile.
3. Sauté 5–6 minutes, stirring, until wilted but not mushy. Add cream and vinegar; stir; cook 2–3 minutes until cream reduces and coats the cabbage.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Season with salt and white pepper; scatter dill or parsley. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (creamy, caraway-fragrant, tangy — classic Central European character).
