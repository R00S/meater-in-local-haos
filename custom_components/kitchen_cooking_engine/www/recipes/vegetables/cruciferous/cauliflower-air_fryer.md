# Cauliflower × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cauliflower (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) in the air fryer achieves crispy, golden-brown florets in 12–16 minutes — faster than a conventional oven and with less oil required. The rapid convection is particularly effective at evaporating the cauliflower's surface moisture (florets are ≈ 92 % water) before Maillard browning begins. Air-fried cauliflower is a globally popular preparation: from Indian gobi tikka (marinated in spiced yoghurt) to Korean spicy *gobi*, Israeli za'atar cauliflower, and plain roasted Western versions. Pull temperature: 80–86 °C (176–187 °F) for golden, fully tender florets. A MEATER probe is inserted through the stem of the largest floret into its centre before the cook begins.

## Styckesprofil

Blomkål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) i airfryern ger knaprig, gyllenbrun blomma på 12–16 minuter — snabbare än en konventionell ugn och med mindre olja. Dela i buketter, olja, krydda. Klar när gyllenbrun och crispy.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madhur Jaffrey — Gobi Tikka (Air Fryer — Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); tikka marinade adapted for cauliflower in the air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 20 min marinating · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower, cut into 3 cm florets
- *Tikka marinade:* 100 g full-fat yoghurt; 1 tbsp sunflower oil; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp grated ginger; 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp garam masala; ½ tsp fine salt; juice of ½ lemon
- Lemon wedges, raw onion rings, fresh coriander, mint chutney, to serve

**Method**
1. Toss florets in marinade; marinate 20 minutes.
2. Place in a single layer in the air fryer basket.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the stem of the largest floret into its centre.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 14–16 minutes**, shaking once at 8 minutes, until the marinade is charred in spots and the florets are golden-brown.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Serve immediately with lemon, coriander, onion rings and mint chutney. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (charred, spiced, juicy — Indian tandoor character without a tandoor).

### 2. Maangchi — Korean Spicy Air-Fried Gobi (Korean tradition)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); Korean spicy cauliflower adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets (3 cm)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- *Korean sauce:* 2 tbsp gochujang; 1 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp honey; 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 garlic clove (minced); 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 1 spring onion, sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Toss florets with neutral oil; place in air fryer basket. Insert MEATER probe through largest floret stem.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 12 min**, shaking once. Remove; toss in Korean sauce; return to air fryer at 190 °C for 2–4 more minutes.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spicy-sweet, gochujang-glazed).

---

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Cauliflower with Za'atar and Yoghurt (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010); air fryer adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp sweet smoked paprika
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- *Yoghurt sauce:* 150 g Greek yoghurt; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tsp lemon juice; salt
- 1 tbsp za'atar, to finish
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish
- 10 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Toss florets in olive oil, cumin, paprika, turmeric and salt.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 14–16 min**, shaking once. Insert MEATER probe through largest floret stem.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Spread yoghurt sauce on a platter; pile florets over; dust with za'atar; scatter pomegranate seeds and parsley. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, spiced, yoghurt-cool).

---

### 4. Harumi Kurihara — Air-Fried Cauliflower Karaage (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); karaage technique applied to cauliflower, adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 15 min marinating · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- *Karaage marinade:* 2 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp sake; 1 tsp sesame oil; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tsp grated ginger
- 40 g (3 tbsp) potato starch (katakuriko), to coat
- Kewpie mayonnaise, lemon wedges and shredded cabbage, to serve

**Method**
1. Toss florets in marinade; rest 15 minutes. Coat lightly in potato starch.
2. Spray or brush with neutral oil; place in air fryer basket. Insert MEATER probe through largest floret stem.
3. Air fry at **200 °C for 14–16 min**, shaking once.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Serve with Kewpie mayo, lemon and shredded cabbage. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (crispy-coated, soy-ginger-fragrant, Japanese karaage character).
