# Cauliflower × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cauliflower (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) oven-roasted in florets at 200–230 °C develops golden, caramelised surfaces and a tender, nutty interior in 20–30 minutes. This is one of the most globally versatile roasted vegetable preparations: with Moroccan spicing (chermoula), Indian masala, Israeli za'atar, Italian *aglio e olio*, or simple olive oil and salt. The outer floret surface undergoes Maillard browning while the interior steams in the oven heat. Pull temperature for golden, fully tender florets: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F). MEATER probe inserted through the stem of the largest floret into its geometric centre.

## Source recipes

### 1. Claudia Roden — Chou-fleur Rôti à la Chermoula (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets (3–4 cm)
- *Chermoula marinade:* 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp sweet paprika; ½ tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp ground ginger; pinch of saffron (dissolved in 1 tbsp warm water); 2 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g fresh coriander (chopped); 10 g fresh flat-leaf parsley; 1 tsp fine salt
- Additional olive oil for roasting
- Lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Blend all chermoula ingredients into a paste. Toss florets in the chermoula; allow to marinate 15 minutes.
2. Spread on a lined sheet pan. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F).
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the stem of the largest floret.
4. Roast 25–30 minutes until golden-brown and caramelised at the tops. 
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Roden: "Fully tender, golden and fragrant — no rawness remaining." Serve with lemon. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (golden, spiced, North African character).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Gobi Roast with Turmeric and Coriander (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine salt
- ½ tsp garam masala, to finish
- Lemon juice and fresh coriander, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss florets with oil and spices. Spread on lined sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the largest floret stem.
3. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once at 15 minutes, until golden and tender.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Dust with garam masala; squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Cauliflower with Capers and Raisins (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fine salt
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g golden raisins
- 2 tbsp capers, rinsed
- 30 g pine nuts, toasted
- 2 tbsp aged balsamic vinegar
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss florets with olive oil, salt and pepper. Spread on sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the largest floret stem.
3. Roast 22–25 minutes until golden. Scatter raisins and capers; return 3–4 more minutes.
4. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Drizzle balsamic; scatter pine nuts and parsley. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (golden, sweet-sour-briny).

---

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Cavolfiore al Forno (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Optional: 2 tbsp breadcrumbs (pangrattato), to scatter in the last 5 min

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Toss florets with olive oil, salt and pepper. Spread on sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the largest floret stem.
3. Roast 20–25 minutes until golden. Add garlic and parsley in last 3–4 min; return briefly.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Hazan: "The garlic must not burn." Scatter breadcrumbs if using for the final 3 minutes. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, garlic-bright, Italian).
