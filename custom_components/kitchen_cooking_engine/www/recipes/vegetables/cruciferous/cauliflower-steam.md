# Cauliflower × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cauliflower (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) steamed preserves more of the glucosinolate compounds (which have health benefits) and the delicate flavour compared to boiling, which leaches these compounds into the water. Florets steamed over boiling water reach their target temperature of 80–85 °C (176–185 °F) in 7–10 minutes depending on size, at which point they are fork-tender with no raw starch taste. The classic test: a floret should yield easily to a paring knife inserted into the stem, but not crumble. A MEATER probe is inserted through the stem of the largest floret into its geometric centre; the probe should be set before the steam begins.

## Styckesprofil

Blomkål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) ångad bevarar fler av glukosinolat-föreningarna (som har hälsofördelar) och den delikata smaken jämfört med kokning. Ånga i 10–15 minuter (buketter) ovanför kokande vatten. Klar när genomstekt och mjuk men inte mosig.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Zhēng Huā Cài (蒸花椰菜 — Chinese Steamed Cauliflower with Sesame Sauce)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower, cut into 3–4 cm florets
- *Sesame sauce:* 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste (or tahini); 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp black rice vinegar (Chinkiang); 1 tsp sesame oil; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 garlic clove grated; 1–2 tbsp cold water, to thin
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- Optional: 1 tsp chilli oil (红油), to drizzle

**Method**
1. Set up a steamer basket over boiling water. Place florets in the basket.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the stem of the largest floret to its geometric centre.
3. Steam 8–10 minutes until completely tender (paring knife meets no resistance).
4. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Dunlop's Chinese sesame sauce style: completely tender florets dressed in a rich, nutty, slightly tangy sauce.
5. Whisk the sesame sauce; drizzle over the hot florets. Scatter spring onion and sesame seeds; drizzle chilli oil if using. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (tender, nutty, sesame-tangy).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Masala Steamed Gobi (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets (3–4 cm)
- *After-steam masala:* 2 tbsp neutral oil; 1 tsp whole mustard seeds; 10 fresh curry leaves; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; ½ tsp fine salt; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Steam florets over boiling water 8–10 minutes. Insert MEATER probe through the largest floret stem.
2. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Transfer florets to a serving bowl.
3. For the masala: heat oil; add mustard seeds (pop); add curry leaves (sizzle 5 s); add turmeric and coriander (20 s). Pour immediately over the steamed florets; add chilli, salt and lemon juice; toss. Garnish with fresh coriander.
4. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (spiced, tender, bright).

### 3. Julia Child — Chou-fleur Vapeur au Beurre Noisette (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- 60 g (4 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Freshly ground white pepper
- 1 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Steam florets over boiling water 8–10 minutes. Insert MEATER probe through the largest floret stem.
2. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
3. While the florets steam, cook the butter in a small pan over medium heat until it turns nut-brown (*beurre noisette*) — 3–4 minutes; remove from heat and add lemon juice (it will spit — be careful). Season with salt and white pepper.
4. Transfer florets to a warm serving bowl; pour the beurre noisette over; scatter parsley.
5. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (tender, nutty-butter, lemon-bright — the classic French preparation).

### 4. Claudia Roden — Steamed Cauliflower with Smen and Preserved Lemon (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g cauliflower florets
- 1 tbsp smen (Moroccan preserved butter) — or substitute: 1 tbsp unsalted butter + ½ tsp salt
- 1 preserved lemon, skin only, finely diced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp sweet paprika
- Pinch of cayenne
- 15 g fresh coriander, chopped
- Lemon juice, to finish

**Method**
1. Steam florets 8–10 minutes. Insert MEATER probe through largest floret stem.
2. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Transfer to a serving platter.
3. Melt smen in a small pan; add preserved lemon, cumin and paprika; warm 1 minute. Pour over the hot florets. Add cayenne and lemon juice. Scatter coriander.
4. Final serving temperature: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (tender, spiced, preserved-lemon-bright, Moroccan).
