# Cauliflower × Whole Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole-roasted cauliflower (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) has become one of the signature vegetable showpieces of contemporary international cooking, popularised especially by Yotam Ottolenghi and the broader Middle Eastern–influenced restaurant world. The whole head is pre-boiled or pre-steamed briefly to begin cooking, then roasted at 190–220 °C for 30–45 minutes, producing a deeply golden, caramelised exterior that contrasts with the creamy interior. Alternatively, it is placed raw in a salt dough or foil enclosure and cooked at lower temperatures for longer. The core-to-outer temperature gradient is significant: the outermost florets reach 90+ °C while the stem/core may still be at 75–80 °C. The MEATER probe must be inserted into the thickest part of the stem/core from the base — this is the temperature that matters, as it is the last to reach doneness. Pull at 85–90 °C (185–194 °F) at the core; the outer florets will then be caramelised and some will be charred.

## Styckesprofil

Helrostad blomkål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *botrytis*) har blivit ett av de moderna internationella matlagningens signatur-vegetabiliska showpieces. Koka först i saltat vatten 10 min, pensla med kryddsmör, rosta vid 200–220 °C i 40–50 minuter. Klar när djupt gyllenbrun på alla sidor.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi — Whole Roasted Cauliflower with Harissa Butter (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min + pre-boiling · **Cook**: 40–45 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole cauliflower (≈ 900 g–1 kg), outer leaves trimmed but some kept for decoration
- *Harissa butter baste:* 40 g unsalted butter, softened; 2 tbsp harissa paste (rose harissa if available); 1 tbsp olive oil; 1 garlic clove grated; ½ tsp fine salt
- *Garnish:* 30 g flaked almonds (toasted); 30 g pomegranate seeds; 15 g fresh mint; 60 g labneh or thick Greek yoghurt; drizzle of olive oil; sumac

**Method**
1. Blanch the whole cauliflower in a large pot of boiling salted water for 10–12 minutes, until just barely tender at the core (test with a skewer: it should pierce the core with some resistance). Drain and cool slightly.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Line a baking dish with foil.
3. Mix the harissa butter. Spread generously over the entire surface of the blanched cauliflower.
4. Place cauliflower stem-side-up first to insert the MEATER probe: push the probe through the base of the stem into the geometric core of the cauliflower. Flip back to crown-side-up in the baking dish.
5. Roast 35–45 minutes, basting once at 20 minutes with more harissa butter, until the outer florets are deeply golden-brown and charred at the tips and the core registers the target temperature.
6. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F)** at the core. Allow to rest 10 minutes before serving.
7. Place on a platter; spoon labneh alongside; scatter almonds, pomegranate seeds and mint; drizzle olive oil; dust with sumac. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** at service (golden-charred, spicy, jewel-bright — a showpiece centrepiece).

### 2. Najmieh Batmanglij — Whole Roasted Kalam Polo (Persian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Najmieh Batmanglij, *Food of Life: Ancient Persian and Modern Iranian Cooking and Ceremonies* (Mage Publishers, 2011)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min + pre-steaming · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole cauliflower (≈ 900 g), outer leaves trimmed
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil or clarified butter (roghan)
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp saffron (dissolved in 2 tbsp warm water)
- 1 tsp fine salt
- *Topping:* 60 g raisins (soaked 10 min); 30 g slivered pistachios; 15 g dried barberries (zereshk); fresh flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Steam the whole cauliflower over boiling water for 15 minutes until just barely tender at the core.
2. Mix oil with turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, saffron water and salt into a golden paste. Brush all over the steamed cauliflower.
3. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Insert the MEATER probe from the base into the cauliflower core.
4. Place crown-side-up in a baking dish. Roast 30–35 minutes, basting once, until deeply golden.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F)** at the core.
6. Garnish with raisins, pistachios, barberries and parsley. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (golden, saffron-fragrant, sweet-sour Persian garnish).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Whole Dum Gobi (Indian Spiced Whole Cauliflower)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min + marinating · **Cook**: 35–45 min · **Rest**: 8 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole cauliflower (≈ 900 g), outer leaves trimmed
- *Masala coating:* 3 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; 1 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp fine salt; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp grated ginger; 2 tbsp thick yoghurt; 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Lemon wedges and fresh coriander, to serve

**Method**
1. Par-cook the whole cauliflower in boiling water 10 minutes; drain. Mix masala coating; rub all over the whole cauliflower. Marinate 15 minutes.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Insert MEATER probe from base into core. Place on a foil-lined tray.
3. Roast 30–35 minutes, basting once at 15 minutes, until deeply golden and spice-charred at the tips.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F)** at the core. Rest 8 minutes. Serve with lemon and coriander. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (spiced, golden, tangy — a dramatic Indian centrepiece).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Ugnsrostad Hel Blomkål (Nordic / Swedish tradition)
**Inspired by**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 50–60 min (no pre-blanching) · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole cauliflower (≈ 900 g), outer leaves trimmed, stem intact
- 3 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 1 tsp flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- *Nordic brown butter:* 60 g unsalted butter cooked to nut-brown (beurre noisette); 1 tbsp roe (whitefish or bleak roe); 1 tbsp chives (finely chopped); ½ tsp lemon juice; flaked salt

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. No blanching — Nilsson roasts entirely from raw for this preparation, yielding a longer cook but more Maillard depth.
2. Rub the cauliflower all over with rapeseed oil; season with flaked salt. Place crown-side-up on a sheet pan.
3. Insert MEATER probe from the base through the stem into the core.
4. Roast 50–60 minutes until the entire surface is deeply golden-brown and the core reads target temperature.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F)** at the core. Rest 10 minutes.
6. Serve whole; pour the Nordic brown butter with roe over the top at the table. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (golden, nutty-butter, Nordic elegance).
