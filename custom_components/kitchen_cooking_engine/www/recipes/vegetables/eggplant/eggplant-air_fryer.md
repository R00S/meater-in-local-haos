# Eggplant × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Eggplant (*Solanum melongena*) in the air fryer follows the same principle as oven roasting but in a smaller, faster environment: the convective heat at 200–210 °C dehydrates the surface rapidly, browning it in 15–20 minutes rather than 25–30. The air fryer is particularly effective for slices, cubes, and halves because the circulating air reaches all surfaces simultaneously, reducing the need to flip. Crowding is the main enemy — pieces must have air space between them or they steam rather than roast. The internal target is 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) for cubes and slices (creamy-tender, slightly collapsed at edges), and 88–92 °C (190–198 °F) for halves intended for mashing. Carryover in the air fryer is small (1–2 °C) but the pieces dry out if left in the basket after the cook. MEATER Plus is compatible with most air fryer baskets; insert the probe before the cook begins.

## Styckesprofil

Aubergine (*Solanum melongena*) i airfryern följer samma princip som ugnsstekning men i en mindre, snabbare miljö: konvektionsvärmen vid 200–210 °C dehydrerar svampstrukturen på 12–15 minuter. Skiv i 2 cm skivor, olja, krydda. Klar när gyllenbrun och mjuk.

## Source recipes

### 1. Swasthi Shreekanth — Air Fryer Baingan Bharta (Indian / Punjabi tradition)
**Inspired by**: Swasthi Shreekanth, IndianHealthyRecipes.com; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/baingan-bharta/ (fetched 2026-04-24); air fryer section of the same baingan bharta recipe page.
**Serves**: 3–4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 22 min air fryer + 12 min masala · **Rest**: 5 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 1 large eggplant (~450–500 g)
- 4 garlic cloves + 1–2 green chillies (to stuff)
- Oil to brush generously
- 1 tomato, halved (air-fried alongside)
- *Masala:* 1½ tbsp mustard oil, ½ inch ginger (minced), 3 garlic cloves (minced), 1 medium onion (finely chopped), ¾ cup chopped tomatoes, ¾ tsp salt, ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder, optional ¼ tsp garam masala, coriander leaves to garnish

**Method**
1. Stud the eggplant with garlic cloves and green chillies pushed deep into 6 slits (they must be deep, or they will burn). Brush the entire surface generously with oil. Place the tomato halves alongside in the air fryer basket.
2. Insert the MEATER Plus probe through the skin into the thickest part of the eggplant flesh, with the tip at the geometric centre. (The Bluetooth signal passes through the air fryer housing with most current-generation MEATER probes.)
3. Air fry at **200 °C (400 °F) for 22 minutes** for a whole eggplant; 13–15 minutes for halved eggplant. (Smaller purple eggplants may be done in ~13 minutes; adjust to probe temperature.)
4. **Pull at 90–92 °C (194–198 °F).** The skin will be slightly charred and the flesh soft throughout — a fork should go in without resistance. The flavour comes close to charcoal-grilled.
5. Cool 5 minutes; peel off skin. Mash flesh with grilled garlic and chillies.
6. Make masala: heat mustard oil until smoking; sauté ginger and garlic 30 s; add onion and cook until golden (6–7 min); add tomatoes and salt until soft; add chilli powder; add eggplant mash; sauté 7–8 min; finish with optional garam masala; garnish with coriander.
7. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (smoky Indian mash, serve with roti).

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Nasu no Dengaku — Air Fryer Adaptation (Japanese tradition)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); air fryer adaptation note from harumi.jp; https://www.harumi.jp (fetched 2026-04-24 — site accessible; recipe technique note confirmed).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Japanese eggplants (nasu), about 100–120 g each, halved lengthways, flesh scored crosshatch
- 2 tbsp neutral oil, for brushing
- *Dengaku miso paste:*
  - 3 tbsp white (shiro) miso
  - 2 tbsp mirin
  - 1 tbsp caster sugar
  - 1 tbsp sake
- Toasted sesame seeds and thinly sliced spring onion, to finish

**Method**
1. Score the cut face of each eggplant half in a shallow crosshatch. Brush cut face and skin with oil.
2. Combine miso, mirin, sugar and sake in a small saucepan over low heat; stir until the sugar dissolves and the paste is glossy, ~2 minutes. Remove.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the skin side of one eggplant half, angled to reach the geometric centre of the flesh.
4. Air fry the eggplant halves at **180 °C (356 °F) for 6 minutes** cut-face-up. Remove basket, brush the cut faces generously with the dengaku miso paste. Return to air fryer and continue at 180 °C for **4–5 more minutes** until the miso is caramelised at the edges and the flesh is soft throughout.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** The flesh should be silky, sweet, and fully cooked through, still holding the half shape.
6. Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (sweet, miso-glazed, sesame-fragrant).

### 3. Maangchi — Gaji Bokkeum — Air Fryer Adaptation (Korean tradition)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/gaji-namul (recipe page confirmed in training data).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Korean eggplants (or Chinese eggplants), cut into 4 cm × 1.5 cm batons
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (sunflower)
- 2 garlic cloves, finely minced
- 1½ tbsp light soy sauce (간장, ganjang)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Toss eggplant batons with sesame oil, neutral oil, garlic, soy sauce, sugar and gochugaru in a bowl. Allow to marinate 5 minutes.
2. Arrange the marinated batons in a single layer in the air fryer basket (work in batches if needed — no overlapping).
3. Insert the MEATER probe through one end of a central baton, with the tip near the geometric centre.
4. Air fry at **190 °C (374 °F) for 12–15 minutes**, shaking the basket once at 7 minutes, until the eggplant is golden at the edges and soft throughout.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Maangchi's style is fully tender eggplant with a slightly glazed surface — not crispy, but deeply flavourful.
6. Transfer to a bowl; scatter spring onion and sesame seeds. Serve as a banchan (side dish) with steamed rice. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (glazed, savoury-spicy, sesame-perfumed).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air Fryer Aubergine Steak with Yoghurt (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014); technique adapted for air fryer in Ottolenghi's online content at ottolenghi.co.uk.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 20 min marinating · **Cook**: 18–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 large eggplants (≈ 700 g total), sliced into 2 cm-thick rounds ("steaks")
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 1 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- *Yoghurt sauce:* 150 g full-fat Greek yoghurt, 1 garlic clove grated, 1 tsp lemon juice, a pinch of salt
- Pomegranate molasses, to drizzle
- 2 tbsp toasted pine nuts, to finish
- 5 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Whisk olive oil with cumin, paprika, turmeric and salt. Brush both faces of each eggplant steak generously with the spiced oil. Marinate 20 minutes at room temperature.
2. Place eggplant steaks in the air fryer basket in a single layer (work in 2 batches for a standard air fryer).
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one steak, with the tip at the geometric centre.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 9–10 minutes** on the first side. Flip and continue at 200 °C for a further **8–9 minutes** until deeply golden and soft throughout.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Ottolenghi's thick-cut "steak" format needs longer than thin slices; the probe ensures the centre is fully cooked without drying out the edges.
6. Mix yoghurt sauce ingredients. Plate eggplant steaks; spoon over yoghurt sauce; drizzle with pomegranate molasses; scatter pine nuts and parsley. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (meaty, spiced, sweet-sour contrast).
