# Eggplant × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Eggplant (*Solanum melongena*) oven-roasted develops rich, caramelised surfaces across the cut faces of slices, halves, or cubes, while the interior transitions from dense and spongy to silky and almost melting. Unlike pan-frying, the dry oven heat draws moisture outward without submersion in oil, producing a less greasy result; the sacrifice is a slower and less intense browning than stovetop contact. The technique suits whole eggplants destined for mashing (baked at 200–220 °C until skin chars and flesh collapses), thick slices or halves (roasted at 200–220 °C cut-face-down on an oiled sheet pan), and cubes or batons (tossed in oil and roasted at 220 °C until deeply golden). A MEATER probe inserted into the thickest part of the flesh monitors the interior: the target for slices or cubes is 85–90 °C (185–194 °F); for a whole eggplant intended for mashing, 90–95 °C is the pull point. Carryover is minimal for vegetables at this degree of doneness.

## Source recipes

### 1. 小麦芽15 — 好吃到连皮都不剩的烤茄子 (Chinese Oven Roasted Eggplant with Garlic-Cumin Sauce)
**Source**: Home cook "小麦芽15" (Xiaomaiya15), Xiachufang.com (China's largest recipe platform); https://m.xiachufang.com/recipe/104415647/ (fetched 2026-04-24, language: Chinese — ingredients and method translated below).
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients** *(translated from Chinese)*
- 2 large eggplants
- 2 tsp (孜然粉) cumin powder
- 1 tbsp (耗油/蚝油) oyster sauce
- 2 tbsp (生抽) light soy sauce
- 6–8 garlic cloves, finely minced
- 15 g (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- Small handful scallions (葱), sliced
- Small amount pickled radish strips (腌萝卜干), optional garnish
- Salt and sugar to taste
- Garlic chilli sauce (蒜蓉辣椒酱), optional

**Method** *(translated from Chinese)*
1. Wash eggplants thoroughly, remove the stems. Using a pastry brush, coat the entire surface of each eggplant with a thin layer of vegetable oil.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Roast the oiled eggplants directly on the rack or on a foil-lined tray for **20 minutes**.
3. Meanwhile, prepare the sauce: mince the garlic; chop the pickled radish and scallions. In a small bowl, combine oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and cumin powder; stir well.
4. Heat the vegetable oil in a small saucepan over medium heat; add the minced garlic and fry briefly until fragrant (30 seconds). Immediately pour the hot garlic-oil into the sauce bowl and stir. Season with salt and sugar to taste.
5. Remove the eggplants from the oven. Insert the MEATER Plus probe through the side of one eggplant into the thickest part of the flesh to check the internal temperature.
6. Using a knife, score the top surface of each eggplant along its length from end to end — cut only through the top skin and upper flesh, leaving the bottom skin intact. Open the eggplant gently to expose the flesh. Score the exposed flesh a few more times crosswise.
7. Spread the garlic-cumin sauce evenly over the exposed flesh. Scatter pickled radish and chilli sauce if using. Return to oven for a further **5 minutes**.
8. Remove, garnish with sliced scallions. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F)** — the flesh should feel completely soft when pressed through the skin. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (tender, smoky-garlicky, cumin-fragrant — eat skin and all).

### 2. Swasthi Shreekanth — Baingan Bharta — Oven Method (Indian / Punjabi tradition)
**Source**: Swasthi Shreekanth, IndianHealthyRecipes.com; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/baingan-bharta/ (fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 3–4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 16–20 min broil + 12 min masala · **Rest**: 5 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 1 large eggplant, 450–500 g
- 4 garlic cloves + 1–2 green chillies (to stuff into slits)
- Oil to brush generously
- 1 medium tomato, halved (roasted alongside eggplant)
- *Masala:* 1½ tbsp mustard oil, ½ inch ginger (minced), 3 garlic cloves (minced), 1 medium onion (finely chopped), ¾ cup chopped ripe tomatoes, ¾ tsp salt, ½ tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder, ¼ tsp garam masala (optional), coriander leaves to garnish

**Method**
1. Cut each eggplant in half lengthwise. Make 2 slits on each cut half; push garlic cloves and chillies deep into the slits. Brush the cut faces and skin with oil.
2. Line a metal baking tray with aluminium foil. Place the eggplant halves cut-face-up on the tray; place the tomato halves alongside.
3. Set oven to broil/grill at 240 °C (460 °F). Broil 16–20 minutes until the top is slightly charred and a fork goes in smoothly.
4. Insert the MEATER probe through the cut face of one eggplant half into the thickest part of the flesh before placing in the oven, with the tip at the geometric centre of the half.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** The flesh should be soft throughout; the broiled eggplant will not achieve the same smokiness as an open flame but the flavour is still excellent.
6. Cool 5 minutes, then peel the skin. Mash the flesh with a fork; chop the grilled garlic and chillies.
7. Make the masala: heat mustard oil; sauté ginger and garlic (30 s), add onion until golden (6–7 min), add tomatoes and salt until soft, add chilli powder, add mashed eggplant, sauté 7–8 minutes.
8. Finish with garam masala if using; garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (smoky Indian mash).

### 3. Claudia Roden — Patlıcan Kizartması (Turkish / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 30 min salting) · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 large or 4 medium eggplants (≈ 700 g total), sliced into 1.5 cm rounds
- 10 g (2 tsp) fine salt for drawing
- 60 ml (4 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil, for brushing
- 4 garlic cloves, finely sliced
- 400 g ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped (or 1 × 400 g can chopped tomatoes)
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1 tsp dried oregano or fresh thyme
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- Fine sea salt and black pepper, to taste
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to finish
- 30 g crumbled feta or white cheese (beyaz peynir), to finish

**Method**
1. Lay eggplant rounds in a single layer on a tray, salt both sides generously. Stand for 30 minutes; press out the liquid and pat very dry with paper towels.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Line two sheet pans with baking parchment.
3. Brush each eggplant round on both sides with olive oil; lay on the sheet pans in a single layer.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one of the central rounds, with the tip at the geometric centre of the slice.
5. Roast 15 minutes on the first side; turn each round and roast a further 10–12 minutes until golden and tender on both sides.
6. Meanwhile, gently fry the garlic in 1 tbsp olive oil 30 seconds; add tomatoes, paprika, oregano, cinnamon, salt and pepper; simmer 10 minutes until saucy.
7. Arrange roasted eggplant rounds on a platter; spoon the tomato sauce over; scatter parsley and cheese. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F)** — rounds should be golden, soft and yielding. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (silky, spiced, aromatic).

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Melanzane al Forno con Aglio e Prezzemolo (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min salting) · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant (2 medium), trimmed, sliced into 1 cm rounds
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt for purging
- 80 ml (5 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated or passed through a press
- 15 g (3 tbsp) flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to finish
- Optional: 60 g Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated, for a gratin finish

**Method**
1. Layer the rounds in a colander, salting each layer; weigh down with a plate. Drain 30 minutes; press out moisture and pat very dry.
2. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Line a heavy sheet pan with parchment and brush generously with some of the olive oil.
3. Lay the eggplant rounds on the pan in a single layer. Brush the tops with the remaining olive oil.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one of the central rounds, tip at the geometric centre.
5. Roast 12–15 minutes until the underside is deep golden brown. Flip each round; roast a further 8–10 minutes until both faces are golden.
6. Mix the grated garlic with the parsley and a pinch of salt. In the last 2 minutes of roasting, scatter the garlic-parsley mixture over the rounds and return to the oven just to warm (not to brown further — Hazan is emphatic that the garlic must stay raw-gold, not darkened).
7. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).** Hazan's standard for *melanzane al forno* is "yielding and caramelised at the faces, never collapsing." Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (golden, olive oil–glossed, garlic-parsley bright).
