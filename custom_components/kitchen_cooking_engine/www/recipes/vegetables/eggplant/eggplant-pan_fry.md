# Eggplant × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Eggplant (*Solanum melongena*; UK aubergine) is a sponge-textured fruit of the nightshade family, structurally a cluster of air-filled cells around a seed-bearing core. Raw, those cells absorb fat aggressively; cooked, the cell walls collapse and the texture turns silky and almost custard-like. Pan-frying in slices or batons concentrates the cooking on the cut surfaces — at 180–200 °C in 5–8 mm of oil, those surfaces caramelise to deep mahogany while the centre transitions from chalky to tender. The classic salt-and-press preparation draws out enough water (and, in older heritage varieties, bitterness) so that the slices brown rather than steam. Carryover is small (1–2 °C) but the slices continue to drink up surface oil after removal — a wire rack drain is standard.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Melanzane Saltate (Italian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Knopf, 1992)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min salting) · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant (2 medium), trimmed and sliced into 1 cm rounds
- 15 g (1 tbsp) fine sea salt for purging
- 120 ml (½ cup) extra-virgin olive oil for frying (more as needed)
- 3 garlic cloves, peeled, lightly crushed
- 15 g chopped flat-leaf parsley
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt, to finish
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper, to finish

**Method**
1. Layer the eggplant rounds in a colander, salting each layer; place a plate on top weighed down with a tin. Drain 30 minutes. Press out moisture and pat very dry.
2. Heat olive oil in a wide heavy frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the crushed garlic; allow it to perfume the oil for 1 minute, then remove.
3. Lay the eggplant rounds in a single layer (work in batches). Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, with the tip in the geometric centre of the slice (the probe will lie almost flat on the pan — choose the slice with sufficient thickness).
4. Fry undisturbed 2–3 minutes per side until each face is deep golden brown.
5. Continue rotating slices for an additional 2–3 minutes if needed; do not press the slices down — Hazan warns that pressing crushes the cell structure and produces leathery rather than silky eggplant.
6. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Hazan describes correct doneness as "creamy at the centre but cohesive in the slice — never mush."
7. Lift out onto a wire rack to drain. Toss with parsley, sea salt and pepper. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (silken, deeply caramelised).

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Pan-Fried Aubergine with Buttermilk (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 20 min salting) · **Cook**: 15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant, sliced lengthwise into 8 thick (1.5 cm) wedges
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt for purging
- 90 ml (6 tbsp) olive oil for frying
- 5 g (1 tsp) za'atar
- 2 g (½ tsp) sumac
- 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
- 100 g (½ cup) thick Greek-style buttermilk or labneh, to serve
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish
- 5 g chopped fresh mint, to finish

**Method**
1. Salt the eggplant wedges lightly and let drain on a rack 20 minutes. Pat very dry.
2. Heat olive oil in a wide heavy frying pan over medium-high heat until shimmering.
3. Lay eggplant wedges cut-side-down in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the central wedge, tip at its geometric centre.
4. Fry 4–5 minutes on the first cut face until deeply golden. Turn to the second cut face; fry 3–4 minutes. Turn to the skin side; fry another 3 minutes.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Ottolenghi targets a melting interior that holds wedge shape — slightly cooked-through-er than Hazan's rounds because the wedges are thicker.
6. Drain on a wire rack. Whisk garlic into the buttermilk with a pinch of salt.
7. Pile wedges on a platter, spoon over buttermilk, dust with za'atar and sumac, scatter pomegranate and mint. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (creamy, tangy, bright).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Begun Bhaja (Bengali tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *A Taste of India* (Pavilion Books, 1985)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant (1 large globe or 2 long Asian), cut into 1 cm rounds (globe) or 5 cm batons (Asian)
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground turmeric
- 5 g (1 tsp) Kashmiri red chilli powder
- 5 g (1 tsp) fine sea salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) caster sugar (Bengali signature — balances the chilli)
- 60 ml (¼ cup) mustard oil (heat to smoking, then cool, before frying — this mellows the pungency)
- 15 g (1 tbsp) chickpea flour (besan), for light dusting

**Method**
1. Mix turmeric, chilli powder, salt and sugar. Rub onto both sides of each eggplant slice. Rest 10 minutes; the salt will draw out a small amount of moisture and the spices will adhere.
2. Lightly dust each slice with chickpea flour, tapping off excess.
3. Heat the mustard oil in a wide heavy frying pan or karahi over medium-high until shimmering (≈ 180 °C).
4. Lay slices in the pan in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at its geometric centre.
5. Fry 2–3 minutes on the first side until deep red-brown (the turmeric darkens dramatically). Flip; fry another 2–3 minutes.
6. **Pull at 80–85 °C (176–185 °F).** Jaffrey's *bhaja* style is fully cooked-through with a crisp surface — the chickpea flour dusting helps achieve this.
7. Lift onto absorbent paper or a wire rack to drain. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (crisp surface, soft creamy interior, spice-bright).

### 4. Diana Henry — Pan-Fried Aubergine with Honey & Sesame (Andalusian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015) and her *Telegraph* recipe column at telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min cold-milk soak) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant, sliced into 8 mm rounds
- 250 ml (1 cup) cold milk for soaking
- 60 g (½ cup) plain flour for dusting
- 60 ml (¼ cup) light olive oil for frying
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cold-pressed olive oil
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) raw honey or runny dark honey, to finish
- 10 g (1 tbsp) toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the rounds in the cold milk for 30 minutes — the Andalusian *berenjenas con miel* technique that draws out bitterness and tenderises the slices. Drain and pat very dry.
2. Dust each slice with flour, tapping off excess.
3. Heat the frying olive oil in a heavy pan over medium-high until shimmering.
4. Lay slices in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at its geometric centre.
5. Fry 2 minutes on the first side until pale gold; flip. Fry another 2 minutes; flip again to crisp the first side a final time.
6. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Henry's Andalusian-tapas style emphasises a thin crisp shell and a creamy interior; she warns that overcooking turns the slice greasy.
7. Lift onto absorbent paper. Drizzle with honey while still hot, scatter with sesame and sea salt. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (crisp, creamy, sweet-savoury).
