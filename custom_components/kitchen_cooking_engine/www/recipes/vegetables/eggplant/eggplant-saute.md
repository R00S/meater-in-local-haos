# Eggplant × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Eggplant (*Solanum melongena*) sautéed in a hot, well-oiled pan produces intensely browned, deeply flavourful pieces in a fraction of the time needed for oven roasting. The dense sponge of the raw flesh first soaks up fat before the surface temperature rises high enough to begin Maillard browning — the secret to even browning without greasiness is a fiercely hot pan and enough oil, so the surface seals before absorption becomes saturating. Slices, cubes, or batons of 1–1.5 cm thickness are ideal; thicker pieces risk underdone centres by the time the crust is dark enough. The internal temperature for fully sautéed eggplant is 78–85 °C (172–185 °F) — at this point the interior is creamy-silky yet the piece holds its shape. Carryover is small (1–2 °C) and the pieces continue to drink residual oil off the heat, making a wire rack the best draining surface.

## Styckesprofil

Aubergine (*Solanum melongena*) sauterad i en het, väl-oljad panna ger intensivt brynnade, djupt smakrika bitar på bråkdelen av den tid som krävs för ugnsstekning. Sautera i 8–10 minuter vid hög värme. Klar när djupt gyllenbrun och mjuk.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Yú Xiāng Qiézi (魚香茄子 — Chinese Fish-Fragrant Eggplant)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Chinese eggplants (long, slender, purple), cut into 4 cm × 1.5 cm batons
- 4 tbsp neutral oil (peanut or sunflower) for frying
- 2 spring onions, white and green separated, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- 3 garlic cloves, finely minced
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tbsp Chinkiang black vinegar (镇江香醋)
- 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp Pixian doubanjiang (郫县豆瓣酱, chilli-bean paste)
- 2 tsp potato starch dissolved in 2 tbsp cold water
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat the eggplant batons dry with paper towels. Combine soy sauce, vinegar, sugar and a pinch of salt in a small bowl (the sauce).
2. Heat a wok or large frying pan over very high heat until smoking. Add the oil; when it shimmers, add the eggplant in a single layer. Do not stir for the first 2 minutes — allow one face to sear.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through one end of a central baton, with the tip near the geometric centre.
4. Toss the batons; continue sautéing 3–4 minutes more, tossing frequently, until golden-brown on multiple faces and the flesh feels soft when pressed.
5. Push the eggplant to the edges; add the white spring onion, ginger, garlic and doubanjiang to the centre of the wok. Stir-fry the aromatics 20–30 seconds in the hot oil until fragrant.
6. Toss everything together; add the sauce and stir. Add the potato starch mixture; toss until the sauce thickens and glazes the eggplant, about 30 seconds.
7. **Pull at 80–85 °C (176–185 °F)** — Dunlop notes the batons should be glossy, completely silky, and slightly collapsing at the edges. Off the heat, drizzle sesame oil and scatter the green spring onion. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (glossy, spicy-sweet-sour, deeply umami).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Masaledar Baingan (Indian / North Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant, cut into 2 cm cubes (globe eggplant, skin on)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 60 ml (4 tbsp) vegetable oil or mustard oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander (dhania)
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder (or sweet paprika + pinch cayenne)
- 2 medium ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
- 1 tsp garam masala, to finish
- 15 g fresh coriander (cilantro), chopped

**Method**
1. Salt the eggplant cubes; toss and leave 15 minutes in a colander. Press out moisture and pat dry.
2. Heat oil in a wide, heavy sauté pan or karahi over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add cumin seeds; sizzle 10 seconds until they sputter and turn a shade darker.
3. Add onion; sauté 5–6 minutes until deep golden.
4. Add garlic and ginger; cook 1 minute. Add turmeric, coriander powder and chilli powder; stir into the onion mixture for 30 seconds.
5. Add tomatoes; cook over medium heat 3–4 minutes until they break down into a masala.
6. Add the eggplant cubes; toss to coat. Insert the MEATER probe into one of the larger cubes in the centre of the pan, with the tip at its geometric centre.
7. Sauté over medium heat 8–10 minutes, turning frequently, until the eggplant is completely tender and the masala clings to the pieces.
8. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Jaffrey's style aims for cubes that have absorbed the masala flavours deeply but still hold their shape. Off the heat, dust with garam masala and scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, soft, fragrant).

### 3. Diana Henry — Berenjenas con Miel (Spanish / Andalusian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Diana Henry, *A Change of Appetite* (Mitchell Beazley, 2014) and her Telegraph recipe column; https://www.telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink (column archived)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant, sliced into 8 mm rounds
- 250 ml (1 cup) cold whole milk, for soaking
- 60 g (½ cup) plain flour
- 5 g (1 tsp) fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (4 tbsp) light olive oil or sunflower oil
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) raw, runny honey, to finish
- 10 g toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- Flaked sea salt, to finish
- ½ lemon, for squeezing

**Method**
1. Soak the eggplant rounds in cold milk for 30 minutes (the traditional Andalusian *berenjenas en leche* step that draws out bitterness and tenderises the flesh).
2. Drain; pat each round very dry with paper towels. Season the flour with salt and pepper. Dust each round in the seasoned flour; shake off the excess.
3. Heat the oil in a wide, heavy frying pan over medium-high heat until shimmering (≈ 180 °C).
4. Lay rounds in the pan in a single uncrowded layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one of the central rounds, with the tip at its geometric centre.
5. Fry 2–3 minutes on the first side until pale gold and lightly crusted. Flip; fry 2–3 minutes more. Flip once more to crisp the first side a final time. Work in batches; do not crowd.
6. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F)** — Henry's Andalusian style emphasises a thin crisp shell with a creamy interior; she notes that overcooking makes the rounds greasy rather than golden.
7. Lift onto a wire rack briefly. Drizzle with honey while still hot; scatter sesame seeds and flaked sea salt; squeeze lemon over. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (crisp-coated, sweet-savoury, lemon-bright).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Aubergine with Buttermilk Sauce (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 20 min salting) · **Cook**: 15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g eggplant, sliced lengthwise into 8 thick wedges (1.5 cm)
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt for purging
- 90 ml (6 tbsp) olive oil, for frying
- 5 g (1 tsp) za'atar
- 2 g (½ tsp) sumac
- 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
- 100 g Greek-style buttermilk or thick labneh, to serve
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish
- 5 g fresh mint leaves, to finish

**Method**
1. Salt the eggplant wedges lightly; drain on a rack 20 minutes. Pat very dry.
2. Heat oil in a wide, heavy frying pan over medium-high heat until shimmering.
3. Lay wedges cut-side-down in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the central wedge with the tip at its geometric centre.
4. Fry undisturbed 4–5 minutes until the first cut face is deeply golden. Turn to the second cut face; fry 3–4 minutes. Turn to the skin side; fry 2–3 minutes.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Ottolenghi targets a melting interior that still holds wedge shape.
6. Drain on a wire rack. Whisk garlic into the buttermilk with a pinch of salt.
7. Pile wedges on a platter; spoon over buttermilk; dust with za'atar and sumac; scatter pomegranate seeds and mint. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (creamy, tangy, herb-fragrant).
