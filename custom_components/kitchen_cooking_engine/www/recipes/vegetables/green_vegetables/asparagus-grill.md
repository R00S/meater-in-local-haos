# Asparagus × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Asparagus (*Asparagus officinalis*) on the grill develops an ideal contrast between the charred, slightly bitter tips and the tender, sweet stems. The spears should be thick enough (≥ 1 cm at the base) to survive direct grill heat without falling through the grates; if thinner, use a grill basket or skewer the spears in bundles. Grill time: 6–10 minutes, depending on thickness, at medium-high to high heat, turning once or twice. Pull temperature: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) at the thickest part of the stem for tender-crisp asparagus with char-marked tips. A MEATER probe is inserted along the long axis of the largest, thickest spear from the cut base end, with the tip at the mid-point of the spear.

## Styckesprofil

Sparris (*Asparagus officinalis*) på grillen utvecklar en idealisk kontrast mellan de förkolnade, lätt bittra topparna och de möra, söta stjälkarna. Grilla oljad sparris direkt på gallret vid hög värme i 4–6 minuter med vändning. Klar när lätt förkolnad med synliga grillränder.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Asparagus Yakimono (Japanese grilled asparagus)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thick green asparagus, woody ends snapped off
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (usukuchi-shoyu)
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 1 tsp sesame oil (finishing drizzle)

**Method**
1. Mix sesame oil, soy sauce, mirin and sugar. Toss asparagus spears in half the marinade; reserve the rest.
2. Preheat grill to medium-high. Place asparagus perpendicular to grill grates.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the long axis of the thickest spear from the cut end, tip at the mid-point.
4. Grill 3–4 minutes, turning once, until char-marked and tender. Brush with remaining marinade; grill 1–2 more minutes until the glaze caramelises.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds; drizzle finishing sesame oil. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, sweet-savoury, sesame-fragrant).

### 2. José Andrés — Espárragos a la Parrilla con Romesco (Spanish tradition)
**Inspired by**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thick asparagus, woody ends removed
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (Spanish)
- Flor de sal (flaked sea salt)
- *Quick romesco:* 60 g roasted red peppers (jar); 30 g toasted blanched almonds; 1 garlic clove; 1 tbsp sherry vinegar; 1 tbsp smoked paprika; 2 tbsp olive oil; salt

**Method**
1. Brush asparagus with olive oil. Preheat grill to high.
2. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest spear from cut end.
3. Grill 6–8 minutes, turning twice, until charred and tender.
4. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Blend romesco ingredients until coarse. Serve asparagus with romesco. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, nutty-smoky romesco).

---

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled Asparagus with Poached Egg and Truffle Oil (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thick asparagus, woody ends removed
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 4 poached eggs, to serve
- 30 g Parmesan shavings
- 1 tsp white truffle oil (or good-quality extra-virgin olive oil)
- 15 g fresh tarragon or flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Brush asparagus with olive oil; season with salt.
2. Grill over high direct heat 6–8 minutes, turning once.
3. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest spear.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Arrange on plates; top each with a poached egg; scatter Parmesan; drizzle truffle oil; scatter herbs. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (charred, rich with egg, elegant).

---

### 4. Deborah Madison — Grilled Asparagus with Meyer Lemon Gremolata (American vegetarian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thick asparagus, woody ends removed
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Sea salt
- *Gremolata:* zest of 1 Meyer lemon (or regular lemon); 2 garlic cloves (finely minced); 15 g flat-leaf parsley (finely chopped)

**Method**
1. Brush asparagus with olive oil; season.
2. Grill over high heat 6–8 min, turning once.
3. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest spear.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Mix gremolata; scatter over hot asparagus. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (charred, lemon-bright, garlic-herb fragrant).
