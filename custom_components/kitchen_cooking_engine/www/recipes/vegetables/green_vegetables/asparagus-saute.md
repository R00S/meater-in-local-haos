# Asparagus × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Asparagus (*Asparagus officinalis*) sautéed in a hot pan is the fastest preparation: 4–6 minutes over medium-high to high heat, producing tender-crisp spears with a lightly browned stem and vivid green colour. The pan must be large enough to hold all spears in a single layer, and the oil or butter must be hot before the asparagus goes in — otherwise the spears will steam rather than sauté. Pull temperature: 78–83 °C (172–181 °F) for tender-crisp asparagus, still with some bite. MEATER probe inserted along the long axis of the thickest spear.

## Styckesprofil

Sparris (*Asparagus officinalis*) sauterad i het panna är den snabbaste beredningen: 4–6 minuter vid medel-hög till hög värme, vilket ger tender-crispy sparrisar med lätt brynnad yta. Klar när knäcks rent vid böjning och lätt gyllenbrun.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Qīng Chǎo Lǜ Sǔn (清炒芦笋 — Chinese Stir-Fried Asparagus)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thin–medium asparagus, woody ends snapped off, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat asparagus dry. Heat wok over maximum heat until smoking; add oil.
2. Add asparagus; insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest piece.
3. Stir-fry 2–3 minutes over maximum heat. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry 30 seconds. Add soy sauce and sugar; toss 30 seconds.
4. **Pull at 80–83 °C (176–181 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion. Final serving temperature: **80–83 °C / 176–181 °F** (tender-crisp, soy-ginger fragrant, vivid green).

### 2. Julia Child — Asperges Sautées au Beurre (French tradition)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, *The French Chef Cookbook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1968)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g medium asparagus, woody ends snapped off
- 40 g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 2 g freshly ground white pepper
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley or tarragon, chopped

**Method**
1. Melt butter in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high heat. When foam subsides, add asparagus in a single layer.
2. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest spear.
3. Sauté 5–7 minutes, turning spears every 2 minutes, until lightly browned and tender-crisp.
4. Season with salt and white pepper; add lemon juice; toss. Scatter herbs.
5. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Child: "The asparagus should be a brilliant green — overcooked asparagus turns army-drab and is unacceptable." Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (butter-bright, lemon-fragrant, vivid green).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Sabzi Til Asparagus (Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g medium asparagus, cut into 4 cm pieces
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole mustard seeds
- ½ tsp turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds (til), to finish
- Lemon juice, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide pan over medium-high. Add mustard seeds (pop 10 s); add asparagus, turmeric and chilli; toss.
2. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest piece.
3. Sauté 4–5 min, tossing every 90 seconds. Season with salt.
4. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds; squeeze lemon. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (spiced, tender-crisp, nutty sesame).

---

### 4. Harumi Kurihara — Asupara Bataa Itame (Japanese Butter-Sautéed Asparagus)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g medium–thick asparagus, woody ends snapped
- 20 g (1½ tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sake or mirin
- A few drops of sesame oil, to finish
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat butter and oil in a wide pan over medium-high. Add asparagus.
2. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest spear.
3. Sauté 4–5 min, turning once. Add soy sauce and sake; toss 30 seconds until glaze caramelises.
4. **Pull at 80–83 °C (176–181 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil; scatter sesame seeds. Final serving temperature: **80–83 °C / 176–181 °F** (butter-glossed, soy-sweet, Japanese).
