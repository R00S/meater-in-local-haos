# Asparagus × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Asparagus (*Asparagus officinalis*) steamed is the most flavour-preserving method: spears rest in a steamer basket above boiling water for 4–8 minutes (depending on thickness), emerging bright green, tender-crisp, and with their delicate grassy-sweet flavour intact. Unlike boiling, the water-soluble sugars and vitamins are not lost. The steamed asparagus is then dressed with various sauces from butter and lemon (French) to sesame and soy (Chinese/Japanese) to hollandaise (classic European). Pull temperature: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F) for tender-crisp; 82–85 °C (180–185 °F) for fully tender. MEATER probe inserted along the long axis of the thickest spear from the cut end, tip at mid-point.

## Source recipes

### 1. Julia Child — Asperges à la Vapeur, Sauce Hollandaise (French tradition)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thick white or green asparagus, woody ends peeled (Child specifies peeling the lower two-thirds of the stalk for even cooking)
- Water for steamer
- *Sauce Hollandaise:* 3 egg yolks; 2 tbsp cold water; 200 g clarified butter, warm; 1–2 tbsp lemon juice; salt; white pepper
- Fine sea salt

**Method**
1. Set up a steamer basket over boiling water. Stand the asparagus upright (tips up) if possible, or lay flat.
2. Insert the MEATER probe along the long axis of the thickest spear from the cut end.
3. Steam 6–10 minutes depending on thickness. Child's test: "A spear should bend slightly but not droop, when you lift it horizontally with tongs."
4. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F)** for tender-crisp; pull at **82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** for fully tender.
5. Arrange on warm plates; spoon hollandaise alongside. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (bright green, tender-crisp, with rich hollandaise — the classic French preparation).

### 2. Fuchsia Dunlop — Steamed Asparagus with Sichuan Spicy Sesame Dressing (Chinese tradition)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thin–medium asparagus, woody ends snapped off
- *Spicy sesame dressing:* 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste (or tahini); 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp black rice vinegar; 1 tsp chilli oil (红油, adjustable); 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 garlic clove grated; 1–2 tbsp cold water to thin
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Steam asparagus over boiling water 5–7 minutes. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest spear.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Arrange on a platter; drizzle dressing; scatter spring onion and sesame seeds.
3. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** or cool to room temperature (the dressing works on warm or cool asparagus — Dunlop notes this works beautifully as a cold preparation too).

---

### 3. Shizuo Tsuji — Steamered Asparagus with Wafu Dressing (Japanese tradition)
**Inspired by**: Shizuo Tsuji, *Japanese Cooking: A Simple Art* (Kodansha International, 1980)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g medium asparagus, woody ends snapped off
- *Wafu dressing:* 3 tbsp ponzu sauce (or 2 tbsp soy + 1 tbsp lemon juice); 1 tsp sesame oil; 1 tsp mirin; pinch of caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds; shredded nori, to finish

**Method**
1. Steam asparagus 5–7 minutes. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Whisk wafu dressing; drizzle over.
3. Scatter sesame seeds and nori. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (tender-crisp, citrus-soy, Japanese).

---

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Asparagi Conditi con Parmigiano (Italian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g thick asparagus, woody ends peeled
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g Parmigiano-Reggiano, freshly grated
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tsp fresh lemon juice

**Method**
1. Steam asparagus 6–8 minutes. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Arrange on a platter; drizzle olive oil; scatter Parmesan; season with salt, pepper and lemon juice.
3. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (tender, Parmesan-bright, olive oil-fragrant, Italian).
