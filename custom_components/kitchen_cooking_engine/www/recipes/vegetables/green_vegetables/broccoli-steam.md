# Broccoli × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Broccoli (*Brassica oleracea* var. *italica*) steamed retains its vivid green colour and the glucosinolate compounds better than boiling. Steam for 6–8 minutes (florets); 8–10 minutes (large crowns). Pull temperature: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F) for tender-crisp; 82–85 °C (180–185 °F) for fully tender. The classic finishing step — butter and lemon (French), sesame dressing (Japanese), tadka (Indian), or olive oil and Parmesan (Italian) — converts the plainly steamed vegetable to a finished dish.

## Styckesprofil

Broccoli (*Brassica oleracea* var. *italica*) ångad bevarar sin livliga gröna färg och glukosinolat-föreningarna bättre än kokning. Ånga i 6–8 minuter (buketter). Klar när genomstekt men fortfarande krispig och klargrön.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steamed Broccoli with Goma-ae Dressing (inspired by Harumi Kurihara)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 7–8 min · **Rest**: 3 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets
- *Goma-ae:* 3 tbsp toasted white sesame seeds; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tsp sugar; 1 tsp sesame oil

**Method**
1. Steam florets 7–8 min. Insert MEATER probe through largest floret stem.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Allow to cool slightly.
3. Grind sesame seeds; mix with soy, mirin, sugar, sesame oil. Toss broccoli in dressing. Final serving: **room temperature to slightly warm** (classic Japanese goma-ae side).

### 2. Steamed Broccoli with Spicy Sesame Sauce (inspired by Fuchsia Dunlop)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 7–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets
- *Sauce:* 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste (or tahini); 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp black rice vinegar; 1 tsp chilli oil; 1 tsp sugar; 1 garlic clove grated; 2 tbsp cold water; spring onion; sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Steam florets 7–8 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Drizzle sauce over; scatter spring onion and sesame seeds. Final serving: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (tender, nutty-spicy, Chinese).

---

### 3. Brocoli Vapeur au Beurre (inspired by Julia Child)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, *The French Chef Cookbook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1968)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets; 40 g butter; ½ tsp salt; white pepper; 1 tbsp lemon juice; parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Steam florets 6–8 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Melt butter to noisette; add lemon. Toss broccoli in butter; season; scatter parsley. Final serving: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (vivid green, buttery, lemon-fragrant, French).

---

### 4. Steamed Broccoli with Tadka (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 7–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets
- *Tadka:* 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp mustard seeds; 10 curry leaves; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp salt; lemon juice; coriander

**Method**
1. Steam florets 7–8 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Heat oil for tadka; mustard (pop); curry leaves (5 s); turmeric and chilli (10 s). Pour over florets; add salt and lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (spiced, bright, South Indian).
