# Brussels Sprouts × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Brussels sprouts (*Brassica oleracea* var. *gemmifera*) in the air fryer become intensely caramelised and golden-charred on the cut faces in 12–16 minutes at 190–200 °C — faster than a conventional oven, with crispier outer leaves. They should be halved through the core (the flat face caramelises maximally), very dry, and generously oiled. Pull temperature: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) for charred-golden outer leaves with a tender, sweet interior. MEATER probe inserted through the outer leaves of the largest sprout into its core.

## Styckesprofil

Brysselkål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *gemmifera*) i airfryern blir intensivt karameliserad och gyllenbrun på snittytan på 12–16 minuter vid 190–200 °C. Halvera, olja, krydda. Klar när snittytan är djupt karameliserad och ytterbladen är knapriga.

## Source recipes

### 1. Korean Air-Fried Brussels Sprouts (inspired by Maangchi)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); Korean spicy technique applied to Brussels sprouts.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved; 2 tbsp neutral oil; salt
- *Korean sauce:* 2 tbsp gochujang; 1 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp honey; 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 garlic clove minced; 1 tsp rice vinegar
- Toasted sesame seeds and spring onion, to finish

**Method**
1. Toss halved sprouts with oil and a pinch of salt; place cut-face-down in air fryer basket.
2. Insert MEATER probe through outer leaves of the largest sprout into its core.
3. Air fry at **200 °C for 10 min**; shake; air fry 2–3 more minutes.
4. Remove; toss in Korean sauce; return at 190 °C for 2–3 min.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, spicy-sweet, Korean).

### 2. Air-Fried Masala Brussels Sprouts (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); spice marinade adapted for Brussels sprouts in the air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 15 min marinating · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp garam masala; ½ tsp salt; 1 tbsp lemon juice; fresh coriander to finish

**Method**
1. Mix oil and spices; toss halved sprouts; marinate 15 min.
2. Place cut-face-down in air fryer. Insert MEATER probe.
3. Air fry at **200 °C for 14–16 min**, shaking once.
4. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (spiced, charred, tangy).

---

### 3. Air-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Za'atar (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt; 1 tbsp za'atar; 30 g pomegranate seeds; 10 g flat-leaf parsley; 1 tbsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Toss halved sprouts with olive oil and salt; place cut-face-down in air fryer. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 14–16 min**, shaking once.
3. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Toss with za'atar, lemon; scatter pomegranate and parsley. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (golden, za'atar-bright).

---

### 4. Air-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic (inspired by Deborah Madison)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 2 tbsp olive oil; salt and pepper; 2 tbsp aged balsamic; 30 g toasted walnuts; 15 g fresh flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Toss halved sprouts with olive oil; season. Place cut-face-down. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 14–16 min**, shaking once.
3. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Drizzle balsamic; scatter walnuts and parsley. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, balsamic-sweet, walnut-rich).
