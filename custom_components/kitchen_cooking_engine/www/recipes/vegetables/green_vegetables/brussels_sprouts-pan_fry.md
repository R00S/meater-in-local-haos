# Brussels Sprouts × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Brussels sprouts (*Brassica oleracea* var. *gemmifera*) pan-fried (halved, cut-face-down in a hot pan) is the fastest technique for intensely caramelised results: 8–12 minutes in a cast-iron or heavy pan achieves charred cut faces and a tender interior. Unlike sautéing (which involves frequent tossing), pan-frying holds the cut face in contact with the hot surface for an extended sear. Pull temperature: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F). MEATER probe through outer leaves of the largest sprout into the core.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Cavolini di Bruxelles in Padella (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved; 40 g pancetta or guanciale, diced (optional — Hazan's standard technique uses pancetta for flavour); 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper; 1 tbsp lemon juice; parsley

**Method**
1. Heat a large cast-iron or heavy pan over medium-high. Add olive oil and pancetta; cook 2 minutes until pancetta begins to crisp.
2. Add halved sprouts cut-face-down; do NOT stir. Cook 5–6 minutes undisturbed.
3. Insert MEATER probe through outer leaves of the largest sprout into its core.
4. Flip; add garlic; cook 3–4 more minutes until the second face is golden.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Season with salt, pepper, lemon juice; scatter parsley. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred-caramelised cut face, pancetta-rich, Italian).

### 2. Fuchsia Dunlop — Pan-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Doubanjiang (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012); dry-fry technique adapted for Brussels sprouts.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 3 tbsp neutral oil; 1 tbsp Pixian doubanjiang; 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp sugar; ½ tsp sesame oil

**Method**
1. Heat a wide, heavy pan over high heat. Add oil; when smoking, place sprouts cut-face-down; do not stir.
2. Insert MEATER probe; cook 5–6 min undisturbed until charred.
3. Add doubanjiang and garlic; toss; cook 2–3 more minutes. Add soy sauce and sugar; toss.
4. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, spicy-umami, Chinese).

---

### 3. Maangchi — Korean Pan-Fried Brussels Sprouts (Korean tradition)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 2 tbsp sesame oil; *sauce:* 2 tbsp gochujang; 1 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp honey; 1 tsp sesame oil; 1 garlic clove minced; sesame seeds and spring onion to finish

**Method**
1. Heat sesame oil in a wide pan over medium-high. Place sprouts cut-face-down; cook undisturbed 5–6 min.
2. Insert MEATER probe; flip; cook 3–4 min. Add sauce; toss 1 min.
3. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, gochujang-sweet, Korean).

---

### 4. Deborah Madison — Pan-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Butter and Sage (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 30 g butter; 1 tbsp olive oil; 6 fresh sage leaves; ½ tsp salt; black pepper; 30 g toasted pine nuts; lemon juice

**Method**
1. Heat butter and oil in a wide pan over medium-high until foaming. Place sprouts cut-face-down; cook undisturbed 5–6 min.
2. Insert MEATER probe; add sage leaves; cook 3–4 more min until golden.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Season; scatter pine nuts; squeeze lemon. Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (charred, sage-fragrant, pine-nut-rich).
