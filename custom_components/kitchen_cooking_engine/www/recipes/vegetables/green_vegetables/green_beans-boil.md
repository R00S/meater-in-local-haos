# Green Beans × Boil — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Green beans (*Phaseolus vulgaris* and *Vigna unguiculata* — runner beans, French haricots verts, yard-long beans) boiled is the classic preparation in French haute cuisine, American home cooking, and the base technique in almost every global kitchen that uses fresh green beans. The target for boiled green beans is bright-green and tender-crisp: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F) inside the bean. Overcooked beans turn army-green, drab and mushy at 88+ °C. The classic French technique is to boil rapidly in a large quantity of salted water (blanching) for 3–5 minutes, then shock in ice water (*refresh*) to stop cooking and fix the bright green. MEATER probe: insert horizontally through the side of the longest/thickest bean at the mid-point; the probe must be in the water and not reading ambient air.

## Styckesprofil

Gröna bönor (*Phaseolus vulgaris* och *Vigna unguiculata* — brytbönor, franska haricots verts, långa bönor) kokade är den klassiska beredningen i fransk haute cuisine, amerikansk husmanskost och asiatisk matlagning. Koka i saltat vatten i 3–6 minuter. Klar när genomstekta men fortfarande krispiga.

## Source recipes

### 1. Haricots Verts Blanchis et Étuvés (inspired by Julia Child)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min boiling + 3–5 min final toss · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g French haricots verts (fine beans), topped and tailed
- 3–4 L water (Child: "large quantity — dilutes the acids that dull the green colour")
- 2 tsp fine sea salt (for blanching water)
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- Fine salt and freshly ground white pepper

**Method**
1. Bring the large pot of salted water to a vigorous rolling boil.
2. Add beans all at once. Insert MEATER probe through the side of the longest bean mid-point.
3. Boil uncovered at maximum heat 5–7 minutes (haricots verts; 4–5 for very thin; 6–7 for thicker runner beans). "The beans are done when they are tender but still bite with a tiny resistance — *al dente* in the French vegetable sense," says Child.
4. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Drain immediately; shock in ice water for 30 seconds (if serving later). If serving immediately: drain, toss in the pan with butter over medium heat for 2 minutes, season.
5. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (vivid green, tender-crisp, butter-glossed).

### 2. Blanched Yard-Long Beans with Chilli Oil (inspired by Fuchsia Dunlop)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 2 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 500 g yard-long beans (*dòu jiǎo*, 豆角), cut into 5 cm pieces (or regular green beans)
- Boiling salted water
- *Dressing:* 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp black rice vinegar; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 tsp chilli oil; 1 garlic clove grated; ½ tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds; 1 spring onion, sliced

**Method**
1. Blanch beans in a large pot of boiling salted water for 4–5 minutes.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the side of the thickest piece.
3. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Drain; spread on a plate to cool briefly.
4. Whisk dressing; pour over cooled beans; toss. Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **slightly warm to room temperature** — Dunlop notes this is excellent as a room-temperature dish.

### 3. Green Beans Boiled with Tomato (inspired by Claudia Roden)
**Inspired by**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 ripe tomatoes, finely diced
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley or coriander, chopped

**Method**
1. Place all ingredients except lemon juice and herbs in a medium pan. Add 125 ml water; bring to a simmer.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the thickest bean.
3. Simmer partially covered 15–18 minutes until beans are fully tender (not crisp) and the sauce has reduced.
4. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F)** — this preparation targets fully cooked, soft beans as is typical in North African cuisine. Finish with lemon juice and herbs. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (soft, spiced, tomato-bright).

---

### 4. Masala Boiled Beans (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min boiling + quick tempering · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed, cut into 4 cm pieces
- Boiling salted water
- *Tadka:* 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp whole mustard seeds; 10 fresh curry leaves; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli; salt
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice; coriander to garnish

**Method**
1. Boil beans in salted water 5–7 minutes. Insert MEATER probe through thickest piece.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Drain.
3. Heat oil for tadka; add mustard (pop); curry leaves (5 s); add beans, turmeric, coriander, chilli, salt; toss 1 min. Add lemon juice; garnish.
4. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (spiced, bright, fragrant).
