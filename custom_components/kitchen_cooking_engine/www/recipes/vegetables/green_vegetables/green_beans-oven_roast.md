# Green Beans × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Green beans (*Phaseolus vulgaris*) oven-roasted at 200–220 °C caramelise and blister in 18–22 minutes, producing a concentrated, slightly charred flavour completely different from boiled or steamed beans. The dry oven heat drives off surface moisture quickly, allowing Maillard browning to develop on the bean skins. The beans should be spread in a single layer with space between them — crowding causes steaming, not roasting. Pull temperature: 82–86 °C (180–187 °F) for tender-crisp roasted beans with blistered skin. MEATER probe inserted horizontally through the thickest bean at the mid-point.

## Styckesprofil

Gröna bönor (*Phaseolus vulgaris*) ugnsrostade vid 200–220 °C karameliseras och blåsar på 18–22 minuter, och ger en koncentrerad, lätt bränd smak helt annorlunda från kokning. Olja generöst, ett lager. Klar när mörkgyllenbrun med brynnade fläckar.

## Source recipes

### 1. Roasted Green Beans with Crispy Capers (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 2 tbsp capers, rinsed and dried
- 1 lemon (zest and juice)
- 30 g toasted pine nuts
- 15 g fresh basil or flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss beans with 2 tbsp oil and salt; spread on sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe horizontally through the thickest bean.
3. Roast 18–20 minutes until blistered and starting to brown. In the last 3 minutes, scatter capers over the beans and return to oven until capers crisp.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Drizzle remaining olive oil and lemon juice; scatter zest, pine nuts and herbs. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (blistered, caper-crisp, lemon-bright).

### 2. Roasted Masala Green Beans (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp fine salt
- Lemon juice and fresh coriander, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss beans with oil and spices; spread on sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe through thickest bean.
3. Roast 20–22 minutes, shaking once, until blistered and golden.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, blistered, golden).

---

### 3. Fagiolini al Forno con Aglio (inspired by Marcella Hazan)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Optional: 1 tbsp breadcrumbs, to scatter in the last 3 min

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C. Toss beans with oil, salt and pepper; spread on sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe through thickest bean.
3. Roast 17–18 minutes. Add garlic; return 2–3 more minutes.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Hazan: "The garlic must not burn." Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, garlic-bright, Italian).

---

### 4. Roasted Green Beans with Almond Gremolata (inspired by Deborah Madison)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans
- 2 tbsp olive oil; salt and pepper
- *Almond gremolata:* 30 g toasted almonds (chopped); 1 garlic clove (minced); zest of 1 lemon; 15 g flat-leaf parsley (chopped); 1 tbsp olive oil

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss beans with oil; spread on sheet pan.
2. Insert MEATER probe through thickest bean.
3. Roast 20–22 min until blistered.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Mix gremolata; scatter over. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (blistered, almond-bright, lemon-fragrant).
