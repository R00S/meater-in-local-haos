# Green Beans × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Green beans (*Phaseolus vulgaris*) sautéed in a hot pan — Chinese *gan bian* dry-frying, Thai stir-fry, Indian tadka-tossed, or French *haricots verts sautés* — produce blistered, slightly charred beans with a concentrated sweet-green flavour. The technique requires very high heat and very dry beans (surface moisture prevents browning). Pull temperature: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F) for tender-crisp with blistered skin. MEATER probe inserted horizontally through the thickest bean mid-point.

## Styckesprofil

Gröna bönor (*Phaseolus vulgaris*) sauterade i het panna — kinesisk *gan bian* torrstekning, thai wok, indisk tadka-tossed eller franska *haricots verts sautés* — ger blåsiga, brynnade bönor. Sautera i 5–8 minuter vid hög värme. Klar när genomstekta och lätt brynnade.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Gān Biān Sìjìdòu (干煸四季豆 — Chinese Dry-Fried Green Beans)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed, patted very dry
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 dried red chillies (线辣椒), snipped, seeds shaken out
- 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp black rice vinegar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil

**Method**
1. Heat wok over maximum heat until smoking. Add oil. Add beans; stir-fry over maximum heat, pressing occasionally, for 5–6 minutes until wrinkled and blistered.
2. Insert MEATER probe through thickest bean.
3. Add chillies; stir-fry 30 s. Add garlic and ginger (20 s). Add soy sauce, sugar, vinegar; toss 30 s.
4. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (wrinkled, blistered, spicy-savoury, the classic Sichuan-style dry-fried bean).

### 2. Pailin Chongchitnant — Pad Tua Fak Yao (Thai Stir-Fried Yard-Long Beans)
**Inspired by**: Pailin Chongchitnant, *Hot Thai Kitchen* (Appetite by Random House, 2016); https://hot-thai-kitchen.com (stir-fried yard-long bean technique).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g yard-long beans or green beans, cut into 4 cm pieces, patted dry
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 1–2 red spur chillies (prik cheefa), diagonally sliced
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- ½ tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp white pepper

**Method**
1. Heat wok to maximum; add oil; add garlic (15 s); add beans. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Stir-fry 4–5 minutes until beans are blistered and tender. Add chillies; add oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar and white pepper; toss vigorously 30 s.
3. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (blistered, savoury-umami, chilli-bright, Thai).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Sabzi Tamater Beans (Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole mustard seeds
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli
- 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
- ½ tsp fine salt
- Lemon juice and coriander to finish

**Method**
1. Heat oil over medium-high; add mustard (pop); add beans, turmeric, coriander, chilli; toss.
2. Insert MEATER probe; sauté 6–7 minutes. Add tomato and salt; cook 3–4 more minutes.
3. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant).

---

### 4. Julia Child — Haricots Verts Sautés au Beurre (French tradition)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, *The French Chef Cookbook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1968)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ pre-blanching 3 min) · **Cook**: 3–4 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g haricots verts, blanched 3 min in boiling salted water, drained
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- ½ tsp fine salt; white pepper
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 1 tsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Blanch beans 3 minutes; drain; shock in ice water briefly; drain again. Pat dry.
2. Melt butter in a wide pan over high heat. Add beans; insert MEATER probe through thickest bean.
3. Sauté 3–4 minutes, tossing, until beans are heated through and beginning to colour.
4. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Season; add lemon and parsley. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (vivid green, buttery, lemon-fragrant, French).
