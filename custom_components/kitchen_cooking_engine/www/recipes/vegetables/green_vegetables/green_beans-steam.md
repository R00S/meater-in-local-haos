# Green Beans × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Green beans (*Phaseolus vulgaris*) steamed retain their bright-green colour, water-soluble vitamins, and clean sweet-grassy flavour better than boiling. Steam-cooking at 100 °C for 5–8 minutes (depending on thickness) produces vivid, tender-crisp beans. The classic follow-up step — a quick toss in butter and seasoning (French), a dressing of sesame and soy (Japanese), a tempering of mustard and curry leaves (Indian), or a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil (Italian/Mediterranean) — transforms the simply steamed bean into a finished dish. Pull temperature: 78–82 °C (172–180 °F). MEATER probe inserted through the side of the thickest bean at the mid-point.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Steamed Beans with Sesame Dressing (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, topped and tailed
- *Sesame dressing (goma-ae):* 2 tbsp toasted white sesame seeds; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 tsp sesame oil

**Method**
1. Steam beans over boiling water 5–7 minutes. Insert MEATER probe through thickest bean.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Allow to cool 5 minutes.
3. Grind sesame seeds in a suribachi (mortar); mix with soy, mirin, sugar and sesame oil. Toss cooled beans in the dressing. Final serving temperature: **room temperature to slightly warm** (the goma-ae dressing is designed for room-temperature or cool beans — this is a classic Japanese *ohitashi*-style preparation).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Steamed Beans with Mustard Seed Tadka (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min steam + 2 min tadka · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green beans, cut into 3 cm pieces
- *Tadka:* 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp whole mustard seeds; 10 fresh curry leaves; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli; ½ tsp fine salt; 1 tbsp desiccated coconut (optional)
- Lemon juice, to finish; fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Steam beans 5–7 minutes. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Transfer to a bowl.
3. Heat oil for tadka; mustard seeds (pop); curry leaves (5 s); turmeric and chilli (10 s). Pour over beans; toss with salt and lemon juice. Scatter coconut if using; garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (spiced, coconut-fragrant, South Indian).

---

### 3. Fuchsia Dunlop — Steamed Long Beans with Garlic and Soy (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 7–9 min · **Rest**: 3 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 500 g yard-long beans, cut into 5 cm pieces (or regular green beans)
- *Dressing:* 3 garlic cloves (minced); 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 tsp black rice vinegar; 1 tsp sesame oil; 1–2 tsp chilli oil; 2 tbsp hot oil (heated peanut or neutral oil — pour over garlic to "blossom" it)

**Method**
1. Steam beans 7–9 minutes (yard-long beans need slightly longer). Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Spread on a plate to cool 3 minutes.
3. Place minced garlic in a bowl; pour 2 tbsp hot oil over to briefly cook the garlic; immediately add soy sauce, sugar, vinegar, sesame oil and chilli oil; stir. Toss beans in this dressing. Final serving temperature: **room temperature** (this preparation is excellent as a Chinese cold dish, *liáng bàn cài*).

---

### 4. Julia Child — Haricots Verts à la Vapeur au Beurre Fondu (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min steam + 2 min butter toss · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g haricots verts, topped and tailed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground white pepper
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley or tarragon, chopped

**Method**
1. Steam beans over boiling water 5–7 minutes. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Drain; toss immediately in melted butter in a warm pan for 1–2 minutes. Season; add lemon juice and herbs.
3. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (vivid green, buttery, lemon-fragrant, classic French side).
