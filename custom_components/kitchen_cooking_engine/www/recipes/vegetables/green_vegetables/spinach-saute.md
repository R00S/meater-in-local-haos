# Spinach × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Spinach (*Spinacia oleracea*) sautéed wilts dramatically in 1–3 minutes in a hot pan with a small amount of oil or butter — fresh spinach loses approximately 90 % of its volume on cooking as moisture is expelled. The target: bright-green, just-wilted leaves with no excess water pooled in the pan. If a pool of water accumulates, the spinach is steaming not sautéing. Internal temperature at just-wilted doneness: 68–75 °C (154–167 °F) — above 75 °C the chlorophyll degrades and the colour turns army-drab. MEATER probe placed horizontally into the densest centre of the spinach pile in the pan.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Qīng Chǎo Bōcài (清炒菠菜 — Chinese Stir-Fried Spinach with Garlic)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 2–3 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh baby or regular spinach, well-washed and thoroughly dried; 2 tbsp neutral oil; 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; ½ tsp fine salt; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish; 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat wok to maximum heat until smoking. Add oil; when shimmering, add spinach all at once.
2. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the densest pile at the centre.
3. Stir-fry 30–60 seconds until beginning to wilt. Add garlic; stir-fry 30 more seconds. Add salt and sugar; toss.
4. **Pull at 68–72 °C (154–162 °F).** Dunlop emphasises: "The spinach should be just wilted — bright, glossy, barely cooked. Seconds matter." Drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion.
5. Final serving temperature: **68–72 °C / 154–162 °F** (bright green, garlicky, sesame-fragrant — serve immediately).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Palak Saag (Indian Sautéed Spinach with Spices)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach, washed and thoroughly dried; 3 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp whole cumin seeds; 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped; 1 green chilli, finely sliced; ½ tsp ground turmeric; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp fine salt; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high. Add cumin seeds (sizzle 10 s). Add garlic and green chilli; cook 30 s. Add turmeric, coriander powder and Kashmiri chilli; stir 10 s.
2. Add all the spinach at once. Insert MEATER probe into the densest pile.
3. Stir vigorously for 1–2 minutes until spinach wilts completely.
4. Season with salt; squeeze lemon juice. **Pull at 70–74 °C (158–165 °F).**
5. Garnish with fresh coriander. Final serving: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (spiced, fragrant, vivid — serve with dal and rice).

### 3. Marcella Hazan — Spinaci Saltati con Aglio e Olio (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 3–4 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach; 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide pan over medium-high. Add garlic; cook 30 s until fragrant but not browned.
2. Add spinach. Insert MEATER probe into the densest pile.
3. Stir 2–3 minutes until just wilted. Season with salt and pepper.
4. **Pull at 68–72 °C (154–162 °F).** Hazan: "The spinach is done the moment it has completely wilted and collapsed." Final serving: **68–72 °C / 154–162 °F** (vivid, garlicky, olive oil-fragrant, Italian).

---

### 4. Julia Child — Épinards Sautés au Beurre (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ 3 min blanching) · **Cook**: 2–3 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach, blanched 30 s in boiling salted water, thoroughly squeezed dry; 30 g butter; ½ tsp fine salt; white pepper; fresh nutmeg; 1 tbsp double cream (crème fraîche) — Child includes cream to soften bitterness

**Method**
1. Blanch spinach briefly; squeeze very dry (this is Child's technique — removes excess bitterness and water).
2. Melt butter in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Add the dry spinach.
3. Insert MEATER probe into the densest pile. Sauté 2–3 min, tossing, until heated through and lightly glossed with butter.
4. Season with salt, white pepper and nutmeg. Add cream; toss.
5. **Pull at 70–74 °C (158–165 °F).** Final serving: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (buttery, cream-enriched, nutmeg-fragrant, classic French *épinards*).
