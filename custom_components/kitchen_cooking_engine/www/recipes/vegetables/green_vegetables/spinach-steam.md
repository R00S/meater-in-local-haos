# Spinach × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Spinach (*Spinacia oleracea*) steamed — held over boiling water in a steamer basket or colander with a lid — wilts in 2–4 minutes, retaining its colour and nutrients better than boiling (which leaches iron and vitamins into the water). Pull temperature: 68–74 °C (154–165 °F). A MEATER probe placed horizontally into the densest pile of leaves in the steamer measures when the spinach has reached wilting temperature. Like sautéed spinach, steamed spinach is then dressed with various seasonings: butter (French), sesame (Japanese), coconut and mustard seeds (South Indian), or lemon and olive oil (Mediterranean).

## Styckesprofil

Spenat (*Spinacia oleracea*) ångad — hålls ovanför kokande vatten i en ångkorg eller durkslag med lock — vissnar på 2–4 minuter och bevarar sin färg och näring bättre än kokning. Klar när fullständigt vissnad och ljusgrön.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Steamed Spinach Ohitashi (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 2–3 min + chilling · **Rest**: 5 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach, washed; *Dashi dressing:* 3 tbsp dashi stock; 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds; optional: katsuobushi (bonito flakes) to garnish

**Method**
1. Set up a steamer basket over boiling water. Steam spinach 2–3 minutes until just wilted.
2. Insert MEATER probe into the densest part of the spinach pile. **Pull at 68–72 °C (154–162 °F).**
3. Transfer immediately to a bowl of ice water; squeeze dry in a clean cloth. Press into a cylinder on a cutting board; slice into 3 cm sections.
4. Pour dashi dressing over; toss gently. Allow to marinate 5 minutes. Scatter sesame seeds and optional katsuobushi. Serve at room temperature. Final serving: **room temperature** — Ohitashi is traditionally served cool; the temperature is irrelevant once chilled.

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Steamed Palak with Coconut Tadka (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010); South Indian coconut tadka method.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 3–4 min steam + 2 min tadka · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach; *Tadka:* 2 tbsp coconut oil; 1 tsp whole mustard seeds; 10 fresh curry leaves; 2 dried red chillies; ½ tsp turmeric; 3 tbsp freshly grated coconut (or desiccated); ½ tsp fine salt; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Steam spinach over boiling water 3–4 minutes. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 70–74 °C (158–165 °F).** Transfer to a serving bowl; squeeze out excess water.
3. Heat coconut oil; mustard seeds (pop); curry leaves and chillies (sizzle); turmeric and coconut (20 s). Pour tadka over spinach; toss; season. Final serving: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (fragrant, coconut-spiced, South Indian).

---

### 3. Fuchsia Dunlop — Steamed Spinach with Sesame Oil and Soy (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012); *liáng bàn* (cold-dressed) spinach.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 2–3 min · **Rest**: 3 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach; *Dressing:* 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp sesame oil; 1 tsp rice vinegar; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tsp chilli oil; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Steam spinach 2–3 minutes. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 68–72 °C (154–162 °F).**
2. Transfer to a bowl; allow to cool slightly; squeeze out excess water. Whisk dressing; pour over; toss; scatter sesame seeds. Final serving: **slightly warm to room temperature** (Dunlop's *liáng bàn* style works best at room temperature).

---

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Steamed Spinach with Olive Oil and Lemon (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 3–4 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fresh spinach; 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; juice of ½ lemon; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper

**Method**
1. Steam spinach over boiling water 3–4 minutes. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 70–74 °C (158–165 °F).**
2. Transfer to a serving bowl; squeeze out excess water lightly.
3. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice; season with salt and black pepper; toss. Final serving: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (vivid green, olive oil-fragrant, lemon-bright, minimalist Italian).
