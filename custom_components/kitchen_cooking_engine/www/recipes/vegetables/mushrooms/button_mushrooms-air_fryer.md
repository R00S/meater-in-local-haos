# Button Mushrooms × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Button mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus*) in the air fryer roast in 8–12 minutes at 200 °C — significantly faster than an oven — with the circulating hot air dehydrating the surface moisture that would otherwise cause steaming and prevent browning. The main enemy of mushroom browning is moisture: the caps contain ≈ 92 % water, and this must be driven off before Maillard browning can occur. The air fryer's rapid convection achieves this faster than any conventional oven method. Internal pull temperature for fully roasted button mushrooms in the air fryer: 80–86 °C (176–187 °F) — at this point the exterior is golden-brown and the interior is silky, juicy and fully cooked. A MEATER probe is inserted through the side of the largest cap into its geometric centre before the cook begins.

## Styckesprofil

Champinjoner (*Agaricus bisporus*) i airfryern rostas på 8–12 minuter vid 200 °C — väsentligt snabbare än ugn — med den cirkulerande heta luften som dehydrerar ytan. Hela eller halverade, lite olja, krydda. Klar när gyllenbrun och skrumpna.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Air-Fried Shuāng Gū (双菇 — Chinese double-mushroom stir-fry, air fryer adaptation)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012); technique adapted for air fryer preparation from her double-mushroom sauté.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, left whole if small (≤ 3 cm) or halved if larger
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce (蚝油)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish
- 1 spring onion (scallion), thinly sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat the mushrooms completely dry with paper towels. Toss with neutral oil, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, oyster sauce and sugar. Marinate 5 minutes.
2. Place the mushrooms in the air fryer basket in a single layer (work in batches if needed — no crowding).
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of the largest mushroom cap into its geometric centre.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 10–12 minutes**, shaking once at 6 minutes, until golden-brown and caramelised at the surface.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Dunlop's style: mushrooms that are deeply flavoured, golden, with a savoury-umami glaze.
6. Transfer to a bowl; drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, savoury-umami, serve immediately with rice).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Air-Fried Mushroom Tikka (Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); tikka marinade method adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 20 min marinating · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms (medium-large), wiped clean
- *Tikka marinade:* 120 g (½ cup) full-fat yoghurt; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp grated ginger; 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp garam masala; ½ tsp fine salt; 1 tbsp neutral oil; juice of ½ lemon
- Lemon wedges and fresh coriander, to serve
- Onion rings and mint chutney, to serve

**Method**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients. Toss the mushrooms in the marinade; allow to marinate at room temperature 20 minutes (or up to 2 hours in the refrigerator).
2. Place the marinated mushrooms in the air fryer basket in a single layer.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of the largest mushroom into its geometric centre.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 10–12 minutes**, shaking once at 6 minutes, until the marinade is charred in spots and the mushrooms are cooked through and golden.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Jaffrey's tikka style: lightly charred, spice-crusted, juicy within. Serve immediately with lemon, coriander, onion rings and mint chutney. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (charred, spiced, tangy).

### 3. Harumi Kurihara — Air-Fried Kinoko no Shio-Yaki (Japanese salted mushrooms)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, halved (or whole if small)
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sea salt (flaked)
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 1 spring onion, finely sliced, to finish
- A few drops of soy sauce, to finish

**Method**
1. Toss mushrooms in sesame oil and salt. Place in air fryer basket, cut side down.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of the largest mushroom into its geometric centre.
3. Air fry at **180 °C (356 °F) for 8–10 minutes**, shaking once, until golden and tender.
4. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Harumi's style: clean, simple, sesame-fragrant mushrooms.
5. Transfer to a bowl; add a few drops of soy sauce; scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (clean, sesame-fragrant, minimal Japanese).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Mushrooms with Herb Oil (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010); technique adapted for air fryer from his roasted-mushroom preparations.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped clean, left whole
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- *Herb oil to finish:* 2 tbsp olive oil; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 1 tbsp capers, roughly chopped; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped; ½ tsp sumac

**Method**
1. Toss mushrooms with olive oil, garlic, thyme, salt and pepper.
2. Place in air fryer basket in a single layer.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of the largest mushroom into its centre.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 10–12 minutes**, shaking once, until deeply golden.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).**
6. Whisk the herb oil ingredients together. Transfer mushrooms to a platter; spoon herb oil over. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, herb-bright, caper-tangy).
