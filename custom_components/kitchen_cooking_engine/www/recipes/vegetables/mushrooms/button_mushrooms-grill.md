# Button Mushrooms × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Button mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus*, immature white form) are roughly 92 % water held in a sponge-like network of chitin-rich fungal cell walls. On a hot grill the surface water flashes off in seconds, the cap caramelises and the mushroom collapses to perhaps two-thirds its raw volume. The grill imparts charred Maillard browning concentrated where the gills or cap touch the bars. Threading mushrooms onto skewers (or using a grill basket) is the only way to keep them from rolling and falling through. Carryover is small but real — about 2–3 °C — because the dehydrated cap insulates the moist interior. Brushing with oil before grilling is essential; mushrooms will stick badly to bare grates.

## Source recipes

### 1. Mark Bittman — Grilled Button Mushrooms with Garlic & Lemon (American tradition)
**Source**: Mark Bittman, *How to Grill Everything* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2018)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g whole button mushrooms (caps 2–3 cm), wiped clean, stems trimmed flush with cap
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) fresh lemon juice, to finish
- 5 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat a gas or charcoal grill to medium-high direct heat (≈ 230 °C surface). Clean and oil the grates.
2. Toss the mushrooms in a bowl with olive oil, grated garlic, lemon zest, salt and pepper.
3. Thread mushrooms onto pairs of bamboo skewers (two parallel skewers stop them spinning), 6–8 per skewer pair, gill-side facing one direction.
4. Insert the MEATER Plus probe horizontally into the cap of one of the central mushrooms on the middle skewer, parallel to the skewer axis, with the tip near the geometric centre of the cap. (Use the smallest cap that comfortably accepts the probe; the probe will register the temperature of that cap and its neighbours.)
5. Place skewers gill-side-down on the grates. Cook 4–5 minutes undisturbed.
6. Turn skewers cap-side-down. Cook another 3–4 minutes until caps are deeply browned and any visible gills look dry and concentrated.
7. **Pull at 75–80 °C (167–176 °F).** Bittman notes mushrooms are done when "they have shrunk noticeably and the gills look concentrated, almost dry."
8. Slide off skewers, toss with lemon juice and parsley. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (juicy, intensely savoury).

### 2. Adam Liaw — Yakitori-style Mushroom Skewers (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Adam Liaw, *Asian Cookery School* (Hardie Grant, 2015) and adamliaw.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min skewer-soak) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, caps 3–4 cm, stems trimmed flush
- 60 ml (¼ cup) Japanese soy sauce
- 60 ml (¼ cup) mirin
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sake
- 15 g (1 tbsp) caster sugar
- 1 small garlic clove, grated
- 5 g grated fresh ginger
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) neutral oil, for brushing
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish
- 2 g toasted white sesame seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Combine soy, mirin, sake, sugar, garlic and ginger in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer and reduce by one-third (3–4 minutes) into a glossy *tare*. Cool.
2. Soak bamboo skewers in water 30 minutes. Thread mushrooms snugly, 5–6 per skewer.
3. Preheat a grill to medium-high direct heat (≈ 220 °C surface). Brush mushrooms lightly with oil to prevent initial sticking.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into one of the central mushrooms on the middle skewer, parallel to the skewer.
5. Grill 2 minutes per side, then begin brushing with the *tare* between turns. Liaw stresses applying glaze in three thin coats rather than one heavy one — sugar burns easily over direct flame.
6. Continue grilling 3–4 minutes more, brushing twice more, until the glaze is set and the mushrooms have shrunk noticeably.
7. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Liaw's *yakitori-ya* style aims for mushrooms that are deeply lacquered and meaty in texture but still juicy.
8. Scatter spring onion and sesame seeds. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (glazed, glossy, savoury-sweet).

### 3. Francis Mallmann — Champiñones a la Parrilla (Argentine/Patagonian tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan, 2009)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g large button mushrooms (4 cm caps preferred), stems left attached
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) coarse sea salt (sal gruesa)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 15 g chopped flat-leaf parsley
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) red-wine vinegar, to finish (chimichurri-style)

**Method**
1. Build a wood or charcoal fire and let it burn down to medium-high coals (≈ 240 °C grate temperature). Mallmann emphasises wood-coal grilling for a cleaner, drier heat than gas.
2. Toss mushrooms in a bowl with olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic and half the parsley. Allow to marinate 5 minutes.
3. Place mushrooms cap-side-down directly on the grate, well spaced — Mallmann eschews skewers in favour of careful tonging.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into a representative central mushroom, parallel to the grate.
5. Grill 4–5 minutes undisturbed; cap should release naturally and show black bar marks.
6. Turn gill-side-down; grill 3–4 minutes more, basting once with leftover oil from the bowl.
7. **Pull at 80–85 °C (176–185 °F).** Mallmann favours a slightly firmer, drier finish than the Japanese style — the mushrooms should be charred at the edges and retain a pronounced "meaty" bite.
8. Drizzle with vinegar; finish with the remaining parsley. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (charred, savoury, herb-bright).

### 4. Anna Jones — Grilled Marinated Mushrooms (British vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Anna Jones, *A Modern Way to Cook* (Fourth Estate, 2015)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 20 min marinade) · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms (3 cm caps), wiped, stems trimmed
- 45 ml (3 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) tamari or light soy sauce
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) balsamic vinegar
- 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 1 g (¼ tsp) dried thyme
- 1 g (¼ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 2 g chopped fresh thyme leaves, to finish
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) lemon juice, to finish

**Method**
1. Whisk olive oil, tamari, balsamic, garlic, dried thyme and pepper in a bowl. Add mushrooms; toss to coat. Marinate 20 minutes at room temperature.
2. Preheat a stovetop ridged griddle pan or outdoor grill to medium-high (≈ 220 °C grate temperature).
3. Thread mushrooms onto pairs of skewers, 6 per skewer pair.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into the centre cap of the middle skewer, parallel to the skewer.
5. Grill 3 minutes gill-side-down for ridge marks, then 3–4 minutes cap-side-down.
6. **Pull at 76–80 °C (169–176 °F).** Jones targets slightly less reduction than Mallmann — the mushrooms should be deeply seasoned but retain visible juiciness when sliced.
7. Slide off skewers, finish with fresh thyme and lemon juice. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F** (juicy, umami-rich, lightly charred).
