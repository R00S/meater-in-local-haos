# Button Mushrooms × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Button mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus*) oven-roasted at 200–220 °C concentrate their flavour dramatically as moisture evaporates, converting from plump white spheres to golden-brown, intensely flavoured morsels. The key is a hot, preheated oven and a dry sheet pan with generous space between caps — this maximises surface contact and airflow so mushrooms roast rather than steam. Roasting time is 20–25 minutes. Whole caps (8–30 g each) need 20–25 minutes; halved or sliced, 15–18 minutes. Pull temperature: 80–86 °C (176–187 °F) for slices/halves; 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) for whole caps. A MEATER probe is inserted through the top of the largest whole cap into its geometric centre.

## Styckesprofil

Champinjoner (*Agaricus bisporus*) ugnsrostade vid 200–220 °C koncentrerar sin smak dramatiskt när fuktigheten avdunstar. Omvandlas från runda vita sfärer till gyllenbrun, skrumpna, intensivt smakrika bitar på 20–25 minuter. Klar när djupt gyllenbrun och skrumpna.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Kinoko no Oven Yaki (Japanese oven-roasted mushrooms)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped clean, left whole
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (usukuchi-shoyu)
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish
- Steamed short-grain rice, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Line a baking sheet with foil (for easy cleanup — mushrooms release concentrated juices).
2. Whisk sesame oil, soy sauce, mirin and sugar. Toss the mushrooms in this mixture.
3. Spread on the sheet pan in a single layer, tops up. Insert the MEATER probe through the top of the largest cap into its geometric centre.
4. Roast 18–20 minutes until the caps are golden-brown on the tops and the pan juices are reduced and syrupy.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Harumi's style: glossy, sweet-savoury caps.
6. Transfer to a bowl with all the pan juices; scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (glossy, sweet-soy, sesame-fragrant).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Dhingri Masala (Mushroom Masala, oven adaptation — Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped, whole if small or halved if large
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated ginger
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ¾ tsp fine salt
- 2 ripe tomatoes, finely diced
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C (410 °F). In a small pan, heat oil; add cumin seeds (10 s); add onion, cook 5 min until golden; add garlic, ginger (1 min); add turmeric, coriander powder, chilli (30 s); add tomatoes, salt; cook 3 min. This is the masala.
2. Toss mushrooms in the masala; spread on a lined sheet pan.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the top of the largest cap into its geometric centre.
4. Roast 18–20 minutes until the mushrooms are deeply golden and the masala is clinging and slightly caramelised.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Scatter garam masala and fresh coriander. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant).

### 3. Claudia Roden — Champignons Rôtis à la Marocaine (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped, halved
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- ½ tsp ground ginger
- ½ tsp fine cinnamon
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp lemon juice, to finish
- 15 g fresh coriander or flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Toss mushroom halves with olive oil, garlic, cumin, paprika, ginger, cinnamon, salt and pepper. Spread on a sheet pan, cut-side-down.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of the largest cap into its centre.
3. Roast 20–25 minutes until golden-brown and caramelised at the cut faces.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Drizzle lemon juice; scatter herbs. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–186 °F** (spiced, golden, North African character).

---

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Funghi Trifolati al Forno (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 22–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, cleaned, sliced 5 mm thick
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped (not minced to paste — Hazan uses coarsely chopped)
- 15 g (3 tbsp) flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Optional: a small amount of dry white wine to deglaze (30 ml)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C (410 °F). Spread mushroom slices on a large sheet pan in a single layer (do not crowd). Drizzle with olive oil; season with salt and pepper; toss.
2. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest slice near the centre of the pan.
3. Roast 18–20 minutes until the mushroom slices are golden-brown and most moisture has evaporated.
4. In the last 3 minutes, scatter the garlic and parsley over the mushrooms; return to oven briefly.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Hazan: "The garlic should be just golden — never darkened." Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, garlic-parsley bright, Italian).
