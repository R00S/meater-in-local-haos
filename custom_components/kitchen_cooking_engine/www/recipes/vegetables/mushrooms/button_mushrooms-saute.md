# Button Mushrooms × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Button mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus*) sautéed in a very hot pan are the classic restaurant technique for achieving maximum flavour concentration in minimum time. The key principle, articulated by Sanjay Aggarwal and confirmed by numerous food scientists: the pan must be scorching hot and uncrowded, so mushrooms brown rapidly before their expelled moisture creates steam. Crowded, slow-cooked mushrooms stew rather than sauté, and their flavour is diluted and flat. The target for sautéed button mushrooms is 78–85 °C (172–185 °F) internal temperature: golden-brown exterior, silky-juicy interior, and the characteristic umami-concentrated flavour of properly sautéed funghi. Carryover is negligible.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Sù Chǎo Shuāng Gū (素炒双菇 — Chinese Stir-Fried Button Mushrooms)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped clean, sliced 5 mm thick
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (peanut or sunflower)
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce (蚝油)
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat mushroom slices bone-dry with paper towels. This single step is the most important for browning.
2. Heat a wok over maximum heat until nearly smoking. Add oil; when it shimmers instantly, add the mushroom slices.
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest slice near the centre.
4. Spread the slices; don't stir for 90 seconds — allow one face to sear. Then toss and spread again for another 60–90 seconds. Repeat until most slices are golden-brown.
5. Push to the edge; add garlic and ginger to the centre; stir-fry 20 seconds.
6. Toss everything together; add soy sauce, oyster sauce and sugar; toss vigorously 30 seconds until glossy.
7. **Pull at 80–85 °C (176–185 °F).** Off the heat, drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (golden, savoury-umami, serve immediately with rice).

### 2. Julia Child — Champignons Sautés (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped clean, quartered (or sliced for faster cooking)
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp dry sherry or dry white vermouth (optional — Child uses this for flavour)
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped, to finish
- Squeeze of lemon juice

**Method**
1. Heat a wide frying pan over high heat until very hot. Add butter and oil together — the oil prevents the butter from browning too quickly while still delivering the butter flavour. When the butter foam subsides, add the mushrooms.
2. Child is emphatic: "Do NOT add salt at this stage — salt draws out moisture and prevents browning. Add salt at the end."
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into a thick quarter near the centre of the pan.
4. Sauté over high heat, tossing or stirring every 2 minutes, 6–7 minutes, until the mushrooms are golden-brown and reduced in volume by about half.
5. Add sherry or vermouth if using; cook 30 seconds until it evaporates. Season with salt and pepper. Squeeze lemon juice; scatter parsley.
6. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Child writes: "The mushrooms are done when they are lightly browned and all the liquid they have given off has evaporated." Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (buttery, golden, herb-bright — the classic French garnish).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Dhingri aur Hari Mirch (Indian mushrooms with green chilli)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped, quartered
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 1–2 green chillies (serrano or Indian green), finely sliced
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 2 ripe tomatoes (medium), finely chopped
- ½ tsp garam masala, to finish
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds; sizzle 10 s. Add garlic, ginger and chillies; cook 30 s.
2. Add mushrooms; toss. Insert MEATER probe into a large quarter near the centre.
3. Sauté over medium-high heat, stirring, 5–6 minutes until the mushrooms are golden.
4. Add turmeric, coriander powder, salt; stir 20 s. Add tomatoes; cook 3–4 min until saucy and absorbed.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Dust with garam masala; garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant — serve with roti).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Sautéed Mushrooms with Labneh (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g button mushrooms, wiped, thickly sliced
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 1 tsp za'atar, to finish
- 200 g labneh (strained yoghurt), to serve
- 1 tbsp lemon juice, to finish
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, to scatter

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, heavy frying pan over high heat. Add mushrooms in a single uncrowded layer; do not stir for 90 seconds.
2. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest slice near the centre.
3. Toss and cook 5–6 more minutes, tossing every 90 seconds, until deeply golden. Add garlic and thyme; cook 1 more minute.
4. Season with salt; drizzle lemon juice.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Spread labneh on a platter; pile mushrooms over; dust with za'atar and scatter parsley. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (golden, garlic-fragrant, cool-creamy contrast with labneh).
