# Portobello Mushrooms × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Portobello mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus* — the same species as button mushrooms, grown to maturity) in the air fryer cook in 12–16 minutes at 190–200 °C. Their large caps (8–15 cm diameter) and dense flesh benefit from the air fryer's rapid convection, which dehydrates the exposed surfaces and concentrates flavour faster than an oven. Portobello caps are typically cooked gill-side-up (to retain their juices) or gill-side-down (to achieve a drier, crisper result). A MEATER probe is inserted through the cap skin (the convex top side) into the thickest central point of the flesh, with the tip just above the gills. Pull temperature: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) for a juicy, fully cooked result.

## Source recipes

### 1. Maangchi — Air-Fried Portobello Gui (Korean tradition)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); https://www.maangchi.com (portobello *gui* method; technique adapted for air fryer).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 10 min marinating · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello mushroom caps (≈ 120–150 g each), stems removed
- *Korean BBQ marinade:* 2 tbsp soy sauce (간장, ganjang); 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tbsp gochujang (fermented chilli paste); 1 tbsp honey or corn syrup (물엿); 2 garlic cloves, minced; 1 tsp grated ginger; 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 1 spring onion (scallion), finely sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Whisk the marinade ingredients together. Spoon 1 tbsp of marinade into the gill side of each cap and spread to coat the gills. Brush the outside (cap top) with marinade too. Marinate 10 minutes.
2. Place caps gill-side-up in the air fryer basket.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin (convex top side) into the geometric centre of the flesh, with tip just above the gills.
4. Air fry at **190 °C (374 °F) for 14–16 minutes**, until the gill side has absorbed the marinade and the mushroom juices are pooling in the cap, and the cap skin is slightly crisped.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** The caps should feel completely firm yet yielding when pressed, with concentrated juices pooled in the gills.
6. Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Serve with steamed rice as a main dish or banchan. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (savoury-sweet, gochujang-glossed, Korean character).

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Air-Fried Portobello with Ponzu (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 portobello caps, stems removed
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp ponzu sauce (citrus soy — yuzu or sudachi based, or lime + soy substitute)
- 1 tsp mirin
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
- Daikon oroshi (grated white radish) and spring onion, to serve

**Method**
1. Whisk sesame oil, ponzu, mirin and sugar. Brush the gills and cap tops generously.
2. Place gill-side-up in the air fryer basket. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the flesh centre.
3. Air fry at **180 °C (356 °F) for 12–14 minutes** until the mushroom juices have pooled and reduced in the gills and the cap is tender.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds. Serve with grated daikon and sliced spring onion, and additional ponzu as dipping sauce. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (citrus-soy, umami-rich, minimal Japanese).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Air-Fried Portobello Tikka (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); tikka method applied to portobello caps.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 20 min marinating · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps (≈ 120 g each), stems removed
- *Tikka marinade:* 120 g full-fat yoghurt; 1 tbsp neutral oil; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp grated ginger; 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; 1 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp ground cumin; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp fine salt; juice of ½ lemon
- Lemon wedges, raw onion rings, fresh coriander, to serve
- Mint chutney, to serve

**Method**
1. Score the gill side of each cap with a crosshatch (for marinade penetration). Apply marinade to both sides; marinate 20 minutes.
2. Place gill-side-up in the air fryer basket. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the centre.
3. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 14–16 minutes** until the marinade is charred in spots and the flesh is cooked through.
4. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Serve immediately with lemon wedges, onion rings, fresh coriander and mint chutney. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (charred, spiced, juicy).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Portobello with Herbed Ricotta (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 14–16 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps, stems removed
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- *Herbed ricotta:* 200 g ricotta; 1 garlic clove grated; zest of 1 lemon; 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves; 2 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, chopped; ½ tsp fine salt
- Rocket (arugula) leaves, to serve
- Aged balsamic or pomegranate molasses, to drizzle

**Method**
1. Brush caps all over with olive oil; season with salt and pepper.
2. Air fry gill-side-up at **200 °C (390 °F) for 10 minutes.** Remove basket; fill each gill cavity with a generous spoonful of herbed ricotta. Return to air fryer at 190 °C for 4–6 more minutes until the ricotta is warmed and lightly golden at edges.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the flesh before the first 10 minute cook. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F)** (probe reading at end of first cook before adding ricotta).
4. Serve caps on rocket; drizzle with balsamic or pomegranate molasses. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (meaty, herb-creamy, elegant).
