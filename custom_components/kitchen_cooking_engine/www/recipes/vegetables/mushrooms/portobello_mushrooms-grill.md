# Portobello Mushrooms × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Portobello mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus* mature) on the grill develop a spectacular smoky char on the cap skin while the gills trap and concentrate the juices that the heat drives from the flesh. The convex cap skin is placed down first on the hot grates, giving time for the dense flesh to heat through; then the cap is flipped gill-side-down briefly (or gill-side-up for a moister, juicier result). Pull temperature: 84–90 °C (183–194 °F), where the flesh is tender, juicy and fully cooked but the cap holds its shape. A MEATER probe is inserted through the cap skin on the convex top into the thickest central part of the flesh. Grilled portobello is used as a meat substitute, a burger base, and as a vehicle for stuffings across many cuisines.

## Styckesprofil

Portabello-svampar (*Agaricus bisporus* mogna) på grillen utvecklar spektakulär rökig förkolning på hattens yta medan gälarna fångar och koncentrerar saften. Grilla hattssidan ned vid medel-hög värme i 4–5 minuter, vänd för 3–4 minuter. Klar när skrumpna och djupt brynnade.

## Source recipes

### 1. Portobello Gui (inspired by Maangchi)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/portobello-gui (Korean BBQ mushroom technique).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 15 min marinating · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps (≈ 120–150 g each), stems removed
- *Korean BBQ (bulgogi-style) marinade:* 3 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tbsp gochujang; 1 tbsp honey; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp grated ginger; 1 tsp caster sugar; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds; 1 spring onion (finely sliced)
- Steamed short-grain rice, lettuce leaves (sangchu), to serve

**Method**
1. Whisk the marinade. Spoon 2 tsp into each gill cavity; brush the cap tops. Marinate 15 minutes.
2. Preheat a gas or charcoal grill to medium-high direct heat. Clean and oil grates.
3. Place caps cap-side-down on the grill. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the thickest centre.
4. Grill cap-side-down 5–6 minutes until the cap skin has char marks and the flesh is half-cooked. Flip gill-side-down; grill 4–5 more minutes.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Scatter extra sesame seeds. Serve sliced over rice with lettuce wraps. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (smoky, sweet-savoury, Korean character).

### 2. Portobello Dengaku Yaki (inspired by Shizuo Tsuji)
**Inspired by**: Shizuo Tsuji, *Japanese Cooking: A Simple Art* (Kodansha International, 1980); dengaku technique applied to large mushrooms.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps, stems removed, wiped clean
- 2 tbsp neutral oil, for brushing
- *Dengaku miso paste:* 3 tbsp white (shiro) miso; 2 tbsp mirin; 1 tbsp caster sugar; 1 tbsp sake
- Toasted sesame seeds and finely sliced spring onion, to finish

**Method**
1. Make the dengaku paste: stir miso, mirin, sugar and sake in a small pan over low heat until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is glossy (2 min).
2. Preheat grill to medium-high. Brush caps generously with neutral oil, both sides.
3. Place gill-side-up on the grill. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the thickest centre.
4. Grill gill-side-up 5–6 minutes. Flip; grill cap-side-up 3 min. Flip back to gill-side-up; spread dengaku paste generously over the gills. Grill 2–3 more minutes until the paste caramelises at the edges.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Tsuji's dengaku style: the paste must not burn — caramelised amber is perfect, black is failure. Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (sweet, miso-glazed, smoky).

### 3. Grilled Portobello with Lemon Herb Dressing (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 portobello caps, stems removed
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- *Lemon herb dressing:* 3 tbsp olive oil; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tbsp capers; 15 g flat-leaf parsley; 5 g fresh tarragon; ½ tsp sumac
- Grilled flatbread, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat grill to high. Brush caps on both sides with olive oil; season with salt and pepper.
2. Place cap-side-down on the grill. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the thickest centre.
3. Grill 5–6 minutes; flip gill-side-down; grill 4–5 more minutes until completely tender.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).**
5. Whisk the dressing ingredients. Transfer mushrooms to a platter; spoon dressing over. Serve with warm flatbread. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (smoky, herb-bright, caper-tangy).

---

### 4. Grilled Portobello Burgers (inspired by Deborah Madison)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min + 20 min marinating · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps (≈ 130 g each), stems removed
- *Marinade:* 3 tbsp olive oil; 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp fresh rosemary (finely chopped); 1 tsp fresh thyme; ½ tsp fine salt; ¼ tsp black pepper
- 4 burger buns, lightly toasted
- Toppings: goat cheese, rocket, sliced tomato, caramelised onions

**Method**
1. Combine marinade; brush caps on both sides. Marinate 20 minutes.
2. Preheat grill to medium-high. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the thickest centre.
3. Grill cap-side-down 5–6 minutes; flip; grill gill-side-down 4–5 minutes.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Serve caps in toasted buns with goat cheese, rocket and tomato. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (meaty, balsamic-fragrant, satisfying vegetarian burger).
