# Portobello Mushrooms × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Portobello mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus* mature) sautéed whole or in thick slices develop a deep umami-richness in a hot pan — the dense flesh concentrates as moisture evaporates, producing an almost meaty character at the target internal temperature of 80–88 °C (176–190 °F). A portobello cap is typically sliced into 1.5–2 cm slabs for sautéing (so the MEATER probe sits in the thickest slab) or sautéed whole if the pan is large enough. The pan must be very hot before adding the mushrooms, and they must not be crowded, or they will steam. Unlike button mushrooms, portobello caps tend to release more liquid, making deglazing an important part of most sauté recipes.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Sautéed Portobello with Black Bean Sauce (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012); stir-fry technique with black bean sauce applied to portobello.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 portobello caps (≈ 500 g total), stems removed, sliced 1.5 cm thick
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 1 tbsp fermented black beans (豆豉, douchi), roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat portobello slices very dry. Heat a wok or wide, heavy pan over maximum heat until smoking. Add oil; heat until it shimmers.
2. Add portobello slices in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest slab near the centre.
3. Sear undisturbed 2–3 minutes on one face. Flip; sear 2 more minutes. The surfaces should be deeply golden.
4. Push to edge; add garlic, ginger and black beans to the centre; fry 20 seconds. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, Shaoxing wine and sugar; toss vigorously. Cook 1 minute until sauce glazes the mushrooms.
5. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil. Serve with rice. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (deeply savoury, black-bean-umami, glossy).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Portobello Masala Sauté (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); mushroom masala technique applied to portobello.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 portobello caps (≈ 500 g), sliced 1.5 cm thick
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated ginger
- 1 green chilli, finely sliced
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds (10 s); add onion, cook 5 min until golden; add garlic, ginger, chilli (1 min); add spices (30 s); add tomatoes and salt, cook 3 min to a thick masala.
2. Add portobello slices; toss to coat. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest slab.
3. Sauté over medium-high heat 6–7 minutes, turning once, until slices are golden on both faces and cooked through.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Dust with garam masala; garnish with coriander. Serve with roti. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant).

### 3. Julia Child — Sautéed Portobello with Herbs (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *The French Chef Cookbook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1968); champignons sautés method applied to large portobello caps.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 portobello caps (≈ 500 g), sliced 1.5 cm thick
- 40 g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp freshly ground white pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry sherry or Madeira
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat a wide, heavy pan over high heat. Add butter and oil together; when the foam subsides, add portobello slices in a single layer. Do NOT salt yet.
2. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest slab near the centre.
3. Sauté over high heat 3–4 minutes per side until deeply golden. Add garlic and thyme; cook 30 seconds.
4. Add sherry; cook 1 minute while it evaporates and deglazes the pan. Season with salt and white pepper. Scatter parsley.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Child: "Portobello mushrooms should be cooked until all their liquid is expelled and the surface is deeply brown — they should be treated exactly like any other mushroom, just larger." Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (buttery, golden, herb-fragrant, French).

### 4. Deborah Madison — Sautéed Portobello with Balsamic and Spinach (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 portobello caps (≈ 500 g), sliced 1.5 cm thick
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 60 ml (¼ cup) aged balsamic vinegar
- 200 g baby spinach leaves (to wilt in the same pan at the end)
- Freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, heavy pan over high heat. Add portobello slices; sear 3–4 minutes per side until deeply golden.
2. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest slab at the start.
3. Push mushrooms to the edge; add garlic and rosemary to the centre; cook 30 seconds.
4. Add balsamic vinegar; toss; cook 1 minute until it reduces to a glaze. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Add spinach; toss until just wilted, 1 minute.
6. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (meaty, balsamic-glazed, with tender spinach).
