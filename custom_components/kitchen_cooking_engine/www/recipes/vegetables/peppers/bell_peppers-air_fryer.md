# Bell Peppers × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Bell peppers (*Capsicum annuum*) in the air fryer roast beautifully in 12–18 minutes without the need for high-heat oven preheating. The circulating air blisters the skin and caramelises the sugars of sliced strips faster than a conventional oven. Whole peppers can be air-fried for peel-and-serve preparations (same result as oven or grill, just faster). Halves or strips aimed for eating without peeling are done at 200 °C (390 °F) in 12–15 minutes, reaching an internal temperature of 82–90 °C (180–194 °F) where the flesh is tender and juicy with slightly blistered skin. A MEATER probe is inserted through the side wall of a whole pepper or horizontally into a thick strip before starting the cook. The air fryer is particularly popular for Asian-spiced pepper dishes since the intense dry heat replicates the wok-scorched texture without a wok.

## Source recipes

### 1. Maangchi — Gochu Jeon (Air-Fried Stuffed Peppers — Korean tradition)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/gochu-jeon (recipe page; technique adapted to air fryer per Maangchi's own website note).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large green bell peppers (or mild Korean peppers — putgochu), halved lengthways and deseeded
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- *Stuffing:* 250 g firm tofu, pressed and crumbled; 2 spring onions, thinly sliced; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp sesame oil; ½ tsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes); salt and pepper
- *Dipping sauce:* 2 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp rice vinegar; ½ tsp sesame oil; ½ tsp gochugaru

**Method**
1. Mix all stuffing ingredients in a bowl; season well.
2. Fill each pepper half with the stuffing, pressing it firmly and mounding it slightly above the rim.
3. Brush the stuffed peppers all over with neutral oil.
4. Insert the MEATER probe through the side wall of one pepper half into the thickest part of the flesh, avoiding the stuffing.
5. Air fry at **190 °C (374 °F) for 12–14 minutes**, until the pepper walls are tender and blistered and the stuffing is cooked through and lightly golden.
6. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F)** for the pepper flesh. The stuffing should be heated to a minimum of 75 °C at its centre.
7. Mix the dipping sauce ingredients. Serve the stuffed peppers immediately with the dipping sauce alongside. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (tender, savoury, mild heat).

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Piiman no Kinpira (Air-Fried Pepper Kinpira — Japanese tradition)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green and red bell peppers, deseeded and cut into 5 mm wide strips (julienned)
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (usukuchi-shoyu)
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish
- 1 tsp dried bonito flakes (katsuobushi), to finish (optional)

**Method**
1. Toss the pepper strips with sesame oil, neutral oil, soy sauce, mirin and sugar in a bowl; allow to marinate 5 minutes.
2. Place the strips in the air fryer basket in a single layer (if needed, cook in batches — do not crowd).
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into one of the thicker strips near the centre of the basket.
4. Air fry at **180 °C (356 °F) for 10–12 minutes**, shaking the basket once at 6 minutes, until the strips are tender, slightly caramelised and starting to crisp at the edges.
5. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Harumi's *kinpira* style aims for tender strips with a sweet-savoury glaze, slightly drier than pan-fried.
6. Transfer to a bowl; scatter sesame seeds and bonito flakes. Serve as a *obanzai* (small side dish) with rice. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (sweet-savoury, sesame-fragrant).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Air-Fried Stuffed Capsicum (Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010); air fryer technique adapted from her oven-stuffed pepper method.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 16–18 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium red or green bell peppers, tops cut off, deseeded (kept as cups)
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- *Stuffing:* 200 g cooked basmati rice; 1 medium onion (finely diced, sautéed golden); 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp garam masala; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli; 1 tsp salt; 15 g fresh coriander leaves
- Lemon juice, to serve

**Method**
1. Combine all stuffing ingredients in a bowl; adjust seasoning.
2. Brush the outside of each pepper cup with oil. Fill tightly with the rice stuffing.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of one pepper, into the thickest wall, with the tip in the flesh (not in the stuffing).
4. Stand peppers upright in the air fryer basket; air fry at **190 °C (374 °F) for 16–18 minutes**, until the pepper walls are tender and the skin is lightly blistered.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F)** — the pepper wall should be fully soft; the stuffing should be steaming hot.
6. Squeeze lemon juice over each pepper. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (spiced rice–filled, fragrant, lemon-bright).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Peppers with Tahini (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014); technique adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large bell peppers (mixed colours), quartered, deseeded
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp sweet smoked paprika
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- *Tahini drizzle:* 3 tbsp tahini; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove (grated); 2–3 tbsp cold water; salt to thin
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish
- 10 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Toss the pepper quarters in olive oil, cumin, paprika and salt. Place in the air fryer basket skin-side-up, in a single layer.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the edge of one quarter into the thickest part of the flesh wall.
3. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 15–18 minutes** until the skins are blistered and caramelised and the flesh is tender.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).**
5. Whisk tahini with lemon juice, garlic and enough water to make a pourable sauce. Season with salt.
6. Arrange peppers on a platter; drizzle tahini over; scatter pomegranate seeds and parsley. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (sweet, smoky-edged, tahini-creamy).
