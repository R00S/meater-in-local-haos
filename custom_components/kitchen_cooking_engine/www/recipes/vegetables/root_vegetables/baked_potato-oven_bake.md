# Baked Potato × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole baked potato (*Solanum tuberosum*) in the conventional oven is the archetype of long, slow dry-heat cooking: 60–80 minutes at 200–220 °C, producing a deeply crispy, salt-encrusted skin and a fluffy, steamy interior. The internal temperature at complete doneness is 96–98 °C (205–208 °F) — below this, the centre remains dense and waxy; above it, the texture is optimal and floury. Russet (Idaho) and baking potatoes (high-starch varieties) achieve the best results; waxy varieties produce a gluey, dense result. A MEATER probe is inserted along the long axis into the geometric centre of the potato before it goes in the oven.

## Source recipes

### 1. Julia Child — Pommes de Terre Rôties au Four (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *The French Chef Cookbook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1968); baked potato with French flavour treatments.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 60–75 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large baking potatoes (≈ 250 g each), scrubbed; 1 tbsp coarse sea salt; *filling:* 40 g (3 tbsp) crème fraîche; 20 g unsalted butter; 2 tbsp fresh chives; fine salt; white pepper; 30 g Gruyère, grated

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Pierce potatoes all over. Rub the skins with a thin film of oil and the coarse sea salt.
2. Insert MEATER probe along long axis into the thickest centre.
3. Place directly on oven rack (not on a tray — Child specifies: "the oven floor heat must circulate under the potato for an evenly crisped skin"). Bake 60–75 minutes.
4. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split immediately; insert a fork to fluff the interior. Dot with butter; spoon crème fraîche; scatter chives and Gruyère; season. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (floury-fluffy, crispy skin, French-inflected).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Masala Baked Potato (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 60–75 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large potatoes; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp fine salt; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp cumin; ½ tsp coriander
- *Filling:* 2 tbsp plain yoghurt; 1 tsp garam masala; fresh coriander; green chilli; lemon juice

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Pierce potatoes; rub with spiced oil. Insert MEATER probe along long axis.
2. Bake directly on oven rack 60–75 min.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split and fill. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (spiced skin, tangy yoghurt filling).

---

### 3. Nigel Slater — Perfect Baked Potato (British tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Tender Vol. I* (4th Estate, 2009)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 70–80 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large Maris Piper potatoes (≈ 250 g each); coarse sea salt; olive oil; *toppings:* salted butter; soured cream; mature Cheddar; spring onions

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Slater specifies: "rub the skins with olive oil and enough salt to form a visible crust — this creates the crispy exterior". Insert MEATER probe.
2. Bake on oven rack 70–80 min. Slater's note: "there is no shortcut — the perfect baked potato needs time."
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; fluff with fork; add all toppings. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (quintessential British baked potato).

---

### 4. Deborah Madison — Herb-Stuffed Baked Potato (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 65–75 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Russet potatoes; 2 tbsp olive oil; salt; *filling:* 120 g ricotta; 2 tbsp fresh thyme; 1 garlic clove grated; zest of 1 lemon; salt and pepper; 30 g Parmesan

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Pierce and rub potatoes with oil and salt. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Bake 65–75 min.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; scoop out interior; mix with ricotta filling; restuff; scatter Parmesan. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (fluffy, herb-ricotta filling, elegant American).
