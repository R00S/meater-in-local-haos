# Roasted Beets × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole beetroot (*Beta vulgaris*) oven-roasted, still in the skin, at 190–200 °C is the most flavour-preserving technique for beets: the skin protects the flesh from direct heat, preventing the water-soluble betalain pigments (which give beets their vibrant red-purple colour) from leaching out, as they would during boiling. Small-to-medium beets (7–10 cm diameter) take 50–70 minutes; large beets (>10 cm) take 70–90 minutes. Individual foil-wrapped beets cook slightly faster. Pull temperature: 92–96 °C (198–205 °F) at the thickest centre — at this point a skewer inserts with no resistance. MEATER probe inserted through the root end into the geometric centre.

## Styckesprofil

Hel rödbeta (*Beta vulgaris*) ugnsrostad fortfarande i skalet vid 190–200 °C är den mest smakbevarande tekniken: skalet skyddar köttet från direkt värme, förhindrar fuktförlust och koncentrerar den naturliga sötan. 50–70 minuter beroende på storlek. Klar när genomstekt och lätt skrumpna; skalet avlägsnas enkelt efter tillagning.

## Source recipes

### 1. Labu Pokhté (Persian Oven-Roasted Beets with Orange and Walnuts) (inspired by Najmieh Batmanglij)
**Inspired by**: Najmieh Batmanglij, *Food of Life: Ancient Persian and Modern Iranian Cooking* (Mage Publishers, 2011)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 60–75 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium beetroots (≈ 200 g each), unpeeled, scrubbed; 1 tbsp neutral oil; *dressing (after roasting):* juice and zest of 1 large orange; 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses; 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; ½ tsp ground cinnamon; ½ tsp fine salt; 30 g walnuts, roughly chopped; 15 g flat-leaf parsley

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Rub beets with oil; wrap each loosely in foil. Place on a baking sheet.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the root end into the geometric centre of the largest beet.
3. Roast 60–75 minutes until completely tender.
4. **Pull at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F).** Open foil carefully (steam); allow to cool 10 minutes until handleable. Rub off the skin with a paper towel. Slice into wedges.
5. Mix dressing; toss beets while still warm. Top with walnuts and parsley. Final serving temperature: **warm to room temperature** (Batmanglij serves this at room temperature as a *meze* salad).

### 2. Roasted Beets with Yoghurt and Walnut Dukka (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 60–75 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium beetroots, unpeeled; 1 tbsp oil; *dressing:* 150 g Greek yoghurt; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tbsp lemon juice; salt; 30 g walnut dukka (or toasted walnuts + za'atar); 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 15 g fresh mint

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Wrap beets in foil; place on baking sheet. Insert MEATER probe through root end of largest beet into its centre.
2. Roast 60–75 min until a skewer meets no resistance.
3. **Pull at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F).** Cool, peel, slice into wedges.
4. Spread yoghurt on a platter; pile beets; drizzle olive oil; scatter dukka and mint. Final serving: **warm to room temperature** (intensely earthy, yoghurt-cool, jewel-coloured).

### 3. Ugnsrostade Rödbetor (inspired by Magnus Nilsson)
**Inspired by**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 55–70 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium beetroots, unpeeled; 2 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil; 1 tsp flaked sea salt; 1 tbsp red-wine vinegar; *dressing:* 60 g crème fraîche; 1 tbsp fresh horseradish (or 1 tsp prepared); ½ tsp lemon juice; salt; 15 g fresh dill

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Toss beets with rapeseed oil and salt; place on baking sheet. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 55–70 min until completely tender.
3. **Pull at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F).** Cool, peel, slice or quarter. Drizzle with red-wine vinegar.
4. Mix horseradish crème fraîche; serve alongside. Scatter dill. Final serving: **warm to room temperature** (earthy, Nordic horseradish-cream, dill-bright).

---

### 4. Roasted Beets with Balsamic and Goat Cheese (inspired by Deborah Madison)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 60–75 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium mixed beetroots (red and golden if available); 1 tbsp olive oil; *dressing:* 2 tbsp aged balsamic vinegar; 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; salt and pepper; 80 g soft goat cheese; 30 g toasted walnuts; 15 g flat-leaf parsley or fresh thyme

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Wrap individual beets in foil; roast on a sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe into largest beet through root end.
2. Roast 60–75 min. **Pull at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F).** Cool; peel; slice into wedges.
3. Drizzle balsamic and olive oil; season. Crumble goat cheese over; scatter walnuts and herbs. Final serving: **warm to room temperature** (earthy, balsamic-sweet, creamy goat cheese, American).
