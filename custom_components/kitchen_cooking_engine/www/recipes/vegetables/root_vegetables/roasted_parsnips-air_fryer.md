# Roasted Parsnips × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Parsnips (*Pastinaca sativa*) in the air fryer become golden, caramelised and crispy at the tips in 16–20 minutes at 190–200 °C — faster than oven roasting (25–30 minutes). Parsnips have a high natural sugar content (≈ 12 g/100 g — significantly higher than carrots) and caramelise readily. They should be cut into batons or long wedges, oil-coated and placed in a single layer. Pull temperature: 86–90 °C (187–194 °F) for caramelised, fluffy-interior parsnips. MEATER probe inserted along the long axis of the thickest baton.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nigel Slater — Air-Fried Honey-Glazed Parsnips (British tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Tender Vol. I* (4th Estate, 2009); honey-roasted parsnip technique adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 18–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g parsnips, peeled and cut into long batons or quarters; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 2 tbsp runny honey; 1 tsp whole grain mustard; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper; 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves

**Method**
1. Mix oil, honey and mustard. Toss parsnips in the mixture; season with salt and pepper.
2. Place in air fryer basket. Insert MEATER probe along the long axis of the thickest baton.
3. Air fry at **190 °C for 16–18 min**, shaking once at 9 min.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Scatter thyme. Slater: "Honey-glazed parsnips are at their best with a caramelised, almost toffee-like crust and a fluffy interior." Final serving: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (caramelised, honey-sweet, thyme-fragrant).

### 2. Magnus Nilsson — Air-Fried Parsnips with Brown Butter (Nordic / Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015); Swedish root vegetable technique.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 18–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g parsnips, peeled and cut into long batons; 2 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil; 1 tsp flaked sea salt; *brown butter:* 40 g unsalted butter cooked to nut-brown; 1 tbsp chives, chopped

**Method**
1. Toss parsnips with rapeseed oil and flaked salt. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Air fry at **190 °C for 18–20 min**, shaking once.
3. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Make brown butter; drizzle over. Scatter chives. Final serving: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (golden, nutty-butter, Nordic simplicity).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Air-Fried Spiced Parsnips (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); spice roasting technique adapted for parsnips in air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 18–20 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g parsnips, peeled and cut into batons; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp cumin seeds; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli; ½ tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp fine salt; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Toss parsnips with oil and spices. Insert MEATER probe. Air fry at **200 °C for 18–20 min**, shaking once.
2. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant).

---

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Parsnips with Za'atar and Labneh (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014); adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 18–20 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g parsnips, peeled into long batons; 3 tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp cumin; salt; *labneh:* 100 g labneh; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tbsp za'atar; olive oil to drizzle; pomegranate seeds and mint to finish

**Method**
1. Toss parsnips with oil, cumin, salt. Insert MEATER probe. Air fry at **200 °C for 18–20 min**, shaking once.
2. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Spread labneh on platter; pile parsnips; dust za'atar; scatter pomegranate and mint. Final serving: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (golden, za'atar-bright, creamy labneh).
