# Roasted Parsnips × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Parsnips (*Pastinaca sativa*) oven-roasted at 190–210 °C become deeply caramelised, golden at the tips and fluffy inside in 25–35 minutes. High-starch, high-sugar content (12 g/100 g natural sugars) makes parsnips excellent roasters — their surface browns readily and their interior fluffs. Parsnips are classically roasted alongside a joint in the UK, but also served solo with honey, miso, or spiced glazes in the contemporary global kitchen. Pull temperature: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F). MEATER probe along the long axis of the thickest piece.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nigel Slater — Honey-Roasted Parsnips with Mustard (British tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Tender Vol. I* (4th Estate, 2009)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g parsnips, peeled and cut into long batons; 3 tbsp sunflower or goose fat (Slater uses goose fat for Sunday roast context); 2 tbsp runny honey; 1 tbsp whole grain mustard; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper; 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves; optional: 1 tbsp fresh rosemary

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Mix fat, honey and mustard. Toss parsnips; spread on a large sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe along the thickest baton axis.
2. Roast 28–32 min, turning once at 15 min, until deeply caramelised and golden.
3. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Scatter thyme and rosemary. Slater: "The parsnip, when perfectly roasted, has a toffee-like, almost burnt-sugar crust and a soft, floury interior." Final serving: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (caramelised, honey-sweet, mustard-fragrant, British roast classic).

### 2. Magnus Nilsson — Ugnsrostade Palsternackor (Nordic / Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 28–32 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g parsnips, peeled and cut into long batons; 3 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil; 1 tsp flaked sea salt; freshly ground white pepper; *brown butter sauce:* 40 g butter cooked to nut-brown; 1 tbsp chives, chopped; ½ tsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Toss parsnips with rapeseed oil, salt and pepper; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 28–32 min, turning once.
3. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Make brown butter; drizzle over; scatter chives; squeeze lemon. Final serving: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (golden, nutty-butter, Nordic).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Masala Roasted Parsnips (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 28–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g parsnips, peeled and cut into batons; 3 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp cumin seeds; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli; 1 tsp fine salt; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C. Toss parsnips with oil and spices; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 28–30 min, turning once.
3. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (spiced, golden, fragrant).

---

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Parsnips with Miso and Sesame (Israeli / East Asian fusion tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 28–32 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g parsnips, halved lengthwise; *miso glaze:* 2 tbsp white (shiro) miso; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tbsp honey; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tbsp water; toasted sesame seeds and spring onion to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Brush parsnips with half the miso glaze; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 20–22 min; brush with remaining glaze; return 8–10 more minutes until deeply caramelised.
3. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (caramelised, miso-sweet, sesame-fragrant).
