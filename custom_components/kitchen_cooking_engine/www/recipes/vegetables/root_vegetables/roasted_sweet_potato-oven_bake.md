# Roasted Sweet Potato × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole baked sweet potato (*Ipomoea batatas*) in the conventional oven at 190–200 °C achieves a deeply caramelised, slightly wrinkled skin and a soft, jammy interior in 50–70 minutes depending on size. The low-and-slow heat allows the amylase enzymes in the sweet potato to convert starches to sugars, producing an intensely sweet result (particularly with Japanese *satsuma-imo* varieties). Pull temperature: 96–98 °C (205–208 °F) at the thickest centre. MEATER probe along long axis into the geometric centre.

## Styckesprofil

Hel bakad sötpotatis (*Ipomoea batatas*) i konventionell ugn vid 190–200 °C uppnår en djupt karameliserad, lätt skrumpna yta och mjuk, syltliknande insida på 50–70 minuter. Klar när insidan är 98–100 °C och sötpotatisskalet är knaprig och karameliserad.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Whole Baked Yakiimo (Japanese tradition)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); the traditional whole baked Japanese sweet potato.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 min · **Cook**: 55–70 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Japanese sweet potatoes (*satsuma-imo* or *naruto-kintoki*, ≈ 200–250 g each), scrubbed; coarse sea salt (for the oven floor if desired — the traditional method uses heated stones, but oven racks work perfectly)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. No oil — the skin crisps naturally in Japanese style. Insert MEATER probe along the long axis into the geometric centre.
2. Place directly on the oven rack. Bake 55–70 minutes. Harumi notes: "The potato is ready when a caramel fragrance fills the kitchen and the skin looks slightly wrinkled."
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Allow to rest 10 minutes.
4. Split and eat plain (Japanese style) or with a pinch of flaked salt. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (intensely sweet, jammy, the quintessential Japanese *yakiimo* experience).

### 2. Maangchi — Korean Oven Baked Goguma (Korean tradition)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); the classic Korean baked sweet potato — a staple street food sold from wheeled vending carts in winter.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 min · **Cook**: 55–65 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Korean sweet potatoes (*goguma*, ≈ 200–250 g each), scrubbed; rock salt, to scatter on the baking sheet (Maangchi specifies rock salt for its insulating and heat-retaining properties — a nod to the street-cart method)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Scatter rock salt in a thin layer on a sheet pan; nestle potatoes on the salt. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest potato into geometric centre.
2. Bake 55–65 minutes, turning once at 30 min.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Maangchi: "The skin should be slightly charred at the bottom; the flesh bright orange and jammy — intensely sweet." Rest 10 min.
4. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (sweet, soft, Korean street food character — eat plain or with a tiny pinch of salt).

---

### 3. Diana Kennedy — Camotes al Horno (Mexican tradition)
**Inspired by**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 55–65 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 orange-fleshed sweet potatoes (≈ 200 g each), scrubbed; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; *accompaniment:* lime wedges; chilli flakes; fine salt; optionally: crema mexicana or crumbled queso fresco

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Pierce potatoes; rub with oil. Insert MEATER probe along axis.
2. Bake directly on oven rack 55–65 min.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; squeeze lime; add chilli flakes and salt. Optionally add crema and cheese. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (soft, caramelised, lime-bright, Mexican character).

---

### 4. Claudia Roden — Batata Mchiya (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Inspired by**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque: A Taste of Morocco, Turkey and Lebanon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005); North African baked sweet potato with argan oil and cumin.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 55–65 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sweet potatoes (≈ 200 g each); *dressing:* 2 tbsp argan oil (or good-quality extra-virgin olive oil); 1 tsp ground cumin; pinch of cayenne; ½ tsp fine salt; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g fresh coriander, chopped

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Pierce potatoes; place on foil-lined tray. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Bake 55–65 min. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; drizzle argan oil; dust cumin and cayenne; squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (caramelised, argan oil-fragrant, Moroccan spiced).
