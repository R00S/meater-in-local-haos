# Acorn Squash × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Acorn squash (*Cucurbita pepo* var. *turbinata*) oven-baked whole or halved at 180–200 °C is a classic American autumn preparation. Halved and seed-scooped, then baked cut-face-down (30–40 min) or cut-face-up with a filling (35–50 min). Pull temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) at the thickest flesh wall. MEATER probe inserted through the skin into the thickest part of the flesh wall.

## Styckesprofil

Ekollon-squash (*Cucurbita pepo* var. *turbinata*) ugnsbakas hel eller halverad vid 180–200 °C. Halverad och urholkad, sedan bakad med snittytan ned. 40–50 minuter. Klar när köttet är genomstekt och lätt brynt vid kanterna.

## Source recipes

### 1. Deborah Madison — Baked Acorn Squash with Brown Sugar and Butter (American vegetarian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45–55 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash (≈ 600 g each), halved and seeds scooped; 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter; 2 tbsp brown sugar; ½ tsp cinnamon; pinch of freshly grated nutmeg; ½ tsp fine sea salt; maple syrup to drizzle at service

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Place squash halves cut-face-up on a baking sheet. Place a dot of butter in each cavity; sprinkle with brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg and salt.
2. Insert MEATER probe through the skin of the thickest squash half into the flesh wall.
3. Bake 45–55 minutes until the flesh is completely tender and the cut face is golden-caramelised.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Drizzle with maple syrup. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (caramelised, buttery-sweet, American autumn classic).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Calabaza Asada con Piloncillo (Mexican tradition)
**Inspired by**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45–55 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds scooped; 60 g piloncillo (or dark brown sugar), crumbled; 1 tsp ground cinnamon; 1 tbsp unsalted butter; lime juice, to finish; crema mexicana (optional)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Place squash halves cut-face-up. Fill cavities with piloncillo, cinnamon and butter. Insert MEATER probe through skin into flesh.
2. Bake 45–55 min until tender and caramelised.
3. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Squeeze lime; add crema if desired. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (caramelised, piloncillo-sweet, Mexican).

---

### 3. Najmieh Batmanglij — Kadu Pokhté (Persian Baked Squash with Walnuts)
**Inspired by**: Najmieh Batmanglij, *Food of Life* (Mage Publishers, 2011)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45–55 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds scooped; 2 tbsp vegetable oil; ½ tsp ground cinnamon; ¼ tsp ground cardamom; 2 tbsp honey; 30 g walnuts, roughly chopped; 15 g flat-leaf parsley; pomegranate seeds to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Brush cut faces with oil; season. Insert MEATER probe through skin into flesh wall.
2. Bake cut-face-down 25 min; flip; brush with cinnamon-honey mixture; bake 20–25 more min.
3. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Scatter walnuts, parsley and pomegranate seeds. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (spiced, honey-sweet, Persian).

---

### 4. Claudia Roden — Baked Squash with Harissa (North African tradition)
**Inspired by**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45–55 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, halved, seeds scooped; 2 tbsp olive oil; 1 tbsp harissa; 1 tsp honey; ½ tsp cumin; salt; 15 g fresh coriander; lemon juice

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Mix oil, harissa, honey, cumin, salt; brush over cut faces and cavities. Insert MEATER probe through skin into flesh.
2. Bake cut-face-up 45–55 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (spiced, harissa-fragrant, North African).
