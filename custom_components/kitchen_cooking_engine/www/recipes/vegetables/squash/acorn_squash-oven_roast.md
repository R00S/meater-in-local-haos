# Acorn Squash × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Acorn squash (*Cucurbita pepo*) oven-roasted in wedges or half-shells at 200–220 °C develops caramelised, golden cut faces in 30–40 minutes. Wedges (the squash cut into 6–8 wedges) give maximum surface browning; half-shells give a more dramatic presentation. Pull temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) at the thickest flesh wall. MEATER probe through the skin into the thickest flesh part.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Acorn Squash with Za'atar and Pomegranate (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, cut into wedges (skin on); 3 tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp cumin; salt; 1 tbsp za'atar; 30 g pomegranate seeds; 100 g labneh; 15 g fresh mint; chilli flakes

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss wedges with oil, cumin, salt; place flat-face-down on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe through skin into thickest flesh.
2. Roast 35–40 min until golden. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Spread labneh on platter; pile wedges; dust za'atar; scatter pomegranate, mint, chilli. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (caramelised, za'atar-bright, jewel-beautiful).

### 2. Magnus Nilsson — Roasted Squash with Rapeseed Oil (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, cut into wedges; 3 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil; 1 tsp flaked sea salt; white pepper; 1 tbsp fresh dill; 60 g crème fraîche; ½ tsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Toss wedges with rapeseed oil; season. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 35–40 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Scatter dill; serve with crème fraîche and lemon. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (golden, Nordic simplicity).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Masala Roasted Squash Wedges (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, cut into wedges; 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp chilli; 1 tsp coriander; 1 tsp salt; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C. Toss wedges with spiced oil; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 35–40 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (spiced, golden).

---

### 4. Deborah Madison — Roasted Squash with Walnut-Herb Pesto (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 acorn squash, cut into wedges; 2 tbsp olive oil; salt; *pesto:* 30 g walnuts; 15 g fresh sage; 1 garlic clove; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt; 30 g Parmesan, grated

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C. Toss wedges with oil; roast 35–40 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Blend pesto; drizzle over. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (caramelised, walnut-sage-fragrant).
