# Butternut Squash × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Butternut squash (*Cucurbita moschata*) oven-roasted in halves or chunks at 190–220 °C is one of the most widely used autumn/winter vegetable preparations globally. Halves roasted cut-face-down for 40–50 min; chunks in 25–35 min. Pull temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) at the thickest flesh wall. MEATER probe through the skin into the thickest flesh.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Butternut with Tahini and Sage (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min (chunks) · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 large butternut squash (≈ 1 kg), peeled and cut into 4 cm chunks; 3 tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp cumin; salt; *tahini:* 3 tbsp tahini; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove grated; 2 tbsp water; 30 g pomegranate seeds; 10 g fresh sage (fried in olive oil until crisp)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss chunks with olive oil, cumin, salt; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest chunk.
2. Roast 35–40 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Drizzle tahini; scatter pomegranate and crispy sage. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (golden, tahini-creamy, sage-crispy, jewel-beautiful).

### 2. Claudia Roden — Roasted Butternut with Chermoula (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg butternut squash, cut into chunks; *chermoula:* 3 tbsp olive oil; 2 garlic cloves; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp paprika; ½ tsp coriander; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g coriander; 1 tsp salt

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C. Toss chunks in chermoula; roast 35–40 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Scatter fresh coriander. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (spiced, golden, North African).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Kaddu ki Sabzi Roast (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg butternut squash, cut into chunks; 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin seeds; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp chilli; 1 tsp coriander; 1 tsp salt; ½ tsp garam masala; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C. Toss chunks with spiced oil; roast 35–40 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Dust with garam masala; squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (spiced, golden, Indian).

---

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Zucca al Forno con Salvia (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg butternut squash, cut into large wedges; 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 6 fresh sage leaves; 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper; 30 g Parmesan, grated

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Toss wedges with oil, salt, pepper; roast 30 min. Add sage and garlic; roast 5–8 more min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Scatter Parmesan. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (golden, sage-fragrant, Parmesan-crusted).
