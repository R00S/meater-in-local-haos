# Butternut Squash × Pressure Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Butternut squash (*Cucurbita moschata*) in a pressure cooker cooks in 8–12 minutes at high pressure (cubed), producing very soft, fully cooked flesh suitable for soups and purées. Pull temperature (measured via MEATER before sealing, or in the depressurised pot): 95–98 °C (203–208 °F). MEATER probe into the thickest cube before closing the lid.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madhur Jaffrey — Pressure-Cooked Kaddu for Dal (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010); pressure-cooker squash for Indian dal or sabzi.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min at pressure · **Rest**: 10 min (natural release)

**Ingredients**
- 800 g butternut squash, peeled and cut into 4 cm cubes; 250 ml water; 1 tsp turmeric; 1 tsp fine salt; *tadka after:* 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin seeds; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp chilli; 15 g fresh coriander; lemon juice

**Method**
1. Place squash cubes in pressure cooker with water, turmeric and salt. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest cube. Seal lid.
2. Cook at high pressure for 8–10 minutes. Natural release 10 minutes.
3. **Pull at 95–98 °C (203–208 °F)** (read from MEATER after depressurising). Prepare tadka; pour over squash; add lemon juice and coriander. Final serving: **soft, for mashing or as a side** — the squash is fully cooked through and ready to use in any recipe.

### 2. Fuchsia Dunlop — Pressure-Cooked Squash Congee (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012); Chinese pressure-cooked squash and rice.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12 min at pressure · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g butternut squash, peeled and cut into 3 cm cubes; 150 g (¾ cup) jasmine rice; 1 L water; 1 tsp fine salt; 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp sesame oil; spring onion; white pepper

**Method**
1. Combine squash, rice, water and salt in pressure cooker. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest squash cube. Seal.
2. Cook at high pressure 12 min; natural release 10 min. **Pull at 95–98 °C (203–208 °F).**
3. The squash will be very soft and partly dissolved into the rice congee. Season with soy sauce, sesame oil, spring onion and white pepper. Final serving: **soft, warming congee porridge**.

---

### 3. Claudia Roden — Pressure-Cooked Squash with Ras el Hanout (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005); North African spiced squash.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min at pressure · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g butternut squash, cut into large cubes; 200 ml water; 1 tsp ras el hanout; ½ tsp cumin; 1 tsp salt; 2 tbsp olive oil; 30 g dried apricots, sliced; 30 g toasted almonds; 15 g fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Place squash with water, ras el hanout, cumin, salt and olive oil in pressure cooker. Insert MEATER probe. Seal.
2. Cook 8 min at high pressure; natural release 10 min. **Pull at 95–98 °C (203–208 °F).**
3. Scatter apricots, almonds and coriander. Final serving: **soft, spiced, Moroccan meze style**.

---

### 4. Deborah Madison — Pressure-Cooked Squash Soup Base (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013); pressure-cooked squash for soup.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10 min at pressure · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg butternut squash, peeled and cubed; 1 onion, roughly chopped; 2 garlic cloves; 750 ml vegetable stock; 1 tsp fine salt; ½ tsp white pepper; *optional finish:* 120 ml cream; nutmeg; chives

**Method**
1. Combine all in pressure cooker. Insert MEATER probe into thickest cube. Seal.
2. Cook 10 min at high pressure; natural release 10 min. **Pull at 95–98 °C (203–208 °F).**
3. Blend smooth; season; add cream and nutmeg if using. Scatter chives. Final serving: **silky soup, 70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** after blending and reheating gently.
