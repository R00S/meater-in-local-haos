# Butternut Squash × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Butternut squash (*Cucurbita moschata*) cut into 3–4 cm cubes and steamed over boiling water produces silky, sweet flesh ideal as a side dish or for mashing and pureeing. Steaming time 12–18 minutes depending on cube size. Pull temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) at the centre of the largest cube. MEATER probe inserted into the largest cube, tip centred. Because the steaming environment is 100 °C, pull promptly once target temp is reached to avoid over-cooking.

## Styckesprofil

Butternut-squash (*Cucurbita moschata*) skuren i 3–4 cm tärningar och ångad ovanför kokande vatten ger silkeslent, sött kött idealiskt som tillbehör eller för mosning och purering. Ånga i 15–20 minuter. Klar när fullständigt genomstekt och lätt att genomborra med gaffel.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steamed Butternut with Ginger and Cumin (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g butternut squash, peeled and cut into 3 cm cubes; 2 tbsp ghee; 1 tsp cumin seeds; 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp chilli flakes; salt; lemon juice; 15 g fresh coriander, chopped

**Method**
1. Fill a large pot with 5 cm water and bring to the boil; place a steamer basket over the water. Add butternut cubes. Insert MEATER probe into the largest cube.
2. Cover and steam 12–15 minutes. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
3. Meanwhile heat ghee; splutter cumin seeds; add ginger, turmeric, coriander, chilli and salt.
4. Toss steamed squash in spiced ghee. Squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (silky, spiced, Indian).

### 2. Steamed Nangua with Doubanjiang Dressing (inspired by Fuchsia Dunlop)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (Bloomsbury, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g butternut squash, cut into 4 cm cubes (skin left on); 1 tbsp doubanjiang (chilli bean paste), finely chopped; 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp black rice vinegar; 2 spring onions, finely sliced; 10 g fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Set up a bamboo steamer over a wok of simmering water. Arrange cubes skin-side-down in the steamer. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest cube flesh.
2. Steam 15–18 minutes. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
3. Mix doubanjiang, sesame oil, soy sauce and vinegar into a dressing. Spoon over cubes. Scatter spring onion and coriander. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (silky, spicy, Sichuan).

---

### 3. Kabocha no Nimono-style Steamed Squash (inspired by Harumi Kurihara)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g butternut squash, cut into 4 cm cubes (skin left on); 3 tbsp dashi; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tbsp sake; 1 tsp light soy sauce; ½ tsp sugar; pinch of salt; 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Set a steamer basket over boiling water. Place cubes skin-side-down. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Steam 12–15 minutes. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
3. Warm dashi, mirin, sake, soy and sugar until sugar dissolves; pour over cubes to glaze. Scatter sesame. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (sweet-savoury, glazed, Japanese).

---

### 4. Courge à la Vapeur (inspired by Julia Child)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. 2* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1970)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g butternut squash, peeled and cut into 3 cm cubes; 30 g unsalted butter; ½ tsp fine sea salt; white pepper; freshly grated nutmeg; 1 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, chopped; 1 tsp chives, finely cut

**Method**
1. Steam cubes over boiling water 12–15 minutes. Insert MEATER probe into the largest cube.
2. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
3. Toss immediately with butter, salt, white pepper and nutmeg until the butter emulsifies into a light glaze. Scatter parsley and chives. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (buttery, nutmeg-fragrant, French classic).
