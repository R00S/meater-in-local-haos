# Spaghetti Squash × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Spaghetti squash (*Cucurbita pepo*) is baked whole or halved at 180–200 °C until the flesh separates into noodle-like strands when scraped with a fork. Halved baked cut-face-down (35–45 min); whole baked (50–65 min with fork pricks). Pull temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) at the thickest flesh wall. MEATER probe inserted through the skin into the flesh for whole baking, or through the skin of a half. The defining visual cue — flesh pulling into strands — coincides with 90–95 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Deborah Madison — Baked Spaghetti Squash with Brown Butter and Parmesan (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 large spaghetti squash (≈ 1.2 kg), halved lengthways, seeds scooped; 2 tbsp olive oil; salt; 40 g unsalted butter; 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves; 40 g Parmesan, grated; black pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Brush cut faces with olive oil; season. Place cut-face-down on a baking sheet. Insert MEATER probe through the skin into the flesh.
2. Bake 40–50 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).**
3. Rest 5 min; flip cut-face-up and scrape strands with a fork. Brown butter in a small pan with thyme; drizzle over strands; scatter Parmesan and pepper. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (strand-tender, brown-butter-fragrant, American).

### 2. Marcella Hazan — Zucca Spaghetti al Forno (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 spaghetti squash (≈ 1.2 kg), halved; 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; 400 g crushed San Marzano tomatoes; salt; black pepper; 8 fresh basil leaves; 60 g ricotta salata, crumbled

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Brush cut faces with 2 tbsp oil; season. Place cut-face-down on a baking sheet. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Bake 40–50 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Flip and scrape flesh into strands.
3. Meanwhile warm remaining oil, add garlic, then tomatoes; simmer 15 min. Toss strands in sauce; scatter basil and ricotta salata. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (strand-tender, Italian classic).

---

### 3. Diana Kennedy — Calabaza Espagueti Horneada (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 spaghetti squash (≈ 1.2 kg), halved; 2 tbsp vegetable oil; salt; 2 tbsp crema mexicana; 1 tsp chipotle en adobo, minced; 60 g queso fresco, crumbled; 15 g epazote or fresh coriander; lime wedges

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C. Brush cut faces with oil; season; place cut-face-down. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Bake 40–50 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Flip and scrape strands.
3. Mix crema with chipotle; toss strands. Scatter queso fresco and epazote; squeeze lime. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (smoky-creamy, Mexican).

---

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Baked Spaghetti Squash with Tahini and Chilli (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 spaghetti squash (≈ 1.2 kg), halved; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt; *tahini sauce:* 3 tbsp tahini; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove, grated; 2–3 tbsp water; 1 tsp chilli flakes; 15 g flat-leaf parsley; pomegranate seeds

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Brush cut faces with oil; season; place cut-face-down. Insert MEATER probe through skin.
2. Bake 40–50 min. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Flip; scrape into strands.
3. Whisk tahini sauce; drizzle over strands. Scatter chilli, parsley and pomegranate. Final serving: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tahini-rich, jewel-bright, Israeli).
