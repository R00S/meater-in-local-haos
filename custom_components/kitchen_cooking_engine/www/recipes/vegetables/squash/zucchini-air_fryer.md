# Zucchini × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Zucchini (*Cucurbita pepo* var. *cylindrica*) cut into 1–2 cm rounds, half-moons or spears air-fried at 190–200 °C for 8–12 minutes produces golden, slightly crisp exteriors with tender centres. Because zucchini is mostly water, the air fryer's forced hot air quickly drives off surface moisture to give browning that is difficult to achieve in an oven. Pull temperature: 82–85 °C (180–185 °F) at the thickest piece — at this temperature the flesh is fully tender while still holding its shape. MEATER probe inserted lengthwise into the thickest spear or along the flat face of the largest round.

## Styckesprofil

Zucchini (*Cucurbita pepo* var. *cylindrica*) skuren i 1–2 cm rundlar, halvmånar eller stavar airfryed vid 190–200 °C i 8–12 minuter ger gyllenbrun, lätt knaprig exteriör med mör insida. Klar när gyllenbrun och genomstekt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Air-Fried Hobak Jeon Style Zucchini (inspired by Maangchi)
**Inspired by**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into 1 cm rounds; 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chilli flakes); 1 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds; 1 tsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp rice vinegar; 2 spring onions, finely sliced; cooking oil spray

**Method**
1. Toss rounds with sesame oil and gochugaru. Spray air fryer basket with oil. Arrange rounds in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest round.
2. Air fry at 200 °C for 10–12 min, turning halfway. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
3. Toss with soy sauce and rice vinegar; scatter spring onion and sesame seeds. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, chilli-sesame, Korean).

### 2. Zucchini Karaage (inspired by Harumi Kurihara)
**Inspired by**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into thick spears; 2 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tsp sesame oil; 2 tbsp potato starch (katakuriko); cooking oil spray; lemon wedges; shichimi togarashi

**Method**
1. Marinate spears in soy, mirin and sesame oil 10 min. Drain; dust with potato starch. Spray with oil.
2. Arrange in a single layer in air fryer basket. Insert MEATER probe. Air fry 200 °C, 8–10 min, turning once. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
3. Serve with lemon and shichimi. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (crisp-coated, soy-sweet, Japanese).

---

### 3. Spiced Air-Fried Zucchini Rounds (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into 1.5 cm rounds; 1 tbsp oil; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; ½ tsp chilli powder; ½ tsp salt; 1 tsp amchoor (dried mango powder); 15 g fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Toss rounds with oil and spices. Arrange in air fryer basket. Insert MEATER probe. Air fry 195 °C for 10–12 min, turning halfway. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
2. Dust with amchoor; scatter coriander. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (spiced, golden, Indian).

---

### 4. Air-Fried Zucchini with Lemon Yoghurt (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into spears; 2 tbsp olive oil; salt; *lemon yoghurt:* 100 g thick Greek yoghurt; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove, grated; salt; 1 tsp chilli flakes; 15 g fresh mint; lemon zest

**Method**
1. Toss spears with olive oil and salt. Air fry at 200 °C for 10–12 min, turning halfway. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
2. Whisk yoghurt with lemon juice and garlic; spoon onto platter. Pile zucchini; scatter mint, chilli and lemon zest. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, lemon-yoghurt-bright, Israeli).
