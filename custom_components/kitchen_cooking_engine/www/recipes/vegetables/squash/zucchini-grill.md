# Zucchini × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Zucchini (*Cucurbita pepo*) cut lengthways into 1–1.5 cm planks or thick rounds is one of the defining summer grill vegetables worldwide. The high dry heat of the grill evaporates surface moisture rapidly, creating golden char marks and a tender interior. Grill time: 4–6 minutes per side over medium-high heat. Pull temperature: 82–85 °C (180–185 °F) at the thickest part — at this internal temp the flesh is fully tender with charred exterior while still holding structure. MEATER probe inserted along the long axis of the thickest plank with the tip at the centre.

## Styckesprofil

Zucchini (*Cucurbita pepo*) skuren längsmed i 1–1,5 cm plankor eller tjocka rundlar är en av de definierande sommargrillgrönsakerna världen över. Grilla oljad zucchini direkt på gallret vid hög värme i 3–4 minuter per sida. Klar när synliga grillränder och genomstekt.

## Source recipes

### 1. Zucchine alla Griglia con Aglio e Prezzemolo (inspired by Marcella Hazan)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g medium zucchini, sliced lengthways 1.2 cm thick; 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 2 garlic cloves, very finely sliced; 20 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped; salt; black pepper; 1 tbsp red wine vinegar (optional)

**Method**
1. Brush planks on both sides with 2 tbsp oil; season. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest plank along the long axis, tip at centre.
2. Grill over medium-high heat 4–6 min per side until clearly char-marked. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
3. Arrange on a platter; scatter garlic, parsley, remaining oil and a thread of vinegar. Rest 3 min before serving. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (charred, garlic-parsley-fragrant, Italian).

### 2. Grilled Zucchini with Herb Yoghurt and Chilli (inspired by Yotam Ottolenghi)
**Inspired by**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g zucchini, halved lengthways; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt; *herb yoghurt:* 150 g Greek yoghurt; 1 garlic clove, grated; 15 g fresh mint; 15 g fresh basil; 1 tbsp lemon juice; salt; 1 tsp chilli flakes; pomegranate seeds; extra virgin olive oil to drizzle

**Method**
1. Brush zucchini halves with olive oil; season. Insert MEATER probe. Grill 4–6 min per side. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
2. Blend yoghurt with garlic, mint, basil, lemon juice and salt. Spoon onto platter; pile zucchini; scatter chilli and pomegranate. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (charred, herb-yoghurt-bright, Israeli).

---

### 3. Masala Grilled Courgettes (inspired by Madhur Jaffrey)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g zucchini, sliced into thick rounds or planks; 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp salt; 1 tsp amchoor; 15 g coriander, chopped; lemon wedges

**Method**
1. Mix oil with spices (not amchoor) to a paste; coat zucchini. Insert MEATER probe. Grill 4–6 min per side. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
2. Dust with amchoor; scatter fresh coriander; squeeze lemon. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (spiced, char-marked, Indian).

---

### 4. Calabacín a la Parrilla con Romesco (inspired by José Andrés)
**Inspired by**: José Andrés, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g zucchini, halved lengthways; 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; salt; *romesco:* 2 roasted red peppers; 30 g toasted almonds; 1 garlic clove; 1 tbsp sherry vinegar; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 4 tbsp olive oil; salt

**Method**
1. Brush cut faces with oil; season. Insert MEATER probe along long axis of thickest half. Grill cut-face-down 4–5 min; flip 3–4 min. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).**
2. Blend romesco ingredients; serve alongside. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (charred, romesco-rich, Spanish).
