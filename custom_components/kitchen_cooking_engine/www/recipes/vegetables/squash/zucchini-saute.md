# Zucchini × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Zucchini (*Cucurbita pepo*) sliced into 1 cm rounds, half-moons or batons is one of the fastest and most universal vegetable sautés. The goal is dry-heat browning on the cut faces before the interior becomes mushy; this requires a hot pan, minimal stirring and working in batches if necessary. Sauté time: 6–10 minutes total in a single layer. Pull temperature: 82–85 °C (180–185 °F) — at this internal temperature the flesh is completely tender while the cut faces show golden colour. MEATER probe inserted horizontally through the thickest round from the cut face.

## Styckesprofil

Zucchini (*Cucurbita pepo*) skivad i 1 cm rundlar, halvmånar eller stavar är en av de snabbaste och mest universella grönsaksautéer. Målet är torrvärme-brynning av snittytan. Sautera i het olja i 6–8 minuter. Klar när gyllenbrun och genomstekt utan att vara mosig.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Stir-Fried Courgette with Black Bean Sauce (Chinese Sichuan tradition)
**Inspired by**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (Bloomsbury, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into batons; 1 tbsp fermented black beans, rinsed; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated; 2 tbsp groundnut oil; 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tsp sesame oil; 2 spring onions, sliced; ½ tsp chilli flakes (optional)

**Method**
1. Heat wok over high heat until smoking. Add oil; stir-fry garlic, ginger and black beans 20 seconds.
2. Add zucchini in a single layer; press flat 30 seconds undisturbed; stir-fry 3–4 min. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest baton.
3. Add wine and soy; toss 1 min. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden-edged, savoury-umami, Chinese).

### 2. Julia Child — Courgettes Sautées au Beurre (French tradition)
**Inspired by**: Julia Child, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. 2* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1970)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into 1 cm rounds; 30 g unsalted butter; 1 tbsp olive oil; 1 shallot, finely minced; salt; white pepper; 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped; ½ tsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Dry the rounds thoroughly on kitchen paper — moisture is the enemy of browning.
2. Heat butter and oil in a large sauté pan over medium-high. Add rounds in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe. Brown undisturbed 3–4 min; turn; cook 3–4 more min.
3. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Add shallot; toss 1 min. Season; scatter parsley; squeeze lemon. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, butter-shallot-fragrant, French classic).

---

### 3. Marcella Hazan — Zucchine Trifolate (Italian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g small zucchini, cut into 1 cm rounds; 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; 2 garlic cloves, finely sliced; salt; black pepper; 10 g fresh basil; 1 tsp lemon juice

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high. When shimmering, add rounds in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe. Cook undisturbed 3–4 min until golden underneath; turn; cook 3–4 min.
2. Add garlic in the last 2 min. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Season; add basil and lemon. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, garlic-basil-fragrant, Italian classic).

---

### 4. Madhur Jaffrey — Turai ki Sabzi (Indian tradition)
**Inspired by**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g zucchini, cut into 1.5 cm half-moons; 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin seeds; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp coriander; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp salt; 1 tsp amchoor; 15 g fresh coriander; lemon juice

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wok over high heat; splutter cumin seeds. Add zucchini in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe. Sauté 4–5 min without stirring to get colour; toss; cook 3–4 more min.
2. Add spices; toss 1 min. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Dust amchoor; scatter coriander; squeeze lemon. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, spiced, Indian).
