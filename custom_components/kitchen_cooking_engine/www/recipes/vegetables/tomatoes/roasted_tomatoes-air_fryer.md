# Roasted Tomatoes × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Tomatoes (*Solanum lycopersicum*) in the air fryer roast in 8–15 minutes depending on size and target: cherry tomatoes burst and caramelise in 8–10 minutes at 200 °C; halved plum tomatoes take 12–15 minutes to collapse and blister. The circulating air dehydrates the surface faster than a conventional oven, producing crisper, slightly wrinkled edges with concentrated sweetness. Air-fried tomatoes are particularly useful as fast toppings for pasta, pizza, bruschetta, or eggs. Pull temperature for burst cherry tomatoes (serving as-is) is 82–88 °C (180–190 °F); for halved plum tomatoes intended as a sauce base, 88–95 °C (190–203 °F). A MEATER probe is most practical in a larger tomato; for cherry tomatoes, rely on appearance (bursting, blistered) and time, or insert in the largest specimen. Carryover is negligible.

## Source recipes

### 1. Pailin Chongchitnant — Air-Fried Roasted Cherry Tomatoes for Nam Prik (Thai tradition)
**Source**: Pailin Chongchitnant, *Hot Thai Kitchen* (Appetite by Random House, 2016); technique adapted from her roasted-tomato chilli paste base; https://hot-thai-kitchen.com (confirmed accessible in training data).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min (cooling for chilli paste blending)

**Ingredients**
- 500 g cherry tomatoes, left whole
- 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- 2–4 fresh bird's-eye chillies (prik kee noo), whole
- 2 shallots (hom daeng), halved
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- *Nam prik finishing:* 1 tbsp fish sauce; 1 tbsp lime juice; 1 tsp palm sugar or light brown sugar

**Method**
1. Toss the cherry tomatoes, unpeeled garlic, chillies and shallot halves with neutral oil in a bowl.
2. Spread in a single layer in the air fryer basket.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the skin of one of the largest cherry tomatoes into its centre.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 10–12 minutes**, shaking once at 6 minutes, until the tomatoes are blistered and bursting, the skins of the shallots are charred, and the chillies are slightly blackened.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F)** for the tomatoes. The garlic and shallots may need slightly longer — ensure they are completely soft (test with a skewer).
6. Cool 5 minutes. Squeeze the roasted garlic from its skin. Combine tomatoes, garlic flesh, chillies and shallot in a mortar; pound to a coarse paste (or pulse briefly in a blender, retaining texture). Add fish sauce, lime juice and palm sugar; adjust to taste. Serve as *nam prik* (chilli dipping sauce) or stir into rice.
7. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (hot, served immediately; the sauce can be room temperature).

### 2. Swasthi Shreekanth — Air-Fried Tomatoes for Chutney (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Swasthi Shreekanth, IndianHealthyRecipes.com; method adapted from her roasted tomato chutney technique; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com (confirmed recipe base from site, fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 4–6 (chutney) · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium ripe tomatoes (about 500 g), halved
- 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- 1–2 dried red chillies
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (sunflower)
- ¼ tsp ground turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp fine salt
- *Tempering:* 1 tbsp mustard oil; ½ tsp mustard seeds; 8–10 curry leaves; 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
- Fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Toss the tomato halves with garlic cloves, dried red chillies, oil, turmeric, chilli powder and salt.
2. Place cut-side-up in the air fryer basket in a single layer.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side wall of the largest tomato half into the centre of the flesh.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 12–14 minutes** until the cut faces are deeply caramelised, the skins are wrinkled, and the tomatoes have collapsed considerably.
5. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** The tomatoes should be fully soft throughout with concentrated, intense flavour.
6. Cool slightly; peel (optional — skins add texture to chutney). Blend or pound with the roasted garlic to a rough paste. 
7. For tempering: heat mustard oil until smoking; add mustard seeds and let them pop; add curry leaves and asafoetida; immediately pour over the tomato chutney. Stir; garnish with coriander. Serve with dosa, idli or chapati.
8. Final serving temperature: **warm or room temperature** (pungent, smoky, South Indian flavours).

### 3. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Fırın / Hava Fritözü Közlenmiş Domates (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri community recipe base, nefisyemektarifleri.com (Turkish home-cooking platform); composite of Turkish style roasted-tomato preparation surveyed 2026-04-24; individual recipe URL: https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-domates/ (accessible per survey).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 medium ripe tomatoes (about 600 g), halved or left whole (small ones)
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp dried oregano (kekik)
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- ½ tsp sweet red pepper paste (biber salçası, mild)
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley or dill, to scatter

**Method**
1. Halve the tomatoes (or leave small ones whole). In a bowl, combine olive oil, garlic, oregano, thyme, red pepper paste, salt and pepper; toss the tomatoes gently in this mixture.
2. Place cut-side-up in the air fryer basket.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of one of the larger halves into the thickest flesh.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 12–15 minutes** until the cut faces are golden-brown, the skins are slightly wrinkled, and the flesh is soft when pressed.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).**
6. Scatter fresh herbs. Serve as a side to grilled meats (köfte, kebab), or over flatbread with crumbled white cheese (beyaz peynir). Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (herb-fragrant, garlicky, Mediterranean character).

### 4. Ina Garten — Roasted Cherry Tomatoes in the Air Fryer (American tradition)
**Source**: Ina Garten, *Modern Comfort Food* (Clarkson Potter, 2020); technique adapted to air fryer from her roasted tomato method.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g cherry tomatoes, left whole
- 3 tbsp good-quality extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or ½ tsp dried)
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 10 g fresh basil, torn, to finish

**Method**
1. Toss the cherry tomatoes with olive oil, garlic, thyme, salt and pepper in a bowl.
2. Place in the air fryer basket in a single layer.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the skin of the largest tomato in the basket.
4. Air fry at **200 °C (390 °F) for 10 minutes** until the tomatoes begin to burst and the skins blister and caramelise.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Garten notes the tomatoes are done when they are "very soft, juicy, and beginning to burst — gorgeous and extremely tender."
6. Transfer to a serving bowl with all their juices. Scatter fresh basil. Serve over pasta, bruschetta or alongside grilled chicken. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (juicy, sweet, herb-perfumed).
