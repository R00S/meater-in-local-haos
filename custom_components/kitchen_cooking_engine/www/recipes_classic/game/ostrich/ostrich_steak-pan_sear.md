## Cut profile

Ostrich (*Struthio camelus*) steak is produced primarily in South Africa, Namibia, and increasingly in France, Belgium, and Germany. Despite being a bird, ostrich meat is red, very low in fat, and treated culinarily exactly like beef or venison. Fan fillet (outerfilé) and top loin are the main steak cuts. The meat is extremely lean and must not be overcooked: medium-rare (54–57 °C) is the South African standard. Above 70 °C the meat becomes tough and liver-like.

Target internal temperature: **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** for medium-rare. Pull at 52 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Straußensteaks aus dem Ofen (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2012321326036993/Straussen-Steaks-aus-dem-Ofen.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Translation: Ostrich steaks from the oven — seared in oil, finished with port wine and cream sauce.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 ostrich steaks, approx. 150–200 g each
- Olive oil
- Fresh thyme
- Black pepper
- 1 splash of port wine
- 100 ml red wine
- 50 ml cream
- Olive oil for the roasting dish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 180 °C. Season steaks with pepper and thyme. Sear in olive oil in a very hot pan — approximately 2–3 minutes per side to form a brown crust.
2. Transfer to a lightly oiled roasting dish. Insert MEATER probe.
3. Deglaze the pan with port and red wine. Reduce briefly, add cream, and pour over the steaks.
4. Finish in the oven until probe reads **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** — approximately 10–15 minutes at 180 °C.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with roasted vegetables and starchy side.
*Final resting temperature: 56–59 °C (133–138 °F).*

### 2. EatOut.co.za — Ostrich steak South African style (South African tradition)
**Source**: EatOut.co.za; https://www.eatout.co.za/recipe/ostrich-steak-south-african/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8 min

*South Africa is the world's leading producer of ostrich meat. The braai (BBQ) and pan traditions.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 ostrich fan fillet steaks, approx. 180 g each
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine oil, garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Rub over steaks. Rest 10–15 minutes.
2. Heat a heavy pan over very high heat. Add a little oil.
3. Sear steaks **2–3 minutes per side**. Do not press down. Do not overcook.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with pap (maize porridge) or roasted sweet potato and chakalaka.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*

### 3. Food24.com — Pan-seared ostrich with mushroom sauce (South African/Continental tradition)
**Source**: Food24.com; https://www.food24.com/recipes/pan-seared-ostrich-steak/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ostrich loin steaks
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 200 g button mushrooms, sliced
- 2 shallots, finely chopped
- 100 ml red wine
- 100 ml beef or game stock
- 2 tbsp cream
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Season steaks with salt and pepper. Melt butter with oil in a hot heavy pan.
2. Sear steaks 2 minutes per side. Remove and keep warm.
3. In the same pan, soften shallots 2 minutes. Add mushrooms, cook 3 minutes.
4. Deglaze with wine, reduce by half. Add stock, reduce again. Stir in cream.
5. Return steaks to pan, warm 1 minute. Insert probe: **54 °C (129 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Serve with rice or creamy potato mash.
*Final resting temperature: 56–58 °C (133–136 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Doneness | Pull temp | Final (after rest) |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Rare | 48–50 °C (118–122 °F) | 51–53 °C (124–127 °F) |
| Medium-rare | 52–54 °C (126–129 °F) | 55–57 °C (131–135 °F) |
| Medium | 60 °C (140 °F) | 62 °C (144 °F) |
| Well-done | **Strongly discouraged** | — |
