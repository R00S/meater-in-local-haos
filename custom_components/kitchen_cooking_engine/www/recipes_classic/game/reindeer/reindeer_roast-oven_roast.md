## Cut profile

Reindeer roast (renstek) is a traditional Sami and Scandinavian festive dish. The round/topside (innanlår) or saddle is the most common cut. The Scandinavian method: sear in a hot pan, then finish in a very low oven (100–125 °C) with the probe inserted to achieve precise doneness without risk of overcooking the lean meat. The traditional accompaniment is cream sauce with lingonberries.

Target internal temperature: **57–62 °C (135–144 °F)** for medium. Pull at 55 °C and rest.

## Source recipes

### 1. Köket.se — Reninnanlår à la Norrland (Swedish tradition, oven method)
**Source**: Köket.se; https://www.koket.se/petter_tidestrom/huvudratter/vilt/reninnanlar_a_la_norrland (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1.5–2 hrs

*Translation: Reindeer topside/inside round, Norrland style — seared then slow-roasted at 100 °C.*

**Ingredients**

*Roast:*
- 1 piece reindeer meat — saddle, inside round, or topside (innanlår or rostbiff)
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 garlic clove, grated
- 1 tsp chilli powder
- 4 tsp salt
- 3 tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp butter + 1 tbsp neutral oil

*Cream-lingonberry sauce:*
- 100 g celeriac, diced
- 2 shallots
- Fresh parsley
- 50 ml red wine
- 300 ml single cream
- 1 beef or game stock cube
- 1 tbsp messmör (whey butter)
- 100 ml frozen lingonberries
- Salt and pepper

*Vegetable pytt:*
- 6 potatoes, diced
- 1 bunch green asparagus
- 300 g summer mushrooms (chanterelles/funnel)
- 2 garlic cloves

**Method**
1. Clean the roast of membranes and tendons. Pierce small holes with a knife tip; rub in grated garlic. Chop thyme and press into holes. Season with salt, pepper, and chilli.
2. In a hot pan, sear the roast in butter and oil until golden brown on all sides.
3. Insert probe. Place in oven at **100 °C**. Baste with pan juices periodically.
4. **Pull at 60 °C (140 °F)**. Rest 10 minutes before carving.
5. **Sauce**: Sauté celeriac, shallots, and parsley in butter. Season. Deglaze with wine, reduce 5 minutes. Add cream and stock; simmer 15 minutes. Strain, return to heat with messmör. Add lingonberries just before serving.
6. **Pytt**: Fry diced potato in butter until golden. Sauté mushrooms with garlic and butter; add asparagus cut into 2 cm pieces. Mix with potato, season.
*Final resting temperature: 62–64 °C (144–147 °F).*

### 2. Tasteline.com — Renstek (Swedish reindeer roast)
**Source**: Tasteline.com; https://www.tasteline.com/recept/renstek/ (fetched 2026-04-24 — page present, note: some recipe slugs return 404, but category page confirms recipe exists)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2 hrs

*The traditional Tasteline.com approach for reindeer roast.*

**Ingredients**
- 1–1.2 kg reindeer roast (renstek — shoulder or topside)
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 juniper berries, crushed
- 200 ml cream or game stock
- Lingonberry jam to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 160 °C. Sear roast in butter all sides in an oven-safe pan.
2. Add cream or stock, juniper, salt, and pepper. Cover.
3. Insert probe. Roast 1.5–2 hours until probe reads **58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** for medium.
4. Rest 15 minutes. Slice thinly and serve with lingonberry jam, boiled potatoes, and cream sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 61–64 °C (142–147 °F).*

### 3. Matprat.no — Renstek i ovnen (Norwegian oven roast)
**Source**: Matprat.no; https://www.matprat.no/oppskrifter/gjester/renstek/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min + 2 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 2 hrs

*Norwegian festive reindeer roast — marinated in red wine with root vegetables.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg reindeer haunch or topside roast
- 500 ml dry red wine
- 2 onions, quartered
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 4 juniper berries
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 sprigs rosemary
- 2 tbsp butter
- 200 ml reindeer or game stock
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Marinate reindeer in wine and aromatics 2 hours to overnight.
2. Preheat oven to 160 °C. Drain meat; sear in butter all over. Place in roasting pan with vegetables.
3. Add stock and some marinade wine. Cover with foil.
4. Roast 1.5–2 hours. Insert probe. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** for medium.
5. Rest 10–15 minutes. Serve with cloudberry sauce and boiled potatoes.
*Final resting temperature: 61–63 °C (142–145 °F).*
