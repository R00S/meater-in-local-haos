# Broccoli × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Broccoli (*Brassica oleracea* var. *italica*) oven-roasted at 200–220 °C develops golden, caramelised floret tips and tender stems in 18–22 minutes. The key is maximum heat, generous spacing, and a completely dry surface. Broccoli contains enough natural sugars that Maillard browning begins quickly at 200+ °C, producing the "roasted broccoli" flavour that converted many vegetable-averse diners to its cause. Pull temperature: 80–85 °C (176–185 °F) for golden-crisp at the tips, tender at the stem.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Broccoli al Forno con Aglio (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets (3–4 cm); 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil; ½ tsp fine sea salt; black pepper; 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; optional: dried chilli flakes

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss florets with oil, salt, pepper; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe through largest floret stem.
2. Roast 17–18 min. Add garlic (and chilli); return 2–4 more minutes.
3. **Pull at 80–84 °C (176–183 °F).** Hazan: "Garlic must not burn." Final serving: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (golden, garlic-bright, Italian).

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Broccoli with Almonds and Honey (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt; 30 g flaked almonds (toasted); 1 tbsp honey; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g flat-leaf parsley; ½ tsp sumac; ½ tsp dried chilli flakes

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss florets with oil and salt; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 20–22 min.
3. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Drizzle honey and lemon; scatter almonds, parsley, sumac and chilli. Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, sweet-sour, almond-bright).

---

### 3. Fuchsia Dunlop — Roasted Broccoli with Doubanjiang Sauce (Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012); roasting adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets; 2 tbsp neutral oil; salt
- *Sauce:* 1 tbsp Pixian doubanjiang; 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp oyster sauce; 1 tsp sesame oil; 1 tsp sugar; 2 garlic cloves grated

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Toss florets with oil and salt; roast 18–20 min. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Whisk sauce; toss roasted florets in sauce; return 2–3 more minutes.
3. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, spicy-umami, Chinese).

---

### 4. Claudia Roden — Roasted Broccoli with Chermoula (Moroccan / North African tradition)
**Source**: Claudia Roden, *Arabesque* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2005)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 20–22 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets
- *Chermoula:* 3 tbsp olive oil; 2 garlic cloves (minced); 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp sweet paprika; ½ tsp ground coriander; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 15 g fresh coriander; 10 g parsley; 1 tsp fine salt; pinch saffron in 1 tbsp warm water

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Blend chermoula; toss florets in it; spread on sheet pan. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Roast 20–22 minutes.
3. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).** Final serving: **82–85 °C / 180–185 °F** (golden, spiced, North African character).
