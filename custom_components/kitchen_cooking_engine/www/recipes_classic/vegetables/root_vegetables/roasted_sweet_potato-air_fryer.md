# Roasted Sweet Potato × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sweet potato (*Ipomoea batatas*) in the air fryer achieves crispy, caramelised skin and a soft, sweet interior in 35–45 minutes (whole) or 20–25 minutes (halved or cubed). Sweet potato contains much higher natural sugars (6–8 g/100 g cooked) than white potato, which means it caramelises more readily. In Japan, *yakiimo* (焼き芋 — stone-roasted sweet potato) is a beloved street food. In Korea, *goguma* (고구마) is roasted and enjoyed as a snack. Pull temperature: 96–98 °C (205–208 °F) at the thickest centre for whole sweet potato. MEATER probe along the long axis into the geometric centre.

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Yakiimo (焼き芋 — Japanese Air-Fried Sweet Potato)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); the classic Japanese stone-roasted sweet potato (*yakiimo*) adapted for the air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 min · **Cook**: 40–45 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Japanese sweet potatoes (naruto or satsuma-imo variety, ≈ 200 g each), scrubbed; optional: flaked sea salt

**Method**
1. Scrub potatoes; do not oil (the skin crisps best dry in Japanese style). Insert MEATER probe along the long axis of the thickest centre.
2. Air fry at **180 °C for 40–45 minutes**, turning once at 20 min. Harumi notes: "A lower temperature than other vegetables allows the sweet potato to cook slowly, maximising the caramelisation of its natural sugars."
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split open (the soft interior will be bright orange-yellow and deeply sweet). Sprinkle with flaked sea salt if desired.
4. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** at serving; yakiimo is also eaten warm/room temperature as a snack.

### 2. Maangchi — Goguma (고구마 — Korean Air-Fried Sweet Potato)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); Korean style roasted sweet potato.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Korean sweet potatoes (*goguma*, ≈ 200 g each), scrubbed; *optional butter:* 20 g unsalted butter + ½ tsp fine salt

**Method**
1. Scrub potatoes; pierce a few times with a fork. Insert MEATER probe along axis of thickest centre.
2. Air fry at **180 °C for 38–42 min**, turning once.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; optionally top with butter and salt. Maangchi notes: "In Korea we eat sweet potatoes plain as a snack, or with salt to contrast the sweetness." Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (soft, caramelised, sweet, Korean snack).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Air-Fried Masala Sweet Potato (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010); Indian sweet potato spice adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sweet potatoes (≈ 200 g each); 2 tbsp sunflower oil; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp cumin; ½ tsp salt; *topping:* 2 tbsp plain yoghurt; 1 tsp chaat masala; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Pierce and rub sweet potatoes with spiced oil. Insert MEATER probe. Air fry at **190 °C for 38–42 min**, turning once.
2. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; top with yoghurt, chaat masala, lemon, coriander. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (caramelised, spiced, tangy Indian topping).

---

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Sweet Potato with Tahini (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); sweet potato with tahini sauce adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sweet potatoes (≈ 200 g each); 2 tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp salt; *tahini sauce:* 3 tbsp tahini; 2 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove grated; 2 tbsp water; salt; pomegranate seeds, fresh coriander, chilli to finish

**Method**
1. Pierce and rub with olive oil and salt. Insert MEATER probe. Air fry at **190 °C for 38–42 min**, turning once.
2. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; drizzle tahini sauce; scatter pomegranate, coriander and chilli. Final serving: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (caramelised, tahini-creamy, jewel-bright).
