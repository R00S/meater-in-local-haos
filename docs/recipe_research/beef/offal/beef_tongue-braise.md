# Beef Tongue × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Beef tongue (*Bos taurus* lingual muscle) is a single dense cylindrical muscle wrapped in a thick, papillae-covered skin. The muscle itself is rich in collagen and intramuscular fat; the skin is inedible and must be peeled after cooking — the standard method is to braise the whole tongue at 85–95 °C until the collagen converts to gelatin, then peel the skin while the tongue is still warm. Cooking times of 3–4 hours for a 1.5–2 kg tongue are standard. Carryover is essentially zero (the surrounding liquid buffers the temperature) but the tongue retains heat well after removal — the skin must be peeled within 5 minutes of lifting from the braise or it cools and tightens. Pull is timed by the texture of the muscle: a paring knife should slide in with no resistance.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fergus Henderson — Salt Ox Tongue with Green Sauce (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Whole Beast: Nose to Tail Eating* (Macmillan, 1999/2004); stjohnrestaurant.com  
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 5 d salt cure if curing) · **Cook**: 3–4 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole ox tongue (1.6–2 kg), pre-salted (cured) or fresh
- 2 large carrots, roughly chopped
- 2 onions, halved
- 1 head garlic, halved across the equator
- 4 stalks celery, halved
- 1 bouquet garni (parsley stalks, thyme, bay leaves, peppercorns)
- 1 tsp black peppercorns
- Cold water to cover
- **Green sauce**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley, 30 g mint, 30 g basil, 4 anchovies, 1 tbsp capers, 1 garlic clove, 30 ml red-wine vinegar, 120 ml extra-virgin olive oil

**Method**
1. If using a fresh tongue, rinse and place in a stockpot. (If salted, soak overnight in cold water and discard the soaking liquid.)
2. Cover with cold water by 5 cm. Add carrots, onions, garlic, celery, bouquet garni and peppercorns.
3. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim foam. Reduce to the gentlest simmer — bubbles should barely break the surface (about 85–90 °C).
4. After the first hour, insert the MEATER probe through the thicker base end of the tongue, pushing horizontally so the tip rests at the geometric centre of the muscle.
5. Cover partially. Continue simmering 2.5–3 hours.
6. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Henderson's test: a paring knife slid into the thickest part should meet no resistance.
7. Lift the tongue onto a board. Within 5 minutes, while still hot, peel off the skin with a paring knife — it should come away in sheets. Trim the base and any gristle.
8. Rest the peeled tongue in a little of the warm braising liquid 15 minutes. Slice 5 mm thick. Serve with the green sauce, boiled potatoes and watercress. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (sliceable, succulent, peeled).

### 2. Anthony Bourdain — Langue de Bœuf Sauce Piquante (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: Anthony Bourdain & José de Meirelles, *Les Halles Cookbook* (Bloomsbury, 2004)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hr · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh beef tongue (1.5–1.8 kg)
- 4 L cold water
- 30 g fine sea salt for the braising liquid
- 1 large onion studded with 4 cloves
- 2 carrots, roughly cut
- 2 leeks, roughly cut and washed
- 1 bouquet garni (thyme, parsley, bay)
- 10 black peppercorns
- **Sauce piquante**: 60 g unsalted butter, 60 g flour, 750 ml strained braising liquid, 30 g Dijon mustard, 30 ml red-wine vinegar, 30 g cornichons (chopped), 30 g capers (drained), 30 g chopped fresh tarragon and parsley

**Method**
1. Place tongue in a large stockpot; cover with cold water; add salt, onion, carrots, leeks, bouquet garni and peppercorns.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim. Reduce to the gentlest simmer.
3. After the first hour, insert the MEATER probe through the thicker base end, tip at geometric centre.
4. Simmer 2.5–3 hours.
5. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Bourdain's standard: the knife slides in like into warm butter.
6. Lift; peel skin within 5 minutes; trim.
7. Make the *sauce piquante*: melt butter, whisk in flour, cook 1 minute. Whisk in 750 ml strained braising liquid; simmer to thicken. Off heat add mustard, vinegar, cornichons, capers and herbs.
8. Slice the warm tongue 5 mm thick; nap with the sauce piquante. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (tender, sharp-mustard sauce).

### 3. Diana Kennedy — Lengua en Salsa Verde (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Cuisines of Mexico* (Harper & Row, 1972; expanded *The Essential Cuisines of Mexico*, Clarkson Potter, 2000)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hr (tongue) + 20 min (salsa) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh beef tongue (1.5 kg)
- 2 onions, quartered
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp salt for braising water
- Cold water to cover
- **Salsa verde cocida**: 700 g tomatillos (husked, rinsed), 3 jalapeño or serrano chillies, 1 small white onion (quartered), 2 garlic cloves, 30 g fresh coriander, 5 g (1 tsp) salt, 15 ml lime juice, 30 ml vegetable oil for the salsa pot

**Method**
1. Place tongue in a heavy stockpot with onions, garlic, bay leaves and salt. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim foam. Reduce to the gentlest simmer (about 85 °C).
3. After 1 hour, insert MEATER probe through the base end, tip at geometric centre. Continue 2.5–3 hours.
4. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Kennedy's *lengua* is done when "el cuchillo entra sin esfuerzo" — the knife enters without effort.
5. Lift; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice 8 mm thick.
6. For the salsa: char tomatillos, chillies, onion and garlic on a comal or under the broiler 6–8 minutes. Blend with coriander, salt and lime. Heat oil in a saucepan; pour in the salsa and fry 5 minutes until colour deepens.
7. Lay tongue slices in the salsa; warm together 5–8 minutes. Rest 5 minutes off heat. Serve in warm corn tortillas. Final serving temperature: **75–78 °C / 167–172 °F** (salsa-coated, tender, bright with lime).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Slow-Cooked Beef Tongue with Tehina (Israeli/Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh beef tongue (1.4 kg)
- 2 onions, halved
- 1 head garlic, halved
- 4 cardamom pods
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 4 cloves
- 1 tsp black peppercorns
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp salt
- Cold water to cover
- **Tehina sauce**: 120 g tahini, 30 ml lemon juice, 1 garlic clove (grated), 60 ml ice water, 3 g salt
- **To serve**: pomegranate seeds, sumac, chopped parsley, warm flatbreads

**Method**
1. Place tongue in a stockpot with onions, garlic, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, peppercorns, bay and salt. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim. Reduce to the gentlest simmer (≈ 85 °C).
3. After 1 hour, insert MEATER probe through the base end, tip at geometric centre. Cover partially. Continue 2.5–3 hours.
4. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Ottolenghi's test is the same paring-knife test: no resistance at the centre.
5. Lift; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice 5 mm thick.
6. Whisk tehina with lemon, garlic, water and salt to a smooth, spoonable sauce.
7. Arrange slices on warm flatbreads. Drape with tehina, scatter pomegranate, sumac and parsley. Rest 5 minutes (the tehina wakes up the warm meat). Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (warm-spiced, creamy-sauced).
