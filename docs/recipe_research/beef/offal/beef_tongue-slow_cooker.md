# Beef Tongue × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Beef tongue cooked in an electric slow cooker is the same long-collagen-conversion process as a stove-top or oven braise, but executed in a sealed environment that holds the cooking liquid at a steady 85–95 °C (low setting) or 95–100 °C (high setting) without monitoring. The slow cooker is well suited to this cut because the unattended 6–10 hour cook completely converts the tongue's collagen to gelatin, the steam-tight environment keeps the muscle fully submerged in its liquid, and there is no risk of accidental boil-over (which would toughen the muscle). The peel-while-warm rule still applies — the skin must come off within 5–10 minutes of lifting from the cooker. Carryover is zero; the tongue stays at cooker temperature until removed.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Pot-au-Feu Tongue (American Southern tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017); hughacheson.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh beef tongue (1.4–1.6 kg)
- 1 large onion, quartered
- 2 carrots, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 2 stalks celery, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 240 ml dry white wine
- Cold water to cover (1.5–2 L)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry vinegar, to finish

**Method**
1. Place tongue at the bottom of a 6-quart (5.7 L) slow cooker. Surround with vegetables, garlic, thyme, bay and peppercorns. Add salt.
2. Pour in the wine, then add cold water until the tongue is fully submerged.
3. Insert the MEATER Plus probe through the base end of the tongue (the thicker root), pushing it horizontally into the centre of the muscle so the tip is at the geometric centre. Use the probe's wireless mode if your cooker is metal-bodied (range may be limited; place the block close to the cooker).
4. Cover. Cook on Low 8 hours.
5. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Acheson's check: a thin metal skewer should pass through the thickest part with no resistance.
6. Lift the tongue out onto a board. Within 5–10 minutes, while still very warm, peel off the skin in sheets with a paring knife; trim the gristle and base.
7. Strain the cooking liquid; reduce 240 ml of it in a saucepan with the sherry vinegar to make a sauce.
8. Slice 5 mm thick; nap with the sauce. Rest 15 minutes warm covered. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (silken-tender, sherry-bright).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Lengua de Res al Crock-Pot, estilo casero (Mexican home tradition adaptation)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *Mexican Regional Cooking* (Harper & Row, 1990), with the slow-cooker note in *From My Mexican Kitchen* (Clarkson Potter, 2003)  
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh beef tongue (1.5–1.8 kg)
- 1 large white onion, quartered
- 1 head garlic, halved across the equator
- 6 black peppercorns, lightly crushed
- 4 cloves
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 30 ml white vinegar
- Cold water to cover

**Method**
1. Rinse the tongue. Place in the slow cooker with onion, garlic, peppercorns, cloves, bay, oregano and salt.
2. Add the vinegar; cover with cold water until tongue is fully submerged.
3. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the base end into the geometric centre of the muscle.
4. Cover. Cook on Low 8 hours.
5. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Kennedy's standard for *lengua* — completely tender, the muscle yields entirely to a paring knife.
6. Lift onto a board; peel within 5 minutes; trim.
7. Slice 5 mm thick or shred for tacos. Serve in warm corn tortillas with salsa verde, chopped onion, coriander and lime. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F**.

### 3. Andrea Nguyen — Lưỡi Bò Hầm in the Slow Cooker (Vietnamese tradition)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *The Pho Cookbook* (Ten Speed Press, 2017) (technique transferred from her braise to slow cooker); vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh beef tongue (1.4 kg)
- 1 large onion, halved (charred under the broiler 5 min)
- 50 g fresh ginger, sliced (also charred)
- 6 star anise pods, lightly toasted
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 4 cloves
- 5 cardamom pods
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fish sauce
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 15 g (1 tbsp) rock sugar (or palm sugar)
- 1.5 L water
- **To serve**: rice noodles, bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, sliced bird's-eye chillies, hoisin and sriracha

**Method**
1. Place tongue in the slow cooker. Add charred onion and ginger, star anise, cinnamon, cloves and cardamom.
2. Stir in fish sauce, salt and rock sugar. Pour in the water (the tongue should be just submerged).
3. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the base end into geometric centre.
4. Cover. Cook on Low 8 hours.
5. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Nguyen tests with a chopstick: it should slide right through the thickest portion.
6. Lift onto a board; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice paper-thin against the grain.
7. Strain the broth; serve over rice noodles with the slices and the herb-bean sprout-chilli condiments. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (spice-perfumed, broth-bathed).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Pickled-Beef-Tongue Method, Slow-Cooker Adaptation (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015) (cure-and-braise method, adapted to the slow cooker)  
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 5 d cure if curing) · **Cook**: 8 hr (low) · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 cured (lightly salt-and-sugar pickled) ox tongue, 1.5 kg, soaked overnight in cold water and drained
- 2 large yellow onions, halved
- 2 carrots, cut in chunks
- 1 leek, white and pale-green only, halved
- 1 small celeriac, peeled and cut in chunks
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 5 allspice berries (kryddpeppar)
- 4 cloves
- Cold water to cover
- **To serve**: peeled cooked potatoes, gräddsås (cream sauce), pickled cucumber, fresh dill

**Method**
1. Drain the soaked tongue. Place in the slow cooker with onions, carrots, leek, celeriac, bay leaves, peppercorns, allspice and cloves.
2. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
3. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the base end into geometric centre.
4. Cover. Cook on Low 8 hours.
5. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Nilsson's *saltkokt tunga* standard — fully tender, but a touch more cohesive than fully shreddable.
6. Lift; peel skin within 5 minutes; trim. Strain liquid for the gräddsås base.
7. Slice 5 mm thick. Serve with potatoes, the cream sauce made from a roux + 240 ml strained liquid + 120 ml grädde + Dijon, and pickled cucumber. Rest 20 minutes warm covered. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (silky, cure-perfumed, dill-bright).
