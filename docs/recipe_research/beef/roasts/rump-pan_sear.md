# Rump (Beef) × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The beef rump, in steak form, is cut from the rump primal — the area at the junction of the
sirloin and the round. In British and Australian butchery, **rump steak** is a well-regarded
budget-premium steak: more flavourful than topside (due to intramuscular fat and connective
tissue in some sub-muscles) but less tender than sirloin or ribeye. In Swedish butchery the
same cut is called **rumpstek**. The rump can include several muscles (biceps femoris, tensor
fasciae latae, and gluteus medius), which is why rump steaks benefit from tenderising
by marination or cutting against the grain after cooking.

**Best pan_sear results**: Because rump has moderate fat and some connective tissue, it is best
served at medium-rare to medium (54–60 °C). Going beyond 65 °C results in a noticeably tougher
chew. The classic approach is a very high-heat pan, 2–3 minutes per side, with optional butter-
basting. Rump releases significant fat during cooking which helps baste the exterior.

**Probe placement**: For a 2–3 cm thick steak, insert from the narrow end (side) into the
geometric centre, avoiding the fat seam where present. Because rump is a composite of 2–3
muscles, insert the probe so it reads the interior of the thickest muscle mass.

**Culinary Group A gap**: Pan-searing a thick whole rump steak (Western style) is not a
documented primary tradition in confirmed-reachable South Asian, East Asian, or SE Asian
recipe sources. Filipino bistek tagalog uses thin-sliced round cuts, not thick rump steaks —
it would belong to a separate "braise" or "stir_fry" method leaf.

**Culinary Group C gap**: No confirmed rump steak pan-sear recipe was found on cheftariq.com,
marocmama.com, or africanbites.com after URL probing.

## Source recipes

### 1. Good Food — Rump Steak (British tradition)
**Source**: Good Food magazine; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/rump-steak  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 3–8 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 × 200 g **rump steak**, about 2–3 cm thick
- 1 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 large garlic clove, bashed once
- 1 sprig thyme or rosemary (optional)

**Method**
1. Take steaks out of fridge 30 min before cooking. Pat dry and season.
2. Heat a non-stick heavy-based frying pan over very high heat. Add oil, then butter, garlic and steaks. Add herbs around them.
3. Turn steaks every 30 sec–1 min for a nice brown crust.
4. **Timings** (as a rough guide): **3 min total (rare), 4–5 min total (medium), 7–8 min (well done).**
5. **If you have a digital cooking thermometer, the middle of the steak should be 50°C for rare, 60°C for medium and 70°C for well done.**
6. If steak has a thick fat layer on the side, hold on its side with tongs and push fat into pan for 2 min until golden brown.
7. Transfer to plate; cover in foil, rest 5 min before serving.

### 2. Köket.se — Rumpstek à la Bercy (Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Köket.se, koket.se/rumpstek-a-la-bercy  
**Serves**: 4 · **Cut used**: 4 rumpstekar (200 g each)

**Ingredients**
- 4 **rumpstekar** (à 200 g)
- Salt, white pepper

**Bercy sauce**
- 2 dl white wine
- 1 shallot
- 1 tbsp concentrated beef stock
- 75 g butter
- 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- Salt, white pepper

**Garnish**
- 250 g green asparagus, cooked
- 4 portions pommes frites

**Method**
1. **Bercy sauce**: Peel and finely chop shallot. Bring wine, shallot, and beef stock to a boil; simmer until nearly reduced. Cool; mix with butter and parsley. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon. Refrigerate until serving.
2. **Steaks**: Fry in a hot frying pan. Season with salt and pepper.
3. **Köttet ska bli rosa med vackert gyllenbrun yta** (the meat should be pink with a beautiful golden-brown surface). **Allow approximately 2 minutes on each side.**
4. Serve immediately with the Bercy butter, cooked asparagus, and pommes frites.

**Notes**: "Rosa" (pink) corresponds to medium-rare. No probe temperature given.

### 3. Good Food — Thai Beef with Coconut Dressing (Thai-inspired, Group B source)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, September 2013; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/thai-beef-coconut-dressing  
**Serves**: 2 · **Cut used**: 250 g **beef steak (we used rump)**

**Ingredients**
- 250 g beef steak (rump used in testing)
- 2 tbsp Thai green curry paste (1 tbsp for rub, 1 tbsp for sauce)
- 2 tsp vegetable oil
- 160 ml can coconut cream
- 1 tbsp soft brown sugar
- 1 red chilli (thinly sliced)
- Zest and juice of 1 lime
- 200 g green beans; 300 g cooked jasmine rice; coriander; crispy onions

**Method**
1. Rub 1 tbsp curry paste over the steak.
2. Heat oil in a frying pan. **Cook the steak for 2 mins on each side for medium rare, or to your liking.**
3. Transfer to plate; cover with foil to keep warm.
4. Add remaining curry paste, coconut cream, sugar, chilli, and lime zest and juice to pan; bubble until slightly thickened. Add steak resting juices; slice steak into strips.

**Notes**: Classic pan-sear method; 2 min per side for medium-rare with a rump steak thickness of ~2–3 cm.

### 4. Good Food — Mexican Steak with Beans (Mexican-inspired, Group B source)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, July 2012; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/mexican-steak-homemade-refried-beans  
**Serves**: 2 · **Cut used**: 2 small **sirloin or rump steaks**, or 1 large

**Ingredients**
- 2 small sirloin or **rump steaks** (or 1 large)
- 2 tsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp chipotle paste
- 2 whole roasted red peppers (from a jar, quartered)
- Avocado, lime, coriander, soured cream

**Refried beans**: onion, cumin, hot paprika, tomato purée, chipotle paste, brown sugar, wine vinegar, black beans, pinto or kidney beans

**Method**
1. Rub steaks with oil, chipotle paste, and black pepper. Leave at room temperature while making beans.
2. Heat a griddle or non-stick frying pan (or barbecue). Season with salt.
3. **Fry about 2 mins on each side, until steaks are cooked to your liking.**
4. Rest a few minutes; slice. Serve over mashed beans with peppers, avocado, lime, coriander, and soured cream.

**Notes**: Griddle/frying pan method; 2 min per side is for a thin-to-medium rump steak. No probe temperature given.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Tradition | Group | Pan temp / timing | Target temp |
|--------|-----------|-------|--------------------|-------------|
| BBC Good Food (Rump Steak) | British | B | Very high heat, 3–8 min total | 50°C rare, **60°C medium**, 70°C well done |
| Köket.se (Rumpstek à la Bercy) | Swedish | D | Hot frying pan, ~2 min/side | "Rosa"/pink = medium-rare (no probe) |
| BBC Good Food (Thai Beef) | British (Thai-inspired) | B | Medium-high, 2 min/side | Medium-rare (no probe temp given) |
| BBC Good Food (Mexican Steak) | British (Mexican-inspired) | B | Griddle/frying pan, 2 min/side | "To your liking" (no probe temp) |

**Key finding**: The one recipe that gives explicit probe temperatures (BBC Good Food, "Rump Steak") confirms **50°C for rare, 60°C for medium, 70°C for well done**. The other three sources align with the 2 min/side timing for medium-rare, which for a 2–3 cm rump steak corresponds to approximately 50–55 °C final (before resting). The system target of **54 °C for medium-rare** is at the centre of this range and is appropriate for pan_sear of rump steak.

**Carryover**: For a thin steak (2–3 cm) in a very hot pan, carryover is 2–4 °C. The BBC probe target of 50°C for rare assumes a rest of 5 min, after which it reaches ~54–55 °C — consistent with the `medium_rare: 54°C` target.

## What makes this method special

Pan-searing a rump steak at very high heat achieves two goals simultaneously: a deep Maillard
crust on the exterior (compensating for the cut's lower intramuscular fat compared to ribeye)
and a tender, pink interior at 54–60 °C. The fat layer on rump steak renders during searing
and bastes the surface, adding flavour. This is rump steak's primary claim as a good-value
option — the pan crust technique maximises its flavour potential.

Rump is particularly well-suited to the **French butter-baste technique** (Bercy-style, Swedish
"rumpstek à la Bercy") where the melting butter picks up rendered beef fat and herb flavour and
is continuously spooned over the steak during the final minutes.
