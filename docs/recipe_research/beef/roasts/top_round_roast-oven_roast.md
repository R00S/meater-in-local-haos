# Top Round Roast × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The top round roast comes from the inner thigh of the hindquarter — the uppermost muscle of the
round primal. In American butchery, "top round" is a distinct sub-primal from the round; in
British butchery the near-equivalent is **topside** (the same inner-thigh muscle, similarly
lean). In Finnish butchery the hindquarter roast cut is called **paahtopaisti**, which can
encompass top round.

**Anatomy and cooking behaviour**: Top round is a single flat-grained, very lean muscle.
It has less intramuscular fat than ribeye or sirloin, and lower connective tissue than chuck.
This makes it well-suited to dry oven heat: it does not need collagen-dissolving temperatures,
only gentle heat to 54–65 °C to become tender. Overcooked top round (above ~70 °C) turns dry
and chalky. Unlike bottom round, top round has an even enough shape that it roasts evenly from
a single probe insertion.

**Temperature approaches**: The two main strategies are (1) high-start / low-finish (200–240 °C
crust, then 160–190 °C until done) and (2) low-and-slow throughout (120–130 °C for 1–2 hours).
The Maillard crust from searing or high-start improves flavour significantly for this lean cut.

**Probe placement**: Insert lengthwise from the end into the geometric centre. The flat grain
of top round means there is no fat seam to avoid, but avoid the slight fat cap on the exterior
surface.

**Culinary Group A gap**: Dry oven roasting a whole top round joint is not documented in
confirmed-reachable South Asian, East Asian, or South-East Asian recipe sources. The gap is
noted.

## Source recipes

### 1. Good Food — Easy Roast Beef (British tradition)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, September 2011; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-beef-carrots-easy-gravy  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 50 min–1h20 (plus 30 min rest)  
**Cut used**: 950 g beef **top rump joint** (British equivalent of top round / upper round)

**Ingredients**
- 950 g beef top rump joint
- 1 tsp plain flour
- 1 tsp mustard powder
- Salt and pepper
- 1 onion, cut into 8 wedges
- 500 g carrots, halved lengthways
- **Gravy**: 1 tbsp plain flour, 250 ml beef stock

**Method**
1. Heat oven to 240 °C / 220 °C fan / gas 9.
2. Mix flour and mustard powder with seasoning; rub all over the joint.
3. Place onion and carrots in roasting tin; sit beef on top. Cook at 240 °C for 20 minutes.
4. Reduce to 190 °C / 170 °C fan / gas 5. Continue roasting: **30 min rare, 40 min medium, 1 hour well done** (after initial 20 min).
5. Remove; cover with foil. Rest 30 minutes. Make gravy from tin juices.

**Notes**: No probe temperature given — time-based method. Recipe tip: "best eaten pink."

### 2. Good Food — Roast Beef with Red Wine & Banana Shallots (British tradition)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, January 2016; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-beef-red-wine-banana-shallots  
**Serves**: 8 · **Cut used**: 2 kg **topside of beef** (British equivalent of top round)

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg topside of beef (with natural fat layer on top)
- 1 tbsp vegetable or sunflower oil
- 1 tbsp English mustard powder
- 1 tsp Maldon sea salt
- 2 large carrots, halved lengthways
- 12 banana shallots, peeled and halved
- 2 bay leaves; 2 tbsp plain flour
- 300 ml full-bodied red wine; 500 ml fresh beef stock; 2 tsp redcurrant jelly

**Method**
1. Allow meat to come to room temperature (1 hour before roasting).
2. Preheat oven to 220 °C / 200 °C fan. Dry beef; rub with oil then mustard powder, salt, and pepper. Carrots in tin as trivet; beef fat-side up.
3. Roast at 220 °C for 15 min, reduce to 180 °C / 160 °C fan. Continue for **55 min (medium)** or **45 min (medium-rare)**.
4. **Probe thermometer in thickest part should read 65 °C for medium.**
5. Rest; make red wine gravy with pan juices.

### 3. Immaculate Bites — Creole-Spiced Roast Beef (West African / Southern American Creole tradition)
**Source**: Imma (Immaculate Bites), africanbites.com/roast-beef/  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 15 min (+overnight) · **Cook**: 1–2 hours  
**Cut used**: Eye of round roast; recipe explicitly notes **top round roast** as a direct substitute

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg eye of round roast (or **top round roast**, bottom round roast, top sirloin roast)
- Black pepper, olive oil or butter, paprika, rosemary, dried thyme
- Minced garlic + whole garlic cloves
- Cayenne pepper
- ½ cup dry red wine (Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
- 1 cup beef broth

**Method**
1. Season and marinate in herb paste overnight.
2. Preheat oven to 250 °F / 120 °C.
3. Stuff slits with whole garlic cloves; sear on all sides until browned.
4. Roast uncovered at 250 °F / 120 °C for 1–2 hours.
5. **Pull at 130–140 °F / 54–60 °C.** Rest 15–20 minutes. Slice against grain.

### 4. Katja Nordlund — Paahtopaisti (Finnish tradition)
**Source**: Katja Nordlund (author), Pia Inberg (photo), soppa365.fi/reseptit/liha/paahtopaisti (published 30 September 2012)  
**Serves**: 10 · **Cook**: ~1.5 hours (+ 1 hr room-temperature rest + 30 min rest in foil)  
**Cut used**: **Paahtopaisti** (Finnish hindquarter/round oven roast)

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg paahtopaisti (Finnish hindquarter roast, round/top round equivalent)
- 1 tbsp butter

**Optional cold marinade**
- 3 garlic cloves (minced), 3 dl warm water, 2 tsp salt, 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tbsp honey, 2 tbsp wine vinegar

**Method**
1. Bring to room temperature at least 1 hour before cooking.
2. Brown in butter on all sides.
3. Transfer to oven dish; insert meat thermometer into thickest part.
4. Roast at **125 °C until thermometer reads 55–60 °C** (~1.5 hours). For well-done, wait for 70 °C.
5. Wrap in foil 30 minutes before slicing (for warm serving) or marinate in sealed bag for cold serving.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Tradition | Group | Pull / target temp | Doneness |
|--------|-----------|-------|--------------------|---------|
| BBC Good Food (Easy Roast Beef) | British | B | Time only (30–40 min post-initial) | Rare to medium |
| BBC Good Food (Red Wine & Shallots) | British | B | 65 °C probe reading | Medium |
| Africanbites.com (Creole) | West African / Southern Creole | C | 54–60 °C / 130–140 °F | Medium-rare to medium |
| Soppa365.fi (Finnish) | Finnish | D | 55–60 °C (or 70 °C well-done) | Medium-rare to medium |

**Key finding**: The explicit probe-temperature sources (Creole, Finnish, BBC Red Wine) bracket the pull range at **54–65 °C**. Carryover from moderate ovens adds 4–7 °C; carryover from low-and-slow (120–125 °C) is minimal. The current system target of 54 °C for medium-rare is at the low end of the confirmed pull range and appropriate when the joint is rested 20–30 minutes.

**Group A gap**: Oven-roasting a whole round joint is not documented in confirmed-reachable South/East/SE Asian recipe sources.

## What makes this method special

Oven roasting suits top round because its lean, even-grained structure becomes tender in the
54–65 °C range without requiring collagen dissolution. The fat cap (if present) bastes the
surface as it renders. Achieving a Maillard crust (via searing or high initial oven temperature)
compensates for the cut's low intramuscular fat, which would otherwise produce a bland exterior.
Slicing against the grain after resting is essential; unlike short ribs, top round has no long
collagen strands to dissolve — its texture comes entirely from not over-heating the muscle fibres.
