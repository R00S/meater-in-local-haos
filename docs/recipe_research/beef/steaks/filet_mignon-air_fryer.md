# Filet Mignon × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air fryer filet mignon is cooked at 204°C (400°F) with the basket circulating hot air on
all surfaces simultaneously. The lean, thick tenderloin cooks more evenly in an air fryer
than a pan sear because there is no hot-spot gradient — every surface gets equal heat.
Result: very consistent medium-rare with a lighter Maillard crust than cast iron. The
absence of butter basting means the delicate flavour is purer but less rich.

## Source recipes

### 1. Air Fryer Plates — Air Fryer Filet Mignon (Perfect Medium-Rare in 12 Min)
- **Source**: airfryerplates.com/air-fryer-filet-mignon-recipe
- **Method**: 204°C (400°F), flip at halfway (6 min), compound butter to finish.
- **Time for medium-rare**: 10-12 min total for 1.5-inch steak
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** internal. Rest 5 min.
- "Most professionals recommend medium-rare for filet mignon in the air fryer."

### 2. Wholesome Yum — Air Fryer Filet Mignon (Fast & Super Tender)
- **Source**: wholesomeyum.com/air-fryer-filet-mignon
- **Method**: Preheat air fryer to 400°F / 204°C, pat dry, light oil, flip halfway.
- **Target**: Medium-rare = **130°F / 54°C** after 10-12 min + 5 min rest.
- "Consistently juicy interiors because the air fryer cooks more evenly than a pan."

### 3. Airfryerly — Perfect Filet Mignon in Air Fryer (Juicy, Hands-Off Steak)

- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** internal (medium-rare recommended)
- "Filet mignon is the best cut for air fryer — its tender structure benefits from

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Air fryer temp | Pull temp | Final (medium-rare) |
|--------|---------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Air Fryer Plates | 204°C | ~52°C | 54-57°C |
| Wholesome Yum | 204°C | ~52°C | 54°C |
| Airfryerly | 204°C | ~52°C | 54-57°C |
| Air Fryer Foods (Ninja) | 200°C | ~52°C | 54-57°C |
| Just the Woods (Ninja) | 204°C | 52°C | 54°C |

**Universal: 54-57°C medium-rare.** Air fryer temperature (204°C) is higher than pan
sear temperature, but the circulating air is less intense than direct cast-iron contact —
the net result on the steak interior is identical to pan sear.

## What air fryer gives vs pan sear for this cut
- **Even 360° surface browning** vs pan sear's one-side-at-a-time crust
- **No butter basting** — purer flavour, less rich
- **More consistent** for home cooks — filet mignon in a pan requires constant attention
- **Less smoke**, easier cleanup
- Same 54°C internal target — the MEATER probe reads identically regardless of method
