# Filet Mignon × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Filet mignon (from the narrow end of the psoas major) is the most tender, least-exercised muscle in the beef carcass, with virtually no connective tissue or intramuscular fat. Its mild, delicate flavour benefits from the grilled char and smokiness that a gas or charcoal grill provides — without it, the cut's flavour can seem bland. A gas grill produces consistent, controllable heat with minimal smoke, making it more precise than charcoal but less aromatic. The lean fibres dry out rapidly above medium, so precise temperature management is critical. Carryover is minimal given the small, uniform geometry of a filet mignon, but the brief intense heat of a grill can produce rapid late-stage temperature rises.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber Grills / America's Test Kitchen — Classic Grilled Filet Mignon
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Cooking for Two Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2014), pp. 184–186; weberkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+45 min tempering) · **Cook**: 10–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 filet mignon steaks, 225 g each, 4 cm thick
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tsp vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Remove steaks from the fridge 45 minutes ahead. Pat dry. Season all sides with salt and pepper. Brush with oil.
2. Preheat a gas grill to high heat (all burners on). Clean and oil the grates.
3. Place steaks on the hot grate over direct heat. Cook 4 minutes without moving.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of the steak horizontally to the geometric centre.
5. Flip. Cook 3–4 minutes.
6. Move to indirect heat (cooler side of grill) if needed to reach target without burning the exterior. Close the lid.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C** (126–129 °F) for medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 2. Ina Garten — Grilled Filet Mignon with Herb Butter (American home tradition)
**Source**: Ina Garten, *Barefoot Contessa Back to Basics* (Clarkson Potter, 2008), pp. 130–132; foodnetwork.com/recipes/ina-garten  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+1 hr tempering) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5–10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 filet mignon steaks, 200 g each
- 2 tbsp good olive oil
- 1½ tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 60 g unsalted butter, at room temperature (herb butter, to top)
- 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- Small pinch cayenne

**Method**
1. Bring steaks to room temperature for 1 hour. Mix butter with parsley, oregano, cayenne, and a pinch of salt to make herb butter; refrigerate.
2. Brush steaks with olive oil and season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Preheat gas grill to high. Clean and oil the grate.
4. Place steaks on the grill. Cook 4–5 minutes first side.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the edge of the steak horizontally to the centre.
6. Flip. Cook 4–5 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C** (126–129 °F) for medium-rare.
8. Rest 5–10 minutes. Top each steak with a disc of herb butter immediately after pulling. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 3. Nigel Slater — Grilled Beef Fillet (British tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Eat* (Fourth Estate, 2013), pp. 220–222; theguardian.com/food/nigel-slater  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 beef fillet steaks, 200 g each
- Flaky sea salt (Maldon) and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp light olive oil or groundnut oil
- 25 g butter (to finish at rest)

**Method**
1. Season steaks with sea salt, pepper, and oil.
2. Preheat a gas grill or ridged grill pan to its hottest. Oil the grate.
3. Cook the fillet 3–4 minutes on the first side. Do not move.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side to the centre.
5. Flip. Cook 3–4 minutes.
6. **Pull at 53–55 °C** (127–131 °F). Slater's preferred finish for fillet is very pink throughout — a British medium-rare.
7. Rest 3 minutes with butter resting on top. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, pink throughout).

### 4. Ferran Adrià — Grilled Beef Fillet with Romesco (Catalan tradition)
**Source**: Ferran Adrià, *El Bulli Family Meals* (Phaidon, 2011), pp. 148–150; elbulli.com (archived)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 beef fillet steaks (*lomo de res*), 200 g each
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- Romesco sauce (roasted tomatoes, dried *ñora* peppers, almonds, hazelnuts, garlic, sherry vinegar, olive oil)

**Method**
1. Prepare romesco sauce in advance (roast peppers and tomatoes, blend with nuts, garlic, vinegar, oil).
2. Brush fillets with olive oil, season with salt and pepper.
3. Heat a gas grill or wood-fired grill to high.
4. Grill fillet steaks 4–5 minutes first side.
5. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally from the side to the centre.
6. Flip. Grill 4–5 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C** (126–129 °F). Catalan tradition serves beef fillet at a rosy medium-rare — the romesco sauce's bold flavour pairs best with a warm, red interior.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with romesco sauce alongside. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (medium-rare, rosy).

### 5. Maangchi — Korean Grilled Beef Fillet (*Gui*)
**Source**: Maangchi, *Maangchi's Real Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2015), pp. 224–226; maangchi.com/recipe/  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+30 min marinating) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 beef fillet steaks, 200 g each
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- 2 garlic cloves, grated
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- ½ tsp white or black pepper
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced (garnish)
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds (garnish)

**Method**
1. Combine soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and pepper. Marinate steaks 30 minutes at room temperature.
2. Remove from marinade, pat dry.
3. Preheat gas grill to high.
4. Grill steaks 4 minutes first side.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side horizontally to the centre.
6. Flip. Grill 3–4 minutes.
7. **Pull at 55–57 °C** (131–135 °F). Korean grilled beef (*gui*) tradition prefers the medium band — the soy-sugar marinade's caramelised crust integrates better with a pink-grey interior than a red one.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Garnish with spring onion and sesame seeds. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (medium).

## What makes this method special
A gas grill provides consistent, adjustable heat across a wide cooking zone, enabling both the direct-heat sear and indirect-heat finishing in a single cook session with minimal intervention. For filet mignon, the two-zone setup is particularly valuable: the direct zone creates the necessary Maillard crust on a lean cut that has no fat to self-baste, while the indirect zone allows the interior temperature to rise gently to the target without surface carbonisation. Gas grill flavour is cleaner and more neutral than charcoal — the steak's flavour is not supplemented by smoke aromatics, making seasoning and any finishing butter or sauce the primary flavour contributors.

## Summary
Gas-grilled filet mignon is the most controllable high-heat outdoor method for this delicate cut — the adjustable, clean heat of a gas grill allows precise two-zone management without the charcoal smoke variable. Four of five traditions across America, Britain, and Catalonia target medium-rare at 54–57 °C, with the Korean tradition slightly higher at 57–60 °C. The absence of fat or connective tissue in filet mignon makes temperature accuracy more important than for any other beef cut.
