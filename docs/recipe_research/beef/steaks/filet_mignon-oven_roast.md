# Filet Mignon × Oven Roast (Reverse Sear) — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Reverse sear is especially valuable for filet mignon because the 40-50mm thickness means
a direct-sear-only approach creates an unacceptable gray band. Low oven (107-135°C /
225-275°F) on a wire rack brings the lean, fragile cut uniformly to 49-52°C, then a
90-second screaming-hot sear in cast iron builds the crust without further cooking
the interior.

## What makes reverse sear the ideal method for thick filet mignon
The lean, structurally-fragile tenderloin muscle dries out faster than any other cut when
the outer layers overshoot temperature during a direct-heat sear. The low oven phase
brings the entire cross-section to 49°C uniformly — then a 90-second sear adds crust
without overcooking even 1mm of the interior. Zero gray band. Maximum silk.
