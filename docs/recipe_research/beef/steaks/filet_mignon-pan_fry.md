# Filet Mignon × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan frying filet mignon uses a moderately high heat (cast iron or stainless steel) with
oil and typically a generous addition of butter, garlic, and herbs part-way through.
The heat is slightly lower than a "pan sear" blast — allowing the thick cut to cook
through without the outside charring before the inside is done. This is the classic
French "poêlé" technique and the standard approach in German and Swedish home kitchens.

## Source recipes

### 1. CookInDocs — How to Pan Fry Filet Mignon: The Ultimate Guide
- **Source**: cookindocs.com/how-to-pan-fry-filet-mignon
- **Method**: Cast iron or stainless, medium-high heat, canola/grapeseed oil.
  Add butter + garlic + thyme after first flip. Baste continually.
- **Target**: Medium-rare = **130-135°F / 54-57°C** internal. Rest 5-10 min.

### 2. Pan-Fried Filet Mignon — How to Cook Meat (howtocookmeat.com)
- **Source**: howtocookmeat.com/recipes/pan-fried-filet-mignon
- **Method**: Heavy pan, medium-high heat, oil first, butter baste with herbs.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** for medium-rare — "widely considered the best
  for filet mignon by top chefs in France and elsewhere."

### 3. Le Chef's Wife — How to Cook Filet Mignon (French technique)
- **Source**: lechefswife.com/how-to-cook-filet-mignon
- **Method**: Poêle, huile + beurre, ail, thym. French basting technique.
  "In France the butter-baste (arrosage) is the defining technique of the filet mignon."
- **Target**: **54-57°C** at heart (rosé result) — French "à point" for filet.

### 4. Grilled Cheese Social — Perfect Pan Seared Filet Mignon with Butter
- **Source**: grilledcheesesocial.com/pan-seared-filet-mignon-recipe
- **Method**: Cast iron, basting butter with aromatics, some use oven finish for very thick.
- **Target**: **130°F / 54°C** for medium-rare. Rest 5 min minimum.
- "Use a meat thermometer — filet mignon can overcook quickly."

### 5. Bake It With Love — Pan Seared Filet Mignon
- **Source**: bakeitwithlove.com/pan-seared-filet-mignon
- **Method**: Screaming-hot pan, thin film of oil, then butter baste with rosemary.
  Optional oven finish for extra-thick cuts.
- **Target**: Medium-rare **130-135°F / 54-57°C**
- Notes for European markets: "German and Swedish cooks often use clarified butter
  (Butterschmalz / klarat smör) instead of mixing oil and butter."

**Universal: medium-rare at 54-57°C.** Pan fry and pan sear reach the same internal
temperature; the distinction is technique (more oil, slightly lower heat) and surface
character (lighter crust in pan fry than blast-sear).

## What pan fry adds vs pan sear for this cut
Pan fry uses clarified butter or oil+butter from the start — producing the richly
aromatic, basted crust that French kitchens prize. The lower initial heat means the
lean tenderloin muscle isn't shocked, and the butter aromatics permeate the surface
gradually. Results in a silkier crust flavour profile than a pure high-heat sear.
Internal target: identical at 54°C.
