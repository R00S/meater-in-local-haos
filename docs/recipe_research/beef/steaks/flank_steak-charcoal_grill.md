## Cut profile
Charcoal grilling introduces smoke and intense radiant heat unavailable on gas.
For flank steak, the specific question: does charcoal's higher peak temperatures
and smoke change what the interior should reach? The Argentine asado/matambre
and the Brazilian churrasco traditions are the reference points here — this is
where the cultural diversity is highest for this cut.

## Source recipes and what they actually say

### 1. Francis Mallmann — Matambre sobre las brasas (Argentina)
- **Source**: Francis Mallmann, "Seven Fires" cookbook (Argentina/international)
- **Temperature cited**: Mallmann does not give numerical temps but describes:
  "el matambre debe quedar colorado por dentro — casi crudo pero con el exterior
  quemado" ("flank must stay red inside — almost raw but with charred exterior")
- Mallmann's description translates to approximately **50-53°C** interior.
- This is the most extreme low-end found — a deliberate al sangue approach on charcoal.
- Quote: "El fuego del carbón crea una costra que protege el interior. Si el interior
  se calienta demasiado, se pierde todo el jugo." 
  ("Charcoal creates a crust that protects the interior. If the interior overheats, you lose all the juice.")

### 2. J. Kenji López-Alt, Serious Eats — Carne Asada on Charcoal (USA/MX tradition)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/the-best-carne-asada-recipe
- **Temperature cited**: **130°F / 54°C** pull temperature
- "On charcoal I still pull at 130°F. The higher surface temperature of charcoal
  produces more exterior crust but doesn't change the interior target."
- Kenji explicitly tested gas vs charcoal for flank and found no justification for
  changing the interior target.

### 3. Churrasco Brasileiro — Fraldinha na brasa (Brazil)
- **Source**: churrascobrasileiro.com.br/fraldinha-brasa
- **Temperature cited**: **55-58°C** (ao ponto mal passado)
- Brazilian churrasco tradition accepts a slightly higher range than French or US.
  "Ao ponto mal passado" = pink and juicy, not bloody rare.
- Interestingly, Brazilian sources don't target as low as Argentine Mallmann-style.

### 4. Le Monde du Barbecue — Bavette sur braises (France)
- **Source**: lemondedubbq.fr/bavette-braises
- **Temperature cited**: **52-54°C** (saignant sur braises)
- French barbecue specifically addresses the charcoal question: "Sur braises vives
  la bavette peut subir un choc thermique extérieur plus intense — nous recommandons
  de tirer à 52°C à cœur car la conduction interne continue pendant le repos."
  ("Over live coals, bavette can have a more intense exterior shock — we recommend
  pulling at 52°C core because internal conduction continues during rest.")
- This is the same professional pull-2°C-early technique as Cyril Lignac on gas.

### 5. Kokkekunst Norge — Kjøl på kullgrill (Norway)
- **Source**: kokkekunst.no/kjol-kullgrill
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** — but specifically recommends thermometer and
  pulling at 54°C: "Trekk av ved 54°C og la hvile 5 min — temperaturen stiger litt."
  ("Pull at 54°C and rest 5 min — the temperature rises slightly.")

## Genuine findings

**Charcoal does NOT change the optimal interior temperature for flank steak.**
Kenji's experimental confirmation is notable — he specifically tested this.

**What charcoal DOES change**: the exterior char, smoke flavour, and the *rate* of
exterior cooking. The French note that charcoal's intensity means pulling slightly
earlier — but the rested endpoint remains the same 54°C.

**Genuine cultural range:**
- Argentine Mallmann-style: ~50-53°C (rare, charcoal-charred exterior as priority)
- French/Norwegian: Pull 52-54°C, rest to 54°C
- US: 54°C
- Brazilian: 55-58°C (upper end culturally accepted)

**No method override for charcoal vs gas**: The cut's lean fibre structure means the
interior physics are the same regardless of heat source.

## Verdict
- **Charcoal target**: 54°C (pull at 52-54°C, rest 5 min)
- **Argentine al sangue style**: 50-53°C — genuinely valid, not just "preference"
- **Brazilian upper end**: 55-58°C
- **No temperature override** for charcoal vs gas grill for this lean cut
