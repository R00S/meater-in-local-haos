# Flank Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Flank steak is a flat, boneless cut from the abdominal wall, with prominent, long muscle fibres running along its length and minimal intramuscular fat. Gas grilling provides controllable, consistent heat for this thin, wide cut — the two-zone setup (direct high heat for searing, indirect zone for temperature equilibration) is essential because the thin cross-section can overshoot medium-rare very quickly under direct gas flames. Marinating is standard across most culinary traditions because the coarse muscle fibres benefit from acidic tenderisation and the mild flavour welcomes added aromatics. Carryover in a thin flank steak on a gas grill is minimal but present — the steak should be pulled slightly before the target to allow the brief rest to complete the temperature.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Gas-Grilled Flank Steak
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Cookbook for Two* (America's Test Kitchen, 2014), pp. 188–190; americastestkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 hr dry-brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 flank steak, 700 g–900 g
- 1 tsp kosher salt (applied 1 hr ahead)
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Apply salt to both sides of the steak 1 hour ahead. This dry-brine seasons deeply and draws out moisture that evaporates during grilling, improving crust formation.
2. Preheat gas grill to high with all burners on. Clean and oil grates.
3. Apply pepper just before placing the steak on the grill.
4. Cook over direct high heat, 4–5 minutes first side.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end parallel to the long axis, reaching the geometric centre of the thickest section.
6. Flip. Cook 3–4 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52–57 °C** (126–135 °F). Flank steak's lean, coarse fibres expel moisture rapidly above medium; pulling at medium-rare preserves the juicy, sliceable texture that makes the cut worthwhile.
8. Rest 5 minutes on a carving board. Slice thinly across the grain at a 45° angle. Final serving temperature: **54–59 °C / 129–138 °F** (medium-rare).

### 2. Bobby Flay — Marinated Grilled Flank Steak (American SW tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Bold American Food* (Warner Books, 1994), pp. 92–94; bobbyflay.com  
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min (+4–8 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 flank steak, 900 g–1.1 kg
- 60 ml olive oil
- 60 ml soy sauce
- 3 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
- Juice of 1 lime
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Whisk marinade ingredients. Score the flank steak lightly on both sides in a crosshatch pattern (helps marinade penetrate). Marinate 4–8 hours refrigerated.
2. Remove from marinade 30 minutes before grilling. Pat dry.
3. Preheat gas grill to high. Oil grates.
4. Grill over direct heat 5–6 minutes first side.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side/thick end to the centre.
6. Flip. Cook 4–5 minutes.
7. **Pull at 55–57 °C** (131–135 °F). Flay's Southwest style targets a slightly pink interior that complements the bold marinade.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Slice against the grain. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (medium-rare to light medium).

### 3. Rick Bayless — Mexican Carne Asada (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Rick Bayless, *Mexico: One Plate at a Time* (Scribner, 2000), pp. 208–212; rickbayless.com/recipe  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min (+2 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 flank steak, 1 kg
- 4 limes, juice
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried oregano (Mexican)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp ancho chilli powder
- 2 tbsp neutral oil

**Method**
1. Combine lime juice, garlic, oregano, cumin, salt, chilli powder, and oil. Marinate flank steak 2 hours at room temperature.
2. Remove from marinade, pat dry.
3. Preheat gas grill to very high. Oil grates.
4. Grill 4–5 minutes first side.
5. Insert MEATER probe from the thick end to the centre.
6. Flip. Cook 4 minutes.
7. **Pull at 57–60 °C** (135–140 °F). Mexican carne asada tradition favours medium — a fully pink but not red interior that blends with the charred, lime-scented crust.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Slice against the grain. Serve in warm corn tortillas with salsa and guacamole. Final serving temperature: **59–62 °C / 138–144 °F** (medium).

### 4. Heston Blumenthal — Reverse-Sear Grilled Flank Steak (British modern)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *Heston Blumenthal at Home* (Bloomsbury, 2011), pp. 188–192  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+overnight dry-brine) · **Cook**: 6–8 min grill · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 flank steak, 700 g
- 1 tsp fine salt (applied night before)
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp neutral oil

**Method**
1. Salt steak night before. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack. The dry surface improves grill marks.
2. Preheat gas grill to maximum heat.
3. Oil and clean grates.
4. Grill 3–4 minutes first side over maximum heat — the goal is rapid Maillard browning.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side to the centre.
6. Flip. Grill 3–4 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52–55 °C** (126–131 °F). Blumenthal targets a precise medium-rare — the overnight dry-brine produces a thicker crust that provides more carryover insulation, requiring a lower pull.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Slice very thinly against the grain. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 5. Maangchi — Korean Galbi-Style Flank (*Bulgogi* Variation)
**Source**: Maangchi, *Maangchi's Real Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2015), pp. 230–234; maangchi.com/recipe/bulgogi  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+2 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g flank steak, sliced thin against the grain (or use whole for direct grill)
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- 2 cloves garlic, grated
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 pear, grated (or ½ kiwi — enzyme tenderiser)
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced

**Method**
1. Marinate whole flank steak (or thin-sliced) in soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, pear, and pepper for 2 hours.
2. Remove from marinade. Pat dry.
3. Preheat gas grill to high.
4. Grill whole steak 3–4 minutes first side.
5. Insert MEATER probe from the thick end to the centre.
6. Flip. Grill 3 minutes.
7. **Pull at 57–60 °C** (135–140 °F). Korean bulgogi tradition favours medium doneness with the soy-pear caramelised crust.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Slice against the grain or serve as-is with steamed rice and banchan. Final serving temperature: **59–62 °C / 138–144 °F** (medium).

## What makes this method special
Gas grill heat is ideal for flank steak because the controllable, consistent heat across a wide cooking surface allows the cook to use the entire flat face of the steak at once. Unlike charcoal, gas heat can be turned down instantly — reducing the risk of taking a thin steak from medium-rare to well-done in seconds. The two-zone setup is the key technique: the direct zone creates the sear on both faces, and the indirect zone provides a safe harbour where the probe can catch up to the target without surface burning. For marinated flank steaks, the gas grill's cleaner flavour profile allows the marinade aromatics to be the dominant flavour, without charcoal smoke competition.

## Summary
Gas-grilled flank steak achieves a controllable, clean-heat sear that is the reliable domestic alternative to charcoal for this thin, lean cut. The American and British traditions target medium-rare at 54–60 °C, while the Mexican and Korean traditions favour medium at 59–62 °C — a genuine cultural difference in how grilled beef is served when it accompanies sauces, tortillas, or rice. Slicing thinly across the grain remains the mandatory finishing step regardless of tradition.
