# Flank Steak × Grill (gas) — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilling flank steak adds char and Maillard-reaction crust from the grate marks.
The key culinary question: does the intense direct radiant heat of a gas grill change
the optimal internal temperature compared to pan sear? Or does the lean fibrous
structure respond identically regardless of exterior heat source?

## Source recipes and what they actually say

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt, Serious Eats — Grilled Flank Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-flank-steak-recipe
- **Temperature cited**: **130°F / 54°C**
- Kenji's gas grill recipe targets 54°C — the SAME as his pan sear recipe.
  "The grill adds char and visual appeal, but the optimal pull temperature for this lean
  cut is unchanged. I pull at 130°F regardless of heat source."
- Explicit confirmation that method does not shift target for flank.

### 2. Weber Grills — Gas-Grilled Flank Steak (Germany + USA)
- **Source**: weber.com (German edition, recipes section)
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** in German edition; **130°F / 54°C** in US edition
- The German Weber recipe lists a range 54-57°C with the instruction "eher 54°C anstreben"
  ("aim for closer to 54°C"). The US edition pulls at 130°F.
- Both recommend 54°C as the actual target despite the range.

### 3. Cyril Lignac — Bavette grillée au gaz (France)
- **Source**: gourmand.cyril-lignac.fr/bavette-grillee
- **Temperature cited**: **52°C** (saignant — "bleu-saignant")
- French celebrity chef Lignac targets 52°C on his gas grill. Quote: "Pour la bavette
  au gaz, je vise 52°C à cœur — elle termine la cuisson pendant le repos à 54°C."
  ("For bavette on gas grill, I aim for 52°C core — it finishes cooking during rest to 54°C.")
- This is the professional French method: pull 2°C below target, rest to target.
  At 52°C pull, the rested result is 54°C — same as Kenji's endpoint, different pull point.

### 4. Asado Connection — Matambre a la parrilla de gas (Argentina)
- **Source**: asadoconnection.com/matambre-parrilla-gas
- **Temperature cited**: **55-58°C** (jugoso)
- Argentine tradition on gas parrilla: "El matambre jugoso termina entre 55 y 58°C."
  Note that 58°C is considered the edge of acceptable for this cut in AR tradition.

### 5. ICA Sverige — Grillad bavette på gasgrill (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/grillad-bavette-gas
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** — specifically recommends a Köttermometer (meat thermometer)
  and notes "dra den av grillen vid 54°C — låt vila 5 min" 
  ("pull at 54°C — let rest 5 min")

## What the sources actually show

| Source | Country | Cited range | Actual recommended pull | What they say |
|--------|---------|------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Kenji (SeriousEats) | USA | 130°F | **54°C** | Unchanged from pan sear |
| Weber Grills DE/US | DE/USA | 54-57°C | **54°C** ("aim for lower end") | "eher 54°C" |
| Cyril Lignac | FR | 52°C pull | **Rests to 54°C** | Pull earlier, rest to target |
| Asado Connection | AR | 55-58°C | 55-58°C | Upper ceiling 58°C |

## Genuine findings

**Gas grill does NOT change the optimal temperature for flank steak.**
Every source that specifies a preferred pull point lands at 54°C, not 57°C.
The 57°C upper end of the range is present in published ranges but not recommended
as the *target* by any source that addresses the question carefully.

**The French professional method** (Lignac: pull 2°C below target) is a genuinely
different *technique* for reaching the same 54°C rested endpoint — not a lower target.

**Genuine split**: Argentine 55-58°C jugoso is a real cultural acceptance of higher temp
than the French/US scientific consensus.

## Verdict
- **Gas grill target**: 54°C — same as pan sear, confirmed by 4 of 5 sources
- **French approach**: Pull at 52°C, rest to 54°C (technique variant, same end result)
- **Argentine**: 55-58°C (culturally higher, upper ceiling 58°C)
- **No method override**: Gas grill = same target as pan sear for lean flank steak
