## Cut profile
Oven roasting flank steak is less common but used for stuffed preparations (Italian
braciole, Argentine matambre relleno) and when grilling isn't possible. The key
question: does the oven — which surrounds the meat with convection heat rather than
applying radiant or contact heat to a surface — change the optimal temperature?

Secondary question: stuffed preparations (spinach, cheese, herbs rolled inside) raise
a food-safety concern about fillings that must be considered separately from the plain
meat target.

## Source recipes and what they actually say

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt, Serious Eats — Oven-Roasted Flank Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/oven-roasted-flank-steak
- **Temperature cited**: **130°F / 54°C** pull, oven at 230°C
- "Even in the oven, I target 130°F for flank. The convection doesn't change the
  fibrous structure — it will still tighten above 60°C. I use a very hot oven
  (450°F) to drive fast crust formation, then the residual heat finishes the interior."
- Recommends sear-then-oven or oven-then-broil, not slow oven.

### 2. Lidia Bastianich — Braciole di Manzo (Italy/USA)
- **Source**: lidiasitaly.com/recipe/braciole-di-manzo (stuffed flank roll)
- **Temperature cited**: **71°C / 160°F** for stuffed braciole
- **Critical note**: Lidia targets 71°C SPECIFICALLY because the stuffing (cheese,
  egg, breadcrumbs, parsley) must reach safe temperature. She explicitly states:
  "For unstuffed flank, 54°C is ideal — but stuffed braciole must reach 71°C
  (160°F) for the filling to be safely cooked."
- **This IS a genuine method-specific temperature override for stuffed oven preparations.**

### 3. La Cuchara de Madera — Matambre relleno al horno (Argentina)
- **Source**: lacucharademadera.com.ar/matambre-relleno-horno
- **Temperature cited**: **65-70°C** (relleno bien cocido — filling well cooked)
- Argentine stuffed matambre (hard-boiled eggs, olives, peppers inside):
  "El matambre relleno debe llegar a los 65-70°C para que el relleno esté bien."
  ("Stuffed matambre must reach 65-70°C for the filling to be properly done.")
- NOTE: This is a safety/food-quality requirement for the filled version, not a
  preference for how to cook the meat itself.

### 4. Gordon Ramsay / Great British Chefs — Stuffed Flank Steak (UK)
- **Source**: greatbritishchefs.com/recipes/stuffed-flank-steak
- **Temperature cited (plain, unstuffed equivalent)**: **54°C** medium-rare
- **Temperature cited (stuffed)**: **"cook through until filling is hot"** ≈ 71-74°C
- Great British Chefs is explicit that unstuffed = 54°C but stuffed = fully cooked through.

### 5. Matprat — Fylt biffrulle i ovn (Norway) [stuffed roll]
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/fylt-biffrulle
- **Temperature cited**: **68-72°C** for stuffed biffrulle (flank rolled with filling)
- "Kjernetemperaturen i rullen bør nå 68-70°C, av hensyn til fyllet."
  ("The core temperature in the roll should reach 68-70°C, considering the filling.")

## Genuine findings

**Plain flank steak in the oven**: target is 54°C — no change from grill or pan.
The oven's convection does not alter the fibrous structure physics.

**Stuffed/rolled flank (braciole, matambre relleno)**: THIS IS A GENUINE METHOD OVERRIDE.
Stuffed preparations require 65-71°C for the filling's safety and quality. This is
not a "preference" — it is a food safety requirement confirmed by multiple culinary
authorities from different traditions.

**The oven roast leaf is split into two distinct cooking scenarios:**
1. Plain flank: 54°C (same as all other methods)
2. Stuffed/rolled: 65-71°C (safety + filling quality requirement)

## Verdict
- **Plain oven-roasted flank**: 54°C — same as all other methods
- **Stuffed braciole/matambre relleno**: 65-71°C — safety override, not a preference
- **French tradition in the oven**: Same saignant preference (52-54°C) as other methods
