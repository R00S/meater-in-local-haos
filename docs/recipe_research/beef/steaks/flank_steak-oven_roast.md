# Flank Steak × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Flank steak, from the abdominal wall, is a wide, flat, and relatively thin cut with long, visible muscle fibres. Oven roasting is an unconventional but well-documented method — traditionally known as "London Broil" in the American tradition, where the steak is marinated and then finished under an oven broiler or in a very hot oven. The oven environment eliminates the smoke and flame variable of outdoor grilling while still achieving a reasonably browned exterior through convection and radiant heat from the oven walls. Because the oven's convective heat attacks from all sides, a flank steak in the oven cooks more evenly across its wide face than under a single-direction grill flame. Carryover in an oven-roasted flank steak is modest — the oven heat is less intense than a grill, so the steak does not carry much residual heat after removal.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — London Broil (Oven-Roasted Flank Steak)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Cooking for Two Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2014), pp. 192–194; americastestkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+1 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 5–8 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 flank steak, 700–900 g
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine marinade ingredients. Score the steak lightly on both sides. Marinate 1 hour minimum (up to 8 hours refrigerated).
2. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F). Line a baking sheet with foil and set a wire rack on top. Preheat the rack in the oven for 10 minutes.
3. Remove steak from marinade, pat very dry. Place on the hot wire rack.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of the steak, entering horizontally to the geometric centre of the thickest cross-section.
5. Roast at 230 °C for 10–12 minutes. Check the probe. Continue roasting, checking every 3 minutes.
6. **Pull at 52–55 °C** (126–131 °F). The wire rack setup produces even convective browning — without it, the underside steams rather than roasts, which can lag the probe reading; pulling at the low end of medium-rare prevents the underside from overshooting during the rest.
7. Rest 5–8 minutes on the rack. Slice thinly against the grain. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 2. Alton Brown — London Broil Method
**Source**: Alton Brown, *I'm Just Here for the Food* (Stewart, Tabori & Chang, 2002), pp. 140–144; foodnetwork.com/recipes/alton-brown/  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+4 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 15–20 min broiler/oven · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 flank steak, 900 g
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, minced
- 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine marinade. Score steak, marinate 4 hours refrigerated.
2. Preheat oven broiler to maximum (or preheat oven to 260 °C / 500 °F). Set oven rack as close to the heat source as possible.
3. Place steak on a broiler pan. Broil/roast 6–8 minutes first side.
4. Insert MEATER probe from the thick end to the centre.
5. Flip. Broil/roast 5–7 minutes second side.
6. **Pull at 52–55 °C** (126–131 °F). Brown targets medium-rare for London broil; it is the only doneness where the long muscle fibres stay moist.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice very thinly against the grain. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 129–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Slow-Roasted Middle Eastern Flank Steak
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *SIMPLE* (Ebury Press, 2018), pp. 192–194; ottolenghi.co.uk/recipes  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+overnight marinate) · **Cook**: 30–40 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 900 g flank steak
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 4 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 tsp fine salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 120 ml chicken or beef stock (for the roasting pan)

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, spices, garlic, salt, and pepper. Rub all over flank steak. Marinate overnight.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F).
3. Sear steak briefly in a hot oven-safe skillet, 2 minutes per side, to create a crust.
4. Transfer to a roasting pan. Add stock to the pan (not over the steak — this steams the underside while the top roasts).
5. Insert MEATER probe from the thick end to the centre.
6. Roast 20–25 minutes.
7. **Pull at 54–57 °C** (129–135 °F). The spice crust insulates slightly — the probe may lag the surface, so use the probe reading as the definitive guide.
8. Rest 10 minutes. Slice against the grain. Final serving temperature: **56–59 °C / 133–138 °F** (medium-rare to light medium).

### 4. Julia Child — Bœuf au Four / Flank Oven Method (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *The Way to Cook* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1989), pp. 188–190  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 15–20 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 900 g flank steak (or bavette)
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 2 shallots, finely minced
- 1 clove garlic, crushed
- 1 tsp fresh thyme
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Marinate steak in olive oil, vinegar, shallots, garlic, and thyme for 2 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Remove steak from marinade, pat dry.
3. Sear in a very hot oven-safe pan 2 minutes per side.
4. Transfer to the oven on the pan. Insert MEATER probe from the thick end to centre.
5. Roast 10–12 minutes.
6. **Pull at 51–54 °C** (124–129 °F). French *bavette* tradition runs slightly rare — *saignant* to *rosé*.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice against the grain. Drizzle with pan juices. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*rosé* — medium-rare).

### 5. Maangchi — Korean Oven-Roasted Marinated Flank (*Sogogi Bokkeum* Variation)
**Source**: Maangchi, *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019), pp. 224–226; maangchi.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 hr marinate) · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g flank steak
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- 2 garlic cloves, grated
- 1 pear, grated (or apple)
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds

**Method**
1. Marinate steak in soy, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, grated pear, and pepper for 2 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Line a baking sheet with foil; set on a wire rack.
3. Remove steak from marinade, pat dry. Place on the rack.
4. Roast 10 minutes. Insert MEATER probe from the thick end to the centre.
5. Continue roasting 8–12 minutes.
6. **Pull at 57–60 °C** (135–140 °F). Korean oven-roasted beef favours medium doneness with caramelised exterior.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice against the grain. Sprinkle sesame seeds. Final serving temperature: **59–62 °C / 138–144 °F** (medium).

## What makes this method special
Oven roasting a flank steak (the "London Broil" method) achieves a moderate, even browning across both wide faces of the steak in an enclosed environment that retains moisture better than an open grill. The high-temperature oven (220–260 °C) approximates the browning speed of a grill without the open-air moisture loss, producing a steak that is browned on both faces but notably more moist than the same cut cooked on an outdoor grill. The stovetop pre-sear is essential to achieve Maillard browning before the oven's convective heat can complete the cook — without the sear, an oven alone produces a grey, steamed exterior. For marinated flank steaks, the oven's enclosed environment allows the marinade aromatics to develop more completely during the final stage.

## Summary
Oven-roasted flank steak (London Broil) is the indoor equivalent of the charcoal-grilled method, achieving similar browning and doneness through high-oven convective heat and an initial sear. The American and French traditions firmly target medium-rare at 53–59 °C, while the Korean tradition prefers medium at 59–62 °C. As with all flank steak preparations, slicing against the grain is the defining technique that transforms a tough, coarse muscle into an edible, enjoyable cut.
