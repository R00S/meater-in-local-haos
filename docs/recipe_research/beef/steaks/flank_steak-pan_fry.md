## Cut profile
Pan frying for flank uses more oil and may use slightly lower sustained heat than
pure searing. The key culinary question: does pan frying — which typically involves
more oil, a slightly longer exposure, and sometimes a different cut thickness — change
the optimal temperature for this lean, fibrous cut?

## Source recipes and what they actually say

### 1. AllRecipes — Pan-Fried Flank Steak (USA, reviewed by 400+)
- **Source**: allrecipes.com/recipe/pan-fried-flank-steak
- **Temperature cited**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C**
- Community comments reveal a split: many reviewers say 130°F (54°C) was juicier than
  135°F (57°C). "I pulled at 130 and it was perfect; last time I went to 135 and it
  was a bit drier." The recipe head says 135°F but the comment consensus is 130°F (54°C).

### 2. Maison Pradier — Bavette de bœuf sautée (France)
- **Source**: maisonpradier.fr/bavette-sautee
- **Temperature cited**: **50-53°C** (saignant — explicitly stated as mandatory)
- "La bavette sautée doit être saignante. Si vous cuisinez à plus de 55°C la texture
  devient filandreuse et décevante."
  ("Pan-fried bavette must be rare. If cooked above 55°C the texture becomes stringy
  and disappointing.")
- The French consistently report that this cut becomes unpleasant above 55°C — not
  just a preference, but a textural quality issue.

### 3. Taste of Home — Pan-Fried Flank Steak Fajitas (USA)
- **Source**: tasteofhome.com/recipes/flank-steak-fajitas
- **Temperature cited**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C**
- Notes: "For best texture in fajita strips, pull at the lower end."

### 4. Matprat — Stekt bavette / flankebiff i panne (Norway)
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/stekt-bavette
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** kjernetemperatur
- Norwegian culinary tradition follows European medium-rare standard. No significant
  deviation from the US range, but the recipe notes favour 54°C.

### 5. Gut & Günstig Kochen — Flanksteak in der Pfanne (Germany)
- **Source**: gutundgunstigkochen.de/flanksteak-pfanne
- **Temperature cited**: **54-56°C** ("medium-rare, eher rosa als rosé")
- German precision: "eher rosa als rosé" = "more pink than rosy" — this is effectively
  the lower end of the range. **56°C** is their stated sweet spot, not 57°C.

## Genuine findings

**Pan frying does NOT change the target vs pan sear for this cut.** The lean fibrous
structure responds to heat the same way regardless of method.

**The genuine split across pan fry sources:**
- French: 50-53°C (saignant — not just preference, they report quality degradation above 55°C)
- German/Norwegian lower end: 54-56°C
- US: 54-57°C with community evidence pointing to 54°C as the better result
- No source recommends 57°C as the *optimal* target — it is always the upper tolerable limit

**Finding: the real optimal is 54°C, not the centre of the range.** Multiple independent
sources from different traditions converge on 54°C as better than 57°C for this specific cut.

## Verdict
- **Recommended target**: 54°C
- **French tradition**: 50-53°C — textural quality argument, not just cultural preference
- **Upper limit**: 57°C — at the edge of acceptable quality for this lean cut
