## Cut profile
Air frying a flat iron leverages the cut's most useful physical property: its uniform
thickness. The rapid circulating hot air of an air fryer behaves like a miniature
convection oven combined with an infra-red broiler — it browns the surface via Maillard
reaction while cooking the interior from all sides simultaneously. The flat iron's even
profile means the centre reaches the target at almost exactly the moment the exterior
reaches desired browning, making this one of the most reliable air-fryer steak results.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Preheat air fryer to 200°C (400°F). Pat steak completely dry; brush with
  oil; season generously. Cook 7–9 min total, flipping halfway, until probe reads 52°C.
  Rest on wire rack 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C** (from air fryer)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Preheating the air fryer is essential to get surface
  browning before the interior overcooks; the flat iron's thickness sits in a
  sweet spot where 7–9 min at 200°C achieves both goals simultaneously.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Steak: Flat Iron (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Air fryer at 200°C; cook 6 min, flip, cook 3–4 more min. Pull when probe
  reads 52–54°C. Rest 5 min uncovered on warm plate.
- **Pull temp**: 125–130°F / **52–54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: ATK testing found the flat iron outperforms thicker steaks
  in the air fryer because its uniform thickness eliminates the underdone-centre
  problem; flipping halfway ensures even browning on both sides.

### 3. Allrecipes — Air Fryer Flat Iron Steak (USA / International user base)
- **Source**: allrecipes.com/recipe/air-fryer-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Season and let sit 10 min at room temp. Air fryer 200°C / 400°F for
  8–10 min total, flipping once. Check temp; rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **55–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: The air fryer's enclosed chamber concentrates heat
  around the entire steak; the flat iron's modest fat content means it benefits from
  a slightly higher pull than ribeye to avoid dryness with the drying effect of
  the circulating air.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Flat iron i airfryer (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/flat-iron-i-airfryer
- **Method**: Värm airfryern till 200°C i 5 min. Torka av biffen; pensla med olja.
  Tillaga 7–8 min, vänd halvvägs. Kärntemperatur 54°C. Vila 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer recipe sites have grown substantially;
  flat iron is a recommended cut because its uniform shape gives predictable
  results in the enclosed hot-air environment.

### 5. Good Food AU — Air-Fryer Flat Iron Steak with Salsa (Australia)
- **Source**: goodfood.com.au/recipes/air-fryer-flat-iron-steak-salsa
- **Method**: Preheat air fryer 200°C. Season steak; cook 4 min per side (flip once).
  Pull at 54°C; rest 5 min under loose foil.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Australian food writers position the flat iron as the ideal
  air-fryer steak cut because its consistent thickness eliminates guesswork; salsa
  verde or chimichurri compensates for the slight lack of smokiness vs. charcoal.
