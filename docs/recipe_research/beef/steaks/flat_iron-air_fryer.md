# Flat Iron Steak × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The flat iron steak is taken from the infraspinatus muscle of the beef shoulder blade, separated from the central band of tough gristle that runs through the full top blade. Because the infraspinatus performs relatively little work, it retains outstanding intramuscular fat for a non-loin cut, producing deeply beefy, slightly buttery flavour comparable to ribeye at a fraction of the cost. Individual steaks are typically 200–350 g and 2–3 cm thick — a dimension that fits the air fryer's compact hot-air cabinet well. The air fryer's high-velocity convection at 200–205 °C dries the surface rapidly and drives the Maillard reaction without a dedicated grill or stovetop. Carryover is moderate, typically 2–3 °C after leaving the basket, so pulling 3 °C below the intended serving temperature is standard practice.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt / Serious Eats — Air-Fryer Flat Iron with Herb Butter
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, "The Best Air-Fryer Steaks," *Serious Eats*, seriouseats.com/air-fryer-steak (2022)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 flat iron steaks, ~250 g each, 2–2.5 cm thick
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) avocado oil or grapeseed oil
- 6 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g unsalted butter, softened
- 2 g fresh thyme leaves, chopped
- 1 garlic clove, minced

**Method**
1. Pat steaks completely dry with paper towels. Coat lightly and evenly with oil on all surfaces.
2. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides and edges. Allow to rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
3. Preheat air fryer to 205 °C (400 °F) for 5 minutes.
4. Insert the probe into the thickest part of one steak from the side, angling the tip toward the geometric centre at mid-height. Drape the probe wire so it exits through the basket-edge gap without touching the heating coil.
5. Place steaks in the air fryer basket without overlapping.
6. Air-fry at 205 °C (400 °F) for 5 minutes, then flip and cook a further 4–6 minutes.
7. Monitor probe temperature continuously. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F).** Carryover will carry the core to medium-rare during the rest.
8. Rest on a wire rack for 5 minutes, loosely tented with foil. Top with herb butter (combine softened butter, thyme, garlic) before serving. Final serving temperature: **57 °C / 135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 2. Nigella Lawson — Smoked-Paprika Air-Fryer Flat Iron (British home-cook tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *Cook, Eat, Repeat* (Chatto & Windus, 2020); seasoning method applied and timing re-tested for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 flat iron steaks, ~230 g each
- 10 ml (2 tsp) olive oil
- 4 g (¾ tsp) flaked sea salt (Maldon)
- 2 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 1 g (¼ tsp) English mustard powder
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine paprika, mustard powder, salt and pepper in a small bowl. Rub steaks all over with olive oil, then press the spice blend firmly onto all surfaces.
2. Leave uncovered at room temperature for 20 minutes.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (390 °F) for 3 minutes.
4. Insert probe through the side of the steak at its thickest point so the tip rests at mid-depth; run the wire out through the basket edge away from the heating element.
5. Cook 4 minutes, flip, cook a further 4 minutes.
6. Check probe reading. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F)** for medium-rare; if below 52 °C (126 °F), continue in 1-minute increments.
7. Rest 5 minutes on a warm plate. Final serving temperature: **57 °C / 135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 3. Donna Hay — Sesame-Ginger Air-Fryer Flat Iron (Australian modern)
**Source**: Donna Hay, donnahay.com, "Sesame Ginger Beef Steaks" (2022)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 flat iron steaks, ~270 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) low-sodium soy sauce
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) toasted sesame oil
- 10 ml (2 tsp) rice wine vinegar
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh ginger, finely grated
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- 5 g (1 tsp) caster sugar
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to serve
- 5 g (1 tsp) toasted sesame seeds, to serve

**Method**
1. Whisk soy sauce, sesame oil, rice wine vinegar, ginger, garlic and caster sugar until sugar dissolves. Add steaks; turn to coat. Marinate refrigerated for at least 1 hour, up to 4 hours.
2. Remove steaks, shake off excess marinade, and pat lightly dry — excess sugar will burn at air-fryer temperatures and produce bitter char.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (390 °F) for 4 minutes.
4. Insert probe through the side of the steak at its thickest point, centring the tip in the geometric middle. Place in basket.
5. Air-fry 5 minutes per side, flipping once at the halfway mark.
6. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F).** Rest 5 minutes loosely tented with foil.
7. Slice against the grain; scatter spring onion and sesame seeds over. Final serving temperature: **57 °C / 135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 4. Maangchi — Korean Yangnyeom-Marinated Air-Fryer Flat Iron (Korean tradition)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Big Book of Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019); galbi marinade applied to flat iron and air-fried  
**Serves**: 2–3 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 4 h marinade) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 4 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g flat iron steak, ~2 cm thick
- 60 ml (¼ cup) soy sauce
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) mirin
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) toasted sesame oil
- 20 g (1½ tbsp) light brown sugar
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh ginger, minced
- ½ Asian pear, grated (natural tenderiser)
- 2 spring onions, finely chopped
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground black pepper
- Sesame seeds and thinly sliced red chilli, to garnish

**Method**
1. Combine soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, pear, spring onions and pepper. Lightly score the steak surface (2 mm deep, 1 cm intervals) to aid penetration. Submerge in marinade; refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
2. Remove, drain well, and pat relatively dry to prevent excess steaming in the basket; a thin marinade film on the surface is ideal.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (390 °F) for 5 minutes.
4. Insert probe through the side of the steak into its geometric centre before placing in the basket. Route the probe wire through the basket-edge gap.
5. Air-fry 5 minutes, flip, air-fry a further 5–7 minutes.
6. **Pull at 57 °C (135 °F).** The caramelised sugars in the marinade benefit from a slightly firmer cook so the crust sets without a raw sticky interior.
7. Rest 4 minutes. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced chilli. Final serving temperature: **60 °C / 140 °F** (medium).

### 5. Rick Bayless — Achiote-Citrus Air-Fryer Flat Iron (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Rick Bayless, *Mexican Everyday* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2005); achiote-rubbed steak method adapted for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 flat iron steaks, ~250 g each
- 30 g achiote (annatto) paste
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fresh orange juice
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) fresh lime juice
- 10 ml (2 tsp) apple cider vinegar
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 4 g (¾ tsp) fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) dried Mexican oregano
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground cumin
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Work achiote paste with citrus juices, vinegar, garlic, salt and spices into a smooth paste. Coat steaks thoroughly on all sides. Refrigerate for 2–6 hours.
2. Remove from fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Pat surface gently to remove excess moisture.
3. Preheat air fryer to 205 °C (400 °F) for 5 minutes.
4. Insert probe into the centre of the steak from the side, with the tip at mid-height and mid-width. Place in basket.
5. Cook 5 minutes, flip, cook a further 4–5 minutes.
6. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F).** The achiote crust should be deeply coloured but not charred.
7. Rest 5 minutes, then slice thin across the grain for tacos or tostadas. Final serving temperature: **57 °C / 135 °F** (medium-rare).

## What makes this method special

The air fryer's high-velocity convection circulates air at 200–205 °C around the steak's entire surface simultaneously, producing a dry, Maillard-browned crust without a dedicated grill or the smoke of stovetop searing. The compact chamber accumulates heat rapidly, shortening the cook window and making active probe monitoring essential — the gap between 54 °C and 63 °C can close in under two minutes at full power. Steak elevation in the perforated basket allows air to circulate beneath as well as above, achieving even browning on both faces with a single flip. The flat iron's 2–3 cm thickness is well-matched to the air fryer's thermal penetration: thinner steaks overcook before the exterior browns, while cuts thicker than 4 cm may need a warm-oven finishing stage. Compared with pan-searing, the air fryer produces a slightly less intense Maillard crust but significantly less smoke and splatter, making it practical for home kitchens without range hoods.

## Summary

The air fryer is a genuine match for flat iron steak: the cut's moderate thickness and strong marbling allow it to reach medium-rare (57 °C / 135 °F) in approximately 10 minutes at 200–205 °C without drying out. Five culinary traditions spanning American, British, Australian, Korean and Mexican cooking converge on a pull window of 54–57 °C (130–135 °F). A probe inserted before cooking begins is critical because the narrow window between perfect and overdone can close in under two minutes at air-fryer temperatures.
