## Cut profile
Over live charcoal the flat iron reveals its shoulder heritage: the Maillard crust
takes on a faintly smoky depth that a gas grill cannot fully replicate, and the
fat-on-fire flares add char notes that complement the cut's mild mineral edge.
Because the flat iron is uniformly thin, the cook works at the boundary where the
exterior chars perfectly before the interior overshoots — a narrow window that makes
temperature awareness essential.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Charcoal-Grilled Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/charcoal-grilled-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Full chimney of lump charcoal; grill positioned 3–4 cm above coals.
  Sear 2–3 min per side, flip once. No lid. Pull at temp, rest 5 min off grill.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Lump charcoal at maximum heat caramelises the cut's
  surface in under 5 minutes total, leaving the interior perfectly rare-to-medium-rare.
  The brief cook time is exactly suited to this thin, tender cut.

### 2. Grillade.se — Flat Iron över kolen (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/flat-iron-over-kolen
- **Method**: Briketter tills askvita; direkt grillning 2–3 cm över glöden.
  2–3 min per sida. Vila 5 min. Skär mot fibrerna.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C** (medium-rare)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grillers have adopted the flat iron as a premium
  affordable cut; charcoal's intense radiant heat creates the char crust that
  elevates this value cut to a premium table.

### 3. Weber Grillakademie — Flat Iron über Holzkohle (Germany)
- **Source**: weber.com/DE/de/rezepte/rindfleisch/flat-iron-holzkohle
- **Method**: Holzkohle-Kugelgrill; volle Glut, Deckel offen. 2–3 min pro Seite
  direkt über der Glut. Kerntemperatur messen; Ruhezeit 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54–57°C** (medium-rare)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57–60°C**
- **Why this method works**: German grill culture prefers a slightly higher target for
  beef that has been marinated; the flat iron absorbs marinades well and tolerates
  the extra degree of doneness without drying.

### 4. Bon Appétit — Charcoal-Grilled Flat Iron Steak with Herb Butter (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/charcoal-grilled-flat-iron-herb-butter
- **Method**: Two-stage charcoal: high direct side and a cooler indirect side.
  Sear 2 min direct per side; check temp. Rest indirect 2 min if underdone.
  Rest off grill 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Two-stage charcoal lets a cook sear then briefly finish
  the flat iron without overshooting — useful for a cut that can dry out if
  pushed past medium.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Flat iron over kull (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/flat-iron-over-kull
- **Method**: Stor kull-grill; direkte over gløden 3 min per side. Termometer settes
  inn diagonalt på tykkeste punkt. Hvil 5 min innpakket i folie.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian food journalists have written extensively about
  the flat iron as a discovery cut; charcoal grilling is the showcase method
  because the smoke flavour justifies the cut's slightly more rustic chew.
