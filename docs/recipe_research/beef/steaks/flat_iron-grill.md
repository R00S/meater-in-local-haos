## Cut profile
The flat iron's uniform thickness and good intramuscular fat make it an exceptional
gas-grill steak — it does not need the extreme-heat specialness of charcoal to
express its character. A two-zone gas grill setup (sear over high, rest over medium)
gives precise control over carryover, and the cut's tenderness means even a few
degrees of overshoot are forgiving. This is the weeknight cut that consistently
rewards careful temperature management.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Flat Iron Steak on Gas Grill (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/beef/flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Preheat gas grill to high (230–260°C). Sear 2–3 min per side direct.
  Move to medium zone; continue 2–3 min to reach target. Rest 5 min, lid closed.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Gas grill's controllable zones suit a flat iron's even
  thickness; searing over high then finishing over medium mimics a restaurant's
  overhead broiler and infrared sear combination.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Two-zone fire; grill over high direct heat 3 min per side. No lid during
  sear. Rest indirect 3 min if needed. Total rest off grill: 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 125–130°F / **52–54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The flat iron's thickness (typically 2–3 cm) means direct
  high heat cooks through to the target in a single direct phase without the indirect
  finishing stage that thicker cuts require.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Grilled Flat Iron Steak with Salsa Verde (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/grilled-flat-iron-steak-salsa-verde
- **Method**: Gas BBQ on high. Oil and season the steak. 3–4 min per side; rest
  covered with foil 5 min before slicing thinly against the grain.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C** (medium-rare)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The salsa verde's acidity balances the cut's mineral
  flavour note; grilling over gas gives the exterior caramelisation that brings out
  the cut's shoulder-beef complexity.

### 4. Matprat — Flat iron-biff på gass (Norway)
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/flat-iron-gass
- **Method**: Gasskomplett (gas grill) på høyeste varme; rist godt oljet. 3 min per
  side. Hviletid 5 min, gjerne under løst folie.
- **Pull temp**: **130°F / 54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian grilling season is short; gas grills are the
  dominant outdoor tool and the flat iron's reasonable price makes it ideal for
  weeknight outdoor cooking.

### 5. Agrodolce — Flat iron alla griglia a gas (Italy)
- **Source**: agrodolce.it/flat-iron-alla-griglia
- **Method**: Griglia a gas portata al massimo (230°C). Asciugare bene la carne; sale
  solo dopo la cottura (tradizione italiana). 3 min per lato. Riposo 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **52–54°C** (al sangue / media)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Italian tradition of salting after cooking preserves
  moisture during the brief grill contact; the cut's lean-to-fat balance works well
  at the Italian saignant target.
