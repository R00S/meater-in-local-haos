## Cut profile
Oven roasting a flat iron is the gentler path: ambient convection heat surrounds the
steak from all sides, eliminating the surface-to-core gradient created by direct pan
contact. The cut's uniform thickness makes it ideal for oven finishing after a quick
sear — the reverse-sear or sear-roast combination gives a flat iron a crust and an
even edge-to-edge interior simultaneously. The shoulder muscle's slightly firmer
texture actually benefits from the slower heat progression.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Reverse-Sear Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/reverse-sear-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Oven at 120°C (250°F) on a wire rack; cook to 5°C below target (49°C).
  Rest 10 min; finish in screaming-hot cast iron 60 seconds per side. Total rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp (oven stage)**: 110°F / **43–46°C**
- **Final after sear + rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The reverse sear removes any grey band from the flat iron;
  the oven phase brings the entire steak to a uniform temperature before the
  final sear creates crust without adding cooking to the interior.

### 2. Bon Appétit — Oven-Roasted Flat Iron with Compound Butter (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/oven-roasted-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Sear 1 min per side in cast iron; transfer (skillet and all) to 200°C
  oven for 6–8 min. Pull at target; rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The oven provides even ambient heat that finishes the
  interior gently while the pre-seared crust remains sealed; the skillet-transfer
  technique keeps the steak in contact with residual pan heat for additional crust.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Oven-Cooked Flat Iron Steak (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/oven-cooked-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Pan-sear in butter and oil 1–2 min per side; transfer to baking tray.
  Oven 200°C fan for 5–6 min. Rest 5 min tented.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: UK home kitchens favour the sear-then-oven technique for
  steak because it fits the domestic workflow; the flat iron's thickness makes it
  the ideal candidate for this method as it is rarely too thin to benefit from
  oven finishing.

### 4. ICA — Flat Iron i ugn (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/flat-iron-ugn
- **Method**: Ugn 175°C. Bryn biffen i het stekpanna 1–2 min per sida; ställ in i ugn
  tills kärntemperatur 54°C. Vila 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish supermarket recipe guides position the flat iron
  as a practical value cut; sear-then-oven is the standard domestic method and gives
  reliable results without specialist equipment.

### 5. Sale&Pepe — Flat Iron al forno (Italy)
- **Source**: salepepe.it/ricette/flat-iron-al-forno
- **Method**: Rosolare in padella con olio d'oliva 1 min per lato; terminare in forno
  a 180°C per 7–9 min. Riposo 5 min sotto carta alluminio.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C** (al sangue-cottura media)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Italian oven method for beef gives the cook complete
  control over internal temperature without constant attention; the flat iron's even
  thickness ensures uniform cooking in the oven phase.
