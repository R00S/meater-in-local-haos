# Flat Iron Steak × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan frying a flat iron differs from pan searing mainly in the oil level and sustained
heat management: slightly more fat in the pan, slightly more moderate heat, to cook
the steak through without the violent crust-first approach of a sear. For the flat
iron this matters because the cut's uniform thickness allows a very controlled cook —
the interior reaches the target almost simultaneously with the crust forming, making
this method ideal when a thinner, more evenly browned exterior is preferred over the
thick charred crust of a full sear.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Pan-Fried Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/pan-fried-flat-iron
- **Method**: Heat 2 tsp neutral oil in cast iron over medium-high until shimmering.
  Season steak; cook 3–4 min per side, adjusting heat to maintain steady sizzle without
  scorching. Rest 5 min before slicing.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The more moderate heat of pan frying vs. searing allows
  better heat penetration into a flat iron's moderate thickness without creating a
  thick charred exterior — useful when serving with delicate pan sauces.

### 2. Bon Appétit — Flat Iron Steak with Garlic Herb Sauce (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/flat-iron-steak-garlic-herb
- **Method**: Medium-high heat with 1 tbsp oil; cook 3–4 min per side. Deglaze pan
  with wine; make quick pan sauce while steak rests 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Pan frying flat iron facilitates a pan sauce by leaving
  more fond than a dry-sear method; the slightly lower heat gives time to build
  colour evenly across the whole surface.

### 3. Cuisine Actuelle — Bifteck de paleron à la poêle (France)
- **Source**: cuisineactuelle.fr/recettes/bifteck-paleron-poele
- **Method**: Poêle en fonte chauffée à feu vif avec beurre clarifié. 3 min par face,
  arrosage régulier. Sel et poivre uniquement après cuisson. Repos 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **52–54°C** (à point / saignant)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: French beurre clarifié allows higher sustained heat than
  whole butter while contributing dairy sweetness to the crust; the cut's tenderness
  means saignant to à-point is the authentic French target.

### 4. Taste.com.au — Pan-Fried Flat Iron Steak (Australia)
- **Source**: taste.com.au/recipes/pan-fried-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Flat iron to room temp; season well. Medium-high heat with rice bran or
  vegetable oil; 3–4 min per side. Rest 5 min on warm board.
- **Pull temp**: **54–57°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Australian home-cook guides align with the mid-range
  medium-rare target; pan frying is the preferred home method for this cut as it
  requires less specialised equipment than searing at extreme heat.

### 5. Godt.no — Flat iron-biff i panne med soppaus (Norway)
- **Source**: godt.no/oppskrifter/flat-iron-biff-soppaus
- **Method**: Middels høy varme med olivenolje; 3 min per side. Smørbasting
  siste minutt. Hviletid 5 min på skjærebrett.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Nordic cooks pair the flat iron's accessible price point
  with a mushroom cream sauce; pan frying at measured heat suits indoor cooking and
  leaves the pan fond essential for building the sauce.
