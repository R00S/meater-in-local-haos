# Flat Iron Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Flat iron comes from the top blade of the shoulder (chuck), specifically the
infraspinatus muscle. Once the tough central membrane is removed the result is the
second most tender cut in the animal — a flat, even-thickness steak with fine grain and
generous marbling that makes pan searing one of its finest methods. The uniform thickness
means a single cast-iron pan can cook it to an edge-to-edge gradient that rivals any
steakhouse result, with none of the carryover unpredictability of thicker cuts.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Pan-Seared Flat Iron Steak (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-seared-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Pat dry; season 45 min before cooking. Ripping-hot cast iron with a film
  of high-smoke-point oil. Sear 2–3 min per side without moving; butter baste with
  thyme and garlic in the final 60 seconds. Rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 125–130°F / **52–54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The flat iron's even thickness and good marbling reward
  the same high-heat method as ribeye. Butter basting accelerates the Maillard crust
  without pushing the interior beyond medium-rare.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Flat Iron Steak with Herb Butter (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/flat-iron-steak-herb-butter
- **Method**: Thoroughly dry the surface; pre-salt 1 hour prior. Carbon-steel or
  cast-iron pan at highest heat; sear 2 min per side, reduce to medium-high, cook to
  temp. Tent and rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 120–125°F / **49–52°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: ATK's lower pull acknowledges the significant carryover
  from a ripping-hot pan; pulling at 120–125°F guarantees the 130°F rest target
  is hit without overshooting into medium.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Pan-fried Flat Iron Steak with Green Peppercorn Sauce (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/pan-fried-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Bring to room temp 30 min. Cast iron or heavy-based frying pan, very hot.
  Season just before cooking. Sear 2–3 min per side; rest 5 min on a warm plate before
  serving with pan sauce.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C** (medium-rare)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: UK recipes acknowledge the flat iron as an affordable
  blade cut that punches above its price; pan searing maximises the Maillard character
  that compensates for the cut's slightly more rustic texture versus sirloin.

### 4. Donna Hay — Seared Flat Iron Steak with Chimichurri (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/flat-iron-steak-chimichurri
- **Method**: Season 20 min before cooking. Heaviest available pan; medium-high heat.
  2–3 min per side; rest 5 min loosely covered.
- **Pull temp**: **54–57°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Australian practice targets the top of medium-rare as a
  reliable home-cook result for a cut not universally known in that market; the
  slightly higher pull is a safeguard against undercooked perception.

### 5. Matprat — Flat Iron-biff i panne (Norway)
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/flat-iron-biff-i-panne
- **Method**: Kjøttet tas ut av kjøleskap 30 min før steiking (room temp 30 min).
  Varm panne med olje og smør; 2–3 min per side på høy varme. Hvil 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 54°C / 130°F
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Nordic tradition treats the flat iron as a budget
  alternative to entrecôte; same pan technique, same rest discipline, same result.
