# Flat Iron Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Flat iron is cut from the shoulder (chuck top blade) with the tough connective tissue
line removed. It has good marbling for a shoulder cut, making it one of the most
tender and flavourful value steaks. Behaves more like a ribeye than a flank —
responds well to butter-basting and resting. Target: medium-rare.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Pan-Seared Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-seared-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Pat dry, season well. Cast iron ripping hot. Sear 3-4 min/side,
  baste with butter, garlic, thyme last 2 min. Rest 5 min.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare
- "Flat iron has enough marbling to benefit from butter-basting, unlike flank."

### 2. Delish — Pan-Seared Flat Iron Steak (USA, 4.8★)
- **Source**: delish.com/cooking/recipe-ideas/pan-seared-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Cast iron, high heat. Sear 3-4 min/side. Butter baste. Rest.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 3. Madblogger.dk — Pandestegt flatiron bøf (Denmark)
- **Source**: madblogger.dk/pandestegt-flatiron-bof
- **Method**: Støbejernspande meget varm. Olie+smør. 3-4 min per side. Baste.
  Hvil 5 min. Kan serveres i skiver.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / medium blodig)

### 4. 750g — Pavé de rumsteak / Flat Iron à la poêle (France)
- **Source**: 750g.com/recette/flat-iron-poele
- **Method**: Poêle en fonte très chaude. Beurre + ail + thym. 3-4 min/côté.
  Arroser régulièrement. Reposer 5 min.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (rosé / saignant pour certains: 52-54°C)

### 5. Taste — Pan-seared flat iron steak (Australia)
- **Source**: taste.com.au/recipes/pan-seared-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Heavy skillet high heat. Oil. 3-4 min/side depending on thickness.
  Butter baste. Rest 5 min minimum.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** medium-rare

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Serious Eats | USA | 54-57°C |
| Delish | USA | 54-57°C |
| Madblogger.dk | DK | 54-57°C |
| 750g | FR | 54-57°C (52-54°C saignant) |
| Taste | AU | 54-57°C |

**Consensus: 54-57°C medium-rare.** Flat iron tolerates slightly higher temps than flank
because of its marbling, but medium-rare is the universal recommendation. Unlike flank,
butter-basting is appropriate and improves the result.
