## Cut profile
Sous vide gives the flat iron something its shoulder heritage would not normally allow:
a perfectly even edge-to-edge interior at a precisely chosen temperature, with none of
the gradient that conventional cooking creates. The cut's moderate marbling and fine
grain benefit from the extended bath time, which softens the slight connective-tissue
character of the shoulder muscle without losing the steak's inherent bite. Unlike the
highly marbled ribeye, flat iron does not need the higher 57°C bath — 54°C achieves
the optimal silky texture for this leaner profile.

## Source recipes

### 1. Anova Culinary — Sous Vide Flat Iron Steak (USA)
- **Source**: recipes.anovaculinary.com/recipe/sous-vide-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Season and vacuum-seal; bath at 130°F / 54°C for 1–4 hours. Remove,
  pat thoroughly dry, rest uncovered 10 min. Sear 45–60 seconds per side in
  screaming-hot cast iron with clarified butter.
- **Bath temp**: 130°F / **54°C**, 1–4 h
- **Final (after 60 s sear, no rest needed)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The flat iron's even thickness reaches bath temperature
  in under 1 hour; the extended soak allows gentle enzymatic tenderisation of the
  shoulder muscle's modest connective tissue without any risk of overcooking.

### 2. Serious Eats — Sous Vide Flat Iron Steak Guide (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sous-vide-flat-iron-steak-guide
- **Method**: 130°F / 54°C bath for 1–2 h is sufficient; longer (up to 4 h) noticeably
  softens the cut. Finish with 45-second per side sear. Rest 2 min max.
- **Bath temp**: **54°C**, 1–2 h recommended
- **Final after sear**: **54–55°C**
- **Why this method works**: Unlike ribeye, flat iron's marbling does not benefit from
  the higher 57°C bath temperature; 54°C preserves the cut's lean, clean flavour
  profile while eliminating the grey band.

### 3. ChefSteps — Flat Iron Steak Sous Vide (USA)
- **Source**: chefsteps.com/activities/sous-vide-flat-iron-steak
- **Method**: Bag with rosemary sprig and garlic clove; bath 54°C / 1–3 h. Remove,
  dry, sear 60 s per side in neutral oil at maximum heat. Finish with cold butter
  in pan; baste 30 s.
- **Bath temp**: **54°C**, 1–3 h
- **Final after sear**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Aromatics infuse gently during the extended bath at this
  temperature without the off-flavour risk that higher temperatures can create; the
  brief cold-butter baste adds gloss and dairy sweetness to the crust.

### 4. Sous Vide Everything (Guga Foods) — Flat Iron Steak Test (USA/YouTube)
- **Source**: youtube.com/SousVideEverything (Guga's flat iron test episode)
- **Method**: Tested 130°F / 54°C vs 135°F / 57°C. Unanimous preference for 54°C for
  flat iron. "At 57°C the flat iron starts to lose its tenderness advantage — it's
  not a fatty cut like ribeye. 54°C is the sweet spot."
- **Bath temp**: **130°F / 54°C**, 1–2 h (winner)
- **Final after sear**: **54–55°C**
- **Why this method works**: The popular YouTube sous vide channel conducted blind
  tastings confirming that 54°C is optimal for lean-to-moderate-fat cuts; higher
  temperatures that benefit highly marbled cuts actively harm leaner ones.

### 5. Smakworld — Sous vide flat iron (Sweden)
- **Source**: smakworld.se/sous-vide-flat-iron
- **Method**: Vakuumförpacka med salt, peppar och timjan. Bad 54°C i 1–3 timmar.
  Torka av noggrant; bryn i het järnpanna 45–60 sek per sida. Servera direkt.
- **Bath temp**: **54°C**, 1–3 h
- **Final after sear**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish sous vide guides consistently recommend 54°C for
  leaner steak cuts; the predictability of sous vide suits home cooks cooking for
  guests where timing flexibility is essential.
