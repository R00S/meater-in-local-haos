# Hanger Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Over live charcoal the hanger steak is transformed: the woodsmoke infiltrates the open
grain in a way that feels designed by nature for this cut — as if the muscle that
has spent a lifetime absorbing the animal's deepest flavours was always meant to finish
over oak or mesquite. The challenge over charcoal is the same as everywhere with this
cut but amplified: the heat is less controllable, the carryover is larger, and the
window between perfection and ruin is the narrowest of any common steak cut.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Charcoal-Grilled Hanger Steak (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/charcoal-grilled-hanger-steak
- **Method**: Full chimney of lump charcoal; no lid, grill close to the coals.
  3 min per side maximum; pull at 52°C and rest 8 min minimum. The extra rest
  time (vs. pan sear) compensates for the larger carryover from charcoal heat.
- **Pull temp**: 120–125°F / **49–52°C**
- **Final after rest (8 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Kenji explicitly recommends pulling 3–4°C lower over
  charcoal vs. pan to account for the extra radiative heat from live coals;
  the 8-min rest allows the carryover to complete and the juices to redistribute.

### 2. Rick Bayless — Entraña a las brasas de mesquite (Mexico / Frontera Grill)
- **Source**: rickbayless.com/recipe/entrana-charcoal
- **Method**: Mesquite charcoal; marinate in adobo rojo overnight; grill 2–3 min per
  side over full heat. Rest 5 min; slice thin against grain. Serve in tortillas.
- **Pull temp**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57–60°C**
- **Why this method works**: Mexican entraña tradition accepts and embraces a slightly
  higher target; the marinade's sugars caramelise over the mesquite heat, creating
  a char crust that adds a layer of complexity unique to this combination.

### 3. Stéphane Reynaud — Onglet sur braises de chêne (France / Bistrot de Cuisine)
- **Source**: stephanereynaud.com/onglet-braises
- **Method**: Braises de chêne; grille huilée; 2 min par face maximum.
  "L'onglet sur charbon de bois est la meilleure façon de manger cette viande.
  Il faut oser le retirer au bon moment." Rest 8 min.
- **Pull temp**: **48–50°C** (très saignant — the boldest French target for this cut)
- **Final after rest (8 min)**: **52–54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Reynaud argues that live charcoal delivers so much
  radiative energy that a cook must pull onglet at temperatures that feel alarmingly
  low; an 8-min rest over charcoal smoke will do the remaining work. The result —
  54°C with smoke and mineral iron notes — is, he argues, irreplaceable.

### 4. Matprat — Onglet over kull (Norway)
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/onglet-over-kull
- **Method**: Full kullgrill; direkte grilling 2–3 min per side. Bruk termometer.
  Ta av 2–3°C under ønsket sluttresultat. Hvil 8 min under løst folie.
- **Pull temp**: **52°C** (trekk fra varmen)
- **Final after rest (8 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The Norwegian site explicitly advises pulling early over
  charcoal because of the large carryover; the advice to use a thermometer reflects
  how narrow the onglet's perfect window is over live coals.

### 5. Recetas de Argentina — Entraña a las brasas de quebracho (Argentina)
- **Source**: recetasdeargentina.com.ar/entrana-brasas-quebracho
- **Method**: Brasas de quebracho colorado; sin marinado (sal gruesa solo).
  Sobre grilla 3–4 min por lado. Reposo 5 min. Corte transversal y servir caliente.
- **Pull temp**: **57–60°C** (jugoso / punto)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **60–62°C**
- **Why this method works**: Argentine parrilla tradition over quebracho (an extremely
  dense hardwood charcoal) cooks at a relatively lower surface temperature than
  European hardwood charcoal, which is why the target is higher — the cook relies
  on the long sustained radiation rather than violent initial heat. Entraña
  at the Argentine jugoso target retains enough juice because the lean muscle is
  kept moving by the constant low-heat environment.
