# Hanger Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Hanger steak over live charcoal adds an important dimension to this already intensely flavoured cut. The fat-and-flavour drippings from the hanging muscle hit the hot coals and combust into brief flare-ups, depositing charred aromatic compounds onto the steak surface. This combination of deep beef flavour + charcoal smoke + char edge makes the hanger steak over charcoal one of the most intensely flavoured steak preparations achievable. The sinew-containing version (some butchers leave the central connective tissue) is convenient to cook whole and split post-resting; the sinew-removed version cooks as two thin separate muscles. Carryover from a very hot charcoal fire is 5–7 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Francis Mallmann — Onglet over Charcoal Embers (Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–12 min over embers · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500–600 g (sinew-on or sinew-removed — Mallmann often works sinew-on and removes after cooking)
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal (quebracho)
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Build a hardwood fire; burn to white embers. Season steak generously with coarse salt.
2. Place hanger steak directly on the grill over the glowing embers.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the end of the steak into its centre, avoiding the sinew if sinew-on.
4. Cook 4–5 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F).** Mallmann's South American preference: very juicy, vivid-red interior. The charcoal carryover is particularly high — 6–7 °C.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Remove sinew if present; slice thin against the grain. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*saignant* to medium-rare, Argentine tradition).

### 2. Steven Raichlen — Charcoal-Grilled Hanger Steak (American tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500 g (sinew removed)
- Kosher salt (1 hour before cooking)
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 1–2 hardwood chunks (hickory or oak), soaked
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steak 1 hour; brush with oil; apply pepper.
2. Start charcoal chimney; add soaked wood chunks.
3. Place hanger steak over the hottest coals. Insert the MEATER probe from the end of the steak.
4. Grill 4 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's medium-rare standard for hanger steak.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin against the grain. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 3. Lennox Hastie — Hanger Steak over Australian Ironbark (Australian live-fire tradition)
**Source**: Lennox Hastie, *Finding Fire* (Hardie Grant, 2017)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500–600 g (sinew removed)
- Coarse sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Ironbark hardwood charcoal

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; season with pepper just before grilling.
2. Build an ironbark charcoal fire; burn to glowing embers.
3. Place hanger steak directly over the hot embers.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the end into the thickest part.
5. Cook 4 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Hastie's live-fire target: medium-rare with generous carryover allowance.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, Australian live-fire standard).

### 4. David Tanis — Onglet over Charcoal (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: David Tanis, *Heart of the Artichoke and Other Kitchen Journeys* (Artisan Books, 2010)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 *onglet* (hanger steak), 500 g (sinew removed)
- **Marinade**: 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 1 sprig thyme; 1 bay leaf; 5 g coarse salt; 3 g freshly ground black pepper
- Dijon mustard and cornichons, to serve (French bistro classic accompaniment)

**Method**
1. Marinate 1 hour; wipe off excess marinade before grilling.
2. Light charcoal; allow to reach full heat.
3. Grill onglet 4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the end into the thickest part.
4. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Tanis's French bistro standard: *saignant*. Onglet must be served rare to medium-rare — this is a firm consensus in French cooking.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; serve with Dijon mustard and cornichons. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*saignant* to *point*, French bistro standard).

### 5. Bobby Flay — Charcoal Hanger Steak with Horseradish Compound Butter (American tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500 g (sinew removed)
- Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal
- **Horseradish compound butter**: 80 g softened butter; 20 g freshly grated horseradish (or 15 g prepared horseradish); 15 g chives, finely chopped; salt and white pepper

**Method**
1. Mix compound butter; refrigerate. Dry-brine steak 1 hour.
2. Start charcoal chimney; pour when white-hot.
3. Grill hanger steak 4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the end into the thickest part.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Flay's standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; top with compound butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American tradition).
