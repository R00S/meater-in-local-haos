# Hanger Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilling a hanger steak over gas is an exercise in speed and confidence: the cut's
open grain and moderate thickness means it gives up moisture rapidly if the heat is
not high enough to form a sealing crust quickly, and it overcooks just as fast if
the heat is too high. A preheated gas grill at maximum temperature, a single flip,
and a clean pull at the probe target produces a hanger steak with grill marks, a
caramelised crust, and the rare-to-medium-rare interior that the cut demands.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Hanger Steak with Chimichurri (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/beef/grilled-hanger-steak
- **Method**: Preheat gas grill on high 15 min; clean and oil the grates. Grill
  3–4 min per side, flipping once. Pull at target; rest 5 min off grill.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Weber's preheat protocol ensures the grates are at
  maximum temperature before the cut touches them — essential for a hanger steak
  where grate contact time per side must create a full crust before the interior
  overshoots.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Hanger Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-hanger-steak
- **Method**: Season 1 h before grilling; blot dry. Gas grill over high direct heat.
  Cook 3–4 min per side, single flip. Rest 5 min; slice at 45° against grain.
- **Pull temp**: 125–130°F / **52–54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Pre-salting 1 hour before improves moisture retention
  in the open grain; the single-flip method prevents the steak losing contact with
  the hot grate that would otherwise stall crust formation.

### 3. Cuisine et Vins de France — Onglet grillé au gaz (France)
- **Source**: cuisineetvinsdefrance.com/onglet-grille-gaz
- **Method**: Gril à gaz préchauffé au maximum; huile d'olive sur la grille.
  3 min par face; repos 5 min. Jamais au-delà de medium-rare (à point).
- **Pull temp**: **52°C** (saignant / limite saignant-à point)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The French culinary press explicitly warns against
  grilling onglet beyond saignant; the grill method is acceptable only if the
  cook is disciplined enough to pull early, as the radiation from the grates
  drives carryover faster than a pan.

### 4. ICA — Hanger Steak på gasgrill (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/hanger-steak-gasgrill
- **Method**: Gasgrill på högsta effekt 10 min; rengör och olja grillgallret.
  Grilla 3 min per sida. Kärntermometer till 54°C; vila 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grocery-store recipe guides present the hanger
  steak as a premium discovery cut; gas grill is recommended because its
  controllability suits a cut where a few seconds' difference matters.

### 5. BBQ Pit Boys — Grilled Hanger Steak (USA / YouTube)
- **Source**: bbqpitboys.com (hanger steak grilling guide)
- **Method**: Gas grill preheated max; light oil on grates. 4 min per side for a
  thick (3 cm) hanger. Probe inserted at thickest point. Rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57–59°C**
- **Why this method works**: BBQ Pit Boys target the higher end of medium-rare for
  outdoor grill audiences who prefer a warmer interior; the 54–57°C range covers
  both preferences without risking the toughening that occurs above 60°C for this cut.
