# Hanger Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Hanger steak (*onglet* in French, *bavette de flanchet* regionally, *butcher's secret* in American tradition — so named because it "hangs" from the diaphragm between the loin and the rib) is a single coarsely-grained, deeply beefy muscle cut from the plate section. There is only one per animal. Its intense beefy, slightly offal-like flavour is beloved in French bistro cooking (*onglet à l'échalote*) and in American steakhouse menus. Hanger steak is highly suited to direct-heat grilling: its loose, open grain browns quickly and the flavourful drippings cause brief flare-ups that add charred notes. It must be served no hotter than medium (57 °C); beyond that the loose grain becomes unpleasantly dry. A central sinew that runs along the steak must be removed before serving — it separates the two muscles. Carryover is 4–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Grilled Hanger Steak (American bistro tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, seriouseats.com/grilled-hanger-steak; *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500–600 g (sinew removed by butcher, or in one piece with sinew to be cut out post-cooking)
- Kosher salt (applied 1 hour before cooking)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; pat dry; apply pepper and oil.
2. Preheat grill to screaming high. Oil grates.
3. Place hanger steak on the hottest part of the grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the thickest part, avoiding the central sinew.
4. Grill 4 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Kenji's standard: medium-rare. Hanger goes from excellent to rubbery quickly above 57 °C.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Cut away the central sinew if present; slice thin against the grain. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American bistro standard).

### 2. David Tanis — Grilled Onglet à l'Échalote (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: David Tanis, *A Platter of Figs and Other Recipes* (Artisan Books, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak (*onglet*), 500–600 g, sinew removed
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) vegetable oil
- **Échalote sauce**: 40 g (4 large) shallots (*échalotes* — French grey shallots), finely minced; 80 ml (⅓ cup) red wine; 30 ml red wine vinegar; 60 g cold unsalted butter, cubed; fine salt and white pepper

**Method**
1. Season onglet; brush with oil.
2. Heat grill to very high. Oil grates.
3. Grill the onglet 4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of the steak into the thickest part.
4. While the steak grills, sauté shallots in 10 g butter; deglaze with wine and vinegar; reduce by half; remove from heat; whisk in cold butter cubes to emulsify.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** French bistro standard for *onglet*: *saignant* (rare-medium-rare). It is universally agreed in French tradition that *onglet* must not be cooked beyond *point*.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; spoon shallot butter sauce over. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*saignant*, French bistro standard).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled Hanger Steak with Spiced Yoghurt (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi and Ramael Scully, *NOPI: The Cookbook* (Ebury Press, 2015)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 hanger steaks, 500–600 g each (sinew removed)
- **Marinade**: 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin; 3 g (½ tsp) ground coriander; 3 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika; fine salt and black pepper
- **Spiced yoghurt**: 200 g Greek yoghurt; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 15 ml tahini; 10 ml lemon juice; ½ tsp *za'atar* (*za'atar* — Middle Eastern dried herb blend of thyme, sumac, sesame, oregano); salt

**Method**
1. Mix marinade; coat steaks; marinate 2 hours. Mix spiced yoghurt; refrigerate.
2. Preheat grill to very high. Remove steaks from marinade; pat dry.
3. Oil grates; grill 4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Ottolenghi's standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin; serve over spiced yoghurt. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Middle Eastern bistro standard).

### 4. April Bloomfield — Grilled Hanger Steak, Balsamic-Onion (American-British tradition)
**Source**: April Bloomfield, *A Girl and Her Pig* (Ecco Press, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500 g (sinew removed)
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Balsamic onions**: 2 red onions, thinly sliced; 30 ml balsamic vinegar; 15 g butter; 5 g sugar; salt

**Method**
1. Cook balsamic onions low and slow 25 minutes; keep warm. Dry-brine steak 1 hour; oil surface.
2. Preheat grill to high.
3. Grill steak 4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the thickest part.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Bloomfield's standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Slice against the grain; top with balsamic onions. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American-British tradition).

### 5. Neil Perry — Grilled Hanger Steak with Gremolata (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 hanger steak, 500–600 g (sinew removed)
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Gremolata**: 20 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped; 1 garlic clove, very finely minced; zest of 1 lemon

**Method**
1. Mix gremolata; set aside. Dry-brine 30 minutes; brush with oil.
2. Preheat grill to very high.
3. Grill steak 4 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the side.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; scatter gremolata over. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
