## Cut profile
Pan frying a hanger steak — slightly more oil, slightly less violent heat than a pure
sear — suits cooks who want the onglet flavour without the narrow window of a
cast-iron sprint. The hanger's coarse grain and moderate thickness mean pan frying
at medium-high heat builds a good crust in a more controllable timeframe. The
technique trades some of the extreme crust depth for reliability, which matters
when this particular cut can go from perfect to overcooked in under a minute.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Pan-Fried Hanger Steak with Shallots (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-fried-hanger-steak-shallots
- **Method**: 1 tbsp neutral oil in cast iron over medium-high. Season right before
  cooking (salt draws moisture from open grain). Cook 3–4 min per side. Rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Medium-high rather than screaming heat gives the cook
  3–4 min per side to develop crust; this is a safer window than the 2-min
  sprint of a full sear and suits a kitchen without commercial-strength gas burners.

### 2. Canal Cocina — Onglet / Entraña a la plancha (Spain)
- **Source**: canalcocina.es/receta/entrana-plancha
- **Method**: Plancha (flat cast iron griddle) over medium-high gas; thin film of
  olive oil. Season after cooking. 3 min per side for a 250–300g piece.
  Rest 5 min; serve on warm plate.
- **Pull temp**: **52–54°C** (poco hecho / en su punto)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The plancha's large even surface gives full contact with
  the hanger's irregular shape; olive oil prevents sticking without adding burnt-
  dairy flavour; Spanish cooking targets a slightly lower doneness than US medium-rare.

### 3. Cuisine et Vins de France — Onglet poêlé aux herbes (France)
- **Source**: cuisineetvinsdefrance.com/onglet-poele-herbes
- **Method**: Poêle épaisse sur feu moyen-vif; beurre + une goutte d'huile. 2–3 min
  par face selon l'épaisseur. Arroser de beurre fondu une fois. Repos 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **50–52°C** (saignant)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **52–54°C**
- **Why this method works**: French recipe magazines maintain the saignant rule even
  for pan frying; the butter-oil mix achieves the high smoke point needed while
  adding beurre noisette notes to the crust.

### 4. Dagbladet Mat — Onglet i panne (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/onglet-i-panne
- **Method**: Jernpanne på middels høy varme; olje og smør. 3 min per side.
  Hvil 5 min. Server med sjalottløk-saus.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian food press has covered the onglet rediscovery
  extensively; the pan fry method is preferred for its controllability indoors,
  and shallot sauce echoes the classic Parisian bistro pairing.

### 5. Donna Hay — Pan-Fried Hanger Steak with Herb Vinaigrette (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/hanger-steak-herb-vinaigrette
- **Method**: Bring to room temp 20 min; season. Pan over medium-high heat with
  rice bran oil; 3–4 min per side. Rest 5 min; slice thinly across grain.
- **Pull temp**: **54–57°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Australian culinary tradition targets mid-range medium-rare;
  Donna Hay's approach with a vinaigrette cuts through the cut's rich mineral flavour
  in a way no heavier sauce can. Pan frying is recommended over grilling for indoor
  use.
