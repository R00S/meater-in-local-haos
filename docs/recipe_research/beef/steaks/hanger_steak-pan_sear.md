# Hanger Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Hanger steak (onglet in French, "butcher's steak" or "hanging tender") is cut from
the diaphragm/plate, suspended from the last rib. One of the most flavourful cuts
on the animal — rich, mineral, beefy. Highly prized in French bistro cooking.
Central sinew must be removed. Cook to medium-rare ONLY — gets livery and tough above.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Pan-Seared Hanger Steak (J. Kenji López-Alt, USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-seared-hanger-steak-recipe
- **Method**: Sinew removed, cut into 2 lobes. Cast iron screaming hot. 2-3 min/side.
  Butter baste. Rest 5 min.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare
- "Hanger above medium is a crime — the iron-rich flavour turns metallic and livery."

### 2. Le Monde des Chefs — Onglet de bœuf à la poêle (France)
- **Source**: lemondedesschefs.fr/onglet-poele
- **French classic**: Onglet is a bistro staple (onglet à l'échalote).
  Poêle en fonte très chaude. Beurre, échalote. 2-3 min/côté.
- **Target**: **52-55°C** (saignant) — French NEVER cook onglet beyond medium-rare.
  "L'onglet bien cuit est immangeable."

### 3. Food52 — Pan-Seared Hanger Steak with Shallot Sauce (USA)
- **Source**: food52.com/recipes/pan-seared-hanger-steak-shallot-sauce
- **Method**: Very hot cast iron. Rendered sinew removed. 2-3 min/side.
  Shallot pan sauce finish.
- **Target**: **130°F / 54°C** — "hanger should be rare to medium-rare, never beyond"

### 4. Dagbladet Mat — Onglet / Kjøl i stekepanne (Norway)
- **Source**: dagbladet.no/mat/onglet-kjol-stekepanne
- **Method**: Stekepanne meget varm. Smør. 2-3 min per side. Hvil 5 min.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / blodig)

### 5. Olivemagazine — Pan-Seared Hanger Steak (UK)
- **Source**: olivemagazine.com/recipes/pan-seared-hanger-steak
- **Method**: Very hot pan, oil. 2-3 min/side. Deglazing sauce optional.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** medium-rare. "Don't overcook — hanger is best pink."

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Target | Style |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Serious Eats | USA | 54-57°C | Butter baste |
| Le Monde des Chefs | FR | **52-55°C** | Saignant — bistro classic |
| Food52 | USA | **54°C** | Lower end |
| Dagbladet | NO | 54-57°C | Medium blodig |
| Olive Magazine | UK | 54-57°C | Medium-rare |

**Consensus: 54-57°C medium-rare. French onglet tradition firmly at 52-55°C (saignant).**
This is one of the cuts where French tradition is most insistent — onglet should never
be cooked beyond medium-rare. Hard ceiling: 60°C (medium) makes it livery and unpleasant.
