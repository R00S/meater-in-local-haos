# Ny Strip × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

New York strip steak cooked in an air fryer is exposed to intense circulated hot air in a compact basket — the result approximates oven roasting but faster, with the hot air producing a slightly crisped exterior. For a steak, the air fryer is not a substitute for a screaming-hot cast-iron sear; it produces a dryer crust than a pan sear but is convenient for a one-appliance preparation. The strip's firm, moderate-marbling muscle responds well to air fryer temperatures — typically 200–220 °C for 8–12 minutes. Many recipes recommend a brief stovetop or broiler finish after the air fryer step to develop a true Maillard crust. Carryover from the air fryer basket is 3–5 °C. The technique is most common in home-cooking publications from the 2020s.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Strip Steak (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Air Fryer Perfection* (America's Test Kitchen, 2019)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt (for dry brine)
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Season steaks with kosher salt 1 hour ahead; refrigerate uncovered on a wire rack.
2. Remove from fridge; apply black pepper.
3. Preheat air fryer to 220 °C (425 °F) for 5 minutes.
4. Place steaks in the basket, not touching.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
6. Air fry at 220 °C for 6 minutes. Flip; continue 4–6 minutes depending on thickness and desired doneness.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** ATK's air fryer steak target is medium-rare; they note the air fryer produces a drier exterior crust than a pan sear but internal doneness is equivalent.
8. Rest 5 minutes on a wire rack. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, ATK air fryer standard).

### 2. Jamie Oliver — Air Fryer Steak with Herb Butter (British tradition)
**Source**: Jamie Oliver, *7 Ways* (Michael Joseph / Penguin, 2020); adapted for air fryer in online content  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 300–350 g each
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) olive oil
- **Herb butter**: 60 g softened unsalted butter; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped; 1 garlic clove, minced; 5 g fresh thyme leaves; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix herb butter; refrigerate.
2. Season steaks with salt and pepper; rub with a tiny amount of olive oil.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
4. Place steaks in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side (total 10–12 minutes), flipping once.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Jamie's target is medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Top with herb butter to melt over the steak. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British standard).

### 3. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Strip Steak with Chimichurri (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *The Fast 400 Cookbook* (HarperCollins Australia, 2020)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 300–350 g each
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) olive oil
- **Chimichurri**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 2 garlic cloves; 30 ml (2 tbsp) red wine vinegar; 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil; red chilli flakes; fine salt

**Method**
1. Blend chimichurri; set aside.
2. Season steaks; rub with oil.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
4. Place steaks in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Hay's standard for strip steak is medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).

### 4. Nigella Lawson — Air Fryer Steak with Mustard Sauce (British home-cooking tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *Cook, Eat, Repeat* (Chatto & Windus, 2020)  
**Serves**: 1 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 New York strip steak, 300–350 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- **Mustard sauce**: 60 ml (¼ cup) crème fraîche; 15 ml (1 tbsp) Dijon mustard; 5 ml (1 tsp) wholegrain mustard; 5 ml (1 tsp) Worcestershire sauce; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix mustard sauce ingredients; warm gently in a saucepan. Keep warm.
2. Season steak; let stand 10 minutes.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
4. Place steak in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nigella's standard: a warm pink steak.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with mustard sauce. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British home-cook standard).

### 5. Neil Perry — Air Fryer Steak with Herb Salsa (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, recipe adapted from *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005) for air fryer technique  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Australian strip steaks, 300–350 g each
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) vegetable oil
- **Herb salsa**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 20 g fresh mint; 2 garlic cloves; 30 ml (2 tbsp) red wine vinegar; 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil; sea salt

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks 30 minutes; pat dry; rub with oil.
2. Blend herb salsa; set aside.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
4. Place steaks in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Perry's Australian standard is medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with herb salsa. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
