# Ny Strip × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The New York strip over live hardwood charcoal benefits from the distinctive smoke compounds — guaiacol, syringol, and related phenolics — that gas grilling cannot replicate. The intense radiant heat from a well-managed charcoal fire sears the strip's firm muscle rapidly, rendering the fat cap and producing a deeply flavoured crust. The charcoal method also permits the cook to modulate the fire by adjusting air vents and coal arrangement; a two-zone fire (direct coals on one side, indirect on the other) allows initial searing then gentle finishing by indirect heat for thick-cut strips. Carryover from a very hot charcoal fire is 5–6 °C for a thick steak; this must be factored into the pull temperature.

## Source recipes

### 1. Francis Mallmann — Charcoal-Grilled Strip Steak (Argentine asado tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–16 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 400–500 g each, 4 cm thick
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal (quebracho or mesquite — Mallmann's preferred fuels for *asado*)
- Chimichurri: 40 g flat-leaf parsley; 15 g fresh oregano; 4 garlic cloves; 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil; 30 ml (2 tbsp) red wine vinegar; red chilli flakes; fine salt

**Method**
1. Light hardwood charcoal; allow to reach white-ash stage (about 45 minutes). The fire should be at its hottest with no visible flames.
2. Season steaks generously with coarse salt and pepper just before placing on the grill.
3. Place steaks directly over the hottest coals.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Cook 5–7 minutes per side. The charcoal heat will render the fat cap and produce distinct char marks.
6. **Pull at 49–51 °C (120–124 °F).** Mallmann pulls early (*jugoso*) to account for the high carryover from a blazing charcoal fire. The Argentine palate for beef is a warm, deep-red interior.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine charcoal standard).

### 2. Steven Raichlen — Strip Steak with Charcoal and Wood Smoke (American BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 strip steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) coarse kosher salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) garlic powder
- 2–3 hardwood chunks (oak, cherry, or hickory) for smoke, soaked 30 minutes
- Compound butter (optional): 80 g softened unsalted butter with herbs

**Method**
1. Season steaks with salt, pepper and garlic powder; refrigerate 1 hour.
2. Build a two-zone fire: mound coals on one side, leave other side empty. Add soaked wood chunks to the coals. Place grill grate; heat 10 minutes.
3. Sear steaks directly over the hot coals 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre after the first sear.
5. Move steaks to the indirect side; close lid. Cook 3–5 minutes more to reach target temperature with the lid down (captures the wood smoke).
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's target is medium-rare; he notes that thick steaks benefit from the indirect finish to avoid overcooked grey bands.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Top with compound butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 3. Adam Perry Lang — Competition-Style Strip Steak over Charcoal (American competition BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Adam Perry Lang, *Serious Barbecue* (Hyperion, 2009)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+overnight marinade) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 strip steaks, 350–450 g each, 3.5–4 cm thick
- **Board dressing**: 60 g softened unsalted butter; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped; 10 ml (2 tsp) Worcestershire sauce; fine salt and black pepper
- Coarse salt and freshly ground black pepper for the steaks
- Hardwood charcoal, chimney-started

**Method**
1. Mix board dressing ingredients; spread on a large cutting board.
2. Season steaks with coarse salt and black pepper.
3. Light charcoal in a chimney; pour into the grill when white-hot. Spread into a uniform layer for direct-heat cooking.
4. Place steaks on the hot grate. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Grill 4–5 minutes per side, flipping once.
6. Stand on fat cap; render 1 minute.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Lang's competition style targets medium-rare.
8. Rest steaks directly on the board dressing — as they rest, the meat juices emulsify with the butter. Slice before serving. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 4. Lennox Hastie — Strip Steak over Live Fire (Australian wood-fire tradition)
**Source**: Lennox Hastie, *Finding Fire* (Hardie Grant, 2017)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 thick-cut strip steaks (Australian Wagyu or premium Angus), 400–500 g each, 4–5 cm thick
- Coarse sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood (ironbark or apple — Hastie's preferred Australian woods)

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks 1 hour with coarse salt; refrigerate uncovered.
2. Build a hardwood fire; allow to burn to glowing coals with minimal smoke.
3. Season steaks with black pepper just before cooking.
4. Place steaks directly on the grill over the coals. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Cook 5–6 minutes per side, adjusting height above the coals based on heat.
6. Stand on fat cap; render 2 minutes.
7. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Hastie's live-fire target is rare to medium-rare — the premium Australian Wagyu benefits from not being cooked past this point.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (medium-rare, Australian live-fire standard).

### 5. Yotam Ottolenghi — Charcoal-Grilled Strip with Spiced Butter (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012), adapted from Israeli mangal (*mangal* — Israeli/Middle Eastern charcoal grill tradition)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 300–350 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground allspice (*bahar* — the Israeli/Arabic spice blend often centred on allspice)
- 3 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon
- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **Spiced butter**: 60 g softened butter; 3 g (½ tsp) ground sumac; 5 g (1 tsp) za'atar; salt

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, allspice, cinnamon, salt and pepper into a paste. Coat steaks; marinate 1 hour.
2. Mix spiced butter; refrigerate.
3. Light charcoal; allow to reach white-ash stage.
4. Place steaks on the grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F).** Ottolenghi's preference is medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Top with spiced butter. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Israeli mangal standard).
