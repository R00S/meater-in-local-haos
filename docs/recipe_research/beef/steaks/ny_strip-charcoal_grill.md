# New York Strip × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
NY strip on charcoal: the two-zone fire (direct 205-230°C for sear, indirect for finish)
adds combustion aromatics that complement the cut's bold beef flavour. Argentine bife de
chorizo (the local equivalent of NY strip) is the most important international reference —
cooked on parrilla over quebracho charcoal with salt only.

## Source recipes

### 1. Grill It Better — How to Grill a New York Strip Steak on Charcoal
- **Source**: grillitbetter.com/how-to-grill-a-new-york-strip-steak-on-charcoal
- **Method**: Two-zone charcoal fire. Direct sear 3-4 min/side, indirect to finish.
- **Target (medium-rare)**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C**

### 2. SHUNgrill — Grilling Guide: Perfecting NY Strip on Charcoal
- **Source**: shungrill.com/article/how-to-charcoal-grill-new-york-strip-steak
- **Method**: Lump charcoal, two-zone. High heat 400-450°F / 205-230°C.
  Rotate 90° for crosshatch grill marks.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 3. Smoked BBQ Source — Grilled New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: smokedbbqsource.com/grilled-new-york-strip-steak
- **Method**: Charcoal two-zone, probe thermometer. Compound butter finish.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C**

### 4. DadCooksDinner — Grilled Thick Cut NY Strip (Sear and Move)
- **Source**: dadcooksdinner.com/grilled-thick-cut-new-york-strip-steaks-sear-and-move-style
- **Method**: Charcoal variant of the "sear and move" method.
- **Target**: Medium-rare **54-57°C**. Japanese yakiniku: thin-sliced, high heat, tare sauce.

### 5. Omaha Steaks — How to Grill a New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: omahasteaks.com/blog/how-to-grill-strip-steak
- **Method**: High heat charcoal grill. Two-zone for safety.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare
- Argentine note: "Bife de chorizo (Argentine NY strip equivalent): salt only,
  quebracho charcoal parrilla, 54-57°C target — same temp, different flavour profile
  from wood smoke and minimal seasoning philosophy."
