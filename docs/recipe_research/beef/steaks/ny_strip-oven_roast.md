## Cut profile
NY strip reverse sear: low oven (120-135°C) on a wire rack to 51°C internal,
then a 1-2 min per side sear in smoking-hot cast iron. The fat cap benefits from
extra 30-sec heat on edge after the face sear.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chef Crown — Reverse Sear New York Strip: A Foolproof Guide
- **Source**: chefcrown.com/reverse-sear-new-york-strip
- **Oven**: 120-135°C / 250-275°F, wire rack
- **Pull from oven (medium-rare)**: 51°C / 124°F (6-8°C below target)
- **Final after sear + rest**: **54-57°C / 130-135°F**

### 2. Alyssa's Kitchen — Reverse Seared NY Strip
- **Source**: alyssaskitchenblog.com/reverse-seared-ny-strip
- **Oven**: 225-250°F / 107-121°C, wire rack, probe inserted.
- **Pull at**: 52°C / 125°F for medium-rare result
- **Final**: **54-57°C** after hot sear + 5-min rest

### 3. Cooking in the Keys — Reverse Seared New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: cookinginthekeys.com/reverse-seared-strip-steak
- **Method**: 225°F / 107°C oven, wire rack. Then screaming hot cast iron.
- **Target**: Medium-rare = **54-57°C** final
- UK/German note: Dry brine with Maldon/sea salt preferred for enhanced crust.

### 4. CookTimePro — Reverse Searing NY Strip Guide
- **Source**: cooktimepro.com/red-meat/beef/beef-new-york-strip/reverse-searing
- **Oven**: 120-135°C. Remove at 51°C. Sear 1-2 min/side.
- **Final medium-rare**: **54-57°C** after rest

### 5. Steak University / MyChicagoSteak — How to Reverse Sear Strip Steak
- **Source**: mychicagosteak.com/blogs/steak-university/reverse-sear-strip-steak
- **Oven**: 225°F / 107°C. Pull at 49-52°C, sear, rest to **54-57°C**.
- "For lean cuts like strip, reverse sear gives the most uniform result."
