# Ny Strip × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting a New York strip steak typically means one of two approaches: a reverse-sear (low oven to near target temperature, then sear) or a high-heat oven roast (400–450 °C, sear first then finish in oven). For thick-cut strip steaks (3.5–6 cm), the oven is an essential partner to the pan because it allows the interior to reach temperature without burning the exterior. The strip's moderate marbling and firm single-muscle structure handle oven heat well; unlike the ribeye, it has no large fat pockets that render unevenly. The reverse-sear method, popularised by Kenji López-Alt, has become one of the most reliable methods for thick steaks: the low oven dries the surface (improving subsequent sear), and carryover after the low oven is minimal, allowing a very precise pull. Carryover from a hot oven finishing step is 5–7 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Reverse-Sear New York Strip (American tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015); seriouseats.com/the-reverse-sear-is-the-best-method-for-thick-steaks  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+overnight dry-brine) · **Cook**: 45–60 min at 135 °C oven + 5 min sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 350–500 g each, 4–5 cm thick (reverse sear is designed for extra-thick steaks)
- Kosher salt (for dry brine overnight)
- Freshly ground black pepper (applied before oven)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for final sear)
- 30 g unsalted butter (for final sear)

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks with kosher salt overnight; refrigerate uncovered on a wire rack. Pat dry before cooking.
2. Preheat oven to 135 °C (275 °F). Place steaks on a wire rack set over a rimmed baking sheet.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre of the meat.
4. Roast in the 135 °C oven until internal temperature reaches 5 °C below target.
5. For medium-rare target: remove at **49 °C (120 °F)** — the low oven generates minimal carryover.
6. Remove from oven; pat surface dry with paper towels (the low oven has already dried the surface — one of the technique's key advantages).
7. Heat a cast-iron pan over maximum heat; add oil until smoking.
8. Sear steaks 45–60 seconds per side maximum. Add butter; baste briefly. The sear is purely for crust.
9. **Pull at 49 °C (120 °F) from the oven.** Final carryover from the brief sear raises the core to medium-rare. Serve immediately after the sear — no additional resting required as the steak spent up to an hour equilibrating. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Kenji reverse-sear standard).

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Oven-Seared Strip Steak (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Cook's Country TV Show Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2015); americastestkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–15 min oven + 3 min sear · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 350–400 g each, 3–4 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Season steaks with salt; rest 1 hour at room temperature (or refrigerate overnight).
2. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F). Preheat a cast-iron pan in the oven for 15 minutes — the ATK seared oven method heats the pan in the oven for the initial sear.
3. Remove hot pan from oven; add oil. Immediately place steaks in the pan — the screaming-hot pan from the oven produces an immediate hard sear.
4. Return pan with steaks to the oven.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre (through the oven door or before returning to oven).
6. Roast at 230 °C for 8–10 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** ATK's oven-sear method builds the crust and the finish simultaneously in the oven; carryover from a hot oven is 4–5 °C, so pull at 52 °C to land at medium-rare (54–57 °C) after 5 minutes of rest.
8. Rest 5 minutes on a wire rack. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 3. Gordon Ramsay — Oven-Finished Strip Steak (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4 min sear + 6–8 min oven · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 350–400 g each
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 3 garlic cloves, skin-on
- 3 sprigs thyme

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Season steaks.
2. Heat an oven-proof sauté pan (cast iron or stainless with no plastic handles) over very high heat on the stovetop. Add oil.
3. Sear steaks 2 minutes per side on the stovetop.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre after the initial sear.
5. Add butter, garlic and thyme; baste briefly on the stovetop.
6. Transfer the entire pan to the preheated oven.
7. Roast 6–8 minutes, basting once midway. Carryover from a 200 °C oven will be approximately 5 °C.
8. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** from the oven. The carryover from the hot pan and oven will bring the steak to medium-rare on resting.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Ramsay restaurant standard).

### 4. Heston Blumenthal — Triple-Cooked Steak (British Modernist tradition)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *Heston Blumenthal at Home* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2011)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 45 min low oven + 2 min sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 400–500 g each, 5 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) high-smoke-point oil

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 50 °C (122 °F) — Blumenthal's ultra-low oven method. Season steaks.
2. Place steaks on a wire rack; insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
3. Cook in the 50 °C oven for 45–60 minutes — the oven temperature equals the desired final temperature, so there is no carryover (the steak cannot exceed the oven temperature). The surface becomes uniformly dry, primed for the hardest possible sear.
4. Remove; pat perfectly dry. Heat a pan to maximum heat; add oil.
5. Sear 60–90 seconds per side — the driest possible surface, the hottest possible pan.
6. **Pull from oven at 50 °C (122 °F)** — the core temperature equals the oven temperature. After searing, the surface is well above this but the core remains at 50 °C. Blumenthal's target for strip steak is rare to medium-rare. Final serving temperature: **50–52 °C / 122–126 °F** at the core (rare to medium-rare, Blumenthal ultra-low standard).

### 5. Fergus Henderson — Roasted Strip Steak (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Complete Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5 min sear + 8–10 min oven · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 350–400 g each
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) dripping (*dripping* — rendered beef or pork fat, Henderson's preferred cooking fat for beef) or vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Season steaks generously.
2. Heat a heavy oven-proof frying pan over very high heat. Add dripping; let it melt and smoke.
3. Sear steaks 2 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Transfer pan to the oven. Roast 8–10 minutes.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Henderson's British standard for strip steak is medium-rare; he writes that "a steak must be pink inside — a grey steak is a sad thing."
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British standard).
