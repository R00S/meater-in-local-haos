## Cut profile
Pan frying NY strip with the fat cap rendered first: hold the steak edge-down against
the hot pan for 30-60 sec before the main face sear. The fat cap softens and bastes
the pan, adding flavour to the subsequent sear. French butter basting (arrosage) with
garlic and thyme, German Butterschmalz, and UK straightforward butter finish all produce
the same internal target.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steak Advisor — Pan Seared Strip Steak with Butter and Herbs (French technique)
- **Source**: steakadvisor.com/steak-recipes/pan-seared-strip-steak
- **Method**: Hot cast iron, oil, flip, then butter+garlic+thyme baste (arroser).
  Render fat cap edge first. Rest.
- **Target (medium-rare)**: **54-57°C / 130-135°F**
- "French chefs baste repeatedly with butter and herbs — the defining touch."

### 2. Dishes With Dad — Pan Seared New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: disheswithdad.com/pan-seared-new-york-strip-steak
- **Method**: Cast iron, very hot, canola oil + butter. Baste.
  Edge-fat rendered first, then face sear.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 3. Chef's Resource — How to Pan-Fry a New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: chefsresource.com/how-to-pan-fry-a-new-york-strip-steak
- **Method**: Cast iron at 204-232°C. Butter baste with herbs after flip.
  German Butterschmalz alternative: "Higher smoke point, cleaner flavour."
- **Target**: **54-57°C / 130-135°F**

### 4. The Kitchen Girl — Juicy New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: thekitchengirl.com/new-york-strip-steak
- **Method**: Cast iron, sear 3-4 min/side, butter baste, rest under foil.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare
- UK note: "British chefs: simple sear, butter+thyme, rest. Maldon salt finish."

### 5. Bake It With Love — Pan Seared New York Strip Steak
- **Source**: bakeitwithlove.com/pan-seared-new-york-strip-steak
- **Method**: Screaming-hot pan, thin oil, then butter baste with rosemary+garlic.
  Render fat cap edge against pan for 30-60 sec before main sear.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C**
- "Rendering the fat cap is the specific NY strip technique — skip it and you lose
  the cut's defining flavour element."
