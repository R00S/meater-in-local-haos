# Ny Strip × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan frying a New York strip steak differs from pan searing in that pan frying typically involves a moderate amount of oil or fat in the pan, cooking at slightly lower temperatures than a dry sear, and often flipping more frequently. In many home-kitchen traditions, "pan frying" a steak means cooking it in a well-oiled pan over medium-high heat with regular attention — a more accessible technique than the screaming-hot pan of restaurant searing. The strip's firm, moderately marbled muscle is very well-suited to pan frying because it does not require the intense heat of a cast-iron sear to produce a good crust; a heavy non-stick or stainless pan works well. Fat-cap rendering against the pan edge and basting with the rendered fat are hallmarks of a well-executed pan-fried strip. Carryover is 3–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nigella Lawson — Pan-Fried Steak with Garlic Butter (British home-cooking tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *How to Eat* (Chatto & Windus, 1998)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min tempering) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 250–350 g each, 2.5 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 40 g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- Handful fresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- Crusty bread and green salad, to serve

**Method**
1. Temper steaks 30 minutes at room temperature. Pat dry; season both sides with salt and pepper.
2. Heat a large, heavy frying pan over medium-high heat. Add oil; let it heat until shimmering.
3. Add steaks. Cook 3–4 minutes per side, flipping once — Nigella's home-cooking method is a simple single-flip technique.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre after placing in the pan.
5. For the last 2 minutes, add butter and garlic; baste.
6. Stand on the fat cap briefly.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nigella's standard: a warm pink interior — she writes that "a steak should yield and blush, not grey and shrink."
8. Rest 5 minutes. Scatter parsley; serve with bread and salad. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British home-cook standard).

### 2. Delia Smith — Pan-Fried Steak with Mushrooms (British traditional home cooking)
**Source**: Delia Smith, *Delia's Complete Cookery Course* (BBC Books, 1989)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks (or sirloin), 250–300 g each, 2.5 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) sunflower or vegetable oil
- 40 g (3 tbsp) butter
- 200 g chestnut mushrooms, sliced
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) brandy or red wine
- 60 ml (¼ cup) double cream
- Fresh thyme, to serve

**Method**
1. Season steaks on both sides. Heat a large, heavy frying pan over medium-high heat with oil.
2. Add steaks. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
3. Cook 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare; flip once.
4. Remove steaks to a warm plate; rest.
5. In the same pan, add 20 g butter; sauté mushrooms and garlic 3–4 minutes.
6. Add brandy; flambé briefly. Add cream; reduce until coating consistency. Season.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Delia's standard for a home-cooked strip steak: "a steak of this thickness should give you a nicely pink interior in 3–4 minutes per side over a good heat."
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with mushroom cream sauce. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British home-cooking standard).

### 3. Julia Child — Steak Sauté with Red Wine Sauce (French classical tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Volume 1* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+20 min tempering) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 *contre-filet* (boneless strip) steaks, 250–300 g each
- Fine salt and freshly ground white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) clarified butter or vegetable oil
- 1 shallot, finely minced
- 80 ml (⅓ cup) red wine (Burgundy or any good dry red)
- 80 ml (⅓ cup) beef stock
- 30 g cold unsalted butter (for sauce)
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Temper steaks 20 minutes. Season immediately before cooking.
2. Heat a wide sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add clarified butter.
3. Place steaks. Cook 3 minutes per side without moving.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre after the first turn.
5. Remove steaks to rest. Drain all but 1 tsp fat from the pan.
6. Sauté shallot 1 minute. Deglaze with red wine; reduce by two-thirds. Add stock; reduce by half. Swirl in cold butter; add parsley.
7. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Julia's target for French-style strip steak pan fry: *saignant* — a warm, bright-red interior.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Spoon red wine sauce over. Final serving temperature: **52–55 °C / 126–131 °F** (*saignant*, French classical standard).

### 4. Nigel Slater — Pan-Fried Strip Steak with Herb Sauce (British contemporary tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Real Food* (Fourth Estate, 1998)  
**Serves**: 1 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 New York strip steak, 250–350 g, 2.5 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil or butter
- 1 garlic clove, halved (for rubbing the pan)
- **Herb sauce**: handful fresh tarragon; handful flat-leaf parsley; 1 garlic clove; 45 ml (3 tbsp) olive oil; 15 ml (1 tbsp) lemon juice; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Blend herb sauce; set aside.
2. Rub the hot pan with the cut garlic clove; add oil.
3. Season steak; place in the moderately hot pan.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Cook 3 minutes per side; flip once. Do not rush — Slater advocates a moderate heat that allows flavour development without charring.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Slater's standard: "a steak should be pink, almost rare — I don't understand grey steak."
7. Rest 5 minutes. Spoon herb sauce over. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*saignant* / rare-medium-rare, British contemporary home-cook standard).

### 5. Stephanie Alexander — Pan-Fried Sirloin (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Stephanie Alexander, *The Cook's Companion* (Viking/Penguin, 1996; revised 2004), beef chapter  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min tempering) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Australian strip loins (*sirloin* in Australian nomenclature), 300–350 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- Sea salt flakes and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil or clarified butter
- 20 g unsalted butter (for finishing)
- Lemon juice, to finish

**Method**
1. Temper steaks 30 minutes. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat a heavy frying pan (cast iron preferred) over high heat. Add oil until shimmering.
3. Place steaks. Cook 3–4 minutes per side; flip once.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre of the meat.
5. Add butter; baste briefly.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Alexander's Australian standard for strip steak is medium-rare — she writes that the Australian definition of medium-rare is a warm pink centre, slightly firmer and warmer than the French *saignant*.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Squeeze lemon juice over; serve. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
