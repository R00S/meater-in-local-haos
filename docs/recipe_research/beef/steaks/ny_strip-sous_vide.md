# Ny Strip × Sous Vide — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

New York strip cooked sous vide is precision-cooked in a sealed bag at the target temperature, then given a rapid high-heat sear for crust development. The strip's firm, moderately marbled muscle is an excellent candidate for sous vide because the long, even cook time (1–4 hours) fully relaxes the muscle fibres, producing a more tender result than pan searing alone, while the precise temperature control eliminates the wide temperature gradient (from surface to core) that occurs with conventional searing. Post-bath searing is brief — 60–90 seconds per side maximum — as the steak is already at the target internal temperature. Carryover from the post-bath sear adds 2–3 °C to the surface but the core stays at the bath temperature. The bath temperature is the target internal temperature.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Sous Vide New York Strip (American tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015); seriouseats.com/the-food-lab-complete-guide-to-sous-vide-steak  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1–4 h at bath temp, then 90-second sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 300–400 g each, 3–4 cm thick
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 sprig fresh thyme or rosemary (optional, in the bag)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for post-bath sear)
- 30 g unsalted butter (for post-bath sear)
- 3 garlic cloves, skin-on (for post-bath sear)

**Method**
1. Season steaks generously with salt and pepper. Place in vacuum bags with a sprig of herbs if using. Seal.
2. Set sous vide circulator to **54 °C (130 °F)** for medium-rare. Cook 1–4 hours. The steak will equilibrate to the bath temperature throughout; longer times do not harm the steak but very long (>4 hours) can cause texture degradation.
3. **Note for MEATER probe**: A probe cannot be inserted during the sealed-bag cook. The circulator temperature is the cook target. After removing from the bag, verify the core temperature with the probe if desired — it should match the bath temperature within 1–2 °C.
4. Remove steaks from bags; pat completely dry with paper towels. Moisture is the enemy of the post-bath sear.
5. Heat a cast-iron or stainless pan over the absolute highest heat. Add oil; heat until smoking intensely.
6. Sear each steak 60–90 seconds per side maximum — the steak is already fully cooked; the sear only develops the crust.
7. Add butter and garlic; baste briefly.
8. **Bath temperature: 54 °C (130 °F).** Post-bath sear adds approximately 2 °C to the surface but not the core. Final serving temperature: **54 °C / 130 °F** at the core (medium-rare, Kenji's sous vide standard).

### 2. Thomas Keller — Sous Vide Strip Steak (French haute cuisine tradition)
**Source**: Thomas Keller, Michael Ruhlman and Sebastián Rouxel, *Under Pressure: Cooking Sous Vide* (Artisan Books, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1 h at 58 °C, then sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 350–400 g each
- Fine salt and freshly ground white pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) grapeseed oil (for bag)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) grapeseed oil (for post-bath sear)
- 60 g unsalted butter (for post-bath basting)
- Fleur de sel, to finish

**Method**
1. Season steaks with fine salt and white pepper. Place in vacuum bags with grapeseed oil. Seal using a vacuum sealer.
2. Set sous vide bath to **58 °C (136 °F)**. Keller's published protocol for strip steak in *Under Pressure* targets 58 °C — a medium preparation that he describes as fully set but supremely moist throughout.
3. Cook 1 hour.
4. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be inserted during the sealed-bag cook. The bath temperature is the cook target (58 °C). Post-bath, verify core temperature immediately after removing from bag.
5. Remove from bags; pat absolutely dry.
6. Heat a stainless pan until smoking; add grapeseed oil.
7. Sear 60–90 seconds per side. Add butter; baste.
8. **Bath temperature: 58 °C (136 °F).** Keller's published sous vide target for strip steak is medium — above the French *saignant* preference but well below the grey overcooked interior. Final serving temperature: **58 °C / 136 °F** at the core (medium, Keller sous vide standard).

### 3. Heston Blumenthal — Sous Vide Perfect Steak (British Modernist tradition)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *The Fat Duck Cookbook* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 45 min at 56 °C, then sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 New York strip steaks, 300–400 g each
- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 10 ml (2 tsp) olive oil (for bag)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) high-smoke oil (for post-bath sear)
- 30 g unsalted butter
- Fresh thyme

**Method**
1. Season steaks; seal in vacuum bags with olive oil.
2. Set bath to **56 °C (133 °F)**. Blumenthal's *Fat Duck* protocol targets 56 °C for approximately 45 minutes for a 3 cm strip steak.
3. Cook 45 minutes.
4. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be inserted during the sealed-bag cook. Bath temperature (56 °C) is the cook target. Post-bath probe verification is possible immediately after bag removal.
5. Remove from bags; pat completely dry.
6. Heat a cast-iron pan to maximum. Sear 60–90 seconds per side. Baste with butter and thyme.
7. **Bath temperature: 56 °C (133 °F).** Blumenthal's target is medium-rare, warmer than Kenji's preference. Final serving temperature: **56 °C / 133 °F** at the core (medium-rare to medium, Blumenthal standard).

### 4. Grant Achatz — Sous Vide Strip Steak with Aromatics (American Modernist tradition)
**Source**: Grant Achatz and Nick Kokonas, *Alinea* (Ten Speed Press, 2008); Achatz's sous vide steak technique is documented in the book  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1.5 h at 54 °C, then sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 350–400 g each
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) unsalted butter (in the bag)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) grapeseed oil (for sear)

**Method**
1. Season steaks; place in vacuum bags with thyme, garlic and butter. Seal.
2. Set bath to **54 °C (130 °F)** for 1.5 hours.
3. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be used during sealed-bag cook. Bath temperature (54 °C) is the cook target.
4. Remove from bags; discard aromatics; pat completely dry.
5. Heat a pan to maximum; sear 60–90 seconds per side.
6. **Bath temperature: 54 °C (130 °F).** Achatz's target is medium-rare. Final serving temperature: **54 °C / 130 °F** at the core (medium-rare, modernist American standard).

### 5. Neil Perry — Sous Vide Strip Steak, Australian Style (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005); sous vide technique added based on Perry's documented modernist cooking approach  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 2 h at 54 °C, then sear · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Australian Wagyu or Angus strip steaks, 350–450 g each
- Sea salt flakes and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary (in bag)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for sear)
- **Bone marrow sauce** (to serve): reserved bone marrow; 100 ml beef stock; 1 shallot, minced; 30 ml (2 tbsp) red wine; 20 g cold butter

**Method**
1. Season steaks; seal in vacuum bags with rosemary.
2. Set bath to **54 °C (130 °F)**; cook 2 hours.
3. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be used during the sealed-bag cook. Bath temperature (54 °C) is the cook target.
4. Make bone marrow sauce while steaks cook.
5. Remove from bags; pat completely dry.
6. Sear in screaming-hot pan 60–90 seconds per side.
7. **Bath temperature: 54 °C (130 °F).** Perry's target is medium-rare — Australian standards are consistent with the international medium-rare range. Final serving temperature: **54 °C / 130 °F** at the core (medium-rare, Australian standard).
