# Picanha × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilled picanha is the centrepiece of Brazilian *churrasco* culture, where the whole *rump cap* is skewered into a horseshoe shape and rotated above charcoal on a vertical spit, the fat cap rendered continuously over the fire. On a standard grill (gas or indirect charcoal), picanha is typically cooked in thick-sliced individual steaks or as a whole piece with a two-zone setup. The defining feature is the thick fat cap — it must be scored and rendered first over direct heat before the meat side is seared, or the fat will contract and curl the steak. Grilling picanha whole on indirect heat then searing is the most reliable approach for home grills. Carryover from a hot grill is 4–6 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Francis Mallmann — Grilled Picanha on the Parilla (Argentine/Brazilian tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–30 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole picanha (rump cap), approximately 1.2–1.5 kg, fat cap intact and scored in a crosshatch
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal or wood embers
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Score fat cap in a 2 cm crosshatch 5 mm deep. Season generously with coarse salt and pepper.
2. Build a two-zone charcoal fire: hot coals on one side, empty side on the other.
3. Place the whole picanha fat-cap-side down directly over the hot coals.
4. Grill fat-cap-side down 6–8 minutes until rendered and golden.
5. Flip; sear meat-side down over direct coals 3–4 minutes.
6. Move to the indirect side, meat-side down. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the meat.
7. Close the grill lid; roast over indirect heat for 10–15 minutes until the probe reads the target temperature.
8. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Mallmann's preference: *jugoso*. The whole picanha has high carryover.
9. Rest 10 minutes. Slice 1 cm thick against the grain. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Brazilian/Argentine churrasco standard).

### 2. Steven Raichlen — Coulotte Steak on the Grill (American tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *Project Fire* (Workman Publishing, 2018)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 picanha steaks (cut 2 cm thick from the whole cap, across the grain), fat cap left on and scored
- Coarse salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- Chimichurri or steak sauce, to serve

**Method**
1. Score fat caps; dry-brine steaks 1 hour; brush with oil.
2. Preheat grill to high. Set up a two-zone fire.
3. Place steaks fat-cap-side down directly over the coals. Grill 3–4 minutes until rendered.
4. Flip; sear meat-side down 3–4 minutes.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
6. Move to indirect heat; close lid; cook 4–6 minutes more.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's target: medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 3. Jock Zonfrillo — Grilled Rump Cap with Native Spice Rub (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Jock Zonfrillo, *Orana: Cooking with the Soul of Australia* (Hardie Grant, 2019)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h rub time) · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole picanha (rump cap), 1.2 kg
- **Native spice rub**: 15 g *wattleseed* (*wattleseed* — roasted and ground seeds of *Acacia* species, with coffee, chocolate and hazelnut notes); 5 g *lemon myrtle* (*lemon myrtle* — *Backhousia citriodora*, intensely lemon-citrus Australian native spice); 5 g dried river mint; 10 g coarse sea salt; 5 g freshly ground black pepper; 5 ml (1 tsp) vegetable oil
- Chimichurri with native pepperberry substituted for black pepper, to serve

**Method**
1. Score fat cap; mix rub ingredients; coat the entire picanha (fat cap and meat sides). Rest 2 hours refrigerated.
2. Preheat grill to high; set up two-zone fire.
3. Place fat-cap-side down over direct heat 5–7 minutes.
4. Flip; sear meat-side down 3–4 minutes.
5. Move to indirect; insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre.
6. Close lid; cook 10–15 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F).** Zonfrillo's standard: medium-rare with the native spice flavour penetrating the meat.
8. Rest 10 minutes. Slice; serve with native-spice chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian native-spice standard).

### 4. Bobby Flay — Grilled Picanha with Spiced Butter (American tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole picanha, 1–1.2 kg, fat cap scored
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- **Spiced butter**: 80 g softened butter; 5 g ancho chilli powder (*ancho* — dried poblano); 5 g smoked paprika; 5 ml (1 tsp) lime juice; salt

**Method**
1. Mix spiced butter; refrigerate. Season picanha; brush with oil.
2. Preheat grill to high; two-zone setup.
3. Grill fat-cap-side down 5–6 minutes; flip; grill meat-side down 4 minutes.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre; move to indirect.
5. Close lid; cook 5–8 minutes.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Flay's target: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; top with spiced butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 5. Neil Perry — Grilled Rump Cap with Salsa Verde (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 18–22 min · **Rest**: 8 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole picanha (rump cap), 1–1.2 kg
- Coarse sea salt and black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Salsa verde**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 15 g mint; 2 garlic cloves; 30 ml olive oil; 15 ml lemon juice; 10 g capers; salt

**Method**
1. Score fat cap; dry-brine 1 hour; brush with oil.
2. Blend salsa verde; set aside.
3. Preheat grill to high; two-zone.
4. Grill fat-cap-side down 5–7 minutes; flip; grill meat-side down 3–4 minutes.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end; move to indirect; close lid; cook 8–12 minutes.
6. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F).** Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 8 minutes. Slice; serve with salsa verde. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
