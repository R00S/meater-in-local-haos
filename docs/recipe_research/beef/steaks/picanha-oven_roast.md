# Picanha × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting picanha is well-suited to the whole muscle and especially effective with a reverse-sear approach: start at a low temperature (120–140 °C) to bring the entire piece to within 10 °C of the target temperature, then sear in a very hot skillet or under the broiler for 90 seconds per side to develop the crust. The fat cap renders slowly in the low oven and continually bastes the lean meat from above, keeping it moist without any manual basting.

For whole picanha without slicing, oven roasting at 180–200 °C roasts the whole piece in 25–45 min depending on size, but produces a larger gradient between the outer edge (more done) and the centre. The reverse sear eliminates this gradient.

Score the fat cap in a crosshatch pattern (cuts through fat, not into muscle) before roasting to help it render more evenly and prevent it from curling. MEATER probe goes lengthwise in the thickest part of the muscle, avoiding the fat cap. Carryover from a 120–140 °C oven is 4–6 °C; from a 200 °C oven, 5–7 °C.

## Styckesprofil

Ugnsrostning av picanha är väl lämpad för hela muskeln och särskilt effektiv med en reverse-sear-metod: börja vid låg temperatur (120–140 °C) för att föra hela stycket till inom 10 °C av måltemperaturen, bryna sedan i en mycket het stekpanna eller under broiler i 90 sekunder per sida för att utveckla skorpan. Fettkappan renderar långsamt i den låga ugnen och basar kontinuerligt det magra köttet uppifrån, vilket håller det fuktigt utan någon manuell basning.

För hel picanha utan skärning rostar ugnsrostning vid 180–200 °C hela stycket på 25–45 min beroende på storlek, men producerar en större gradient mellan den yttre kanten (mer tillagad) och centrumet. Reverse sear eliminerar denna gradient.

Skåra fettkappan i ett rutmönster (snitt genom fett, inte in i muskel) före rostning för att hjälpa den att rendera jämnare och förhindra att den krullar sig. MEATER-sond går längs i den tjockaste delen av muskeln, undvikande fettkappan. Eftervärme från en 120–140 °C ugn är 4–6 °C; från en 200 °C ugn, 5–7 °C.
## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Oven-Roasted Picanha (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Cook's Country TV Show Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2015)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 5 min (+overnight dry brine) · **Cook**: 30–40 min + stovetop sear · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole picanha (rump cap), 1.2–1.5 kg, fat cap scored in a 2 cm crosshatch
- Kosher salt (applied overnight)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for the optional stovetop sear)

**Method**
1. Score fat cap; apply kosher salt overnight; refrigerate uncovered.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Pat dry; apply pepper.
3. Place fat-cap-side up on a wire rack over a rimmed baking sheet.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the meat, below the fat cap.
5. Roast 30–40 minutes.
6. **Pull at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F)** (for a final medium-rare after a brief sear). If no sear: pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).
7. Optional: sear fat-cap-side down in a ripping-hot cast-iron pan 2 minutes before serving.
8. Rest 10 minutes. Slice against the grain. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Roast Rump Cap (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–45 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 picanha (rump cap), 1.2 kg, fat cap scored
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 4 garlic cloves, skin-on
- 4 sprigs thyme
- 30 g unsalted butter

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Score fat cap; season all over.
2. Heat a large oven-proof pan over high heat; add oil. Sear fat-cap-side down 3–4 minutes until golden.
3. Flip; sear meat-side down 2 minutes. Add garlic and thyme; dot with butter.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre.
5. Transfer the pan to the oven; roast 25–35 minutes, basting every 10 minutes with rendered fat.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Ramsay's standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 10 minutes. Slice. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, restaurant standard).

### 3. Heston Blumenthal — Reverse-Sear Picanha (British Modernist tradition)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *Heston Blumenthal at Home* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2011)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1 h at 120 °C + 5 min sear · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 picanha, 1.2 kg, fat cap scored
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) vegetable oil (for final sear)

**Method**
1. Score fat cap; season; place fat-cap-up on wire rack over tray.
2. Set oven to 120 °C (250 °F). Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre.
3. Roast at low temperature until internal temperature reaches 48–50 °C — approximately 45–60 minutes.
4. **Pull from low oven at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F).** The surface is perfectly dry — ideal for searing.
5. Heat cast-iron pan to absolute maximum; add oil. Sear fat-cap-side down 2–3 minutes; sear meat sides briefly.
6. Sear adds 3–5 °C at surface. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, Blumenthal reverse-sear standard).

### 4. Neil Perry — Roast Rump Cap with Herb Crust (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 30–40 min · **Rest**: 8 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 picanha, 1–1.2 kg, fat cap scored
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Herb crust**: 40 g panko breadcrumbs; 20 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped; 15 g Parmesan, finely grated; 1 garlic clove, minced; 15 ml olive oil; salt

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 30 minutes; mix herb crust.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Sear fat-cap-side down in a hot pan 3–4 minutes; flip; sear meat side 2 minutes.
3. Transfer to a wire rack. Press herb crust onto the meat side. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end.
4. Roast 20–25 minutes.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 8 minutes. Slice. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).

### 5. Donna Hay — Roasted Picanha with Chimichurri (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *The New Easy* (HarperCollins Australia, 2012)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 30–40 min · **Rest**: 8 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 picanha, 1–1.2 kg, fat cap scored
- Sea salt and black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- Chimichurri: 30 g parsley; 2 garlic cloves; 30 ml red wine vinegar; 60 ml olive oil; salt; chilli flakes

**Method**
1. Score fat cap; dry-brine 1 hour; brush with oil. Blend chimichurri; set aside.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Place fat-cap-side up on a roasting rack.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre.
4. Roast 30–40 minutes.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Hay's Australian standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 8 minutes. Slice; serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
