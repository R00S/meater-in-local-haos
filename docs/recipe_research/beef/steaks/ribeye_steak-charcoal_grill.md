# Ribeye Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The ribeye over charcoal is the premium grilling experience — charcoal provides both radiant heat and wood smoke flavour that gas grills cannot match. In Italy, the thick-cut Bistecca alla Fiorentina (typically a T-bone or Chianina ribeye) is the definitive charcoal-grilled beef: exterior well-coloured, interior al sangue (rare). The Maillard reaction across the fat cap creates a crust unlike any other cooking method. The steak must be at least 3 fingers thick (6–8 cm for Fiorentina) and allowed to reach room temperature before grilling.

Target internal temperatures (charcoal grill):
- Rare: **50–52 °C (122–126 °F)**
- Medium-rare: **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** — international norm
- Medium: **60–63 °C (140–145 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. GialloZafferano — Bistecca alla fiorentina (Italian tradition)
**Source**: GialloZafferano (with master butcher Dario Cecchini); https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Bistecca-alla-fiorentina.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *The authoritative Italian charcoal-grilled ribeye/T-bone: Chianina beef, aged 20–30 days, grilled over brace (charcoal of quercia/leccio — oak and holm oak).*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2–3 hr tempering · **Cook**: 20 min · **Difficulty**: Difficile

*Translation: Florentine Steak — a second course (secondo piatto) of Tuscan tradition. The exterior is well cooked, the interior al sangue (bloody) as tradition demands.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Core temperature 55–60 °C for medium-rare. Al sangue (rare) is the traditional Florentine target at 50–55 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 1 thick-cut ribeye or T-bone, approx. 1.2 kg (at least 3 finger-widths, 6–8 cm thick)
- Coarse sea salt (added only after cooking)
- Extra-virgin olive oil (added after cooking, cold)
- Charcoal: oak (quercia) and holm oak (leccio) — necessary for sustained high temperature

**Method**
1. Bring steak to room temperature for several hours before cooking. Dry-aged 20–30 days minimum (recommended by Cecchini).
2. Prepare brace: burn charcoal until it reaches maximum temperature with a faint white veil on the coals. Place the grill grate over.
3. Stand steak vertically on its bone edge beside (not over) the coals to warm gradually for a few minutes.
4. Lay flat over the coals. Grill **8 minutes per side**; turn once — visible grill lines confirm correct crust formation.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the ribeye, away from bone. **Pull at 50–53 °C (122–127 °F)** for traditional al sangue.
6. Stand upright on bone again briefly; the bone insulates and stabilises the temperature.
7. Transfer to a chopping board. Rest a few minutes. Season with coarse salt; drizzle cold extra-virgin olive oil; massage in by hand.
8. Carve alongside the bone into thick slices. Serve immediately, bone presented upright.
*Final resting temperature: 52–57 °C (126–135 °F).*

### 2. Madens Verden — Ribeye på gasgrill: Sådan griller du bøffen perfekt (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ribeye-paa-gasgrill/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Danish gas-grill method with frequent turning — same principle applies to charcoal: high direct heat, minimal seasoning, frequent turns.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 hr (gourmet salting) · **Cook**: 6–10 min

*Translation: Ribeye on the grill — the perfect steak method. The rich marbling means the steak never dries out.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Turn every 30 seconds for the most even result. 2–3 minutes per side total (6–8 minutes for a 250 g steak).

**Ingredients**
- 4 ribeye steaks, 200–250 g each (Greater Omaha or Wagyu quality recommended)
- Salt — 1 level tsp per side applied 2 hours ahead (gourmet salting / tørsaltning)
- Freshly ground pepper — added after grilling
- Rapeseed oil (for greasing grill grate)
- Knob of butter per steak (to top when resting)

**Method**
1. Salt steaks generously 2 hours before grilling; leave to brine at room temperature.
2. Prepare charcoal until hot and covered with grey ash. Grease grill grate with oil-soaked kitchen paper in tongs.
3. Lay steaks on the hottest part of the grill. Turn every 30 seconds throughout the cook — this is the key technique for even browning.
4. Total cook time: 6–8 minutes for medium-rare (250 g steak). Adjust for thicker cuts.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)**.
6. Rest 4–5 minutes with a knob of butter melting on top.
7. Season with freshly ground pepper (never during grilling — pepper burns). Serve with Danish-style spidskål salad or roasted potatoes.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*

### 3. Directo al Paladar — Chuletón a la brasa (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: Directoalpaladar.com; https://www.directoalpaladar.com/recetas-de-carnes-y-aves/receta-de-chuleton-con-salsa-de-vino-y-fresas (fetched 2026-04-24). *Spanish chuletón (beef ribeye/rib chop) on a hot plancha or charcoal grill — classic Spanish simplicity: high heat, minimal seasoning, fleur de sel added after.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 30 min (tempering) · **Cook**: 6–8 min

*Translation: Rib-eye on the grill/charcoal — Spanish beef tradition: the meat speaks for itself.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Para una carne tan sabrosa, lo recomendable es solamente marcar." (For such flavourful beef, just mark/sear it — medium-rare is the recommendation.) Pull at 52–55 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 1 chuletón (ribeye), thick cut, approx. 400–600 g
- Fleur de sel or coarse sea salt (added only after cooking)
- A drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil (applied to meat, not grill)

**Method**
1. Remove ribeye from refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking to temper.
2. Light charcoal (or preheat a plancha/griddle) to maximum heat.
3. Brush meat lightly with olive oil (not the grill). No seasoning before cooking.
4. Grill 2–3 minutes per side over direct high heat, achieving prominent grill marks.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 52–55 °C (126–131 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Add fleur de sal only after pulling from the grill.
7. Rest 2–3 minutes. Serve whole or sliced with a wine-strawberry sauce or simply with good olive oil.
*Final resting temperature: 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).*
