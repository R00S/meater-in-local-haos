# Ribeye Steak × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting is especially well-suited to the ribeye because the heavy marbling acts as a heat buffer — even in a high oven (220–230 °C), the liquefying fat slows the rate at which the interior heats, giving the cook a wider window before overcooking. The reverse-sear method (low oven at 120–135 °C to near-target temperature, followed by a very hot cast-iron sear for 60–90 seconds per side) produces the most even result for thick ribeyes (3 cm+): zero grey overcooked band, maximum edge-to-edge doneness, then a deep Maillard crust on the surface.

For thinner ribeyes, a one-step high-heat oven roast (220–230 °C, 8–15 min depending on thickness) with an initial stovetop sear works well; the oven provides ambient heat that avoids the uneven surface contact of a stovetop-only method.

The fat seam between the eye and rib cap in the oven does not cause problems — both sections reach near the same temperature given enough time. MEATER probe in the centre of the eye, away from the fat seam and bone. Carryover from a 120 °C oven: 4–6 °C. From a 220 °C oven: 5–7 °C.

Target internal temperatures:
- Medium-rare: pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F), rest to 57 °C (135 °F)
- Medium: pull at 57–59 °C (135–138 °F)

## Source recipes

### 1. ichkoche.at — Rib-eye-Steak mit Rosmarinpommes (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/rib-eye-steak-mit-rosmarinpommes-rezept-230639 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Austrian pan-then-oven method: hot pan sear 3–4 min per side, then low oven at 120 °C for 10–15 min to rest and finish. 32 user ratings.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 25 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Pan sear at very high heat 3–4 min per side; rest in oven at 120 °C for 10–15 minutes. "Steaks ungewürzt in sehr heißem Öl anbraten." Season only after the oven rest.

**Ingredients**
- 2 ribeye steaks, 250–300 g each
- Oil (neutral, high smoke point)
- Salt and pepper (applied after oven rest)
- Rosemary sprigs (in oven roasting pan)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 120 °C. Heat a cast-iron steak pan to very hot.
2. Fry steaks **unseasoned** in very hot oil, **3–4 minutes per side** — high caramelisation crust.
3. Transfer seared steaks to an oven roasting dish lined with rosemary sprigs.
4. Place in warm oven for **10–15 minutes** — juices settle, interior evens out.
5. Insert MEATER probe before the oven step. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Season with salt and pepper only after oven rest. Serve immediately.
*Final resting temperature: 54–57 °C (129–135 °F).*

### 2. BBC Good Food — Rib-eye steak with basil hollandaise (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/rib-eye-steak-basil-hollandaise (fetched 2026-04-24). *British method: olive oil and herb marinade, sear 3 min per side, then roast in a 200 °C oven for 15 minutes.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min + 10 min marinate · **Cook**: 20 min oven + 15 min rest

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Sear on high heat; slide into **200 °C fan oven for 15 minutes** turning halfway. Rest 15–20 minutes uncovered on a rack.

**Ingredients**
- 1 large bone-in ribeye, approx. 600 g
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves (unpeeled)
- Handful of thyme sprigs
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and black pepper

*Basil hollandaise (served alongside):*
- 100 ml white wine vinegar
- Small bunch basil (stalks for reduction, leaves for finishing)
- 2 egg yolks · 200 ml clarified butter · squeeze of lemon

**Method**
1. Rub steak with 2 tbsp olive oil, black pepper, and sea salt. Rest at room temperature 10 minutes.
2. Heat a heavy ovenproof frying pan until searingly hot.
3. Add steak with oil, garlic, thyme, and bay. Sear **3 minutes on first side**, basting with oil and herbs. Drain off oil; add 2 tbsp fresh oil; sear second side.
4. Quickly brown the edges. Slide the pan into oven at **200 °C fan (180 °C/gas 6). Roast 15 minutes**, turning halfway.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull from oven at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Transfer to a rack over a roasting tin. Tip the oil, herbs, and garlic from the pan over the steak. **Rest 15–20 minutes uncovered** — covering with foil makes it steam and lose its crust.
7. Serve with basil hollandaise and steak-frites.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Rindersteaks sanft garen mit Niedrigtemperatur (German tradition)
**Source**: Hobbykochen, Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1870431303884255/Rindersteaks-sanft-garen-mit-Niedrigtemperatur.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Low-temperature oven method: sear 2 min per side, then 85 °C oven for 45–50 minutes. 259 ratings, 4.8/5 stars.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr (de-chill) · **Cook**: 56 min total

*Translation: Beef steaks — gently cooked at low temperature. "The steaks come out butter-tender."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Sear 2 minutes per side, then 85 °C oven 45–50 minutes. At this temperature, steaks reach a stable medium (handballentest: thumb to ring finger).

**Ingredients**
- 4 beef steaks (ribeye recommended, any thickness)
- Oil for frying
- Salt and pepper from the mill

**Method**
1. Remove steaks from refrigerator **1 hour ahead** to de-chill.
2. Wash and pat very dry with kitchen paper.
3. Preheat oven to **85 °C Ober-/Unterhitze** (no fan). Place an oven-safe dish on the middle rack to pre-warm.
4. Heat oil in a frying pan on high. Sear steaks **2 minutes per side**. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Transfer seared steaks to the warm oven dish. Cook at **85 °C for 45–50 minutes**.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** for medium.
7. Warm serving plates in the oven or microwave during the oven phase.
8. Serve immediately with sauce of choice.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*
