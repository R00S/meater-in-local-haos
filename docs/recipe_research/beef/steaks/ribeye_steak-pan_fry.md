# Ribeye Steak × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The ribeye (also known as entrecôte, Scotch fillet, antrikot, costata, chuletón, Rib-Eye, or μπριζόλα rib eye) is cut from the rib section of the beef, ribs 7–12. It is richly marbled with intramuscular fat, making it one of the most flavourful and forgiving cuts for pan frying — the fat bastes the meat from within as it renders. Thickness of 2–3 cm is ideal for pan frying; thicker cuts should be finished in the oven.

Target internal temperatures (pan fry, resting temp):
- Rare: **50 °C (122 °F)**
- Medium-rare: **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** ← most common for ribeye
- Medium: **60–63 °C (140–145 °F)**
- Well done: **70+ °C (158+ °F)**

## Styckesprofil

Entrecôte (även kallad entrecôte, Scotch fillet, antrikot, costata, chuletón, Rib-Eye eller μπριζόλα rib eye) skärs från nötdjurets ribsektion, revben 7–12. Den är rikligt marmorerad med intramuskulärt fett, vilket gör den till ett av de mest smakrika och förlåtande snitten för pannstekning — fettet bastar köttet inifrån under tillagningen och smälter ut i pannan och bidrar till en rik, aromatisk fond. Innertemperatur: 54–57 °C medium-rare. Eftertillagning: 3–4 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. ichkoche.at — Rib-eye-Steak mit Rosmarinpommes (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/rib-eye-steak-mit-rosmarinpommes-rezept-230639 (fetched 2026-04-24). Austrian home-cooking classic: ribeye pan-fried then rested briefly in a warm oven. Rated 4+ stars, 32 user ratings.
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Pan-fry 3–4 minutes per side (high heat), then rest in 120 °C oven 10–15 minutes until juices redistribute and centre reaches desired doneness. Medium-rare pull temperature approx. 54 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 2 ribeye steaks (each 250–300 g, 2 cm thick)
- Oil for frying (neutral high-smoke-point oil)
- Salt and pepper
- Several sprigs fresh rosemary (for oven rest tray)

*Rosemary fries (Austrian side):*
- 4 medium starchy potatoes
- Oil for deep frying
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Peel potatoes and cut into fry strips. Fill fryer with oil and heat.
2. Bundle rosemary; cut topmost 1.5 cm into tiny pieces. Combine cut rosemary and salt in a large bowl. Place remaining rosemary sprigs in a roasting pan.
3. Preheat oven to 120 °C. Heat a steak pan until very hot. Fry steaks **unseasoned** in very hot oil, 3–4 minutes per side until a dark crust forms.
4. Lay seared steaks on the rosemary in the roasting pan. Place in the warm oven for 10–15 minutes to rest and finish.
5. Insert MEATER probe before the oven step. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Fry potato strips until golden and crispy. Toss in rosemary-salt bowl.
7. Season steaks with salt and pepper after the oven rest. Serve with rosemary fries.
*Final resting temperature: 54–57 °C (129–135 °F).*

### 2. Argiro.gr — Rib Eye στο τηγάνι με σάλτσα βουτύρου (Greek tradition)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou, Argiro.gr; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/rib-eye-sto-tigani-me-saltsa-voutyrou/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Greek celebrity chef Argiro Barbarigou's classic cast-iron pan sear with butter basting sauce.*
**Serves**: 1–2 · **Prep**: 1 hr (salting + tempering) · **Cook**: 8 min

*Translation: Rib Eye in the pan with butter sauce — a Greek steakhouse classic.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Είναι έτοιμο ψημένο medium όταν δείξει η ένδειξη του θερμομέτρου φτάσει στους 54ºC." (It is ready cooked medium when the thermometer reaches 54 °C.)

**Ingredients**
- 1 ribeye slice, approx. 600–700 g, 3–4 cm thick
- A little fleur de sel
- 2 tbsp mild olive oil
- 1 garlic clove
- 2–3 sprigs fresh thyme
- 20 g butter
- Freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Ask the butcher to cut the ribeye 3–4 cm thick. Pat very dry with kitchen paper.
2. Rest on a rack and season both sides with salt. Rub salt into the surface. Leave at room temperature for 30–60 minutes. When moisture appears on the surface (~30–40 min), wipe it completely dry again.
3. Heat a cast-iron pan on high until very hot.
4. Add olive oil; place ribeye in pan with tongs. Allow 30 seconds to brown, then flip.
5. Continue cooking, turning every minute for approximately 4 minutes total, building a caramelised crust all around.
6. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter, thyme, and garlic. Butter melts immediately.
7. After 1 minute remove the garlic.
8. Baste the steak continuously with a spoon, pouring the pan juices and butter all over it.
9. Keep turning and basting. **Pull at 54 °C (129 °F)** for medium.
10. Transfer to a board and cover loosely for 5–6 minutes. Slice and serve immediately with seasonal salad or dauphinoise potatoes.
*Final resting temperature: 57 °C (135 °F).*

### 3. BBC Good Food — Pan-fried rib-eye steak (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/premium/pan-fried-ribeye-steak (fetched 2026-04-24). *Classic British pan-fry with butter, garlic, and thyme basting.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ up to 8 hrs dry-brining) · **Cook**: 4–10 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "50C for rare, 60C for medium and 70C for well done."

**Ingredients**
- 2 ribeye steaks, each about 227 g, 2 cm thick
- 1 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tbsp/25 g butter
- 1 garlic clove, bashed
- Thyme sprigs (optional)
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Up to 8 hours before cooking, pat steaks dry and season with salt and pepper. Refrigerate.
2. Heat oil in a heavy-based frying pan over high flame. When shimmering, reduce to medium-high; add butter. Once sizzling, lay steaks in, tucking garlic and herbs at the sides.
3. Sear and turn every 30 seconds to 1 minute for a brown crust. Guide: **4 min total** for rare; **5–6 min** for medium; **8–10 min** for well done.
4. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 48 °C (118 °F)** for rare, **58 °C (136 °F)** for medium.
5. Rest at least 5 minutes. Optionally make a red wine sauce while resting.
6. Slice thickly against the grain and serve.
*Final resting temperature: 50–60 °C (122–140 °F) depending on doneness.*
