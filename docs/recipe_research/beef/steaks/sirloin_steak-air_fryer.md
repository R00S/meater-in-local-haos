## Cut profile
Air fryer sirloin at 200°C / 400°F: the 360° circulating hot air creates even browning
on all surfaces simultaneously. For a lean cut like sirloin, this is particularly
valuable — there is no hot-cold gradient across the steak as with pan searing. The
risk of drying out the lean muscle is lower in an air fryer than in a pan because the
air temperature is more controlled and uniform.

## What air fryer gives vs pan sear for lean sirloin
The even 360° air circulation reduces the temperature gradient that causes the lean
muscle to dry unevenly. Pan searing gives superior crust; air fryer gives more
consistent interior doneness with less risk of overshoot. The MEATER probe reads
the same temperature regardless of the method.
