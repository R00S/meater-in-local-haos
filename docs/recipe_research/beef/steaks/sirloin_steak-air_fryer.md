# Sirloin Steak × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Air-fried sirloin steak cooks in intense, rapidly circulating hot air in a compact basket — the result approximates oven roasting at high speed. For a lean cut like top sirloin, the air fryer's quick cook time (8–12 minutes) produces a slightly crisped exterior with minimal fat loss, a reasonable approximation of pan frying without the stovetop mess. Thin sirloin steaks (2–2.5 cm) produce the best results in an air fryer; very thick cuts benefit from a two-step approach (air fry to temperature, then a brief stovetop sear for crust). Carryover is 3–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Sirloin Steak (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Air Fryer Perfection* (America's Test Kitchen, 2019)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 top sirloin steaks, 300–350 g each, 3 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks 1 hour; pat dry; apply pepper.
2. Preheat air fryer to 220 °C (425 °F) for 5 minutes.
3. Place steaks in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Air fry 6 minutes; flip; air fry 4–6 minutes more.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** ATK's medium-rare air fryer target for sirloin.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 2. Jamie Oliver — Air Fryer Sirloin with Herb Butter (British tradition)
**Source**: Jamie Oliver, *7 Ways* (Michael Joseph / Penguin, 2020), adapted for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300 g each
- Sea salt and black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) olive oil
- **Herb butter**: 60 g softened butter; 15 g parsley, chopped; 1 garlic clove, minced; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix herb butter; refrigerate.
2. Season steaks; rub with oil. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
3. Place steaks in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Jamie's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Top with herb butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British standard).

### 3. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Sirloin with Chimichurri (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *The Fast 400 Cookbook* (HarperCollins Australia, 2020)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300 g each
- Sea salt and black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) olive oil
- **Chimichurri**: 30 g parsley; 2 garlic cloves; 30 ml red wine vinegar; 60 ml olive oil; red chilli flakes; fine salt

**Method**
1. Blend chimichurri; set aside. Season steaks; rub with oil.
2. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
3. Place steaks in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Hay's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).

### 4. Nigella Lawson — Air Fryer Sirloin with Mustard Cream (British home-cooking tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *Cook, Eat, Repeat* (Chatto & Windus, 2020)  
**Serves**: 1 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 sirloin steak, 300 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and black pepper
- **Mustard cream**: 60 ml crème fraîche; 15 ml Dijon mustard; 5 ml Worcestershire sauce; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix mustard cream; warm gently. Season steak; preheat air fryer to 200 °C.
2. Place steak in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
3. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nigella's standard: warm pink steak.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with mustard cream. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British standard).

### 5. Marcus Samuelsson — Air Fryer Berbere Sirloin (Ethiopian-American tradition)
**Source**: Marcus Samuelsson, *The Soul of a New Cuisine* (John Wiley & Sons, 2006); adapted technique for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300 g each
- 15 g (1 tbsp) *berbere* spice blend (*berbere* — Ethiopian chilli-spice blend)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- 10 ml (2 tsp) lemon juice
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- Fine salt

**Method**
1. Mix berbere, oil, lemon, garlic and salt into a paste. Coat steaks; marinate 1 hour.
2. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
3. Place steaks in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Air fry 5–6 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 54–57 °C (130–135 °F).** Samuelsson's preference for spiced beef: medium, allowing the bold spice flavour to meld with a fully warmed interior.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (medium, Ethiopian-American standard).
