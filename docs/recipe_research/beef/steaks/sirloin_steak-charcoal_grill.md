## Cut profile
Charcoal grilling sirloin uses a two-zone fire with hardwood lump charcoal at 230°C /
450°F+. The smoky aromatics from combustion penetrate the lean muscle more noticeably than
with marbled cuts — adding complexity to the clean beef flavour. Regional variations include
South African braai (simple salt, hardwood), Japanese yakiniku (thin sliced, soy/sake
marinade), UK (salt and garlic butter), and Argentine asado (slower, wood coals, salt only).

## What charcoal adds for lean sirloin
Unlike marbled ribeye where fat-drip smoke is the main charcoal benefit, with lean sirloin
the combustion aromatics themselves (from hardwood) directly penetrate and add complexity.
Argentine quebracho, South African Namibian bush charcoal, and Japanese binchotan all
produce different smoke character but identical internal temperature targets.
