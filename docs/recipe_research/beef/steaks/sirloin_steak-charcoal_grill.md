# Sirloin Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Sirloin steak over live charcoal acquires the distinctive smokiness and char flavour that only real charcoal combustion products can provide. The lean top sirloin muscle — firmer and less marbled than the ribeye or NY strip — forms a particularly satisfying crust over very hot coals because the dry surface chars rapidly. Korean BBQ traditions feature thinly sliced sirloin grilled directly over charcoal (*charcoal-grilled bulgogi*); South American asado traditions use thick bone-in sirloin; American competition BBQ uses direct high heat. Carryover from very hot charcoal fire is 5–7 °C, making early pulling essential.

## Source recipes

### 1. Francis Mallmann — Sirloin over Charcoal Embers (Argentine asado tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–16 min over embers · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 thick top sirloin steaks, 500–600 g each, 4–5 cm thick (Mallmann works with very large cuts for asado)
- Coarse salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal or quebracho wood (burned down to embers)
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Burn hardwood charcoal to white-ash embers with no visible flames.
2. Season steaks with coarse salt and pepper just before grilling — Mallmann never marinates asado beef.
3. Place steaks directly over the hottest section of the ember bed.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Grill 6–8 minutes per side without moving. The ember heat is intense but not as hot as gas; the smoke is essential.
6. **Pull at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F).** Mallmann's Argentine preference: *jugoso* (very juicy, deep red interior). The very high charcoal carryover means pulling 5–7 °C early.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine asado standard).

### 2. Steven Raichlen — Sirloin over Direct Charcoal (American tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) coarse salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 2–3 hardwood chunks (oak preferred)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks 1 hour. Start a charcoal chimney; pour when white-hot.
2. Add soaked wood chunks to the hot coals.
3. Brush steaks with oil. Place over the hottest part of the grill.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre.
5. Grill 4–5 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American charcoal standard).

### 3. Maangchi — Charcoal-Grilled Galbi / Sirloin (Korean tradition)
**Source**: Maangchi (Emily Kim), *Maangchi's Real Korean Cooking* (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2015)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+4 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g top sirloin (*deungsim* — Korean sirloin), thinly sliced 5 mm or cut into thin 1.5 cm steaks
- **Bulgogi marinade**: 60 ml (¼ cup) soy sauce (*ganjang*); 30 ml (2 tbsp) pear juice (or grated Asian pear — enzymes tenderise the lean beef); 15 ml (1 tbsp) sesame oil; 15 g (1 tbsp) caster sugar; 3 garlic cloves, minced; 5 g (1 tsp) grated fresh ginger; 2 spring onions, finely sliced; 2 g (¼ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- Sesame seeds and spring onions, to garnish
- Steamed short-grain rice, to serve

**Method**
1. Combine marinade ingredients; coat sirloin thoroughly. Marinate 4 hours (or overnight refrigerated).
2. Drain excess marinade from the surface of each piece.
3. Light charcoal grill (a portable Korean charcoal tabletop grill is traditional — *숯불구이*). Heat to high.
4. For thin slices, a MEATER probe is impractical — monitor by visual colour change (the soy marinade will caramelise quickly). For thicker 1.5 cm steaks, insert the probe from the side into the centre of the thickest piece.
5. Grill thin slices 1–2 minutes per side; thicker steaks 3 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).** Korean BBQ tradition typically cooks beef fully through — the soy and sugar glaze caramelises and the thin sirloin should be cooked to opaque throughout.
7. Rest 3 minutes. Serve wrapped in lettuce leaves with kimchi and rice. Final serving temperature: **60–62 °C / 140–144 °F** (fully cooked, Korean charcoal standard).

### 4. Lennox Hastie — Sirloin over Australian Ironbark Coals (Australian wood-fire tradition)
**Source**: Lennox Hastie, *Finding Fire* (Hardie Grant, 2017)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 premium Australian sirloin steaks, 400–500 g each, 4 cm thick
- Coarse sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Ironbark hardwood (or apple wood — Hastie's preferred Australian fuel for beef)

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks 1 hour. Build a hardwood fire; burn to deep embers.
2. Season steaks with salt and pepper just before cooking.
3. Place steaks directly over the glowing coals. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Grill 5–6 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Hastie's live-fire target for premium sirloin: medium-rare with strong carryover allowance.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, Australian live-fire standard).

### 5. Bobby Flay — Sirloin with Chipotle Butter over Charcoal (American Southwest tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sirloin steaks, 300 g each
- Sea salt and black pepper
- **Chipotle butter**: 80 g softened butter; 1 chipotle pepper in adobo (*chipotle en adobo* — smoked jalapeño in tomato-vinegar sauce), finely mashed; 5 g (1 tsp) fresh lime juice; 5 g (1 tsp) fresh coriander, chopped; fine salt

**Method**
1. Mix chipotle butter; refrigerate.
2. Start charcoal chimney; pour when white-hot.
3. Season steaks; place over the hottest coals.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Flay's standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Top with chipotle butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American Southwest standard).
