## Cut profile
Reverse sear for sirloin: low oven (100-120°C) on a wire rack to 47-49°C internal,
then a screaming-hot pan sear. The lean muscle benefits enormously from this technique —
the slow oven phase eliminates all moisture loss from hot-surface shock, and the final
sear adds crust with minimal carryover. British, Swedish, and German home kitchens all
increasingly use this method for thick sirloin joints.

## What makes oven roast uniquely valuable for lean sirloin
Lean sirloin dries rapidly when outer layers overshoot during a high-heat sear. The low
oven phase (100-120°C) brings the ENTIRE cross-section to 49°C uniformly before any
searing. The lean muscle retains nearly all its moisture, and the 90-second final sear
adds crust without any additional interior cooking. The result is a perfectly pink,
moisture-rich interior that a purely pan-seared sirloin rarely achieves.
