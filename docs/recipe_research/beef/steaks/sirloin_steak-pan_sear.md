# Sirloin Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The sirloin steak is cut from the sirloin primal, which sits between the short loin (where the NY strip originates) and the round. Top sirloin is the most common steak cut from this primal — it is a firm, relatively lean steak with a bold beefy flavour from the greater muscle activity of this region of the carcass. Compared to the NY strip, top sirloin has less marbling and a slightly coarser grain, making it less forgiving of overcooking; compared to the round, it is noticeably more tender. Pan searing suits sirloin well because the high heat compensates for the reduced marbling by creating a flavourful crust — the Maillard reaction is the primary flavour contribution to a seared top sirloin. The cap muscle (*picanha* region of the sirloin cap) is sometimes separated and considered a distinct cut; this leaf concerns the main top sirloin steak. Carryover is 4–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Pan-Seared Top Sirloin (American tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015); seriouseats.com/perfect-pan-seared-steaks-recipe  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 top sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- Kosher salt (for dry brine, applied 1 h before cooking)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 3 garlic cloves, skin-on, crushed
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme

**Method**
1. Apply kosher salt to all surfaces 1 hour ahead; refrigerate uncovered. Pat dry before cooking.
2. Season with black pepper immediately before searing.
3. Heat a cast-iron pan over the highest heat for 5 minutes until smoking. Add oil.
4. Place steaks in the pan. Flip every 30 seconds using Kenji's rapid-flip technique.
5. After 2 minutes, insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre, clear of any fat seams.
6. Add butter, garlic and thyme after 2 minutes of flipping; baste continuously.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Kenji's medium-rare target. For top sirloin, which is leaner than ribeye, he notes that cooking past medium-rare significantly degrades the eating quality.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, warm pink interior).

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Pan-Seared Sirloin with Pepper Sauce (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min tempering) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 40 g unsalted butter
- 3 garlic cloves, skin-on, smashed
- **Pepper sauce**: 2 shallots, minced; 1 tbsp whole black peppercorns, coarsely crushed; 50 ml (3½ tbsp) brandy; 150 ml (⅔ cup) beef stock; 80 ml (⅓ cup) double cream

**Method**
1. Remove steaks from fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Pat dry; season.
2. Heat a heavy pan over very high heat. Add oil until smoking.
3. Sear steaks 3 minutes per side without moving.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre after the first flip.
5. Add butter and garlic; baste 2 minutes.
6. Remove steaks to rest. Make pepper sauce in the same pan: sauté shallots and crushed peppercorns; add brandy (flambe if desired); add stock; reduce; add cream; reduce to sauce consistency.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Ramsay's standard for sirloin: medium-rare with a firm pink interior.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with pepper sauce. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, restaurant standard).

### 3. Nobu Matsuhisa — Pan-Seared Sirloin with Teriyaki Glaze (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Nobu Matsuhisa and Mark Edwards, *Nobu: The Cookbook* (Kodansha International, 2001)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 250–300 g each, 2.5 cm thick
- **Teriyaki marinade**: 60 ml (¼ cup) soy sauce (*shoyu*); 45 ml (3 tbsp) mirin; 30 ml (2 tbsp) sake; 15 g (1 tbsp) caster sugar; 5 ml (1 tsp) sesame oil
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- Steamed rice and shredded spring onion, to serve

**Method**
1. Combine teriyaki ingredients in a saucepan; bring to a simmer; cook 3 minutes; cool. Reserve half for basting; marinate steaks in the other half for 30 minutes.
2. Remove from marinade; pat dry.
3. Heat a cast-iron or carbon-steel pan over high heat. Add oil.
4. Place steaks. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Sear 3 minutes per side; brush with reserved teriyaki glaze in the final minute.
6. **Pull at 54–57 °C (130–135 °F).** Nobu's teriyaki preparation targets medium — the soy glaze masks the visual cues, so the probe is critical.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice; serve with rice and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (medium, Japanese glaze standard).

### 4. Francis Mallmann — Pan-Seared Sirloin in Cast Iron (Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) beef dripping or vegetable oil
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Season steaks generously with coarse salt and pepper just before cooking.
2. Heat a heavy cast-iron pan over the highest heat until smoking. Add dripping.
3. Place steaks. Sear 3–4 minutes per side without moving.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into the centre.
5. Stand on edge briefly to render any fat strip.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Mallmann's Argentine preference is *jugoso* — warm red interior, the Argentine definition of the ideal.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine standard).

### 5. Stephanie Alexander — Pan-Fried Sirloin (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Stephanie Alexander, *The Cook's Companion* (Viking/Penguin, 1996; revised 2004), beef chapter  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min tempering) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 top sirloin steaks (Australian grade, preferably MSA or Wagyu crossbred), 300–350 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- Sea salt flakes and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil or clarified butter
- 20 g unsalted butter
- Lemon juice and fresh herbs, to serve

**Method**
1. Temper steaks 30 minutes. Season just before cooking.
2. Heat a heavy frying pan (cast iron preferred) over high heat. Add oil.
3. Sear steaks 3–4 minutes per side; flip once.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre of one steak.
5. Add butter; baste briefly.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Alexander's Australian standard for sirloin is medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Squeeze lemon over; scatter herbs. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
