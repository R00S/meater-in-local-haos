# Sirloin Steak × Sous Vide — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Top sirloin cooked sous vide benefits especially from the method because its lean, firmer muscle responds dramatically to prolonged low-temperature cooking: the collagen in the slightly tougher sirloin muscle has time to soften without exceeding the target temperature, producing a more tender result than a quick pan sear at the same final temperature. At 54–57 °C for 2–4 hours, top sirloin achieves the tenderness of a much more expensive cut. The bath temperature is the target internal temperature; carryover from the post-bath sear is 2–3 °C at the surface only.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Sous Vide Top Sirloin (American tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015); seriouseats.com/the-food-lab-complete-guide-to-sous-vide-steak  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1–4 h at 54 °C + 90-second sear · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 top sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each, 3–4 cm thick
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for post-bath sear)
- 30 g unsalted butter (for baste)

**Method**
1. Season steaks; seal in vacuum bags. Set bath to **54 °C (130 °F)**; cook 1–4 hours. Longer times (4 h) produce noticeably more tender sirloin.
2. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be inserted during sealed-bag cook. Bath temperature (54 °C) is the cook target.
3. Remove; pat completely dry.
4. Heat cast-iron pan to absolute maximum; sear 60–90 seconds per side.
5. **Bath temperature: 54 °C (130 °F).** Final serving temperature: **54 °C / 130 °F** at the core (medium-rare, Kenji sous vide standard).

### 2. Thomas Keller — Sous Vide Sirloin (French haute cuisine tradition)
**Source**: Thomas Keller, *Under Pressure: Cooking Sous Vide* (Artisan Books, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 1 h at 57.5 °C + sear · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each
- Fine salt and white pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) grapeseed oil (in bag)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) grapeseed oil (for sear)
- 60 g unsalted butter (for baste)

**Method**
1. Season; seal in bags with grapeseed oil. Set bath to **57.5 °C (136 °F)**. Cook 1 hour.
2. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be used during sealed-bag cook. Bath temperature is the cook target.
3. Remove; pat dry. Sear 60–90 seconds per side; baste with butter.
4. **Bath temperature: 57.5 °C (135 °F).** Keller's published sous vide target for sirloin — medium. Final serving temperature: **57.5 °C / 135 °F** at the core (medium, Keller standard).

### 4. Grant Achatz — Sous Vide Sirloin with Aromatic Bag (American Modernist tradition)
**Source**: Grant Achatz and Nick Kokonas, *Alinea* (Ten Speed Press, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 2 h at 54 °C + sear · **Rest**: 0 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each
- Fine salt and black pepper

- 15 ml (1 tbsp) unsalted butter (in bag)

**Method**
1. Season; seal in bags with thyme, garlic and butter. Set bath to **54 °C (130 °F)**; cook 2 hours — the extended time tenderises the sirloin significantly.
2. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe cannot be inserted during sealed-bag cook. Bath temperature (54 °C) is the cook target.
3. Remove; pat dry. Sear 60–90 seconds per side.
4. **Bath temperature: 54 °C (130 °F).** Achatz's modernist target: medium-rare. Final serving temperature: **54 °C / 130 °F** at the core (medium-rare, American modernist standard).
