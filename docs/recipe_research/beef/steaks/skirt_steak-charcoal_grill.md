# Skirt Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Skirt steak over live charcoal is arguably its highest form — in taquero tradition, *arrachera asada* (literally "grilled skirt steak") means skirt steak cooked over mesquite or hardwood charcoal, where the fat drippings ignite the coals into brief, flavour-depositing flare-ups. The smoky charcoal notes complement the intense beefy flavour of the diaphragm muscle. Mexican *taquero* tradition uses very hot mesquite charcoal; Argentine *parrilla* tradition uses quebracho wood embers. The probe challenge is the same as on a gas grill: thin steak, end insertion required. Carryover from very hot charcoal is 5–7 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Diana Kennedy — *Arrachera Asada* over Mesquite (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *My Mexico: A Culinary Odyssey* (Clarkson Potter, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 5–6 min over mesquite · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak (*arrachera*)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) lime juice
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) dried Mexican oregano (*orégano mexicano*)
- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Mesquite charcoal
- Corn tortillas, lime, salsa, sliced white onion, coriander, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix marinade from garlic, lime, cumin, oregano, salt and pepper; coat arrachera; marinate 1 hour.
2. Light mesquite charcoal. Allow to reach full-heat white-ash stage.
3. Wipe excess marinade from steak surface.
4. Place directly over the hottest part of the mesquite coals.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end, angling along the length toward the thickest part.
6. Grill 2–3 minutes per side. Mesquite runs very hot; flare-ups will occur — they add flavour.
7. **Pull at 49–51 °C (120–124 °F).** Very high charcoal carryover (5–7 °C) means very early pulling for a medium-rare finish.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin on a bias, against the grain, with a sharp knife. Serve on warm corn tortillas with lime, salsa, coriander and white onion. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, Mexican street-food tradition).

### 2. Francis Mallmann — Skirt over Charcoal Embers (Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g skirt steak (*entraña fina* — Argentine term for outside skirt)
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Hardwood charcoal (quebracho)
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Season steak with coarse salt only — Mallmann's tradition for asado cuts is minimal seasoning.
2. Light quebracho charcoal; burn to glowing white embers.
3. Place *entraña* directly over the hottest ember section.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. Grill 2–3 minutes per side — the thin skirt cooks extremely quickly over the hot quebracho.
6. **Pull at 48–50 °C (118–122 °F).** Mallmann's target: *jugoso*. The quebracho heat is intense; carryover is 6–7 °C.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine traditional standard).

### 3. Steven Raichlen — Charcoal-Grilled Skirt Steak (American BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *Planet Barbecue!* (Workman Publishing, 2010)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak
- **Achiote marinade**: 30 g *achiote paste* (*achiote* — annatto seed paste, used in Yucatecan and Caribbean cooking for colour and earthy flavour); 2 garlic cloves, minced; 30 ml orange juice; 15 ml lime juice; 5 ml olive oil; 5 g ground cumin; salt and pepper
- Hardwood charcoal; mesquite wood chunks (optional)
- Warm tortillas, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix achiote marinade; coat steak; marinate 2 hours.
2. Light charcoal; set up for direct high heat.
3. Wipe excess marinade; place steak over coals.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. Grill 2–3 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Raichlen's standard: medium-rare with charcoal carryover allowance.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin against the grain. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 4. Bobby Flay — Charcoal-Grilled Skirt Steak with Chipotle (American Southwest tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak
- **Marinade**: 2 chipotle peppers in adobo (*chipotle en adobo*), minced; 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 3 garlic cloves, minced; 15 ml lime juice; 5 g ground cumin; 5 g smoked paprika; salt
- Hardwood charcoal
- Lime crema and avocado, to serve

**Method**
1. Marinate steak 1 hour; wipe excess.
2. Light charcoal; direct high heat.
3. Grill 2–3 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Flay's target: medium-rare with charcoal carryover.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice thin; serve with lime crema and avocado. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, American Southwest standard).

### 5. Lennox Hastie — Outside Skirt over Australian Live Coals (Australian live-fire tradition)
**Source**: Lennox Hastie, *Finding Fire* (Hardie Grant, 2017)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g outside skirt steak
- Coarse sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Ironbark or banksia hardwood charcoal

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 30 minutes; season with pepper just before grilling.
2. Build an ironbark charcoal fire; burn to glowing embers.
3. Place steak directly over embers.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. Grill 2–3 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 49–51 °C (120–124 °F).** Hastie's live-fire target for thin cuts: medium-rare with very high carryover allowance.
7. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, Australian live-fire tradition).
