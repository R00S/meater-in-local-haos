# Skirt Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Skirt steak (outside skirt from the diaphragm, or inside skirt from the plate) is a
long, thin, heavily grained cut with pronounced beefy flavour and relatively high fat
content for a working muscle. Over live charcoal the fat drips and flares, creating
bursts of smoke and char that penetrate the shallow grain in a way no other method
can replicate. The thinness means the window between charred exterior and a perfect
interior is measured in seconds — this is a cut that rewards absolute attention.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Grilled Skirt Steak (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-skirt-steak
- **Method**: Set up a full chimney of hardwood lump charcoal; grill 2–3 cm above the
  coals. Cook the whole skirt in 3–4 min per side over screaming-hot charcoal, flipping
  once. Rest 5 min, cut against the grain at 45°.
- **Pull temp**: 125–130°F / **52–54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Screaming-hot charcoal delivers maximum Maillard reaction
  across the entire thin surface before the interior can overshoot. Flare-ups from
  rendered fat add smoke character that is inseparable from classic charcoal skirt.

### 2. Rick Bayless — Arrachera a las brasas (Mexico / Frontera Grill)
- **Source**: rickbayless.com/recipe/grilled-skirt-steak-arrachera
- **Method**: Marinate in citrus, garlic, chilli; grill over mesquite charcoal at
  extreme heat 3–4 min per side. Rest 5 min loosely tented with foil. Slice thin.
- **Pull temp**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57–60°C**
- **Why this method works**: Mesquite charcoal at very high heat chars the marinade
  crust into a smoky, caramelised shell while keeping the interior juicy. The slightly
  higher target suits Mexican taco tradition where a touch of "bite" is desirable.

### 3. Stéphane Reynaud — Jupe de bœuf aux braises (France / Bistrot de Cuisine)
- **Source**: stephanereynaud.com/jupe-braise
- **Method**: Chêne (oak) or châtaignier (chestnut) charcoal; grill directly over the
  glow 2–3 min per side, maximum. Dress only with fleur de sel and cornichons after
  resting 5 min.
- **Pull temp**: **50–52°C** (saignante vive)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **52–54°C**
- **Why this method works**: French tradition demands the juices flow when sliced;
  charcoal achieves the char without the sustained heat that ruins this cut's long fibres.

### 4. Weber Grillakademie — Flank und Skirt über Holzkohle (Germany / Weber DE)
- **Source**: weber.com/DE/de/rezepte/rindfleisch/gegrilltes-skirt-steak
- **Method**: Kugelgrill (kettle grill) with a full load of Holzkohle; direct heat,
  lid off. 3–4 min per side. Pull and tent; slice across grain on a warm board.
- **Pull temp**: **54–57°C** (medium-rare)
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **57–60°C**
- **Why this method works**: Kettle geometry over charcoal concentrates radiant heat
  from below and reflected heat from the dome, driving Maillard crust formation even
  faster than an open grate — ideal for a cut where speed protects tenderness.

### 5. Matprat — Kjøl på kull (Norway / Opplysningskontoret for kjøtt)
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/kjol-pa-kull
- **Method**: Full brikettgrill or lump charcoal; direct grilling at highest heat.
  2–3 min per side for a thin cut. Pull at 54°C, rest 5 min, slice thinly against grain.
- **Pull temp**: **130°F / 54°C**
- **Final after rest (5 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Nordic approach applies the same discipline as pan sear but
  outdoors — pull early and let rest do the finishing work. Charcoal smoke adds a
  dimension that cold-climate cooks prize in an otherwise lean flavour profile.
