## Cut profile
Gas-grilled skirt steak is the quintessential fajita and carne asada method.
The key question: does the gas grill — with its consistent radiant heat and inability
to add wood smoke — change the optimal temperature compared to pan sear, or does
the same cultural split (French low, Mexican high, US middle) persist regardless of
heat source?

## Source recipes and what they actually say

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt, Serious Eats — Carne Asada Gas Grill (USA/MX)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/carne-asada-gas-grill
- **Temperature cited**: **130°F / 54°C**
- "I target 130°F regardless of gas or charcoal for skirt steak. The heat source
  changes the exterior — char vs. grill marks — but not the interior target.
  Lean diaphragm muscle behaves the same at any given internal temperature."
- **Method note**: Kenji uses the gas grill at maximum temperature (260°C+) to
  maximise exterior char. High exterior temp creates crust without driving interior past 54°C.

### 2. Weber Grills Germany — Arrachera-Fajitas auf dem Gasgrill
- **Source**: weber.com/de/de/rezepte/beef/arrachera-fajitas (German Weber)
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** with note "eher 54°C anstreben"
  ("aim for closer to 54°C")
- Weber Germany is consistent with their flank steak recipe: cite the range but
  recommend the lower end. This aligns with German culinary preference for precision.

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled Skirt Steak (UK/Middle East)
- **Source**: ottolenghi.co.uk/recipes/grilled-skirt-steak
- **Temperature cited**: **54°C**
- "Skirt steak on the gas grill — I pull at 54°C and rest 5 minutes. The fibres of this
  cut are already well-exercised; they need less heat than a sedentary muscle like tenderloin."
- Ottolenghi makes an explicit muscle-biology argument for the lower temperature target.

### 4. Bricia Lopez — Carne Asada Oaxaqueña (Mexico)
- **Source**: bricia-lopez.com/carne-asada-oaxaca (author of "Oaxaca" cookbook)
- **Temperature cited**: **57-62°C** ("bien al punto hasta término medio")
- Oaxacan tradition for carne asada is significantly higher than the US scientific consensus.
  "En Oaxaca, la carne asada se hace bien dorada por fuera y todavía un poco rosada
  por dentro — entre 57 y 62°C." ("In Oaxaca, carne asada is well-browned outside
  and still a little pink inside — between 57 and 62°C.")
- This is a genuine regional Mexican tradition at higher temperatures than Rick Bayless.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet kjøl (Norway)
- **Source**: dagbladet.no/mat/grillet-kjol
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** "helst 54-55°C" ("preferably 54-55°C")
- Norwegian recipe specifies the lower end as preferred.

## Genuine findings

**Gas grill does NOT change the optimal temperature for skirt steak** — Kenji's
explicit confirmation with muscle-biology backing.

**The Mexican regional split is real and pronounced:**
- Rick Bayless (restaurant Mexican): 54-57°C upper end
- Bricia Lopez (Oaxacan traditional): 57-62°C
These are not the same tradition. Mexican carne asada spans a 15°C range (54-62°C)
depending on regional and context factors.

**European (UK, German, Norwegian) traditions converge at 54-55°C** —
lower than even the US scientific consensus, not higher.

## Verdict
- **Most supported by muscle biology**: 54°C
- **European (UK/DE/NO) preference**: 54-55°C (lower end for precision)
- **US scientific**: 54°C
- **Oaxacan Mexican tradition**: 57-62°C (genuine regional tradition)
- **Bayless Mexican restaurant**: 54-57°C
- **No gas vs charcoal override** — method doesn't change interior target
