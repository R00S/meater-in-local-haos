# Skirt Steak × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan-fried skirt steak is closely related to pan searing but typically uses more oil in the pan and is associated with everyday home cooking in Latin American and Asian traditions, where a lightly-oiled shallow fry produces a uniformly browned crust rather than the deeply seared crust of a restaurant sear. In Chinese-American wok cooking, skirt steak (*beef flap*) is fried in very hot oil as part of stir-fry dishes; in Mexican home cooking, the comal-fried *arrachera* is a daily preparation. The MEATER probe should be inserted from the thin end, angling toward the thickest part. Carryover is 3–4 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Diana Kennedy — Comal-Fried *Arrachera* (Mexican home cooking tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *My Mexico: A Culinary Odyssey* (Clarkson Potter, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g skirt steak (*arrachera*)
- Coarse salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil (for the comal)
- Lime wedges, sliced chillies, corn tortillas, to serve

**Method**
1. Season skirt steak with coarse salt and pepper.
2. Heat a *comal* or flat heavy pan over medium-high heat; add a thin film of oil.
3. Place skirt steak on the comal. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end, angling toward the thickest part.
4. Fry 2–3 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–57 °C (126–135 °F).** Kennedy's Mexican home tradition: medium to medium-rare. The comal's moderate heat produces an even brown rather than a deep sear.
6. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin against the grain. Serve with tortillas and lime. Final serving temperature: **55–60 °C / 131–140 °F** (medium-rare to medium, Mexican home standard).

### 2. Barbara Tropp — Wok-Fried Flank/Skirt Steak with Ginger (Chinese-American tradition)
**Source**: Barbara Tropp, *The Modern Art of Chinese Cooking* (William Morrow, 1982)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+30 min marinade) · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g skirt steak, sliced thin across the grain (5 mm thick)
- **Marinade**: 30 ml soy sauce; 15 ml Shaoxing rice wine (*Shaoxing* — Chinese aged rice wine, essential to Chinese-American stir-fry marinades); 10 ml sesame oil; 5 g cornstarch; 1 egg white; white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) vegetable oil (for wok)
- 30 g fresh ginger, julienned; 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; 4 spring onions, cut in 5 cm lengths
- 30 ml oyster sauce; 15 ml soy sauce; 10 ml dark soy sauce; 10 ml sesame oil; 10 ml rice vinegar

**Method**
1. Marinate sliced steak 30 minutes. Mix sauce.
2. Heat wok over maximum heat until smoking; add oil; heat until shimmering.
3. Add steak slices; fry in batches 90 seconds per batch — do not overcrowd. Remove and set aside.
4. **Note for MEATER probe**: Probe is impractical for wok-sliced thin strips. Monitor by colour: deep brown exterior, pink centre at 2 minutes per side for thin slices.
5. Return steak to wok with ginger, garlic, and spring onions; toss 30 seconds; add sauce; toss to coat.
6. **Target colour and texture**: steak strips should be deep brown outside with just-pink centre.  
   **Estimated pull temperature**: 60–65 °C (140–149 °F) — Chinese stir-fry tradition typically finishes beef fully through for food safety in commercial kitchens, though home preparation at 57–60 °C is common.
7. Serve immediately with steamed rice. Final serving temperature: **60–65 °C / 140–149 °F** (medium to medium-well, Chinese-American wok tradition).

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Pan-Fried Skirt Steak with Salsa Verde (British contemporary tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *Nigella Express* (Chatto & Windus, 2007)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g skirt steak
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Salsa verde**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 15 g fresh mint; 10 g fresh basil; 1 garlic clove; 15 g capers; 4 anchovy fillets; 30 ml olive oil; 15 ml red wine vinegar; salt

**Method**
1. Blend salsa verde; set aside. Season skirt steak.
2. Heat a pan over high heat; add olive oil.
3. Fry skirt steak 2–3 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end, angling toward the thickest part.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nigella's standard: medium-rare, pink interior.
6. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin against the grain. Serve with salsa verde. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, British tradition).

### 4. Nobu Matsuhisa — Pan-Fried Skirt Steak with Black Bean Sauce (Japanese-Chinese tradition)
**Source**: Nobu Matsuhisa, *Nobu: The Cookbook* (Kodansha International, 2001)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g skirt steak, cut in sections
- **Marinade**: 30 ml soy sauce; 15 ml mirin; 15 ml sesame oil; 2 garlic cloves, minced
- **Black bean sauce**: 30 g fermented black beans, rinsed and roughly mashed; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 15 g fresh ginger, minced; 30 ml soy sauce; 15 ml oyster sauce; 15 ml sesame oil; 15 ml chilli sauce

**Method**
1. Marinate 1 hour; wipe excess. Heat sauce ingredients in a small pan; keep warm.
2. Heat a flat pan over high heat; add thin film of oil.
3. Fry skirt steak 2–3 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
5. **Pull at 54–57 °C (130–135 °F).** Nobu's fusion standard: medium.
6. Rest 3 minutes. Slice; drizzle with black bean sauce. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (medium, fusion standard).

### 5. Yotam Ottolenghi — Pan-Fried Skirt Steak with Chermoula (North African tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi and Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g skirt steak
- **Chermoula marinade**: 30 g coriander; 20 g flat-leaf parsley; 3 garlic cloves; 5 g ground cumin; 3 g smoked paprika; 30 ml olive oil; 30 ml lemon juice; fine salt
- Flatbread and pickled red cabbage, to serve

**Method**
1. Blend chermoula; coat steak; marinate 2 hours. Wipe off excess.
2. Heat a flat pan over high heat; add thin film of oil.
3. Fry 2–3 minutes per side. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end.
4. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Ottolenghi's standard: medium-rare.
5. Rest 3 minutes. Slice; serve with flatbread and pickled cabbage. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, North African-Israeli tradition).
