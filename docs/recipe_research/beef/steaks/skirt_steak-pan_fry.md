# Skirt Steak × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan frying skirt steak (slightly more oil, slightly lower sustained heat than searing)
is the primary method for fajita strips. The key question specific to this method:
when the cut is cooked as thin strips for fajitas rather than as a whole steak and
probed, does the textural target remain the same?

Note: A MEATER probe is not practical for thin fajita strips — this leaf addresses
pan frying the whole skirt steak (probed) before slicing, not pre-sliced strip frying.

## Source recipes and what they actually say

### 1. Cook's Illustrated / America's Test Kitchen — Fajita Skirt Steak (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/fajita-skirt-steak
- **Temperature cited**: **130°F / 54°C** with specific note on pan frying
- ATK tested multiple methods and temperatures for fajita skirt. Finding: "We cook the
  skirt whole to 130°F / 54°C before slicing, not as strips. Pre-sliced strips get
  uneven temps; whole steak with thermometer produces consistent results."
- ATK is the most methodical US source: 54°C confirmed for pan fry.

### 2. Donna Hay — Skirt Steak Pan-Fried (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/skirt-steak-pan-fry
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare)
- Donna Hay targets the mid-range. "Pull at 55°C and rest — it will reach 57°C."
  Australian culinary tradition is close to UK, slightly higher than French.

### 3. Pilar Akaneya — Arrachera a la sartén (Mexico)
- **Source**: pilarakaneya.mx/arrachera-sarten
- **Temperature cited**: **57-60°C** (término medio)
- Mexican restaurant tradition for pan-fried arrachera: 57-60°C.
  "La arrachera a la sartén debe tener un poco de resistencia al morder."
  ("Pan-fried arrachera should have a little resistance when biting.")
  This is deliberately chewier than the French 52-54°C — a textural choice.

### 4. Julie Andrieu — Jupe de bœuf sautée (France)
- **Source**: julieandrieu.com/recettes/jupe-boeuf-sautee
- **Temperature cited**: **52-54°C** (saignante)
- "Je suis la même règle que pour la bavette — la jupe de bœuf ne supporte pas
  la chaleur prolongée. 52-54°C maximum." 
  ("Same rule as bavette — skirt steak cannot take prolonged heat. 52-54°C maximum.")
- French TV chef reiterating the French rule for this category of cuts.

### 5. Matprat — Kjøl stekt i panne (Norway)
- **Source**: matprat.no/oppskrifter/kjott/stekt-kjol
- **Temperature cited**: **54-57°C** — recommends 54-55°C specifically for the pan fry
  method. "Fjern fra pannen ved 54°C — temperaturen stiger litt under hvilen."
  ("Remove from pan at 54°C — temp rises slightly during rest.")

## Genuine findings

**Pan fry for skirt steak shows the same cross-cultural temperature split as pan sear.**
The method itself (pan fry vs pan sear) does not change the cultural targets.

**The Mexican-French split is the most pronounced for this cut** — Mexican tradition
accepts (and in some cases prefers) 57-60°C for skirt steak, where French tradition
draws a hard line at 54°C maximum. Both positions are argued on textural grounds:
- French: above 54°C = deteriorating texture
- Mexican: "a little resistance" is desirable for tacos and fajitas

This is a genuine cultural disagreement about what constitutes the best result for this cut.

## Verdict
- **US/scientific (ATK)**: 54°C — cook whole with probe, then slice
- **French**: 52-54°C maximum — same rule as bavette
- **Mexican restaurant tradition**: 57-60°C (preferred "resistance" texture for fajitas)
- **Australian**: 55°C pull
- **No pan fry specific override** — same as pan sear
