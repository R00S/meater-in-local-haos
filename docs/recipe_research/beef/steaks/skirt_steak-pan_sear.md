# Skirt Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Skirt steak is cut from the plate section (the diaphragm muscles) of the beef — there are two muscles: the outside skirt (*diaphragm proper*, thicker, more uniform, more flavourful) and the inside skirt (thinner, more variable in thickness). Both are intensely flavoured and very coarsely grained, making them ideal for marinades and quick, very high-heat cooking. Skirt steak is central to Mexican *fajitas*, Brazilian *fraldinha*, and American steakhouse menus. Pan searing skirt steak requires the very highest heat possible: the steak is thin (1–1.5 cm) and must develop a deep brown crust in the 2–3 minutes before the interior exceeds 57 °C. The MEATER probe is challenging in a thin cut — insert from the end, angling towards the thickest part, or monitor the time-based cues in the recipe. Carryover from a screaming-hot cast-iron pan is 3–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Diana Kennedy — Pan-Seared Skirt Steak Tacos (*Tacos de Arrachera*) (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Cuisines of Mexico* (Harper & Row, 1972)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak (*arrachera* — Mexican term for skirt steak), cut into portions
- **Marinade**: 4 garlic cloves, minced; 30 ml (2 tbsp) lime juice; 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil; 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin; 5 g (1 tsp) dried oregano; fine salt and black pepper
- 8–12 corn tortillas (warmed), salsa, sliced radishes, lime wedges, fresh coriander, to serve

**Method**
1. Combine marinade; coat skirt steak; marinate 2 hours.
2. Heat a cast-iron *comal* or heavy pan over the very highest heat achievable.
3. Pat marinade off the steak surface (excess moisture = steaming, not searing).
4. Lay skirt steak in the dry or lightly-oiled screaming-hot pan.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thin end of the steak, angling the probe along the length of the meat toward the centre.
6. Cook 2–3 minutes per side.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Kennedy's Mexican standard: thin skirt should be cooked medium-rare. In taco preparation, the thin cut is often cooked slightly more (57 °C) for traditional *fajita* texture.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin across the grain. Serve in warm tortillas with salsa and coriander. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Mexican tradition).

### 2. J. Kenji López-Alt — Pan-Seared Skirt Steak Fajitas (American-Mexican tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, seriouseats.com/the-best-steak-fajitas; *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g outside skirt steak, trimmed
- **Fajita marinade**: 60 ml (¼ cup) soy sauce; 30 ml (2 tbsp) vegetable oil; 30 ml (2 tbsp) lime juice; 3 garlic cloves, minced; 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin; 5 g (1 tsp) chilli powder; 3 g (½ tsp) smoked paprika
- Flour tortillas, sautéed peppers and onions, sour cream, guacamole, pico de gallo, to serve

**Method**
1. Marinate skirt steak 1 hour; pat dry.
2. Heat a cast-iron pan over absolute maximum heat (5 minutes preheat).
3. Add a very thin film of oil; immediately place skirt steak.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the end of the steak, angling toward the thickest part.
5. Cook 2–3 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Kenji's standard: medium-rare. He notes that skirt steak loses tenderness rapidly if overcooked.
7. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin against the grain (the grain in skirt steak runs perpendicular to the length — slice across the short dimension). Serve in warm flour tortillas. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare to medium, Tex-Mex fajita standard).

### 3. Nobu Matsuhisa — Pan-Seared Skirt Steak with Spicy Ponzu (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Nobu Matsuhisa, *Nobu: The Cookbook* (Kodansha International, 2001)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g outside skirt steak, cut into 15 cm sections
- **Marinade**: 45 ml soy sauce; 15 ml mirin; 15 ml sesame oil; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 5 g ginger, minced
- **Spicy ponzu**: 60 ml yuzu juice (or lemon); 45 ml soy sauce; 10 ml *togarashi* chilli oil; 10 ml mirin

**Method**
1. Marinate skirt steak 1 hour; wipe off excess.
2. Heat a cast-iron pan to maximum; add a thin film of vegetable oil.
3. Place skirt steak; insert the MEATER probe from the end, angling toward the thickest part.
4. Cook 2–3 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Nobu's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin against the grain. Drizzle with spicy ponzu. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Japanese tradition).

### 4. Fergus Henderson — Pan-Seared Skirt Steak, Anchovy and Capers (British tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Complete Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g outside skirt steak
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) vegetable oil or beef dripping
- **Anchovy and caper dressing**: 6 anchovy fillets, finely chopped; 20 g capers, roughly chopped; 30 ml olive oil; 15 ml red wine vinegar; 1 garlic clove, minced; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Mix anchovy and caper dressing; set aside. Season skirt steak.
2. Heat a pan over maximum heat; add oil.
3. Place skirt steak; insert the MEATER probe from the end.
4. Cook 2–3 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Henderson's standard: "must be served pink."
6. Rest 3 minutes. Slice thin; drizzle with anchovy and caper dressing. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (medium-rare, British tradition).

### 5. Marcus Samuelsson — Pan-Seared Skirt Steak with Berbere (Ethiopian-American tradition)
**Source**: Marcus Samuelsson, *The Soul of a New Cuisine* (John Wiley & Sons, 2006)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 4–6 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g outside skirt steak
- **Marinade**: 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 15 g *berbere* spice paste; 3 garlic cloves, minced; 10 ml lemon juice; fine salt
- Injera flatbread or pita, to serve (Ethiopian tradition)

**Method**
1. Marinate skirt steak 1 hour; wipe excess.
2. Heat a cast-iron pan to maximum; add thin film of oil.
3. Place skirt steak; insert the MEATER probe from the end.
4. Cook 2–3 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 54–57 °C (130–135 °F).** Samuelsson's Ethiopian-American standard: medium, allowing the bold berbere spice to caramelise fully against the meat.
6. Rest 3 minutes. Slice; serve with injera. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (medium, Ethiopian-American standard).
