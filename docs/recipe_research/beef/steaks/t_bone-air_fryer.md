# T-Bone Steak × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Air-frying a T-bone steak is practical for steaks up to 3 cm thick and 700 g — the bone makes the T-bone bulkier than simple steak cuts, so it must fit in the air fryer basket. Air fryers circulate extremely hot air at 200–220 °C and produce a crisped surface resembling oven-roasted rather than pan-seared beef, without the mess of oil splatter. The two-muscle T-bone geometry (striploin + fillet) requires monitoring the striploin temperature — the MEATER probe goes in the striploin side, away from the bone. The result is less dramatic in crust quality than a cast-iron sear, but significantly more convenient for weeknight cooking. Carryover from the air fryer basket is 3–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer T-Bone Steak (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Air Fryer Perfection* (America's Test Kitchen, 2019)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone steak, 700 g, 3 cm thick (must fit in air fryer basket — check dimensions first)
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt (applied 1 hour ahead)
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Dry-brine T-bone 1 hour; pat completely dry; apply black pepper.
2. Preheat air fryer to 220 °C (425 °F) for 5 minutes.
3. Place T-bone in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
4. Air fry 7 minutes; flip; air fry 5–7 minutes more.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. ATK's medium-rare air fryer target for T-bone.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, American air fryer standard).

### 2. Jamie Oliver — Air Fryer T-Bone with Garlic Butter (British tradition)
**Source**: Jamie Oliver, *7 Ways* (Michael Joseph / Penguin, 2020), adapted for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) olive oil
- **Garlic butter**: 80 g softened butter; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped; salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix garlic butter; refrigerate. Season T-bone; rub with oil.
2. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
3. Place T-bone in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
4. Air fry 6–7 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Jamie's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Top with garlic butter. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, British standard).

### 3. Gordon Ramsay — Air Fryer T-Bone, Peppercorn Sauce (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012); adapted for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700 g
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 5 ml (1 tsp) vegetable oil
- **Peppercorn sauce**: 15 g unsalted butter; 2 shallots, finely minced; 15 g whole black peppercorns, lightly crushed; 60 ml Cognac; 150 ml double cream; 60 ml beef stock; salt

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 30 minutes; rub with oil; season with pepper. Make peppercorn sauce on stovetop; keep warm.
2. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F).
3. Place T-bone in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
4. Air fry 6–7 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Ramsay's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with peppercorn sauce. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, British standard).

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Air Fryer T-Bone with Tarragon Butter (British contemporary tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *Cook, Eat, Repeat* (Chatto & Windus, 2020)

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700 g
- Sea salt and black pepper

**Method**

2. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
3. Place T-bone in basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
4. Air fry 6–7 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Nigella's standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Top with tarragon butter. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, British contemporary standard).
