# T-Bone Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

A T-bone steak over live charcoal is one of the iconic preparations of American BBQ and Argentine *parrilla* tradition. The charcoal heat is less precisely controllable than gas, which means the two-muscle geometry of the T-bone (the faster-cooking tenderloin fillet and the slower-cooking striploin) requires active management: sear both sides over the hottest coals, then move to the cooler side of the grill with the bone facing the heat. Argentine *parrilleros* (grill masters) rotate the steak on its bone edge for a period, allowing the bone to conduct heat into the meat and providing an even cook without additional flare-ups. The bone-edge sear also produces crackling rendered fat deposits that are some of the most prized bites on the steak. Carryover from very hot charcoal is 5–7 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Francis Mallmann — T-Bone over Charcoal Embers (Argentine *parrilla* tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 14–18 min over embers · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone (*bife con hueso*), 800 g–1 kg, 4 cm thick
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Quebracho hardwood charcoal
- Chimichurri, to serve

**Method**
1. Season generously with coarse salt and pepper.
2. Burn quebracho charcoal to white-ash embers; set up a two-zone fire.
3. Sear T-bone directly over the hottest embers 4–5 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, 2–3 cm from the bone, angling into the centre of the striploin muscle.
5. Stand the T-bone on its bone edge over the coals for 2–3 minutes (Mallmann's technique — the bone conducts heat into the meat evenly and the fat edge renders beautifully).
6. Move to the indirect side; bone facing the coals; close lid; cook 5–7 minutes more.
7. **Pull at 49–51 °C (120–124 °F)** in the striploin. Mallmann's Argentine preference: *jugoso* (juicy red interior). The very high quebracho carryover (6–7 °C) demands early pulling.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine traditional standard).

### 2. Steven Raichlen — T-Bone over Live Charcoal (American BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700–900 g, 3–4 cm thick
- Kosher salt (1 hour in advance)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal; optional wood chunks (oak or cherry)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; apply pepper and oil.
2. Light charcoal chimney; pour when white-hot; add wood chunks if using.
3. Two-zone setup. Sear over direct coals 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
5. Move to indirect; close lid; cook 5–7 minutes.
6. **Pull at 51–53 °C (124–127 °F)** in the striploin. Raichlen's charcoal-carryover standard: slightly earlier than gas grill.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 3. Lennox Hastie — T-Bone over Australian Hardwood (Australian live-fire tradition)
**Source**: Lennox Hastie, *Finding Fire* (Hardie Grant, 2017)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–16 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 Australian T-bone, 800 g–1 kg, 4 cm thick
- Coarse sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Ironbark or river red gum hardwood charcoal

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; season with pepper before grilling.
2. Build a two-zone ironbark fire.
3. Sear T-bone over direct coals 4–5 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
5. Move to indirect; bone facing the coals; close lid; cook 5–6 minutes.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Hastie's target: medium-rare with charcoal carryover allowance.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian live-fire standard).

### 4. Bobby Flay — Charcoal T-Bone with Garlic Herb Crust (American Southwest tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 800 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- **Herb crust**: 30 g coarse breadcrumbs; 15 g fresh parsley, chopped; 1 garlic clove, minced; 15 ml olive oil; salt
- Hardwood charcoal

**Method**
1. Season T-bone. Start charcoal chimney; two-zone fire when white-hot.
2. Sear T-bone over direct coals 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
4. Move to indirect. Press herb crust onto the striploin side. Close lid; cook 4–5 minutes.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F)** in the striploin. Flay's target: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 5. Neil Perry — Charcoal T-Bone, Café de Paris Butter (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 Australian T-bone, 800 g–1 kg
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- **Café de Paris butter**: 100 g softened butter; 5 g Dijon mustard; 2 anchovy fillets, mashed; 1 tsp capers, finely chopped; 5 g flat-leaf parsley; 5 g thyme; 1 garlic clove, minced; 2 g curry powder; pinch of cayenne; lemon juice; salt
- Hardwood charcoal

**Method**
1. Mix Café de Paris butter; refrigerate. Dry-brine T-bone 1 hour.
2. Light charcoal; two-zone fire.
3. Sear over direct coals 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
5. Move to indirect; close lid; cook 5–6 minutes.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Top with Café de Paris butter. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian steakhouse standard).
