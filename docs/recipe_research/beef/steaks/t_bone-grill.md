# T-Bone Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilling a T-bone steak is the quintessential American steakhouse tradition — the bone-in loin cut with both the striploin and the fillet together, cooked over direct high heat on a gas or charcoal grill. The bone and its two muscle geometry create a specific protocol: usually a hot sear on both sides over direct heat, followed by a move to indirect heat (or the coolest part of the grill) with the bone-side facing the heat to protect the tenderloin from overcooking, since the tenderloin cooks faster than the strip. The MEATER probe goes in the striploin (the larger muscle, away from the bone) for the most accurate representative reading. Carryover from a hot grill is 4–6 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steven Raichlen — Grilled T-Bone (American BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone steak, 700–900 g, 3–4 cm thick
- 15 g (1 tbsp) coarse salt (applied 1 hour before)
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; brush with oil; apply pepper.
2. Preheat grill to screaming high. Set up a two-zone fire (direct and indirect).
3. Sear T-bone over direct heat 3–4 minutes per side to establish grill marks.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, 2 cm from the bone, into the centre of the striploin muscle.
5. Move to indirect heat with the bone facing the heat source (the bone protects the tenderloin fillet). Close lid; cook 4–5 minutes.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Raichlen's standard: medium-rare. The tenderloin will be 2–3 °C higher at this point.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 2. Bobby Flay — Grilled T-Bone with Blue Cheese Sauce (American tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 800 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- **Blue cheese sauce**: 100 g Gorgonzola or Maytag blue cheese, crumbled; 60 ml double cream; 5 ml Worcestershire sauce; 5 ml hot sauce; pinch of cayenne

**Method**
1. Season T-bone; brush with oil.
2. Preheat grill to high; two-zone setup.
3. Sear over direct heat 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
5. Move to indirect; close lid; cook 4–5 minutes.
6. Warm blue cheese sauce in a small pan.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F)** in the striploin. Flay's standard: medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with blue cheese sauce. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American tradition).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled T-Bone with Herb Oil (Mediterranean tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi and Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+30 min marinate) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700–800 g
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- **Herb oil**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 15 g fresh oregano; 2 garlic cloves; 60 ml extra virgin olive oil; 15 ml lemon juice; salt

**Method**
1. Blend herb oil; set aside. Season and oil the T-bone.
2. Preheat grill to high; two-zone setup.
3. Sear over direct heat 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
5. Move to indirect; close lid; cook 4–5 minutes.
6. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F)** in the striploin. Ottolenghi's standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Drizzle with herb oil. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Mediterranean tradition).

### 4. Neil Perry — Grilled T-Bone, Australian Steakhouse Style (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 Australian T-bone, 800–1000 g, 3–4 cm thick
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- Béarnaise sauce or green peppercorn sauce, to serve

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; oil; season with pepper. Preheat grill to high; two-zone.
2. Sear over direct heat 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
4. Move to indirect; close lid; 4–5 minutes.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with béarnaise or green peppercorn sauce. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian steakhouse standard).

### 5. Francis Mallmann — T-Bone on the Parilla (Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–16 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone (*bife con hueso*), 800 g–1 kg
- Coarse sea salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal or wood
- Chimichurri

**Method**
1. Season with coarse salt and pepper. Build a two-zone charcoal fire.
2. Grill over direct heat 4–5 minutes per side.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side.
4. Move to indirect side, bone facing the coals. Cook 5–6 minutes more.
5. **Pull at 50 °C (122 °F)** in the striploin. Mallmann's preference: *jugoso*.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine standard).
