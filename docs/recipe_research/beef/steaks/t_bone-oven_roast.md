## Cut profile
Reverse sear T-bone: low oven (107-135°C) brings BOTH muscles to near-target uniformly,
then a 1-2 min/side sear. The bone acts as a heat bridge — the tenderloin side normally
cooks faster but the slow oven equalises them. Pull from oven with probe in tenderloin.

## Source recipes

### 1. MyChicagoSteak / Steak University — How to Reverse Sear T-Bone Steak
- **Source**: mychicagosteak.com/blogs/steak-university/reverse-sear-t-bone-steak
- **Oven**: 225-250°F / 107-121°C. Wire rack. Probe in tenderloin.
- **Pull from oven (medium-rare)**: 49-52°C / 120-125°F
- **Final after sear + rest**: **54-57°C / 130-135°F**

### 2. CookTimePro — Reverse Searing Beef T-Bone (Germany-friendly guide)
- **Source**: cooktimepro.com/red-meat/beef/beef-t-bone/reverse-searing
- **Oven**: 120-135°C / 250-275°F. Wire rack. 30-50 min depending on thickness.
- **Pull at**: 52-54°C for medium-rare result.
- **Final**: **54-57°C** after 1-2 min/side sear in cast iron.

### 3. Cast Iron Skillet Cooking — T-Bone Reverse Sear Recipe
- **Source**: castironskilletcooking.com/t-bone-reverse-sear
- **Oven**: 225°F / 107°C. Probe in tenderloin (more delicate side).
- **Pull at**: 120-125°F / 49-52°C
- **Final**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C**

### 4. HowTo.Cooking — Foolproof Reverse Sear Technique (UK style)
- **Source**: howto.cooking/recipes/perfect-oven-steak-reverse-sear
- **Oven**: 225°F / 107°C. "T-bone benefits enormously from reverse sear because
  the two muscles equalize in temperature in the low oven."
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** final medium-rare

### 5. Whisked Away Kitchen — Reverse Sear T-Bone Steak
- **Source**: whiskedawaykitchen.com/reverse-sear-steak
- **Oven**: 250°F / 121°C. Wire rack. Probe in tenderloin.
- **Pull at**: 49-52°C, sear 1-2 min/side, rest to **54-57°C**.
