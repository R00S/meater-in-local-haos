# T-Bone Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The T-bone steak is defined by the T-shaped vertebral bone that separates two distinct muscles: the larger striploin (*longissimus dorsi*) on the long side of the bone, and the smaller tenderloin (*psoas major*) fillet on the shorter side. This presents a unique cooking challenge: the two muscles have different ideal internal temperatures (the tenderloin cooks faster and is best at 52–54 °C, the strip benefits from 55–57 °C), and the bone itself acts as an insulator — the meat nearest the bone always lags behind by 3–5 °C. Pan searing a T-bone requires a pan large enough to accommodate the steak flat, very high heat, and careful probe placement. The MEATER probe should be placed in the striploin side (the larger portion), away from the bone, for the most representative reading. Carryover is 4–5 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Pan-Seared T-Bone (American steakhouse tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, seriouseats.com/perfect-pan-seared-t-bone; *The Food Lab: Better Home Cooking Through Science* (W. W. Norton & Company, 2015)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone steak, 700–800 g, 3 cm thick
- Kosher salt (applied 1 hour before cooking)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 40 g unsalted butter
- 2 garlic cloves, smashed
- 2 sprigs thyme

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 1 hour; pat bone-dry; apply pepper.
2. Heat a large cast-iron pan (large enough for the T-bone to lie flat) over maximum heat for 5 minutes. Add oil.
3. Place T-bone; it is important the entire surface contacts the pan. Press lightly with a spatula.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, between the bone and the edge of the steak, angling toward the centre of the striploin muscle. Avoid the bone.
5. Sear 3–4 minutes per side. Add butter, garlic and thyme; baste the striploin for 2 minutes.
6. Stand the steak on its fat-cap edge for 1 minute.
7. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Note: the tenderloin reads 2–3 °C higher than the striploin in Kenji's testing — when the strip is medium-rare, the tenderloin is already at medium.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** in the striploin (medium-rare); tenderloin side will be slightly higher. American steakhouse standard.

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Pan-Seared T-Bone with Brown Butter (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone steak, 700 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 50 g unsalted butter
- 3 garlic cloves, skin-on
- 4 sprigs thyme
- Splash of Cognac (optional)

**Method**
1. Season T-bone generously. Heat a large cast-iron pan over maximum heat; add oil.
2. Place T-bone flat; sear 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
4. Add butter, garlic and thyme; the butter will foam and brown (*noisette*). Baste continuously over medium heat for 2 minutes.
5. Optionally, briefly flambé with Cognac for the browning effect.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (126–130 °F)** in the striploin. Ramsay's restaurant standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, British restaurant standard).

### 3. Julia Child — *Côte de Bœuf* au Beurre (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Volume 1* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961) — addressing bone-in loin steaks including the equivalent of the T-bone (*côte de bœuf*)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–12 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone (*côte de bœuf*), 700 g, 3 cm thick
- Fine salt and white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) clarified butter (*beurre clarifié*)
- 60 g cold unsalted butter, cubed (for sauce)
- 30 ml dry white wine or dry Vermouth
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Season steak. Heat clarified butter in a large, heavy pan over high heat.
2. Sear T-bone 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
4. Remove to rest. Drain fat from pan; deglaze with white wine; reduce; swirl in cold butter to create a simple *beurre blanc*-style sauce; add parsley.
5. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Julia's French standard: *saignant* to *à point* for bone-in beef.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with sauce. Final serving temperature: **53–57 °C / 127–135 °F** (*saignant* to medium-rare, French bistro standard).

### 4. Fergus Henderson — Pan-Seared T-Bone with Watercress (British tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Complete Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 T-bone, 700–800 g
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) beef dripping or vegetable oil
- Watercress, to serve
- English mustard, to serve

**Method**
1. Season T-bone generously. Heat a large heavy pan with dripping over maximum heat.
2. Place T-bone; sear 3–4 minutes per side.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone, into the centre.
4. **Pull at 48–52 °C (118–126 °F)** in the striploin. Henderson's standard: "must be rare, gushing pink and pink."
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with peppery watercress and sharp English mustard. Final serving temperature: **52–56 °C / 126–133 °F** (rare to medium-rare, British nose-to-tail standard).

### 5. Neil Perry — Pan-Seared T-Bone (Australian steakhouse tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 Australian T-bone, 700–900 g, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- 40 g unsalted butter

**Method**
1. Dry-brine 30 minutes; pat dry; apply pepper.
2. Heat a large cast-iron pan over maximum heat; add oil.
3. Place T-bone; sear 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the striploin side, away from the bone.
5. Add butter; baste 2 minutes.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F)** in the striploin. Perry's Australian steakhouse standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian premium standard).
