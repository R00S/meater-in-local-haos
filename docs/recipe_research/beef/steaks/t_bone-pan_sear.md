# T-Bone / Porterhouse × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
T-bone/porterhouse has TWO muscles separated by the bone:
1. **Strip side** (NY strip) — larger, denser, takes heat more slowly
2. **Tenderloin side** (filet mignon) — smaller, rounder, cooks FASTER

The MEATER probe should go into the **tenderloin side** (the more delicate muscle).
When the tenderloin reads 54°C, the strip side will typically read 52-54°C — both
are in the medium-rare range. Pan sear technique: position the tenderloin away from
the hottest part of the pan if possible, and flip frequently.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steak Advisor — Pan Seared T-Bone Steak (With Butter, Garlic, and Herbs)
- **Source**: steakadvisor.com/steak-recipes/pan-seared-t-bone-steak
- **Method**: Heavy cast iron, high heat, oil first. Sear 1-2 min per side, flip
  frequently. Add butter+garlic+rosemary. Baste. Optional oven finish for thick cuts.
- **Probe placement**: "In the tenderloin side — it cooks faster and is more delicate."
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** (tenderloin reading). Rest 5-10 min.

### 2. Food Network Kitchen — Pan Seared T-Bone Steak Recipe
- **Source**: foodnetwork.com/recipes/food-network-kitchen/pan-seared-t-bone-steak-recipe-2103356
- **Method**: Cast iron, high heat. Sear 1-2 min/side, frequently. Butter baste.
  Oven finish at 204-213°C for 3-7 min if thick.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** internal (tenderloin side)

### 3. Barbecue FAQ — Perfect Pan Seared T-Bone Steak Recipe
- **Source**: barbecuefaq.com/pan-seared-t-bone-steak-recipe
- **Method**: Dry brine 2-24 hrs. Cast iron very hot. Frequent flips for even crust.
  "Also check strip side after resting to account for any temperature difference."
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare (probe in tenderloin)

### 4. MyChicagoSteak / Steak University — How to Pan Sear T-Bone Steak
- **Source**: mychicagosteak.com/blogs/steak-university/pan-sear-t-bone-steak
- **Method**: Cast iron, oil, sear, butter baste. Probe in tenderloin.
- **Pull at**: 52-54°C from heat; **rest to 54-57°C** (medium-rare)
- "The tenderloin cooks faster — monitoring it ensures the entire steak stays juicy."

### 5. Kitchen Swagger — T-Bone Steak with Garlic and Rosemary
- **Source**: kitchenswagger.com/t-bone-steak-garlic-and-rosemary-recipe
- **Method**: Cast iron, high heat. Garlic+rosemary+butter baste. Optional oven finish.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare
- "For thick T-bones: sear then oven-finish at 400°F / 204°C. Check tenderloin temp."
