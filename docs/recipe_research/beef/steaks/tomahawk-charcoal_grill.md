# Tomahawk Steak × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
A tomahawk over live charcoal is the most theatrical and the most demanding of the
cut's cooking methods. The long rib bone extends far beyond any standard grill, the
fat cap drips onto the coals and the resulting flares and smoke become part of the
cooking; the crust formed over live charcoal has a complexity — slightly acrid, faintly
smoky, deeply caramelised — that no gas grill can replicate. Cooking a tomahawk over
charcoal is not just technique: it is a statement of intent, a performance for the
table, and a test of the cook's patience at the highest level.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Charcoal-Grilled Tomahawk Steak (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/charcoal-grilled-tomahawk-steak
- **Method**: Two-zone charcoal — full chimney on one side, empty other side.
  Sear over direct coals 3 min per side. Move to indirect, bone facing the coals,
  lid on. Cook 25–35 min until probe reads 49–52°C. Rest 15 min off grill.
- **Pull temp**: 120–125°F / **49–52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Charcoal radiates more heat than gas, so Kenji recommends
  pulling 3–4°C lower than the gas-grill target; the bone-facing-coals position
  allows the long rib bone to conduct heat into the cut from the side opposite the
  muscle mass, helping warm the interior more evenly.

### 2. Heston Blumenthal — Tomahawk over Live Charcoal (UK / The Fat Duck)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (live fire tomahawk technique)
- **Method**: Oak or beech lump charcoal; two-zone. Salt 48 h before. Sear over direct
  coals 2–3 min per side. Finish indirect at 140–150°C grill ambient; probe to 54°C.
  Rest 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C** (Heston targets serving temp, not pull temp)
- **Final at rest**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Heston's approach controls the indirect-phase temperature
  tightly; by targeting 54°C (not pulling lower) he relies on the moderate carryover
  of the indirect zone rather than the violent carryover of the direct sear.

### 3. Guga Foods — Tomahawk over Charcoal Taste Test (USA / YouTube)
- **Source**: youtube.com/GugaFoods (tomahawk charcoal episode)
- **Method**: Kingsford charcoal plus wood chunks for smoke; two-zone. Direct sear
  2 min per side; indirect at 250°F / 120°C until 52°C. Rest 15 min on board.
  "Every bite is butter. Charcoal is the only way to cook a tomahawk."
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The wood-chunk addition over charcoal creates a mild smoke
  that complements the tomahawk's extreme marbling; the low indirect temperature
  (120°C) slows the approach so the cook has maximum control over a cut worth
  protecting.

### 4. Weber Grillakademie — Tomahawk auf Holzkohle (Germany)
- **Source**: weber.com/DE/de/rezepte/rindfleisch/tomahawk-holzkohle
- **Method**: Holzkohle-Kugelgrill; zwei Zonen. Direktes Anbraten 3 min pro Seite.
  Indirektes Garen bei 180°C bis 54°C Kerntemperatur. Ruhezeit 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: The Weber kettle dome retains smoke and ambient heat
  during the indirect phase, reducing overall cooking time vs. an open-pit charcoal
  setup; the sealed environment makes temperature management more predictable.

### 5. Grillade.se — Tomahawk på kol (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/tomahawk-pa-kol
- **Method**: Full kolgrill med ekbriketter; tvåzonsuppläggning. Direkt grillning 3 min
  per sida. Indirekt till 54°C kärntemperatur med lock på. Vila 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish premium BBQ events increasingly feature the
  tomahawk as the centrepiece cut; charcoal grilling is the theatrical method
  of choice and oak briquettes are recommended for the cleanest smoke character
  that suits the ribeye muscle's rich marbling.
