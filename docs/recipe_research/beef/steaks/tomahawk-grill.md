# Tomahawk Steak × Grill (gas) — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Gas grill tomahawk requires two-zone setup — the thickness (5-7cm) makes direct-only
cooking impossible. Start indirect at 150°C until 45-50°C internal, then blast on
direct high heat for crust. The bone acts as a handle and a heat conductor.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Tomahawk Steak (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/us/en/recipes/beef/tomahawk-steak-gas-grill
- **Method**: Two-zone gas grill. Indirect 150°C until internal 45°C. Then direct
  500°C+ heat 2-3 min/side. Rest 10 min.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 2. Bon Appétit — Gas-Grilled Tomahawk for Two (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/grilled-tomahawk-two
- **Method**: Indirect gas grill 150°C, 40-50 min until 125°F. Blast direct heat
  2 min/side. Rest 10 min.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 3. ICA Recept — Grillad tomahawk (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/grillad-tomahawk
- **Method**: Gasgrill tvåzon. Indirekt 150°C till 45°C kärna. Sedan direkt hög
  värme 2-3 min per sida. Vila 10 min.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / medium blodig)

### 4. Man Fire Food — Tomahawk on Gas Grill (USA)
- **Source**: manfirefood.com/tomahawk-gas-grill
- **Method**: Reverse sear on gas: 150°C indirect to 45°C internal. Then sear 2 min/side.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** medium-rare

### 5. Fleischdepot — Tomahawk-Steak auf dem Gasgrill (Germany)
- **Source**: fleischdepot.de/tomahawk-gasgrill
- **Method**: Gasgrill 2-Zonen. Indirekt 150°C bis 45°C Kern. Direkt scharf 2-3 Min.
  Ruhe 10 Min. Kerntemperatur 54-57°C.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare)

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Target | Method |
|--------|---------|--------|--------|
| Weber Grills | USA | 54-57°C | 2-zone indirect then direct |
| Bon Appétit | USA | 54-57°C | Indirect then blast |
| ICA Recept | SE | 54-57°C | 2-zone |

**Consensus: 54-57°C medium-rare.** Two-zone/reverse sear is MANDATORY for tomahawk.
Start indirect at 150°C to 45°C core, then direct blast for crust.
