## Cut profile
A tomahawk on a gas grill requires a two-zone setup that would be considered
optional for any other steak — here it is mandatory. The 5–7 cm thickness means
direct heat alone will char the surface long before the centre warms through.
The rib bone adds a visual drama to the grill that turns the cooking itself into
the experience: the bone extends beyond any grill grate, and guests watching the
cook at the grill understand immediately that something exceptional is happening.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Gas-Grilled Tomahawk Steak (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/beef/gas-grilled-tomahawk
- **Method**: Preheat all burners high. Sear over direct heat 3–4 min per side.
  Turn off half the burners; move to indirect side. Close lid; grill at 180–200°C
  ambient until probe reads 52°C (20–30 min). Rest 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: The two-zone gas grill is the most controllable way to
  cook a tomahawk outdoors; the initial sear over all burners creates the Maillard
  crust while the indirect finish brings the thick centre to temperature gently.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Tomahawk Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-tomahawk-steak
- **Method**: Dry brine 24 h. Two-zone gas grill. Sear 2 min per side direct; move
  to indirect at 150°C; cook 25–35 min until probe reads 52°C. Rest 15 min.
  Stand bone in the rest to drain any juices from the bone cavity.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The lower indirect temperature (150°C) for the finishing
  phase slows the approach to target, giving a wide margin for error; the bone-
  standing rest position is a practical tip specific to the tomahawk's anatomy.

### 3. Gordon Ramsay — Gas-Grilled Tomahawk (UK)
- **Source**: gordonramsay.com/recipes/grilled-tomahawk
- **Method**: Season with coarse sea salt and cracked pepper. Two-zone grill; direct
  sear 3 min per side. Indirect 180°C with lid closed until 52°C at thickest.
  Rest 15 min off grill with bone pointing up.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130–134°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Ramsay's outdoor approach mirrors his indoor sear-then-oven
  technique; the bone-pointing-up rest position is recommended for all bone-in steaks
  to prevent the marrow fat from dripping and creating a mess on the resting surface.

### 4. Grillade.se — Tomahawk på gasgrill (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/tomahawk-gasgrill
- **Method**: Gasgrill fullt uppvärmd 15 min. Direkt stekyta 3 min per sida. Indirekt
  180°C tills 54°C kärntemperatur (25–30 min). Vila 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish BBQ guides have elevated the tomahawk to a
  celebration-occasion cut; the gas grill is recommended for home use over charcoal
  because the temperature control is more predictable for a cut this thick.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Tomahawk på gass (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/tomahawk-gass
- **Method**: Gasskomplett maksimal varme 15 min. Direkte grilling 3 min per side.
  Indirekte 180°C med lokk; termometer til 54°C. Hvil 15 min, ben opp.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian food writers have published extensively on the
  tomahawk as a status cut for outdoor entertaining; gas grill control suits the
  Nordic climate where outdoor cooking is done in a narrower seasonal window.
