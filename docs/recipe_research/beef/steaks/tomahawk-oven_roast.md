## Cut profile
Oven roasting a tomahawk is the most controllable and the most forgiving of its five
methods — and for a cut this expensive, forgiving matters. The ambient heat of a hot
oven surrounds the entire steak without hot spots, without flare-ups, without the
narrow searing windows of a cast-iron pan. The reverse-sear technique — low oven
first, then a high-temperature sear at the end — was invented for exactly this type
of thick, expensive cut, and the tomahawk is where it performs best.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Reverse-Sear Tomahawk in the Oven (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/reverse-sear-tomahawk-oven
- **Method**: Season and refrigerate uncovered 24 h (dry brine). Oven at 120°C / 250°F,
  wire rack on baking sheet; roast until probe reads 43–46°C (45–75 min). Rest 10 min.
  Sear in screaming-hot cast iron 60–90 s per side including edges. Rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp (oven phase)**: 110–115°F / **43–46°C**
- **Final after sear + rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The tomahawk's extreme thickness makes it the ideal reverse-
  sear candidate; no other cooking method achieves the same zero-grey-band, edge-to-edge
  medium-rare with a deep crust that the combination of low oven and brief cast-iron
  sear produces.

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Oven-Roasted Tomahawk (UK)
- **Source**: gordonramsay.com/recipes/oven-roasted-tomahawk
- **Method**: Season 24 h. Room temp 1 h. Sear all faces in cast iron; transfer to
  rack in roasting tray; oven 200°C / 400°F for 15–20 min until 52°C. Rest 15 min,
  bone standing upright if possible.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130–134°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Ramsay's forward-sear approach (sear first, oven second)
  for the tomahawk creates a thicker, darker crust than the reverse sear, at the
  cost of a slightly wider gradient; for a showpiece cut the deep char crust is
  part of the visual presentation.

### 3. Heston Blumenthal — Low-Temperature Oven Tomahawk (UK)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (precision steak guide)
- **Method**: 55°C oven ambient for 40–50 min; the whole steak reaches 54°C uniformly
  (low oven acts as sous vide without immersion). Rest 10 min. Sear 60 s per side.
  No further rest needed — the steak is already at serving temperature.
- **Pull temp (55°C low oven)**: **52–54°C** (at equilibrium with oven)
- **Final after sear**: **55–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: A 55°C oven is essentially a dry sous vide — the steak
  reaches equilibrium with the oven temperature and cannot overshoot. The brief
  sear at the end adds only crust, not internal heat. This is the most precise and
  forgiveness-maximising indoor method for the tomahawk.

### 4. ICA — Tomahawk i ugn (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/tomahawk-ugn
- **Method**: Ugn 160°C; lägg tomahawken på galler i långpanna. Stek tills
  kärntemperatur 54°C (ca 30–40 min). Vila 15 min med ben uppåt.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish supermarket recipe guides recommend the low-oven
  approach for the tomahawk specifically because the higher price point of the cut
  justifies the extra time spent achieving a perfect result rather than risking
  overshoot on a high-heat sear-only approach.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Oven-Roasted Tomahawk Steak (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/oven-roasted-tomahawk
- **Method**: Season and bring to room temp 30 min. Oven 200°C / 180°C fan; place on
  rack in roasting tin; cook 20–25 min until 52°C. Rest 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: UK recipe guides recommend the no-sear oven method as
  the simplest approach for home cooks who may not have a cast-iron pan large
  enough for a tomahawk; the high fan-oven temperature provides enough browning
  without a preliminary sear.
