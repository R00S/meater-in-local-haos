# Tomahawk Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The tomahawk is a bone-in ribeye with the entire rib bone left intact — typically
40–60 cm — making it one of the most dramatic presentations in all of beef cookery.
The cut shares the ribeye's exceptional intramuscular fat but is typically 5–7 cm thick,
which means indoor pan searing alone is not sufficient: the exterior would carbonise
before the interior reaches temperature. The canonical method is reverse sear or
sear-then-oven, exploiting the cast iron's crust-forming power and the oven's gentle
ambient heat in sequence. This is a centrepiece cut for a table of people who
understand that what they are about to eat is the pinnacle of the animal's flavour.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Reverse-Sear Tomahawk Steak (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/reverse-sear-tomahawk-steak
- **Method**: Oven at 120°C / 250°F on a wire rack, bone pointing up. Roast until probe
  at centre (clear of bone) reads 43–46°C (45–60 min). Rest 10 min.
  Sear in screaming-hot cast iron (or over coals) 60–90 s per side including edges.
  Final rest 5 min.
- **Pull temp (oven phase)**: **43–46°C** (aim: 5°C below target)
- **Final after sear + rest (5 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The reverse sear is the definitive tomahawk method
  because the cut's extreme thickness means any forward-sear method creates a thick
  grey band. Low oven heat brings the marbled interior to a uniform rare before
  the brief sear drives the temperature the final 8–10°C. The long rib bone
  conducts no significant heat into the muscle, so temperature at the bone tip
  is measured separately.

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Tomahawk Steak with Bone Butter Baste (UK)
- **Source**: gordonramsay.com/recipes/tomahawk-steak
- **Method**: Season 24 h before. Room temp 1 h. Sear in cast iron (no oil — render
  fat cap first by holding cut on its side 2 min) then sear flat faces 3 min each.
  Transfer to 200°C oven 15–20 min until 52°C. Rest 10–15 min loosely foiled.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Ramsay's side-sear to render the fat cap before searing
  the flat faces is unique to this cut — it prevents the fat from creating flare-ups
  during the flat sear and ensures the rendered fat bastes the muscle as it cooks.

### 3. Heston Blumenthal — Perfect Tomahawk: Precision Cooking (UK / The Fat Duck)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (tomahawk technique notes)
- **Method**: Salt heavily 48 h before; bring to 5°C above fridge temp (not full room
  temp — Heston's counter-intuitive position). Oven at 55°C / 131°F for 45 min
  (low-temp oven precision). Sear 90 s per side in the hottest possible pan.
- **Pull temp (low oven)**: **52–55°C** (bath-style low oven)
- **Final after sear**: **55–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Heston's low-oven approach (55°C oven ambient) achieves
  effectively the same result as sous vide without immersion; for a tomahawk the method
  avoids the bag-size problem while delivering the same edge-to-edge precision.

### 4. ICA — Tomahawk i panna och ugn (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/tomahawk-steak
- **Method**: Bryn alla sidor av tomahawken i het järnpanna, 2 min per sida inklusive
  sidorna och benänden. Klart i ugn 175°C tills 54°C kärntemperatur. Vila 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish home cooks approach the tomahawk as a celebration
  cut; the sear-then-oven method is the mainstream domestic technique and the 175°C
  oven is more forgiving than the higher temperatures that can overshoot the target.

### 5. Sale&Pepe — Tomahawk in padella con finitura al forno (Italy)
- **Source**: salepepe.it/ricette/tomahawk-padella-forno
- **Method**: Portare a temperatura ambiente 1 ora. Rosolare in padella di ghisa
  tutte le facce 2 min ciascuna. Terminare in forno a 180°C fino a 54°C al cuore
  (ca 20–25 min). Riposo 10–15 min. Non bucare mai la carne — controllare la
  temperatura solo attraverso la sonda.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Italian tradition of never piercing the meat during
  cooking is especially important for the tomahawk because the large exposed bone
  conducts heat in unpredictable ways; the probe inserted through the flat face
  (clear of bone) gives the most reliable reading.
