## Cut profile
Santa Maria-style tri-tip over oak is one of the defining barbecue dishes of the
American West Coast — a cut, a wood, and a technique that belong together.
Over live charcoal the fat on the surface of the tri-tip melts into the coals,
creating flavoured smoke that rises back over the meat in a self-basting cycle.
The triangular shape that can be awkward for precise temperature management becomes
an advantage: natural variation in thickness produces a range of doneness in a single
cut, so the table never needs to agree.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Santa Maria-Style Charcoal-Grilled Tri-Tip (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/santa-maria-tri-tip-charcoal
- **Method**: Red oak or almond wood charcoal; two-zone fire. Sear over direct coals
  5 min per side; move to indirect and cook 20–30 min until probe at thickest reads 52°C.
  Rest 10–15 min.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C** (thickest point)
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Red oak smoke is the soul of Santa Maria BBQ; two-zone
  charcoal allows the cook to manage a thick roast without burning the exterior;
  pulling at 52°C gives a long rest that distributes the juices through the whole cut.

### 2. Adam Perry Lang — Charcoal-Smoked Tri-Tip (USA / Serious Barbecue)
- **Source**: Adam Perry Lang, "Serious Barbecue" (Hyperion, 2009) — tri-tip chapter
- **Method**: Full chimney lump charcoal; set up for indirect with drip pan.
  No direct sear; slow-smoke indirect at 150–160°C grill-ambient until 52°C probe.
  30–40 min total. Finish with 60-second blitz over direct heat. Rest 10 min.
- **Pull temp (indirect phase)**: **49–52°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Low-and-slow indirect charcoal cooking gives the entire
  tri-tip a uniform interior with no grey band; the final high-heat blitz creates
  a crust that could not form during the indirect phase.

### 3. Sunset Magazine — Santa Maria Tri-Tip Over Oak Charcoal (USA / California)
- **Source**: sunset.com/food-wine/santa-maria-tri-tip
- **Method**: Oak charcoal in a California open-pit grill (no lid). Flip every
  5 min, moving around the grill to manage flare-ups. Pull at 57°C at thickest.
  Rest 10 min. Slice thin, cutting across grain in two directions.
- **Pull temp**: 135°F / **57°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **59–62°C**
- **Why this method works**: The traditional California open-pit method without a
  lid requires a higher pull temperature because carryover is lower without the
  retained heat of a domed grill; the frequent-flip technique manages the irregular
  heat distribution of a wood-fire.

### 4. Grillade.se — Tri-tip på kol (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/tri-tip-pa-kol
- **Method**: Kullgrill med lövträ (ek eller lönn); tvåzonsbränning. Direkt grillning
  4 min per sida; indirekt till 54°C kärntemperatur. Vila 10–15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grillers have discovered tri-tip through American
  BBQ culture; oak charcoal is now widely available in Sweden and the two-zone
  technique is the recommended approach in all major Swedish BBQ guides.

### 5. Weber Grillakademie — Tri-Tip auf Holzkohle (Germany)
- **Source**: weber.com/DE/de/rezepte/rindfleisch/tri-tip-holzkohle
- **Method**: Holzkohle-Kugelgrill; zwei Zonen. Direktes Anbraten 4 min pro Seite;
  indirektes Garen 160°C bis 54°C Kerntemperatur. Ruhezeit 10–15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: **57°C**
- **Why this method works**: Weber Germany's kettle-grill approach applies German
  precision to an American cut; the sealed dome retains smoke and ambient heat
  for the indirect phase, reducing the cooking time compared to an open pit.
