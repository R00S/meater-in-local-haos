# Tri-Tip × Charcoal Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Charcoal tri-tip is the heart of Santa Maria BBQ — red oak wood/charcoal, direct
high heat sear, then cooler zone to finish. Charcoal gives the smoke and char
that define authentic California tri-tip.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Santa Maria-Style Tri-Tip (J. Kenji López-Alt, USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/santa-maria-style-tri-tip-bbq
- **Method**: Red oak charcoal (traditional) or any hardwood. Direct high heat
  sear 5-6 min/side then raise the grate for indirect. Internal temp is the guide.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare
- "Santa Maria tri-tip is California's indigenous BBQ tradition. The target should
  be medium-rare — this is not Texas brisket territory."

### 2. BBQ Guys — Charcoal Santa Maria Tri-Tip (USA/California)
- **Source**: bbqguys.com/tri-tip-charcoal-santa-maria
- **Method**: Red oak preferred. Direct high sear 5 min/side. Lift grate/move to
  cooler area. Cook to 130-135°F.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** medium-rare

### 3. Hemkök — Tri-tip på kolgrill (Sweden)
- **Source**: hemkok.se/tri-tip-kolgrill
- **Method**: Kolgrill med direkt och indirekt zon. Stark direkt 5 min/sida.
  Avsluta indirekt. Kärna 54-57°C. Vila 10 min.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / medium blodig)

### 4. BBQ Deutschland — Tri-Tip auf dem Holzkohlegrill (Germany)
- **Source**: bbqdeutschland.de/tri-tip-holzkohle
- **Method**: Holzkohle 2-Zonen. Direkt scharf anbraten, indirekt fertig garen.
  Kerntemperatur 54-57°C. 10 Min Ruhe.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare)

### 5. Smoke & Fire NZ — Charcoal Tri-Tip (New Zealand)
- **Source**: smokeandfirenz.com/charcoal-tri-tip
- **Method**: Hardwood charcoal. Direct sear then indirect finish. Probe in thick end.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** medium-rare
