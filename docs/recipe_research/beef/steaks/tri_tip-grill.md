# Tri-Tip × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Gas grilling is the everyday California method for tri-tip when a full Santa Maria
wood-fire setup is not available. The cut's triangular cross-section requires a
two-zone setup: sear over high heat to build the crust, then move to the indirect zone
to finish the thick centre without burning the now-caramelised surface. The result —
medium-rare at the thickest point, slightly more done at the thinner tip — is not a
flaw but a built-in feature that lets the cook serve every preference from a single cut.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — California Tri-Tip on Gas Grill (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/beef/california-tri-tip-gas-grill
- **Method**: Preheat gas grill high. Sear tri-tip on all sides directly over heat,
  3–4 min per side. Move to indirect medium heat; cook with lid closed until probe
  reads 52–54°C at thickest point. Rest 10 min off grill.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C** (thickest point)
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56–58°C**
- **Why this method works**: The two-zone gas-grill method replicates the Santa Maria
  live-fire technique in a controllable way; the indirect phase gently brings the
  thick centre to temperature while the crust set by the initial sear holds.

### 2. Serious Eats — Gas-Grilled Tri-Tip Roast (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/gas-grilled-tri-tip-roast
- **Method**: Dry brine 12 h uncovered in fridge. Sear over direct high heat 2 min
  per side; move to indirect medium; cook to 52°C. Rest 15 min with foil tent.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: Dry brining creates the pellicle surface that browns
  dramatically over high heat; the 15-min rest for a tri-tip is significantly
  longer than a thin steak because the thick centre continues cooking via carryover.

### 3. Santa Maria BBQ Association — Traditional Gas-Grilled Tri-Tip (USA / California)
- **Source**: santamariavalley.com/tri-tip-bbq-guide
- **Method**: Gas grill preheated high (260°C). Season with Santa Maria rub (garlic,
  black pepper, salt). Direct sear 4–5 min per side; move to indirect 180°C and cook
  to 57°C. Rest 10 min. Slice thin across grain — both directions for the triangular cut.
- **Pull temp**: 135°F / **57°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **59–62°C**
- **Why this method works**: The Santa Maria tradition targets a slightly higher done-
  ness than Kenji's approach because the regional audience prefers medium-rare with
  warm pink throughout rather than rare at the centre; slicing across the grain in
  two directions is essential because the tri-tip's grain runs in two planes.

### 4. Grillade.se — Tri-tip på gasgrill (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/tri-tip-gasgrill
- **Method**: Gasgrill till max temperatur; direkt grillning 3 min per sida.
  Indirekt värme 180°C tills 54°C kärntemperatur. Vila 10 min. Skär tunnt.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grill guides have adopted tri-tip as a premium
  affordable roast; gas grill with two zones is the dominant tool and gives the
  precise heat control the cut requires.

### 5. Agrodolce — Tri-tip alla griglia (Italy)
- **Source**: agrodolce.it/tri-tip-griglia
- **Method**: Griglia a gas preriscaldata al massimo; 3 min lato diretto,
  poi fuoco indiretto 180°C fino a 54°C. Riposo 10 min sotto alluminio.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Italian BBQ culture has embraced American cuts including
  tri-tip; the recipe treats it as a large bistecca, using the two-zone grill
  technique with the same discipline applied to T-bone and costata.
