## Cut profile
Roasting a whole tri-tip in the oven is the workhorse weeknight version of the
California tradition — no fire, no drama, the same exceptional result. The ambient
convection heat of the oven wraps the entire cut without hot spots, and the low-roast
approach gives the cook a gentle approach-rate to the target temperature with a
forgiving margin. Tri-tip in the oven is the cut that makes Sunday-night entertaining
look effortless: season it, put it in, pull it at the beep.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Slow-Roasted Tri-Tip in the Oven (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/oven-roasted-tri-tip
- **Method**: Dry brine overnight uncovered in fridge. Oven at 120°C / 250°F on wire
  rack; roast until probe reads 49–52°C (1–1.5 h). Sear 60 seconds per side in
  cast iron. Rest 10 min.
- **Pull temp (low-roast phase)**: 120–125°F / **49–52°C**
- **Final after sear + rest (10 min)**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: The reverse-sear oven approach gives a tri-tip zero grey
  band and a perfectly even interior; the low temperature phase (120°C) allows the
  whole cut to approach temperature uniformly before the final sear.

### 2. Food Network — Classic Oven-Roasted Tri-Tip (USA)
- **Source**: foodnetwork.com/recipes/oven-roasted-tri-tip
- **Method**: Preheat oven to 190°C / 375°F. Sear tri-tip in oven-safe skillet 2–3 min
  per side; transfer skillet directly to oven; roast 20–25 min until 54°C.
  Rest 10 min in skillet.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56–58°C**
- **Why this method works**: The skillet-to-oven method keeps the cut in contact with
  residual pan heat during the oven phase; the 190°C oven temperature is higher
  than the reverse-sear approach, trading some gradient control for a shorter
  cooking time.

### 3. Bon Appétit — Oven-Roasted Tri-Tip with Chimichurri (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/oven-roasted-tri-tip-chimichurri
- **Method**: Rub with garlic and herbs; let sit at room temp 30 min. Roast at 200°C
  (400°F) on a rack set in a rimmed baking sheet until probe reads 52°C (20–25 min).
  Rest 15 min. Slice thin against grain.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Why this method works**: The rack-in-tray setup allows air to circulate under the
  tri-tip; 15-min rest for a whole roast is critical to let the juice redistribute
  throughout the cut before slicing.

### 4. ICA — Tri-tip i ugn (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/tri-tip-ugn
- **Method**: Ugn 175°C; lägg tri-tipen på ett galler i långpanna. Stek tills
  kärntemperaturen visar 54°C (ca 25–30 min). Vila 10–15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: **56–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: The Swedish home oven approach with a grill rack produces
  even browning on all sides without requiring a cast-iron sear; the 175°C temperature
  gives a gentle, controllable approach to the target.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Oven-Roasted Tri-Tip Steak (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/oven-roasted-tri-tip
- **Method**: Season and bring to room temp 30 min. Oven 200°C fan; place on wire
  rack in roasting tin; cook 20–25 min until 54°C. Rest 10 min, slice thin.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: UK recipes present tri-tip as an American cut that suits
  the Sunday roast tradition; 200°C fan gives browning without a preliminary sear,
  trading the deep Maillard crust for simplicity.
