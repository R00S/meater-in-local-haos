# Tri Tip × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan-searing a tri-tip requires choosing between two approaches: (1) sear the whole roast and finish in the oven — best for a whole tri-tip 700 g+; (2) slice into 2 cm steaks and pan-sear each one — fastest method. For whole sear, the constant-turning method (chefkoch.de) produces a better crust than leaving it static. The lean sections of tri-tip will overcook faster than the marbled centre, so the MEATER probe is essential for accurate pull temperature. Slice against the grain — the grain changes direction at the centre seam.

Target internal temperatures (pan sear):
- Medium-rare: **pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Das perfekte Steak mit Steakkruste (German tradition)
**Source**: Lumacone, Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3258661484569054/Das-perfekte-Steak-mit-Steakkruste.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Constant-turning technique for sliced tri-tip steaks: turn every 5–10 seconds to build a Maillard crust without burning. 4.8/5, 90 ratings.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 2–2.5 hr de-chill · **Cook**: 6–8 min + 4 min rest

*Translation: The perfect steak with steak crust. Key method: ständiges Wenden (constant turning) every few seconds.*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "The meat firms with each turn. When the crust transitions from light to dark brown — that is your stop point." For medium: approximately 6–8 minutes total.

**Ingredients**
- 2 tri-tip steaks (1.5–2 cm slices) or 1 whole small tri-tip
- Neutral oil (rapeseed, sunflower)
- Salt and pepper (applied after crust forms)
- 1–2 knobs of butter
- 1 rosemary sprig
- 1 small onion or shallot (for pan sauce)
- 1 garlic clove

**Method**
1. Remove tri-tip from fridge **2–2.5 hours ahead** — room temperature is critical to avoid meat seizing in the hot pan.
2. Lightly coat with oil.
3. Heat a heavy pan (or cast iron) to very hot. Add a dash of oil.
4. Place tri-tip in pan. Wait **5 seconds**. Flip. Wait 5–10 seconds. Flip again. Continue this constant-turning rhythm throughout the cook.
5. After the 3rd–4th turn, a golden crust begins to form. Continue turning until the crust progresses from light gold to deep dark brown.
6. The meat firms noticeably as it cooks — judge doneness by firmness and crust colour.
7. Turn off heat. Add butter to pan. Season with salt and pepper. Toss the steak briefly in the melted butter.
8. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 54–57 °C (129–135 °F)**.
9. Wrap in foil; rest **2–4 minutes**.
10. For pan sauce: reheat pan at medium; add sliced onion, garlic, rosemary. Sauté 2–3 minutes. Spoon over the plated steak.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 2. Madens Verden — Ball tip bøffer (Danish pan steak method) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ball-tip/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Sliced ball tip (tri-tip) steaks in a hot pan — the same principle as any premium steak. "Skær kødet i passende stykker, ikke for tynde, og steg eller grill dem ved en god varme." (Cut the meat into good-sized pieces, not too thin, and fry or grill at good heat.)*
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 30 min de-chill · **Cook**: 6–8 min + rest

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "Steg eller grill dem ved en god varme. Krydres med salt og peber." High heat throughout. Core temperature 56 °C = tender, pink, juicy.

**Ingredients**
- 700 g–1 kg tri-tip, sliced into 2–3 cm steaks
- Neutral oil (for the pan)
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Slice the tri-tip perpendicular to the grain into 2–3 cm steaks. Make sure to identify the grain direction (it changes at the centre of the roast).
2. Remove from fridge 30 minutes before cooking.
3. Heat pan to high; add oil.
4. Sear steaks on high heat for 3–4 minutes per side. Do not crowd the pan — use batches.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest piece. **Pull at 54–56 °C (129–133 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Season with salt and pepper.
7. Rest 3–5 minutes. Serve with Hasselback potatoes or pan sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 56–59 °C (133–138 °F).*

### 3. Immaculate Bites (africanbites.com) — stovetop sear (tri-tip oven roast pan-sear method) (Californian / West African tradition)
**Source**: Imma Adamu, africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/oven-tri-tip-roast/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Stovetop pan-sear before oven: cast-iron skillet, fat-side down 4–5 min, flip 2 more min. Creates the crust for the oven roast — as a standalone pan-sear, extend time until desired core temperature.*
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinade · **Cook**: 8–12 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Sear fat-side down 4–5 minutes until thoroughly browned; flip 2 minutes. For a standalone pan sear without oven: continue 2–3 more minutes per side at medium heat to reach target internal temperature.

**Ingredients**
- 1 tri-tip (or steak slices), with fat scored
- Red wine marinade (garlic, thyme, smoked paprika, basil, olive oil, red wine, onion powder, salt, pepper) — marinate 1 hour minimum
- 1 tbsp olive oil for pan

**Method**
1. Marinate tri-tip as per recipe.
2. Heat a large cast-iron skillet over high. Add 1 tbsp olive oil.
3. Sear fat-side down for **4–5 minutes** until deeply browned. Lower heat to medium-high; flip and cook **2 more minutes**.
4. For medium-rare without oven finish: reduce to medium; continue 3–4 more minutes, turning occasionally, until core reaches 57 °C.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull at 57 °C (135 °F)** for medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Slice in half at the seam, then slice each half against the grain.
7. Reduce reserved marinade in a saucepan to a sauce. Drizzle over.
*Final resting temperature: 58–62 °C (136–144 °F).*
