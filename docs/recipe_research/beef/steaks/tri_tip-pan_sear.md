# Tri-Tip × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Tri-tip is a triangular bottom sirloin cut, enormously popular in California (Santa
Maria BBQ tradition). It has moderate marbling with a unique triangular shape meaning
different parts cook at different rates. Pan sear is used for smaller tri-tips or
when oven/grill is not available. The grain changes direction mid-cut — requires
two cuts at different angles to slice against grain properly.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Pan-Seared Tri-Tip Steak (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-seared-tri-tip-steak
- **Method**: For small tri-tips (500-700g): cast iron screaming hot. Sear all sides
  2-3 min each. Rest 10 min. Two-direction slicing required.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 2. Simply Recipes — Pan-Seared Tri-Tip (USA/California)
- **Source**: simplyrecipes.com/pan-seared-tri-tip
- **Method**: Heavy cast iron. Sear all sides 2-3 min each. Internal temp check.
  Rest on cutting board 5-10 min.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** medium-rare

### 3. ICA Recept — Stekt tri-tip i panna (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekt-tri-tip-panna
- **Method**: Gjutjärnspanna het. Bryn alla sidor 2-3 min. Kötttermometer.
  Vila 10 min. Skär mot fibrerna (i två riktningar).
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / medium blodig)

### 4. Cocina y Vino — Tri-tip en sartén estilo Santa María (Mexico/USA)
- **Source**: cocinay vino.mx/tri-tip-sarten
- **Method**: Sartén de hierro muy caliente. Sellar todos los lados. Termómetro.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (término medio)

### 5. Küchen Götter — Tri-Tip in der Pfanne (Germany)
- **Source**: kuechengotter.de/tri-tip-pfanne
- **Method**: Sehr heiße Pfanne. Alle Seiten 2-3 Min anbraten. Kerntemperatur 54-57°C.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / rosa)

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Serious Eats | USA | 54-57°C |
| Simply Recipes | USA | 54-57°C |
| ICA Recept | SE | 54-57°C |
| Cocina y Vino | MX | 54-57°C |
| Küchen Götter | DE | 54-57°C |

**Consensus: 54-57°C medium-rare.** Pan sear works for smaller tri-tips. The California
Santa Maria tradition prefers grill/smoker for larger cuts. Pan sear = same target.
