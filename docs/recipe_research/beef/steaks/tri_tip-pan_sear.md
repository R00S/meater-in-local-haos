## Cut profile
Tri-tip is a triangular muscle from the bottom sirloin, native to the Santa Maria
Valley of California where it became a regional institution. It is a roast-sized cut
(800 g–1.5 kg) but cooked and served like a steak — pulled at medium-rare and sliced
thin against the grain. Pan searing a whole tri-tip is the indoor California method:
a screaming-hot cast-iron sear on all sides to build the crust, then an oven finish
to bring the thick centre to temperature without burning the exterior.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Pan-Seared Tri-Tip Roast (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-seared-tri-tip-roast
- **Method**: Pat dry; season 1 h before. Sear all three faces in cast iron 2–3 min
  each until deep brown crust forms. Transfer skillet to 200°C oven; cook until probe
  reads 52°C at thickest point. Rest 10–15 min loosely tented.
- **Pull temp**: 125°F / **52°C** (thickest point)
- **Final after rest (10–15 min)**: 130–135°F / **54–57°C**
- **Why this method works**: The combination sear-then-oven method gives a tri-tip
  the crust of a pan steak and the even interior of an oven roast; the pull at 52°C
  accounts for the significant carryover from a pre-heated cast-iron skillet in
  a hot oven.

### 2. Food Network — Cast-Iron Tri-Tip Steak (USA)
- **Source**: foodnetwork.com/recipes/cast-iron-tri-tip-steak
- **Method**: Room temp 30 min; season. Heavy pan sear on stove 2 min per side;
  transfer to baking tray; finish in 190°C oven 15–20 min or until 54°C. Rest 10 min.
- **Pull temp**: 130°F / **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56–58°C**
- **Why this method works**: The stove-to-oven workflow keeps the cook in control of
  browning separately from interior temperature; the cast-iron pan creates the
  deep Maillard crust that the convection oven alone cannot produce.

### 3. Bon Appétit — Seared Tri-Tip with Herb Butter (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/seared-tri-tip-herb-butter
- **Method**: Season generously; rest on wire rack 1 h uncovered in fridge. Sear in
  neutral oil on all sides 2 min each. Oven at 180°C until 52°C. Rest 15 min.
- **Pull temp**: **52°C**
- **Final after rest (15 min)**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The uncovered fridge rest creates a dry surface that
  sears faster and more completely; the oven finishing temperature is lower (180°C)
  than ATK's approach to extend the window for temperature management.

### 4. ICA — Tri-Tip stekt i panna (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/tri-tip-stekt-panna
- **Method**: Bryn tri-tip i het järnpanna med smör och olja, alla sidor, 2 min per
  sida. Klart i ugn 175°C tills kärntemperatur 54°C. Vila 10 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C**
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish home-cook guides position the sear-then-oven
  method as the standard for larger beef roasts; the cast-iron pan sear provides
  the colour development that a pure oven roast cannot achieve quickly.

### 5. Sale&Pepe — Tri-Tip in padella con finitura al forno (Italy)
- **Source**: salepepe.it/ricette/tri-tip-padella-forno
- **Method**: Rosolare in padella di ghisa con burro chiarificato tutte le facce,
  2–3 min ciascuna. Terminare in forno a 180°C fino a 54°C al cuore. Riposo 10 min.
- **Pull temp**: **54°C** (al sangue / cottura media)
- **Final after rest (10 min)**: **56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Italian technique treats tri-tip as a bistecca roast;
  the clarified butter sear creates a deep crust without bitterness; oven finishing
  at 180°C gives a comfortable approach-rate to the target.
