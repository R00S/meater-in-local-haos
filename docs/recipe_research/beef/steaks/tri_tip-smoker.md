# Tri-Tip × Smoker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Smoked tri-tip is the California-meets-Texas BBQ style. Unlike Texas brisket (cooked
to 93-96°C for collagen breakdown), tri-tip is smoked at LOW-AND-SLOW but pulled at
MEDIUM-RARE temperatures — it is not a collagen-heavy cut. This is the defining
characteristic that separates tri-tip from brisket. The result: smoke ring, smoke
flavour, but pink medium-rare interior.

## Source recipes

### 1. Traeger / Serious Eats — Smoked Tri-Tip (USA)
- **Source**: traegergrills.com/recipes/smoked-tri-tip + seriouseats crossref
- **Method**: Smoker 135°C (275°F), oak or hickory wood. 1.5-2 hours to internal 130-135°F.
  Optional: sear on hot grill after smoking (reverse sear). Rest 10 min.
- **Target**: **130-135°F / 54-57°C** — "Tri-tip is NOT brisket. Pull at medium-rare."

### 2. Hey Grill Hey — Smoked Tri-Tip Recipe (USA)
- **Source**: heygrillhey.com/smoked-tri-tip
- **Method**: Smoker 135°C. Cherry or oak wood. 1.5-2.5 hours. Pull at 130°F.
  Sear on cast iron or grill 1-2 min/side for crust. Rest 10 min.
- **Target**: **130°F / 54°C** pull temp, **rest to 57°C**
- "The smoke ring you get on a properly smoked tri-tip is spectacular."

### 3. Pitmaster X — Smoked Tri-Tip (Netherlands/international)
- **Source**: pitmasterx.com/smoked-tri-tip
- **Method**: Offset smoker or pellet smoker 135°C. Oak. 1.5-2 hours to 54-57°C.
  Reverse sear optional. Rest 10 min.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare)

### 4. Grill och BBQ — Rökt tri-tip (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillochbbq.se/rokt-tri-tip
- **Method**: Rökare 135°C, ek- eller körsbärsträ. 1.5-2 timmar till kärna 54-57°C.
  Vila 10 min. Skär mot fibrerna.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare / medium blodig)

### 5. Meathead Goldwyn / AmazingRibs — Low & Slow Tri-Tip (USA)
- **Source**: amazingribs.com/tested-recipes/beef-recipes/santa-maria-style-tri-tip
- **Method**: Smoke at 120-135°C with oak. Pull at 130°F / 54°C. DO NOT cook to
  brisket temps — tri-tip is a lean-ish steak, not a collagen cut.
- **Target**: **130°F / 54°C** pull. Rest to 57°C.
- "Many BBQ beginners mistake tri-tip for a slow-cook-to-fall-apart cut. It is not."

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Target | Notes |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Traeger/SeriousEats | USA | **54-57°C** | NOT brisket temps |
| Hey Grill Hey | USA | **Pull 54°C** | Rest to 57°C |
| Pitmaster X | NL | 54-57°C | International |
| Grill och BBQ | SE | 54-57°C | Standard |
| AmazingRibs | USA | **Pull 54°C** | Emphatic — not a braise cut |

**Consensus: 54-57°C medium-rare.** The CRITICAL insight for smoker tri-tip:
this cut is smoked at low temps but pulled at STEAK temperatures, NOT brisket temperatures.
Smoking is a method — it does NOT change the target vs other methods for this cut.
