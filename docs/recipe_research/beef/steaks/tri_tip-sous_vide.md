# Tri-Tip × Sous Vide — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sous vide tri-tip is increasingly popular — the precise temp control addresses the
triangular shape's uneven thickness perfectly. Common to do a reverse sear finish
after the bath. Like smoker tri-tip, stays at medium-rare temps — no collagen breakdown
advantage at higher temps for this cut.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Sous Vide Tri-Tip (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sous-vide-tri-tip-recipe
- **Method**: 130°F / 54°C for 2-4 hours. Finish with sear on cast iron or charcoal grill.
  "The evenness you get from sous vide completely solves the thick-end thin-end problem."
- **Target**: **130°F / 54°C** for 2-4 hours

### 2. ChefSteps — Sous Vide Tri-Tip (USA/international)
- **Source**: chefsteps.com/activities/tri-tip-sous-vide
- **Method**: 57°C / 135°F for 3 hours. Sear finish. The extra 3°C vs Kenji accounts for
  the slight connective tissue present.
- **Target**: **57°C** for 3 hours

### 3. Sous Vide Everything — Tri-Tip Sous Vide (USA)
- **Source**: sveverything.com/sous-vide-tri-tip
- **Method**: 130-135°F / 54-57°C for 3-4 hours. Cast iron sear finish 1 min/side.
  Chimichurri recommended.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** for 3-4 hours

### 4. BBQ og Grill — Sous vide tri-tip (Norway)
- **Source**: bbqoggrill.no/sous-vide-tri-tip
- **Method**: 54°C i 3-4 timer. Avslutt med kraftig steking i støpejern.
  Kjernetemperatur på 54-57°C etter hvil.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** (medium-rare)

### 5. Sous Vide Ways — Tri-Tip Sous Vide Guide (USA)
- **Source**: sousvideways.com/tri-tip-sous-vide
- **Method**: 130-135°F / 54-57°C for 2-4 hours. High-heat sear to finish.
- **Target**: **54-57°C** — same target as other methods

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Country | Target | Time |
|--------|---------|--------|------|
| Serious Eats | USA | **54°C** | 2-4 hours |
| ChefSteps | USA | **57°C** | 3 hours |
| SVEverything | USA | 54-57°C | 3-4 hours |
| BBQ og Grill | NO | 54-57°C | 3-4 hours |
| Sous Vide Ways | USA | 54-57°C | 2-4 hours |

**Consensus: 54-57°C for 2-4 hours.** ChefSteps 57°C addresses any minor connective tissue.
Sous vide perfectly handles the triangular shape's uneven thickness. Same final target
as all other methods.
