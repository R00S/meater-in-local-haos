# Tri-Tip × Sous Vide — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sous vide unlocks a version of tri-tip that even the best charcoal and oak fire cannot
produce: a perfectly uniform edge-to-edge medium-rare throughout the entire triangular
cut, from the thick centre to the thinnest tip. No gradient, no overcooked edges,
no raw interior — just the precise temperature the cook chose, held exactly for as
long as needed. The roast's large size (800 g–1.5 kg) means the bath time is longer
than for steaks, which also benefits the cut's slightly firm sirloin muscle by
allowing gentle enzymatic tenderisation throughout.

## Source recipes

### 1. Anova Culinary — Sous Vide Tri-Tip Roast (USA)
- **Source**: recipes.anovaculinary.com/recipe/sous-vide-tri-tip
- **Method**: Season generously; vacuum-seal whole. Bath at 130°F / 54°C for 2–6 hours.
  Remove, pat dry, rest uncovered 15 min. Sear all faces 60–90 seconds each in
  screaming-hot cast iron. Rest 5 min before slicing.
- **Bath temp**: 130°F / **54°C**, 2–6 h
- **Final after sear + rest**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The wide 2–6 hour bath window makes tri-tip an ideal sous
  vide cut for entertaining; the whole roast can go in hours before guests arrive and
  the window is wide enough to accommodate any schedule change.

### 2. Serious Eats — Sous Vide Tri-Tip Roast Guide (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sous-vide-tri-tip-roast
- **Method**: 130°F / 54°C for 3–4 h gives the best texture balance — long enough
  for enzymatic tenderisation but not so long the texture becomes mushy. Finish with
  45-second per side sear in cast iron with clarified butter.
- **Bath temp**: **54°C**, 3–4 h (recommended)
- **Final after sear**: **54–55°C**
- **Why this method works**: The moderate marbling of tri-tip does not benefit from
  57°C the way ribeye does; 54°C preserves the clean sirloin flavour while the
  extended bath time softens the muscle texture.

### 3. ChefSteps — Sous Vide Tri-Tip Roast (USA)
- **Source**: chefsteps.com/activities/sous-vide-tri-tip
- **Method**: Bag with aromatics (garlic, rosemary, thyme); 130°F / 54°C for 4–6 h.
  Remove, pat dry, rest 15 min. Sear all sides over screaming-hot charcoal or
  cast iron 45–60 s per face.
- **Bath temp**: **54°C**, 4–6 h
- **Final after sear**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: The charcoal finish for a sous-vide tri-tip adds the smoke
  character that the water bath cannot provide; the long bath pre-renders the aromatics'
  flavours into the meat surface before the final sear drives them into the crust.

### 4. Smakworld — Sous vide Tri-tip (Sweden)
- **Source**: smakworld.se/sous-vide-tri-tip
- **Method**: Vakuumförpacka med salt, peppar och krossad vitlök. Bad 54°C i 3–5 timmar.
  Torka noga. Bryn alla sidor i het järnpanna 60 sek per sida. Vila 5–10 min.
- **Bath temp**: **54°C**, 3–5 h
- **Final after sear**: **54–56°C**
- **Why this method works**: Swedish sous vide guides recommend tri-tip for dinner
  parties because the wide bath window removes all timing pressure; the size of
  the cut means a full rest after searing is important before slicing.

### 5. Sous Vide Everything (Guga Foods) — Tri-Tip Sous Vide Test (USA/YouTube)
- **Source**: youtube.com/SousVideEverything (tri-tip episode)
- **Method**: Tested 130°F / 54°C (winner) vs 140°F / 60°C. "54°C is the clear
  winner — tender, juicy, the perfect pink. 60°C is too dry for this cut."
  Bath 3 h; finish in cast iron 45 s per face.
- **Bath temp**: **130°F / 54°C**, 3 h (confirmed winner)
- **Final after sear**: **54–55°C**
- **Why this method works**: Popular comparison testing confirmed 54°C as optimal for
  tri-tip's lean-to-moderate fat content; higher temperatures cause moisture loss
  in a cut without the abundant marbling to compensate.
