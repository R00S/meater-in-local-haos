# Salmon Fillet × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Salmon fillet in the air fryer is cooked by rapid convective circulation of superheated air around the fish in a compact enclosed basket; this simulates roasting at intensified speed, producing a surface that browns faster than a conventional oven bake while the interior remains moist. The skin crisps well if the surface is dried thoroughly before cooking. The compact geometry of most air fryer baskets means portions must be sized to fit without overlapping; single fillets of 150–200 g are ideal. Carryover from an air fryer is 3–5 °C, similar to a hot convection oven, and residual heat from the basket continues to cook the fish briefly after extraction. The rapid cook time — typically 8–12 minutes — means that soy, honey or sugar-based glazes caramelise well without burning if applied mid-cook or just before the final 2 minutes.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air-Fryer Salmon (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Air Fryer Perfection* (America's Test Kitchen, 2019)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+15 min brine) · **Cook**: 9–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on salmon fillets, 170–200 g each, even thickness
- 480 ml (2 cups) water
- 30 g (2 tbsp) table salt (for brining)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- Lemon wedges and fresh herbs, to serve

**Method**
1. Dissolve table salt in water. Submerge salmon fillets in the brine for 15 minutes. The brief brine seasons the flesh and helps prevent white albumin from weeping during cooking.
2. Remove from brine; pat completely dry with paper towels.
3. Brush all surfaces of the salmon with vegetable oil.
4. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes. Lightly spray the basket with cooking spray.
5. Place salmon skin-side down in the basket, without overlapping. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of one fillet, angling into the centre of the flesh, keeping the probe cable clear of the basket drawer.
6. Air fry at 200 °C for 9–12 minutes depending on thickness (9 minutes for 2 cm fillets; 12 minutes for 3.5 cm fillets).
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** ATK's air fryer salmon target is medium — the flesh should just be opaque throughout, moist and barely set, with no albumin weeping on the surface.
8. Rest 2 minutes in the basket (basket off). Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium, opaque, tender).

### 2. Hetty McKinnon — Air-Fryer Teriyaki Salmon (Japanese-Australian tradition)
**Source**: Hetty McKinnon, *To Asia, With Love* (Prestel Publishing, 2021)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min marinade) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on salmon fillets, 180 g each
- **Teriyaki glaze**: 45 ml (3 tbsp) soy sauce (*shoyu*); 30 ml (2 tbsp) mirin; 15 ml (1 tbsp) sake; 10 g (2 tsp) caster sugar; 5 ml (1 tsp) sesame oil
- Toasted sesame seeds and sliced spring onions (*scallions*), to garnish
- Steamed short-grain rice, to serve

**Method**
1. Combine soy sauce, mirin, sake and caster sugar in a small saucepan; bring to a simmer. Cook 3–4 minutes until slightly thickened. Remove from heat; stir in sesame oil.
2. Reserve half the teriyaki glaze for basting. Coat salmon fillets with the other half; marinate 30 minutes at room temperature.
3. Preheat air fryer to 190 °C (375 °F). Line basket with baking paper cut to fit.
4. Pat excess marinade from the fillets; place skin-side down in the basket.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of one fillet into the centre of the flesh.
6. Air fry 7 minutes. Brush with the reserved teriyaki glaze. Continue air frying 3–4 minutes until the glaze is caramelised and glossy.
7. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F).** McKinnon's preference is for a fully cooked but still moist salmon — the glaze obscures visual cues, so the probe is the primary indicator.
8. Rest 2 minutes. Garnish with sesame seeds and spring onions. Final serving temperature: **56–58 °C / 133–136 °F** (medium to medium-well, glazed and fragrant).

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Air-Fryer Salmon with Spice Crust (British tradition)
**Source**: Jamie Oliver, *5 Ingredients — Quick & Easy Food* (Michael Joseph / Penguin, 2017); adapted for air fryer in his online recipes at jamieoliver.com  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on salmon fillets, 150–180 g each
- 5 g (1 tsp) smoked paprika
- 3 g (½ tsp) ground cumin
- 2 g (¼ tsp) ground coriander
- 5 ml (1 tsp) olive oil
- Flaked sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 lemon, juice and zest
- Natural yoghurt, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix smoked paprika, cumin, coriander, olive oil, lemon zest, salt and pepper into a paste.
2. Coat the flesh side of the salmon fillets with the spice paste.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 2 minutes. Lightly oil the basket.
4. Place salmon skin-side down in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the flesh.
5. Air fry at 200 °C for 10–12 minutes. The spice crust will darken and become fragrant; the skin will crisp.
6. **Pull at 52–55 °C (126–131 °F).** Oliver's cookbooks target medium — he writes "still slightly rosy in the middle, moist and flaky."
7. Squeeze lemon juice over the fillets immediately after pulling. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with yoghurt. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium, spiced crust, moist interior).

### 4. Nigella Lawson — Sweet Miso-Glazed Air-Fryer Salmon (fusion, British-Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Nigella Lawson, *Cook, Eat, Repeat* (Chatto & Windus, 2020)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 salmon fillets, skinless, 160–180 g each
- 30 g (2 tbsp) white *shiro miso* (*shiro miso* — mild pale Japanese fermented soybean paste)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) mirin
- 10 ml (2 tsp) soy sauce
- 10 g (2 tsp) honey
- 5 ml (1 tsp) rice wine vinegar
- Sesame seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Whisk miso, mirin, soy sauce, honey and rice wine vinegar together until smooth.
2. Place salmon in a shallow dish; spoon marinade over both sides. Cover and refrigerate 1 hour.
3. Remove from fridge 15 minutes before cooking. Scrape most of the marinade off — excess marinade burns in the air fryer.
4. Preheat air fryer to 180 °C (355 °F). Line basket with foil.
5. Place salmon on the foil. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the flesh.
6. Air fry 8–10 minutes. The residual miso glaze will caramelise to a golden-brown.
7. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F).** Lawson's standard is barely cooked through — she notes that salmon is best when still "slightly slack in the middle."
8. Scatter sesame seeds. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **54–55 °C / 130–131 °F** (medium, glazed, moist).

### 5. Donna Hay — Lemon-Herb Air-Fryer Salmon (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *The Fast 400 Cookbook* (HarperCollins Australia, 2020)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on salmon fillets, 180–200 g each
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) finely grated lemon zest
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh thyme leaves
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper

**Method**
1. Combine olive oil, garlic, lemon zest, thyme, salt and pepper in a small bowl.
2. Pat salmon dry. Coat flesh side with the oil-herb mixture; leave skin side plain for crisping.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes. Lightly spray basket.
4. Place salmon skin-side down in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the flesh.
5. Air fry at 200 °C for 10 minutes. The skin should be golden and crisp; the flesh side should be fragrant and golden at the edges.
6. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F).** Hay's standard is medium — she writes that the salmon should be "cooked through with a slightly translucent centre" and notes Australian farmed salmon handles this temperature well.
7. Rest 2 minutes in the basket. Final serving temperature: **56–57 °C / 133–135 °F** (medium, opaque but moist).
