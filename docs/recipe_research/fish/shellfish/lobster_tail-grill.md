# Lobster Tail × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Lobster tail is the large tail segment of a lobster (*Homarus americanus*, the American/Maine lobster, or *Homarus gammarus*, the European/Breton lobster) separated from the body and claws; frozen and fresh spiny lobster tails (*Panulirus argus*) from the Caribbean and Pacific are also widely used. The edible flesh is the tail muscle — firm, white-to-pale-pink, sweet and rich in natural glutamates. Grilling is one of the most popular preparations for lobster tail because the direct radiant heat produces char and caramelisation on the cut flesh surface while the shell acts as a natural heat shield and flavour-retaining vessel. Butterflying the tail (cutting through the shell dorsally and splaying the flesh upward) is the dominant grill preparation because it exposes more flesh surface to direct heat and allows basting to penetrate the meat. Overcooking tightens the fibres sharply; the pull window is 58–62 °C before the meat becomes dry and rubbery. Carryover is 3–4 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steven Raichlen — Grilled Butterflied Lobster Tail with Herb Butter (American tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 lobster tails, 170–230 g each (fresh or thawed from frozen)
- 60 g (4 tbsp) unsalted butter, softened
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh tarragon or chives, finely chopped
- 5 ml (1 tsp) fresh lemon juice
- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra virgin olive oil
- Lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Make herb butter: mix softened butter with garlic, parsley, tarragon and lemon juice; season with salt and pepper. Shape into a log in plastic wrap; refrigerate.
2. Butterfly the lobster tails: using kitchen shears, cut along the centre of the top shell from the open end toward the tail fan, cutting through the shell but not the flesh beneath. Spread the two halves of the shell apart; with your fingers, lift the flesh up through the slit and rest it on top of the shell. The flesh sits above the shell like a saddle.
3. Score the flesh with a sharp knife in a crosshatch pattern, approximately 5 mm deep — this allows butter to penetrate and speeds heat transfer.
4. Brush flesh with olive oil; season with salt and pepper.
5. Preheat grill to medium-high, approximately 200–220 °C. Clean and oil grates.
6. Place lobster tails flesh-side down on the grill. Grill 5 minutes without moving — the scored flesh should char lightly and the natural sugars caramelise.
7. Flip to shell-side down. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick head end of the tail, angling through the shell opening into the centre of the tail muscle.
8. Place a pat of herb butter on the flesh. Continue grilling shell-side down for 5–7 minutes, basting with additional melted herb butter as it pools in the shell.
9. **Pull at 60 °C (140 °F).** Raichlen's target for lobster tail is fully cooked but still moist — the flesh should be white and opaque throughout, slightly firm but not rubbery.
10. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with remaining herb butter and lemon. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (fully cooked, white throughout, moist).

### 2. Gordon Ramsay — Grilled Lobster Tail with Garlic Butter (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Ultimate Cookery Course* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 lobster tails, 200–250 g each
- 80 g (5 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 5 ml (1 tsp) fresh lemon juice
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- Lemon halves, to serve

**Method**
1. Melt butter with garlic in a small saucepan; stir in parsley and lemon juice. Season.
2. Butterfly the tails: using kitchen shears, cut down the centre of the top shell. Open the shell and pull the meat upward, resting it on top.
3. Brush exposed meat with olive oil; season.
4. Preheat a gas or charcoal grill to high.
5. Place tails flesh-side down. Grill 4 minutes — Ramsay presses briefly with tongs to ensure contact with the grill.
6. Flip to shell-side down. Insert the MEATER probe from the head end through the shell opening into the centre of the tail muscle.
7. Baste generously with garlic butter. Grill shell-side down 4–6 minutes, basting every 2 minutes.
8. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F).** Ramsay's target: "the flesh should be white and have a slight spring when pressed, but never dry."
9. Rest 2 minutes in the shell. Serve with remaining garlic butter and lemon halves. Final serving temperature: **60–63 °C / 140–145 °F** (fully cooked, juicy, fragrant with garlic).

### 3. Joël Robuchon — Homard Grillé au Beurre Blanc (French haute cuisine tradition)
**Source**: Joël Robuchon, *The Complete Robuchon* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2008)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 European or American lobster tails, 200–250 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) neutral oil
- Fine fleur de sel and freshly ground white pepper
- **Beurre blanc sauce**: 2 shallots, minced; 80 ml (⅓ cup) dry white wine; 30 ml (2 tbsp) white wine vinegar; 150 g cold unsalted butter, cubed; fine salt and white pepper; 1 tbsp fresh chervil or tarragon, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Prepare beurre blanc: combine shallots, wine and vinegar; reduce to a syrup. Over gentle heat, whisk in cold butter cube by cube. Stir in fresh herbs. Season; keep warm.
2. Split tails in half lengthwise with a heavy chef's knife, cutting through the shell and flesh to expose the meat. Season with fleur de sel and white pepper; brush with oil.
3. Preheat grill to high.
4. Place tails shell-side down. Insert the MEATER probe from the head end, angling into the centre of the lobster flesh.
5. Grill shell-side down 6–8 minutes with the lid closed — the shell insulates and steam-cooks the flesh gently from above while the shell chars below.
6. Flip to flesh-side down; grill 2–3 minutes to develop light char marks.
7. **Pull at 58 °C (136 °F).** Robuchon's French tradition targets lobster that is just cooked — white throughout but still very moist, with a slight translucency at the very centre if the tail is thick.
8. Rest 2 minutes. Serve shell-side up with beurre blanc pooled around. Final serving temperature: **60–62 °C / 140–144 °F** (just cooked, white and moist, Robuchon standard).

### 4. Nobu Matsuhisa — Grilled Spiny Lobster Tail with Yuzu Miso (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Nobu Matsuhisa and Mark Edwards, *Nobu: The Cookbook* (Kodansha International, 2001)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 spiny lobster tails (*ise-ebi* — Japanese spiny lobster, or Caribbean spiny lobster as substitute), 200–250 g each
- **Yuzu miso glaze**: 60 g white *shiro miso*; 30 ml (2 tbsp) mirin; 30 ml (2 tbsp) sake; 15 g (1 tbsp) caster sugar; 30 ml (2 tbsp) fresh yuzu juice (or lemon-lime blend)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- Shredded daikon and pickled ginger (*gari*), to serve

**Method**
1. Combine miso, mirin, sake and sugar in a saucepan; bring to a simmer. Stir until smooth and glossy, about 3 minutes. Cool; stir in yuzu juice.
2. Split tails lengthwise; remove vein. Coat flesh generously with yuzu miso; marinate 2 hours refrigerated.
3. Remove from fridge 20 minutes before grilling. Wipe excess marinade from the shell — leave residual glaze on the flesh.
4. Preheat grill to medium (not high — the miso sugars burn at high heat).
5. Place tails flesh-side down. Grill 3–4 minutes.
6. Flip. Insert the MEATER probe from the head end through the shell into the centre of the tail muscle.
7. Grill shell-side down 6–8 minutes, basting with remaining yuzu miso glaze.
8. **Pull at 60 °C (140 °F).** Nobu's standard: white, opaque flesh with a caramelised miso crust.
9. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with daikon and pickled ginger. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (fully cooked, miso-glazed).

### 5. Jock Zonfrillo — Grilled Rock Lobster with Warrigal Greens Butter (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Jock Zonfrillo, *Orana: Cooking with the Soul of Australia* (Hardie Grant, 2019)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Southern Rock Lobster (*Jasus edwardsii*) tails, 200–250 g each (Australian green or red rock lobster, the premium species of the Southern Ocean)
- 80 g (5 tbsp) unsalted butter, softened
- 30 g *warrigal greens* (*warrigal greens* — native Australian spinach, *Tetragonia tetragonioides*, blanched and finely chopped; substitute baby spinach), blanched, squeezed dry, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) lemon myrtle (*lemon myrtle* — *Backhousia citriodora*, an intensely lemon-scented Australian native spice leaf, dried and ground), or substitute lemon zest
- 5 ml (1 tsp) native saltbush (*saltbush* — *Atriplex nummularia*, an Australian native salt-flavoured leaf), finely crushed (or substitute flaked sea salt)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil

**Method**
1. Mix softened butter with warrigal greens, lemon myrtle and saltbush. Form into a log; refrigerate.
2. Split lobster tails in half lengthwise. Season flesh with olive oil.
3. Preheat grill to medium-high.
4. Place tails flesh-side down. Grill 4 minutes.
5. Flip to shell-side down. Insert the MEATER probe from the head end into the centre of the tail muscle.
6. Place a round of warrigal green butter on the exposed flesh. Grill shell-side down 6–8 minutes until butter melts and bastes the flesh.
7. **Pull at 60 °C (140 °F).** Zonfrillo's standard for Australian rock lobster is fully cooked — white throughout, sweet, buttery, fragrant with native Australian aromatics.
8. Rest 2 minutes. Serve immediately. Final serving temperature: **62–64 °C / 144–147 °F** (fully cooked, moist, fragrant).
