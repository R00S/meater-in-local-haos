# Tuna Steak × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Tuna steak — most commonly bluefin (*hon maguro*), yellowfin (*ahi*), or bigeye (*mebachi*) — is cut transversely from the loin muscles of a large pelagic fish. Tuna flesh is deep crimson (from high myoglobin content), very lean, and densely muscled compared to most fish; it has almost no connective tissue between muscle segments, which means it behaves more like a red meat steak than a typical white-flake fish. Pan searing suits tuna precisely because the high heat drives rapid Maillard browning on the lean surface, while the brief cook time leaves the interior at the desired rare state. Overcooking is the defining risk: above 60 °C the proteins tighten dramatically, and the lean flesh becomes dry and crumbly. The conventional sushi-bar preference for *tataki* (briefly seared, cold-raw centre) means the entire temperature spectrum from raw to fully cooked is culinarily acknowledged. Carryover in a seared tuna steak is 2–3 °C and dissipates quickly.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nobu Matsuhisa — Tuna Tataki with Ponzu (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Nobu Matsuhisa and Mark Edwards, *Nobu: The Cookbook* (Kodansha International, 2001)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 2–3 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 400 g sashimi-grade bluefin (*hon maguro*) or yellowfin (*ahi*) tuna loin, approximately 5 × 5 cm cross-section, 10 cm long (block cut for tataki)
- 10 g (2 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable or rice bran oil
- **Ponzu sauce**: 60 ml (¼ cup) fresh yuzu or lemon juice; 45 ml (3 tbsp) soy sauce (*shoyu*); 15 ml (1 tbsp) mirin; 10 ml (2 tsp) rice vinegar
- Grated daikon and sliced spring onion (*negi*), to serve

**Method**
1. Remove tuna from the fridge 10 minutes before cooking. Pat completely dry — moisture impedes the Maillard reaction. Season all four long sides with salt and pepper.
2. Mix ponzu ingredients; set aside.
3. Heat a carbon-steel or cast-iron pan over the highest possible heat for 4–5 minutes until smoking intensely. Add the oil; it will smoke immediately.
4. Insert the MEATER probe lengthwise into one end of the tuna block, sliding it along the long axis so the tip rests at the geometric centre — this is the correct approach for a block-shaped cut.
5. Sear each of the four sides for 30–45 seconds each, turning with tongs to ensure even charred crust on all sides. Total cook time: 2–3 minutes.
6. **Pull at 28–32 °C (82–90 °F).** *Tataki* is served with a warm, deeply crusted exterior and a completely raw centre. Nobu's published standard targets a seared surface with a cold-raw interior.
7. Rest on a rack 1 minute. Slice immediately into 5 mm slices across the grain. Fan on a plate; drizzle with ponzu. Serve with grated daikon and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **30–35 °C / 86–95 °F** at the centre (raw, slightly warmed by conduction from the crust).

### 2. J. Kenji López-Alt — Pan-Seared Ahi Tuna Steaks (American tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, seriouseats.com/pan-seared-tuna-steaks  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 3–4 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ahi (yellowfin) tuna steaks, 200–250 g each, 3–4 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) kosher salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) vegetable oil
- Sesame seeds (optional, pressed into flesh before searing)
- Soy sauce and pickled ginger, to serve

**Method**
1. Pat tuna steaks completely dry. Season generously with salt and pepper on all sides. If using sesame seeds, press them firmly onto both flat faces of the steak now.
2. Heat a cast-iron or carbon-steel skillet over the absolute highest heat. Add oil; heat until smoking.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of the steak (through the thinnest dimension) into the very centre of the steak.
4. Place the tuna in the pan. Sear undisturbed for 60–90 seconds per side. The crust should be a deep golden-brown or dark sesame seed crust in 90 seconds at maximum heat.
5. Sear the thin edges briefly (10–15 seconds each) if the steak is thick enough to justify it.
6. **Pull at 42–46 °C (108–115 °F).** Kenji's published target for tuna steak is medium-rare — a warm red-pink interior. He notes the window between this and overcooked (grey, dry, crumbly) is narrow, so pulling early is always preferable.
7. Rest 1 minute. Slice against the grain. Final serving temperature: **43–48 °C / 110–118 °F** (medium-rare, warm pink-red interior).

### 3. Rick Stein — Charred Tuna with Salsa Rossa (Mediterranean / Italian tradition)
**Source**: Rick Stein, *Rick Stein's Seafood* (BBC Books, 2001)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 tuna steaks (bluefin or yellowfin), 200–250 g each, 3 cm thick
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **Salsa rossa**: 2 red peppers, roasted and peeled; 1 garlic clove; 30 ml olive oil; 10 ml red wine vinegar; 1 tsp tomato purée; salt; pinch cayenne

**Method**
1. Blend salsa rossa ingredients to a slightly rough consistency. Season; set aside.
2. Brush tuna steaks with olive oil; season with salt and pepper.
3. Heat a ridged cast-iron grill pan (or heavy skillet) over the highest heat until smoking.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one tuna steak into the centre of the flesh.
5. Sear tuna 2 minutes per side. The surface should have clear, deep char lines if using a ridged pan.
6. **Pull at 46–50 °C (115–122 °F).** Stein's Seafood book targets medium for tuna — he writes "still pink in the centre, the flesh just starting to turn opaque." The Mediterranean tradition accepts a warm pink centre.
7. Rest 1 minute. Serve with salsa rossa alongside. Final serving temperature: **48–52 °C / 118–126 °F** (medium, warm pink-orange centre).

### 4. Paula Wolfert — Thon à la Chermoula (Moroccan tradition)
**Source**: Paula Wolfert, *The Food of Morocco* (Ecco/HarperCollins, 2011)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 tuna steaks, 180–200 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- **Charmoula**: 40 g fresh coriander (cilantro); 20 g flat-leaf parsley; 4 garlic cloves; 10 g (2 tsp) ground cumin; 5 g (1 tsp) sweet paprika; 3 g (½ tsp) ground turmeric; 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil; 30 ml (2 tbsp) fresh lemon juice; 5 g (1 tsp) fine salt; pinch cayenne

**Method**
1. Blend charmoula ingredients to a rough paste. Coat tuna steaks generously; marinate 2 hours refrigerated.
2. Remove from fridge 20 minutes before cooking. Scrape excess marinade from surface.
3. Heat a heavy iron pan over high heat until very hot. Add a small amount of oil.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one tuna steak into its centre.
5. Sear the tuna 3–4 minutes per side — the Moroccan preparation for tuna traditionally cooks the fish through more than the Japanese or American preference, as tuna is not eaten raw in traditional Moroccan cuisine.
6. **Pull at 54–57 °C (130–135 °F).** Wolfert's Moroccan tradition targets a fully cooked tuna — the flesh will be opaque throughout, slightly firm but still moist from the marinade.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with lemon wedges and flatbread. Final serving temperature: **57–60 °C / 135–140 °F** (well-done, opaque throughout, moist from marinade).

### 5. Gordon Ramsay — Seared Sesame Tuna with Ginger Dressing (British restaurant tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Secrets* (Quadrille Publishing, 2003)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min marinade) · **Cook**: 3–4 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 yellowfin tuna steaks, 200–230 g each, 3–4 cm thick
- 30 g (2 tbsp) mixed sesame seeds (black and white)
- 5 ml (1 tsp) soy sauce
- 5 ml (1 tsp) sesame oil
- **Ginger dressing**: 30 ml (2 tbsp) soy sauce; 15 ml (1 tbsp) rice wine vinegar; 10 ml (2 tsp) sesame oil; 5 g (1 tsp) finely grated fresh ginger; 5 g (1 tsp) caster sugar
- Vegetable oil, for searing
- Micro cress or watercress, to serve

**Method**
1. Brush tuna steaks with soy sauce and sesame oil; press sesame seeds firmly onto both flat faces.
2. Mix ginger dressing ingredients; set aside.
3. Heat a heavy pan with vegetable oil over the highest heat until smoking.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one tuna steak into its centre, avoiding the sesame seed surface.
5. Sear sesame-coated tuna for 90 seconds per side — the sesame seeds toast and set into a crunchy crust.
6. Sear the two thin edges briefly.
7. **Pull at 44–46 °C (111–115 °F).** Ramsay's published target for sesame-crusted tuna is rare to medium-rare — a deep red centre showing no grey.
8. Rest 1 minute. Slice into 1 cm slices; fan on plate. Drizzle with ginger dressing; top with micro cress. Final serving temperature: **45–48 °C / 113–118 °F** (rare to medium-rare, red centre).
