# Cod Fillet × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Air-fried cod fillet is cooked in a compact, rapidly circulating convective hot-air environment that mimics deep frying or high-heat oven roasting but with much less oil. The short cook time (10–14 minutes) and intense circulating air produce a lightly crisped exterior on coated fillets, or a slightly caramelised surface on uncoated ones, while the interior cooks through with minimal moisture loss. Cod's lean flesh responds well to the air fryer because it does not require the self-basting effect of fat-rich fish — the rapid air circulation is sufficient to cook the fillet evenly. Crumb- or batter-coated versions (a lighter version of fish and chips) are the most popular air-fryer cod preparation. The major practical challenge is that very thin tail-end portions of cod cook faster than thick loin portions; probing the thickest part is essential. Carryover is 3–4 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air-Fryer Cod (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Air Fryer Perfection* (America's Test Kitchen, 2019)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skinless cod fillets, 170–200 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 3 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) garlic powder
- 5 ml (1 tsp) vegetable oil
- 30 g panko breadcrumbs
- 20 g (1½ tbsp) unsalted butter, melted
- 5 g (1 tsp) finely grated lemon zest
- Tartar sauce and lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Pat cod dry. Season both sides with salt, pepper and garlic powder.
2. Mix panko with melted butter and lemon zest. Press mixture firmly onto the top of each fillet, forming an even crust.
3. Brush the bottom of the air fryer basket with vegetable oil, or use cooking spray.
4. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes.
5. Place fillets in the basket, crust-side up, without overlapping.
6. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of one fillet, under the panko crust, into the centre of the flesh.
7. Air fry at 200 °C for 12–15 minutes until the crust is deep golden-brown.
8. **Pull at 62–64 °C (144–147 °F).** ATK's standard: the cod should flake easily with a fork, the flesh large and moist, the crust crisp.
9. Rest 2 minutes in the basket. Serve with tartar sauce and lemon. Final serving temperature: **64–66 °C / 147–151 °F** (fully cooked, large moist flakes, crisp crust).

### 2. Jamie Oliver — Air-Fryer Fish Fingers (British tradition)
**Source**: Jamie Oliver, *7 Ways* (Michael Joseph / Penguin, 2020); adapted for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 300 g cod fillet, skinless, cut into finger-sized pieces (approximately 8 × 3 × 2 cm)
- 50 g plain flour
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 80 g fine natural breadcrumbs or panko
- 5 g (1 tsp) smoked paprika
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Cooking spray
- Tomato ketchup or tartare sauce, to serve

**Method**
1. Set up a three-stage breading station: flour in one dish, beaten egg in another, breadcrumbs mixed with paprika, salt and pepper in a third.
2. Pat cod pieces dry. Dredge each piece in flour, then egg, then breadcrumbs, pressing the crumbs firmly to adhere.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F). Spray the basket with cooking spray.
4. Place breaded cod fingers in a single layer, not touching.
5. Spray the tops of the fingers with cooking spray — this helps the breadcrumbs brown.
6. Note: individual fingers are too small for a probe. For the thickest finger, insert the probe from one end into the centre of the flesh before placing in the air fryer.
7. Air fry 10–12 minutes, flipping halfway, until deep golden-brown on all sides.
8. **Pull at 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).** Jamie's standard for fish fingers: fully cooked through, the crumb coating golden and crisp.
9. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with ketchup. Final serving temperature: **65–68 °C / 149–154 °F** (fully cooked, crispy, classic British fish fingers).

### 3. Donna Hay — Lemon-Herb Air-Fryer Cod (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *The Fast 400 Cookbook* (HarperCollins Australia, 2020)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skinless cod fillets, 180–200 g each
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) finely grated lemon zest
- 5 g (1 tsp) dried mixed herbs (thyme, rosemary, oregano)
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- Steamed baby broccoli and lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, garlic, lemon zest and dried herbs into a paste. Season.
2. Pat cod dry. Coat all surfaces with the herb paste.
3. Preheat air fryer to 190 °C (375 °F) for 3 minutes.
4. Place fillets in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of one fillet.
5. Air fry 12–14 minutes until the surface is lightly golden and the flesh is opaque.
6. **Pull at 62 °C (144 °F).** Hay's standard: "just cooked through — flakes easily, still moist."
7. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with steamed greens and lemon. Final serving temperature: **64–66 °C / 147–151 °F** (fully cooked, fragrant).

### 4. Ottolenghi — Spiced Air-Fryer Cod with Tahini (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Simple* (Ebury Press, 2018), adapted for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+20 min marinade) · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skinless cod fillets, 180–200 g each
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) olive oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 5 g (1 tsp) sumac (*sumac* — ground dried berry with tartness, Levantine spice)
- 3 g (½ tsp) fine salt
- **Tahini drizzle**: 40 g tahini; 30 ml (2 tbsp) lemon juice; 30 ml (2 tbsp) cold water; 1 garlic clove, minced; fine salt
- Flat-leaf parsley, to garnish

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, cumin, coriander, sumac and salt into a paste. Coat cod fillets; marinate 20 minutes.
2. Whisk tahini drizzle ingredients until smooth. Set aside.
3. Preheat air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 3 minutes. Lightly oil the basket.
4. Place marinated fillets in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of one fillet.
5. Air fry 12–14 minutes.
6. **Pull at 62–64 °C (144–147 °F).** Ottolenghi's standard: fully cooked, flaking, with a caramelised spice coating.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Drizzle tahini over the top; garnish with parsley. Final serving temperature: **64–66 °C / 147–151 °F** (fully cooked, spiced, tahini-finished).

### 5. Marcus Samuelsson — Air-Fryer Berbere-Spiced Cod (Ethiopian-American tradition)
**Source**: Marcus Samuelsson, *The Soul of a New Cuisine: A Discovery of the Foods and Flavors of Africa* (John Wiley & Sons, 2006); adapted technique for air fryer  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marination) · **Cook**: 12–14 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skinless cod fillets, 180–200 g each
- 15 g (1 tbsp) *berbere* spice blend (*berbere* — Ethiopian chilli-spice blend containing chilli, fenugreek, coriander, ginger, black pepper, cardamom, clove and allspice)
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- 10 ml (2 tsp) fresh lemon juice
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- Fine salt
- Plain yoghurt (optional, to serve)

**Method**
1. Mix berbere, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic and a pinch of salt into a paste. Coat cod fillets generously; marinate at least 1 hour refrigerated.
2. Remove from fridge 15 minutes before cooking.
3. Preheat air fryer to 190 °C (375 °F) for 3 minutes. Lightly oil the basket.
4. Place marinated fillets in the basket without overlapping. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of one fillet.
5. Air fry at 190 °C for 12–14 minutes. The berbere will darken and the surface will be deeply fragrant.
6. **Pull at 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).** Samuelsson's standard for spiced white fish is fully cooked through — the bold spice marinade demands a fully opaque, flaking fish interior.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with yoghurt if desired to balance the heat. Final serving temperature: **65–67 °C / 149–153 °F** (fully cooked, boldly spiced, Ethiopian-American standard).
