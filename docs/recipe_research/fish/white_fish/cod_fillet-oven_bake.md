# Cod Fillet × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven-baked cod fillet is cooked by convective heat circulating around the fish in the oven, with radiant heat from the oven walls contributing secondary browning on any exposed surfaces. Cod's lean, flaky flesh — large, moist flakes separated by thin connective tissue layers — responds well to baking because the relatively gentle heat (compared to pan frying) preserves moisture content and allows the connective tissue to set without rupturing. Common baking approaches include naked fillets at high heat (200–220 °C for 10–15 minutes), crumb-or-herb-crusted fillets, Portuguese-style bacalhau with olive oil and potatoes, and en papillote preparations. The major risk with oven-baked cod is dryness: the lean flesh has no internal fat to compensate for evaporative moisture loss, so pull timing at the lower end of fully-cooked (62–65 °C) is critical. Carryover is 3–5 °C from a hot oven.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Baked Cod with Crispy Panko Crust (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *The Complete Light Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2016); americastestkitchen.com/recipes/8527-baked-cod  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cod fillets, 150–170 g each, 2–3 cm thick (skinless preferred for even crust adhesion)
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 3 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 g (1 cup) panko breadcrumbs
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter, melted
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) lemon zest
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) fresh lemon juice (to drizzle before serving)

**Method**
1. Adjust oven rack to the middle-upper position. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil; spray with cooking spray.
2. Pat cod dry; season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. Mix panko with melted butter, parsley, lemon zest and garlic. Press the mixture firmly onto the top (flesh side up) of each fillet, forming a 5 mm thick crust.
4. Place fillets on the prepared baking sheet.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of one fillet, under the panko crust, into the centre of the flesh.
6. Bake at 220 °C for 15–18 minutes until the panko crust is deep golden-brown and crisp.
7. **Pull at 62–64 °C (144–147 °F).** ATK's standard: the fish should flake easily when probed with a fork, with the large moist flakes intact, and no visible raw translucency.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Drizzle with lemon juice. Final serving temperature: **64–66 °C / 147–151 °F** (fully cooked, large moist flakes, crisp panko crust).

### 2. Rick Stein — Bacalhau à Gomes de Sá (Portuguese tradition)
**Source**: Rick Stein, *Rick Stein's Long Weekends* (BBC Books, 2016)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 24 h soaking (salt cod) + 20 min prep · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g salt cod (*bacalhau*) (*bacalhau* — Portuguese salt-dried cod, the most important ingredient in Portuguese cuisine; must be soaked 24–48 h in multiple changes of cold water to desalinate)
- 800 g waxy potatoes, boiled in their skins, peeled and sliced 5 mm thick
- 2 large white onions, thinly sliced
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 120 ml (½ cup) extra virgin olive oil
- 4 hard-boiled eggs, quartered
- 80 g pitted black olives
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- Freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Soak salt cod for 24–48 hours, changing water every 8 hours. Taste a small piece — it should be pleasantly salty, not overwhelmingly so. Drain; pat dry; break into large flakes, removing any bones and skin.
2. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Lightly oil a large, deep baking dish (*assadeira* — the traditional wide Portuguese earthenware dish).
3. Sauté onion and garlic in 60 ml of the olive oil over medium heat until softened and golden, about 10 minutes.
4. Layer half the potatoes in the baking dish. Add the cod flakes; top with sautéed onion-garlic mixture. Layer remaining potatoes over the top.
5. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest section of the layered assembly — angle it into the cod layer, not the potato, to read the fish temperature.
6. Drizzle remaining olive oil generously over the surface.
7. Bake 25–30 minutes until the top layer is golden and the mixture is piping hot throughout.
8. **Pull at 65–68 °C (149–154 °F).** The salt-cod standard is fully heated through — the fish has already been fully cooked and dehydrated during salting; the baking step reconstitutes and heats the assembled dish. The higher target temperature (versus fresh cod) ensures the potato layers are also fully cooked.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Scatter olives, quartered eggs and parsley over the surface. Finish with remaining olive oil. Final serving temperature: **68–70 °C / 154–158 °F** (hot throughout, Portuguese standard).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Baked Cod with Tahini and Sumac (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Simple* (Ebury Press, 2018)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cod fillets, 180–200 g each, skin-on
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground sumac (*sumac* — ground dried red berry with citric tang, a staple of Levantine cooking)
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **Tahini sauce**: 80 g tahini (*tahini* — raw sesame paste); 60 ml (¼ cup) cold water; 30 ml (2 tbsp) lemon juice; 1 garlic clove, minced; fine salt
- Sliced spring onions and roughly chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Whisk tahini sauce ingredients until smooth and pourable; add more water if too thick.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Line a baking tray with baking paper.
3. Toss cod fillets with olive oil, sumac, cumin, salt and pepper. Place skin-side down on the tray.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of one fillet into the centre of the flesh.
5. Bake 15–18 minutes until the flesh is opaque throughout and beginning to flake at the edges.
6. **Pull at 62–64 °C (144–147 °F).** Ottolenghi's standard: cod should be "just cooked — large flakes, moist, no translucent centre."
7. Rest 3 minutes. Drizzle tahini sauce over the top; scatter spring onions and parsley. Final serving temperature: **64–66 °C / 147–151 °F** (fully cooked, Middle Eastern spiced).

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Merluzzo al Forno con Pomodoro (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 cod or hake fillets, 180–200 g each, skin-on
- 400 g canned whole San Marzano tomatoes (*pomodori pelati*), crushed by hand
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra virgin olive oil
- 15 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) dried oregano
- Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Pinch of red chilli flakes

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Oil a medium baking dish.
2. Combine crushed tomatoes, garlic, 45 ml of the olive oil, oregano, chilli flakes and half the parsley; season with salt and pepper. Spread in the baking dish.
3. Season cod fillets on both sides with salt. Place skin-side down on top of the tomato mixture.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of one fillet into the centre of the flesh.
5. Drizzle remaining olive oil over the top.
6. Bake 20–25 minutes, basting with the tomato sauce once or twice.
7. **Pull at 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).** Hazan's Italian baked fish standard: fully cooked, moist, the sauce having penetrated the surface of the flesh with a gentle acidity from the tomatoes.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Scatter remaining parsley. Serve directly from the dish. Final serving temperature: **65–68 °C / 149–154 °F** (fully cooked, Italian standard).

### 5. Donna Hay — Lemon-Herb Baked Cod (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *Fresh and Light* (HarperCollins Australia, 2012)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 15–18 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 skinless cod fillets, 170–200 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 10 g (2 tsp) finely grated lemon zest
- 10 g fresh thyme leaves, stripped
- 10 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- Steamed greens and lemon wedges, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Line a baking tray with baking paper.
2. Mix olive oil, garlic, lemon zest, thyme and parsley into a paste. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Pat cod dry. Place on the tray; spread herb paste evenly over the top surface.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of one fillet.
5. Bake 15–18 minutes. The herb paste will caramelise lightly on the surface.
6. **Pull at 62 °C (144 °F).** Hay's standard: "just cooked through — the fish should flake cleanly but still be moist and have no dry edges."
7. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with steamed greens. Final serving temperature: **64–66 °C / 147–151 °F** (fully cooked, fragrant with herbs and lemon).
