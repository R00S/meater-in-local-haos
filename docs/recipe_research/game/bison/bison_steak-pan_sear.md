## Cut profile

Bison (*Bison bison*) steak — most commonly cut from the ribeye, loin, or top sirloin — is leaner than beef but similarly structured, with a deep ruby colour and a slightly sweet, earthy flavour. Because bison has very little intramuscular fat it overcooks rapidly; pan-searing over high heat to medium-rare and resting is the standard approach.

Target internal temperature: **54–57 °C (130–135 °F)** for medium-rare. Pull at 52 °C (126 °F) to account for carry-over. Well-done is strongly discouraged: above 70 °C the meat becomes dry and tough.

The European tradition — particularly German and Swiss cooks who receive bison via specialist game suppliers — finishes pan-seared bison at 80 °C oven temperature after an initial sear (the "low-and-slow after sear" method). North American tradition is typically direct high-heat pan only.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Bisonsteak mit Büffelmozzarella auf Wildpilzgemüse (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2619231411385428/Bisonsteak-mit-Bueffelmozzarella-auf-Wildpilzgemuese.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Translation from German: Bison steak with buffalo mozzarella on wild mushroom vegetables.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 bison steaks
- 2 buffalo mozzarella, sliced
- 200 ml game stock (Wildfond)
- 500 g wild mushrooms (chanterelles, porcini, oyster mushrooms, or button)
- 2 tbsp clarified butter (Butterschmalz)
- 1 tbsp oil
- 2 tbsp dried parsley
- 4 shallots, finely chopped

**Method**
1. Preheat the oven with an ovenproof dish to 80 °C.
2. Heat the oil in a frying pan until very hot. Sear the steaks hard on each side (about 2 min per side), then transfer to the ovenproof dish and top with mozzarella slices.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of one steak. Place in the oven.
4. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F)** for medium; the low oven means very gentle carry-over.
5. While the steaks rest, clean and dice the shallots and mushrooms. Melt the clarified butter in the frying pan and sauté mushrooms and shallots until golden. Deglaze with game stock and reduce slightly.
6. Season with salt, pepper, and parsley. Serve steaks on the mushroom bed with e.g. rösti.
*Final resting temperature: 57–59 °C (135–138 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Bisonsteak in Rosmarin (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2841471436190764/Bisonsteak-in-Rosmarin.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 40 min

*Translation: Bison steak with rosemary, 80 °C slow method.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 bison steaks
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
- Salt and black pepper
- Oil or clarified butter for searing

**Method**
1. Pat the steaks dry and season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil in a cast-iron pan over high heat until smoking. Sear each side for 2–3 minutes.
3. Transfer steaks to an ovenproof dish with rosemary sprigs. Insert probe.
4. Roast at 80 °C for 30–40 min until probe reads **54 °C (130 °F)** for medium.
5. Rest 5 minutes. The slow oven finish gives exceptionally even doneness throughout.
*Final resting temperature: 56–58 °C (133–136 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Bisonsteak auf Paprika (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2841491436191027/Bisonsteak-auf-Paprika.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 40 min

*Translation: Bison steak on a bed of bell peppers, 80 °C oven finish.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 bison steaks (approx. 250 g each)
- 2 bell peppers (mixed colours), sliced
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic
- Oil for searing
- Salt, pepper, paprika spice

**Method**
1. Sauté the pepper and onion slices with garlic in oil until softened. Season with paprika, salt, pepper. Spread in a baking dish.
2. Sear bison steaks in a hot pan 2 min per side. Place on top of the peppers.
3. Insert probe. Oven at 80 °C for 30–40 min until **54 °C (130 °F)**.
4. Rest and serve on the pepper bed.
*Final resting temperature: 56–58 °C (133–136 °F).*

### 4. Daskochrezept.de — Gegrilltes Bison Filet Steak mit süß-scharfer Bier-Chili-Marinade (German/Austrian tradition)
**Source**: Daskochrezept.de; https://www.daskochrezept.de/rezepte/gegrilltes-bison-filet-steak-suess-scharfer-bier-chili-marinade (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 hours marinade · **Cook**: 20 min

*Translation: Grilled bison fillet steak with sweet-hot beer and chilli marinade.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 bison fillet steaks (approx. 200 g each)
- 200 ml dark beer
- 2 tbsp honey
- 1 tbsp chilli flakes
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper
- Oil for the pan/grill

**Method**
1. Combine beer, honey, chilli flakes, garlic, and smoked paprika to make the marinade. Submerge steaks and marinate for at least 2 hours.
2. Remove from marinade, pat dry. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Heat a heavy frying pan or grill plate over high heat until very hot. Sear steaks 2–3 min per side for a caramelised crust.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F)** for medium-rare. Rest 5 minutes.
5. Serve with the reduced marinade as a glaze if desired.
*Final resting temperature: 56–58 °C (133–136 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Doneness | Pull temp | Final (after rest) |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Medium-rare | 52–54 °C (126–130 °F) | 56–58 °C (133–136 °F) |
| Medium | 57–60 °C (135–140 °F) | 60–63 °C (140–145 °F) |
| Well-done | Not recommended for bison | — |

*Note: Bison has lower fat content than beef. Always prefer medium-rare to medium.*
