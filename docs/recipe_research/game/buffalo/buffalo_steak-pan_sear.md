## Cut profile

Pan-seared water buffalo (carabeef) steak is traditional across South and Southeast Asia. In the Philippines the classic *bistek* preparation uses soy sauce and citrus; in India it is marinated in yoghurt and spices; in Vietnam it is seasoned with lemongrass and fish sauce. The meat is leaner than beef and cooks slightly faster; high heat and brief searing is standard.

Target internal temperature: **60–65 °C (140–149 °F)** for medium.

## Source recipes

### 1. Panlasang Pinoy — Bistek Tagalog (Filipino beef/carabeef steak)
**Source**: Panlasang Pinoy (Vanjo Merano); https://panlasangpinoy.com/bistek-tagalog-filipino-beefsteak-recipe/ (web search 2026-04-24). *Classic Filipino beef steak — directly applicable to carabeef/water buffalo.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinate · **Cook**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g water buffalo or beef sirloin, thinly sliced (5 mm)
- 5 tbsp soy sauce
- 4 tbsp calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
- 1 medium onion, sliced into rings
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- 2 tbsp cooking oil

**Method**
1. Combine soy sauce, calamansi juice, and pepper. Marinate beef slices 1 hour.
2. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Sauté garlic briefly.
3. Add meat (reserve marinade). Pan-fry until browned on both sides — approx. 2 minutes per side.
4. Add reserved marinade. Simmer 5–8 minutes.
5. Add onion rings in the last 2 minutes (for crunchier onion) or at the beginning (for softer).
6. Insert probe. **Check 60–63 °C (140–145 °F)** for medium.
7. Serve over steamed white rice.
*Final temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Bisonbraten (German oven-pot tradition — also applies to buffalo)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/234021095857919/Bisonbraten.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 45–60 min

*Translation: Bison/Buffalo roast with juniper, cardamom, cloves, thyme — braised in red wine and stock. Also applicable as a pan braise for steak portions.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg bison or buffalo roast (or 4 thick steaks)
- Salt, pepper, thyme, rosemary
- 1 onion, roughly chopped
- 2 garlic cloves
- 2 cardamom pods, crushed
- 5 juniper berries
- 3 cloves
- 500 ml meat stock
- 200 ml red wine
- Olive oil

**Method**
1. Pat meat dry. Season with thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy pan (or Bräter) and sear the meat on all sides over high heat.
3. Add onion and garlic; sauté briefly. Pour over wine and stock. Add cardamom, juniper, and cloves.
4. Cover and cook on low heat (or in oven at 200 °C) for 45–60 minutes, basting occasionally.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 60 °C (140 °F)** for medium (steak portions: shorter time; roast: full time).
6. Strain sauce; season and thicken if desired. Slice thinly.
7. Serve with potato dumplings or noodles.
*Final temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*

### 3. IndianHealthyRecipes.com — Pepper Beef/Buffalo Fry (Kali Mirch) — Indian tradition
**Source**: IndianHealthyRecipes.com; https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/pepper-beef-fry/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Kerala-style pepper buffalo/beef pan fry.*

**Ingredients**
- 500 g buffalo steak, cut into strips or 150 g steaks
- 2 tbsp oil
- 1 large onion, thinly sliced
- 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1½ tsp black pepper (freshly ground, generous)
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- Salt to taste
- Curry leaves

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wok or heavy pan over high heat. Fry sliced onion until golden.
2. Add ginger-garlic paste; cook 1 minute. Add all spices and curry leaves; stir 30 seconds.
3. Add buffalo meat. Sear over high heat 5 minutes, stirring.
4. Add 2 tbsp water, cover, cook on medium heat 15–20 minutes until cooked through.
5. Insert probe. **Pull at 72–75 °C (162–167 °F)** for this braised/sautéed method.
6. Serve with Kerala parotta (layered flatbread) or steamed rice.
*Final temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*
