## Cut profile

Whole roasted goat leg is a festive preparation in Germany (Ziegenkeule), Greece (katsiki sto fourno), the Caribbean, South and West Africa, and across the Middle East. The meat has moderate fat and benefits from marinating (especially in buttermilk, yoghurt, or wine) to reduce gaminess. Slow oven roasting at 160–200 °C for 2–3 hours gives the best result.

Target internal temperature: **75–82 °C (167–180 °F)** for fully cooked, moist goat.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Ziegenkeule im Römertopf (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/45381015326785/Ziegenkeule-im-Roemertopf.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Rated 4.3/5.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 72 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 2 h 30 min

*Translation: Goat leg in a clay roaster (Römertopf) — 3-day buttermilk marinade to tenderise, then roasted in a sealed clay pot. The clay pot seams in all the moisture.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg goat leg meat (Ziegenkeule)
- Buttermilk (enough to submerge the leg)
- 2 tsp sweet paprika powder
- ½ tsp rosemary
- 2 garlic cloves, pressed
- 2 tbsp sharp mustard
- 1 bunch soup vegetables (carrot, celery, leek, parsley)
- 250 ml meat stock
- 1 tbsp cornflour
- 150 ml single cream
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Submerge the goat leg in buttermilk in a sealed container. Refrigerate for 3 days, turning occasionally.
2. Remove from buttermilk and pat dry. Season with salt, pepper, garlic, rosemary, paprika, and mustard. Rub all over.
3. Place the goat leg in a pre-soaked Römertopf (clay roaster). Surround with chopped soup vegetables and pour in the meat stock.
4. Cover the Römertopf and place in a cold oven. Set to 200 °C and bake for 2–2.5 hours.
5. Remove the goat. Insert MEATER probe. **Verify 80 °C (176 °F)** for a fully tender result.
6. Strain braising juices. Bring to the boil with cream, then thicken with cornflour mixed in cold water.
7. Serve with boiled potatoes and the cream sauce.
*Final temperature: 82–85 °C (180–185 °F).*

### 2. African Bites — Asun Goat (Nigerian roasted leg tradition)
**Source**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/asun-spicy-roast-goat/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min

*Asun: West African oven-roasted goat, traditionally made by the Yoruba people of Nigeria. The leg is scored, spiced, and roasted until charred outside and tender inside.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg goat leg (bone-in)
- 1 tbsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 scotch bonnet peppers, blended
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 onion, sliced

**Method**
1. Score the goat leg deeply. Mix spices, oil, and garlic; rub into all cuts.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Roast uncovered 1 hour.
3. Add scotch bonnet blend and sliced onion. Toss, reduce oven to 170 °C, roast 30 more minutes.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 80–82 °C (176–180 °F)** when tender.
5. Serve hot with toothpicks as party appetiser, or with jollof rice.
*Final temperature: 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).*

### 3. Laylita.com — Seco de Chivo (Ecuadorian goat leg braised whole)
**Source**: Laylita.com; https://www.laylita.com/recipes/seco-de-chivo/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 2 hrs

*Translation: Slow-braised goat in chicha de jora (corn beer) and achiote — a festive Ecuadorian dish.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg goat leg (bone-in, whole or cut into large pieces)
- 500 ml chicha de jora or pale beer
- 4 tomatoes, chopped
- 1 red onion, chopped
- 4 garlic cloves
- 1 tsp achiote
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 bunch fresh coriander
- Salt and pepper
- 3 tbsp oil

**Method**
1. Sear goat in a large, heavy casserole or oven pot in oil over high heat. Season with salt, cumin, and achiote.
2. Add onion, garlic, tomatoes, and beer. Cover and braise in oven at 160 °C for 2 hours.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 82 °C (180 °F)** when meat is falling off the bone.
4. Garnish with coriander. Serve with white rice, patacones, and avocado.
*Final temperature: 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).*
