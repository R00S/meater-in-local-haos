# Goat Shank × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Braising is the definitive method for goat shank — the only method that reliably converts its dense collagen to gelatin. The shank goes into a braising vessel with aromatic vegetables, liquid (stock, wine, beer, or water), and spices covering the lower two-thirds of the shank. Covered, low oven (160–170 °C / 325 °F) for 2.5–3 hours; or stovetop simmer; or slow cooker on LOW for 6–8 hours.

Target: 88–93 °C internal AND sustained time at that temperature — temperature alone is insufficient; the collagen needs 1–1.5 hours at 88 °C or above to fully hydrolyse. MEATER probe horizontally through the thickest muscle, parallel to the bone. The shank is done when the meat pulls away from the bone easily; the braising liquid will have concentrated into a rich, gelatin-thickened sauce.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Slow-Braised Goat Shanks with Pomegranate & Cumin (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil for searing
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 30 g fresh ginger, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin + 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander + 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon + 2 g (½ tsp) ground allspice
- 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 200 ml pomegranate molasses
- 400 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 2 cinnamon sticks
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme; 4 sprigs fresh oregano
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley + 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish
- **To serve**: warm flatbreads, tahini sauce

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season generously.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear shanks on all sides until deeply browned, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions until soft (8 minutes). Add garlic and ginger; cook 1 minute. Stir in spices and paprika; cook 30 seconds.
4. Add pomegranate molasses and stock. Return shanks (mostly submerged); add bay, cinnamon sticks, thyme and oregano.
5. Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank, parallel to the bone.
6. Braise 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes; the meat should reach 88–93 °C internal in the first hour and rest there for the remaining time.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides into the muscle without resistance — collagen has fully gelatinised. The internal temperature alone is necessary but not sufficient; time at temperature is the decisive variable.
8. Rest 15 minutes uncovered in the braising liquid. Skim fat. Strew with parsley and pomegranate seeds. Serve with flatbreads and tahini. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (fork-tender, deeply spiced, jewel-bright).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Långbräserade Getlägg med Rödvin & Rotfrukter (Swedish/Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009) — getlägg adaptation; köket.se — viltsektionen  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml rapsolja for searing
- 2 large gula lökar, finely chopped
- 1 large morot + 1 palsternacka, diced
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) tomatpuré
- 1 bottle (750 ml) torrt rödvin
- 400 ml kalvfond
- 4 fresh bay leaves
- 4 enbär (juniper berries), crushed
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground kryddpeppar
- 4 sprigs fresh timjan; 4 sprigs fresh rosmarin
- 30 g chopped fresh persilja, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season generously.
2. Heat rapsolja in a heavy gjutjärnsgryta over medium-high heat. Sear shanks all over, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions, carrot and parsnip until soft (8 minutes). Add garlic and tomato purée; cook 1 minute.
4. Add red wine; reduce by half (10 minutes). Add stock, bay, juniper, allspice, thyme and rosemary.
5. Return shanks (mostly submerged). Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Braise 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides through without resistance — Nordström's standard for getlägg.
8. Rest 15 minutes in liquid. Skim fat. Strew with parsley. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (juniper-perfumed, deeply savoury, fork-tender).

### 3. Diana Henry — Slow-Braised Goat Shanks with Red Wine & Rosemary (British/Mediterranean tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* — companion *Roast Figs, Sugar Snow* technique adapted in *How to Eat a Peach* (Mitchell Beazley, 2018); telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil for searing
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 1 large carrot, diced; 1 celery stick, diced
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g tomato purée
- 750 ml dry red wine (Chianti)
- 400 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 4 sprigs fresh rosemary; 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- Zest of 1 orange (in long strips); 2 anchovy fillets (Henry's secret umami booster)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear shanks on all sides, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions, carrot and celery until soft (8 minutes). Add garlic, tomato purée and anchovies; cook until anchovies dissolve.
4. Add wine; reduce by half (10 minutes). Add stock, bay, rosemary, thyme and orange zest.
5. Return shanks (mostly submerged). Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Braise 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides through — Henry's standard for slow-braised goat shanks.
8. Rest 15 minutes in liquid. Skim fat. Discard rosemary stems and orange peel. Serve with creamy polenta. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (red-wine-rich, orange-bright, fork-tender).

### 4. Maricel Presilla — Cabrito Estofado / Slow-Braised Goat Shanks (Latin American tradition)
**Source**: Maricel E. Presilla, *Gran Cocina Latina: The Food of Latin America* (W. W. Norton, 2012); mariselpresilla.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hours · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goat shanks (about 400 g each)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil for searing
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 8 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 30 g fresh oregano leaves; 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin; 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked pimentón
- 2 chipotles in adobo, finely chopped
- 1 (400 g) tin chopped tomatoes
- 400 ml dry white wine
- 400 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves
- 30 g chopped fresh coriander, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shanks dry; season.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear shanks on all sides, 3 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions until soft (8 minutes). Add garlic, oregano, cumin and pimentón; cook 1 minute. Stir in chipotles.
4. Add tomatoes, wine and stock. Add bay. Return shanks (mostly submerged). Bring to a simmer.
5. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the largest shank.
6. Braise 3.5 hours, basting every 45 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork slides through — Presilla's standard for cabrito estofado.
8. Rest 15 minutes in liquid. Skim fat. Strew with coriander. Serve with arroz blanco. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (chipotle-smoky, citrus-bright, fork-tender).
