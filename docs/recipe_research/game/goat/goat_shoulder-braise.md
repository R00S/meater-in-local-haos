# Goat Shoulder × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Goat shoulder (Swedish *getskuldra* / *getbog*) is the upper foreleg — heavily worked, dense with collagen and intramuscular fat. Like the lamb shoulder, it is a quintessentially low-and-slow cut: tough beyond chewing if cooked to medium-rare, but transformed by long, wet cooking into fork-tender, gelatin-rich meat that pulls in long strands. Internal temperature is necessary but insufficient: the cut needs time at 88–93 °C internal for 2–3 hours after reaching that temperature. Probe horizontally through the thickest part of the muscle, away from bone or large connective sheets.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Slow-Braised Goat Shoulder with Pomegranate, Cumin & Walnuts (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 5 hours · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil
- 3 large onions, finely chopped
- 8 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 60 g fresh ginger, finely grated
- 10 g (2 tsp) ground cumin + 10 g (2 tsp) ground coriander + 5 g (1 tsp) ground cinnamon + 5 g (1 tsp) ground allspice + 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 200 ml pomegranate molasses
- 600 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 2 cinnamon sticks
- 100 g toasted walnuts, roughly chopped
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley + 60 g pomegranate seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shoulder dry; season generously.
2. Heat olive oil in a large heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder on all sides until deeply browned, 4 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic and ginger; cook 1 minute. Stir in spices and paprika; cook 30 seconds.
4. Add pomegranate molasses and stock. Return shoulder (mostly submerged). Add bay and cinnamon sticks.
5. Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of the muscle, away from bone.
6. Braise 5 hours, basting every hour. Internal should reach 88–93 °C in the first 1.5 hours and rest there for the remaining time.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when the meat pulls apart with a fork without resistance.
8. Rest 20 minutes uncovered in liquid. Pull meat into chunks. Skim fat from sauce. Strew meat with walnuts, parsley and pomegranate seeds. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (deeply spiced, walnut-rich, jewel-bright, fork-tender).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Långbräserad Getskuldra med Rödvin (Swedish/Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se — viltsektionen  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 5 hours · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml rapsolja
- 3 large gula lökar, finely chopped
- 2 large morötter + 2 palsternacka, diced
- 8 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) tomatpuré
- 1 bottle (750 ml) torrt rödvin + 600 ml kalvfond
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 6 enbär crushed; 5 g (1 tsp) ground kryddpeppar
- 6 sprigs fresh timjan + 6 sprigs fresh rosmarin
- 30 g chopped fresh persilja, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shoulder dry; season.
2. Heat rapsolja in a heavy gjutjärnsgryta over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder all over, 4 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions, carrots and parsnips until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic and tomato purée; cook 1 minute.
4. Add wine; reduce by half (15 minutes). Add stock, bay, juniper, allspice, thyme and rosemary.
5. Return shoulder (mostly submerged). Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into thickest part of muscle.
6. Braise 5 hours, basting every hour.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when the meat pulls apart with a fork — Nordström's standard.
8. Rest 20 minutes in liquid. Pull meat into chunks. Skim fat. Strew with parsley. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (juniper-perfumed, fork-tender).

### 3. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Slow-Braised Goat Shoulder with Cider (British River Cottage tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *The River Cottage Meat Book* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2004); rivercottage.net  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 5 hours · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml rapeseed oil
- 3 large onions, sliced
- 2 large carrots, diced; 8 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 g (2 tbsp) tomato purée
- 750 ml dry cider + 400 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves; 6 sprigs fresh thyme; 8 sage leaves
- 30 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shoulder dry; season.
2. Heat rapeseed oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder all over, 4 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions and carrots until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic and tomato purée; cook 1 minute.
4. Add cider; reduce by half (15 minutes). Add stock, bay, thyme and sage.
5. Return shoulder (mostly submerged). Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into thickest part of muscle.
6. Braise 5 hours, basting every hour.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when a fork pulls the meat apart — Fearnley-Whittingstall's standard.
8. Rest 20 minutes. Pull meat into chunks. Skim fat. Strew with parsley. Serve with creamy mash. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (cider-mellow, sage-perfumed, fork-tender).

### 4. Maricel Presilla — Cabrito en Salsa Verde / Slow-Braised Goat Shoulder (Latin American tradition)
**Source**: Maricel E. Presilla, *Gran Cocina Latina: The Food of Latin America* (W. W. Norton, 2012)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 5 hours · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 bone-in goat shoulder (2.5–3 kg)
- 10 g (2 tsp) salt; 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil
- 3 large onions, finely chopped; 10 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 30 g fresh oregano leaves; 10 g (2 tsp) ground cumin
- **Salsa verde base**: 500 g tomatillos (husked, blackened in a pan) + 4 jalapeños + 1 large bunch coriander + 30 ml lime juice — blended
- 600 ml chicken stock
- 4 fresh bay leaves
- 30 g chopped fresh coriander, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat shoulder dry; season.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear shoulder all over, 4 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Reduce to medium; sweat onions until soft (10 minutes). Add garlic, oregano and cumin; cook 1 minute. Stir in salsa verde; cook 5 minutes.
4. Add chicken stock and bay. Return shoulder (mostly submerged). Bring to a simmer. Cover; transfer to oven. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest part of muscle.
5. Braise 5 hours, basting every hour.
6. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F)** AND when the meat pulls apart with a fork — Presilla's standard for cabrito en salsa verde.
7. Rest 20 minutes. Pull meat into chunks. Skim fat. Strew with coriander. Serve with arroz blanco and warm tortillas. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (tomatillo-bright, jalapeño-warm, fork-tender).
