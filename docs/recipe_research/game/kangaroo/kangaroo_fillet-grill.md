# Kangaroo Fillet × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Grilling the kangaroo fillet (backstrap) requires maximum heat and minimum time. The extreme leanness means the grill grates must be oiled and preheated to the highest possible temperature before the fillet is placed on them — a moderately warm grill will stew the surface before browning occurs. Direct, high-heat charcoal or gas grilling at 230–260°C / 450–500°F at grate level produces the most intense Maillard crust and the distinctive charred-exterior character prized in Australian outdoor cooking. Over charcoal, the aromatic smoke contribution is subtle but adds complexity to the gamey, iron-rich flavour. Grill time is 2–3 minutes per side maximum; probe insertion parallel to the fillet into its thickest part gives the most reliable reading. The fillet continues cooking briefly after removal from the grill, but carryover is very small (2–3°C / 4–5°F) due to the thin, lean profile. Rest 5 minutes covered.

## Styckesprofil

Grillning av känguru-filé (ryggsträng) kräver maximal värme och minimal tid. Den extrema magreten (under 1,5 % fett) innebär att grillgallret måste oljas och förvärmas till högsta möjliga temperatur — ett måttligt varmt galler ångkokar ytan innan brynning sker.

Direkt hög kol- eller gasgrillning vid 230–260 °C vid gallernivå ger den intensivaste Maillard-skorpan. Tillagningstid: max 2–3 minuter per sida; sond parallellt med filén in i den tjockaste delen. Vila 5 minuter täckt. Innertemperatur: **52–55 °C (126–131 °F)** för medium-rare. Eftertillagning: 2–3 °C på grund av den tunna, magra profilen.
