## Cut profile

Kangaroo (*Macropus* spp.) steak, most commonly from the hindquarter/rump or loin fillet, is one of the leanest red meats available — typically less than 2% fat. The meat is deep crimson, with a slightly gamy, iron-rich flavour. Because of its extreme leanness, kangaroo must be cooked hot and fast to prevent drying: pan-searing for 2–3 minutes per side over high heat achieves a seared crust while keeping the interior rare to medium-rare. Cooking beyond medium results in a dry, liver-textured product.

Target internal temperature: **54–57 °C (130–135 °F)** for medium-rare. Many Australian cooks pull at 50–52 °C for a warm-red centre. Well-done is strongly discouraged.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Kängurusteak in Pfeffermarinade und Pfeffersahnesauce (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3881521590591139/Kaengurusteak-in-Pfeffermarinade-und-Pfeffersahnesauce.html (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 45 min marinade · **Cook**: 20 min

*Translation from German: Kangaroo steak in pepper marinade with pepper cream sauce, served with rosemary potato wedges and garlic-butter beans.*

**Ingredients**

*Steaks:*
- 2 kangaroo steaks or fillet slices, approx. 150 g each
- 1 tbsp olive oil (for rubbing)
- 2 level tsp ground black pepper
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil (for frying)

*Rosemary potato wedges:*
- 4 waxy potatoes, cut into wedges (approx. 24 wedges)
- 1 tbsp clarified butter
- 1 heaped tsp dried rosemary needles

*Garlic-butter beans:*
- 1 rasher smoked bacon, finely diced
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 1 tin fine French beans (200–350 g), drained
- 2 garlic cloves, finely sliced
- A little olive oil
- 1 tbsp butter

*Pepper cream sauce:*
- 6 tbsp cream
- 1 red chilli, sliced
- 1 green pointed pepper (Dolma), sliced
- 1 heaped tbsp pickled Indian green peppercorns
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Rub the kangaroo steaks with olive oil and press black pepper firmly into both sides. Leave to marinate in a bowl at room temperature for 45 minutes (do not refrigerate).
2. Prepare the potato wedges: in a frying pan with clarified butter, fry over medium heat, turning often. After half the cooking time add the rosemary and remaining onion. Continue until golden.
3. Render the bacon in a separate pan; add butter, half the onion, drained beans, and sliced garlic. Fry gently until tender.
4. For the steaks: heat olive oil in a third pan over gentle-to-medium heat. Pan-fry steaks **3 minutes per side** — not high heat, as the lean meat needs gentler treatment.
5. Add cream, chilli rings, pepper strips, and green peppercorns to the steak pan. Simmer gently at low heat, turning steaks once or twice.
6. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest steak. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F)** for medium-rare.
7. Season everything with salt and pepper. Plate steaks, wedges, and beans together; spoon pepper sauce over the steak.
*Final resting temperature: 56–58 °C (133–136 °F).*

### 2. Game Meat Australia / national tradition — Simple pan-seared kangaroo fillet
**Source**: Australian Government recommended guidelines and national kangaroo industry standards; referenced via Kangaroo Industry Association of Australia (KIAA) cooking guides. See also: https://www.kangameat.com.au/cooking-kangaroo/
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min

*Standard approach recommended across Australian restaurants and game meat suppliers.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 kangaroo rump or loin fillets (approx. 180–200 g each)
- 2 tbsp macadamia or olive oil
- Salt and cracked black pepper
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 1 garlic clove, lightly crushed

**Method**
1. Remove kangaroo from the fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Pat dry.
2. Season generously with salt and cracked pepper on both sides.
3. Heat a heavy-based pan (cast iron preferred) over high heat until nearly smoking. Add oil.
4. Lay fillets in the pan. Do not move. Sear for **2–3 minutes** on the first side until a deep brown crust forms. Add rosemary and garlic to the pan alongside.
5. Flip and cook **2 minutes** on the second side, basting with any pan juices.
6. Insert probe. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F)** for a warm red centre (Australian preference is rarer than European), or 54 °C for medium-rare.
7. Remove from pan, rest on a warm plate for **5 minutes** (mandatory — kangaroo tightens immediately if not rested).
8. Slice against the grain. Serve with bush tomato chutney and seasonal vegetables.
*Final resting temperature: 55–58 °C (131–136 °F).*

### 3. Meatsmith (Melbourne) — Kangaroo fillet with native pepper berry
**Source**: Meatsmith butcher Melbourne cooking guide; https://www.meatsmith.com.au/blogs/recipes (referenced in national food press)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 kangaroo fillets (backstrap/loin), approx. 160 g each
- 1 tbsp native pepper berry, lightly crushed
- 1 tbsp sea salt flakes
- 2 tbsp macadamia oil
- 50 g butter, for basting
- Lemon myrtle leaves, optional garnish

**Method**
1. Mix pepper berry and salt flakes. Press onto both sides of fillets.
2. Heat oil in a heavy pan over high heat. Sear fillets 2 minutes per side.
3. Add butter to the pan and baste the fillets for 1 additional minute.
4. **Pull at 54 °C (130 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 4–5 minutes before slicing.
*Final resting temperature: 56–58 °C (133–136 °F).*

| Doneness | Pull temp | Final (after rest) |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Rare | 48–50 °C (118–122 °F) | 50–53 °C (122–127 °F) |
| Medium-rare | 52–54 °C (126–130 °F) | 55–58 °C (131–136 °F) |
| Medium | **Not recommended** | — |
| Well-done | **Strongly discouraged** | — |

*Kangaroo is extremely lean. Australian and German recipes consistently emphasise rare to medium-rare only.*
