## Cut profile

Moose steak (Elchsteak / älgbiff) is cut from the loin or haunch. It is very lean with a rich, earthy flavour. Grilling over high heat for a short time is the traditional Nordic approach; the meat should be seared quickly and pulled at medium-rare to prevent dryness. Marinating in oil and acidic components (wine, vinegar, lemon) before grilling is standard practice.

Target internal temperature: **57–62 °C (135–144 °F)** for medium-rare to medium.

## Source recipes

### 1. Tasteline.com — Grillad älgbiff (Swedish grilled moose steak)
**Source**: Tasteline.com; https://www.tasteline.com/ingrediens/alg/ (fetched 2026-04-24; moose steak temperature guidance 58–70 °C)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 30 min marinade · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 moose loin steaks, approx. 180 g each
- 3 tbsp rapeseed oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- Salt and coarse black pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, garlic, thyme, and vinegar. Coat steaks and marinate 30 minutes at room temperature.
2. Preheat gas or charcoal grill to high. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Grill 3–4 minutes per side over direct heat.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with grilled vegetables and lingonberry sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 61–64 °C (142–147 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Elchsteak vom Grill (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de recipe search results for Elchsteak; https://www.chefkoch.de/rs/s0/elch/Rezepte.html (fetched reference 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 2 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 15 min

*Translation: Grilled moose steak, German-Scandinavian tradition.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 moose steaks (approx. 200 g each)
- 4 tbsp rapeseed oil
- 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp gin (or dry vermouth)
- 4 juniper berries, crushed
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, soy, gin, juniper, and paprika. Marinate steaks 2 hours.
2. Hot grill, direct heat. Sear 3–4 min per side.
3. Move to indirect heat, continue until probe reads **60 °C (140 °F)**.
4. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with potato wedges and lingonberry jam.
*Final resting temperature: 63–65 °C (145–149 °F).*

### 3. Matprat.no — Grillet elgbiff (Norwegian BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Matprat.no (Norwegian Meat and Poultry Research Centre); https://www.matprat.no/oppskrifter/gjester/grillet-elgbiff/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinade · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 moose loin steaks, approx. 150 g each
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary
- 1 tbsp coarse salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- Cloudberry jam (*moltesyltetøy*) for serving

**Method**
1. Rub steaks with oil, rosemary, salt and pepper. Marinate 1 hour.
2. Very hot grill. Sear 2–3 min per side.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 60 °C (140 °F)** for medium (Norwegian preference).
4. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with cloudberry jam.
*Final resting temperature: 62–65 °C (144–149 °F).*
