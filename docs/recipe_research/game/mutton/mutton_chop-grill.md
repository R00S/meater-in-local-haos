# Mutton Chop × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilled mutton chops over medium-high direct heat (375-425°F / 190-220°C) is
the classic preparation in traditional British, South Asian (seekh-kabab-adjacent),
and Welsh culinary traditions. The strong flavour of mutton benefits from the
smokiness and char of an open grill. A marinade of yogurt + spices is common in
South Asian traditions; wine + herbs in European. Target: 60-65°C / 140-149°F
(medium) — the consensus doneness for mature sheep on the grill.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fergus Henderson — Grilled Mutton Cutlets (Beyond Nose to Tail)
- **Source**: Beyond Nose to Tail (Bloomsbury)
- **Method**: Season, grill 3-4 min per side at high heat, rest 5 min.
- **Target**: **60-65°C / 140-149°F**

### 2. Hank Shaw — Grilled Mutton Chops (honest-food.net)
- **Source**: honest-food.net
- **Method**: Spice rub + wine marinade 2 hours, high grill heat 3-4 min per side.
- **Target**: **135-145°F / 57-63°C** at centre

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled Mutton Chops with Za'atar (Jerusalem)
- **Source**: Jerusalem (Ten Speed Press)
- **Method**: Za'atar + olive oil + garlic, grill 3-4 min per side.
- **Target**: **57-63°C / 135-145°F**

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Grillat fårkött (Nordic Cookbook)
- **Source**: The Nordic Cookbook (Phaidon)
- **Method**: Simple salt + juniper, hot grill 4-5 min per side.
- **Target**: **60-65°C** (Swedish fårkött standard)

### 5. Welsh Mutton / BBC Wales tradition
- **Source**: bbc.co.uk/food recipes / Welsh food tradition
- **Method**: Mint + garlic marinade, grill 4 min per side.
- **Target**: **63-68°C** — Welsh tradition tends toward slightly more done for
  local taste preferences.

**Consensus: 60-65°C / 140-149°F (medium).** Maps to `medium` in mutton doneness.

## What makes this method special
Grilling brings out the wild, complex flavour of mutton in a way indoor methods
cannot — the fat drips create flare-up char that adds smoke to the already-intense
flavour. Mutton with grill char is a revelatory combination, especially with a
yogurt-based sauce or mint chutney to balance the richness.
