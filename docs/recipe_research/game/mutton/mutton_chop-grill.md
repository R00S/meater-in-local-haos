## Cut profile

Grilled mutton chops are popular throughout the Middle East, Pakistan, India, North Africa, and the UK (particularly as part of a traditional English grill). The strong flavour pairs well with spice marinades, char, and aromatic smoke. Common marinade bases: yoghurt (South Asian), harissa (North African), olive oil with herbs (Mediterranean), or dry rubs with cumin and paprika.

Target internal temperature: **70–74 °C (158–165 °F)**.

## Source recipes

### 1. Pakistani tradition — Mutton Chops Seekh (grilled marinated)
**Source**: Zaiqa.com (Pakistani recipe site); https://www.zaiqa.com/recipe/mutton-chops-grilled (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 2 hrs marinate · **Cook**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 8 mutton loin chops
- ½ cup plain yoghurt
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1 tsp red chilli powder
- 1 tsp cumin (roasted, ground)
- ½ tsp black pepper
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 2 tbsp oil
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Mix all marinade ingredients. Score chops and coat well. Marinate 2 hours minimum (overnight preferred).
2. Preheat charcoal grill or gas grill to medium-high.
3. Grill chops 5–7 minutes per side over direct heat.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 72 °C (162 °F)** for medium-well.
5. Serve with naan, mint chutney, and sliced onions.
*Final resting temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*

### 2. Madensverden.dk — Grillede lammekotelet (adapted for mutton — Danish tradition)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/grillede-lammekoteletter/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 6–8 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 12 min

*Applies equally to mutton chops — rosemary and garlic marinade.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 mutton chops (approx. 250 g each)
- ½ dl rapeseed oil
- 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, pressed
- 1 tsp salt
- Butter for serving

**Method**
1. Mix oil, rosemary, garlic, and salt. Pat chops dry and rub with marinade. Seal in a bag and refrigerate 6–8 hours.
2. Grill over direct medium-high heat, 3–4 minutes per side, turning every 1.5 minutes for a juicier result.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 72 °C (162 °F)** for medium-well.
4. Season with cracked pepper. Top with a knob of butter when serving.
5. Serve with bulgur salad and crusty bread.
*Final resting temperature: 73–76 °C (163–169 °F).*

### 3. African Bites — West African Grilled Mutton Chops (Nigerian tradition)
**Source**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/grilled-lamb-chops/ (fetched 2026-04-24 — also applies to mutton in West African cooking)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinate · **Cook**: 20 min

*Suya spice blend adapted for mutton chops — a West African grilling tradition.*

**Ingredients**
- 8 mutton chops
- 2 tbsp peanut oil
- 1 tsp suya spice (Yaji) or: 1 tsp each ground ginger, paprika, chilli, garlic, onion powder
- 1 tbsp groundnut (peanut) powder
- ½ tsp salt

**Method**
1. Mix oil and all spices. Coat chops and marinate 1 hour.
2. Hot charcoal grill. Grill 5–8 minutes per side.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 72–74 °C (162–165 °F)**.
4. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with suya rolls, onion, and tomato.
*Final resting temperature: 74–77 °C (165–171 °F).*
