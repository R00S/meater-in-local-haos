## Cut profile

Mutton shoulder from an older animal (12 months or more) is one of the best possible cuts for the slow cooker. The denser connective tissue and stronger flavour that make mutton challenging by quick methods become assets in the slow cooker: over 7–9 hours on LOW, the collagen fully dissolves, the fat renders into a rich braising liquid, and the shoulder yields an extraordinarily deep, concentrated flavour that younger lamb cannot match. Indian, Turkish, and Greek traditions all have centuries-old equivalents of the slow-cooked mutton shoulder dish; the slow cooker is simply the modern implementation. The MEATER probe is inserted into the thickest muscle of the shoulder, away from the shoulder blade.

Target internal temperature: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)** — mutton requires higher temperatures than lamb due to denser, older muscle fibres.

## Styckesprofil

Fåraxel från ett äldre djur (12+ månader) är ett av de bästa möjliga snitten för slow cooker. Den tätare bindväven och den starkare smaken som gör fårkött utmanande med snabba metoder blir tillgångar i slow cooker: under 7–9 timmar på LÅG löses kollagenet fullständigt, fettet smälter ned i en rik braiseringsvätska och axeln ger en extraordinärt djup, koncentrerad smak som yngre lamm inte kan matcha.

MEATER-sonden stickas in i den tjockaste muskeln i axeln, borta från skulderbladsben. Innertemperatur: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)** — fårkött kräver högre temperaturer än lamm på grund av tätare, äldre muskelfibrer. Eftertillagning: noll.

## Source recipes

### 1. Indian Healthy Recipes — Mutton Korma (slow cooker adaptation) (Indian / Mughlai tradition)
**Source**: Indian Healthy Recipes (Swasthi Shreekanth); https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/mutton-korma-recipe/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Mutton Korma — Mughlai-style braised mutton in yoghurt and spices, adapted for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 4–5 · **Prep**: 20 min + overnight marinate · **Cook**: 7 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe simmers on the stovetop for 1–1.5 hours at very low heat. Slow cooker LOW for 7 hours produces an equivalent or superior result.*

**Ingredients**

*Marinade:*
- 500 g bone-in mutton shoulder pieces
- 1 cup full-fat yoghurt
- 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1 tsp salt

*For the braise:*
- 2 tbsp ghee
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 3–4 green cardamoms, 1 black cardamom
- 4–5 cloves
- ½ tsp black peppercorns
- 2–3 green chilies, slit
- 1 cup browned or deep-fried onions (birista)

**Method**
1. Marinate mutton in yoghurt and spices overnight in the refrigerator.
2. In a frying pan, heat ghee and briefly fry whole spices (cinnamon, cardamoms, cloves, peppercorns) until fragrant, 30 seconds.
3. Add birista onions and stir well. Transfer spice-onion mixture to slow cooker.
4. Add marinated mutton with all the yoghurt marinade. Add green chilies.
5. Stir to combine. Cook on LOW for **7 hours**, stirring once or twice if accessible.
6. Insert MEATER probe before starting. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F)** when meat is very tender. Mutton may reach 92–95 °C during extended LOW cooking — this is acceptable.
7. Stir in a spoonful of ghee before serving. Garnish with fresh green chilies and coriander.
8. **Rest 5–10 min**. Serve with naan, roti, or basmati rice.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 2. Nefis Yemek Tarifleri — Kuzu Kol Tandır (slow cooker adaptation) (Turkish tradition)
**Source**: Nefis Yemek Tarifleri; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-kuzu-kol-tandir-11409971/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Fırında Kuzu Kol Tandır — Turkish tandır shoulder, adapted for the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8–9 hr (LOW)

*Slow-cooker adaptation: the original recipe wraps the shoulder in foil and bakes at 220 °C for 2.5 hours. For the slow cooker, place the rubbed shoulder directly in the pot with added liquid, LOW 8–9 hours.*

**Ingredients**
- 1 mutton shoulder, bone-in (~1.5 kg)
- 6–8 garlic cloves, whole
- 1 tsp coarse salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp Turkish baharat (mixed spice)
- Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, quartered
- 300 ml water or mutton/lamb stock

**Method**
1. Pierce shoulder all over; push garlic cloves into the cuts.
2. Rub shoulder thoroughly with salt, pepper, baharat, and olive oil.
3. Optional: brown in a hot frying pan on all sides for colour and deeper flavour.
4. Place onions on the base of the slow cooker as a trivet. Rest shoulder on top.
5. Pour stock/water around the base. Lay rosemary and thyme over the meat.
6. Cook on LOW for **8–9 hours**.
7. Insert MEATER probe before starting. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F)**.
8. **Rest 15–20 min**. Serve with rice pilav and yoghurt sauce.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Lammhaxen aus dem Slowcooker (shoulder adaptation) (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/4089241641380759/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Lammhaxen aus dem Slowcooker — German slow-cooker lamb, technique adapted for mutton shoulder."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7–8 hr (LOW)

*Mutton adaptation: the same slow-cooker technique used for lamb shanks adapts directly to mutton shoulder pieces. Extend cooking time to 7–8 hours for mutton.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.2–1.5 kg mutton shoulder, cut into large chunks or bone-in
- 3 carrots, roughly chopped
- 5 garlic cloves, halved
- 1 large onion, roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 400 ml lamb or beef stock
- 400 ml dry red wine
- 100 ml water
- Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Season mutton with salt and pepper. Brown on all sides in oil over high heat.
2. Sauté carrots, onion, and garlic in the same pan. Add tomato paste; stir 1 minute.
3. Deglaze with 200 ml red wine; scrape up browned bits.
4. Transfer vegetables and liquid to slow cooker. Add mutton pieces. Pour in remaining wine, stock, and water. Add herbs.
5. Cook on LOW for **7–8 hours**.
6. Insert MEATER probe before start. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F)**.
7. Strain and reduce cooking liquid for a sauce. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with mashed potatoes.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 4. Argiro.gr — Αρνάκι στο ταψί (slow cooker adaptation) (Greek tradition)
**Source**: Argiro Barbarigou; https://www.argiro.gr/recipe/arnaki-sto-tapsi-me-laxanika-kai-votana/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *"Αρνάκι στο ταψί με λαχανικά και βότανα — Greek tray-bake technique adapted for mutton shoulder in the slow cooker."*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 8 hr (LOW)

*Mutton adaptation: the Greek lamb tray-bake at 170 °C for 2 hours becomes a slow-cooker LOW 8-hour braise for mutton shoulder.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg mutton shoulder, bone-in, cut into large pieces
- 2 onions, roughly chopped
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 4 medium potatoes, halved
- 50 g butter
- 200 ml dry white wine
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Fresh thyme, oregano, rosemary
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Rub mutton pieces with mustard, salt, pepper, and herbs.
2. Brown on all sides in butter and olive oil over high heat.
3. Place potatoes, onions, garlic, and peppers in slow cooker. Add browned mutton pieces.
4. Pour wine over everything.
5. Cook on LOW for **8 hours**.
6. Insert MEATER probe before starting. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F)**.
7. **Rest 10 min**. Serve with crusty bread and a simple green salad.
*Final temperature: 90–92 °C (194–198 °F).*

## Temperature summary

| Stage | Target temp | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| USDA minimum — lamb/mutton | 63 °C (145 °F) | Not relevant for slow-cooked shoulder |
| Fall-off-the-bone (lamb) | 85–88 °C (185–190 °F) | Younger animal |
| Fall-off-the-bone (mutton) | 88–92 °C (190–198 °F) | Older animal, denser tissue |
| Extended low cook (Indian) | 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) | Yoghurt-braise, very long cook time |
