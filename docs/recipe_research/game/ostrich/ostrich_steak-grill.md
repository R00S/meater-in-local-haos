## Cut profile

Ostrich (*Struthio camelus*) steak is produced primarily in South Africa, Namibia, and increasingly in France, Belgium, and Germany. Despite being a bird, ostrich meat is red, very low in fat, and treated culinarily exactly like beef or venison. Fan fillet (outerfilé) and top loin are the main steak cuts. The meat is extremely lean and must not be overcooked: medium-rare (54–57 °C) is the South African standard. Above 70 °C the meat becomes tough and liver-like.

Target internal temperature: **54–57 °C (129–135 °F)** for medium-rare. Pull at 52 °C and rest.

## Source recipes

### 1. South African Braai tradition — Classic braaied ostrich steak
**Source**: KleinKaroo.co.za (ostrich farming region of South Africa); https://www.kleinkaroo.com/ostrich-steak-braai-recipe/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min marinate · **Cook**: 8 min

*The Klein Karoo region of South Africa is the world centre of ostrich farming. Braai = South African BBQ.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 ostrich fan fillet steaks, approx. 160 g each
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, lemon, garlic, and spices. Coat steaks and marinate 30 minutes.
2. Preheat braai/grill to very high. Oil grates.
3. Grill steaks **2–3 minutes per side** over direct heat. Do not press down.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with pap, chakalaka, and grilled mielies (corn on the cob).
*Final resting temperature: 54–57 °C (129–135 °F).*

### 2. Namibian Ostrich Festival tradition — Grilled ostrich with African chilli butter
**Source**: OstrichFarming.co.na (Namibia); https://www.ostrichfarming.co.na/recipes/grilled-ostrich-steak (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ostrich steaks
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil
- Salt and black pepper

*African chilli butter:*
- 100 g soft butter
- 1 red chilli, finely chopped
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh coriander
- Zest of ½ lime

**Method**
1. Mix butter, chilli, garlic, coriander, and lime zest. Form a log in clingfilm and refrigerate.
2. Season steaks with oil, salt, and pepper. Hot grill.
3. Grill 2–3 min per side. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)**.
4. Rest 4 minutes. Top with a slice of chilli butter.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*

### 3. Chefkoch.de — Straußensteak auf dem Grill (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rs/s0/strauss/Rezepte.html (fetched reference 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 2 hrs marinate · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 ostrich steaks (Straußensteaks)
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 1 sprig rosemary
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, vinegar, garlic, and rosemary. Marinate steaks 2 hours.
2. Hot grill. Season with salt and pepper before cooking.
3. Grill 2–3 min per side.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)**.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with roasted vegetables and mashed potato.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*
