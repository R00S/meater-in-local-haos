## Cut profile

Rabbit (*Oryctolagus cuniculus*) legs (front and hind) are the most commonly braised cut. The hind legs carry more meat and are the prime braising cut; the front legs are often included for flavour. Rabbit is lean, low in fat, and prone to drying if overcooked — braising in wine, stock, or cream keeps the meat moist while allowing the connective tissue to break down.

Target internal temperature: **74–77 °C (165–175 °F)** — cooked through, juicy, not dry.

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Roast Rabbit with Thyme (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-rabbit-thyme (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 50 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole rabbit, jointed (or 4 hind legs plus 2 front legs)
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- Juice and zest of 1 lemon
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 3 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- Salt and black pepper
- 150 ml white wine or chicken stock

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C (170 °C fan). Mix olive oil, lemon juice and zest, garlic, thyme, rosemary in a bowl.
2. Season rabbit pieces with salt and pepper. Toss in the herb oil until well coated.
3. Arrange in a single layer in a roasting tin. Pour wine/stock around (not over) the rabbit.
4. Roast uncovered for 40–50 minutes, turning pieces once at the halfway point, until golden and cooked through.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the largest hind leg. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with roasted potatoes and a dressed green salad.
*Final resting temperature: 76–78 °C (169–172 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Kaninchenkeulen aus dem Ofen mit Kräuterkruste (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de oven rabbit recipes; https://www.chefkoch.de/rs/s0/kaninchen+ofen/Rezepte.html (fetched reference 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min + 2 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 50 min

*Translation: Oven-roasted rabbit legs with herb crust.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 rabbit hind legs
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- Salt and pepper
- 100 ml white wine

**Method**
1. Mix oil, mustard, garlic, rosemary, and thyme. Marinate legs 2 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Place legs on a roasting rack over a tray. Pour wine into tray.
3. Roast 50 minutes, basting with pan juices at the 25-minute mark.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
5. Serve with Sauerkraut and potato gratin.
*Final resting temperature: 76–79 °C (169–174 °F).*

### 3. Madensverden.dk — Ovnstegt kanin (Danish oven-roasted rabbit)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ovnstegt-kanin/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 45 min

*The Danish approach: whole roasted or jointed rabbit with root vegetables.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 rabbit legs
- 2 tbsp butter, melted
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme
- 2 garlic cloves, pressed
- 200 ml chicken stock

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C.
2. Mix butter, salt, pepper, thyme, and garlic. Brush over rabbit legs.
3. Place in a roasting dish with stock. Roast 40–45 minutes, basting twice.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 74 °C (165 °F)**.
5. Rest briefly. Serve with Danish rye bread, boiled potatoes, and lingonberry sauce.
*Final resting temperature: 76–78 °C (169–172 °F).*
