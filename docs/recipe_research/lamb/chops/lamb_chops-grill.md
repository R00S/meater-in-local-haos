## Cut profile

Lamb rib chops and loin chops are the classic cuts for grilling, popular worldwide from Australia and New Zealand (the world's largest lamb exporters) to the UK, Mediterranean, Middle East, and West Africa. Rib chops are smaller, leaner, and more delicate; loin chops are larger and fattier. Both benefit from high direct heat on a barbecue or grill pan. The rib or 'rack' end is typically served slightly pink (medium-rare to medium).

Target internal temperatures (from Africanbites.com):
- Rib chops: **55 °C (131 °F)** for medium-rare
- Loin chops: **57–60 °C (135–140 °F)** for medium

## Source recipes

### 1. African Bites — Grilled Lamb Chops with Peppercorn Sauce (West African/International tradition)
**Source**: Africanbites.com (Imma Adamu); https://www.africanbites.com/grilled-lamb-chops/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"These juicy and smoky grilled lamb chops are marinated in a 5-ingredient blend oozing with rosemary, garlic, and herby flavors with a little kick in between bites."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr marinate · **Cook**: 10 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "For rib chops, aim for an internal temperature of 130°F (55°C). For loin chops, anywhere from 135–140°F (57–60°C) is ideal."

**Ingredients**

*Marinade (5 ingredients):*
- 4–8 lamb rib or loin chops
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- ½ tsp red pepper flakes (or cayenne)
- Salt and cracked black pepper

*Peppercorn sauce:*
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1 small onion, finely diced
- 100 ml chicken or lamb stock
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 50 ml double cream
- 1 tsp cracked black peppercorns

**Method**
1. Mix oil, garlic, rosemary, pepper flakes, salt, and pepper into a paste. Coat lamb chops well. Marinate 1 hour (or overnight for deeper flavour).
2. Preheat grill or BBQ to high heat. Grill lamb chops 3–4 minutes per side for rib chops; 4–5 minutes per side for loin chops.
3. Insert MEATER probe. **Pull rib chops at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)**, loin chops at **55–57 °C (131–135 °F)** for medium-rare.
4. Rest 10 minutes.
5. **Peppercorn sauce**: Melt butter, sauté onion 2 minutes. Add stock and mustard; reduce by half. Stir in cream and pepper. Simmer to sauce consistency.
6. Serve with fondant potatoes, Greek salad, or grilled vegetables.
*Final resting temperature: 55–60 °C (131–140 °F).*

### 2. Madensverden.dk — Grillede lammekoteletter med rosmarin og hvidløg (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/grillede-lammekoteletter/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 6–8 hrs marinade · **Cook**: 12 min

*Translation: Grilled lamb chops with rosemary and garlic — Danish summer grilling classic.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 large lamb loin chops (approx. 250 g each)
- ½ dl rapeseed oil
- 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, pressed
- 1 tsp salt
- Knob of butter to top when serving

**Method**
1. Combine oil, rosemary, garlic, and salt. Pat chops dry and rub with marinade. Seal in a bag; refrigerate 6–8 hours.
2. Bring to room temperature 30 minutes before grilling.
3. Hot grill, direct heat. Grill 3–4 minutes per side, turning every 1.5 minutes for a juicier result.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 55–57 °C (131–135 °F)** for medium (pink interior).
5. Season with cracked pepper. Top with a knob of butter before serving.
6. Serve with bulgur salad and grilled vegetables.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 3. Nefisyemektarifleri.com — Izgara Kuzu Pirzola (Turkish grilled lamb chops)
**Source**: Nefisyemektarifleri.com; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/izgara-kuzu-pirzola/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min marinade · **Cook**: 15 min

*Translation: Grilled lamb chops — a classic Turkish meze-and-main.*

**Ingredients**
- 8 lamb rib chops
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- Salt and black pepper

**Method**
1. Mix oil, tomato paste, herbs, and garlic. Coat chops and marinate 30 minutes.
2. Hot grill. Cook 3–5 minutes per side.
3. Insert probe. **Pull at 52–55 °C (126–131 °F)** for medium-rare.
4. Serve with flatbread, yoghurt, grilled pepper, and tomato.
*Final resting temperature: 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).*
