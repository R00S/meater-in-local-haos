## Cut profile

Pan-searing lamb chops is the everyday indoor method used throughout the UK, Europe, South Asia, and the Middle East. A very hot cast-iron or heavy stainless pan, 3–4 minutes per side, then resting — the fat rendering into the pan creates a natural sauce base. Rosemary-garlic butter basting is the French/British standard; spice rubs and yoghurt marinades are the Indian/Middle Eastern approach.

Target: rib chops **55 °C (131 °F)** medium-rare; loin chops **57–60 °C (135–140 °F)** medium.

## Source recipes

### 1. Vahrehvah.com — Spicy Lamb Chops (Indian/South Asian tradition)
**Source**: Vahrehvah.com (Chef Sanjay Thumma, Hyderabad); https://www.vahrehvah.com/spicy-lamb-chops (fetched 2026-04-24). *Published 20 February 2019.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min marinate · **Cook**: 30 min

*Indian-style spicy lamb chops — yoghurt and spice marinade, pan-fried in corn oil.*

**Ingredients**
- 10 lamb chops
- 150 ml corn oil
- 1½ tbsp vinegar
- ½ tsp lemon juice
- Salt to taste
- 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- ½ tsp red chilli powder
- 1 bunch fresh coriander, chopped
- 3 tbsp curd (yoghurt)
- 1 tbsp coriander powder
- ¼ tsp pepper powder
- Pinch of sugar
- 1 tsp plain flour (maida)

**Method**
1. Combine ginger-garlic paste, red chilli powder, pepper powder, curd, salt, vinegar, sugar, coriander powder, coriander leaves, lime juice, and flour in a bowl. Mix well to form a marinade.
2. Coat lamb chops with the marinade. Rest 20 minutes.
3. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add marinated chops in a single layer. Cook 5 minutes undisturbed.
4. Turn over. Reduce heat to very low. Cover and cook 20–30 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and golden brown.
5. Insert probe. **Verify 70–72 °C (158–162 °F)** for fully cooked (Indian tradition prefers cooked through).
6. Serve with rice, naan, or roti.
*Final resting temperature: 72–75 °C (162–167 °F).*

### 2. BBC Good Food — Pan-fried Lamb Chops with Rosemary and Garlic (British/French tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/pan-fried-lamb-chops (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb loin chops
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 30 g butter
- Salt and black pepper

**Method**
1. Season chops generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil in a heavy frying pan over high heat until very hot. Add chops.
3. Sear 3–4 minutes per side until golden. In the final 2 minutes, add butter, garlic, and rosemary. Baste continuously.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 55–57 °C (131–135 °F)** for medium (pink interior).
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with minted new potatoes and peas.
*Final resting temperature: 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).*

### 3. Africanbites.com — Pan-Grilled Lamb Chops (West African tradition)
**Source**: Africanbites.com; https://www.africanbites.com/grilled-lamb-chops/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Grill pan variation of the recipe.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 1 hr · **Cook**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4–6 lamb rib or loin chops
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary
- ½ tsp cayenne pepper
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix marinade. Coat chops and marinate 1 hour.
2. Heat a ridged grill pan over high heat. Brush with oil.
3. Cook chops 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert probe. Rib chops: **pull at 52–54 °C (126–129 °F)**. Loin chops: **pull at 55–57 °C (131–135 °F)**.
5. Rest 10 minutes. Serve with three-bean salad.
*Final resting temperature: 55–60 °C (131–140 °F).*
