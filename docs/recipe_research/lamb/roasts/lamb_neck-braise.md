# Lamb Neck × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The lamb neck (*lammnacke*, "scrag end" or "neck fillet") is a heavily worked, well-marbled cut from the cervical region. Bone-in (whole neck) or boneless (neck fillet), it is high in collagen and benefits from long slow moist heat that converts connective tissue to gelatin. Target internal 88–94 °C (190–201 °F) — the "pulled" doneness. Pulled below 86 °C the meat is still fibrous; above 96 °C it begins to dry and shred. Probe placement: in the deepest, thickest muscle, parallel to any bone. Carryover ~2–3 °C. Rest 15 min covered before shredding or serving.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi — Slow-Cooked Lamb Neck with Pomegranate (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ten Speed Press, 2012), pp. 230–232 (slow-cooked lamb shoulder method, regularly applied to neck per Ottolenghi.co.uk)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg lamb neck fillet (or 2 whole bone-in necks ~1.5 kg total)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, sliced
- 6 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
- 400 ml lamb or chicken stock
- 200 ml red wine
- 2 cinnamon sticks, 4 cardamom pods, 2 bay leaves, 2 tsp ground allspice
- 60 g pomegranate seeds, 2 tbsp chopped flat-leaf parsley (to finish)
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Salt lamb generously. Heat oven to 160 °C (320 °F).
2. In a heavy casserole, brown the lamb in oil on all sides, 8 min. Remove.
3. Soften onions and garlic 10 min. Stir in pomegranate molasses, allspice; cook 1 min.
4. Return lamb. Add stock, wine, cinnamon, cardamom, bay so liquid reaches halfway.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest muscle.
6. Cover; braise 2.5–3 hr in oven, turning at 90 min.
7. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F)** — meat should yield to a fork.
8. Rest 15 min in pot. Strain and reduce sauce. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (silky, jewelled with pomegranate).

### 2. Diana Henry — Slow-Cooked Lamb Neck with Anchovies and Rosemary (British/Italian tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* and *From the Oven to the Table* (Mitchell Beazley, 2019), pp. 138–140 (slow-cooked lamb chapter)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg boneless lamb neck fillet, in 3–4 pieces
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, finely sliced
- 6 anchovy fillets in oil, chopped
- 6 garlic cloves
- 4 sprigs rosemary, leaves picked
- 300 ml dry white wine
- 400 ml lamb stock
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt lamb. Heat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Brown lamb in oil, 8 min; remove.
2. Soften onions 10 min. Add anchovies; mash into onions. Add garlic, rosemary, lemon zest. Pour in wine; reduce by half.
3. Add stock; return lamb.
4. Insert MEATER into thickest piece.
5. Cover; braise 2.5–3 hr until **internal 90–92 °C (194–198 °F)**.
6. Rest 15 min. Final serving temperature: **75–78 °C / 167–172 °F** (savoury, melt-tender).

### 3. Anissa Helou — Tagine of Lamb Neck with Prunes (Moroccan tradition)
**Source**: Anissa Helou, *Feast: Food of the Islamic World* (Ecco, 2018), pp. 312–314 (tagine of lamb)  
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg bone-in lamb neck, in 4 cm slices (ask butcher)
- 2 tbsp olive oil + 1 tbsp ghee
- 2 onions, grated
- 4 garlic cloves, grated
- 1 tsp ground ginger, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, ½ tsp saffron threads, ½ tsp ground black pepper
- 200 g prunes, pitted
- 2 tbsp honey
- 1 tbsp orange flower water
- 30 g toasted blanched almonds, 30 g toasted sesame seeds (to finish)
- Salt

**Method**
1. Brown lamb in oil and ghee in a heavy tagine or casserole, 10 min. Remove.
2. Sweat onions and garlic 8 min; add spices; cook 1 min. Return lamb. Add water just to cover.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest piece.
4. Cover; simmer over very low heat 2 hr.
5. Add prunes and honey; cook 30 min uncovered to glaze. **Pull at 90–94 °C (194–201 °F).**
6. Stir in orange flower water; rest 10 min. Scatter almonds and sesame. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F**.

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Lammhalsbraisé (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *Fäviken* (Phaidon, 2012) and *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 264–266 (long-braised lamb)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg bone-in lamb neck, in thick slices
- 1 tbsp butter, 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 onion, 1 carrot, 1 leek, 4 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp dry juniper berries crushed, 4 bay leaves, 6 sprigs thyme
- 200 ml dark beer
- 400 ml lamb or beef stock
- 1 tbsp redcurrant jelly
- Salt

**Method**
1. Salt lamb. Heat butter and oil in a heavy pot; brown lamb 10 min; remove.
2. Sweat vegetables 10 min; add juniper, bay, thyme; pour in beer; reduce by half. Add stock.
3. Return lamb. Insert MEATER into deepest meat.
4. Cover; simmer very gently 2.5 hr.
5. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).**
6. Rest 15 min. Strain sauce; whisk in redcurrant jelly. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F**.
