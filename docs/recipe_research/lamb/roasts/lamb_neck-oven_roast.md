# Lamb Neck × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Lamb neck (*lammnacke*) oven-roasted is most commonly done in the long, low-and-slow style: a covered braise-roast at 140–160 °C for 3–4 hr, optionally finished uncovered at 220 °C to colour. Whole bone-in necks or boneless neck fillets reach pulled-tender texture at 88–94 °C (190–201 °F) internal. Probe placement: deepest muscle, parallel to bone. Carryover ~3–4 °C in covered roast. Rest 15–20 min loosely tented before carving or shredding.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nigel Slater — Slow-Roast Lamb Neck with Quince and Bay (British tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Tender Volume II: A Cook's Guide to the Fruit Garden* (Fourth Estate, 2010), pp. 432–435 (slow-roast lamb chapter)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hr · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whole bone-in lamb necks, ~1.5 kg total (or 1.2 kg neck fillet)
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions quartered, 4 garlic cloves
- 2 quinces, peeled, cored, quartered
- 6 fresh bay leaves
- 2 sprigs rosemary
- 1 tbsp honey
- 250 ml dry white wine
- 250 ml lamb stock
- Sea salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Salt lamb generously; rub with 1 tbsp oil. Heat oven to 150 °C (300 °F).
2. Brown lamb in remaining oil in a casserole, 8 min; remove. Add onions, quince, bay, rosemary; stir to coat in fat.
3. Return lamb. Drizzle with honey; pour wine and stock around.
4. Insert MEATER into thickest muscle alongside the bone.
5. Cover with foil. Roast 3 hr, basting at 90 min.
6. Uncover; raise oven to 220 °C (430 °F); roast 20 min to colour.
7. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).**
8. Rest 20 min loosely tented. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F** (perfumed with bay, sweet quince).

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Slow-Roasted Lamb Neck with Sumac and Onions (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* and *Ottolenghi Simple* (Ebury Press, 2018), pp. 254–257 (slow-roast lamb chapter)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 4 hr · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg boneless lamb neck fillet
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 4 onions, finely sliced
- 6 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp sumac
- 1 tbsp ground cumin, 1 tbsp ground coriander, 1 tsp cinnamon, ½ tsp ground allspice
- Zest of 2 lemons
- 30 g pine nuts (toasted)
- 200 ml chicken stock
- 30 g chopped flat-leaf parsley
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Mix half the sumac, all dry spices, lemon zest, salt, pepper; rub into lamb. Refrigerate 2 hr.
2. Heat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Heat 2 tbsp oil in a casserole; brown lamb 8 min; remove.
3. Cook onions and garlic in remaining oil 12 min until deep golden. Return lamb. Pour stock around.
4. Insert MEATER into thickest part.
5. Cover; roast 3 hr, basting at 90 min.
6. Uncover; roast 30 min to colour.
7. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Rest 20 min loosely covered.
8. Scatter remaining sumac, pine nuts, parsley. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F**.

### 3. Niklas Ekstedt — Långbakad Lammnacke i Vedugn (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Niklas Ekstedt, *Ekstedt: The Nordic Art of Analogue Cooking* (Pavilion, 2020), pp. 142–145 (slow-roast lamb chapter, oven adaptation)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hr salt + 15 min · **Cook**: 4 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg bone-in lamb neck
- For dry rub: 25 g salt, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tbsp crushed juniper, 2 tsp black pepper
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 onion sliced, 1 carrot diced, 4 garlic cloves
- 250 ml dark beer
- 250 ml lamb or beef stock
- 4 sprigs thyme, 4 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp lingonberry preserve (to finish)

**Method**
1. Rub salt mixture into lamb; refrigerate 12 hr.
2. Pat dry. Heat oven to 145 °C (293 °F). Brown lamb in oil in a heavy casserole, 8 min; remove.
3. Sweat vegetables 8 min; deglaze with beer; add stock, herbs.
4. Return lamb. Insert MEATER into thickest meat.
5. Cover; roast 3 hr, basting once.
6. Uncover; raise to 220 °C (430 °F); roast 25 min.
7. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Rest 15 min uncovered.
8. Strain pan juices; whisk in lingonberry. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F**.

### 4. Fergus Henderson — Slow-Roast Lamb Scrag End (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Whole Beast: Nose to Tail Eating* (Bloomsbury, 2004), pp. 156–158 (lamb scrag end slow roast)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 3.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg bone-in lamb neck (scrag end), in thick slices
- 2 tbsp duck fat or olive oil
- 1 large onion, sliced
- 1 head garlic, halved horizontally
- 6 anchovy fillets
- 4 sprigs rosemary, 2 bay leaves
- 250 ml red wine
- 250 ml lamb stock
- Sea salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt lamb. Heat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Brown in fat in a heavy casserole, 8 min; remove.
2. Sweat onion and garlic 10 min; melt anchovies into the onions; add herbs; deglaze with wine; add stock.
3. Return lamb. Insert MEATER into thickest piece.
4. Cover; roast 3 hr, turning at 90 min.
5. Uncover; raise to 210 °C (410 °F); roast 20 min.
6. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Rest 15 min.
7. Final serving temperature: **75–80 °C / 167–176 °F** (deeply savoury).
