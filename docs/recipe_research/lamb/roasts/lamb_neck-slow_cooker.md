# Lamb Neck × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Lamb neck (*lammnacke*, neck fillet or whole bone-in neck) in a slow cooker is the easiest way to its perfect endpoint: ~90–95 °C internal, 7–9 hours on LOW. The 90–100 °C ambient drives slow gelatin formation; the closed pot keeps surface dry-out near zero. Probe placement: thickest muscle, parallel to bone. Carryover ~1–2 °C. Rest 10–15 min on warm before shredding or serving.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Lamb Neck Stew (American tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017), pp. 120–122 (slow-cooked lamb chapter)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck, in 5 cm chunks
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 2 carrots, 2 celery stalks, diced
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 250 ml red wine
- 500 ml lamb stock
- 4 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves
- 250 g pearl barley (rinsed)
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Salt lamb. Brown in oil over high in a skillet, 8 min. Transfer to cooker.
2. Sweat onion, carrot, celery, garlic 6 min; add tomato paste; cook 2 min; deglaze with wine. Pour into cooker.
3. Add stock, herbs, barley. Stir.
4. Insert MEATER into the thickest lamb chunk.
5. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Barley should be soft but intact.
7. Rest 10 min. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (silky stew).

### 2. Stephanie O'Dea — Slow-Cooked Greek Lamb Neck (Greek-American tradition)
**Source**: Stephanie O'Dea, *365 Slow Cooker Suppers* (Hyperion, 2013), pp. 198–200; ayearofslowcooking.com archive  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 min · **Cook**: 7 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck fillet, whole
- 1 onion, sliced
- 6 garlic cloves
- 1 tbsp dried oregano, 1 tbsp dried mint, 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 200 ml dry white wine
- Juice and zest of 1 lemon
- 200 ml lamb stock
- 2 bay leaves, 4 sprigs rosemary
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Place onion in the cooker. Sit lamb on top.
2. Mix garlic, herbs, cinnamon, tomato paste, wine, lemon juice and zest, stock, bay, rosemary; pour over.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest part of fillet.
4. Cover; cook LOW 7 hr.
5. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).**
6. Rest 10 min. Shred or slice. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F** (oregano-bright, melt-tender).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Slow Lamb Curry with Neck (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010), pp. 180–183 (lamb curry method)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck, in 4 cm pieces
- 2 onions, finely sliced and lightly fried
- 30 g ginger, grated; 6 garlic cloves grated
- 2 tbsp ghee
- 2 tsp ground coriander, 2 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli, 1 tsp garam masala
- 400 g chopped tomatoes
- 200 ml water
- 100 g full-fat yoghurt
- Salt
- Fresh coriander to finish

**Method**
1. Heat ghee in a skillet; add spices; bloom 30 sec; add onions, ginger, garlic; cook 5 min; add tomatoes; cook 8 min to a thick masala.
2. Transfer masala to slow cooker; add lamb, water; stir.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest lamb piece.
4. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
5. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Stir in yoghurt; cook on LOW 10 min more.
6. Rest 10 min. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Slow Lammnacke med Rotsaker (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 264–266 (Nordic slow lamb braise, slow-cooker adaptation)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg lamb neck, bone-in slices
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 carrots, 1 parsnip, 1 swede (rutabaga), all diced 2 cm
- 2 tbsp crushed juniper, 1 tbsp black peppercorns, 4 bay leaves
- 250 ml dark beer
- 250 ml lamb stock
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- Salt
- 2 tbsp chopped dill (to finish)

**Method**
1. Layer vegetables in cooker; sit lamb on top.
2. Mix juniper, peppercorns, bay, beer, stock, sugar, 1 tbsp salt; pour over.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest meat.
4. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
5. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).**
6. Rest 10 min. Stir in dill. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F** (rustic, juniper-scented).
