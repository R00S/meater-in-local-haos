# Lamb Shank × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The lamb shank (*lammlägg*) oven-roasted is essentially a long, covered braise-roast at 150–170 °C for 2.5–3.5 hr — the same internal target as a slow cooker (92–96 °C, 198–205 °F) but with the option of an uncovered finish to glaze the surface. Probe placement: deepest meat alongside the bone, tip not on the bone. Carryover ~3–4 °C in covered roast. Rest 15–20 min loosely tented.

## Source recipes

### 1. Gordon Ramsay — Slow-Roasted Lamb Shanks with Mediterranean Vegetables (British tradition)
**Source**: Gordon Ramsay, *Gordon Ramsay's Home Cooking* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2013), pp. 188–190  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, quartered
- 4 carrots, in chunks
- 1 head garlic, halved horizontally
- 4 sprigs rosemary, 4 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 250 ml red wine
- 500 ml lamb stock
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt shanks; rub with 1 tbsp oil. Heat oven to 160 °C (320 °F).
2. Brown shanks in remaining oil in a heavy casserole, 10 min; remove.
3. Sweat vegetables 10 min; add tomato paste; cook 2 min; deglaze with wine; reduce by half; add stock, herbs.
4. Return shanks. Insert MEATER into thickest meat alongside the bone.
5. Cover; roast 2.5 hr, basting at 90 min.
6. Uncover; raise oven to 220 °C (430 °F); roast 25 min, basting twice, to colour and reduce sauce.
7. **Pull at 93–95 °C (199–203 °F).** Rest 15 min loosely tented.
8. Strain sauce. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (meat falling from bone, glossy gravy).

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Lamb Shanks with Pomegranate, Quince and Coriander (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Jerusalem* (Ten Speed Press, 2012), pp. 224–226  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, sliced
- 6 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
- 250 ml red wine
- 500 ml chicken stock
- 2 quinces, peeled, cored, in eighths
- 2 cinnamon sticks, 6 cardamom pods, 1 tsp ground allspice, 1 tbsp ground coriander
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, coriander leaves (to finish)
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt shanks. Heat oven to 170 °C (340 °F). Brown in oil in a casserole, 10 min; remove.
2. Soften onions and garlic 10 min; add ground spices; cook 1 min; add pomegranate molasses; deglaze with wine; add stock and quinces.
3. Return shanks; tuck whole spices around. Insert MEATER into thickest meat.
4. Cover; roast 2.5 hr.
5. Uncover; raise to 200 °C (392 °F); roast 25 min to glaze.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Rest 15 min.
7. Scatter pomegranate and coriander. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.

### 3. Niklas Ekstedt — Långbakad Lammlägg med Lingon (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Niklas Ekstedt, *Ekstedt: The Nordic Art of Analogue Cooking* (Pavilion, 2020), pp. 138–141 (lamb shank chapter, oven adaptation)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hr salt + 15 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- For dry rub: 25 g salt, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tbsp crushed juniper, 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 onion sliced, 2 carrots diced, 4 garlic cloves
- 250 ml dark beer
- 300 ml lamb stock
- 4 bay leaves
- 2 tbsp lingonberry preserve (to finish)

**Method**
1. Rub salt mixture into shanks; refrigerate 12 hr.
2. Pat dry. Heat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Brown in oil in a casserole, 10 min; remove.
3. Sweat vegetables 8 min; deglaze with beer; reduce by half; add stock and bay.
4. Return shanks. Insert MEATER into thickest meat.
5. Cover; roast 2.5 hr.
6. Uncover; raise to 220 °C (430 °F); roast 25 min.
7. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Rest 15 min.
8. Strain pan juices; whisk in lingonberry. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.

### 4. Anissa Helou — Slow-Roasted Lamb Shanks with Honey and Saffron (Moroccan tradition)
**Source**: Anissa Helou, *Feast: Food of the Islamic World* (Ecco, 2018), pp. 308–310  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 2 tbsp olive oil + 1 tbsp ghee
- 2 onions, grated
- 6 garlic cloves grated
- 1 tsp ground ginger, ½ tsp saffron threads, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, ½ tsp black pepper
- 3 tbsp honey
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 200 ml chicken stock
- 30 g toasted blanched almonds (to finish)
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds (to finish)
- Salt

**Method**
1. Brown shanks in oil and ghee in a heavy tagine or casserole, 10 min; remove.
2. Sweat onions and garlic 8 min; add ground spices; cook 1 min; add honey, cinnamon sticks, stock.
3. Return shanks; turn in sauce. Insert MEATER into thickest meat.
4. Heat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Cover; roast 2.5 hr, turning at 90 min.
5. Uncover; baste; roast 25 min until sauce is glossy.
6. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Rest 15 min.
7. Scatter almonds and sesame. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.
