# Lamb Shank × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The lamb shank (*lammlägg*) is the lower portion of the leg — bone-in, dense in collagen, encased in silverskin and a marrow-rich femur. In a slow cooker the shank reaches its perfect "fork-pull" texture at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F) internal after 7–9 hr on LOW. The closed pot maintains a bath of moisture; the bone conducts heat to the muscle centre. Probe placement: deepest meat next to the bone, tip not on the bone. Carryover ~1–2 °C. Rest 10–15 min on warm before serving.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Lamb Shanks with Red Wine and Olives (American tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017), pp. 108–110  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks (~400 g each)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 carrots, diced
- 2 celery stalks, diced
- 6 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 250 ml red wine
- 400 ml lamb or chicken stock
- 4 sprigs rosemary, 4 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves
- 100 g black olives, pitted
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt shanks. Brown in oil over high in a skillet, 10 min total. Transfer to cooker.
2. Sweat onion, carrot, celery, garlic 6 min; add tomato paste; cook 2 min; deglaze with wine. Pour into cooker.
3. Add stock, herbs.
4. Insert MEATER through the meat alongside the shank bone, tip well into the muscle.
5. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
6. **Pull at 93–95 °C (199–203 °F).** Stir in olives and lemon zest; rest in cooker 10 min.
7. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (meat sliding from bone).

### 2. Donna Hay — Slow-Cooker Moroccan Lamb Shanks (Australian/Moroccan tradition)
**Source**: Donna Hay, *The New Easy* (Fourth Estate, 2014), pp. 168–170 (slow-cook lamb chapter)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 7 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, grated; 30 g ginger grated
- 2 tsp ras el hanout, 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 preserved lemon, finely chopped
- 400 g chopped tomatoes
- 200 ml chicken stock
- 100 g dried apricots, halved
- 2 tbsp honey
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt shanks; brown in oil 10 min; transfer to cooker.
2. Sweat onions, garlic, ginger 8 min; add spices; cook 1 min; add tomatoes, stock, preserved lemon, apricots, honey. Pour into cooker.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest shank meat.
4. Cover; cook LOW 7 hr.
5. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).**
6. Rest 10 min. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.

### 3. Stephanie Alexander — Slow Lamb Shanks with Gremolata (Italian-Australian tradition)
**Source**: Stephanie Alexander, *The Cook's Companion* (Lantern, 2004 ed.), pp. 466–468 (lamb shanks chapter)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 onions, finely sliced
- 4 carrots, in 2 cm rounds
- 6 garlic cloves
- 250 ml dry white wine
- 400 g chopped tomatoes
- 300 ml lamb stock
- 4 sprigs rosemary
- For gremolata: 30 g flat-leaf parsley chopped, zest of 2 lemons, 2 garlic cloves grated
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Salt shanks; brown in oil 10 min; transfer to cooker.
2. Sweat onions, carrots, garlic 6 min; deglaze with wine; add tomatoes, stock, rosemary. Pour into cooker.
3. Insert MEATER into the thickest meat alongside the bone.
4. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
5. **Pull at 93–95 °C (199–203 °F).**
6. Rest 10 min. Strain sauce; reduce if loose. Sprinkle gremolata to serve. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Slow Lammlägg med Smör och Honung (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 268–270 (slow lamb braising; slow-cooker adaptation)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 lamb shanks
- 1 tbsp butter, 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 large onion, sliced
- 2 carrots, 1 parsnip, diced
- 4 garlic cloves
- 1 tbsp crushed juniper berries
- 250 ml dark beer
- 300 ml lamb or beef stock
- 2 tbsp honey
- 4 bay leaves, 4 sprigs thyme
- Salt

**Method**
1. Salt shanks; brown in butter and oil 10 min; transfer to cooker.
2. Sweat onion, carrot, parsnip, garlic 8 min; add juniper; deglaze with beer; reduce by half; add stock, honey, bay, thyme. Pour into cooker.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest meat.
4. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
5. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).**
6. Rest 10 min. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F**.
