## Cut profile

Lamb shoulder is fattier and more flavourful than leg, with more connective tissue that melts into gelatin during long, slow roasting. It is the cut for slow-roasted lamb dishes — 4–6 hours at 150–160 °C (or 7–8 hours at 130 °C). The result is pull-apart meat that falls off the bone with a fork. The MEATER probe target is **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** for pull-apart.

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Slow-Roasted Shoulder of Lamb (British tradition)
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-roasted-shoulder-lamb (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 4.5 hrs

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg bone-in lamb shoulder
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lemon, juice
- 200 ml white wine
- 400 ml lamb or chicken stock
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C. Score shoulder all over. Insert garlic slivers and rosemary sprigs into cuts.
2. Rub with oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
3. Roast uncovered at 220 °C for 20 minutes to colour.
4. Reduce to 150 °C. Add wine and stock to roasting tin. Cover tightly with foil.
5. Roast 4–4.5 hours until probe reads **85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** and meat pulls off the bone.
6. Rest 20 minutes. Shred with two forks. Serve with roasted vegetables.
*Final temperature: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).*

### 2. RecipeTinEats.com — Slow Roasted Lamb Shoulder (Australian tradition)
**Source**: RecipeTinEats.com (Nagi Maehashi); https://www.recipetineats.com/slow-roasted-lamb-shoulder/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7 hrs

*"The best lamb shoulder you've ever tasted — 7 hours at low heat, effortlessly fall-apart tender."*

**Ingredients**
- 2.5 kg bone-in lamb shoulder
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp dried rosemary
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp pepper
- 500 ml beef or lamb stock

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 130 °C. Rub lamb with oil and all seasonings.
2. Place in a deep roasting tin. Pour stock around (not over).
3. Cover tightly with 2 layers of foil. Roast 7 hours.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F)** for complete pull-apart.
5. Rest 20 minutes. Serve shredded over polenta or with roasted vegetables.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 3. Nefisyemektarifleri.com — Fırında Kuzu Kol (Turkish slow-roasted lamb shoulder)
**Source**: Nefisyemektarifleri.com; https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/firinda-sebzeli-kuzu-kol/ (web search 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hrs

*Translation: Oven-roasted lamb shoulder with vegetables — Turkish tradition.*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg lamb shoulder (bone-in)
- 4 potatoes, quartered
- 3 carrots, chopped large
- 2 onions, quartered
- 3 garlic cloves
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp each: salt, black pepper, paprika, oregano
- 200 ml water

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Rub shoulder with oil and all spices. Surround with vegetables and water.
2. Cover tightly with foil. Roast 2 hours.
3. Uncover; roast further 1 hour to brown.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 82–85 °C (180–185 °F)** when tender.
5. Serve directly from the pan with yoghurt and flatbread.
*Final temperature: 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).*
