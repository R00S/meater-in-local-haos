# Secreto × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The secreto (Spanish: "secret") is a hidden fat-marbled cut from the shoulder/neck area of the Iberian pig (or any pork). It is thin, uneven, and highly marbled, making it one of the most flavourful pork cuts for the griddle or grill. Because it is thin and irregular, it is cut into 3–4 uniform pieces before cooking. Key rules: de-chill 2 hours; dry sear without oil; no salt before; one flip only; rest 1–2 min then season. Target: 65–70 °C (pink to fully done) — internal temperature rises fast due to the thin cross-section.

Target internal temperatures (pan sear):
- Pink inside: **63–65 °C (145–149 °F)**
- Well done (recommended for high-fat secreto): **68–72 °C (154–162 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Directo al Paladar — Secreto de cerdo a la plancha (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: Directo al Paladar; https://www.directoalpaladar.com/recetas-de-carnes-y-aves/receta-secreto-cerdo-a-plancha-trucos-te-quede-muy-crujiente-jugoso-interior (fetched 2026-04-24). *The definitive Spanish plancha method for secreto: atemperar 2 hr; dry griddle, no oil; sear 3–4 min one side, 2–3 min other; no salt before; one flip with tongs; rest 1–2 min then season.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 2 hr de-chill · **Cook**: 6–8 min

*Translation: "The secret is a cut from between the shoulder and the panceta — important marbling that makes it very flavourful and juicy, provided it is cooked correctly."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "3–4 minutes first side, 2–3 minutes second side; for thinner pieces, 2 minutes each side." Inspect the edge (canto) when turning to judge doneness. Rest 1–2 min; season after.

**Ingredients**
- 1 secreto de cerdo ibérico (or pork secreto), approx. 600 g
- Salt (applied after cooking)
- Black pepper (applied after cooking)
- Vegetables for garnish (grilled peppers, salad, etc.)

**Method**
1. Remove from fridge **2 hours** before cooking to de-chill completely.
2. Cut secreto into **3 or 4 even pieces** for uniform cooking.
3. Preheat a flat griddle/cast-iron pan very well. No oil.
4. Place pieces flat-side down. Do NOT add salt yet.
5. Place a hamburger press or heavy weight on top to maximise contact.
6. Cook **3–4 minutes** first side (2 min for thin pieces). Do not touch.
7. Flip once with tongs (never pierce with a fork). Check the edge — it should be opaque roughly two-thirds up.
8. Cook second side **2–3 minutes** (2 min for thin pieces).
9. Remove from pan. Rest **1–2 minutes**.
10. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 65–70 °C (149–158 °F)**.
11. Season with flaked salt and black pepper. Serve with grilled vegetables or fries.
*Final temperature: 68–72 °C (154–162 °F) for well-done secreto.*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Secreto vom Ibericoschwein (German tradition)
**Source**: Carco, Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/3267791485695361/Secreto-vom-Ibericoschwein.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *"The 'secret fillet' without fuss — pure, unadulterated meat pleasure." Ghee (Butterschmalz) pan, 3–4 min per side, then 5 min rest at 80 °C in oven.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 8 min + 5 min oven rest

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: "In a hot pan with Butterschmalz, depending on thickness, 3 to 4 minutes per side. Then 5 minutes at approx. 80 °C in the preheated oven to rest."

**Ingredients**
- 2 secreto pieces (approx. 400 g each)
- 1 tbsp Butterschmalz (ghee) or lard
- Salt and pepper
- Optional: herb butter to serve

**Method**
1. Trim any excess fat (do not remove all); cut very thin edges off.
2. Heat pan with Butterschmalz to high.
3. Sear secreto **3–4 minutes per side** (adjust for thickness).
4. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Transfer to oven preheated at **80 °C × 5 minutes** to rest under even heat.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 65–70 °C (149–158 °F)**.
7. Serve with herb butter, Bratkartoffeln, or Ofenkartoffeln.
*Final temperature: 65–70 °C (149–158 °F).*

### 3. Madensverden.dk — Pandestegning reference (adapted from svinekotelet technique) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/pandestegning-af-koteletter/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *The Danish pandestegning (pan frying) method applied to secreto — since secreto is equivalent to a marbled collar cut. High heat, butter, herbs; cook through to juicy well-done.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Cook**: 6–8 min

**Temperature guidance**: 2 min per side on high, then 2 more min per side on reduced heat with butter baste. For secreto: cook to 68–70 °C for a properly rendered result.

**Ingredients**
- 600 g secreto de cerdo (cut into pieces)
- Butter
- 2 garlic cloves
- Thyme, rosemary
- Salt and pepper (after)

**Method**
1. De-chill 30–60 min ahead. Cut into portions.
2. Heat pan to high. Add butter; let it foam.
3. Sear secreto portions **2 minutes per side** without moving.
4. Reduce heat; add garlic, thyme, rosemary. Baste with melted butter **2 more min per side**.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 68–70 °C (154–158 °F)** for the high-fat secreto.
6. Rest 2–3 min. Season with salt and pepper. Serve.
*Final temperature: 68–72 °C (154–162 °F).*
