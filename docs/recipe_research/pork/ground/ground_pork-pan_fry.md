# Ground Pork × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Ground pork (minced pork) is made by grinding pork shoulder, belly, or a blend of primal trimmings. Standard ground pork is typically 70–80% lean with 20–30% fat; the higher fat content gives it a juicy, cohesive texture when cooked and makes it more forgiving than ground beef or turkey. Ground pork is the basis of countless preparations worldwide — from Italian ragù and Swedish köttbullar to Chinese dumplings and Mexican chorizo. Because grinding distributes any surface bacteria throughout the mass, the USDA minimum safe internal temperature is 71°C (160°F). Carryover is negligible.

Target internal temperatures (pan fry):
- Safe/cooked through: **71–75 °C (160–167 °F)**
- ⚠️ Ground pork MUST reach 71 °C — no pink allowed

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Hakkebøffer (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/hakkeboeffer/ (referenced 2026-04-24). *Classic Danish "hakkebøf" (ground pork/beef patty): high quality meat, seasoned, seared in butter 3–4 min per side, served with fried onions.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 8–10 min

**Temperature guidance**: Pan fry in butter 3–4 min per side on medium-high. Core must reach 71 °C (74–75 °C for safety margin).

**Ingredients**
- 500 g ground pork (hakket svinekød), 10–15% fat
- 1 egg
- 3 tbsp breadcrumbs
- 2 tbsp ice-cold water
- 1 small onion, grated
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix ground pork with egg, breadcrumbs, ice water, grated onion, salt, and pepper.
2. Form 4 oval patties; press flat.
3. Heat pan to medium-high; add a generous knob of butter.
4. Fry patties **3–4 minutes per side** without moving.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 71–74 °C (160–165 °F)**.
6. Serve with fried onion rings, boiled potatoes, and pickled beetroot.
*Final temperature: 73–75 °C (163–167 °F).*

### 2. ichkoche.at — Faschiertes (Austrian style ground pork patty)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/faschiertes-braten-rezept-13558 (referenced 2026-04-24). *Austrian ground pork pan method: shaped Laibchen (small loaves/patties), fried in hot oil 3–4 min per side. Common seasoning: marjoram, garlic, onion.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 8–10 min

**Temperature guidance**: 3–4 min per side at medium-high. "Thoroughly cooked through — no pink interior." Core: 74–75 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 500 g ground pork (Faschiertes vom Schwein)
- 1 egg
- 2–3 tbsp breadcrumbs
- 1 onion, finely grated
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- 1 tsp dried marjoram
- Salt and pepper
- Oil for frying

**Method**
1. Mix all ingredients; form 4–6 oval Laibchen (patties).
2. Heat oil to medium-high.
3. Fry **3–4 minutes per side** until golden-brown.
4. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 74–75 °C (165–167 °F)**.
5. Serve with Kartoffelpüree (mashed potatoes) and green salad.
*Final temperature: 74–75 °C (165–167 °F).*

### 3. Directo al Paladar — Mici rumanos / albóndigas de cerdo a la plancha (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: Directo al Paladar; https://www.directoalpaladar.com/recetas-de-carnes-y-aves/receta-mici-rumanos-mititei-popular-salchicha-carne-picada-a-brasa (referenced 2026-04-24). *Romanian mici (sausage-shaped ground pork rolls) cooked on a plancha/griddle: 1.5 min per side × 4 sides. Seasoned with garlic, thyme, bicarbonate, olive oil.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min rest · **Cook**: 6 min

*Translation: "Romanian mici — popular ground meat sausages on the plancha, golden outside and juicy inside."*

**Temperature guidance**: 1.5 min per side × 4 sides on medium-high. Core: 71–74 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 500 g ground pork (or mix pork + beef)
- 1 tsp garlic powder (or 1 clove, crushed)
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- Pinch of black pepper
- Pinch of bicarbonate of soda
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 tbsp olive oil

**Method**
1. Mix all ingredients. Shape into short, thick sausage rolls (~70 g each, like a thick cigar).
2. Rest in fridge **15 minutes** to firm up.
3. Heat griddle pan (plancha) to medium-high.
4. Cook mici **1.5 minutes per side**, rotating through all 4 sides = approx. 6 min total.
5. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 71–74 °C (160–165 °F)**.
6. Serve with bread, mustard, and beer.
*Final temperature: 73–75 °C (163–167 °F).*
