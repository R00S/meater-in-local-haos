# Fresh Ham × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
A fresh (uncured, unsmoked) ham is the entire hind leg of a pig — bone-in, skin-on, with the femur and aitchbone running through the centre and a thick layer of subcutaneous fat under the rind. Anatomically distinct from cured (julskinka) or smoked ham preparations: the muscle has its full natural moisture, the rind is supple rather than salted-firm, and the fat has not been altered by brine. Roasting requires a long, moderate-temperature cook (140–160 °C) that drives the centre to safe temperature without overcooking the surface meat under the fat. Carryover is significant — 4–6 °C in a 4–6 kg leg — because the bone, fat and dense muscle store heat well. Rest 30–45 minutes loosely tented before carving.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Slow-Roasted Fresh Ham with Cider Glaze (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Cook's Illustrated Cookbook* (America's Test Kitchen, 2011), pp. 421–423; cooksillustrated.com  
**Serves**: 12–14 · **Prep**: 30 min (+ overnight dry-brine) · **Cook**: 4–5 hr · **Rest**: 30–45 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh ham (uncured, bone-in, skin on), 5–6 kg, shank or butt portion
- 90 g (½ cup) kosher salt for the dry brine
- 30 g (3 tbsp) light brown sugar for the dry brine
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 8 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 g (2 tbsp) Dijon mustard
- 30 g (2 tbsp) fresh thyme leaves
- **Cider glaze**: 480 ml (2 cups) apple cider, 80 g brown sugar, 30 ml cider vinegar, 30 g whole-grain mustard, 1 cinnamon stick

**Method**
1. The day before: score the rind in a 2 cm crosshatch through fat to muscle. Mix salt and brown sugar; rub all over the leg, including under the rind. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack 18–24 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Set ham on a rack in a heavy roasting pan. Pat dry. Mix grated garlic, Dijon, thyme and pepper into a paste; smear over the ham.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre of the largest muscle, entering from the side, away from the bone and the surface fat layer.
4. Roast 4–5 hours, basting with pan juices every 60 minutes.
5. Make the glaze: simmer cider, brown sugar, vinegar, mustard and cinnamon in a saucepan, reducing by half to syrupy (15 minutes).
6. After 3.5 hours, brush the ham with the glaze every 20 minutes.
7. **Pull at 67–69 °C (153–156 °F).** ATK targets 71–74 °C after the long rest; the leg carries 4–6 °C in standard preparations.
8. Rest 30–45 minutes loosely tented with foil. Carve perpendicular to the bone in 5 mm slices. Final serving temperature: **71–74 °C / 160–165 °F** (juicy, glazed, fully cooked).

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Slow-Roasted Fresh Ham with Pomegranate & Walnuts (Levantine fusion tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Sweet & Savoury* technique extended in *Ottolenghi Simple* (Ebury Press, 2018) and ottolenghi.co.uk; specifically the slow-roast pork chapter  
**Serves**: 12 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 5 hr · **Rest**: 30 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh ham (5 kg), bone-in, skin on, scored
- 60 g flaked sea salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g sweet smoked paprika
- 8 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 60 ml extra-virgin olive oil
- 60 g pomegranate molasses
- 240 ml chicken stock for the pan
- **To finish**: seeds of 1 large pomegranate, 80 g toasted chopped walnuts, 30 g chopped fresh mint, drizzle of pomegranate molasses

**Method**
1. The day before: score rind in a 2 cm crosshatch. Mix salt, pepper, paprika and garlic with olive oil and pomegranate molasses; rub all over the leg, into the scored rind. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack 18 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Set ham on a rack in a heavy roasting pan. Pour stock around (not over).
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre of the largest muscle, entering from the side, away from the bone.
4. Roast 4.5–5 hours, basting with pan juices every 45 minutes.
5. **Pull at 68–70 °C (154–158 °F).** Ottolenghi targets a slightly more cooked-through finish; carryover takes it to 72–74 °C.
6. Rest 30 minutes loosely tented. Carve. Strew with pomegranate seeds, walnuts and mint; drizzle with extra molasses. Final serving temperature: **72–74 °C / 162–165 °F** (sweet-savoury, fruit-and-nut crowned).

### 3. Tina Nordström — Helstekt Färsk Skinka (Swedish home tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009), pp. 184–186; köket.se  
**Serves**: 12 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 4–4.5 hr · **Rest**: 30 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 färsk skinka (uncured pork leg), 4.5–5 kg, bone-in, skin on, scored 2 cm cross-hatch
- 60 g flaksalt
- 5 g (1 tsp) vitpeppar (white pepper)
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar (allspice)
- 6 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 ml senap (Swedish sweet mustard)
- 30 g brown sugar
- 240 ml äppelmust (apple juice) for the pan
- 240 ml chicken stock for the pan
- **Glaze**: 60 g whole-grain senap + 60 g äppelmust + 30 g honey + 5 ml soya sauce

**Method**
1. The day before: rub the salt, white pepper, allspice and garlic into the leg, including the scored rind. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack 18–24 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Set leg on a rack in a heavy roasting pan. Smear with mustard and brown sugar; pour äppelmust and stock around (not over) the leg.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre of the largest muscle, away from the bone.
4. Roast 3.5–4 hours, basting every 60 minutes.
5. After 3 hours, brush with the glaze every 20 minutes.
6. **Pull at 67–69 °C (153–156 °F).** Nordström targets the Swedish standard — fully cooked but still juicy at 72–74 °C after carryover.
7. Rest 30 minutes loosely tented with foil. Carve perpendicular to the bone, 5 mm thick. Serve with rödkålssallad (red cabbage salad), kokt potatis and the strained pan juices. Final serving temperature: **71–74 °C / 160–165 °F** (juicy, glazed, allspice-perfumed).

### 4. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Slow-Roast Fresh Ham with Cider, Honey & Bay (British River Cottage tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *The River Cottage Meat Book* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2004), pp. 312–315; rivercottage.net  
**Serves**: 12 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 5 hr · **Rest**: 40 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 fresh ham (5 kg), bone-in, skin on, scored
- 90 g flaked sea salt for dry-brining
- 30 g (2 tbsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 12 fresh bay leaves, bashed
- 6 garlic cloves, sliced
- 480 ml dry cider for the pan
- 240 ml chicken or vegetable stock for the pan
- **Glaze**: 80 g raw honey + 30 g English mustard + 30 ml cider vinegar + 30 ml cider

**Method**
1. The day before: rub the leg with salt and pepper; press bay leaves into slits in the rind, tuck garlic slices into the same slits. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack 18 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Set leg on a rack in a heavy roasting pan. Pour cider and stock around the leg.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the geometric centre of the largest muscle, entering from the side.
4. Roast 4.5 hours, basting every 60 minutes with the pan liquor.
5. Mix glaze ingredients. Brush all over the ham; continue 30 minutes more, brushing every 10 minutes.
6. **Pull at 68–70 °C (154–158 °F).** Fearnley-Whittingstall's guideline: probe at 70 °C with a clear, light-rose juice running.
7. Rest 40 minutes loosely tented. Carve. Skim and reduce the pan juices to a sauce. Final serving temperature: **72–74 °C / 162–165 °F** (cider-glazed, herbal, sweet-savoury).
