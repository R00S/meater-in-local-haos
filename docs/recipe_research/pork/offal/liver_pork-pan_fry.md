## Cut profile

Pork liver is a large, smooth, mahogany-brown organ weighing 1–1.5 kg in a whole lobe, most commonly sold pre-sliced into 6–12 mm thick portions. It is milder and less intensely mineral than beef liver, with a finer-grained texture and a thinner capsule. Because it contains virtually no connective tissue, it responds entirely to direct heat: the goal is a hard, rapid sear on both sides to develop a deeply browned crust while leaving the centre pink and just safe at 60–71 °C / 140–160 °F. The standard USDA minimum is 71 °C / 160 °F, but culinary tradition across most of Europe targets a pink interior at 60–65 °C / 140–149 °F.

**Anatomy and structure**: pork liver is a single-lobe organ divided into lobes by thin capsule-covered fissures. The flesh is dense, granular (hepatocyte-rich), and uniformly thin in cross-section when pre-sliced. There is no intramuscular fat or connective tissue to break down — the only textural goal is Maillard-browning the surface while preserving a tender, just-set interior. Overcooking to 75 °C+ produces a dry, grainy, strongly mineral-tasting result.

**Temperature target**: pull at 60–65 °C / 140–149 °F for a pink, tender centre (culinary standard across French, Italian and Scandinavian traditions). For those who require USDA compliance, pull at 71 °C / 160 °F. Carryover is 1–2 °C in thin slices; pull early.

**Probe placement**: insert horizontally into the centre of the thickest slice in the pan, with the tip at the geometric centre of the slice. There are no bones or thick fat pockets to avoid. Avoid placing the probe in a very thin or edge slice as it will overcook before the thick centre reaches target temperature.

## Styckesprofil

Fläsklever är ett stort, blankt, mahognyfärgat organ som väger 1–1,5 kg hel, vanligtvis sålt förskuren i 6–12 mm tjocka portioner. Det är mildare och mindre intensivt mineralt än nötlever, med en finare textur och tunnare kapsel.

Pannsteka i smör vid hög värme, 2–3 minuter per sida; lever är fardig vid 70–75 °C. Innertemperatur: **70–75 °C (158–167 °F)** för genomstekt (standard); **65 °C** för lite rosa mitt (EFSA-godkänd om frisk källa). Eftertillagning: 2–3 °C. Sond insatt horisontellt in till mitten av skivan. Servera omedelbart — liver överkokkas snabbt och förlorar sin mjuka konsistens.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Fegato di Maiale alla Salvia (Italian tradition, Culinary Group B)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997); see also `pork_liver-pan_fry.md` (same cut, same source, slug `pork_liver`)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork liver, sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g (½ cup) plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 12 fresh sage leaves
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry white wine
- 5 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat very dry.
2. Mix flour, salt and pepper on a plate. Dust slices, tapping off excess.
3. Heat olive oil and 15 g butter in a wide heavy pan over high heat until the butter foam subsides.
4. Add sage leaves; let crisp 10 seconds. Lay slices in the pan in a single layer.
5. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at geometric centre.
6. Sear 60–90 seconds; flip; sear 60 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Hazan's standard: "rosato al centro" — pink at the centre. The crust must be deeply browned.
8. Add wine; let it sizzle 30 seconds; swirl in the remaining 15 g butter. Sprinkle parsley. Rest 1 minute. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (sage-perfumed, faint pink centre).

### 2. Tina Nordström — Stekt Fläsklever med Lingon (Swedish tradition, Culinary Group D)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); see also `pork_liver-pan_fry.md` (same cut, same source, slug `pork_liver`)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fläsklever (pork liver), sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g vetemjöl (plain flour)
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar (allspice)
- 50 g smör (butter)
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced thin
- 60 g rårörda lingon (uncooked stirred lingonberries with sugar)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry-ättika (sherry vinegar), to finish
- 5 g chopped persilja (parsley), to finish

**Method**
1. Soak slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat dry.
2. In a wide heavy pan, melt 25 g butter over medium heat; cook onions slowly 15 minutes until golden. Set aside warm.
3. Mix flour, salt, white pepper and allspice; dust the slices.
4. In the same pan, melt the remaining 25 g butter over high heat until foam subsides.
5. Lay slices in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
6. Sear 60 seconds; flip; sear 60 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Nordström's *stekt fläsklever* standard: "rosa men inte rå" — pink but not raw, the Swedish home preparation.
8. Return onions; toss briefly. Splash with vinegar. Sprinkle with parsley. Spoon a generous tablespoon of lingon onto each plate. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (allspice-perfumed, sweet-tart counterpoint).
