# Pork Cheek × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pork cheek (*carrillera de cerdo*, *guanciale di maiale*, *griskind*) is the small dense muscle of the jaw — roughly 80–120 g per cheek, marbled with thin sheets of intramuscular fat and rich in collagen from the constant chewing motion the muscle performs in life. For pan frying (rather than the more common braise), the cheeks are typically butterflied to expose the interior, dusted lightly, and seared at high heat — the goal is to crust the surface while keeping the centre at a tender medium-rare. This works because the cheek's collagen network, while extensive, is fine-grained enough that a brief flash sear (rather than a long braise) leaves the muscle bouncy rather than rubbery. The carryover is small (2–3 °C) given the thin butterflied cuts.

## Source recipes

### 1. José Pizarro — Carrillera al Horno con Pimentón (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: José Pizarro, *Andalusia* (Hardie Grant, 2019); josepizarro.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ overnight marinade) · **Cook**: 90 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks (about 12), trimmed
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 10 g (2 tsp) sweet smoked paprika (pimentón dulce)
- 2 g (½ tsp) hot smoked paprika (pimentón picante)
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) dried oregano
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry sherry (fino or oloroso)
- 240 ml (1 cup) chicken stock for the pan
- **To finish**: 30 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, lemon wedges

**Method**
1. The night before: mix olive oil, garlic, both paprikas, cumin, oregano, salt and pepper into a paste. Rub all over the cheeks. Refrigerate overnight.
2. Preheat oven to 170 °C (340 °F). Bring cheeks to room temperature 30 minutes.
3. Arrange the cheeks in a heavy oven-proof gratin dish in a single snug layer. Pour the sherry and stock around (not over) the cheeks; the liquid should reach about 1 cm up the sides.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
5. Roast 80–90 minutes, basting every 20 minutes with the pan juices.
6. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F).** Pizarro's test: a knife slides into the cheek with no resistance and the surface is deeply browned.
7. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented. Spoon the pan juices over the cheeks; sprinkle with parsley; add a lemon wedge. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (paprika-edged, sherry-perfumed, fork-tender with a roast crust).

### 2. Marcella Hazan — Guancia di Maiale Arrosto (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997) (the *al forno* variant of her braise)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 80 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks, trimmed
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 sage leaves
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 6 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 240 ml (1 cup) dry white wine
- 240 ml (1 cup) chicken stock
- A grating of nutmeg
- **To finish**: 5 g chopped fresh sage, 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 175 °C (350 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy oven-proof pan over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks in batches 2 minutes per side; remove briefly.
3. Return cheeks to the pan in a single snug layer. Tuck sage, rosemary and garlic between them. Pour wine and stock around; grate over a little nutmeg.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
5. Transfer to the oven, uncovered. Roast 70–80 minutes, basting every 20 minutes.
6. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F).** Hazan's test: the cheeks have caramelised tops and a paring knife meets no resistance.
7. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented. Reduce the pan juices on the stovetop to a glossy sauce; off heat finish with the second 30 ml of olive oil and fresh sage. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (herbal, wine-bright, glossy-crusted).

### 3. Diana Henry — Roast Pork Cheeks with Apples & Sage (British tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015) and her *Telegraph* recipe column; telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 90 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks, trimmed
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 2 medium onions, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 8 fresh sage leaves
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 360 ml (1½ cups) dry cider
- 240 ml (1 cup) chicken stock
- 2 large Bramley or Cox apples, peeled and cut into 8 wedges (added in last 30 minutes)
- 100 ml double cream, to finish (optional)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 170 °C (340 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil and butter in a heavy oven-proof pan over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks 2 minutes per side; remove.
3. Add onions; sauté 8 minutes. Add garlic and sage; 1 minute.
4. Return cheeks to the pan in a single snug layer. Pour cider and stock around (not over) the cheeks.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
6. Transfer to the oven, uncovered. Roast 60 minutes, basting every 20 minutes. Add apple wedges; continue 30 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F).** Henry's test: the cheeks are deeply caramelised on top, a knife slides in with no resistance.
8. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented. Optional: lift cheeks and apples; reduce pan juices; off heat swirl in cream. Spoon over the cheeks. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (apple-sweet, cider-bright, fork-tender, crusted).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Ugnsbakade Griskindar med Senap (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015) — oven variant of the cheek braise  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 75 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1.2 kg pork cheeks (griskind), trimmed
- 30 ml cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 60 g grov senap (whole-grain Swedish mustard)
- 30 ml soya sauce
- 30 g acacia honey
- 240 ml ljus öl (light lager) or chicken stock
- 1 tbsp whole kryddpeppar (allspice berries), bruised
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 4 bay leaves
- **To finish**: 15 g chopped fresh dill, 1 small shallot finely chopped, 30 g cold cultured butter

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 175 °C (350 °F). Pat cheeks dry; season with salt and white pepper.
2. Whisk mustard, soya and honey into a glaze.
3. Heat rapeseed oil in a heavy oven-proof gryta over medium-high heat. Sear cheeks 2 minutes per side; remove.
4. Return cheeks to the gryta in a single snug layer. Brush thickly with the mustard glaze. Pour the beer or stock around (not over) the cheeks; scatter allspice, thyme and bay.
5. Insert MEATER probe into the centre of one of the largest cheeks.
6. Transfer to oven, uncovered. Roast 60–75 minutes, basting every 20 minutes.
7. **Pull at 91–93 °C (196–199 °F).** Nilsson's test: a knife slides through the cheek with no resistance and the glaze has caramelised.
8. Rest 10 minutes loosely tented. Reduce the pan juices; off heat swirl in cold butter. Spoon over cheeks; sprinkle with dill and shallot. Serve with kokt potatis. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (mustard-glossed, allspice-perfumed, fork-tender).
