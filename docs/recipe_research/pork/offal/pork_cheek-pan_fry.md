# Pork Cheek × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan frying pork cheeks requires butterflying each cheek to ~1 cm thickness to expose the interior muscle fibre. The goal is rapid surface crust formation while keeping the centre below full collagen conversion temperature — the resulting texture is bouncy, pink-centred, and spring rather than gelatinous. Because the butterflied geometry is thin, cooking is fast (3–4 minutes total) and carryover is small (2–3 °C). Pull temperature is 65–67 °C (149–153 °F) — above USDA safe for ground meat but below the conversion temperature where the cheek collagen would tighten and toughen the fibre. High heat and dry-pan technique are essential; any liquid or steam shifts the result toward braised texture.

## Source recipes

### 1. José Pizarro — Carrillera Salteada (Spanish pintxo tradition)
**Source**: José Pizarro, *Spanish Flavours* (Kyle Books, 2012); josepizarro.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork cheeks (about 6 cheeks), trimmed of silver-skin and butterflied to 1 cm thickness
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked paprika (pimentón dulce)
- 2 g (½ tsp) hot smoked paprika (pimentón picante)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 60 ml (¼ cup) Pedro Ximénez sherry
- 5 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish
- A squeeze of lemon, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat cheeks very dry. Mix salt, pepper and both paprikas; rub over the butterflied cheeks just before cooking.
2. Heat olive oil in a wide heavy pan over high heat until shimmering. Add garlic for 60 seconds to perfume; remove.
3. Lay the cheeks in a single layer. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the centre of the thickest cheek, tip at geometric centre.
4. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds.
5. **Pull at 65–67 °C (149–153 °F).** Pizarro's target is "rosado al centro" (pink at the centre) — the pintxo standard.
6. Add sherry; let it sizzle 30 seconds; baste the cheeks. Off the heat, sprinkle parsley and lemon juice. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **68–70 °C / 154–158 °F** (paprika-edged, pink-centred, sherry-bright).

### 2. Marcella Hazan — Guancia di Maiale Saltata in Padella (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997) (cheek preparation extended from the cured *guanciale* notes)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork cheeks, trimmed and butterflied to 1 cm
- 30 g (¼ cup) plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 4 fresh sage leaves
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry Marsala wine
- A small handful of capers, drained, to finish
- 5 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat cheeks dry. Season with salt and pepper. Dust in flour, tapping off excess.
2. Heat oil and 15 g butter in a wide heavy pan over high heat until the butter foam subsides.
3. Add sage leaves; 10 seconds. Lay cheeks in the pan in a single layer.
4. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre of the thickest cheek.
5. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds more.
6. **Pull at 65–67 °C (149–153 °F).** Hazan's standard for the pan-fried cheek is "rosato all'interno" (pink inside) — the cheek must remain bouncy rather than firm.
7. Add Marsala; let it bubble and reduce 30 seconds; swirl in the remaining 15 g butter and capers. Off heat, sprinkle with parsley. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **68–70 °C / 154–158 °F** (sage-perfumed, sweet-Marsala glazed).

### 3. Diana Henry — Pan-Fried Pork Cheek with Apple & Cider (British tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015) and her *Telegraph* column at telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink — cheek section  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork cheeks, trimmed and butterflied to 1 cm
- 30 g (¼ cup) plain flour
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 small Cox or Russet apple, peeled and cut into 5 mm slices
- 1 small shallot, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) fresh thyme leaves
- 120 ml (½ cup) dry cider
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) apple cider vinegar, to deglaze
- 60 ml (¼ cup) double cream, to finish
- 5 g chopped fresh parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat cheeks dry; season; dust in flour, tapping off excess.
2. Heat oil and butter in a wide heavy pan over high heat. Add the apple slices and shallot; sauté 3 minutes until lightly browned. Remove to a warm dish.
3. Lay cheeks in the pan in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest cheek, tip at geometric centre.
4. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds.
5. **Pull at 65–67 °C (149–153 °F).** Henry's check: the cheeks have a deep golden crust and feel bouncy when pressed.
6. Remove cheeks to warm plates with the apple and shallot. Pour in cider; reduce by half (2 minutes); add vinegar; swirl in cream. Spoon over the cheeks; sprinkle with parsley. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **68–70 °C / 154–158 °F** (apple-sweet, cream-rich, herbal).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Pan-Seared Griskind with Brown Butter & Capers (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015) — pork cheek section  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork cheeks (griskind), trimmed and butterflied to 1 cm
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 60 g cultured unsalted butter
- 30 g salted capers, rinsed and patted dry
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cider vinegar
- 15 g chopped fresh dill, to finish
- 1 small shallot, finely chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat cheeks very dry. Season with salt and white pepper just before cooking.
2. Heat rapeseed oil in a wide heavy pan over high heat until shimmering and just hazing.
3. Lay cheeks in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre of the thickest cheek.
4. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds more.
5. **Pull at 64–66 °C (147–151 °F).** Nilsson's Nordic standard is on the cooler side of pink — he calls it "blank rosa" (gleaming rose) and warns that any further cooking turns the cheeks rubbery.
6. Move cheeks to warm plates. Off heat, add the butter to the pan; let it brown to *beurre noisette* (60–90 seconds), then add capers and vinegar. Spoon over the cheeks; sprinkle with dill and shallot. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **66–68 °C / 151–154 °F** (cool-rose centre, brown-butter halo, sharp caper counterpoint).
