# Pork Liver × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pan frying pork liver follows the same core technique as beef liver: hot fat, high direct heat, minimal time. The key difference from pan searing is the presence of a starchy coating (flour, fine breadcrumbs, or cornstarch) and the use of enough fat to create a shallow-fry environment where the crust forms by contact with the hot fat, not just the hot pan surface. A flour dusting draws out surface moisture, forms a thin protective crust, and promotes even browning across the entire face of the slice.

Standard liver and onions: caramelise sliced onions first (10–15 min in butter over medium heat), remove from pan; increase heat to high; add more butter and oil; fry liver slices 1–2 minutes per side. The onions are returned to the pan for final 30 seconds and serve together. The liver must not stack — crowding drops pan temperature and causes steaming. Carryover: 1–2 °C for thin slices. Pull at 60–65 °C for a pink, tender centre; 71 °C for USDA compliance.

## Styckesprofil

Pannstekning av fläsklever följer samma kärntekniken som nötlever: het fett, hög direkt värme, minimal tid. Skillnaden mot pannstekning (pan sear) är närvaron av ett stärkelsehölje (mjöl, fina brödsmulor eller majsstärkelse) och användning av tillräckligt fett.

Mjölhölje + smör + hög värme: 2–3 minuter per sida. Innertemperatur: **70–75 °C (158–167 °F)** för genomstekt. Eftertillagning: 2–3 °C. Sond insatt horisontellt in till mitten av skivan. Servera omedelbart — lever överkokkas snabbt och förlorar sin mjuka konsistens.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Fegato di Maiale alla Salvia (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork liver, sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g (½ cup) plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 12 fresh sage leaves
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry white wine
- 5 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat very dry.
2. Mix flour, salt and pepper on a plate. Dust slices, tapping off excess.
3. Heat olive oil and 15 g butter in a wide heavy pan over high heat until the butter foam subsides.
4. Add sage leaves; let crisp 10 seconds. Lay slices in the pan in a single layer.
5. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at geometric centre.
6. Sear 60–90 seconds; flip; sear 60 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Hazan's standard: "rosato al centro" — pink at the centre. The crust must be deeply browned.
8. Add wine; let it sizzle 30 seconds; swirl in the remaining 15 g butter. Sprinkle parsley. Rest 1 minute. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (sage-perfumed, faint pink centre).

### 2. Anthony Bourdain — Foie de Porc à la Lyonnaise (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: Anthony Bourdain & José de Meirelles, *Les Halles Cookbook* (Bloomsbury, 2004) (the *foie de porc* variant of his calf's liver Lyonnaise)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 12 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork liver, sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g plain flour for dusting
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 50 g unsalted butter, divided
- 2 large onions, very thinly sliced
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) red-wine vinegar
- 15 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat dry.
2. Heat 25 g butter in a wide heavy pan over low-medium heat. Add onions; cook slowly 18 minutes, stirring, until deeply caramelised. Set aside warm.
3. Mix flour, salt and pepper; dust the slices.
4. In the same pan over high heat, add the sunflower oil. Lay the slices in a single layer.
5. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
6. Sear 60–90 seconds; flip; sear 60 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Bourdain's "*rose au milieu*" — pink in the middle.
8. Move slices to warm plates. Off heat, add the remaining 25 g butter and vinegar to the pan; let bubble 30 seconds. Spoon over the slices with the warm onions; sprinkle parsley. Rest 1 minute. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (sweet-onion crowned, vinegar-bright).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Suar ka Kaleji (North Indian / Goan tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982) (pork variation noted in the Goan section)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g pork liver, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 45 ml (3 tbsp) ghee or vegetable oil
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 2 green chillies, finely chopped
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground turmeric
- 2 g (½ tsp) Kashmiri red chilli powder
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) tamarind paste (Goan signature, balances richness)
- 2 g (½ tsp) garam masala
- 5 g chopped fresh coriander, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat ghee in a wide heavy karahi or pan over high heat until shimmering.
2. Add onion; sauté 4 minutes. Add garlic, ginger, chillies; 1 minute.
3. Bloom coriander, cumin, turmeric, chilli powder and cinnamon in the oil 30 seconds.
4. Add the liver cubes. Insert MEATER probe into the largest central cube.
5. Stir-fry 90–120 seconds — the cubes will sear and seal.
6. Add tamarind, salt and 30 ml water; cook 60–90 seconds more, stirring.
7. **Pull at 63–66 °C (145–151 °F).** Jaffrey's standard: cubes firm to the touch, rose-pink centre.
8. Off heat, sprinkle with garam masala and coriander. Final serving temperature: **65–68 °C / 149–154 °F** (spice-crusted, tangy-bright, succulent).

### 4. Tina Nordström — Stekt Fläsklever med Lingon (Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g fläsklever (pork liver), sliced 6 mm thick, membranes removed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 50 g vetemjöl
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar (allspice)
- 50 g smör (butter)
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced thin
- 60 g rårörda lingon (uncooked stirred lingonberries with sugar)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry-ättika, to finish
- 5 g chopped persilja (parsley), to finish

**Method**
1. Soak slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat dry.
2. In a wide heavy pan, melt 25 g butter over medium heat; cook onions slowly 15 minutes until golden. Set aside warm.
3. Mix flour, salt, white pepper and allspice; dust the slices.
4. In the same pan, melt the remaining 25 g butter over high heat until foam subsides.
5. Lay slices in a single layer. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
6. Sear 60 seconds; flip; sear 60 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Nordström's *stekt fläsklever* standard: "rosa men inte rå" — pink but not raw, the Swedish home preparation.
8. Return onions; toss briefly. Splash with vinegar. Sprinkle with parsley. Spoon a generous tablespoon of lingon onto each plate. Serve with potatismos. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (allspice-perfumed, sweet-tart counterpoint).
