# Pork Liver × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan-seared pork liver, like its beef counterpart, is a high-heat technique applied to a thicker slice (8–12 mm rather than 5–6 mm) intended to produce an aggressively crusted exterior with a deliberately rare-to-medium-rare centre. Pork liver's milder, slightly sweeter flavour profile makes it more forgiving than beef or calf's liver at slightly cooler internal temperatures — the classic restaurant target is 58–60 °C, on the cooler side of *rosé*. Carryover is moderate (2–3 °C) given the thicker slice; pulling 2 °C below the desired serving temperature is essential because the slice will continue rising on a warm plate.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fergus Henderson — Pig's Liver, Bacon and Onions (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *Beyond Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury, 2007); stjohnrestaurant.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 thick slices (≈ 180 g each, 12 mm thick) pig's liver, membranes carefully removed
- 8 thick rashers smoked streaky bacon
- 2 large white onions, halved and sliced
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) duck fat or sunflower oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry vinegar, to deglaze
- 15 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat half the butter in a heavy pan over low-medium heat. Add onions with a pinch of salt; cook 25 minutes until soft and golden, stirring. Set aside warm.
2. In the same pan over medium-high, fry bacon until crisp; remove to drain on paper.
3. Pat liver slices very dry. Season with salt and pepper just before cooking.
4. Add duck fat to the bacon fat; bring to a hard sear over high heat — the fat should ripple and barely smoke.
5. Lay slices in the pan well spaced. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice, tip at geometric centre.
6. Sear 90 seconds undisturbed; flip; sear 60–90 seconds more.
7. **Pull at 58–61 °C (136–142 °F).** Henderson's "blushing pink" — mahogany crust, deeply rose centre.
8. Remove slices to warm plates. Deglaze the pan with sherry vinegar; reduce by half, swirl in the remaining 15 g butter. Top liver with onions, bacon, pan sauce and parsley. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **60–63 °C / 140–145 °F** (deeply rosé, crisp-edged).

### 2. José Pizarro — Hígado de Cerdo a la Plancha (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: José Pizarro, *Spanish Flavours* (Kyle Books, 2012); josepizarro.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 slices pork liver (≈ 180 g each, 10 mm thick)
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 5 g (1 tsp) sweet smoked paprika (pimentón dulce)
- 2 g (½ tsp) hot smoked paprika (pimentón picante)
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 60 ml (¼ cup) Pedro Ximénez sherry
- 5 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat slices very dry. Season with salt and pepper; dust with both paprikas just before cooking.
2. Heat olive oil in a wide heavy pan over high heat until shimmering. Add garlic; cook 60 seconds until pale gold; remove.
3. Lay slices in the pan well spaced. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
4. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds.
5. **Pull at 60–63 °C (140–145 °F).** Pizarro's "rosado pero no crudo" — pink but not raw.
6. Remove to warm plates. Pour in the sherry; let it sizzle and reduce 30 seconds. Pour over the liver. Rest 1 minute. Sprinkle with parsley. Final serving temperature: **62–65 °C / 144–149 °F** (paprika-edged, sweet-sherry glossed).

### 3. Magnus Nilsson — Seared Fläsklever with Brown Butter & Capers (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015) (the pork-liver variant of his calf's liver method)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 slices pork liver (≈ 160 g each, 10 mm thick), trimmed of membrane
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 60 g cultured unsalted butter
- 30 g salted capers, rinsed and patted dry
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cider vinegar, to deglaze
- 60 g (¼ cup) raw stirred lingonberries (rårörda lingon)
- 15 g chopped fresh dill, to finish

**Method**
1. Pat slices very dry. Season with salt and white pepper just before cooking.
2. Heat the rapeseed oil in a wide heavy pan over high heat until shimmering and just beginning to haze.
3. Lay slices in the pan well spaced. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
4. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds more.
5. **Pull at 56–58 °C (132–136 °F).** Nilsson's Nordic standard: "blank rosa" (gleaming rose) — on the cooler side of pink.
6. Move slices to warm plates. Off heat, add butter; let it brown to *beurre noisette* (60–90 seconds), add capers and vinegar. Spoon over the liver; top each slice with a spoon of lingonberries; sprinkle dill. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **59–61 °C / 138–142 °F** (cool-rose centre, brown-butter halo, sweet-tart berry counterpoint).

### 4. Diana Henry — Pan-Seared Pork Liver with Mustard & Apples (British modern tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015) and her *Telegraph* recipe column at telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+ 30 min milk soak) · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 slices pork liver (≈ 175 g each, 10 mm thick), trimmed
- 240 ml whole milk, for soaking
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 50 g unsalted butter, divided
- 1 small Cox or Russet apple, peeled and cut into 5 mm slices
- 1 small shallot, finely chopped
- 30 g Dijon mustard
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) cider vinegar
- 60 ml (¼ cup) double cream, to finish
- 5 g chopped fresh thyme leaves, to finish

**Method**
1. Soak the slices in milk 30 minutes. Drain; pat very dry.
2. Heat oil and 25 g butter in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add apple slices and shallot; sauté 3 minutes; remove to a warm dish.
3. Increase heat to high. Lay slices well spaced. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the centre slice.
4. Sear 90 seconds; flip; sear 60–90 seconds.
5. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F).** Henry's "rose-pink in the middle" — the spice crust deeply browned, the interior soft.
6. Remove slices to warm plates with the apple and shallot. Off heat, whisk Dijon and vinegar into the pan, scrape up fond, swirl in cream and the remaining 25 g butter. Spoon over the liver; sprinkle with thyme. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **60–63 °C / 140–145 °F** (apple-bright, mustard-cream sauced, rosé centre).
