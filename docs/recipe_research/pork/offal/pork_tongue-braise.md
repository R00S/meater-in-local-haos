# Pork Tongue × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pork tongue is the smaller, more delicate sibling of beef tongue — typically 250–400 g per tongue versus 1.5–2 kg for beef. The structure is identical: a single dense lingual muscle wrapped in a thick, papillae-covered skin that must be peeled after cooking. The smaller mass means a shorter braise (60–90 minutes rather than 3–4 hours) and a milder, sweeter flavour. Pork tongue is fundamental to many traditions — Mexican *lengua de cerdo*, Chinese cha-shu pig tongue, French *langue de porc sauce ravigote*, Polish *ozór wieprzowy*. The peel-while-warm rule still applies, and the tongue is best sliced thin and either dressed with a sharp vinaigrette or warmed in a piquant sauce. Carryover is essentially zero (the surrounding liquid buffers temperature).

## Source recipes

### 1. Fergus Henderson — Pig's Tongue Salad with Mustard & Capers (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *Beyond Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury, 2007), pp. 122–124; stjohnrestaurant.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 90 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (about 1.8 kg total)
- 2 large carrots, roughly chopped
- 2 onions, halved
- 1 head garlic, halved across the equator
- 4 stalks celery, halved
- 1 bouquet garni (parsley stalks, thyme, bay)
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- Cold water to cover
- **Salad dressing**: 30 g Dijon mustard, 60 ml red-wine vinegar, 120 ml extra-virgin olive oil, 30 g salted capers (rinsed), 1 small shallot finely chopped, 30 g flat-leaf parsley chopped, salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Place tongues in a stockpot. Cover with cold water by 5 cm. Add carrots, onions, garlic, celery, bouquet garni, peppercorns and salt.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim foam. Reduce to the gentlest simmer (about 85 °C).
3. After 30 minutes, insert MEATER probe through the base end of one tongue, pushing horizontally to the geometric centre.
4. Cover partially. Continue 60 minutes more.
5. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Henderson's test: a paring knife slid into the thickest part should meet no resistance.
6. Lift tongues onto a board. Within 5 minutes, while still hot, peel off the skin; trim the gristle and base.
7. Slice each tongue thin (3 mm) on the diagonal. Whisk dressing and toss with the warm tongue, capers, shallot and parsley. Rest 10 minutes warm covered. Final serving temperature: **45–55 °C / 113–131 °F** (sliced, dressed warm — Henderson's "warm salad" service).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Lengua de Cerdo en Salsa de Chile Pasilla (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *Mexican Regional Cooking* (Harper & Row, 1990), pp. 248–250  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 90 min (tongue) + 20 min (sauce) · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (about 1.8 kg)
- 1 large white onion, quartered
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bay leaves
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- Cold water to cover
- **Salsa de chile pasilla**: 6 dried chile pasilla (stemmed, seeded), 4 garlic cloves, 1 small onion (quartered), 4 plum tomatoes (or 1 tin), 1 tsp Mexican oregano, 5 g salt, 240 ml braising liquid, 30 ml vegetable oil

**Method**
1. Place tongues in a stockpot with onion, garlic, bay and salt. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim. Reduce to the gentlest simmer.
3. After 30 minutes, insert MEATER probe through the base end of one tongue. Continue 60 minutes more.
4. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Kennedy's standard: knife passes through with no resistance.
5. Lift onto a board; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice each tongue 5 mm thick on the diagonal.
6. For the salsa: toast the chiles dry in a comal 30 seconds per side until pliable. Soak in hot water 15 minutes. Char the tomatoes, onion and garlic on a comal 5 minutes. Blend chiles, tomatoes, onion, garlic, oregano, salt and 240 ml braising liquid until smooth.
7. Heat oil in a saucepan; pour in the salsa; fry 5 minutes until colour deepens.
8. Lay tongue slices in the salsa; warm together 5 minutes. Rest 5 minutes off heat. Serve in warm corn tortillas. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (chile-deep, sauced, tender).

### 3. Anthony Bourdain — Langue de Porc Sauce Ravigote (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: Anthony Bourdain & José de Meirelles, *Les Halles Cookbook* (Bloomsbury, 2004), pp. 192–194 (the pork-tongue variant of his beef tongue piquante)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 90 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (1.8 kg)
- 2 carrots, roughly chopped
- 2 leeks (white and pale-green), roughly chopped
- 1 onion studded with 4 cloves
- 1 bouquet garni (thyme, parsley, bay)
- 10 black peppercorns
- 30 g salt
- Cold water to cover
- **Sauce ravigote**: 30 ml Dijon mustard, 60 ml red-wine vinegar, 120 ml peanut oil + 60 ml extra-virgin olive oil, 30 g cornichons (chopped), 30 g capers (drained), 1 small shallot (finely chopped), 30 g chopped fresh tarragon and parsley, 1 hard-cooked egg yolk (chopped fine, optional)

**Method**
1. Place tongues in a stockpot with carrots, leeks, clove-studded onion, bouquet garni, peppercorns and salt. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim. Reduce to gentle simmer.
3. After 30 minutes, insert MEATER probe through the base end of one tongue. Continue 60 minutes more.
4. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Bourdain's standard: knife passes through "as into warm butter."
5. Lift; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice each tongue 5 mm thick on the diagonal.
6. Whisk mustard with vinegar; emulsify in oil; stir in cornichons, capers, shallot, tarragon, parsley and egg yolk if using. Salt and pepper.
7. Arrange tongue slices on a platter; spoon sauce over. Rest 10 minutes warm covered. Final serving temperature: **45–55 °C / 113–131 °F** (warm, herb-bright, mustard-sharp).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Inkokt Grisetunga med Pepparrotssås (Swedish/Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 390–391 — the pork-tongue variant of his salt-cured beef tongue  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 90 min · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (1.8 kg)
- 2 large carrots, roughly chopped
- 2 yellow onions, halved
- 1 small celeriac, peeled and chunked
- 1 leek (white and pale-green), halved
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp whole kryddpeppar (allspice berries)
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 30 g salt
- Cold water to cover
- **Pepparrotssås (horseradish cream sauce)**: 30 g unsalted butter, 30 g flour, 480 ml strained braising liquid, 120 ml grädde (cream), 30 g freshly grated horseradish, 5 g salt, white pepper, 5 ml cider vinegar

**Method**
1. Place tongues in a stockpot with carrots, onions, celeriac, leek, bay, allspice, peppercorns and salt. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
2. Bring slowly to a simmer; skim. Reduce to gentle simmer (≈ 85 °C).
3. After 30 minutes, insert MEATER probe through the base end of one tongue. Continue 60 minutes more.
4. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Nilsson's standard: knife passes through with no resistance.
5. Lift; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice each tongue 5 mm thick on the diagonal.
6. Make the sauce: melt butter, whisk in flour, cook 1 minute. Whisk in 480 ml strained braising liquid; simmer to thicken. Stir in cream, horseradish, salt, pepper and vinegar.
7. Lay tongue slices in a warm gratin dish; nap with the pepparrotssås. Rest 15 minutes warm covered. Serve with kokt potatis and inlagda gurkor. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (silky, horseradish-bright, allspice-perfumed).
