# Pork Tongue × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pork tongue cooked in an electric slow cooker is the same long-braise preparation as the stove-top method, executed in a sealed environment that holds the cooking liquid steady at 85–95 °C without monitoring. Because the tongue is small (250–400 g per tongue), the cook time is shorter than for beef tongue — 5–6 hours on Low or 3 hours on High is standard for a batch of 6 tongues. The peel-while-warm rule still applies; the skin must come off within 5–10 minutes of lifting from the cooker. Carryover is zero — the tongue stays at cooker temperature until removed. Sear the tongue base briefly in a hot skillet first if you want a deeper-flavoured stock, but it is optional.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Pork Tongue Confit (American Southern tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017); hughacheson.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 6 hr (low) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (1.8 kg)
- 1 large onion, quartered
- 2 carrots, roughly cut
- 2 stalks celery, roughly cut
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 240 ml dry white wine
- Cold water to cover (1.5 L)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sherry vinegar, to finish
- **To serve**: crusty bread, Dijon mustard, cornichons, watercress

**Method**
1. Place the tongues at the bottom of a 6-quart slow cooker. Surround with vegetables, garlic, thyme, bay and peppercorns. Add salt.
2. Pour in the wine, then cover with cold water until tongues are submerged.
3. Insert the MEATER Plus probe through the base end of one tongue, pushing it horizontally into the centre of the muscle.
4. Cover. Cook on Low 6 hours.
5. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Acheson's check: a thin metal skewer passes through the thickest part with no resistance.
6. Lift onto a board. Within 5–10 minutes, while still very warm, peel the skin in sheets; trim the gristle and base.
7. Strain the cooking liquid; reduce 240 ml of it with the sherry vinegar to a sauce. Slice each tongue thinly on the diagonal. Rest 15 minutes warm covered. Serve sliced with bread, mustard, cornichons and watercress. Final serving temperature: **45–55 °C / 113–131 °F** (warm-confit style, peeled, mustard-sharp counterpoint).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Lengua de Cerdo en el Crock-Pot (Mexican tradition adaptation)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *Mexican Regional Cooking* (Harper & Row, 1990) — slow-cooker note in *From My Mexican Kitchen* (Clarkson Potter, 2003)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 6 hr (low) · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (1.8 kg)
- 1 large white onion, quartered
- 1 head garlic, halved
- 6 black peppercorns
- 4 cloves
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 30 ml white vinegar
- Cold water to cover

**Method**
1. Rinse tongues. Place in slow cooker with onion, garlic, peppercorns, cloves, bay, oregano and salt. Add vinegar; cover with cold water until submerged.
2. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the base end of one tongue.
3. Cover. Cook on Low 6 hours.
4. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Kennedy's standard: completely tender, the muscle yields to a paring knife with no effort.
5. Lift; peel within 5 minutes; trim.
6. Slice 5 mm thick or shred for tacos. Serve in warm corn tortillas with salsa verde, chopped onion, coriander and lime. Rest 10 minutes warm. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F**.

### 3. Andrea Nguyen — Lưỡi Heo Hầm in the Slow Cooker (Vietnamese tradition)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (Ten Speed Press, 2019) — pork tongue variation; vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 6 hr (low) · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 fresh pig's tongues (1.8 kg)
- 1 large onion, halved (charred 5 min under broiler)
- 50 g fresh ginger, sliced (also charred)
- 4 star anise pods
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 4 cloves
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fish sauce
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 15 g rock sugar
- 1.5 L water
- **To serve**: rice noodles, bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, sliced chillies, hoisin and sriracha

**Method**
1. Place tongues in slow cooker with charred onion and ginger, star anise, cinnamon and cloves. Add fish sauce, salt and rock sugar. Pour in water (tongues just submerged).
2. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the base end of one tongue.
3. Cover. Cook on Low 6 hours.
4. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Nguyen's chopstick test — slides right through the thickest portion.
5. Lift; peel within 5 minutes; trim. Slice paper-thin against the grain.
6. Strain broth; serve over rice noodles with the slices and the herb-bean-sprout-chilli condiments. Rest 10 minutes warm. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (spice-perfumed, broth-bathed).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Slow-Cooker Saltkokt Grisetunga (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015) — slow-cooker adaptation of his salt-cured tongue  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 24 hr cure if curing) · **Cook**: 6 hr (low) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 6 lightly cured (salt-and-sugar pickled) pig's tongues, 1.8 kg, soaked overnight in cold water and drained
- 2 large yellow onions, halved
- 2 carrots, cut in chunks
- 1 leek, white and pale-green only, halved
- 1 small celeriac, peeled and chunked
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 5 allspice berries (kryddpeppar)
- 4 cloves
- Cold water to cover
- **To serve**: peeled cooked potatoes, gräddsås, pickled cucumber, fresh dill

**Method**
1. Drain the soaked tongues. Place in the slow cooker with onions, carrots, leek, celeriac, bay, peppercorns, allspice and cloves. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
2. Insert MEATER Plus probe through the base end of one tongue.
3. Cover. Cook on Low 6 hours.
4. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Nilsson's *saltkokt tunga* standard — fully tender but a touch more cohesive than fully shreddable.
5. Lift; peel skin within 5 minutes; trim. Strain liquid for the gräddsås base.
6. Slice 5 mm thick. Serve with potatoes, the cream sauce (roux + 240 ml strained liquid + 120 ml grädde + Dijon), and pickled cucumber. Rest 15 minutes warm covered. Final serving temperature: **70–75 °C / 158–167 °F** (silky, cure-perfumed, dill-bright).
