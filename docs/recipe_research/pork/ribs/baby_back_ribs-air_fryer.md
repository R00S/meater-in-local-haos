# Baby Back Ribs × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Baby back ribs (also called loin ribs or kamben in Danish; Rippchen in German) are the upper ribs from the pork loin area — leaner, more curved, and smaller than spare ribs. In an air fryer the high-velocity hot air circulates around each section, cooking them much faster than a conventional oven (60–90 min vs. 2.5–3 hrs) while still achieving tender, fall-off-the-bone results. Most air-fryer methods either pre-boil the ribs (to accelerate internal tenderness) or rely on a long low-temperature cook phase followed by a short high-temperature crisping phase. The silver skin on the bone side must always be removed. Target internal: 88–95 °C for tender, collagen-rendered ribs; at 63 °C they are technically safe but not yet fall-off-bone.

Target internal temperatures (air fryer):
- Fall-off-bone tender: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)**
- ⚠️ Below 80 °C ribs are food-safe but still tough

## Styckesprofil

Baby back ribs (även kallad *loin ribs* eller *kamben* på danska; *Rippchen* tyska) är de övre revbenen från fläskloin-området — magrare, mer krökta och mindre än spareribs. I air fryer ger den höghastighets varma luften ett lätt rökigt resultat i 30–40 minuter vid 180–200 °C.

Innertemperatur: **88–95 °C (190–203 °F)** för fall-från-benet (USDA-standard för revben). Eftertillagning: försumbar. Sond insatt horisontellt längs benets längsriktning in till mitten av köttmassan, borta från benet.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Ribben i airfryer (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/ribben-i-airfryer/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"My recipe for ribs in the airfryer — the good bones, either from scratch with fresh ribs that you boil yourself, or pre-bought." 4.8/5 stars.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 40 min boil + 20 min air fryer

*Translation: Ribs in air fryer — "The pre-bought ribs only need 15 min at 180°C directly on the rack. The last 4–5 min brush with marinade."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 180 °C throughout; pre-boil reduces airfryer time to 20 min total; watch closely.

**Ingredients**
- 600 g ribben (baby back ribs)
- 1 løg (onion), coarsely chopped (for boiling)
- 3 laurbærblade (bay leaves)
- Salt
- 15 sorte peberkorn (black peppercorns)
- 1 portion BBQ sauce (homemade or shop-bought)

**Method**
1. Cut the ribs into sections. Remove the membrane from the back of each rack.
2. Place ribs in a suitable pot; cover with water just above the ribs.
3. Add onion, bay leaves, salt and peppercorns. Boil **40 minutes** with lid on; remove.
4. Make or prepare BBQ marinade.
5. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest section of one rack, tip at the geometric centre (away from bone).
6. Place ribs bone-side down on the air fryer rack. Cook at **180 °C × 10 minutes**.
7. Turn ribs; brush underside with BBQ marinade. Cook **5 minutes** at 180 °C.
8. Turn again; brush top surface with marinade. Cook **5 minutes** more.
9. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** Ribs should be tender with caramelised marinade glaze.
10. Rest 10 minutes (ribs remain very hot). Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (tender, sticky, caramelised).

### 2. Matprat — Ribbe i airfryer (Norwegian tradition)
**Source**: Matprat.no; https://www.matprat.no/oppskrifter/kos/ribbe-i-airfryer/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Norwegian pork ribs in the airfryer — low-and-slow phase then crisp-svor finish. No pre-boiling.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 2 days salting (or skip with pre-seasoned) · **Cook**: 2 hr 10–15 min · **Rest**: few min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 150 °C × 2 hours for tenderness, then 200 °C × 10–15 min for crackling. "Add about 2 dl water to the bottom of the air fryer." Monitor closely during the high-heat phase.

**Ingredients**
- 600 g ribbe (baby-back/spare rib rack with skin-on if using Norwegian-style)
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 tsp pepper

**Method**
1. Score through the rind with a sharp knife in a cross-hatch pattern parallel to the rib bones. Rub well with salt and pepper, massaging into the scored cracks. Cover and refrigerate **2 days** (or skip with pre-seasoned ribs from the shop).
2. Preheat air fryer to 150 °C. Pour approximately 2 dl water into the bottom tray.
3. Place ribs in the basket, rind side up. Cook at **150 °C × 2 hours**.
4. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest section from the side, tip in the geometric centre above the bone.
5. Increase temperature to **200 °C** and cook until the rind has fully puffed and crisped — **10–15 minutes**. Watch carefully to avoid burning.
6. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).**
7. Rest a few minutes. Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (tender, with crispy top).

### 3. ichkoche.at — Spareribs aus der Heißluftfritteuse – Saftig und Knusprig (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/spareribs-aus-der-heissluftfritteuse-rezept-232223 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Juicy and crispy spareribs from the air fryer with overnight marinade. 1 250 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: overnight marinade · **Cook**: 60–90 min

*Translation: "Spareribs from the hot-air fryer — juicy and crispy."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 150 °C for 60–90 minutes; baste with marinade during cooking for extra glaze.

**Ingredients**
- 2 racks spareribs / Ripperln (baby back ribs)
- For the marinade: olive oil, honey, soy sauce, brown sugar, chopped tomato, chilli powder, apple juice (quantities to taste — the recipe text notes "mix all marinade ingredients")
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Remove the silver skin (Silberhaut) from the bone side of each rack: score lightly, then pull off with a firm tug.
2. Mix marinade ingredients together. Massage into ribs on all sides.
3. Marinate overnight in the refrigerator, covered.
4. Allow ribs to come toward room temperature for 30 min.
5. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest section, tip clear of bone.
6. Place ribs in the air fryer basket. Drain excess marinade but keep for basting.
7. Cook at **150 °C × 60–90 minutes**. Optionally baste with reserved marinade partway through.
8. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Ribs should be tender and caramelised.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (juicy, deeply glazed).

### 4. Cucino per Passione (blog.giallozafferano.it) — Costine in friggitrice ad aria (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Barbara, Cucino per Passione; https://blog.giallozafferano.it/cucinoperpassione/ricetta-costine-in-friggitrice-ad-aria/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Simple Italian pork ribs in the air fryer — herbs only, no marinade. Fat drips away so ribs stay non-greasy.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 30 min

*Translation: "Pork ribs in the air fryer — a simple and tasty second course. The ribs stay soft; the fat melts and drips to the bottom so they are not greasy."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 180 °C × 15 min per side = 30 min total. "Cook time may vary depending on which air fryer you use."

**Ingredients**
- 500 g costine di maiale (pork ribs / baby back)
- q.b. sale (salt, to taste)
- q.b. erbe aromatiche (aromatic herbs — the recipe suggests roast-specific herbs, e.g., rosemary, thyme, sage)

**Method**
1. Dust the ribs generously with finely chopped aromatic herbs and salt on both sides.
2. Arrange ribs on the air fryer basket in a single layer.
3. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest rib section, tip at centre.
4. Cook at **180 °C × 15 minutes**.
5. Open basket; turn ribs over. Continue at **180 °C × 15 minutes** (total 30 min).
6. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** Barbara notes ribs should be tender and slightly golden.
7. Remove from basket and slice. Serve hot immediately. Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (tender, herb-scented, not greasy).

### 5. Directo al Paladar — Costillas de cerdo en freidora de aire (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: Carmen Tía Alia, Directoalpaladar.com; https://www.directoalpaladar.com/recetas-de-carnes-y-aves/como-hacer-costillas-cerdo-freidora-aire-tiernas-saboras-que-se-deshacen-boca (fetched 2026-04-24). *"So tender and tasty they melt in the mouth." BBQ sauce marinade, simple 20 min cook. 11 votes.*
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ overnight marinade) · **Cook**: 30 min

*Translation: "How to make pork ribs in the airfryer — tender and tasty enough to melt in the mouth."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: 180 °C × 20 min (flip halfway at 10 min). Note: "these are indicative parameters that you will probably need to adjust depending on the power of your air fryer and the size of the pieces."

**Ingredients**
- 1 costillar de cerdo / approx. 1 kg pork rib rack
- BBQ sauce (shop-bought or homemade), enough to coat generously
- Salt
- Freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Cut the rack into 2–3 sections as needed to fit the air fryer basket.
2. Season with salt and pepper. Cover generously with BBQ sauce.
3. Place in a deep container, cover with cling film and refrigerate several hours or overnight to absorb flavours.
4. Remove from fridge 1 hour before cooking to bring toward room temperature.
5. Place ribs in the basket, meaty side down.
6. Insert MEATER probe horizontally into the thickest section, tip at centre.
7. Cook at **180 °C × 20 minutes**, flipping halfway at 10 minutes.
8. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** Check: "for them not to overcook, the ideal is to check occasionally."
9. Remove carefully from basket; serve immediately. Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (tender, caramelised BBQ glaze).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Madens Verden (DK) | pre-boil + 180 °C × 20 min | 88–93 °C |
| Matprat (NO) | 150 °C × 2 hr + 200 °C × 10–15 min | 88–93 °C |
| ichkoche.at (AT) | 150 °C × 60–90 min | 90–95 °C |
| Cucino per Passione (IT) | 180 °C × 30 min | 88–93 °C |
| Directo al Paladar (ES) | 180 °C × 20 min | 88–93 °C |

**Consensus pull range**: 88–95 °C (190–203 °F). Most sources converge on 88–93 °C for tender-but-sliceable ribs; 93–95 °C gives full fall-off-the-bone texture.
