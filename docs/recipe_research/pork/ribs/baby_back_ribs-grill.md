# Baby Back Ribs × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Baby back ribs on the grill: the most popular method is indirect heat at ~150 °C for 1 hour wrapped in foil (no pre-boiling), then unwrap and finish 5–7 min per side with BBQ sauce. Alternatively: pre-boil 40–50 min then 20–25 min at 220 °C indirect. Both methods produce fall-off-the-bone ribs. The membrane on the back must be removed for any method. Target internal: 85–95 °C (ribs don't have a strict "safe" temperature beyond 63 °C for safety, but they need to be at 85–95 °C to become tender as the collagen renders).

Target internal temperatures (grill):
- Tender/fall-off-bone: **85–95 °C (185–203 °F)**
- ⚠️ Below 80 °C ribs are technically safe but not yet tender

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Verdens bedste spareribs (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/verdens-bedste-spareribs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"World's best spareribs — no pre-boiling needed." Dry rub; foil-wrapped; 1 hr at 150 °C direct grill; then BBQ sauce finish 5–7 min per side.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 1 hr foil + 14 min open

*Translation: "You don't need to pre-boil these. The trick is to wrap the ribs in foil, where they get to become tender. At the same time you retain all the flavour in the packets."*

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Keep grill temperature below 150 °C during the foil phase. "Ribs risk splitting if they go over 80 °C too quickly." Final phase: medium heat 5–7 min per side with BBQ sauce.

**Ingredients (dry rub)**
- 4 racks baby back ribs (spareribs)
- 1 tbsp paprika, 1 tbsp garlic powder, 1 tbsp dried thyme, 1 tbsp salt
- 1 tsp dried chilli, freshly ground pepper
- Apple juice (small bottle, for spraying)
- 1 portion BBQ sauce (homemade or bought)

**Method**
1. Remove membrane from the back of each rack — insert a knife tip between bone and membrane, pull off.
2. Cut each rack into 2–3 sections.
3. Mix all dry rub ingredients. Season both sides generously.
4. Place each section on double-layer foil. Spray or drizzle with apple juice. Seal foil packages tightly (careful of rib-bone punctures).
5. Place foil packages on grill at **low direct heat** (~150 °C). Grill **1 hour**, turning packages occasionally. Keep lid closed.
6. Remove packages; rest **10 minutes**. Make BBQ sauce.
7. Unwrap ribs; place on grill at **medium heat**. Brush with BBQ sauce.
8. Grill **5–7 minutes per side**, brushing each turn. Watch for scorching.
9. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F)** for tender.
10. Serve immediately.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 2. Madens Verden — Spareribs i grill (pre-boil method) (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/spareribs/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *Pre-boil 45–50 min; then indirect grill 220 °C × 20–25 min; brush with marinade at 15 min.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45 min boil + 25 min grill

**Temperature guidance**: Indirect grill at 220 °C. After boiling, ribs only need 20–25 min to finish with sauce glaze.

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg kamben (baby back ribs)
- 3 bay leaves, 1 red onion (halved), 20 black peppercorns, 2 tsp salt (for boiling)
- 1 portion BBQ/grill marinade (homemade)

**Method**
1. Cut racks to fit in a large pot; cover with water; add salt, bay leaves, onion, and peppercorns.
2. Simmer **45–50 min** at gentle heat; skim foam in first 5 min.
3. Remove; place in a tray.
4. Set up grill for **indirect heat** at 220 °C. Place ribs on rack or rack-stand in the centre. Close lid.
5. After **15 minutes**, brush with marinade on both sides.
6. Continue **5–10 more minutes** until caramelised.
7. Optionally: finish 2 min over direct heat for extra char.
8. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 88–93 °C (190–200 °F)** for tender.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*

### 3. ichkoche.at — Spare Ribs (adapted for grill) (Austrian tradition)
**Source**: ichkoche.at; https://www.ichkoche.at/spare-ribs-rezept-16908 (fetched 2026-04-24). *Overnight garlic-oil marinade; start at 200 °C then reduce to 150 °C × 45 min; adapted here for indirect grill use.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: overnight marinade · **Cook**: 60 min indirect grill

**Temperature guidance**: Start at 200 °C × 15 min (indirect), then reduce to 150 °C × 45 min. Baste with sauce several times. Core: 88–93 °C.

**Ingredients**
- 2 kg Ripperln (baby back ribs)
- 6 garlic cloves, 125 ml olive oil, salt and pepper (overnight marinade)
- Sauce: tomato ketchup, vinegar, honey, chilli, mustard, paprika

**Method**
1. Coat ribs in garlic-olive oil marinade overnight in fridge.
2. Make sauce: heat ketchup, vinegar, honey, chilli, mustard, paprika; simmer briefly.
3. Set up indirect grill zone.
4. Brush ribs with sauce; place on indirect zone.
5. Grill at **200 °C × 15 min**, then **reduce to 150 °C** and grill **45 more min**, turning several times and basting with sauce each turn.
6. Insert MEATER probe. **Core: 88–93 °C (190–200 °F)**.
*Final temperature: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).*
