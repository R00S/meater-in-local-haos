## Cut profile

Oven roasting is the most accessible method for St. Louis-style ribs, producing results comparable to a backyard smoker or grill using standard domestic equipment. Because St. Louis ribs are thick, fat-layered spare rib sections (cut from ribs 3–12 after breastbone removal), oven roasting is not a uniform technique — it covers two fundamentally different approaches with different textural targets:

**Low-and-slow approach (88–96 °C target)**: oven temperature 150–170 °C / 300–325 °F, duration 3–4 hours covered (then uncovered for a final baste-and-caramelise). The ribs cook fully submerged in their own rendered fat under foil; collagen dissolves to gelatin over this duration and the meat slides off the bone. Pull temperature at the thick intercostal section: 88–96 °C / 190–205 °F. This is the American BBQ oven method.

**High-heat approach (74–82 °C target)**: oven temperature 190–210 °C / 375–410 °F, duration 1–1.5 hours with basting. The exterior glazes and caramelises; the meat is cooked through and pulling slightly from the bone but retains more chew than the low-and-slow result. Pull temperature: 74–82 °C / 165–180 °F. This is characteristic of Asian char siu-style preparations.

The MEATER probe must be inserted lengthwise along the widest bone in the centre of the rack, with the tip in the thickest continuous lean intercostal section, 1–2 cm clear of the bone surface. Run parallel to the bone. Insert before the foil is sealed for low-and-slow; can be inserted just before the baste-and-finish phase for high-heat methods.

## Source recipes

### 1. Judy and Bill Leung, Kaitlin and Sarah Leung — Char Siu-Style Oven Baked Ribs (Cantonese-American tradition, Culinary Group A)
**Source**: Judy and Bill Leung (with Kaitlin and Sarah), thewoksoflife.com/oven-baked-ribs/  
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 2 hr (marinating) + 10 min active · **Cook**: 1 hr · **Rest**: 5 min

**Note**: This recipe uses baby back ribs (thinner than St. Louis). The char siu method at high heat targets a caramelised lacquered exterior — a different textural goal than low-and-slow fall-off-the-bone ribs. For St. Louis ribs at the same temperature, add 30–45 minutes to cook time. The temperature target remains the same: safe and pulling at the edges but still with some chew, not fully tender.

**Ingredients**
- 3 lb (1.4 kg) baby back pork ribs (substitute St. Louis ribs with extra cook time)
- ¼ cup (50 g) granulated sugar
- 2 tsp (12 g) salt
- ½ tsp five spice powder
- ¼ tsp white pepper
- ½ tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
- 2 tsp molasses
- ⅛ tsp red food colouring (optional)
- 3 garlic cloves, finely minced

**Method**
1. Mix all ingredients except the ribs in a large bowl to make the marinade. Reserve approximately ⅓ of the marinade for basting.
2. Rub ribs with the remaining marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
3. Preheat oven to **400 °F / 204 °C**. Place ribs on a rack over a baking sheet. Pour 1 cup (240 ml) water into the pan beneath the rack.
4. **Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of the rack lengthwise along the widest bone, with the tip in the thickest continuous intercostal muscle section, 1–2 cm clear of the bone surface.**
5. Transfer to oven. Roast for **30 minutes**.
6. Remove ribs; baste with the reserved marinade. Return to oven; roast for a further **30 minutes**.
7. The char siu marinade sugars will caramelise and char slightly, creating the signature dark, lacquered crust.
8. **Pull at 74–82 °C (165–180 °F).** The recipe does not state an explicit internal temperature; the described doneness ("sugars in the marinade create the signature crust and perfectly, slightly charred appearance") corresponds to this range at 400 °F / 204 °C for 1 hour. The meat will be cooked through and beginning to pull at the edges but is not fall-off-the-bone tender. For St. Louis ribs, extend cook time to 1.5–2 hours to reach the same stage.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Slice and serve. Final serving temperature: **74–80 °C / 165–176 °F** (caramelised char siu glaze, five-spice perfume, Chinese BBQ flavour profile, some chew retained).

### 2. Good Food Test Kitchen — Easy Oven-Baked Pork Ribs (British tradition, Culinary Group B)
**Source**: BBC Good Food Test Kitchen, bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/easy-oven-baked-pork-ribs  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 3 hr 10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Note**: This recipe specifies baby back ribs. For St. Louis ribs, increase covered cook time to 3.5–4 hours at the same temperature. The low-and-slow method produces fall-off-the-bone tenderness at 88–93 °C internal.

**Ingredients**
- 500 g baby back pork ribs (substitute St. Louis ribs with extra cook time)
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tbsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper
- Large pinch of salt
- 3 tbsp water (added to baking tray)

*BBQ glaze (step 2)*:
- 4 tbsp ketchup
- 2 tbsp light brown soft sugar
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- Dash of hot sauce (optional)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **160 °C / 320 °F (140 °C fan / 325 °F conventional, gas 3)**. Use a small sharp knife to remove the white membrane (periosteum) from the underside of the ribs; discard.
2. Mix cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne and a big pinch of salt in a small bowl. Rub spice mix all over the ribs.
3. Place ribs on a baking tray. Add 3 tbsp water to the tray. **Cover tightly with foil.**
4. **Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end of the rack, lengthwise along the central bone, into the thickest intercostal muscle section, 1–2 cm from the bone surface.**
5. Bake at 160 °C for **3 hours** (baby back) or 3.5–4 hours (St. Louis), until meat is "falling off the bone".
6. Meanwhile, combine all glaze ingredients in a small saucepan; bring to a simmer and cook 1–2 minutes until slightly reduced.
7. Remove foil. Brush glaze generously over ribs. Return to oven, **uncovered**, for 10 minutes until sticky.
8. **Pull at 88–93 °C (190–199 °F).** The recipe's criterion "falling off the bone" corresponds to complete collagen-to-gelatin conversion in this temperature range. At 160 °C oven temperature with foil cover, the ribs reach this range after 3–3.5 hours. The probe will confirm when the interior reaches 88 °C — the meat will be visibly separating from the bones.
9. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **88–93 °C / 190–199 °F** (pull-apart tender, sweet smoky paprika-BBQ glaze, British BBQ style).
