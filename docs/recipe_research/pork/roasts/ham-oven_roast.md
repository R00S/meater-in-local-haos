# Ham × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Oven roasting is the defining cooking method for ham. Unlike almost any other cut,
ham arrives in the oven already transformed: curing (salt and/or smoke applied over
days) has tightened the proteins, expelled moisture and converted raw pork into a
preserved product with a firm, dense texture and a pronounced savoury-sweet flavour
that raw pork leg does not have. What the oven does is finish that transformation —
rendering surface fat, setting a lacquered glaze, and heating the interior to a safe,
palatable temperature.

Ham comes in two states that change how oven roasting works:

**Ready-to-serve (fully cooked/smoked) ham** — already pasteurised during processing.
The oven is only heating it through to serving temperature (49–63 °C / 120–145 °F)
and caramelising the glaze. Collagen conversion is irrelevant; the goal is a glossy,
mahogany crust over warm, sliceable meat. Temperatures range from 325 °F / 163 °C
oven heat for 10–12 minutes per pound.

**Raw/uncooked ham (gammon)** — must reach USDA minimum 63 °C / 145 °F (or 72 °C /
162 °F in Scandinavian practice). The oven cooks a full bone-in leg that may weigh
3–6 kg, which requires 60–70 minutes per kilogram at 160–175 °C / 320–347 °F. The
characteristic method in Northern European traditions is to cover the ham tightly in
foil for the first stage (retaining moisture) then uncover and glaze at higher heat
for the final 15–45 minutes.

**Probe placement** — insert the probe from the thicker cut end, aiming to the
geometric centre of the largest lean muscle section, clear of the central bone. Ham
has multiple muscle groups separated by fat seams; avoid resting the tip in a fat
seam as it gives a lagging reading. On a bone-in leg, the shank bone runs at an angle
— probe from the opposite end to avoid the bone.

Glazing is universal across traditions: sweet (maple syrup, brown sugar, honey,
pineapple, fruit jam) with a tangy counterpoint (mustard, vinegar, citrus juice) and
warm spice (cloves, allspice, cinnamon). The glaze is applied in the final 20–45
minutes and basted repeatedly so it builds in layers.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nagi Maehashi — Maple Glazed Ham (Australian / Western tradition)
**Source**: Nagi Maehashi, recipetineats.com/easy-maple-sticky-glazed-ham/  
**Serves**: 30–40 as part of a spread · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1.5–2 hr · **Rest**: 20 min+

**Ingredients**
- 5 kg / 10 lb leg ham, bone-in, skin-on (fully cooked, smoked)
- 30 cloves (optional, for studding)
- 2 oranges, cut into quarters
- 250 ml (1 cup) water

**Glaze**
- 185 ml (¾ cup) maple syrup
- 165 g (¾ cup packed) brown sugar
- 3 tbsp Dijon mustard
- ¾ tsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp all-spice (or nutmeg)

**Method**
1. Remove ham from fridge 1 hour before cooking.
2. Preheat oven to 160 °C / 320 °F conventional (140 °C fan).
3. Whisk glaze ingredients together.
4. Remove rind: run a small knife around the bone handle, down each side, and under the rind on the cut face. Slide fingers under and peel back the rind. Leave as much fat as possible.
5. Score the fat in 2.5 cm (1-inch) diamonds across the surface, cutting about 75% through the fat. Insert a clove in each diamond intersection.
6. Place ham in a large baking dish. Squeeze orange juice over the ham, then place the orange pieces in the dish. Brush/spoon half the glaze over the ham surface. Pour water into the dish.
7. Bake 1.5–2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with remaining glaze and pan juices. Cover any sections browning too fast with small foil patches.
8. Rest at least 20 minutes before carving. Baste generously with the syrupy pan juices after removing from the oven — the glaze thickens as it cools, building a thick lacquered finish.
9. **Temperature note**: This recipe uses a fully cooked/smoked ham. No internal temperature is specified — the ham is considered done when the glaze is sticky and golden. USDA guideline for reheated cooked ham is **60 °C / 140 °F**.

### 2. John Torode — Baked Glazed Ham (British / Western tradition)
**Source**: John Torode, bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/baked-glazed-ham  
**Published**: *Good Food* magazine, November 2016  
**Serves**: 8 generously with leftovers · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 5 hr 45 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 raw smoked gammon on the bone, approximately 5–6 kg, rind left on
- 100 ml maple syrup
- 200 g apricot jam
- 300 g light brown soft sugar
- 12 whole cloves
- 50 ml water

**Method**
1. Heat oven to 180 °C / 160 °C fan / gas 4. Place ham on a wire rack or trivet set over a large roasting tin. Pour in 480 ml warm water, cover tightly with foil. Put the ham in the oven, immediately reduce heat to 160 °C / 140 °C fan / gas 3 and cook for **5 hours**.
2. Meanwhile, put the maple syrup, apricot jam, and brown sugar in a heavy-based pan with 50 ml water. Stir and bring to the boil, then remove from the heat and leave to cool.
3. Remove ham from the oven. Increase temperature to 200 °C / 180 °C fan / gas 6. Open the foil carefully (watch for steam). Remove the rind by inserting a sharp knife at the knuckle end, gently lifting it away from the fat (keep as much fat as possible).
4. Score the fat with a knife into large diamond shapes (4 cm apart), running from corner to corner then parallel. Stud each diamond corner with a clove.
5. Brush the ham with the sugar mixture, pushing the glaze into the scored fat.
6. Return the ham to the oven on the trivet over the water. Bake a further 45 minutes until deeply coloured and glazed.
7. **Pull at approximately 75 °C / 167 °F** for raw UK gammon (UK Food Standards Agency safe temperature for whole cuts of pork). Torode's visual indicator: deeply coloured, caramelised glaze set over the fat. Serve hot or cold. Keeps in the fridge up to 1 week.

### 3. Anna och Fanny Bergenström — Ugnsbakad Julskinka med Senapsgriljering (Swedish / Northern tradition)
**Source**: Anna och Fanny Bergenström, koket.se/anna-och-fanny-bergenstrom/varmratter/korv-chark/ugnsbakad-julskinka-med-senapsgriljering  
**Serves**: 8–10 · **Prep**: 10 min + 2–4 hr soaking · **Cook**: 60–70 min/kg + 15 min griljering

**Ingredients**
- 3–4 kg skinka (Swedish Christmas ham, cured/lightly salted pork leg)

**Griljering (mustard glaze)**
- 3 msk (tbsp) svensk färdiglagad senap (Swedish prepared mustard)
- 1 ägg (egg)
- 3 msk (tbsp) ströbröd (dried breadcrumbs)

**Method**
1. Soak the ham in a large container of cold water for 2–4 hours to reduce saltiness. Remove from water.
2. Insert the meat thermometer diagonally from the top of the ham. On a long, flat ham, insert from the short end. Push the tip to the exact centre of the thickest muscle, away from the bone.
3. Wrap the ham in aluminium foil. Place in a roasting tin and bake at **175 °C / 347 °F** for **60–70 minutes per kilogram**.
4. **Pull when the thermometer reads 72 °C / 162 °F.** Remove from oven immediately.
5. Leave the foil-wrapped ham to rest 15 minutes. Open, remove the thermometer. When slightly cooled, peel off the rind (svål).
6. **Griljering:** Heat oven to 225 °C / 437 °F. Whisk 1 egg with 3 tbsp Swedish mustard. Brush the mixture over the surface where the rind was. Sift 3 tbsp breadcrumbs over the top.
7. Return to the oven for about 15 minutes, watching closely — the mustard glaze browns quickly. Serve warm or cold as traditional julskinka.
8. **Pull temperature: 72 °C / 162 °F** (thermometer-confirmed). Swedish food safety standard for cured pork leg. This is above the USDA minimum of 63 °C / 145 °F for whole pork cuts.

### 4. Angela Lawrence — Jamaican Glazed Baked Ham (Jamaican / Southern tradition)
**Source**: Angela Lawrence (Cook Like a Jamaican), 196flavors.com/jamaica-glazed-baked-ham/  
**Serves**: 12 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 2 hr

**Ingredients**
- 4 lb (1.8 kg) ready-to-serve ham
- A few whole cloves

**Glaze**
- 1 cup cranberry sauce (or strawberry or raspberry jam)
- ¼ cup orange juice
- ¼ cup pineapple juice
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- ½ cup brown sugar (if using cranberry sauce instead of sweet jam)
- 1 teaspoon mustard

**Method**
1. Remove wrapping from ham. Score the fat in a diamond pattern and stud with cloves.
2. Bake in a 325 °F / 165 °C oven for 2 hours or until a meat thermometer registers **130 °F / 55 °C**.
3. In a saucepan, stir together the cranberry sauce or jam, orange juice, pineapple juice, lemon juice, brown sugar (if using), and mustard. Simmer uncovered for 5 minutes to thicken.
4. During the last 30 minutes of baking, spoon glaze over the ham every 10 minutes.
5. Serve with any remaining glaze on the side.
6. **Pull temperature: 130 °F / 55 °C** (thermometer-confirmed). This is for fully cooked, ready-to-serve ham where the goal is heating through to serving temperature, not cooking raw pork. The holiday tradition in Jamaica — introduced during British colonial rule — uses fully cooked imported ham and focuses on the sweet-fruit glaze.

### 5. Vanjo Merano — Homemade Cured Ham with Brown Sugar Honey Glaze (Filipino / Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Vanjo Merano, panlasangpinoy.com/homemade-cured-ham-with-brown-sugar-honey-glaze/  
**Serves**: 10 · **Prep**: 3 days (curing) + 15 min · **Cook**: 3 hr 10 min

**Ingredients**

*Curing brine*
- 5–6 lbs (2.3–2.7 kg) fresh ham shank end
- 2 cups coarse sea salt
- 2 cups brown sugar
- 2½ tablespoons Prague powder #1 curing salt
- 8 pieces dried bay leaves
- 2 quarts hot water
- 4 quarts cold water
- 2 cups ice cubes

*Glaze*
- 1 cup brown sugar
- ¼ cup honey
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 10 tablespoons pineapple juice

**Method**
1. **Cure:** Combine sea salt, brown sugar, Prague powder and bay leaves in a large container. Add hot water and stir to dissolve. Add cold water and ice. Submerge the fresh ham in the brine. Refrigerate for **3 days**.
2. Wash the cured ham thoroughly with running water. Discard brine. Soak in clean water for 3 hours to reduce saltiness.
3. Preheat oven to 320 °F / 160 °C.
4. Place ham on a roasting rack. Cover with aluminium foil. Roast for **2 hours**.
5. Remove foil. Increase heat to 400 °F / 204 °C. Continue roasting for **1 hour** (3 hours total roasting time).
6. **Make glaze:** Combine brown sugar, honey, Dijon mustard and pineapple juice in a saucepan. Heat and stir until well blended. Cook over low-medium heat until reduced by half.
7. Remove ham from oven. Brush Brown Sugar Honey Glaze all over. Return to oven for **10 more minutes** until browned and lacquered.
8. Let cool before carving.
9. **Temperature note**: No pull temperature is specified. A fresh ham cured with Prague powder #1 (nitrite cure) must reach at least 63 °C / 145 °F (USDA) to be safe; the 3-hour roast at 160–204 °C will exceed this. The Filipino *hamon* tradition — a legacy of Spanish colonial influence and the Noche Buena feast — uses pineapple juice in the glaze, which is characteristic of Philippine-style cured meats.

## Temperature consensus

| Tradition | Ham type | Pull temp | Oven temp |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Australian (Western) | Fully cooked, smoked | No explicit temp | 160 °C (320 °F) |
| British (Western) | Raw smoked gammon | ~75 °C / 167 °F | 160–200 °C (320–392 °F) |
| Swedish (Northern) | Cured, lightly salted | **72 °C / 162 °F** | 175 °C (347 °F) |
| Jamaican (Southern) | Fully cooked | **55 °C / 130 °F** | 165 °C (325 °F) |
| Filipino (Eastern) | Fresh, home-cured | ≥63 °C / 145 °F (implied) | 160–204 °C (320–400 °F) |

**Key insight:** Ham temperature targets split entirely by ham type, not by tradition:
- **Fully cooked/smoked ("ready-to-serve")**: heat to serving temperature only — 49–60 °C / 120–140 °F. The Jamaican recipe targets 55 °C, Immaculate Bites (US) targets 49 °C.
- **Raw/fresh or lightly cured ham that requires cooking**: must reach USDA minimum 63 °C / 145 °F. Swedish tradition targets 72 °C / 162 °F for traditional julskinka (slightly above minimum for extra safety).
- The **glaze** temperatures are consistent across all traditions: final 15–45 minutes at 200–225 °C to caramelise.

## What makes this method special

Oven roasting is the universal method for ham because ham's cured structure does not benefit from the collagen-dissolving extended cook times that braises provide — the curing has already done that work. What the oven does is:

1. **Caramelise the glaze**: the sugars in maple, brown sugar, fruit jam or honey undergo Maillard reactions and caramelisation at 180–225 °C, building a lacquered, mahogany crust that is the defining aesthetic of baked ham.
2. **Render the scored fat**: scoring the fat into a diamond pattern maximises surface area for glaze penetration and allows fat to render and pool in the score lines, basting the surface from within.
3. **Control moisture loss**: fully cooked ham is essentially a cooked protein that dries out rapidly if over-cooked; the combination of foil (first stage) and low oven temperature (160–175 °C) keeps the interior juicy while the exposed surface glazes.

The MEATER probe is particularly useful here because the difference between a good result (55–72 °C internal) and a dried-out result (85 °C+) is only 10–15 minutes in a hot oven.

## Summary

Ham is a cured pork leg that arrives in the oven either fully cooked (needing only to be heated through and glazed) or raw/fresh (needing to reach 63–72 °C). The glaze — always sweet (maple, sugar, fruit) with a tangy-spiced counterpoint (mustard, citrus, cloves) — is applied in the last 15–45 minutes at high heat and basted repeatedly to build a lacquered finish. Pull temperature depends entirely on ham type: 55–60 °C for fully cooked, 63–72 °C for raw. MEATER probe placement: from the cut end, to the geometric centre of the largest lean muscle, clear of the bone.
