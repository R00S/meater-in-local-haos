# Pork Belly × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork belly (svinekam / Schweinebauch / pancetta fresca) is one of the richest cuts for low-and-slow cooking. The thick fat cap renders over long cooking times, making the meat exceptionally moist and tender. "Slow cook" here means any low-and-slow method: a crockpot/slow cooker appliance, a Dutch oven in a low oven (160–180 °C), or an overnight oven braise. All three achieve the same result — collagen melts, fat renders, the meat becomes spoonable-tender.

Target internal temperatures (slow cook method):
- **Safe minimum**: **65 °C (149 °F)** — pasteurised, still quite firm
- **Tender slicing**: **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** — preferred for serving in slices or wedges
- **Pull-tender**: **85–90 °C (185–194 °F)** — for shredding/pulled styles
- Most recipes target 75–80 °C when slicing; crockpot recipes rarely probe but 4 h HIGH or 6–8 h LOW reliably reaches 80+ °C

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Slow Cooker Pork Belly (UK, crockpot method)
**Source**: BBC Good Food, bbcgoodfood.com; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-cooker-pork-belly (fetched 2026-04-24). *Garlic-ginger-soy slow cooker pork belly. Simple 2-step recipe, HIGH 4 hours, finish in a frying pan for colour.*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 4 hr 20 min · **Total**: ~4.5 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Cook on **HIGH for 4 hours**. No explicit probe temperature stated; HIGH crockpot setting (~149 °C ambient) over 4 hours ensures the 1–1.2 kg strips reach at least **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** internally. Finish in a frying pan on high heat until browned.

**Ingredients**
- 3 garlic cloves, crushed
- Thumb-sized piece root ginger, finely grated
- 3 tsp flaky sea salt
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1–1.2 kg pork belly, cut into 3 long strips, rind trimmed but fat left on
- 2 onions, sliced

**Method**
1. Mix together the garlic, ginger, salt and soy sauce. Rub all over the pork belly strips.
2. Put sliced onion into the bottom of the slow cooker. Place pork belly pieces on top.
3. Cook on **HIGH for 4 hours**.
4. Remove pork carefully. Transfer to a non-stick frying pan with a spoonful of cooking juices. Sizzle on high heat, turning occasionally, until all sides are browned and liquid has evaporated.
5. Slice or cut into wedges. Serve with mashed potato, steamed green vegetables and a gravy made from the cooking juices.
6. **MEATER target**: Insert probe before starting. Pull when internal = **75–80 °C (167–176 °F)** for tender slices. Pull at **85 °C (185 °F)** if you want a more pull-apart texture.

**Gravy tip**: Pour cooking juices into a jug, let fat rise (2–3 min), skim fat. Caramelise the cooked onions in a pan, add 1 tsp flour, then the reserved juices. Cook until glossy.

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Bayerischer Krauttopf mit Wammerl (German/Bavarian CrockPot)
**Source**: downhillcat, Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2535721397215723/Downhillcats-bayerischer-Krauttopf-mit-Wammerl.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Bavarian Krauttopf (sauerkraut stew) with Wammerl (pork belly) — a recipe for the CrockPot / Slow Cooker." 4.4/5, multiple ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 35 min · **Cook**: 6–7 hr (LOW) · **Total**: ~6.5–7.5 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Cook on **lowest setting (low) for 6–7 hours**. No probe temperature stated; LOW crockpot over 6–7 hours ensures pork belly slices (600 g, ~4–5 slices) reach well above 75 °C — likely **80–85 °C (176–185 °F)**.

**Ingredients**
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 cloves
- 2 juniper berries
- 1 onion, roughly chopped
- 4 medium potatoes, diced
- 1 medium apple, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1 pack mild sauerkraut (with juice)
- 1 tsp caraway seeds (whole)
- 600 g pork belly, in thick slices (~4–5 slices)
- 150 g Spanish paprika salami, in thick slices
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 250 ml seasoned meat stock (Fleischbrühe)
- Salt and black pepper

**Method** *(for a 3.5 L CrockPot)*
1. Place bay leaf, cloves and juniper berries in the ceramic pot.
2. Add onion, potato and apple pieces. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Cover with sauerkraut (including juice). Scatter caraway on top of the sauerkraut.
3. Lay pork belly slices offset on top. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
4. Distribute the salami slices over the belly. Sprinkle everything with the brown sugar.
5. Pour over the stock — liquid should reach almost up to the meat slices; add extra if needed. Put lid on.
6. Cook on **LOW for 6–7 hours**.
7. Serve directly from the pot, or slice belly smaller and mix everything together.
8. **MEATER target**: Insert probe into the thickest belly slice. Pull when internal = **80–85 °C (176–185 °F)** — fully tender, melting into the kraut.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Slow-Cooked Pork Belly (UK, low-oven method)
**Source**: Jamie Oliver, jamieoliver.com; https://www.jamieoliver.com/recipes/pork-recipes/slow-cooked-pork-belly/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Together" cookbook recipe. Get-ahead low-oven slow cook (180 °C × 2 hours), refrigerate overnight, crisp-up next day. Serves 12.*
**Serves**: 12 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ overnight rest) · **Cook**: 2 hr + 30 min crisp · **Total**: 2 days (mostly passive)

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Low oven **180 °C / 350 °F / gas 4**, covered tightly in foil, **2 hours** — the long covered roast is equivalent to a slow-cook braise, keeping the belly moist. After overnight rest and slicing, a second oven blast at **200 °C × 30 min** crisps the exterior. No explicit probe temperature; 180 °C × 2 hours on a 1.5 kg piece ensures internal of **80–85 °C (176–185 °F)**.

**Ingredients**
- 4 fresh bay leaves
- 1 heaped tsp smoked paprika
- 1 heaped tsp ground coriander
- 1 heaped tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 heaped tsp ground allspice
- 1.5 kg boneless pork belly, skin off and reserved separately
- Olive oil
- Sea salt and black pepper

**Method**
**GET AHEAD (Day 1)**
1. Preheat oven to **180 °C / 350 °F / gas 4**. Pound bay leaves in a mortar, add spices and 1 tsp each salt and pepper. Loosen with olive oil. Rub all over pork belly (not the reserved skin).
2. Sit belly in a snug-fitting roasting tray. Cover **tightly** with tin foil. Roast for **2 hours**.
3. Remove, cool, refrigerate **overnight** (cold makes slicing much easier; flavour deepens).

**ON THE DAY (Day 2)**
4. Preheat oven to **200 °C / 400 °F / gas 6**.
5. Season pork skin with salt and pepper. Lay on a baking tray. Roast at top of oven **1 hour** until golden and crunchy crackling.
6. Transfer the cold belly to a board. Slice **1 cm thick**. Lay across a large baking tray (reserve roasting juices). Roast **30 min** until crisp and golden.
7. Drain fat from crackling tray, snap up crackling. Serve for taco building or Sunday roast.
8. **MEATER target (Day 1)**: Insert probe before placing in oven. Pull when internal = **80–85 °C (176–185 °F)**. The belly then rests overnight and is ready for Day 2 finishing.

## Temperature summary

| Source | Country | Method | Setting | Time | Pull temp |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| BBC Good Food | UK | Crockpot HIGH | 149 °C equiv. | 4 hr | ~75–80 °C (167–176 °F) |
| Chefkoch.de (downhillcat) | DE | Crockpot LOW | ~93 °C equiv. | 6–7 hr | ~80–85 °C (176–185 °F) |
| Jamie Oliver | UK | Oven (foil-covered slow roast) | 180 °C | 2 hr | ~80–85 °C (176–185 °F) |

**Recommended MEATER setting**: Pull at **80 °C (176 °F)**. This ensures tender, sliceable belly across all methods. For pull-apart texture go to 88 °C (190 °F).
