# Pork Shank × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The pork shank (pork hock, *fläsklägg*) is the lower portion of the pig's leg — bone-in, skin-on or skinned, dense with collagen and surrounded by a thick rind. It is unsuited to fast cooking: success depends on long, slow, moist heat that hydrolyses connective tissue into gelatin while the meat passes well beyond the medium-doneness window into a "pulled" state. Internal temperatures of 90–96 °C (194–205 °F) are normal targets; below 88 °C the shank still resists the fork, above 96 °C it begins to dry. Probe placement: deepest muscle adjacent to (but not touching) the femur. Carryover after a 2.5–3 hr braise is small (2–3 °C) since the cut is moist; rest 15–20 minutes covered before serving.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Stinco di Maiale Brasato al Vino Bianco (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Knopf, 1992), pp. 444–445  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 2.5–3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork shanks (hind), bone-in, ~1 kg each, rind scored
- 60 ml extra virgin olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
- 2 carrots, finely chopped
- 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 500 ml dry white wine (Trebbiano or Pinot Grigio)
- 400 ml whole-peeled tomatoes (½ tin), crushed by hand
- 2 bay leaves, 4 sprigs fresh sage
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Pat shanks dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
2. In a heavy lidded pot just large enough to hold the shanks, heat olive oil over medium-high. Brown shanks on all sides, 12–15 min total. Remove.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion, carrot, celery, garlic; sweat 8 min until soft.
4. Return shanks. Add wine; let bubble 2 min. Add tomatoes, bay, sage, 200 ml water.
5. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest meat alongside the bone, entering from the rind side, tip in the muscle centre.
6. Cover and reduce to a bare simmer (or transfer to a 150 °C oven). Turn shanks every 45 min. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F)** when a probe slides in with no resistance, ~2.5–3 hr.
7. Rest 15 min in the covered pot off the heat. Skim fat from the sauce; reduce uncovered if loose. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (meat falling from bone, sauce silken).

### 2. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Slow-Braised Pork Knuckle with Cider & Apples (British tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *The River Cottage Meat Book* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2004), pp. 374–376  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork shanks, ~1 kg each, rind scored in 1 cm crosshatch
- 2 tbsp lard or sunflower oil
- 2 onions, sliced
- 2 leeks, white and pale green, sliced
- 4 celery sticks, sliced
- 6 garlic cloves
- 500 ml medium-dry cider
- 300 ml chicken or pork stock
- 2 bay leaves, 6 sprigs thyme
- 2 Bramley apples, peeled, cored, quartered
- 1 tbsp wholegrain mustard
- Sea salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Salt the shanks well and leave 30 min at room temperature. Heat oven to 140 °C (284 °F).
2. Heat lard in a heavy casserole; brown shanks all over, 10 min. Lift out.
3. Soften onions, leeks, celery, garlic 10 min in the same pan. Pour in cider; reduce by half. Add stock, bay, thyme.
4. Return shanks rind-up; tuck apple quarters around them. Insert MEATER into the thickest part, parallel to the bone.
5. Cover with lid; braise in oven 2.5 hr, basting twice.
6. Uncover for the final 30 min so the rind colours.
7. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Rest 20 min loosely covered.
8. Stir mustard into the strained sauce. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F** (shred-tender, sweet-savoury sauce).

### 3. Heinz Wuth — Schweinshaxe Geschmort (Bavarian tradition)
**Source**: Heinz Wuth, *Wissenschaft & Kochen* (Becker Joest Volk Verlag, 2021), Schweinshaxe chapter; consistent with Alfons Schuhbeck, *Meine bayerische Küche* (ZS Verlag, 2018), pp. 156–158  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr braise (then optional roast) · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 hind hocks (Schweinshaxe), ~1.2 kg each, rind scored
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 large onions, halved
- 1 carrot, 1 celeriac wedge, chopped
- 6 garlic cloves
- 1 tbsp caraway seeds, 1 tsp black peppercorns, 4 cloves
- 500 ml dark Bavarian beer (Dunkles)
- 500 ml dark veal or beef stock
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt

**Method**
1. Salt hocks generously, leave 1 hr.
2. Heat oven to 160 °C (320 °F). Brown hocks in oil in a roasting tin, 12 min.
3. Add vegetables, garlic, spices; deglaze with beer; add stock and bay so liquid reaches halfway up the hocks.
4. Insert MEATER probe deep into the meat next to the femur, tip away from the bone.
5. Cover with foil; braise 2 hr, turning at 60 min.
6. Remove foil; raise oven to 220 °C (430 °F); roast uncovered 25–35 min basting every 10 min until the rind crackles.
7. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Rest 15 min.
8. Strain and reduce the sauce. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (crackled rind, fork-tender meat).

### 4. Fuchsia Dunlop — Hong Shao Zhou Zi (Red-Braised Pork Knuckle, Sichuan tradition)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *The Food of Sichuan* (Bloomsbury, 2019), pp. 142–144  
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 large pork knuckle (~1.5 kg), bone-in, skin on
- 2 tbsp groundnut oil
- 4 tbsp Shaoxing wine
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp rock sugar
- 4 spring onions, white parts (knotted), 30 g ginger sliced
- 2 star anise, 1 cinnamon stick, 3 dried red chillies
- 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns
- ~1 L water

**Method**
1. Blanch the knuckle in boiling water 5 min; drain, rinse, pat dry.
2. Heat oil in a heavy clay pot or Dutch oven. Add rock sugar; melt to amber caramel.
3. Add knuckle; brown all sides in the caramel, 5 min. Add wine, both soys, aromatics, water just to cover.
4. Insert the MEATER through the rind into the thickest meat, parallel with bone.
5. Bring to a boil; reduce to bare simmer; cover. Cook 2 hr, turning every 30 min.
6. Uncover; raise heat to reduce sauce to a glossy glaze, 20 min, basting the knuckle constantly.
7. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Rest 10 min.
8. Lift to a platter; spoon glaze over. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (chopstick-tender, lacquered).
