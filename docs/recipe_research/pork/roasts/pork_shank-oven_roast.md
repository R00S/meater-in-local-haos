# Pork Shank × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The pork shank (*fläsklägg*, German *Schweinshaxe*) roasted in the oven targets dual outcomes: pulled-tender meat (internal 92–96 °C / 198–205 °F) and crackled rind. The classic technique is a long moderate-heat braise (140–160 °C, covered) followed by a hot finish (220–240 °C, uncovered) to puff the rind. Probe placement: deepest muscle alongside the femur, tip not on the bone. Carryover after a long roast: 3–4 °C. Rest 15–20 min loosely tented before carving — but do NOT cover the rind, or it softens.

## Source recipes

### 1. Alfons Schuhbeck — Schweinshaxe aus dem Ofen (Bavarian tradition)
**Source**: Alfons Schuhbeck, *Meine bayerische Küche* (ZS Verlag, 2018), pp. 156–158  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 hind hocks (~1.2 kg each), rind scored 1 cm crosshatch
- 2 tbsp coarse salt
- 1 tbsp caraway seeds
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 onions quartered, 1 carrot, 1 leek, 4 garlic cloves
- 500 ml dark beer (Dunkles), 500 ml beef stock
- 2 bay leaves

**Method**
1. Rub hocks with salt, caraway, pepper. Leave 1 hr at room temp.
2. Heat oven to 160 °C (320 °F).
3. Place vegetables in a roasting tin; sit hocks on top. Pour beer and stock around (not over rind).
4. Insert the MEATER probe deep into the meat next to the femur, entering from the rind side.
5. Cover loosely with foil. Roast 2 hr.
6. Remove foil. Raise oven to 230 °C (446 °F). Roast uncovered 30–45 min, basting the rind every 10 min with the pan juices to crackle it.
7. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** The rind should be glassy and crisp.
8. Rest 15 min uncovered. Strain pan juices. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F** (crackling rind, fork-pull meat).

### 2. Heston Blumenthal — Slow-Roast Pork Shank (modern British technique)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *Heston Blumenthal at Home* (Bloomsbury, 2011), pp. 200–204 (slow-roast pork method, scaled to shank)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 24 hr salt + 20 min · **Cook**: 7 hr · **Rest**: 20 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork shanks (1 kg each), rind scored
- 30 g salt for dry brine
- 2 tbsp rapeseed oil
- 4 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves, 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 1 onion, 1 carrot, 1 celery stick (chopped)

**Method**
1. Rub shanks with salt; refrigerate uncovered on a rack 24 hr.
2. Pat dry. Heat oven to 100 °C (212 °F). Place shanks on a rack over a tray with vegetables, herbs, peppercorns and 200 ml water beneath.
3. Insert MEATER into deepest meat parallel to bone.
4. Roast 6 hr at 100 °C, until **internal reaches 88 °C** (190 °F).
5. Remove from oven; raise heat to 240 °C (464 °F). Brush rind lightly with oil. Return shanks; roast 30–40 min, watching closely, until rind has fully crackled.
6. **Pull at 93–95 °C (199–203 °F).**
7. Rest 20 min, rind exposed. Final serving temperature: **80–85 °C / 176–185 °F**.

### 3. Magnus Nilsson — Fläsklägg i Ugn (Nordic/Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 287–289  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hr cure + 15 min · **Cook**: 4 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 fresh pork shanks (~1 kg each), rind scored
- For dry cure: 30 g salt, 10 g brown sugar, 2 tsp crushed juniper, 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 onion quartered, 1 carrot, 4 allspice berries, 2 bay leaves
- 250 ml dark Swedish beer
- 200 ml veal or chicken stock

**Method**
1. Rub cure into shanks; refrigerate 12 hr.
2. Rinse, pat dry. Heat oven to 150 °C (300 °F).
3. Place vegetables and aromatics in a tin; place shanks on top. Pour beer and stock around.
4. Insert MEATER into deepest muscle.
5. Cover with foil; roast 3 hr.
6. Uncover; raise to 220 °C (430 °F); roast 30–45 min until rind crackles.
7. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Rest 15 min uncovered.
8. Serve with brown sauce and pickled vegetables. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F**.

### 4. J. Kenji López-Alt — Crispy Roast Pork Hock (modernist American technique)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Food Lab* (W.W. Norton, 2015), pork shoulder/hock crackling section, pp. 489–493 (technique adapted to hock)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 24 hr air-dry + 15 min · **Cook**: 4 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork hocks (~1 kg each), rind scored deeply
- 30 g kosher salt for dry brine
- 1 tsp baking powder (for rind)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 onion sliced, 4 garlic cloves, 4 thyme sprigs
- 250 ml chicken stock

**Method**
1. Mix salt with baking powder; rub into rind only. Salt the meat normally. Refrigerate uncovered 24 hr to dry the rind.
2. Heat oven to 135 °C (275 °F). Place onion, garlic, thyme in a tin; sit hocks on top; pour stock around (not on rind).
3. Insert MEATER into deepest muscle.
4. Roast 3 hr until **internal 88 °C / 190 °F**.
5. Raise oven to 245 °C (475 °F). Brush rind with oil. Return; roast 30–45 min until rind has fully puffed and is amber. Rotate tray as needed.
6. **Pull at 93–95 °C (199–203 °F).**
7. Rest 15 min uncovered. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (puffed crackling, juicy meat).
