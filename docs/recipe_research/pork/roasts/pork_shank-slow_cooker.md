# Pork Shank × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The pork shank (*fläsklägg*) is a heavy-collagen, bone-in cut from the lower leg. In a slow cooker the moist 90–100 °C ambient drives slow gelatin formation over 7–9 hours. Target internal 92–96 °C (198–205 °F) — the same "pulled" endpoint as a stove-top braise but without surface evaporation, so the meat finishes wetter and the rind stays soft (typical for slow-cooker preparations; for crackling, finish under a grill). Probe placement: deepest muscle parallel to the femur. Carryover small (1–2 °C). Rest 10–15 min in the cooker on warm before serving.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Pork Shanks with Beans (American tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017), pp. 142–144  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork shanks, bone-in, ~1 kg each
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 2 carrots, diced
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 250 ml dry white wine
- 500 ml chicken stock
- 250 g dried cannellini beans, soaked overnight, drained
- 4 sprigs rosemary, 2 bay leaves
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Salt shanks; brown in oil over high in a skillet, ~10 min total. Transfer to slow cooker.
2. In same skillet, sweat onion, garlic, carrot 6 min. Add tomato paste; cook 2 min. Deglaze with wine. Pour into cooker.
3. Add stock, beans, herbs.
4. Insert MEATER through the rind into the thickest muscle alongside the bone, ensuring tip is well submerged.
5. Cover; cook on LOW 8 hr, turning shanks once at 4 hr.
6. **Pull at 93–95 °C (199–203 °F).** Beans should be creamy.
7. Rest 10 min; lift shanks; reduce cooking liquid in a saucepan if loose. Final serving temperature: **80–84 °C / 176–183 °F** (fall-off-the-bone with bean ragù).

### 2. Stephanie O'Dea — Slow-Cooked Pork Hocks with Sauerkraut (German-American tradition)
**Source**: Stephanie O'Dea, *Make It Fast, Cook It Slow* (Hyperion, 2009), pp. 178–180; ayearofslowcooking.com archive  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork hocks, ~900 g each
- 750 g jarred sauerkraut, drained, lightly rinsed
- 1 tart apple, peeled and grated
- 1 medium onion, sliced
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp caraway seeds
- 250 ml dry hard cider
- 1 tbsp grainy mustard
- Salt, black pepper

**Method**
1. Layer onion in cooker; pile sauerkraut, apple, sugar, caraway over.
2. Nestle hocks into kraut; pour cider around.
3. Insert MEATER deep into the meaty side of the hock, away from bone.
4. Cover; cook LOW 8 hr.
5. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).**
6. Lift hocks; rest 10 min. Stir mustard into kraut. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (tender meat, sweet-sour kraut).

### 3. Magnus Nilsson — Lågtemperatur Skinkstek på Lägg (Nordic slow-cook tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 287–289 (low-temperature whole-leg method, scaled to shank)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hr salt cure + 15 min · **Cook**: 9 hr LOW · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 pork shanks, ~1 kg each
- For dry cure: 30 g salt, 10 g sugar, 1 tsp white pepper, 1 tsp juniper berries crushed
- 1 onion quartered, 1 carrot, 4 allspice berries, 2 bay leaves
- 250 ml light beer or water
- 200 ml chicken stock

**Method**
1. Rub cure all over shanks; refrigerate 12 hr in a covered dish.
2. Rinse and pat dry. Place in slow cooker with onion, carrot, spices, beer and stock.
3. Insert MEATER into thickest meat next to bone.
4. Cover; cook LOW 9 hr.
5. **Pull at 90–93 °C (194–199 °F).** Optional: transfer to 220 °C oven 15 min to colour the rind.
6. Rest 15 min. Final serving temperature: **76–80 °C / 169–176 °F** (mildly cured, juicy).

### 4. Andrea Nguyen — Vietnamese Slow-Cooked Caramelised Pork Hock (Thịt Heo Kho) (Vietnamese tradition)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (Ten Speed Press, 2019), pp. 184–186 (kho method)  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 7 hr LOW · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 large pork hock (~1.5 kg), cut by butcher into 3 cm rounds
- 4 tbsp palm sugar
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 100 ml fresh young coconut water
- 4 garlic cloves crushed, 30 g ginger sliced
- 2 chillies split, 4 spring onions
- ½ tsp ground white pepper

**Method**
1. In a small saucepan, melt palm sugar over medium heat to dark caramel; carefully add 3 tbsp water. Pour into slow cooker.
2. Blanch pork rounds 3 min in boiling water; drain. Add to cooker.
3. Add fish sauce, soy, coconut water, aromatics, pepper. Top up with water just to cover.
4. Insert MEATER into the meatiest round, deep into the muscle alongside the bone.
5. Cover; cook LOW 7 hr.
6. **Pull at 92–95 °C (198–203 °F).** Skim fat.
7. Rest 10 min in cooker. Final serving temperature: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (caramel-glossy, tender).
