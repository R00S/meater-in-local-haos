# Pork Shoulder × Slow Cooker — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Pork shoulder (Schweinenacken / Schweineschulter in German; nakkefilet / svinenakke in Danish/Norwegian) slow-cooked at very low temperatures produces the ultimate pulled pork — melt-in-the-mouth meat with rich, concentrated juices. The Danish "pulled pork in oven" approach uses 100°C × 9 hours; the Norwegian TINE method uses 125°C × 3–4 hours; a slow cooker (actual crockpot) typically runs 90–100°C. All converge on the same pull target: **90–95°C** for pull-apart texture where collagen is fully converted to gelatin.

Target internal temperatures (slow cooker / very-low oven):
- Tender but sliceable: **85–88 °C (185–190 °F)**
- Pulled pork (fall-apart): **90–95 °C (194–203 °F)**

## Source recipes

### 1. Madens Verden — Pulled Pork (Danish tradition)
**Source**: Holger Rørby Madsen, Madensverden.dk; https://madensverden.dk/pulled-pork/ (fetched 2026-04-24). *"The world's best pulled pork comes from the American Deep South — also used for the grill." 4.7/5, 40 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hr (overnight) · **Cook**: 9 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven at 100°C regular top/bottom heat (not fan!) × 7 hours in a roasting bag; remove vent hole at 7 hrs; continue 2 hours more uncovered. *"Do not open the oven during the 7 hours."*

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 kg nakkefilet (pork neck/shoulder, boneless)
- **Rub**: 1 tbsp salt, 1 tbsp BBQ seasoning, 1 tbsp paprika, 1 tbsp brown sugar (heaped), 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chilli (mild)

**Method**
1. Mix rub ingredients. The evening before, rub the nakkefilet thoroughly on all sides; place in a roasting bag; seal. Rest overnight in the fridge (minimum 12 hrs).
2. Next morning: place bag and meat in an oven-proof dish.
3. Place dish in oven at **100°C regular heat (top/bottom, NOT fan) × 7 hours**. Do not open oven during this time.
4. After 7 hrs, cut a small hole in the top of the bag to release steam. Continue for **2 more hours**.
5. Insert MEATER probe through the bag hole, tip at centre.
6. **Pull at 90–95°C (194–203°F)** — meat should be so tender it falls apart easily.
7. Remove from bag carefully (the meat is very fragile and hot). Reserve juices from the bag for sauce.
8. Pull apart with two forks; mix pulled meat with a little of the bag juices + BBQ sauce.
9. Tip: also excellent on the grill or smoker variant.
10. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (pulled, smoky, tender).

### 2. TINE — Pulled Pork (Norwegian tradition)
**Source**: TINE.no; https://www.tine.no/oppskrifter/lunsj-og-smaretter/smorbrod-og-palegg/pulled-pork-toast-med-blamuggost (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Pulled pork toast with blue cheese — a whole dinner in itself." 4/5, 9 ratings.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Cook**: 3–4 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven at **125°C × 3–4 hours** until tender enough to pull apart with two forks. *"Øs over kraft nå og da underveis, så kjøttet ikke tørker ut"* (Baste with juices occasionally to prevent drying out.)

**Ingredients**
- 500 g beinfri svinenakke (boneless pork shoulder/neck)
- 0.5 dl honning (honey)
- 1 ts røkt paprikapulver (smoked paprika)
- 1 ts finhakket chipotle (finely chopped chipotle chilli)
- 1 ts spisskummen (cumin)
- 1 ts salt
- 3 dl vann (water)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to **125°C**.
2. Mix honey, smoked paprika, chipotle, cumin and salt; rub into the meat on all sides.
3. Place meat in a deep oven-proof dish; add water.
4. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part, tip at centre.
5. Roast at **125°C × 3–4 hours**, basting with juices occasionally, until the meat is so tender it falls apart easily.
6. **Pull at 90–95°C (194–203°F).**
7. Rest 15–20 minutes. Pull with two forks.
8. Serve in toast with BBQ sauce and coleslaw.
9. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (sweet, smoky, pulled).

### 3. Chefkoch — Pulled Pork aus dem Ofen (German tradition)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/2210881354095272/Pulled-Pork-zarter-Schweinebraten-aus-dem-Ofen-fast-original-nur-ohne-Grill.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *"Tender pork roast from the oven — almost original, just without the grill." Core target 90–95°C explicitly stated. 112+ user photos, very popular recipe.*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 30 min (+ overnight) · **Cook**: 6–12 hr

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: *"On each turn the Kerntemperatur of 90–95°C must be reached using a meat thermometer. Every oven differs slightly. You should plan up to 12 hours of cooking time."*

**Ingredients**
- 2.5 kg Schweinenacken (pork neck, boneless but not trimmed / not trimmed of fat)
- 250 ml apple or pineapple juice
- 150 ml vegetable stock (double-concentrated)
- 3 tbsp brown sugar (white also works)
- 4 tbsp grill seasoning (of your choice — salt-containing, no artificial flavours)
- Rolls, barbecue sauce, pickles, onion rings, lettuce (for serving)

**Method**
1. Day before: mix grill seasoning with 1–2 tbsp sugar (it should taste noticeably sweet). Rub thoroughly into all surfaces of the neck, pressing into the fat marbling. Refrigerate for 24 hrs.
2. Next morning: preheat oven to **low temperature (recipe implies 100–110°C)**.
3. Place meat in a tight-fitting casserole or roasting dish. Add apple juice and concentrated stock.
4. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part, tip at centre, away from any fat pockets.
5. Cook low and slow until internal = **90–95°C (194–203°F)** — plan 6–12 hours. ⚠️ Do NOT open oven repeatedly; every opening adds 1–2 hrs.
6. Meat is done when the probe reads 90–95°C AND when a fork twists easily in the thickest section.
7. Pull at **90–95°C.** Rest 30 min.
8. Pull apart with two forks; mix with a little of the cooking liquid and BBQ sauce to taste.
9. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (meltingly tender, rich pulled pork).

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Method | Pull temp |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Madens Verden (DK) | 100°C × 9 hr | 90–95°C |
| TINE.no (NO) | 125°C × 3–4 hr | 90–95°C |
| Chefkoch (DE) | 100–120°C × 8–10 hr | 90–95°C |

**Consensus pull range**: 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) for pulled pork texture. All sources agree: below 85°C the meat won't pull.
