# Chicken Breast × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying chicken breast at 375-400°F / 190-200°C for 18-20 minutes produces a
lightly golden, slightly crisped exterior with the moist interior of a properly
cooked breast. The circulating air is significantly faster than a standard oven and
requires no preheating. Pounding the breast to even 2-3 cm / 0.75-1 inch thickness
before air frying ensures even cooking throughout. Target: 165°F / 74°C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Chicken Breast
- **Source**: seriouseats.com
- **Method**: Pound to even thickness, season, light oil spray, 380°F / 193°C
  18-20 min flipping at 10 min.
- **Target**: **165°F / 74°C** at thickest point

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Chicken Breast
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com
- **Method**: Brine 30 min, 400°F 15-18 min.
- **Target**: **160°F / 71°C** pull, rest to 165°F

### 3. Ninja Kitchen — Air Fryer Chicken Guide
- **Source**: ninjakitchen.com
- **Method**: 375°F 18-22 min.
- **Target**: **165°F / 74°C** (USDA)

### 4. ICA/Coop Sweden — Kycklingbröst i luftfritös (ica.se)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept
- **Method**: 200°C air fryer 18-20 min.
- **Target**: **74°C** at thickest (Swedish standard)

### 5. Gordon Ramsay (adapted) — Air Fryer Chicken
- **Source**: gordonramsay.com technique
- **Method**: Season well, 200°C preheated, 18-20 min.
- **Target**: **165°F / 74°C** — "juices run clear"

**Universal consensus: 165°F / 74°C.** Maps to `well_done`.

## What makes this method special
Air frying produces a crisped chicken breast with zero added fat in 20 minutes
from cold — faster and crispier than a conventional oven, and healthier than pan
frying. The most practical method for meal-prep cooking of multiple chicken breasts
simultaneously.
