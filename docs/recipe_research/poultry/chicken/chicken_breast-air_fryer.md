## Cut profile

Chicken breast is a lean, boneless cut that dries out quickly when overcooked. Pounding to even thickness and brining are the two most effective techniques for retaining moisture. Because there is minimal fat to buffer heat, precise temperature monitoring is critical.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — USDA minimum for poultry

## Source recipes

### 1. Skinnytaste — Air Fryer Chicken Breast
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/air-fryer-chicken-breast/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min + 1–2 hr brine · **Cook**: ~10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 380 °F (193 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C)
- Time: Small 5–6 oz → 8–10 min; Medium 7–8 oz → 10–12 min; Large 9+ oz → 12–14 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts (5–9 oz each)
- 4 tbsp kosher salt (for brine)
- 6 cups water (for brine)
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp dried parsley
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Method**
1. Dissolve 4 tbsp kosher salt in 6 cups cold water in a large bowl to make brine.
2. Submerge chicken breasts, cover, refrigerate 1–2 hours.
3. Remove chicken from brine, rinse under cold water, pat thoroughly dry with paper towels.
4. If breasts are uneven thickness, place between plastic wrap and pound to even thickness with a meat mallet.
5. In a small bowl, combine smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, parsley, salt, and pepper.
6. Brush chicken with olive oil, then rub seasoning mixture evenly over both sides.
7. Preheat air fryer to 380 °F (193 °C) for 3 minutes.
8. Place chicken in a single layer in the basket (do not overlap).
9. Cook according to weight — small: 8–10 min, medium: 10–12 min, large: 12–14 min — flipping halfway through.
10. Check internal temperature in the thickest part; pull at 165 °F (74 °C).
11. Rest on a cutting board 5 minutes before slicing.

### 2. Natasha's Kitchen — Air Fryer Chicken Thighs (temperature reference for dark-meat context)
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/air-fryer-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 18–22 min (bone-in) · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 375 °F (191 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) minimum; 175 °F (79 °C) preferred for dark meat
- Time: Bone-in skin-on thighs → 18–22 min total; Boneless thighs → 12–15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4–6 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (or boneless)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 3/4 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Fresh parsley for garnish

**Method**
1. Pat chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels — this is critical for crispy skin.
2. Combine olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
3. Rub mixture evenly over both sides of the thighs, including under the skin if possible.
4. Preheat air fryer to 375 °F (191 °C).
5. Arrange thighs skin-side down in the basket without overlapping.
6. Cook 9–11 minutes for bone-in (6–7 min for boneless), then flip.
7. Continue cooking until internal temp reaches 165–175 °F (74–79 °C).
8. For toaster-oven-style air fryers allow 18–20 min total; basket-style allow 20–22 min total.
9. Rest 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Chicken Breasts
**Source**: Samuel Goldsmith, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-chicken-breasts (archived: https://web.archive.org/web/20241007181927/https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-chicken-breasts, fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 18–20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 180 °C (356 °F)
- Internal target: 70 °C (158 °F) — pierce thickest part to check juices run clear
- Time: 18–20 min total, turning after 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 chicken breasts
- ½ tbsp rapeseed oil
- 1 tsp salt
- 1½ tsp garlic granules
- 1 tsp smoked sweet paprika
- 2 tsp mixed herbs
- ½ tsp pepper
- Steamed rice and greens or salad, to serve (optional)

**Method**
1. Coat the chicken in the oil and set aside. In a bowl, combine the salt, garlic, paprika and mixed herbs with a good grinding of black pepper, then scatter onto a plate.
2. Roll each oiled chicken breast in the seasoning and put in your air fryer basket. Cook at 180 °C for 18–20 mins, turning after 10 mins. To check it is cooked, pierce the chicken with a knife at the thickest part to see if the juices run clear. Or insert a temperature thermometer and it should read 70 °C. Serve with rice and greens or a salad.

## Practical tips

### Brining guidance
- **Why brine**: Lean breast loses ~30% of its weight in water during cooking without brining; a simple salt brine reduces that loss significantly.
- **Timing**: 1 hour is good; 2 hours is ideal. Beyond 4 hours the texture can become spongy.
- **Dry brine alternative**: Pat dry, salt generously, refrigerate uncovered on a rack 1–8 hours. Produces drier surface for better browning without the mess of wet brining.

### Air fryer model differences
- Basket-style air fryers run hotter than toaster-oven-style. If your air fryer runs hot, drop to 360 °F and add 1–2 minutes.
- Always preheat for consistent results unless the recipe specifically says not to.
- Avoid stacking chicken — if pieces overlap, the bottom one steams rather than crisps.

### Carry-over cooking
- Breast pulled at 160 °F (71 °C) will rise to 165 °F (74 °C) during a 5-minute rest.
- Slicing immediately destroys this effect — the juices run out and the meat appears dry.
- Tent loosely with foil during rest; do not seal tightly or the skin will steam and lose crispiness.

### Scaling
- For batches larger than 4 breasts, cook in two separate batches rather than stacking.
- Allow the air fryer to re-preheat 2 minutes between batches.

### Seasoning variations
| Variation | Swap the rub for... |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Lemon herb | Lemon zest, oregano, garlic powder, olive oil |
| Cajun | Cayenne, smoked paprika, thyme, garlic, onion powder |
| Italian | Basil, oregano, garlic powder, sun-dried tomato powder |
| BBQ glazed | Dry rub first, brush BBQ sauce last 2 min of cook |

### Storage and reheating
- Refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours; keep up to 4 days in an airtight container.
- Reheat in air fryer at 350 °F for 4–5 minutes — this restores some moisture without over-drying.
- Slice cold leftovers thinly for sandwiches; the cold breast is easier to slice thin.

### MEATER probe placement
- Insert probe into the thickest part of the breast, parallel to the long axis.
- Avoid the centre hollow (if any) and any bones from the sternum strip.
- Target alert: **160 °F (71 °C)** — pull, rest 5 min, carryover to 165 °F.
- For MEATER+: ambient probe monitors air temp; ensure it is not touching the basket wall.

> **Summary**: Air fry at 380 °F; brine first; pull at 160 °F; rest 5 min → 165 °F safe.
> Small 5–6 oz breast: 8–10 min · Medium 7–8 oz: 10–12 min · Large 9+ oz: 12–14 min.
