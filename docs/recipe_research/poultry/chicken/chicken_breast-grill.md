## Cut profile

Boneless skinless chicken breast is one of the most popular grilling cuts. Because it is very lean, it benefits enormously from a marinade that adds moisture and flavour before the high, dry heat of the grill. Slicing into thin cutlets dramatically reduces cook time and edge-to-edge doneness problems.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — USDA minimum for poultry

## Source recipes

### 1. Skinnytaste — Grilled Chicken Breast
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/grilled-chicken-breast/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min + 20–30 min marinate · **Cook**: 6 min · **Rest**: 3–5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill temp: medium-high heat
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C)
- Time: Thin cutlets (sliced horizontally) → 2–3 min per side; Thick whole breasts → 5–7 min per side

**Ingredients**
- 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken breast cutlets (8 thin pieces)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3/4 tsp dried Italian herbs (basil, oregano, thyme blend)
- 2.5 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Slice thick chicken breasts horizontally to create thin cutlets of even thickness.
2. Whisk together olive oil, minced garlic, Italian herbs, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
3. Add chicken cutlets to marinade, toss to coat, cover, and refrigerate 20 minutes to 2 hours.
4. Preheat gas or charcoal grill to medium-high heat. Clean and oil grates.
5. Remove chicken from marinade, letting excess drip off.
6. Place cutlets on grill in a single layer. Do not crowd.
7. Grill thin cutlets 2–3 minutes per side until grill marks form and internal temp reads 165 °F (74 °C).
8. For whole unsplit breasts, grill 5–7 minutes per side.
9. Transfer to a plate, tent loosely with foil, rest 3–5 minutes before serving.

### 2. Natasha's Kitchen — Juicy Grilled BBQ Chicken Breast
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/juicy-grilled-chicken-breast/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 8 · **Prep**: 10 min + overnight marinate · **Cook**: ~20 min · **Rest**: 15 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill preheat temp: 400 °F (204 °C), then reduce to medium-low when chicken goes on
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C)
- Time: Large whole breasts → approximately 10 min per side (rotate 90° at 5 min for crosshatch grill marks)

**Ingredients**
- 3–4 lbs whole boneless skinless chicken breasts (large)
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1/2 cup ranch dressing
- 6–8 garlic cloves, pressed
- 2 tsp Johnny's seasoning (or substitute: 1 tsp salt + 1/2 tsp paprika + 1/4 tsp pepper + 1/4 tsp garlic powder)
- Your favourite BBQ sauce, for basting
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Method**
1. Combine mayonnaise, ranch dressing, pressed garlic, and seasoning in a zip-lock bag or bowl.
2. Add chicken breasts, turn to coat thoroughly in marinade.
3. Seal and refrigerate 10–24 hours (overnight preferred for maximum juiciness).
4. Remove chicken from refrigerator 30 minutes before grilling to take the chill off.
5. Preheat grill to 400 °F (204 °C), then reduce burners to medium-low just before adding chicken.
6. Clean grates well and oil lightly.
7. Place marinated breasts on grill. Cook 5 minutes undisturbed, then rotate 90° for crosshatch marks.
8. Cook 5 more minutes, then flip. Brush generously with BBQ sauce.
9. Continue cooking 10 more minutes on second side (rotating at 5 min), basting occasionally.
10. Pull chicken when internal temp reaches 165 °F (74 °C) in the thickest part.
11. Tent loosely with foil and rest 15 minutes before slicing across the grain.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Cajun Chicken

**Source**: Good Food team, BBC Good Food magazine (September 2007); https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/cajun-chicken (archived: https://web.archive.org/web/20241010111210/https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/cajun-chicken, fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill/barbecue or griddle pan
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) — cooked through
- Time: 5–6 minutes per side (total 10–12 min)

**Ingredients**
- 4 skinless boneless chicken breast fillets
- 1 tbsp paprika
- 1 tsp dried onion flakes
- ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
- 2 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tbsp sunflower oil
- 200g guacamole, to serve

**Method**
1. Dry chicken with kitchen paper, then cut diagonal slashes over the smooth side. Mix spices with seasoning, then tip onto a plate.
2. Brush the chicken on both sides with the oil, then coat all over with the spice mix. Heat a barbecue, griddle pan or grill, then cook the chicken for 5–6 mins on each side until cooked through. Serve each portion with a dollop of guacamole.

## Practical tips

### Preventing drying on the grill
- Marinades that include acid (vinegar, lemon, buttermilk) tenderise the outer fibres and help retain moisture.
- Oil in the marinade prevents the meat from sticking to grates and creates a moisture-locking barrier.
- Do not over-marinate: acid marinades left more than 4–6 hours begin to denature proteins and create a mushy texture at the surface.

### Grill temperature management
- Medium-high heat (around 400 °F / 204 °C at grate level) is ideal for thin cutlets: fast sear, less time for moisture to escape.
- Larger whole breasts benefit from searing on medium-high then finishing on indirect heat (lid closed) to reach 165 °F (74 °C) without burning the outside.
- Let grates heat fully (10 minutes with lid closed) before adding chicken. Cold grates cause sticking.

### Grill marks
- Rotate 90° at the halfway point of each side for professional crosshatch grill marks.
- Never press down on the breast with a spatula — this expels moisture.

### Safe handling
- Reserve marinade before it touches raw chicken if you plan to baste.
- Discard any marinade that touched raw poultry — do not use it as a sauce.

### Seasoning variations for grilled breast
| Variation | Marinade |
|-----------|----------|
| Teriyaki | Soy sauce, brown sugar, ginger, garlic, rice vinegar |
| Lemon-herb | Lemon juice + zest, olive oil, rosemary, thyme, garlic |
| Honey mustard | Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, garlic |
| Buttermilk | Buttermilk, hot sauce, garlic, paprika (classic Southern) |

### Storage
- Slice rested breasts before refrigerating — makes reheating and serving faster.
- Store up to 4 days refrigerated; up to 3 months frozen (sliced in zip-lock bags).
- Reheat gently: microwave at 50% power with a damp paper towel over the container, or warm in a covered pan with 2 tbsp water or broth over low heat.

### MEATER on the grill
- The ambient temperature probe is exposed to flame-radiated heat on a grill — position the chicken away from direct flame to get accurate ambient readings.
- Insert probe lengthwise into the thickest part of the breast.
- For thin cutlets (under 1/2 inch), an instant-read thermometer is more practical than the MEATER probe.
- Target alert: **160 °F (71 °C)** — rest 5 min.

### Scaling and batch cooking
- For batches over 8 breasts, use indirect heat zone to hold already-cooked pieces while finishing the rest.
- Large batches: cook in a wave — put the thickest pieces on first, thinner cutlets last.
- Resting all pieces together under a foil tent works well for batch serving.

> **Summary**: Marinate 20–30 min min; grill medium-high; thin cutlets 4–6 min total; whole breast 10–14 min total.
> Pull at 160 °F (71 °C); rest 5–15 min; carryover to 165 °F (74 °C).

**Quick reference**: Thin cutlets → 4–6 min total at medium-high. Whole thick breast → 10–14 min. Always marinate.
