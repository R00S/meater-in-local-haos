## Cut profile

Pan-seared chicken breast delivers a golden crust through direct contact with a hot pan while keeping the interior juicy. The key techniques are pounding to even thickness so the whole breast finishes simultaneously, drying the surface for maximum Maillard browning, and finishing with a butter baste. A cast-iron or stainless-steel skillet retains heat better than non-stick.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — USDA minimum for poultry

## Source recipes

### 1. Jessica Gavin — How to Cook Chicken on the Stove
**Source**: Jessica Gavin, jessicagavin.com; https://www.jessicagavin.com/how-to-cook-chicken-on-the-stove/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–16 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Pan heat: medium-high for sear, reduce to medium after flip
- Internal target: 160–165 °F (71–74 °C)
- Time: ~5–7 min per side depending on thickness (1/2–3/4 inch target thickness)

**Ingredients**
- 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts (6–10 oz each)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp Italian seasoning

**Method**
1. Place each chicken breast between two sheets of plastic wrap or in a zip-lock bag.
2. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, pound to an even 1/2–3/4 inch thickness.
3. Pat both sides very dry with paper towels — moisture is the enemy of a good crust.
4. Mix salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and Italian seasoning in a small bowl.
5. Season both sides of the chicken generously with the spice blend.
6. Heat a cast-iron or stainless-steel skillet over medium-high heat until the pan is very hot (about 2 minutes).
7. Add olive oil and swirl to coat; the oil should shimmer and barely smoke.
8. Lay chicken breasts presentation-side down in the pan. Press firmly with a spatula to ensure full surface contact.
9. DO NOT MOVE the chicken for 5–7 minutes — letting it sit undisturbed builds the crust.
10. Check: the chicken should release from the pan naturally when the crust is set; if it sticks, wait.
11. Flip chicken. Reduce heat to medium. Add 1 tbsp butter to the pan.
12. As butter melts and foams, tilt the pan and spoon the butter over the chicken repeatedly (baste) for 1–2 minutes.
13. Continue cooking until internal temperature in the thickest part reads 160–165 °F (71–74 °C).
14. Transfer to a cutting board and rest 5 minutes before slicing.

### 2. Skinnytaste — Pan-Seared Chicken Breast (adapted from grilled cutlets method)
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/grilled-chicken-breast/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min + 20 min marinate · **Cook**: 10–14 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Pan heat: medium-high
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C)
- Time: Thin cutlets → 2–3 min per side; Thick whole breasts → 5–7 min per side

**Ingredients**
- 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken breast cutlets (thin-sliced or pounded)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3/4 tsp dried Italian herbs
- 2.5 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Method**
1. Slice or pound chicken breasts to an even thin cutlet (about 1/2 inch).
2. Mix olive oil, garlic, Italian herbs, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
3. Marinate chicken 20–30 minutes at room temperature (or up to 2 hours refrigerated).
4. Heat a large skillet (preferably cast iron) over medium-high heat.
5. Add a thin film of oil; heat until shimmering.
6. Remove chicken from marinade, shake off excess, and lay flat in the pan.
7. Cook thin cutlets 2–3 minutes per side until golden and cooked through to 165 °F (74 °C).
8. For thicker unsplit breasts, cook 5–7 minutes per side.
9. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

### 3. Sevcan Kayabaş — Tavuk Göğüs Sote (Turkish Pan-Sautéed Chicken Breast with Vegetables)
**Source**: Sevcan Kayabaş, Nefis Yemek Tarifleri (Turkey's largest user-recipe platform); https://www.nefisyemektarifleri.com/tavuk-gogus-sote/ (fetched 2026-04-24, language: Turkish — ingredients and method translated below).
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Pan heat: medium-high in a wide skillet with olive oil
- Internal target: 75 °C (167 °F) — Turkish food-safety standard for poultry
- Time: ~20 min total; diced breast pieces cook faster than whole breasts; check thickest cube

**Ingredients** *(translated from Turkish)*
- 3 chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized cubes
- 500 g ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
- 2 red bell peppers, sliced
- 4 green mild peppers (sivri biber), sliced
- 1 large onion, sliced into half-rings
- 1 cup (240 ml) olive oil
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 tsp chilli flakes (pul biber)
- 1 tsp ground black pepper

**Method** *(translated from Turkish)*
1. Heat olive oil in a wide skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add onion and cook until softened, about 4 minutes.
3. Add the chicken breast cubes; sauté, turning occasionally, until sealed on all sides — about 5 minutes.
4. Add red and green peppers; stir-fry 3 minutes.
5. Add chopped tomatoes, salt, chilli flakes, and black pepper.
6. Stir, reduce heat to medium, cover and cook a further 8–10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked through (no pink remaining; internal probe reads 75 °C / 167 °F in the thickest cube) and the tomatoes have broken down into a sauce.
7. Serve immediately with bread or rice.

## Practical tips

### Pan selection matters
- **Cast iron**: retains heat best for a consistent, even sear; requires preheating 2–3 minutes.
- **Stainless steel**: excellent browning, responsive to heat changes, requires more oil to prevent sticking.
- **Non-stick**: prevents sticking but does not achieve the same depth of crust as the above two.

### Avoiding the rubbery breast problem
- Pounding to even thickness is non-negotiable. A breast that is 1.5 inches at the thick end and 0.5 inches at the thin end will have an overcooked thin end before the thick end is safe.
- Dry the surface thoroughly with paper towels. Moisture steams the meat instead of searing it, preventing crust formation and making the breast rubbery.

### Butter basting
- Add butter only after flipping — butter burns quickly and the first side already has its crust.
- Tilting the pan and spooning hot butter over the breast for 60–90 seconds adds flavour and helps the top surface cook without needing to flip again.
- Add aromatics to the butter baste (crushed garlic clove, thyme sprigs) for elevated flavour.

### Deglazing for a pan sauce
- After removing the chicken to rest, do not discard the fond (browned bits).
- Deglaze with 1/4 cup white wine or chicken broth, scraping the fond.
- Swirl in 1 tbsp cold butter for a simple pan sauce to spoon over the rested breast.

### Finishing options
| Finish | How |
|--------|-----|
| Lemon-caper sauce | Deglaze with white wine, add capers + lemon juice, swirl in cold butter |
| Marsala | Deglaze with Marsala wine, add mushrooms, reduce, finish with cream |
| Piccata | Lemon juice, capers, butter sauce while breast rests |
| Plain pan jus | Deglaze with chicken stock, reduce by half, spoon over breast |

### Storage
- Rest, slice, cool to room temperature, then refrigerate within 2 hours.
- Keep refrigerated up to 4 days. Freeze up to 3 months.
- Reheat in covered skillet over low with 2 tbsp chicken broth to prevent drying.

### MEATER probe placement
- Insert probe into the thickest part of the breast, parallel to the long axis.
- Avoid the centre hollow (if any) and any bones from the sternum strip.
- Target alert: **160 °F (71 °C)** — pull, rest 5 min, carryover to 165 °F.
- For MEATER+: ambient probe monitors air temp; ensure it is not touching the basket wall.

> **Summary**: Pound to 1/2–3/4 inch; dry surface; med-high sear 5–7 min; flip + butter baste.
> Pull at 160 °F (71 °C); rest 5 min → 165 °F (74 °C).
