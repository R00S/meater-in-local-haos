## Cut profile

Chicken thighs are a forgiving, flavourful dark-meat cut. Their higher fat content makes them well-suited to the air fryer's rapid circulating heat, which renders the fat and crisps the skin beautifully without drying the meat out. Bone-in skin-on thighs take longer than boneless but deliver the crispiest skin. Dark meat benefits from cooking above the USDA minimum to 175 °F (79 °C), where collagen fully converts to gelatin.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** minimum — **175 °F (79 °C)** preferred for dark meat

## Source recipes

### 1. Natasha's Kitchen — Air Fryer Chicken Thighs
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/air-fryer-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 18–22 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 375 °F (191 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) minimum; 175 °F (79 °C) preferred for juiciest dark meat
- Time: Bone-in skin-on → 18–20 min (toaster-oven style) or 20–22 min (basket style); Boneless → 12–15 min
- Flip halfway through

**Ingredients**
- 4–6 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (or boneless skinless)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 3/4 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

**Method**
1. Pat chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels on both sides — this is the most important step for crispy skin.
2. In a small bowl, combine olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper.
3. Rub the mixture all over the thighs, working it under the skin where possible for maximum flavour.
4. Preheat air fryer to 375 °F (191 °C) for 3 minutes.
5. Arrange thighs skin-side down in the basket in a single layer. Do not overlap.
6. Cook 9–11 minutes for bone-in (first half of cooking), or 6–7 minutes for boneless.
7. Flip with tongs.
8. Cook remaining time: bone-in 9–11 min more; boneless 6–8 min more.
9. For toaster-oven-style air fryers, total time is 18–20 min; basket-style 20–22 min.
10. Check internal temperature in the thickest part of the thigh, avoiding the bone: target 175 °F (79 °C).
11. Rest 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley.

**Notes**
- If cooking 6 thighs that fill the basket, cook in two batches for even air circulation.
- Boneless thighs finish significantly faster — do not rely on bone-in times for boneless cuts.

### 2. Skinnytaste — Air Fryer Chicken Thighs
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/air-fryer-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 24 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 400 °F (204 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) minimum
- Time: 24 minutes total, flipping halfway at 12 min

**Ingredients**
- 4–6 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
- Juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1 tsp herbs de Provence (or Italian seasoning)
- 1 tsp olive oil

**Method**
1. Pat chicken thighs dry with paper towels.
2. In a small bowl, mix lemon juice, salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, sweet paprika, herbs de Provence, and olive oil.
3. Rub seasoning mixture all over the thighs, including under the skin.
4. Preheat air fryer to 400 °F (204 °C).
5. Arrange thighs skin-side up in the basket in a single layer.
6. Cook at 400 °F for 12 minutes.
7. Open basket, flip thighs skin-side down.
8. Cook another 12 minutes (24 min total).
9. Check internal temp in thickest part (avoid bone): at least 165 °F (74 °C); ideally 175 °F (79 °C).
10. If cooking 6 thighs that won't fit in one layer, cook in two batches.
11. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

## Practical tips

### Bone-in vs. boneless in the air fryer
- Bone-in thighs: richer flavour, more forgiving, significantly longer cook time (18–22 min at 375 °F vs. 12–15 min for boneless).
- Boneless thighs: cook faster, easier to serve, still very moist due to dark-meat fat.
- Skin-on: always produces crispier results. Skinless thighs are quicker but won't develop the same golden crackle.

### The 175 °F sweet spot for dark meat
- USDA minimum is 165 °F (74 °C) for all poultry. This is safe.
- However, chicken thighs contain more collagen than breast. Collagen converts to gelatin (which makes meat taste juicy) at temperatures above 170 °F (77 °C).
- Thighs pulled at 175 °F (79 °C) are noticeably juicier and more tender in texture than those pulled at 165 °F.
- Thighs are forgiving — unlike breast, they do not dry out significantly between 165 °F and 185 °F.

### Air fryer basket management
- Thighs should not overlap in the basket. Overlapping traps steam between pieces and prevents crisping on that contact surface.
- If cooking for more than 4–6 people, cook in two batches and keep the first batch warm in a 200 °F oven.
- Some air fryers have a "preheat" function — use it for 3 minutes before adding thighs for the most consistent cook.

### Sauce applications
- Apply sticky sauces (BBQ, teriyaki, honey-garlic) only in the last 3–4 minutes of cooking — sugar in sauces burns quickly at 375–400 °F.
- Dry rubs can go on at the beginning and will form a flavourful crust throughout the cook.

### Seasoning variations
| Style | Rub / Marinade |
|-------|----------------|
| Classic | Garlic powder, paprika, Italian seasoning, salt, pepper |
| Lemon herb | Lemon zest + juice, thyme, oregano, garlic, olive oil |
| Honey garlic | Apply last 4 min: honey, soy, garlic |
| Cajun | Cayenne, smoked paprika, thyme, garlic powder |
| Korean BBQ | Gochujang, soy, sesame, garlic, brown sugar — apply last 3 min |

### Storage and reheating air-fried thighs
- Refrigerate leftovers up to 4 days.
- Reheat in air fryer at 375 °F for 5–6 min — this restores most of the crispy skin texture.
- Avoid microwaving: the skin becomes soft and chewy.

> **Summary**: Air fry at 375–400 °F; bone-in 18–22 min; boneless 12–15 min; flip halfway.
> Target 175 °F (79 °C) for best dark-meat texture. Rest 5 min.
