## Cut profile

Chicken thighs are the ideal grilling cut: their higher fat content means they stay moist even over high, direct heat and they are far more forgiving than breast if slightly overcooked. Bone-in skin-on thighs take 25–30 minutes; boneless thighs are quicker at 10–15 minutes. A good marinade and BBQ baste add deep flavour and help char the exterior while keeping the interior juicy.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** minimum — **170 °F (77 °C)** preferred for dark meat

## Source recipes

### 1. Jessica Gavin — Grilled Chicken Thighs
**Source**: Jessica Gavin, jessicagavin.com; https://www.jessicagavin.com/grilled-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min + 30 min marinate · **Cook**: 25–30 min (bone-in) · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill temp: preheat 375–400 °F (191–204 °C), oil grates
- Internal target: 165–170 °F (74–77 °C) in thickest part
- Time: Bone-in → 25–30 min total (sear 5 min each side, then indirect); Boneless → 10–15 min total

**Ingredients (marinade)**
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice + 1 tsp lemon zest
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp honey
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tbsp water (to thin marinade slightly)

**Chicken**
- 2–3 lbs bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (or boneless skinless)

**Method**
1. Whisk all marinade ingredients together in a bowl.
2. Reserve 3 tbsp marinade in a separate small bowl for basting (do not re-use marinade that touched raw chicken).
3. Place thighs in a zip-lock bag or shallow dish. Pour remaining marinade over.
4. Seal and refrigerate 30 minutes minimum (up to 4 hours for deeper flavour).
5. Remove chicken from refrigerator 15 minutes before grilling.
6. Preheat grill to 375–400 °F (191–204 °C). Clean grates thoroughly and oil with a paper towel.
7. Remove thighs from marinade, shake off excess. Discard used marinade.
8. Place thighs skin-side down on hot grates. Sear 5 minutes undisturbed to build char.
9. Flip to bone-side down. Brush with reserved marinade.
10. For bone-in: move to indirect heat, cover grill, cook 15–20 more minutes, basting every 5 minutes.
11. For boneless: cook direct heat 5 more minutes per side, basting each flip.
12. Pull when internal temp in thickest part (avoid bone) reads 165–170 °F (74–77 °C).
13. Rest under foil 5 minutes before serving.

### 2. Natasha's Kitchen — Juicy BBQ Chicken Thighs
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/juicy-barbecued-chicken-thighs-recipe/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 10 min + 6 hr marinate · **Cook**: 24–30 min · **Rest**: 5–10 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill temp: oil grates, preheat on medium-high, reduce to low when chicken goes on
- Internal target: juices run clear; use thermometer to confirm 165–170 °F (74–77 °C)
- Time: 12–15 min per side on low heat

**Ingredients**
- 3 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 1 tsp sea salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp seasoning blend (Mrs. Dash or similar, or: 1/2 tsp garlic powder + 1/2 tsp onion powder)
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder (additional)
- 1 tbsp mayonnaise (mixed into marinade for moisture and browning)
- 1 medium onion, sliced (layered around chicken in marinade)
- Your favourite BBQ sauce, for basting

**Method**
1. Combine salt, pepper, seasoning blend, garlic powder, and mayonnaise in a large bowl to form marinade paste.
2. Add chicken thighs and sliced onion. Toss to coat thoroughly.
3. Cover and refrigerate 6 hours minimum; overnight is ideal.
4. Remove from refrigerator 20 minutes before grilling.
5. Preheat grill on medium-high with lid closed to build heat. Oil grates generously.
6. Reduce heat to low just before placing chicken.
7. Remove thighs from marinade, shaking off excess (discard marinade and onion).
8. Lay thighs on grate. Cook 12–15 minutes on first side without moving.
9. Brush top generously with BBQ sauce before flipping.
10. Flip, brush the cooked side with BBQ sauce.
11. Cook another 12–15 minutes on low, basting once more halfway through.
12. Pull when internal temperature confirms 165–170 °F (74–77 °C).
13. Rest 5–10 minutes before serving. Serve with extra BBQ sauce.

### 3. Immaculate Bites — Hawaiian Grilled Chicken Thighs
**Source**: Imma Adamu, Africanbites.com (Cameroonian-born food blogger, based in USA); https://www.africanbites.com/hawaiian-grilled-chicken-thighs/ (fetched 2026-04-24).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min + 3 hr–overnight marinating · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill heat: preheat to medium-high; clean and oil grates
- Internal target: **165 °F (74 °C)** — probe in thickest part of thigh, away from bone
- Time: 4–5 min first side (until grill marks form), then flip and continue flipping every 3–4 min, ~25–30 min total
- Baste with reduced marinade in the final 5 minutes

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg bone-in skin-on chicken thighs
- Salt and black pepper to taste (use sparingly — soy sauce is salty)
- 120 ml pineapple juice (or passion fruit juice)
- 60 ml soy sauce
- 60 g brown sugar
- 2 tbsp sriracha (or sambal oelek)
- 2 tbsp ketchup
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- Juice of ½ lemon
- 3 cloves garlic, minced

**Method**
1. Season chicken thighs lightly with salt and pepper; set aside.
2. Whisk together pineapple juice, soy sauce, brown sugar, sriracha, ketchup, ginger, cumin, lemon juice, and garlic in a medium bowl.
3. Pour marinade over chicken in a zip-lock bag or covered bowl; massage to coat all surfaces.
4. Refrigerate at least 3 hours, preferably overnight for maximum flavour penetration.
5. Remove chicken from fridge 30 minutes before grilling; drain and reserve the marinade.
6. In a small saucepan, bring the reserved marinade to a boil; simmer 5 minutes. Set aside for basting.
7. Clean grill grates; brush lightly with oil. Preheat grill to medium-high.
8. Place chicken skin-side down; cook 4–5 minutes until grill marks form.
9. Flip and continue cooking, flipping every 3–4 minutes to avoid burning, until internal temperature reaches **165 °F (74 °C)** — total approximately 25–30 minutes.
10. Baste with cooked marinade during the final 5 minutes of grilling.
11. Rest 5 minutes; garnish with sliced green onions. Serve with steamed white rice.

## Practical tips

### Bone-in vs. boneless on the grill
- Bone-in skin-on thighs: require longer cook time (25–30 min) but produce more flavourful, juicy results because the bone acts as a heat buffer slowing cooking near the joint.
- Boneless skinless thighs: cook in 10–15 min, are easier to eat but more prone to drying if left on too long.
- If boneless, pound to an even thickness (~1/2 inch) for consistent cooking across the whole thigh.

### Two-zone grilling for bone-in thighs
- Sear skin-side down over direct high heat 3–4 minutes to render fat and char the skin.
- Move to indirect heat (no flames below) and cook covered the remaining 20 minutes.
- This prevents flare-ups from dripping fat while still delivering charred flavour.

### BBQ sauce timing
- Apply BBQ sauce only in the last 5 minutes — sugars in BBQ sauce burn easily over direct flame.
- Apply one coat, close lid 2–3 min, flip, apply second coat, close lid 2–3 min.
- Multiple thin coats build better flavour and lacquer than one thick coat.

### Marinade safety
- Always discard marinade that has been in contact with raw chicken.
- If you want to use the marinade as a finishing sauce or for basting, set aside a portion before adding raw chicken.

### Seasoning and glaze ideas
| Style | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Classic BBQ | Dry rub + BBQ glaze last 5 min |
| Teriyaki | Teriyaki marinade, glaze with sauce last 3 min |
| Jerk | Scotch bonnet, allspice, thyme, cinnamon marinade |
| Greek | Lemon, garlic, oregano, olive oil, yogurt marinade |

### Storage
- Leftover grilled thighs refrigerate up to 4 days.
- Reheat in a covered skillet over medium-low with 2 tbsp water, or in a 350 °F oven for 10–12 min.
- Slice cold leftover thighs thinly for sandwiches or grain bowls.
