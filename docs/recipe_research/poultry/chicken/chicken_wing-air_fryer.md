## Cut profile

Chicken wings (wingettes and drumettes) are small, high-surface-area dark-meat pieces that become irresistibly crispy in the air fryer without deep frying. The single most effective technique for extra-crispy skin is tossing wings with a small amount of baking powder before cooking — it raises the skin's pH, causing it to blister and brown faster. Patting wings dry is equally critical.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — USDA minimum for poultry

## Source recipes

### 1. Natasha's Kitchen — Air Fryer Chicken Wings
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/air-fryer-chicken-wings/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 16 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 400 °F (204 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C); wings are done when golden and crispy
- Time: 8 min + shake/flip + 8 min = 16 min total (no preheating needed)

**Ingredients**
- 2 lbs chicken wing pieces (wingettes and drumettes, separated)
- 1 tsp oil (to help seasoning adhere)
- 1 tsp garlic salt
- 1 tsp lemon pepper seasoning
- 1 tsp baking powder (NOT baking soda — this is the key to crispy skin)

**Method**
1. Pat chicken wings completely dry with paper towels — this is the most important step.
2. In a large bowl, toss wings with oil until lightly coated.
3. Add garlic salt, lemon pepper, and baking powder. Toss thoroughly to coat every wing.
4. Arrange wings in a single layer in air fryer basket. Do not overlap — work in batches if needed.
5. Air fry at 400 °F (204 °C) for 8 minutes.
6. Open basket, shake or flip wings.
7. Air fry another 8 minutes until golden brown and crispy (16 min total).
8. No preheating is required for this recipe.
9. Check that internal temp reads 165 °F (74 °C) at the thickest part.
10. Serve immediately for maximum crispiness.

**Notes on baking powder**:
- Use aluminium-free baking powder if possible to avoid a metallic taste.
- Ratio: approximately 1 tsp baking powder per 1 lb of wings.
- The alkaline baking powder accelerates Maillard browning and desiccates the skin surface for an extra-blistered texture.

### 2. Skinnytaste — Air Fryer Chicken Wings
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/air-fryer-chicken-wings/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 16–22 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Air fryer temp: 400 °F (204 °C)
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C)
- Time: 16–22 min total, shaking basket halfway through

**Ingredients**
- 2 lbs chicken wingettes and drumettes
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp dried oregano
- 1/4 tsp dried thyme
- 1/4 tsp dried sage
- 1 tsp baking powder (for crispiness)
- Cooking spray

**Method**
1. Pat wings very dry with paper towels.
2. In a large bowl, combine salt, garlic powder, onion powder, sweet paprika, oregano, thyme, sage, and baking powder.
3. Add wings and toss to coat evenly in the dry seasoning mixture.
4. Lightly spray air fryer basket with cooking spray.
5. Arrange wings in a single layer in the basket.
6. Air fry at 400 °F (204 °C) for 8–11 minutes.
7. Open basket, shake to redistribute or flip individual pieces.
8. Air fry another 8–11 minutes until crispy and golden all over (16–22 min total).
9. Internal temp should read 165 °F (74 °C).
10. Serve immediately with your favourite dipping sauce.

## Practical tips

### The baking powder secret
- Baking powder (aluminium-free) is the most effective ingredient for achieving deep-fried-level crispiness in the air fryer.
- The alkaline environment created by baking powder accelerates the Maillard browning reaction at lower temperatures.
- It also desiccates the skin surface, creating tiny blisters that crisp up perfectly.
- Use exactly 1 tsp per pound of wings — more creates a chalky aftertaste.
- **Do not substitute baking soda** — it is more alkaline, creates an off taste, and turns the skin orange.

### Drying wings before cooking
- Pat wings with paper towels immediately before seasoning.
- For maximum dryness: place seasoned wings on a wire rack over a baking sheet, refrigerate uncovered 1–8 hours. The cold dry air of the refrigerator further desiccates the skin.

### Sauce application for air fryer wings
- Toss cooked wings in sauce immediately after cooking while they are still hot — the hot surface helps the sauce adhere and the residual heat thickens it slightly.
- For sauces containing butter (Buffalo): melt butter + hot sauce, toss wings, serve immediately.
- For sticky glazes (honey garlic, teriyaki): toss, then return wings to air fryer for 2–3 minutes at 400 °F to set the glaze.

### Serving
- Wings lose crispiness within 15–20 minutes of cooking as steam from the interior softens the skin.
- Serve on a wire rack rather than stacked on a plate — stacking traps steam under the wings.
- For party preparation, cook wings 5 minutes short, hold at 200 °F oven, then blast at 400 °F for 4 min to finish just before serving.

### Sauce variations for air fryer wings
| Sauce | Ingredients |
|-------|-------------|
| Classic Buffalo | 1/3 cup hot sauce + 3 tbsp melted butter + 1 tsp garlic powder |
| Honey-garlic | 3 tbsp honey + 2 tbsp soy sauce + 3 cloves minced garlic |
| Lemon-pepper | Melted butter + lemon zest + cracked black pepper + parsley |
| Teriyaki | Teriyaki sauce — apply last 3 min, then 400 °F 2 min to set |
| Dry Korean BBQ | Gochugaru, brown sugar, sesame oil, soy sauce (in dry rub) |

### MEATER and wings
- Wings are too small to probe reliably with a MEATER.
- Use the established temperature/time guidelines (400 °F, 16–22 min) and visual cues (golden, crispy, juices run clear).
- As a safety check, use an instant-read thermometer in the meatiest part of a drumette.

### Storage
- Wings are best eaten immediately. If storing, refrigerate up to 3 days.
- Reheat in air fryer at 400 °F for 4–5 min to restore crispiness.
- Toss in sauce again after reheating if skin has dried out.

> **Summary**: Air fry at 400 °F for 16–22 min (shake/flip at halfway). Key: pat dry + baking powder.
> 165 °F (74 °C) internal. Sauce immediately after cooking for best adhesion.
