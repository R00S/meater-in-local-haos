## Cut profile

Grilling chicken wings over direct heat creates a combination of radiant (from the fire) and convective (hot air above the grate) heat that no other method replicates. The skin renders more slowly than in an air fryer or deep fryer because the heat is applied intermittently as the wings are flipped, but the smoke flavour and char marks make grilled wings distinctively different. The key challenge is avoiding flare-ups: as the subcutaneous fat renders and drips onto hot coals or burners, it ignites and can char the skin before the interior reaches safe temperature.

Medium heat (approximately 350 °F / 175 °C at grate level) with a covered grill allows the heat to build up inside the lid, cooking the wing through more evenly while still producing crisped skin. High heat (450 °F / 230 °C) over direct fire gives a faster, more intensely charred result but requires constant attention and flipping.

Wings are dark meat and can tolerate slight overcooking; pulling them at 165–175 °F (74–79 °C) internal produces fully safe, juicy meat. Carryover is 1–2 °C only, due to the high surface-to-mass ratio of wings.

## Source recipes

### 1. Taste of Home — Grilled Chicken Wings with Buffalo Sauce
**Source**: Nicole Doster, reviewed by Lindsay D. Mattison; Taste of Home; https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/grilled-chicken-wings/ (fetched 2026-04-27).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill temp: **medium heat** (~350 °F / 175 °C)
- Internal target: not explicitly stated; recipe criterion is "juices run clear" (~165 °F / 74 °C per USDA poultry standard)
- Time: 20–25 minutes covered, turning occasionally

**Ingredients**
- 4 lbs chicken wing pieces (wingettes and drumettes), separated
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp cornstarch
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper

*Buffalo sauce:*
- ½ cup Louisiana-style hot sauce
- 2 tsp white wine vinegar
- ½ tsp garlic powder
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter

**Method**
1. Separate whole wings into drumettes and wingettes; discard tips or freeze for stock.
2. In a large bowl, toss wings with olive oil until lightly coated.
3. In a small bowl, combine cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Sprinkle over wings and toss to coat.
4. Preheat grill to medium heat. Oil the grill rack (use tongs and an oil-soaked paper towel — do not spray directly onto hot grate).
5. Place wings on grill. Cook covered over medium heat for **20–25 minutes**, turning occasionally, until outsides are crisp and juices run clear.
6. Meanwhile, make sauce: in a small saucepan over medium heat, combine hot sauce, vinegar, and garlic powder. Whisk in butter until melted. Keep warm.
7. Transfer wings to a large bowl. Add sauce, toss to coat. Serve immediately.

**Notes**:
- Cornstarch in the coating promotes Maillard browning and helps achieve a lightly crispy exterior on the grill.
- Recipe is explicitly for a gas or charcoal grill.

### 2. Immaculate Bites — Crispy Grilled Chicken Wings
**Source**: Imma Adamu, Africanbites.com (Cameroonian-born food blogger, based in USA); https://www.africanbites.com/grilled-chicken-wings/ (fetched 2026-04-27).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 10 min (+overnight dry-brine option) · **Cook**: 30 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill heat: **450 °F / 230 °C** (high); clean hot grates + lightly oiled
- Internal target: **165 °F (75 °C)** — explicitly stated in recipe FAQ section
- Time: 30 minutes total, flipping every 5 minutes

**Ingredients**
- 1 kg chicken wing pieces (flats and drumettes, separated)
- 2 tsp garlic powder
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp baking powder *(key for crispiness)*
- Light oil spray

**Method**
1. Pat wings thoroughly dry with paper towels.
2. Combine spices, salt, pepper, and baking powder in a small bowl.
3. Toss wings in spice mix until fully coated. Add a light drizzle of oil to help prevent sticking.
4. **For best results**: lay seasoned wings on a wire rack uncovered in the refrigerator overnight.
5. Remove from fridge 30 minutes before cooking.
6. Clean grill grates; preheat to high (450 °F / 230 °C). Oil the grates.
7. Place wings directly on grate; grill 30 minutes, flipping every 5 minutes. Watch for flare-ups.
8. Internal temperature in the thickest part of the drumette should read **165 °F (75 °C)**.
9. Serve with dipping sauces of choice.

## Temperature summary

| Source | Country / tradition | Grill temp | Time | Internal target |
|--------|---------------------|-----------|------|----------------|
| Taste of Home | USA (Culinary Group B) | Medium (~350°F / 175°C) | 20–25 min | ~165°F (74°C) implied |
| Immaculate Bites | Cameroon/USA (Culinary Group C) | High (450°F / 230°C) | 30 min | **165°F (75°C)** explicit |

**Consensus**: 165 °F (74 °C) is the universal safe minimum. High-heat grilling (450 °F) requires less time and produces more char; medium-heat covered grilling is more forgiving. Baking powder in the dry rub is a cross-tradition technique for crispier skin on the grill.

**MEATER settings**:
- Set alert at **160 °F (71 °C)** to allow 2 min rest + carryover to reach 165 °F
- Wings are small — insert probe into the thickest drumette to get the most reliable reading
