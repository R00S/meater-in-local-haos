# Ground Chicken × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Braised ground chicken is the long-simmered ragù-style preparation — coarsely-ground chicken thigh (the standard for flavour and moisture, 12–18 % fat) is browned hard for Maillard depth, then simmered in liquid until the texture turns plush and the crumble has fully bound to the sauce. Ground chicken thigh produces a richer, juicier braise than ground breast because of the higher fat and connective-tissue content. The cooking goal is twofold: hold the meat well above the food-safety threshold for ground poultry (74 °C / 165 °F) for long enough to develop deep flavour, and reduce the sauce to a clinging, glossy texture. Carryover is irrelevant in a braise — the meat sits at simmer temperature until removed.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Ragù di Pollo (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997), pp. 158–160 (the chicken variant of her *ragù bianco* technique)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 75 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground chicken thigh
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 1 medium carrot, finely chopped
- 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
- 4 sage leaves
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 240 ml dry white wine
- 240 ml whole milk
- 240 ml chicken stock
- A grating of nutmeg
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil and butter in a heavy braising pan over medium heat. Add onion, carrot and celery; sauté 8 minutes until pale gold.
2. Add sage and rosemary; 30 seconds.
3. Add chicken in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
4. Brown 6–8 minutes, breaking apart with a wooden spoon.
5. Add wine; simmer until almost evaporated (5 minutes).
6. Add milk and nutmeg; simmer 8 minutes until milk is absorbed.
7. Add stock; bring to a bare simmer. Cover partially and reduce to lowest heat. Simmer 50 minutes.
8. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Hazan's *ragù bianco di pollo* is done when the sauce is thick, glossy, and the chicken has turned nearly dissolving in the mouth — well above the food-safety threshold and at the texture inflection point.
9. Rest 5 minutes off heat for fat to layer. Toss with fresh tagliatelle. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (creamy, herbal, wine-perfumed).

### 2. Andrea Nguyen — Gà Kho with Ground Chicken (Vietnamese caramelised braise)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (Ten Speed Press, 2019), pp. 134–136 — the ground-chicken variant of her *gà kho* technique; vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground chicken thigh
- 30 g caster sugar (for the caramel)
- 15 ml water
- 30 ml neutral oil
- 4 shallots, finely chopped
- 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 30 ml fish sauce
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 240 ml coconut water
- 2 spring onions, sliced, to finish
- 1 long red chilli, sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Make the caramel: combine sugar and water in a heavy pan; cook over medium heat, swirling, until deep amber (5 minutes). Off heat add 30 ml warm water carefully (it will spit). Set aside.
2. Heat oil in a wide heavy braising pan over medium-high heat. Add shallots; sauté 4 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
3. Add chicken in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
4. Brown 5–6 minutes, breaking apart.
5. Add fish sauce, pepper and the prepared caramel; stir to coat. Pour in coconut water; bring to a simmer.
6. Cover partially; reduce to low. Simmer 30 minutes, stirring twice.
7. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Nguyen's test: liquid reduced to a glossy clinging sauce, chicken is glossy-brown.
8. Rest 5 minutes off heat. Top with spring onion and chilli. Serve over jasmine rice. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (caramel-savoury, fish-sauce funky, glossy).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Spiced Chicken Mince with Tomato & Yoghurt (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014) technique extended; ottolenghi.co.uk recipes — chicken-mince adaptations  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground chicken thigh
- 30 ml olive oil
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin + 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander + 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon + 2 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 200 g chopped tomatoes
- 240 ml chicken stock
- 30 g pine nuts, toasted, to finish
- 100 g thick Greek yoghurt, to serve
- 5 g chopped mint and parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a heavy braising pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; cook 6 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Bloom cumin, coriander, cinnamon and paprika 30 seconds.
3. Add chicken in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
4. Brown 5 minutes, breaking apart.
5. Add tomatoes, stock and salt; bring to a simmer. Cover partially; reduce to low. Simmer 35–40 minutes.
6. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Ottolenghi targets sauce-bound, glossy mince that holds shape on a spoon.
7. Rest 5 minutes off heat. Top with pine nuts, mint, parsley and dollops of yoghurt. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (spice-rich, creamy-cool counterpoint).

### 4. Tina Nordström — Kycklingfärssås (Swedish ragù tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009), pp. 90–92 — the chicken variant of her *köttfärssås*; köket.se  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 70 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g kycklingfärs (ground chicken thigh)
- 30 g smör (butter)
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- 1 medium morot (carrot), finely diced
- 1 stick selleri (celery), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 ml tomatpuré
- 240 ml dry white wine
- 400 g chopped tomatoes
- 240 ml chicken stock
- 5 ml Worcestershire-sås
- 2 bay leaves, 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt, 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper, 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar
- 100 ml grädde (cream), added at the end

**Method**
1. Melt butter in a heavy gryta over medium heat. Sauté onion, carrot and celery 8 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Push veg aside; add färs in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Brown 5–6 minutes, breaking apart. Mix with vegetables.
4. Stir in tomatpuré; cook 2 minutes. Add wine; reduce by half (4 minutes). Add tomatoes, stock, Worcestershire, herbs, salt, white pepper and allspice.
5. Cover partially; reduce to low. Simmer 55 minutes, stirring every 20 minutes.
6. Stir in cream; simmer 5 minutes more.
7. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Nordström targets a glossy, cream-rounded ragù that clings to pasta.
8. Rest 10 minutes off heat. Toss with fresh tagliatelle or serve over potatismos. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (creamy, herbal, savoury, allspice-perfumed).
