# Ground Chicken × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan-fried ground chicken — the loose-mince preparation rather than patties — is a quick, weeknight technique that produces small browned crumbles for stir-fries, lettuce wraps, ragùs and tacos. Ground chicken from thighs (the standard for flavour and moisture) carries 12–18 % fat and yields a juicier, more browned crumble than ground from breast (3–5 % fat, prone to dryness). The cooking goal is twofold: drive every part of the meat past the food-safety threshold for ground poultry (74 °C / 165 °F), and develop deep Maillard browning on the crumble surfaces. Probe the largest meat clump rather than the pan to confirm the centre passes 74 °C. Carryover is irrelevant — the meat sits at pan temperature until removed.

## Source recipes

### 1. Andrea Nguyen — Larb Gai with Ground Chicken (Lao / Northern Thai tradition)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *Asian Dumplings* (Ten Speed Press, 2009) (filling); technique extended in her *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (2019); vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground chicken thigh
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) neutral oil
- 4 shallots, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 30 g (2 tbsp) toasted rice powder (khao khua)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fish sauce
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) lime juice
- 5 g (1 tsp) palm sugar
- 5 g (1 tsp) Thai chilli flakes (prik bon)
- 30 g sliced mint, 30 g sliced coriander, 4 spring onions
- Iceberg or butter lettuce leaves, to serve

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add shallots; sauté 3 minutes. Add garlic and ginger; 1 minute.
2. Add the chicken in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump, tip at centre.
3. Cook 5–6 minutes, breaking apart with a wooden spoon and stirring frequently.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Nguyen's standard for Larb is fully cooked but still juicy — the lime-fish sauce dressing requires a hot crumble.
5. Off heat, stir in toasted rice powder, fish sauce, lime juice, palm sugar and chilli flakes. Toss with mint, coriander and spring onions. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Serve in lettuce cups. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (lime-bright, fish-sauce funky, herb-scented).

### 2. Hugh Acheson — Pan-Fried Chicken Mince with Sage & Lemon (American Southern tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *A New Turn in the South* (Clarkson Potter, 2011); hughacheson.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground chicken thigh
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 8 fresh sage leaves, sliced
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, to finish
- 5 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil and butter in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; sauté 4 minutes. Add garlic and sage; 1 minute.
2. Add chicken in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Cook 6 minutes, breaking apart and stirring.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Acheson's standard for ground chicken: fully cooked through, the crumble browned on multiple faces.
5. Off heat, stir in lemon zest, juice, salt and pepper. Sprinkle with Parmesan and parsley. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Serve over polenta or pasta. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (sage-perfumed, lemon-bright, cheese-rich).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Spiced Chicken Mince with Pine Nuts & Yoghurt (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014) technique extended; ottolenghi.co.uk recipes — chicken mince adaptations  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground chicken thigh
- 30 ml olive oil
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon
- 2 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 30 g pine nuts, toasted, to finish
- 200 g thick Greek yoghurt, to serve
- 5 g chopped mint and parsley, to finish
- A drizzle of pomegranate molasses, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; cook 6 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Bloom cumin, coriander, cinnamon and paprika 30 seconds.
3. Add chicken in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
4. Cook 6–7 minutes, breaking apart.
5. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Ottolenghi targets fully cooked, deeply spiced and well browned.
6. Off heat, season with salt. Top with pine nuts, mint, parsley, dollops of yoghurt and a drizzle of pomegranate molasses. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (spice-rich, creamy-cool, sweet-tart).

### 4. Tina Nordström — Stekt Kycklingfärs på Lök (Swedish home tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009); köket.se  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g kycklingfärs (ground chicken thigh)
- 30 g smör (butter) + 30 ml rapsolja (rapeseed oil)
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar (allspice)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) Worcestershire-sås
- 100 ml grädde (cream), added at the end
- 15 g chopped fresh dill, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat butter and oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; sauté 5 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Add färs in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Cook 6 minutes, breaking apart and stirring.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Nordström's standard: fully cooked through, crumble browned, the Swedish home preparation.
5. Off heat, stir in salt, white pepper, allspice, Worcestershire and cream. Sprinkle dill. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Serve with kokt potatis and rårörda lingon. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (allspice-perfumed, cream-rounded).
