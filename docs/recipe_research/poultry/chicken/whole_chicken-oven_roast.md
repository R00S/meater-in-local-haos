## Cut profile

Oven roasting is the most common method for whole chicken worldwide and the method best suited to producing the golden, crispy-skinned result that most recipes aim for. The oven surrounds the bird with hot air (175–230 °C depending on technique), rendering subcutaneous fat, desiccating the skin, and driving Maillard browning across the entire exposed surface. The key challenge is the temperature differential between breast and thigh: the breast, exposed at the top and with less fat, heats faster and dries out if allowed to go above 165 °F (74 °C), while the thigh benefits from reaching 170–175 °F (77–79 °C).

Two common approaches: (1) high heat throughout (220–230 °C) — produces very crispy skin in ~60–75 min but requires careful probe placement and pulling at thigh 165–170 °F; (2) moderate heat (180–190 °C) — more even cooking, longer time (~75–90 min), less risk of breast overcook.

The MEATER probe is placed in the thigh, from the cavity side, directed towards the thigh bone without touching it. The thigh temperature is the limiting factor — the breast will be safe when the thigh reaches 165 °F. Carryover in the thigh is 3–5 °C; in the breast, 4–7 °C. Rest the bird uncovered on a rack for 10–15 minutes before carving.

## Source recipes

### 1. Jessica Gavin — Whole Roasted Chicken
**Source**: Jessica Gavin, jessicagavin.com; https://www.jessicagavin.com/roasted-chicken/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: ~60 min · **Rest**: 20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 475 °F (246 °C) throughout
- Breast target: 160–165 °F (71–74 °C)
- Thigh target: 165–170 °F (74–77 °C)
- Time: 30 min breast-side down + flip + ~30 min breast-side up = ~60 min total for a 3.5–4 lb bird

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole chicken, 3.5–4 lbs, giblets removed
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted (for brushing)
- 1 tsp kosher salt (for brushing)
- 1/2 tsp black pepper (for brushing)
- Kitchen twine (for trussing)

**Cavity seasoning**
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 3 cloves garlic, crushed
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary

**Roasting bed (aromatics)**
- 2 celery stalks, roughly chopped
- 2 medium carrots, roughly chopped
- 1 medium onion, quartered

**Method**
1. Remove chicken from refrigerator 30–45 minutes before roasting to bring to near room temperature.
2. Preheat oven to 475 °F (246 °C).
3. Pat the entire outside of the chicken completely dry with paper towels, including inside the cavity.
4. Season the cavity generously with salt and pepper. Stuff with crushed garlic, thyme, and rosemary.
5. Truss the bird: tuck wing tips behind the back; tie legs together with kitchen twine. Trussing promotes even cooking and a compact shape that fits better on the roasting rack.
6. Scatter chopped celery, carrots, and onion in the bottom of a cast-iron skillet or roasting pan. These aromatics act as a natural rack and flavour the pan drippings.
7. Place chicken breast-side DOWN on the vegetable bed — this allows the thigh and leg (which need the most heat) to be on top while protecting the lean breast.
8. Brush exposed back and legs with half the melted butter. Season with salt and pepper.
9. Roast breast-side down at 475 °F for 30 minutes.
10. Remove from oven. Using tongs or two large spoons inserted into the cavity, carefully flip chicken breast-side UP. (The bird is very hot — work carefully to avoid steam burns.)
11. Brush breast and legs with remaining melted butter. Season lightly.
12. Return to oven. Roast breast-side up another 25–30 minutes.
13. Check temperatures: insert thermometer into the thickest part of the breast (not touching bone) — target 160–165 °F (71–74 °C). Then check the thigh — target 165–170 °F (74–77 °C).
14. If breast is done but thigh is not, tent breast loosely with foil and return to oven for 5–10 more minutes.
15. Transfer chicken to a cutting board. Rest UNCOVERED 20 minutes. This is not optional — resting allows juices to redistribute and carryover cooking to equalise temperatures throughout the bird.
16. Carve and serve. Use pan drippings to make a simple pan sauce.

### 2. Natasha's Kitchen — Juicy Whole Roast Chicken (adapted from roast turkey method)
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/juicy-roast-turkey/ (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 20 min (for 4–5 lb bird at 325–350 °F) · **Rest**: 20 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 325–350 °F (163–177 °C) — lower and slower than high-heat method
- Breast target: 160–165 °F (71–74 °C)
- Thigh target: 165–170 °F (74–77 °C)
- Time: approximately 20 min per lb at 325 °F; cover breast with foil until last 30 min to prevent drying

**Ingredients (flavoured butter)**
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp lemon zest

**Chicken**
- 1 whole chicken, 4–5 lbs
- Kosher salt and black pepper

**Cavity aromatics**
- 1/2 medium onion
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1/2 lemon, halved
- 4–5 sprigs fresh parsley

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 325 °F (163 °C).
2. Mix softened butter with minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, pepper, and lemon zest to form flavoured compound butter.
3. Pat chicken dry inside and out with paper towels.
4. Season cavity generously with salt and pepper. Stuff cavity with onion half, smashed garlic, lemon, and parsley sprigs.
5. Using your fingers, carefully loosen the skin over the breast and thighs without tearing it.
6. Push spoonfuls of compound butter under the skin directly onto the meat. Smooth the butter evenly under the skin with your fingers.
7. Rub remaining butter over the outside skin.
8. Season outside of bird generously with salt and pepper.
9. Place breast-side up in a roasting pan on a rack (or on a vegetable bed).
10. Loosely tent the breast with a piece of foil — this protects the lean breast from drying while the thighs catch up.
11. Roast at 325 °F for approximately 20 min per lb (80 min for a 4 lb bird, 100 min for a 5 lb bird).
12. Remove foil tent 30 minutes before estimated finish time to allow skin to brown.
13. Baste with pan drippings every 30 minutes.
14. Check internal temps: breast 160–165 °F (71–74 °C), thigh 165–170 °F (74–77 °C).
15. Rest on cutting board 20 minutes before carving.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Simple Roast Chicken
**Source**: Good Food team, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/simple-roast-chicken (fetched 2026-04-24). Published: Good Food magazine, October 2011.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 70 min · **Rest**: 15 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Phase 1: 220 °C / 200 °C fan / gas 7 for 20 min (initial blast to brown and begin rendering)
- Phase 2: 190 °C / 170 °C fan / gas 5 for a further 45 min
- Doneness: juices run clear when the thigh is pierced with a knife
- Rest 15 min before carving

**Ingredients**
- 1.8–2 kg / 4–4½ lb chicken
- 2 garlic cloves, bashed
- 2 bay leaves
- 50 g / 2 oz butter, softened
- Juice of 1 lemon, plus the squeezed halves for the cavity
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Heat oven to 220 °C / 200 °C fan / gas 7. Put the chicken in a large roasting tin, then stuff the garlic and bay leaves into the cavity. Rub the outside of the chicken with butter, then squeeze over the lemon juice and add the lemon halves to the inside of the chicken. Season inside and out, then cook for 20 min.
2. Turn the oven down to 190 °C / 170 °C fan / gas 5 and cook for a further 45 min, or until the chicken juices run clear when you pierce the thigh with a knife. Leave to rest for 15 min before carving.

### 4. Suzy Karadsheh — Easy Oven Roasted Chicken (Egyptian / Mediterranean tradition)
**Source**: Suzy Karadsheh (Egyptian-American food writer), The Mediterranean Dish; https://www.themediterraneandish.com/easy-oven-roasted-whole-chicken/ (fetched 2026-04-25). *Egyptian-inspired whole roast chicken: a garlic-lemon-herb paste (fresh garlic, lemon zest, olive oil, dried herbs) is rubbed under the skin, on the skin, and inside the cavity; optionally air-dried 24 hours for extra-crispy skin; roasted at 375 °F (190 °C) for 80 minutes. The garlic-lemon-olive oil base reflects the flavour profile common across Egyptian, Lebanese, and Levantine roasted poultry.*
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ optional 24 hr dry brine) · **Cook**: 80 min

**Temperature guidance (from recipe)**: Oven at **375 °F (190 °C)**; roast for **1 hour and 20 minutes**, or until an instant-read thermometer in the thickest part of the breast reads **165 °F (74 °C)**. (Note: thigh should also read ≥165 °F — probe placement in the thigh gives a more conservative safety reading for whole birds.)

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole chicken (~1.8 kg / 4 lb)

**Rub**
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- Zest of 1 lemon + juice of ¼ lemon
- 1 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1½ Tbsp Italian seasoning (or: 1 tsp each dried thyme, oregano, basil + ½ tsp rosemary)
- 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper

**Final season before baking**
- 1 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp Italian seasoning (or herb blend as above)

**Cavity stuffing**
- 1 small onion, peeled and quartered
- Remaining quartered lemon pieces

**Method**
1. Mash the minced garlic and salt together on a cutting board to form a paste. Transfer to a small bowl; stir in lemon juice, lemon zest, olive oil, herb seasoning, and black pepper.
2. Pat the chicken dry inside and out with paper towels — remove as much surface moisture as possible for golden skin.
3. Loosen the skin from the breast and legs by inserting fingers gently between skin and flesh. Rub one-third of the herb paste on the flesh under the skin, one-third on the exterior, and one-third inside the cavity.
4. *Optional (recommended)*: Truss the chicken by crossing kitchen twine over the breast and tying the legs together. Tuck wings under the back.
5. *Optional 24-hour air dry*: Place the chicken uncovered on a rack in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. This concentrates flavour and gives crispier skin.
6. Preheat oven to **375 °F (190 °C)**.
7. Rub the exterior with 1 Tbsp olive oil; sprinkle with ½ tsp salt and remaining herb seasoning. Stuff the cavity with the quartered onion and lemon.
8. Place in a cast-iron skillet or ovenproof dish. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest part of the thigh, away from the bone.
9. Roast for approximately **80 minutes** until probe reads **165 °F (74 °C)** in the breast. If the thigh probe hasn't reached 165 °F, continue roasting.
10. Rest **15 minutes** before carving. Skim excess fat from pan juices; serve as a jus.
*Final resting temperature: 165 °F (74 °C) breast, 165–170 °F (74–77 °C) thigh.*

## Practical tips

### Breast-down vs. breast-up starting position
- **Breast-down start** (Jessica Gavin method): the thigh and leg are exposed to direct pan heat for the first half of cooking, allowing dark meat to get a head start. The breast is shielded by the backbone and the roasting pan edges. Result: breast and thigh finish closer together in temperature.
- **Breast-up with foil tent** (Natasha's Kitchen method): the foil slows breast cooking while the thigh cooks more freely. Good for lower-and-slower cooking.
- Both methods are valid — choose based on your oven and your confidence flipping a hot bird.

### Resting is non-negotiable
- A 20-minute rest after roasting allows the temperature to equalise throughout the bird.
- Juices that retreated to the centre during cooking redistribute into the outer meat during resting.
- Cutting immediately after pulling from the oven sends a flood of juice onto the cutting board instead of into your mouth.
- Rest uncovered (or very loosely tented) — a tightly sealed foil tent traps steam and softens the skin.

### Trussing benefits
- Trussing holds the legs close to the body, preventing them from overcooking (the thighs cook slower and the legs faster; spreading the legs exposes them to more direct heat).
- A trussed bird also has a more compact shape that fits better in smaller ovens and roasting pans.
- If you don't have twine, tuck the wing tips behind the back and cross-tuck the drumstick ends.

### Using a probe thermometer for whole chicken
- Insert probe into the thigh (the coldest part) — the thigh will always finish last.
- Avoid touching the thigh bone; the bone conducts heat at a different rate and gives a falsely elevated reading.
- When the thigh reads 163 °F (73 °C), pull and rest — carryover brings it to 165–170 °F during the 20-minute rest.
