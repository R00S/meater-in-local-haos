# Duck Leg Confit × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

⚠️ **STATUS: COLLECTION IN PROGRESS** — 2 of 4–6 required source recipes collected (both Group B). Needs Group A, C, and D sources before the Temperature consensus and Data applied sections can be written.

## Cut profile

Duck leg confit cooked in the oven is the definitive application of the confit method: bone-in, skin-on duck legs are first dry-cured in salt, herbs, and spices for 12–24 hours to season the meat deeply, then fully submerged in rendered duck fat in a close-fitting roasting pan and slow-cooked at a low oven temperature (85–100 °C / 185–210 °F) for 3–8 hours. The long low-temperature fat bath converts collagen to gelatin, renders intramuscular fat, and produces meltingly tender meat without drying — the fat acts as both moisture barrier and flavour carrier. After the confit phase the duck is removed from the fat and returned to a very hot oven (220–240 °C / 430–465 °F) on a rack to crisp the skin. The MEATER probe must be inserted before the fat bath phase begins, from the thigh end into the centre of the thigh muscle.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nagi Maehashi (RecipeTin Eats) — Duck Confit / Confit de Canard (French bistro tradition, Australian adaptation, Culinary Group B)
**Source**: Nagi Maehashi (with Chef Jean-Baptiste Alexandre), recipetineats.com/duck-confit/  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12–24 hr cure + 20 min · **Cook**: 8 hr + 40 min crisping · **Rest**: none needed (meat already cooked)

**Ingredients**
- 4 duck legs (bone-in, skin-on), approximately 250 g / 8 oz each
- 750 ml / 25 oz rendered duck fat (enough to submerge legs)

*Dry cure*:
- 4 tsp cooking/kosher salt
- ⅓ cup fresh thyme sprigs
- ⅓ cup fresh sage sprigs
- ⅓ cup fresh rosemary sprigs
- ⅓ cup fresh oregano sprigs
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed skin-on
- 1 eschalot (French shallot), peeled and sliced 7 mm thick
- 1 tbsp black peppercorns
- 1 tbsp coriander seeds
- 8 star anise
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 tbsp juniper berries
- 8 cloves

**Method**
1. Place duck legs and all dry-cure ingredients in a large bowl; toss thoroughly with hands to coat. Transfer to a ceramic or glass dish (legs in a single layer). Cover and refrigerate 12–24 hours.
2. Remove from cure, rinse thoroughly under cold water to remove all salt and herb particles, and pat completely dry with paper towels.
3. Place duck legs snugly in a small metal roasting pan with high sides (approximately 27 × 21 cm / 10.5 × 8 inches) in a single layer — they should fit tightly. **Insert the MEATER probe from the thick cut end of the thigh, pushing the tip to the geometric centre of the thigh muscle, clear of the femur.**
4. Pour duck fat over legs until virtually fully submerged. Place pan on stove over medium-high heat until fat is bubbling energetically across the whole surface.
5. Turn off stove. Cover duck with baking/parchment paper (cut to fit flat on the fat surface). Cover tightly with double-layer foil.
6. Transfer carefully to oven preheated to **100 °C / 210 °F (80 °C fan)**. Slow-cook for **8 hours** (overnight is typical).
7. Remove from oven. The meat should be ultra-tender and "fall apart at a touch" when tested with a fork on the exposed flesh side, or the leg bends easily at the joint under gentle pressure.
8. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).** At this internal temperature the collagen has converted to gelatin and the meat yields easily to a fork. The recipe does not state an explicit internal temperature; this range is derived from the described doneness criterion ("fall apart at a touch") in the context of the 100 °C / 210 °F confit environment where the fat temperature stabilises at approximately 85–88 °C at this oven setting.
9. Remove duck from fat; pat dry. Place on a rack set over a roasting pan containing 2.5 cm / 1 inch of boiling water, skin side up. Roast at **240 °C / 465 °F (220 °C fan)** for **40 minutes** until skin is deep golden and crispy.
10. Serve immediately (skin loses crispiness as it sits). Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (meltingly tender meat, crackling skin, perfumed by herbs and star anise).

### 2. Bon Appétit Test Kitchen — Duck Confit (French-American bistro tradition, Culinary Group B)
**Source**: Bon Appétit Test Kitchen, bonappetit.com/recipe/duck-confit  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 24 hr cure + 15 min · **Cook**: 3 hr 30 min + 15 min crisping · **Rest**: none needed

**Ingredients**
- 4 duck legs (bone-in, skin-on)
- Duck fat (rendered from trimmed duck skin/trimmings + additional fat as needed)
- 1 cup water (for rendering fat)

*Spice rub / cure* (exact quantities in recipe card; below are approximate):
- Whole spices (ground fine): black peppercorns, coriander, other aromatics
- Coarse salt
- Garlic cloves

**Method**
1. Finely grind the first four ingredients (whole spices) in a spice mill. Trim excess fat from duck legs and reserve trimmings in a covered bowl in the refrigerator.
2. Rub salt mixture all over duck legs. Layer legs and garlic cloves in a large resealable plastic bag. Seal and refrigerate for 24 hours.
3. Preheat oven to **325 °F / 163 °C**.
4. Simmer duck fat, duck trimmings, and 1 cup water in a large saucepan over medium-low heat until water evaporates and simmering slows, about 30 minutes (fat is rendered and clarified).
5. Thoroughly rinse salt mixture from duck and discard garlic; pat completely dry.
6. Place duck in a large deep roasting pan. **Insert the MEATER probe from the thick cut end of the thigh into the centre of the thigh muscle, clear of the femur.** Pour hot rendered fat over duck legs; cover with foil.
7. Place in oven. Cook until meat is tender and falling off the bone, about **3 hours 30 minutes**.
8. **Pull at 88–90 °C (190–194 °F).** The recipe's criterion "tender and falling off bone" corresponds to full collagen-to-gelatin conversion at this temperature range. At 325 °F / 163 °C oven temperature the fat bath will stabilise above 100 °C, driving the meat to a higher end of the confit range faster than the low-temperature method above.
9. Uncover; cool slightly. The duck may be served immediately or chilled covered in fat for up to 1 month.
10. To serve from cold: preheat oven to 400 °F / 200 °C; scrape fat from duck; cook skin side down in a heavy skillet over high heat for 1 minute, then transfer skin side down to a rimmed baking sheet; roast 15 minutes until skin is crisp. Final serving temperature: **88–90 °C / 190–194 °F** (fall-off-the-bone tender, classic French bistro presentation).
