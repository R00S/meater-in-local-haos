## Cut profile

Goose legs are large, tough, highly flavoured cuts rich in connective tissue and intramuscular fat. Braising — browning the legs in fat, then cooking them submerged or partially submerged in aromatic liquid at low heat for 2–3 hours — is the optimal method for transforming this tough cut into fork-tender, fall-off-the-bone meat. The collagen-rich connective tissue converts to gelatin above 160 °F, but the most tender, succulent results come at 175–185 °F (79–85 °C) after extended braising. USDA minimum for poultry is 165 °F. Unlike duck confit, braising adds moisture; the braising liquid becomes a rich, gelatin-laden sauce.

Target internal temperature: **175–185 °F (79–85 °C)** — well above USDA minimum; extended time at this temperature melts collagen and produces best texture. Minimum safe: 165 °F (74 °C).

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Slow-Roast Duck (low-and-slow fat-rendering principle adapted for goose leg braise)
**Source**: BBC Good Food editorial team, BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/slow-roast-duck (fetched 2025-07-15).
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2.5–3 hr · **Rest**: 10 min

> **Note**: BBC source is whole slow-roast duck at very low temperature for 13 hours. The philosophy —
> low heat, covered, allowing time to break down connective tissue and render fat — applies directly
> to braised goose leg. Adapted here as a braise rather than a dry roast, using stock and aromatics.

**Temperature guidance**:
- Brown legs: high heat stovetop
- Braise oven temp: 325 °F (163 °C)
- Internal target: 175–185 °F (79–85 °C)
- Time: 2.5–3 hours braising
- Final: uncover last 20 min to crisp skin

**Ingredients**
- 4 goose legs (leg and drumstick attached, approximately 12–16 oz each)
- 2 tbsp goose fat or neutral oil (for browning)
- 2 tsp sea salt, 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp ground cumin or juniper berries, crushed
- 2 onions, roughly chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 2 carrots, chopped
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 2 bay leaves, 4 sprigs thyme
- 250 ml (1 cup) dry white or red wine
- 500 ml (2 cups) chicken or duck stock
- 1 tbsp tomato paste

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 325 °F (163 °C). Pat goose legs completely dry; season generously with salt, pepper, cumin.
2. Heat goose fat or oil in a large, heavy oven-safe pot (Dutch oven) over high heat. Brown legs skin-side down 4–5 minutes until deep golden. Flip; brown flesh side 2–3 minutes. Work in batches if needed. Remove legs; set aside.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Sauté onions, carrots, celery in remaining fat 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic, tomato paste; cook 1 minute.
4. Add wine; scrape up any browned bits. Simmer 3 minutes to reduce slightly.
5. Add stock, bay leaves, thyme. Bring to a simmer.
6. Nestle goose legs into pot, skin-side up. The liquid should come ¾ up the sides but not submerge skin.
7. Cover with tight-fitting lid. Transfer to 325 °F oven. Braise 2.5–3 hours.
8. At 2-hour mark, check internal temperature. Target: 175–185 °F (79–85 °C). If not yet there, continue covered.
9. Remove lid for final 20 minutes to allow skin to brown and crisp slightly.
10. Remove legs; rest 10 minutes. Strain braising liquid; reduce by half on stovetop for sauce.
11. Serve with dumplings, braised cabbage, or root vegetables.

### 2. Classic Culinary Technique — Braised Goose Legs with Root Vegetables
**Source**: Standard culinary technique for braised game and waterfowl; general knowledge synthesis.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2.5–3 hr · **Rest**: 10 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Stovetop browning: high heat, 5–6 min per side
- Braise oven: 325 °F (163 °C)
- Internal target: 175–185 °F (79–85 °C) for collagen breakdown
- Time: 2.5–3 hours in oven

**Ingredients**
- 4 goose legs, trimmed of excess fat
- Salt, black pepper, fresh thyme
- 2 tbsp olive oil or duck fat
- 1 large onion, diced; 2 carrots, diced; 3 garlic cloves
- 200 ml red wine; 600 ml game or chicken stock
- 1 orange, zested and juiced; 1 tbsp honey; 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp smoked paprika

**Method**
1. Season goose legs generously with salt, pepper, thyme 1–2 hours ahead (or overnight in refrigerator).
2. Preheat oven to 325 °F (163 °C). Heat fat in Dutch oven over high heat.
3. Brown legs all over in batches, 4–5 min skin-side; set aside.
4. Sauté aromatics in same pot 5 min. Add paprika, orange zest; cook 1 min. Deglaze with wine; reduce 2 min. Add stock, orange juice, honey, bay.
5. Return legs to pot, skin-side up. Cover tightly.
6. Braise in oven 2.5–3 hours until internal temp 175–185 °F and meat pulls from bone easily.
7. Uncover last 20–30 min for skin to crisp.
8. Remove legs; strain and reduce sauce; serve over polenta, potato, or cabbage.

## Notes

- Braising produces the best result for goose leg because it keeps the meat moist while breaking down connective tissue.
- Do not rush — pulling at 165 °F (USDA minimum) produces acceptable but chewy results. 175–185 °F is the target for silky, tender texture.
- Braising liquid reduced to a sauce is exceptional — the gelatin from the collagen makes it rich and glossy.
- Goose legs are larger than duck legs; recipes sized for duck may need 30–45 minutes more time.
