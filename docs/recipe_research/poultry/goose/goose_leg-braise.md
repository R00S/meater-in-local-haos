# Goose Leg × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Goose leg (the entire drumstick-and-thigh complex, often a single 350–500 g piece) is the dark-meat, hardest-working part of the bird — extensively used connective tissue, abundant intramuscular fat, and a thick fat-skin layer. Like duck legs, goose legs are at their best after long, gentle cooking that fully renders the fat and converts the collagen to gelatin. The classic preparation is a confit (slow-poached in fat) but a more practical home preparation is a moist braise in stock with aromatics — same temperature target, same texture transformation, with the bonus of a usable braising liquid that becomes the sauce. Pull is timed by texture rather than carryover (which is zero in a braise): the meat must be falling-off-the-bone tender when probed.

## Source recipes

### 1. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Slow-Braised Goose Legs with Cider & Apples (British River Cottage tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *The River Cottage Meat Book* (Hodder & Stoughton, 2004), pp. 396–398; rivercottage.net  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goose legs, 400 g each, skin on
- 30 g flaked sea salt for dry brine (overnight)
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml goose fat or sunflower oil
- 2 large onions, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 4 bay leaves
- 720 ml dry cider
- 480 ml chicken stock
- 2 medium Bramley apples, peeled and cut in 8 wedges (added in last 30 minutes)

**Method**
1. The day before: rub salt and pepper into the legs; refrigerate uncovered on a rack 18 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat legs dry. Heat goose fat in a heavy oven-proof Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear legs skin-side-down 6–8 minutes until deep mahogany; flip; sear 2 minutes; remove.
3. Add onions; sauté 8 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute. Add thyme and bay.
4. Pour in cider; reduce by half (8 minutes). Add stock.
5. Return legs skin-side-up; nestle three-quarters submerged.
6. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one thigh into the centre of the muscle, away from the bone.
7. Cover; transfer to oven. Braise 2 hours; add apples; continue 30 minutes.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Fearnley-Whittingstall's test: the meat starts to release from the bone, no resistance to the fork.
9. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Lift legs; reduce strained liquid to a glossy sauce. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (cider-deep, apple-warmed, falling-tender).

### 2. Magnus Nilsson — Långbräserade Gåsklubbor i Mörkt Öl (Swedish/Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015), pp. 298–300  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goose legs (gåsklubbor), 400 g each, skin on
- 30 g flaked sea salt for dry brine (overnight)
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground kryddpeppar (allspice)
- 30 g cultured butter
- 30 ml cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 2 large yellow onions, sliced
- 2 carrots, chunked
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 500 ml mörkt öl (dark Nordic ale)
- 240 ml beef or chicken stock
- 30 g brown sugar
- 30 ml soya sauce
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp whole kryddpeppar
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- **To finish**: 30 g cold cultured butter, 80 g rårörda lingon, 15 g chopped fresh dill

**Method**
1. The day before: rub salt, white pepper and allspice into the legs; refrigerate uncovered on a rack 18 hours.
2. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300 °F). Pat dry. Heat butter and oil in a heavy oven-proof gryta over medium-high. Sear legs skin-side-down 6–8 minutes; flip 2 minutes; remove.
3. Add onions and carrots; sauté 10 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
4. Pour in dark beer; reduce by one-third (5 minutes). Add stock, brown sugar, soya, bay, allspice and thyme.
5. Return legs skin-side-up; nestle three-quarters submerged.
6. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one thigh.
7. Cover; transfer to oven. Braise 2.5 hours.
8. **Pull at 92–94 °C (198–201 °F).** Nilsson: meat slides off the bone with chopstick pressure.
9. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Lift legs; reduce strained liquid; off heat swirl in cold butter. Spoon sauce; top with lingonberries and dill. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (beer-deep, allspice-perfumed, sweet-tart counterpoint).

### 3. Anthony Bourdain — Cuisses d'Oie Braisées au Vin Rouge (French bistro tradition)
**Source**: Anthony Bourdain & José de Meirelles, *Les Halles Cookbook* (Bloomsbury, 2004), pp. 200–202 — the goose-leg variant of his classic *coq au vin* technique  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goose legs, 400 g each, skin on
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 30 ml peanut oil
- 200 g lardons (smoked bacon, diced)
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 carrots, finely chopped
- 6 garlic cloves, smashed
- 30 ml tomato paste
- 720 ml full-bodied red wine (Burgundy)
- 480 ml chicken stock
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme, 2 bay leaves, 6 parsley stalks (bouquet garni)
- 250 g button mushrooms (added in last 30 minutes)
- 12 baby onions, peeled and braised separately, added at the end

**Method**
1. Pat legs dry; season.
2. Heat butter and oil in a heavy oven-proof Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Render lardons until crisp; remove. Sear legs skin-side-down 6–8 minutes; flip 2 minutes; remove.
3. Add onion and carrots; sauté 8 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste; 2 minutes.
4. Pour in wine; reduce by half (10 minutes). Add stock and bouquet garni.
5. Return legs and lardons; nestle three-quarters submerged.
6. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one thigh.
7. Cover; oven 165 °C (325 °F). Braise 2 hours; add mushrooms; continue 30 minutes.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Bourdain: meat falling off the bone, fork-no-resistance.
9. Add baby onions in the last 5 minutes. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Reduce strained liquid to a glossy sauce. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (Burgundy-deep, lardon-savoury, falling-tender).

### 4. Diana Henry — Goose Legs Braised with Pomegranate, Walnuts & Saffron (Persian-inspired British tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *Crazy Water, Pickled Lemons* (Mitchell Beazley, 2002) and her *Telegraph* column at telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink — *fesenjan*-style chapter  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 25 min · **Cook**: 2.5 hr · **Rest**: 15 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 goose legs, 400 g each, skin on
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml olive oil
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground cardamom
- A generous pinch saffron threads, soaked in 60 ml warm water
- 200 g shelled walnuts, finely ground
- 60 ml pomegranate molasses
- 600 ml chicken stock
- 30 g honey
- **To finish**: seeds of 1 pomegranate, 30 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, 60 g toasted walnuts (whole)

**Method**
1. Pat legs dry; season.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy oven-proof Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear legs skin-side-down 6–8 minutes; flip 2 minutes; remove.
3. Add onions; sauté 12 minutes until deeply softened. Add garlic and ginger; 2 minutes. Bloom cumin, coriander and cardamom 30 seconds.
4. Add saffron-water, ground walnuts, pomegranate molasses, stock and honey; whisk to combine.
5. Return legs skin-side-up; nestle three-quarters submerged.
6. Insert MEATER probe through the side of one thigh.
7. Cover; oven 160 °C (320 °F). Braise 2.5 hours.
8. **Pull at 91–94 °C (196–201 °F).** Henry: meat starts to release from the bone, sauce coats the back of a spoon and turns chocolate-walnut brown.
9. Rest 15 minutes loosely covered. Spoon sauce; strew with pomegranate seeds, parsley and toasted walnuts. Serve with rice. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (sweet-tart, walnut-rich, saffron-perfumed).
