## Cut profile

A whole goose (typically 4–6 kg / 9–13 lb) is by far the fattiest of the common roasting birds — a significant proportion of its body weight is subcutaneous fat that must be rendered away during cooking. This sets it apart from chicken and turkey: the cook's main task is not to avoid dryness but to efficiently remove vast quantities of fat while keeping the dark, richly flavoured meat from toughening. Goose legs and thighs need 165–175 °F (74–79 °C) to become tender; the breast is best at a lower temperature but in whole-bird cookery inevitably finishes higher. The USDA minimum for whole goose (like all whole poultry) is **165 °F (74 °C)** measured deep in the thigh without touching bone. Many European recipes roast to 175–185 °F (80–85 °C) in the thigh to ensure the dark meat is fully tender and the fat rendered. Carryover adds 3–5 °C after resting.

A medium goose (4.5 kg / 10 lb) typically takes 2.5–3 hours at 190 °C (375 °F) when started breast-side-up. Fat must be drained from the roasting tin every 30–45 minutes or it will smoke aggressively.

## Source recipes

### 1. Heston Blumenthal — Classic Roast Goose (British tradition)
**Source**: Heston Blumenthal, *Heston at Home* (Bloomsbury, 2011); bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/heston-blumenthal-roast-goose  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 30 min + overnight dry-brine · **Cook**: 2.5–3 hr · **Rest**: 30–40 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp Phase 1 (blast): 230 °C / 210 °C fan (446 °F / 410 °F fan) × 20 min
- Oven temp Phase 2: 190 °C / 170 °C fan (375 °F / 338 °F fan) × 2–2.5 hr
- Internal target (thigh, deepest point): 165 °F (74 °C) minimum; 175 °F (79 °C) for more tender legs
- Rest: 30–40 minutes, loosely tented under foil

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole goose, 4.5–5 kg (10–11 lb), giblets removed, rinsed and dried
- 25 g (5 tsp) flaked sea salt for dry-brine (day before)
- 10 g (2 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 1 orange, quartered
- 1 lemon, quartered
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 3 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 2 large onions, roughly chopped (for the roasting tin base)
- 500 ml water or light chicken stock (for the roasting tin base)

**Method**
1. **Day before**: Thoroughly dry the goose with paper towels inside and out. Rub all over with sea salt (inside cavity included) and refrigerate uncovered overnight. The dry brine firms the skin and draws out excess moisture.
2. Remove from refrigerator 1.5 hours before cooking. Preheat oven to 230 °C (446 °F).
3. Pat the goose dry again. Use a skewer or sharp metal skewer to prick the skin all over — especially the fatty areas of the breast, thighs and rump. Do not pierce the flesh; only the fat layer.
4. Season all over with black pepper. Stuff the cavity loosely with orange, lemon, thyme, rosemary and garlic. Truss the legs with kitchen string.
5. Place the chopped onions and water in the base of a large roasting tin. Set the goose breast-side-up on a roasting rack above the liquid. This catches the fat and prevents it from burning on the hot tin base.
6. Insert the MEATER probe into the thigh at its thickest point, away from the bone.
7. **Phase 1**: Roast at 230 °C for 20 minutes to begin skin browning.
8. **Phase 2**: Reduce to 190 °C. Continue roasting, **draining fat from the tin every 30–40 minutes** (carefully tilt the tin or use a ladle — the fat will be abundant). Save the goose fat in a jar.
9. **Target pull temperature: 160 °F (71 °C)** in the thigh. Remove from oven, tent loosely with foil and rest 30–40 minutes. Carryover will bring the thigh to 165–170 °F (74–77 °C).
10. Total roasting time for a 4.5 kg goose: approximately 2 hr 30 min to 3 hr after Phase 1.
11. Carve and serve immediately. Strain roasting pan juices for gravy.

### 2. Nigel Slater — Slow-Roast Goose with Lemon and Garlic (British tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *Tender Vol. II* (Fourth Estate, 2010); theguardian.com/food  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 3–3.5 hr · **Rest**: 30 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp: 170 °C / 150 °C fan (338 °F / 302 °F fan) throughout — slow and low
- Internal target (thigh): 175 °F (79 °C) — Slater prefers fully tender legs
- Rest: 30 minutes

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole goose, 4–4.5 kg (9–10 lb), giblets removed
- 20 g (4 tsp) coarse sea salt
- 2 lemons, halved
- 1 whole head garlic, halved crosswise
- 6 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 g (½ tsp) ground white pepper
- 500 ml water or light vegetable stock (for the roasting tin)

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 170 °C (338 °F). Remove goose from refrigerator 1 hour before cooking.
2. Pat the goose completely dry. Prick skin all over (especially over the breast) with a metal skewer without piercing the flesh.
3. Season inside the cavity generously with salt. Fill with the halved lemons, garlic, thyme, rosemary and bay. Season the skin all over with remaining salt and white pepper.
4. Place the goose breast-side-up on a rack in a deep roasting tin. Pour water or stock into the tin.
5. Insert the MEATER probe into the thigh, tip away from bone.
6. Roast at 170 °C for 3–3.5 hours, **draining accumulated fat every 45 minutes**. At this lower temperature the fat renders more gradually and the meat stays more tender.
7. **Pull at 170–175 °F (77–79 °C)** in the thigh. At this finish the legs are fully tender and the breast is richly cooked. Rest 30 minutes under a loose foil tent.
8. **Note**: The skin will not be as shatteringly crisp as the high-heat method; Slater accepts this trade-off for tenderness.
9. To crisp the skin at the end: raise oven to 220 °C for the final 15 minutes.

### 3. Signe Johansen — Norwegian Julegraut Goose (Nordic Christmas tradition)
**Source**: Signe Johansen, *Scandikitchen: The Essence of Scandinavian Cooking* (Ryland Peters & Small, 2016); scandicuisine.com  
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 40 min + overnight dry-brine · **Cook**: 3–4 hr · **Rest**: 30 min

**Temperature guidance**:
- Oven temp Phase 1: 200 °C / 180 °C fan (392 °F / 356 °F fan) × 30 min
- Oven temp Phase 2 (stuffed bird): 160 °C / 140 °C fan (320 °F / 284 °F fan) × 2.5–3 hr
- Internal target: 165 °F (74 °C) USDA minimum; 175 °F (79 °C) Scandinavian standard for dark meat tenderness

**Ingredients**
- 1 whole goose, 5 kg (11 lb), giblets reserved
- 20 g coarse salt for dry-brine
- **Stuffing**: 300 g prunes, stoned and halved; 2 Bramley apples, peeled, cored, roughly chopped; 2 large onions, roughly chopped; 5 g caraway seeds; 10 g fresh thyme leaves; 8 fresh sage leaves; 5 g salt; 2 g black pepper
- 500 ml dry red wine + 500 ml goose or chicken stock (for the roasting tin)
- 2 carrots and 2 sticks celery, roughly chopped (for the roasting tin base)

**Method**
1. **Day before**: Salt the goose all over; refrigerate uncovered.
2. Make the stuffing: mix prunes, apples, onion, caraway, thyme, sage, salt and pepper. The fruit moisture keeps the cavity humid during the long cook.
3. Preheat oven to 200 °C. Fill the cavity loosely with stuffing; truss. Prick skin thoroughly.
4. Place the vegetables in a large roasting tin. Set the goose on a rack above them. Pour wine and stock into the tin.
5. Insert MEATER probe deep into one thigh, away from bone.
6. **Phase 1**: Roast at 200 °C for 30 minutes — initial browning and fat rendering.
7. **Phase 2**: Reduce to 160 °C. Roast 2.5–3 hours, draining fat every 45 minutes.
8. **Pull at 165–175 °F (74–79 °C)**. Rest 30 minutes loosely tented with foil.
9. Remove stuffing to a warm bowl; serve alongside. Use the pan drippings and wine to make a rich dark gravy.

## Temperature summary

| Source | Method temp | Weight | Total time | Target internal |
|--------|-------------|--------|------------|-----------------|
| Heston Blumenthal | 230 °C → 190 °C (446 °F → 375 °F) | 4.5–5 kg | ~2.5–3 hr | 160 °F pull → 165 °F rested |
| Nigel Slater (slow) | 170 °C (338 °F) + optional blast | 4–4.5 kg | ~3–3.5 hr | 170–175 °F (77–79 °C) thigh |
| Signe Johansen (Nordic) | 200 °C → 160 °C (392 °F → 320 °F) | ~5 kg | ~3–3.5 hr | 165–175 °F (74–79 °C) thigh |
| USDA minimum | Any | Any | Any | 165 °F (74 °C) |

**Recommended MEATER settings for whole goose**:
- Insert probe deep into the thigh at its thickest point, well away from the bone
- Set alert at **160 °F (71 °C)** → rest 30–40 minutes → carryover brings to 165 °F+
- For the slow-roast method (Slater), set alert at **165 °F (74 °C)** and rest 30 minutes
- **Always drain fat every 30–45 minutes** — goose fat is plentiful and will smoke if left to accumulate and burn

## Notes

- A 4.5 kg / 10 lb whole goose yields approximately 700 ml–1 litre of rendered fat; this fat is the most prized by-product of the roast. Strain and refrigerate in a sealed jar for up to 3 months.
- Score the skin before roasting; unlike chicken, goose skin is thick and fat-laden enough that scoring plus pricking with a skewer is necessary to allow fat to escape.
- Dry-brining overnight is strongly recommended — without it, goose skin tends to be chewy rather than crisp.
- A whole goose fed 6 adequately but the carcass is ideal for making a deeply flavoured stock or adding to a pot of beans.
