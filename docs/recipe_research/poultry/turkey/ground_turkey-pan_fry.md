# Ground Turkey × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan-fried ground turkey is the loose-mince technique applied to the leaner, slightly more delicate American/UK Thanksgiving bird. Ground turkey thigh (the standard for flavour and moisture, 8–14 % fat) yields a juicier, more browned crumble than ground breast (1–4 % fat, prone to dryness). The cooking goal is identical to ground chicken: drive every part of the meat past the food-safety threshold for ground poultry (74 °C / 165 °F) and develop deep Maillard browning. Probe the largest meat clump rather than the pan to confirm the centre passes 74 °C. Carryover is irrelevant — the meat sits at pan temperature until removed. Aromatic-heavy preparations work best because turkey's mild flavour leans on its companions.

## Source recipes

### 1. Diana Henry — Pan-Fried Turkey Mince with Sage, Apple & Cider (British modern tradition)
**Source**: Diana Henry, *A Bird in the Hand* (Mitchell Beazley, 2015); telegraph.co.uk/food-and-drink  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground turkey thigh
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) sunflower oil
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 1 small Cox or Russet apple, peeled and finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 8 fresh sage leaves, sliced
- 60 ml dry cider, to deglaze
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 100 ml double cream, to finish (optional)
- 5 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat oil and butter in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion and apple; sauté 4 minutes. Add garlic and sage; 1 minute.
2. Push the vegetables aside; add the turkey in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump, tip at centre.
3. Cook 6 minutes, breaking apart with a wooden spoon and stirring frequently.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Henry's standard for ground turkey: fully cooked through, the crumble lightly browned and bound with the apple-onion mixture.
5. Off heat, season with salt and pepper. Pour in cider; let it bubble 30 seconds; off heat swirl in cream if using. Sprinkle parsley. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (sage-perfumed, apple-bright, cream-rounded).

### 2. Hugh Acheson — Pan-Fried Turkey Mince with Sweet-Onion Gravy (American Southern tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *A New Turn in the South* (Clarkson Potter, 2011) — turkey variant of his chicken-mince technique; hughacheson.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground turkey thigh
- 30 ml olive oil + 30 g unsalted butter
- 1 medium Vidalia (sweet) onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 8 fresh sage leaves
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, to finish
- 5 g chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil and butter in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; sauté 4 minutes. Add garlic and sage; 1 minute.
2. Add turkey in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Cook 6 minutes, breaking apart and stirring.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Acheson's standard: fully cooked, browned on multiple faces.
5. Off heat, stir in lemon zest, juice, salt and pepper. Sprinkle with Parmesan and parsley. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Serve over polenta. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (sage-perfumed, lemon-bright, cheese-rich).

### 3. Andrea Nguyen — Larb Gai Tay with Ground Turkey (Lao / Northern Thai tradition adaptation)
**Source**: Andrea Nguyen, *Vietnamese Food Any Day* (Ten Speed Press, 2019) — turkey adaptation of her Larb technique; vietworldkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground turkey thigh
- 30 ml neutral oil
- 4 shallots, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 5 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 30 g toasted rice powder (khao khua)
- 30 ml fish sauce
- 30 ml lime juice
- 5 g (1 tsp) palm sugar
- 5 g (1 tsp) Thai chilli flakes (prik bon)
- 30 g sliced mint, 30 g sliced coriander, 4 spring onions
- Iceberg or butter lettuce leaves, to serve

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add shallots; sauté 3 minutes. Add garlic and ginger; 1 minute.
2. Add turkey in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Cook 5–6 minutes, breaking apart.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Nguyen's standard for Larb: fully cooked, juicy, ready for the lime-fish sauce dressing.
5. Off heat, stir in toasted rice powder, fish sauce, lime juice, palm sugar and chilli flakes. Toss with mint, coriander and spring onions. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Serve in lettuce cups. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (lime-bright, fish-sauce funky, herb-scented).

### 4. Tina Nordström — Stekt Kalkonfärs på Lök (Swedish home tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009) — turkey variant of her *kycklingfärs* technique; köket.se  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 9 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g kalkonfärs (ground turkey thigh)
- 30 g smör + 30 ml rapsolja
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper
- 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar (allspice)
- 30 ml Worcestershire-sås
- 100 ml grädde (cream), added at the end
- 15 g chopped fresh dill, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat butter and oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; sauté 5 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Add färs in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Cook 6 minutes, breaking apart and stirring.
4. **Pull at 74–76 °C (165–169 °F).** Nordström's standard: fully cooked through, crumble browned, the Swedish home preparation.
5. Off heat, stir in salt, white pepper, allspice, Worcestershire and cream. Sprinkle dill. Rest 2 minutes off heat. Serve with kokt potatis and rårörda lingon. Final serving temperature: **70–74 °C / 158–165 °F** (allspice-perfumed, cream-rounded).
