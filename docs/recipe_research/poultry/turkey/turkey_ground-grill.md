## Cut profile

Ground turkey patties (turkey burgers) are formed from minced turkey, typically 93/7 lean/fat blend. They are denser and less fatty than beef burgers, meaning they dry out quickly and must be monitored carefully on the grill. Binding additions (breadcrumbs, egg, grated onion, or zucchini) are standard to hold the patty together and retain moisture. Patties should not be pressed on the grill — this expels moisture.

Ground turkey must reach 74°C (165°F) throughout per USDA requirements. Unlike beef burgers which are commonly served at lower internal temperatures, there is no culinary preference to serve ground turkey below the safe minimum. The grill is the most popular method for turkey burgers in North American, Italian, and Mediterranean traditions.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — pull immediately at this temperature to preserve moisture. There is no "optimal above safe" preference for ground turkey — higher temperatures dry the patty.

### 1. Taste of Home Test Kitchen (Kelsey Dimberg) — Turkey Burgers
**Source**: Kelsey Dimberg, Taste of Home; https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/grilled-ground-turkey-burgers/ (fetched 2026-04-27). Recipe attributed to Taste of Home Test Kitchen.
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min
**Culinary tradition**: North American (Group B)

**Temperature guidance**:
- Grill: medium heat
- Time: 5–6 minutes per side (≈ 10–12 min total)
- **Explicit thermometer instruction**: "Begin to take the temperature with an instant-read thermometer after about nine minutes. Remove it from the grill as soon as it hits your target temperature — for turkey, that's 165°F."
- Pull: **165 °F / 74 °C**

**Ingredients**
- 1 large egg, lightly beaten
- ⅔ cup soft whole wheat bread crumbs
- ½ cup finely chopped celery
- ¼ cup finely chopped onion
- 1 tablespoon minced fresh parsley
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon pepper
- 1¼ pounds (≈ 570 g) lean ground turkey
- 6 whole wheat hamburger buns, split

**Method**
1. In a small bowl, mix egg, bread crumbs, celery, onion, parsley, Worcestershire sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper.
2. Add the ground turkey in pieces over the bowl; lightly mix together. Form into 6 patties (about 1.3 cm / ½ inch thick). Press a shallow dimple in the center of each patty.
3. Grease the grill grates. Heat to medium.
4. Cook patties over medium heat, about 5–6 minutes per side.
5. Begin checking temperature with an instant-read thermometer after about 9 minutes total.
6. **Pull at 165 °F (74 °C).** Remove from grill immediately when this temperature is reached.
7. Rest 2 minutes. Final serving temperature: **165 °F / 74 °C** (safe, well done).
8. Serve on toasted whole wheat buns.

### 2. GialloZafferano — Hamburger di Tacchino (Italian Turkey Burger)
**Source**: GialloZafferano (Italy's largest food media website); https://ricette.giallozafferano.it/Hamburger-di-tacchino.html (fetched 2026-04-27). Language: Italian — ingredients and method translated below.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ 15 min rest in fridge) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: — (serve immediately)
**Culinary tradition**: Italian (Group B)
**Technique note**: Grilled on a ridged grill pan (piastra/griglia rovente — hot grill). No explicit temperature; uses time-based doneness. Italian grilling tradition does not typically specify a probe temperature for poultry burgers.

**Ingredients** (translated from Italian)
- 600 g ground turkey (tacchino macinato)
- 4 burger buns (panini per hamburger)
- 480 g eggplant/aubergine (melanzane), sliced ½ cm thick
- 320 g vine tomatoes (pomodori ramati), sliced
- 80 g Sardinian fresh pecorino cheese (Pecorino sardo fresco, dolce), sliced
- 60 g mixed green salad leaves (insalata verde)
- 10 g mustard (senape)
- Fresh rosemary, oregano, and thyme (to taste)
- Fine salt and black pepper (to taste)
- 10 g extra virgin olive oil

**Method**
1. Finely chop the rosemary, oregano, and thyme.
2. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey with mustard and the herb mixture. Season with salt and pepper. Mix well by hand.
3. Cover and rest the mixture in the refrigerator for 15 minutes to firm up.
4. Prepare garnishes: grill sliced eggplant on a very hot, lightly oiled grill pan (a few minutes per side). Wash and dry salad leaves. Slice tomatoes and cheese.
5. Shape the turkey mixture into 4 patties using a round cookie cutter (11 cm diameter) placed on baking parchment. Brush each patty with a little olive oil.
6. Place patties on the hot grill (griglia rovente) and cook 4 minutes per side — do not press.
7. Add pecorino slices to each patty in the final 1–2 minutes; cover briefly to melt the cheese.
8. **Pull when cooked through** — GialloZafferano states no probe temperature; Italian culinary tradition assumes poultry is safe when fully cooked through with no visible pink. Equivalent to USDA **165 °F / 74 °C**.
9. Toast bun halves briefly on the hot grill. Assemble: salad, tomato, eggplant, turkey patty with melted cheese. Serve immediately.

### 3. BBC Good Food Editorial Team — Thai Turkey Burgers
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/thai-turkey-burgers (fetched 2026-04-28). *Recipe from Good Food magazine, June 2006.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Time**: 30 min (including 20 min chilling)
**Culinary tradition**: Thai-inspired (Group A)
**Technique note**: Ground turkey patties seasoned with red Thai curry paste, grilled under a grill or on a barbecue. Thai curry paste provides characteristic Southeast Asian flavour. No probe temperature stated — visual/tactile doneness ("firm, golden and cooked through").

**Ingredients**
- 500 g turkey mince
- 85 g fresh breadcrumbs
- 3 tbsp red Thai curry paste (2 tbsp if using an authentic hot Thai brand)
- ½ bunch spring onions, finely chopped
- 1 egg, beaten
- 1 tbsp sunflower oil
- Mango pickle mayo and spicy mango salsa to serve
- 4 ciabatta rolls, baby gem lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes

**Method**
1. Place mince, breadcrumbs, curry paste, spring onions, and egg in a large bowl. Season lightly.
2. Mix well with hands to combine. Divide into 4 and shape into burgers.
3. Chill for 20 minutes.
4. Brush the burgers with oil.
5. **Grill for 7 minutes each side**, or until firm, golden and cooked through. Alternatively, cook on the barbecue.
6. **Pull when cooked through** — Thai-style turkey patties; "firm, golden and cooked through" corresponds to USDA **165 °F / 74 °C** for ground poultry.
7. Toast the rolls cut-side up under the grill. Serve with lettuce, cucumber, tomato, and mango mayo or salsa.
