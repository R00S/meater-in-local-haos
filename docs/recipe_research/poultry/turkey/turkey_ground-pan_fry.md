## Cut profile

Ground turkey in a skillet (pan_fry) is used in three main forms:
1. **Loose crumble** — the mince is broken apart as it cooks and used as a base for sauces, tacos, hash, or skillet dinners
2. **Formed patties (stovetop burgers / kotlety)** — patties are pan-fried with a lid on for the first side then uncovered for browning
3. **Meatballs / mince dumplings** — rolled balls fried in oil then finished covered or in a sauce

The skillet pan_fry method is used across Eastern European, Mediterranean, and North American cuisines. Ground turkey cooks quickly due to low fat content; loose crumble takes 10–15 minutes at medium heat; patties take 3–5 minutes per side covered plus 1–3 minutes for browning.

**Temperature note**: None of the accessible sources for this leaf explicitly state a probe pull temperature. All use time-based or visual doneness indicators ("no longer pink," "cook through"). The USDA minimum of 74°C (165°F) is the applicable standard for all ground poultry. This limitation is documented in the temperature data table.

Target internal temperature: **165 °F (74 °C)** — USDA minimum for ground poultry.

## Source recipes

### 1. Natasha Kravchuk — Lean Turkey Kotlety with Mushroom Filling
**Source**: Natasha Kravchuk, Natasha's Kitchen; https://natashaskitchen.com/lean-turkey-kotlety-with-mushroom-filling/ (fetched 2026-04-27). Ukrainian-American tradition.
**Serves**: 8 (makes 18–20 kotlety) · **Prep**: 30 min · **Cook**: 30 min · **Rest**: 2 min
**Culinary tradition**: Eastern European (Group D)
**Technique note**: Kotlety are a classic Russian/Ukrainian pan-fried patty made with ground meat, breadcrumbs, and egg. This version uses ground turkey mixed with ground beef. No probe temperature given — time-based method.

**Ingredients**
- 450 g (1 lb) lean ground turkey
- 225 g (½ lb) lean ground beef (9% fat)
- ½ large onion, finely grated (with juice)
- 1 garlic clove, finely grated or pressed
- ½ medium zucchini (≈ ¾ cup, finely grated)
- 1 large egg
- 1 tsp sea salt
- ⅛ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp flour (+ 1 cup for dredging)
- Oil for sautéing (extra light olive oil)

*Mushroom buckwheat filling (stuffed inside kotlety):*
- ¼ large onion, finely chopped
- 225 g (½ lb) mushrooms, sliced
- 1 cup cooked buckwheat (grechka)
- Salt to taste

**Method**
1. Make the mushroom-buckwheat filling: sauté onion in oil 2 min, add mushrooms, sauté 4–5 min until browned, add cooked buckwheat, season with salt. Set aside to cool.
2. Mix the meat: combine ground turkey, ground beef, grated onion (with juice), grated garlic, grated zucchini, egg, salt, pepper, mayonnaise, and 1 tbsp flour in a mixer bowl (or by hand). Mix until cohesive.
3. Set up assembly line: bowl of cold water (to keep hands wet), meat mixture, mushroom filling, shallow bowl of flour.
4. Wet hands. Form a flat patty of meat mixture in one hand, press a well in the center, add 1–1½ tsp filling, seal edges around filling, shape into an oval patty. Dredge lightly in flour.
5. Heat a large non-stick pan over medium heat, add 2 tbsp oil. When hot, add 5 kotlety.
6. Cover and cook 4 minutes undisturbed. Remove cover; cook second side 3 minutes uncovered. Brown on sides 1 minute.
7. **Pull when cooked through** — Natasha's Kitchen: "cook until cooked through." Equivalent to USDA **165 °F / 74 °C** for ground poultry.
8. Rest 2 min covered. Wipe pan between batches; add fresh oil.

### 2. Suzy Karadsheh — Ground Turkey Skillet Dinner with Spinach, Tomatoes, White Beans and Homemade Croutons
**Source**: Suzy Karadsheh, The Mediterranean Dish; https://www.themediterraneandish.com/healthy-ground-turkey-skillet-dinner-recipe/ (fetched 2026-04-27). Egyptian-American Mediterranean tradition.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30 min · **Rest**: —
**Culinary tradition**: Egyptian/Mediterranean (Group C)
**Technique note**: Ground turkey is cooked as a loose crumble in a skillet with vegetables — sautéed with fennel, oregano, and Aleppo pepper, then finished with spinach and beans. No probe temperature.

**Ingredients**
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil (divided)
- 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 450 g (1 lb) lean ground turkey
- 1 tsp ground fennel
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- ½ tsp Aleppo pepper (or red pepper flakes)
- 1 pint grape tomatoes
- 2–3 handfuls baby spinach
- 2 roasted red peppers, roughly chopped
- 1 × 440 g can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- Salt and freshly ground pepper
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 2 cups crusty bread, cut into 1-inch cubes (croutons)

**Method**
1. Make croutons: heat 2 tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add bread cubes, 1 tsp salt, and pepper. Toast, stirring, 3–4 minutes until lightly browned and crisp. Transfer to a plate.
2. Return skillet to stove. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil. Add onion; sauté ≈ 5 minutes until softening.
3. Add garlic, ground turkey, ground fennel, oregano, thyme, Aleppo pepper, ½ tsp salt, and pepper. Break turkey into small bits as it cooks. Add grape tomatoes.
4. Cook 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until turkey is fully cooked through and tomatoes begin to burst.
5. **Pull when cooked through** — recipe states "fully cooked through" (no probe temperature). Equivalent to USDA **165 °F / 74 °C**.
6. Add roasted red peppers, spinach, and beans. Stir until combined and spinach wilts. Add vinegar and croutons. Toss to combine.
7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately.

### 3. Gina Homolka — Turkey Burgers with Zucchini (Stovetop Method)
**Source**: Gina Homolka, Skinnytaste; https://www.skinnytaste.com/turkey-burgers-with-zucchini/ (fetched 2026-04-27). American tradition.
**Serves**: 5 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10 min · **Rest**: 2 min
**Culinary tradition**: North American (Group B)
**Technique note**: Turkey-zucchini patties cooked in a non-stick skillet at high heat, then reduced to low. The recipe offers both stovetop (pan_fry) and grill instructions; stovetop is documented here.

**Ingredients**
- 170 g (6 oz) grated zucchini, moisture squeezed out (yields ≈ 120 g / 4.25 oz after squeezing)
- 450 g (1 lb) 93% lean ground turkey
- ¼ cup seasoned whole wheat breadcrumbs
- 1 garlic clove, grated
- 1 tbsp grated red onion
- 1 tsp kosher salt and fresh black pepper
- Oil spray

**Method**
1. Squeeze all moisture from the grated zucchini using paper towels.
2. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, zucchini, breadcrumbs, garlic, onion, salt, and pepper. Form into 5 equal patties (≈ 115 g / 4 oz each), not too thick so they cook through.
3. Heat a large non-stick skillet over high heat. When hot, lightly spray with oil. Add patties; reduce heat to low immediately.
4. Cook on one side until browned; flip. Flip several times to prevent burning and ensure cooking all the way through.
5. **Pull when cooked through** — Skinnytaste: "no longer pink in the center." Equivalent to USDA **165 °F / 74 °C** for ground poultry.
6. Rest 2 minutes. Serve in buns with desired toppings.

### 4. BBC Good Food Editorial Team — Spiced Turkey Burgers
**Source**: BBC Good Food; https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/spiced-turkey-burgers (fetched 2026-04-28). *Recipe from Good Food magazine, November 2007.*
**Serves**: 4 · **Time**: 20 min
**Culinary tradition**: South Asian spiced / British Indian-inspired (Group A)
**Technique note**: Ground turkey patties seasoned with Madras curry powder and pan-fried at high heat until cooked through. The curry-spiced flavour profile represents Indian-influenced cooking. No probe temperature stated — visual doneness.

**Ingredients**
- 500 g turkey mince
- ½ red onion, grated
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- 2 tsp Madras curry powder
- Handful chopped coriander
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp sunflower oil
- 4 burger buns, mango chutney or lime pickle, salad, to serve

**Method**
1. In a large bowl, mix together turkey mince, grated onion, garlic, Madras curry powder, coriander, and egg yolk with salt and pepper.
2. Combine well with hands, then shape into 4 flat burger patties.
3. Heat oil in a frying pan over a high heat.
4. Cook the burgers for **5 minutes each side**, or until cooked through.
5. Toast the cut sides of the burger buns.
6. **Pull when cooked through** — recipe specifies visual/time doneness; Madras-spiced ground turkey, equivalent to USDA **165 °F / 74 °C**.
7. Serve in toasted buns with mango chutney or lime pickle and salad.
