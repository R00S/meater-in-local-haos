# Turkey Leg × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The turkey leg includes the drumstick (lower leg, below the knee joint) and sometimes the thigh
(attached above the knee). When sold as a "turkey leg" it is almost always the drumstick only.
Turkey leg is dark meat — significantly higher in fat and myoglobin than the breast, making it
far more forgiving in the oven. The leg can be safely roasted to 74–80 °C without drying out,
whereas the breast dries above ~71 °C.

**USDA/EU safe minimum**: 74 °C / 165 °F. Unlike pork and beef, there is no recognised "medium-
rare" for turkey — all turkey must reach at least 74 °C for food safety, and the system correctly
has only a `safe` doneness tier at 74 °C / 165 °F.

**Practical target for turkey leg**: Most sources and chefs prefer 75–82 °C for turkey legs and
thighs because the higher fat content keeps the meat juicy at these temperatures. There is no
quality downside to targeting 79–82 °C for turkey dark meat specifically.

**Culinary Group A gap**: Turkey is not a native bird in South/East/SE Asia. While there are
Filipino, Thai, and Indonesian turkey recipes, none were found on confirmed-reachable Group A
sources. This gap is legitimate.

## Source recipes

### 1. Good Food (provided by Apetit Online) — Roast Turkey Leg with Garlic & Rosemary (Czech tradition)
**Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roast-turkey-leg-with-garlic-rosemary — recipe provided by Apetit Online (apetitonline.cz), Czech food site  
**Serves**: N/A · **Cut used**: Turkey legs (drumsticks)

**Ingredients** (per 2 legs)
- Turkey legs
- Garlic (cut into pieces for inserting under skin)
- Rosemary (needles pushed under skin)
- Oil (brush)
- Salt, pepper

**Method**
1. Cut skin in several places; insert garlic into cuts. Push rosemary needles under skin.
2. Season lightly, brush with oil; marinate in fridge ≥ 30 min or overnight.
3. Heat oven to 200 °C / 180 °C fan. Place in roasting tray, cover with foil.
4. After 10 min reduce to 180 °C / 160 °C fan; cook 30 min.
5. Insert skewer into thickest part near the bone. **If juices are still pink, continue until they run clear.**
6. Raise to 200 °C / 180 °C fan; cook 10 more min.
7. Rest 10 min. Remove thin bones and tendons; cut into portions and serve.

**Notes**: Doneness is visual (clear juices), no probe temperature given. "Juices run clear" for turkey = approximately 74–80 °C internal.

### 2. Good Food magazine — Classic Roast Turkey — thigh doneness test (British tradition)
**Source**: Good Food magazine, December 2006; bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/classic-roast-turkey  
**Serves**: 8 · **Bird**: 4.5–5.6 kg bronze turkey

**Ingredients**
- 4.5–5.6 kg turkey (giblets removed)
- 85 g softened butter; whole nutmeg; 10 rashers streaky bacon
- 1 glass red wine; onion; bay leaves; optional stuffing
- Pigs-in-blankets for serving

**Method (relevant extract)**
1. Oven: 190 °C / 170 °C fan / gas 5.
2. **Calculate cooking time at 20 min per kg + 90 min.**
3. Foil-wrapped until 90 min before end; then open foil and baste several times.
4. **Doneness test**: "Push a skewer into the **thickest part of the thigh** — the juices should run clear. If they are pinkish, cook for 15 mins more, then test again."
5. Rest up to 30 min before carving.

**Notes**: "Thickest part of the thigh" is the doneness probe location for the whole bird, confirming the thigh/leg area is the last to reach safe temperature.

### 3. Köket.se — Kalkon – Så Blir Den Saftig och God (Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Köket.se; koket.se/kalkon-sa-blir-den-saftig-och-god  
**Serves**: 8 · **Bird**: 5 kg turkey

**Ingredients**
- 5 kg turkey
- 2 clementines; 1–2 tbsp butter; carrots; onions; celery
- Herb butter: 200 g butter, rosemary, thyme, lingonberries / cranberries, black pepper, flake salt
- Sauce: whipping cream, water, chicken stock cube, balsamic vinegar, Worcester sauce, soy

**Method**
1. Herb butter under breast skin. Fill cavity with vegetables and clementines; sew closed.
2. Start oven at maximum temperature; when hot, place turkey and reduce to 180 °C.
3. **Allow approximately 35–40 min per kg.** A 4 kg turkey = ~2.5 hours total.
4. **"Stick a meat thermometer where the breast is thickest — the turkey is done when it is approximately 75 °C."**
5. Rest; tilt to drain juices; make cream gravy.

**Notes**: 75 °C (167 °F) probe at thickest part of breast; this equals or slightly exceeds USDA minimum. Dark meat (leg/thigh) typically reads 2–4 °C warmer than breast at same time.

### 4. Immaculate Bites — Roasted Turkey Thighs (West African / Southern Creole tradition)
**Source**: Imma (Immaculate Bites); africanbites.com/perfectly-roasted-turkey-thighs/  
**Serves**: 4 · **Cut used**: Turkey thighs (recipe note: "Turkey legs also work well in this recipe")

**Ingredients**
- Turkey thighs (turkey legs also work)
- Herb butter: butter, garlic, sage, rosemary, thyme, paprika, parsley
- Onions, celery, thyme (roasting bed)

**Method**
1. Season thighs with salt and pepper, slip salt under skin.
2. Make herb butter; rub generously under and over skin. Refrigerate up to 24 hours.
3. Preheat oven to 400 °F (200 °C).
4. Place on vegetable bed; **roast 30 min, then reduce to 325 °F (163 °C).**
5. **Continue roasting until instant-read thermometer registers 165 °F (74 °C) in the thickest part of the thigh** — "an hour or more, depending on the size."
6. Tent with foil if browning too fast.
7. Rest 10–15 min; make gravy from drippings.

**Notes**: Explicitly uses an instant-read thermometer. "Turkey legs also work well in this recipe" — source applies to both turkey_leg and turkey_thigh.

## Temperature consensus

| Source | Tradition | Group | Method | Doneness cue |
|--------|-----------|-------|--------|--------------|
| BBC / Apetit Online | Czech | D | 200°C→180°C→200°C, foil | Clear juices (visual) |
| BBC Good Food | British | B | 190°C, 20 min/kg + 90 min | Clear juices from thigh (visual) |
| Köket.se | Swedish | D | 180°C, 35–40 min/kg | **75 °C** probe at breast |
| Africanbites | West African / S. US Creole | C | 200°C→163°C | **74 °C / 165 °F** probe in thigh |

**Key finding**: Two sources give explicit probe temperatures: Köket.se (75 °C at breast; leg/thigh will be ~77–79 °C) and Africanbites (74 °C at thigh / 165 °F). The system target of **74 °C / 165 °F** matches the USDA minimum and the Africanbites probe target exactly. Köket.se at 75 °C is ~1 °C above minimum, also consistent.

Both visual-only sources (BBC/Apetit and BBC Good Food) use "clear juices" which corresponds to approximately 74–80 °C. No source suggests pulling turkey leg below 74 °C.

**Carryover**: For a whole turkey leg (400–700 g), carryover from oven to resting is 2–3 °C. This is already accounted for in the 74 °C target since USDA 165 °F ≥ 74 °C.

**Preferred target for dark meat**: Many professional cooks target 79–82 °C for turkey legs and thighs because dark meat stays juicy above 74 °C and becomes even more tender at higher temperatures. The current system target (74 °C) is the safe minimum; users who want fall-off-the-bone texture should aim for 79–82 °C.

**Group A gap**: Turkey oven-roasting is not documented in confirmed-reachable South/East/SE Asian sources.

## What makes this method special

Oven roasting turkey legs achieves crispy skin (via the Maillard reaction at 180–200 °C) while
bringing the dark meat to a safe 74–82 °C. Because leg/thigh dark meat contains more myoglobin,
fat, and collagen than breast, it can reach 74–82 °C without drying out — making it far more
forgiving than roasting a whole turkey. Separating the legs from the whole bird and roasting
them as individual pieces is increasingly common because it solves the breast/leg timing
mismatch of whole-bird roasting.

The **start-high, reduce-heat** approach (200°C → 180°C or 400°F → 325°F) used by both Africanbites
and BBC/Apetit ensures good skin crisping while finishing to safe internal temperature.

Four verified oven-roast sources for turkey leg: Czech (Group D, BBC/Apetit), British (Group B,
