## Cut profile
Pan frying onions with slightly more fat and slightly less aggressive heat than a pure
sauté is the method for thick-cut onion rings and wedges that need to cook through
and caramelise without burning — different from the thin-sliced caramelised onion of
the French onion soup tradition. The fat level (more than a sauté) provides a deeper
frying environment that ensures even contact between the onion and the hot surface,
and the moderate heat gives the cook time to develop colour on each face before flipping.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Pan-Fried Caramelized Onion Rings (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-fried-onion-rings-caramelized
- **Method**: 1-cm thick rings; 2 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter over medium heat. Lay flat;
  cook 5–6 min per side; do not stir. Internal ~85–88°C; deeply golden rings.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden-amber on both faces; completely soft throughout;
  slightly caramelised at edges.
- **Why this method works**: The flat-lay single-layer approach creates uniform contact
  with the pan for each ring; medium heat prevents burning the sugars before the centres
  are cooked through.

### 2. Nigella Lawson — Pan-Fried Onion Wedges (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/pan-fried-onion-wedges
- **Method**: Large wedges (root intact); oil over medium heat; 5–7 min per side.
  Internal ~85°C; golden on both faces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden on both flat faces; interior completely soft; root holds
  wedge intact.
- **Why this method works**: Root-intact wedges stay together through the extended pan
  fry at medium heat; the root and flat faces both colour while the interior cooks
  through over the longer medium-heat time.

### 3. Delia Smith — Pan-Fried Onion Slices (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/pan-fried-onion-slices
- **Method**: 2-cm slices; butter and oil; medium heat; 5 min per side undisturbed.
  Internal ~85°C; uniformly golden on both faces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Uniform gold; completely soft; buttery aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The undisturbed approach allows full caramelisation to
  develop on each face; the butter-oil medium raises the effective smoke point while
  adding flavour depth.

### 4. ICA — Panstekta lökar (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/panstekta-lokar
- **Method**: Tjocka lökskivor; olja och smör på medelvärme. 5–6 min per sida utan
  omrörning. ~85°C inuti; gyllenbrun på båda sidorna.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun på båda sidor; mjuk inuti; söt och aromatisk.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend the undisturbed medium-heat
  method for thick-sliced onions; the approach is presented as an upgrade to simply
  adding onions to a pan and stirring constantly.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Pan-Fried Sticky Onions (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/pan-fried-sticky-onions
- **Method**: Wedges; oil and butter; medium heat 8–10 min per side; splash of red wine
  vinegar in last 2 min. Internal ~88°C; sticky and glazed.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Sticky and glazed; deep amber; completely soft and sweet.
- **Why this method works**: Red wine vinegar added at the end creates a sticky sweet-sour
  glaze as the vinegar reduces against the caramelised onion surface; the result is
  a caramelised, glazed onion that works as a steak accompaniment or topping.
