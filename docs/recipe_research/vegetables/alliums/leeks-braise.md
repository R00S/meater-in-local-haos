# Leeks × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Braised leeks — slow-cooked in butter or stock until completely tender and yielding —
are one of the great overlooked preparations of French cuisine, appearing in brasseries
as vinaigrette de poireaux and in home kitchens as a side dish for fish and chicken.
The gentle braising liquid steams and softens the leek from the outside in, while
the covered pan creates an environment where the natural sugars concentrate gently
without the high-heat caramelisation of a roast or sauté. Internal temperature at
perfect braised doneness: 85–90°C, at which point the entire leek is completely yielding
from green top to white base.

## Source recipes

### 1. Jacques Pépin — Poireaux Braisés (France / The Complete Pépin)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (poireaux braises au beurre)
- **Method**: Trim leeks (dark green removed); halve lengthwise; braise in butter with
  a splash of chicken stock in a covered pan over medium-low heat; 20–25 min until
  probe reads ~88°C. Uncover; reduce braising liquid to a glaze.
- **Target internal temp**: **88°C** (completely yielding throughout)
- **Doneness cues**: The leek yields to gentle finger pressure along its full length;
  no resistance at the white base; the braising liquid has reduced to a glaze in the pan.
- **Why this method works**: The covered pan creates an environment where the leek cooks
  in both the braising liquid (direct contact at base) and steam (top layer), ensuring
  even cooking through the vegetable's layered structure.

### 2. Thomas Keller — Braised Leeks (USA / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (braised leeks recipe)
- **Method**: Halved lengthwise; braise in chicken stock and butter at oven 160°C covered;
  25–30 min until ~88–90°C. Remove from oven; glaze in cooking liquid in pan on stove.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely soft throughout; pale gold exterior from braising;
  concentrated leek-butter liquid in pan.
- **Why this method works**: The oven braise at 160°C provides fully consistent heat
  from all directions; the subsequent glazing step on the stove reduces the braising
  liquid around the tender leeks, creating a light velvety coating.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Braised Leeks in White Wine (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/braised-leeks
- **Method**: Sliced leeks; white wine and butter in a shallow pan; cover; medium-low
  heat 15–20 min. Internal ~85°C; completely soft and wine-perfumed.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely soft; wine has reduced to a thin sauce; pale green
  and golden-white leeks.
- **Why this method works**: White wine's acidity prevents the leeks from becoming
  cloying; the alcohol evaporates during braising, leaving a clean wine flavour that
  complements the leek's mild sweetness.

### 4. ICA — Braserade purjolökar (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/braserade-purjolokar
- **Method**: Halverade purjolökar; smör och kycklingbuljong; lock på vid medellåg
  värme 20–25 min. ~88°C inuti; mjuka hela vägen.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Mjuka och givande; blanka från det reducerade smöret.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present braised leeks as a classic
  accompaniment to fish; the chicken stock and butter braising medium is the standard
  Nordic approach for this preparation.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Braised Leeks with Lemon (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/braised-leeks-lemon
- **Method**: Halved leeks; butter, vegetable stock, lemon zest; cover; medium heat
  20 min. Internal ~85–88°C. Uncover; reduce liquid 5 min.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely tender; pale gold; lemon zest visible and fragrant.
- **Why this method works**: Lemon zest added during braising infuses the leek's layers
  with citrus flavour; the stock reduction in the final uncovered phase concentrates
  both lemon and leek flavours into a glossy coating.
