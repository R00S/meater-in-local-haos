# Leeks × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Leeks (*Allium ampeloprasum* var. *porrum*) oven-roasted achieve a caramelised sweetness and charred outer layer that the braised version lacks. At 200–220 °C, the outer layers shrivel and blister while the inner white shaft steams in its own moisture, yielding a dual texture: slightly charred and papery outside, silky and jammy within. Leeks are typically halved lengthwise and roasted cut-side-down (or cut-side-up for more moisture retention) for 25–35 minutes. The internal temperature of a fully roasted leek is 86–92 °C (187–198 °F), where the inner layers are completely soft and sweet. A MEATER probe inserted along the axis into the thickest white base gives the most reliable reading; insert before the oven so the probe is set from the start.

## Source recipes

### 1. Yotam Ottolenghi — Charred Leeks with Salsa Verde (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 8 medium leeks (≈ 900 g after trimming), white and pale-green only, halved lengthwise
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- *Salsa verde:* 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 10 g fresh mint; 10 g fresh tarragon; 1 tsp capers; 1 small garlic clove; ½ tsp Dijon mustard; 3 tbsp olive oil; 1 tbsp lemon juice; salt

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Brush a heavy sheet pan with olive oil.
2. Lay leeks cut-side-down on the pan. Brush the skin sides with more oil; season.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek from the root end, with the tip in the centre of the white shaft.
4. Roast 30–35 minutes until the cut faces are deeply charred and the outer skins are blistered and papery, and the inner layers are completely soft.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Ottolenghi wants the outer layers "almost burnt — the char is intentional and provides the smoky bitterness that balances the sweetness of the interior."
6. Meanwhile, blend salsa verde: pulse all ingredients until coarsely blended; season.
7. Arrange leeks on a platter; spoon salsa verde over. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (charred-sweet, herb-bright).

### 2. Magnus Nilsson — Bakad Purjolök (Nordic / Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 8 medium leeks, outer leaves removed, left whole (not halved — Nilsson roasts them whole for this preparation)
- 3 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- Flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 g cold unsalted butter, cut into thin slices, to serve
- 2 tsp red-wine vinegar, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Trim the leeks but leave them whole. Brush all over with rapeseed oil; season with flaked sea salt and white pepper.
2. Place on a heavy baking tray. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis into the root end of the thickest leek, pushing it to the centre of the white base.
3. Roast 35–40 minutes, turning once at 20 minutes, until the outer leaves are deeply charred and the entire leek is yielding when squeezed gently with tongs.
4. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Nilsson notes the outer charred layers must be peeled back and discarded before serving — they are protection, not food.
5. Peel back and discard the charred outer layer. Place the cleaned, silky-soft leeks on a platter; drizzle with cold rapeseed oil, lay cold butter slices on top (they melt over the hot leeks), and add a few drops of red-wine vinegar. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (silky, Nordic-minimalist, sweet).

### 3. Diana Kennedy — Poro al Horno (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g leeks (4–6 medium), white and pale-green, halved lengthwise and rinsed
- 2 tbsp corn or sunflower oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 fresh serrano chillies, finely sliced (seeds included)
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 100 ml (⅓ cup) chicken or vegetable stock
- 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
- Juice of ½ lime, to finish
- 15 g fresh coriander, roughly chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Brush a large baking dish with oil.
2. Lay the leek halves cut-side-up. Scatter garlic and chillies; drizzle oil; season with salt. Add stock and sprinkle oregano over.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek half, with the tip at the white base centre.
4. Roast 25–30 minutes until the leeks are soft and lightly caramelised on the exposed cut faces, and the stock has reduced to a sticky glaze.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** The leeks should be completely tender; squeeze lime juice over and scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (chilli-bright, fragrant, Mexican character).

### 4. Deborah Madison — Roasted Leeks with Breadcrumbs and Lemon (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g leeks (4–5 medium), white and pale-green, halved lengthwise, thoroughly rinsed
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (divided)
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 30 g (2 tbsp) dry breadcrumbs (panko or homemade)
- Zest of 1 large lemon
- 20 g Pecorino Romano or Parmesan, finely grated
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice, to finish
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Brush a sheet pan with 1 tbsp olive oil.
2. Lay the leek halves cut-side-up. Drizzle with 2 tbsp olive oil; season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Insert the MEATER probe along the axis of the thickest leek, tip in the white base.
4. Roast 20–25 minutes until nearly tender. Mix breadcrumbs with lemon zest and grated cheese; scatter evenly over the cut faces. Return to oven for 8–10 more minutes until the breadcrumbs are golden and the leeks are completely soft.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Finish with lemon juice and parsley. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (golden-crumbed, lemon-bright, tender-sweet).
