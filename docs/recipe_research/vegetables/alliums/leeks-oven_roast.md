# Leeks × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Oven-roasted leeks develop a character that neither braising nor sautéing can produce:
the outer layers char and crisp into dark papery ribbons, the inner layers soften and
caramelise, and the cut ends develop golden-brown Maillard crust. The contrast between
charred exterior and sweet, yielding interior is the defining quality of roasted leeks.
Internal temperature at doneness: around 88–93°C at the thickest white base, at which
point the leek is completely soft throughout and the outer layers are beginning to char.

## Source recipes

### 1. Ottolenghi — Charred Leeks with Vinaigrette (UK / Plenty More)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Halved leeks; brush with oil; oven 220°C for 25–35 min or until deeply
  charred on top and completely soft throughout. Internal ~90°C at thick white base.
  Dress with sherry vinegar vinaigrette.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Outer layers deeply charred and slightly crispy; inner layers
  caramelised and golden; completely tender at the base.
- **Why this method works**: Charring the leek's outer layers deliberately is the
  technique Ottolenghi built his leek preparations around; the char provides a bitter-
  smoky counterpoint to the sweet caramelised inner layers.

### 2. Jamie Oliver — Roasted Leeks (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/roasted-leeks
- **Method**: Whole leeks cut in half; drizzle oil; oven 200°C for 30–35 min.
  Internal ~88°C; outer layers beginning to colour; interior completely soft.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Outer leaves golden-brown; white base completely yielding; leek
  has reduced in diameter as it softens.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's approach uses whole halved leeks which hold their
  structural integrity better than sliced; the 200°C temperature gives good browning
  of the outer leaves while the interior cooks through.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Leeks with Thyme (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-leeks-thyme
- **Method**: Halved lengthwise; drizzle with oil and scatter thyme; oven 200°C for
  25–30 min. Internal ~88°C; caramelised cut surfaces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut faces golden-caramelised; thyme crispy; leek completely soft.
- **Why this method works**: Thyme's resinous oils infuse the caramelised cut surface
  of the leek during roasting; cutting the leek lengthwise (rather than crosswise)
  exposes the most surface area to the oven heat.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostade purjolökar (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-purjolokar
- **Method**: Halverade purjolökar; olja och salt; ugn 200°C i 25–30 min.
  ~88–90°C inuti; ytterbladen karamelliserade eller kolnade.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Ytterblad kolnade eller gyllena; inre delar helt mjuka.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present oven-roasted leeks as a
  Nordic autumn side dish; the 200°C temperature and 25–30 min window suits the size
  of standard Swedish supermarket leeks.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Leeks with Parmesan (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-leeks-parmesan
- **Method**: Halved leeks; drizzle oil; scatter parmesan over cut surface; oven
  200°C fan for 20–25 min. Internal ~85–88°C; parmesan golden-crispy, leeks tender.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Parmesan golden and crispy on the cut surface; outer leaves
  beginning to char; completely tender at base.
- **Why this method works**: Parmesan placed on the cut surface caramelises at the same
  rate as the leek's own natural sugars, creating a golden, savoury crust that adds
  both flavour and texture to the finished dish.
