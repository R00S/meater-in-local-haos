## Cut profile
Sautéed leeks — sliced into rounds or half-moons and cooked in butter until completely
soft and beginning to caramelise at the edges — are the most versatile allium preparation
in this collection. They serve as the aromatic base for dozens of preparations: the start
of a soup, the filling for a tart, the bed for a piece of fish. The key is patience at
moderate heat (10–15 minutes for full softening) and generous butter. Internal temperature
at doneness: 82–85°C — the leeks are completely soft but still holding their shape,
with some golden caramelisation at the edges of the thicker pieces.

## Source recipes

### 1. Thomas Keller — Sautéed Leeks (USA / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (sautéed leek technique)
- **Method**: Sliced 5 mm rings; large pan; clarified butter over medium heat; 12–15 min,
  stirring every 2–3 min. Add a splash of cream at end; reduce. Internal ~83°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**83°C** (completely soft, edges beginning to colour)
- **Doneness cues**: All rings completely soft; edges beginning to golden; the cream
  has thickened around the leek rings; sweet aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Clarified butter allows the medium heat to continue without
  burning during the 12–15 min cook; the cream addition in the last 2 min enriches the
  pan juices into a light sauce that coats the leeks.

### 2. Nigella Lawson — Buttery Sautéed Leeks (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/sauteed-leeks
- **Method**: Sliced leeks; generous butter; medium-low heat; 10–15 min. Internal ~82°C.
  "The leeks should be completely soft and glossy, with golden patches here and there."
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Soft; glossy; golden patches; no raw crunch remaining.
- **Why this method works**: Generous butter (more than most recipes call for) lubricates
  all the individual ring layers, ensuring even heat transfer and a rich, silky finish.

### 3. Delia Smith — Leeks in Butter (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/leeks-in-butter
- **Method**: Sliced; butter over low-medium heat; 12–15 min covered. Uncover; season;
  serve. Internal ~82–85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely soft and yielding; pale golden; buttery and fragrant.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's covered approach creates a partial steam environment
  that accelerates the softening; uncovering for the final 2–3 min allows any excess
  moisture to evaporate, concentrating the butter-leek flavour.

### 4. ICA — Stekta purjolökar (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekta-purjolokar
- **Method**: Skivade purjolökar; smör på medelvärme; 10–15 min med regelbunden
  omrörning. ~82–85°C inuti; mjuka och gyllenrösade kanter.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Mjuka ringar; gyllenrosa kanter; smörig och söt doft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipes use sautéed leeks extensively as a base
  for fish sauces and quiches; the 10–15 min time window is consistent across Nordic
  recipes for medium-sliced rings.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Sautéed Leeks with Mustard (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/sauteed-leeks-mustard
- **Method**: Sliced leeks; butter and a little oil; 10–12 min medium heat. Add
  1 tbsp wholegrain mustard; toss to combine. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely soft; golden edges; mustard seed texture visible.
- **Why this method works**: Wholegrain mustard added at the end of cooking adds acid,
  texture, and savoury depth without cooking out; the seeds' crunch contrasts with the
  soft yielding leek rings.
