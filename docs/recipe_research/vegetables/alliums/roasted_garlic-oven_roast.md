# Roasted Garlic × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole garlic heads (*Allium sativum*) oven-roasted are entirely transformed: the sharp, pungent raw allicin flavour mellows over 35–50 minutes at 180–200 °C into a sweet, nutty, spreadable paste. The papery outer skin chars slightly; the cloves themselves absorb their own concentrated sugars and caramelise within their individual sheaths. The classic preparation exposes the cloves by slicing the top off a whole head, drizzling with olive oil, and wrapping in foil or placing cut-side-up in a small dish. Internal clove temperature at full softness is 90–96 °C (194–205 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted from the top of the head, between two central cloves, to the geometric centre of the head.

## Source recipes

### 1. José Andrés — Ajo Asado (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–45 min · **Rest**: 10 min (cooling)

**Ingredients**
- 4 whole garlic heads (bulbs), papery outer skin left on
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (Spanish, good quality)
- ½ tsp flaked sea salt (flor de sal)
- 4 fresh thyme sprigs
- Crusty bread or baguette, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Using a sharp serrated knife, slice approximately 1.5 cm off the top of each garlic head, exposing the tops of all the cloves.
2. Place each head cut-side-up in a small baking dish or individual ramekins. Drizzle 1 tbsp olive oil into the cut top of each head; season with flaked salt. Place a thyme sprig on each.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the top between two central cloves, pushing to the geometric centre of the head.
4. Cover the dish tightly with aluminium foil (or cover each ramekin). Roast 35–40 minutes. Uncover; roast a further 5–8 minutes until the tops of the cloves are golden-brown and the garlic is caramelised.
5. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Andrés describes correct roasted garlic as "honey-coloured, spreadable as butter, and sweet without any pungency."
6. Cool 10 minutes; squeeze the roasted garlic from the cloves onto crusty bread or use as a spread for meats and vegetables. Final serving temperature: **room temperature to warm** (sweet, nutty, caramelised).

### 2. Najmieh Batmanglij — Sir-e Biriyan (Persian oven-roasted garlic)
**Source**: Najmieh Batmanglij, *Food of Life: Ancient Persian and Modern Iranian Cooking and Ceremonies* (Mage Publishers, 2011)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 50–60 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 whole garlic heads, outer skins on, root end intact (not trimmed at the top — Persian style roasts the whole uncut head)
- 2 tbsp olive oil or vegetable oil
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses (rob-e anar), to serve
- Fresh walnuts and flatbread (lavash), to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 180 °C (350 °F). Rub each garlic head with oil and a little salt.
2. Wrap each head individually in two layers of aluminium foil, sealing tightly.
3. Insert the MEATER probe by piercing through the foil into the side of one head between cloves, with the tip in the centre. Re-seal the foil around the probe.
4. Roast 50–60 minutes until completely soft — squeeze the head gently through the foil; it should give with no resistance.
5. **Pull at 92–96 °C (198–205 °F).** Batmanglij's Persian whole-roasted garlic is served as a traditional appetiser — peel each clove at the table and eat with fresh walnuts, lavash and pomegranate molasses.
6. Unwrap; allow to cool 10 minutes. Peel back the skin and squeeze cloves onto a plate. Drizzle with pomegranate molasses. Final serving temperature: **warm to room temperature** (sweet, mellow, Persian appetiser).

### 3. Deborah Madison — Roasted Garlic Heads with Herbs (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 45–50 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 whole garlic heads, top 1 cm sliced off to expose cloves
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 2 tbsp dry sherry or white wine
- Toasted sourdough, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 190 °C (375 °F). Place the garlic heads in a small baking dish, cut-side-up, in a single layer.
2. Drizzle olive oil evenly over the tops; add the sherry or white wine to the bottom of the dish. Tuck rosemary and thyme alongside. Season with salt.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the top between central cloves, tip at the geometric centre of a head.
4. Cover the dish tightly with foil. Roast 40 minutes, then uncover and roast a further 8–10 minutes until the tops are golden.
5. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Madison describes the ideal as cloves that "surrender entirely — they collapse from their skins with the gentlest squeeze." Spread on toasted sourdough. Final serving temperature: **warm** (nutty, sweet, herb-scented).

### 4. Harumi Kurihara — Ninniku no Roast (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 whole garlic heads
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- Cooked short-grain rice, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Slice the top 1 cm off each garlic head; place cut-side-up in a snug baking dish.
2. Whisk sesame oil, soy sauce, mirin and sugar; drizzle 1 tsp of this glaze into each exposed head. Reserve the rest.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the top between two central cloves to the geometric centre.
4. Cover tightly with foil. Roast 30 minutes; uncover; brush with more glaze; roast a further 5–10 minutes until the clove tops are caramelised amber.
5. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Harumi's Japanese-style roasted garlic is a soft, sweet, soy-mirin-glazed result — eat the squeezed-out cloves over steaming rice. Final serving temperature: **warm** (sweet, soy-glazed, Japanese character).
