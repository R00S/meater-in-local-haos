# Roasted Garlic × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Roasting a whole head of garlic is the most elegant single-ingredient transformation
in vegetable cookery: the sharp, pungent allicin compounds that make raw garlic biting
convert in the oven's heat into sweeter, gentler ajoene and allyl sulphide compounds;
the cloves soften from firm and waxy to a buttery, spreadable paste; and the flavour
deepens into something nutty, caramelised, and almost entirely different from its raw
counterpart. The internal temperature at perfect doneness — soft throughout, beginning
to colour on the cut surface, spreadable — is around 96°C. Below 90°C the centre
cloves are still raw; above 99°C the outer cloves begin to burn.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Perfect Roasted Garlic (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/how-to-roast-garlic
- **Method**: Slice 5 mm off the top of a whole head; drizzle with olive oil; wrap in
  foil. Oven 200°C / 400°F for 40–50 min until probe reads ~96°C at the centre clove.
  Cool 10 min; squeeze cloves out.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C** (completely soft, spreadable)
- **Doneness cues**: Cloves are golden-amber and have begun to colour; squeeze and they
  slide out of their skins as a smooth paste; caramel-sweet aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The foil creates a steam environment that cooks the garlic
  from all directions simultaneously; without foil the outer cloves would char before
  the centre is soft.

### 2. Thomas Keller — Ail Confit / Roasted Garlic (USA/France / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (garlic confit and roasted garlic technique)
- **Method**: Whole unpeeled head; drizzle oil; 180°C oven in a small ramekin covered
  with foil; 45–55 min. Probe centre clove ~96°C. Cool and squeeze.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep amber colour on exposed cut tops; cloves slip out of skins
  with gentle pressure; intensely sweet and nutty aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The small covered ramekin creates a concentrated steam
  environment; Keller's lower temperature (180°C vs 200°C) extends the cooking time
  and develops more complex flavour than higher-temperature fast roasting.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Garlic (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-garlic
- **Method**: Whole heads; top sliced off; drizzle oil; wrap in foil. Oven 200°C for
  35–40 min. Internal ~96°C; golden and spreadable.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden tops; squeeze and cloves come out smooth; sweet not sharp.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's version follows the same principle as Kenji's;
  35–40 min at 200°C works for smaller heads; larger heads (7+ cm diameter) need 50 min.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad vitlök (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-vitlok
- **Method**: Topp av vitlökshuvet skärs bort; ringla olivolja; in i folie. Ugn 200°C
  i 35–45 min. ~96°C i innersta klyftan.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbruna toppar; klyftorna lossnar lätt; söta och mjuka.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present roasted garlic as a key
  ingredient for compound butters and spreads; the foil method is universal across
  Nordic recipes for the same reasons as elsewhere.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Garlic (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-garlic
- **Method**: Head(s); top sliced; wrapped in foil with oil; oven 200°C for 35–40 min.
  Probe ~96°C. Serve spread on toast or stirred into mash.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tops golden; cloves squeeze out easily; sweet and caramelised.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food's approach is universal and consistent
  with international technique; the 35–40 min at 200°C is the reliable window
  for standard UK supermarket garlic head sizes.
