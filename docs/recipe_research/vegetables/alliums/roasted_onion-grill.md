# Roasted Onion × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilling onions transforms them with an intensity that no oven or pan can fully
replicate: the direct heat from the grill grate char-stripes the cut surface, the
volatile sulphur compounds react with the wood or gas smoke, and the sugars caramelise
against the bars into a slightly bitter-sweet crust that gives way to a soft, sweet
interior. The temperature target at the thickest point of the thickest slice is around
82–88°C — at this point the interior is sweet and soft while the exterior has the
char and caramelisation unique to grilling.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Onions (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-onions
- **Method**: Thick slices (2–3 cm) or halved; brush with oil; grill over direct
  medium heat 8–12 min per side. Internal ~85°C; char marks and caramelised surface.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Clear char marks; surfaces golden-amber; interior completely
  soft when probed.
- **Why this method works**: Medium heat (not high) is essential for onion slices —
  high heat chars the surface before the 2–3 cm interior has time to soften; medium
  heat allows the interior to cook through before the exterior burns.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Onions (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-onions
- **Method**: Halved through root end (holds together on grill); brush with oil;
  grill 6–8 min per side over direct medium heat. Rest 3 min; internal ~85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks on cut face; outer layers slightly charred; interior
  soft and sweet; root holds slices together.
- **Why this method works**: Cutting through the root rather than the equator keeps
  the onion intact during grilling; the root's connective tissue holds the layers
  together even when the interior is fully soft.

### 3. Grillade.se — Grillade lökar (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillade-lokar
- **Method**: Halverade lökar med oljepensling; direkt grillning på medelvärme
  6–8 min per sida. ~85°C inuti; grillränder och karamellisering.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillränder; mjuk inuti; söt och rökig smak.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides recommend halved onions as an
  accompaniment to grilled meats; the root-end halving technique is specified to
  prevent the onion from falling apart on the grill.

### 4. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Onions (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-onions
- **Method**: Sliced into thick rounds; oiled; grilled on BBQ or griddle pan over
  medium-high heat 5–7 min per side. Internal ~85°C; char marks and sweet interior.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Distinctive char marks; surfaces caramelised; interior tender.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's thick slices prevent the onion from falling through
  grates while maximising the surface area for char development; the griddle pan
  alternative makes this technique indoor-capable.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillede løk (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillede-lok
- **Method**: Halverte løk penslet med olje; middels sterk varme; 6–8 min per side.
  ~82–85°C inuti; grillstriper og karamellisert overflate.
- **Target internal temp**: **82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper; karamellisert; myk og søt inni.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian grilling of onions as a burger and steak
  accompaniment is a national summer tradition; the halved-through-root method
  is universally recommended in Norwegian BBQ guides.
