# Roasted Onion × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole onions (*Allium cepa*) on the grill are a traditional technique in cultures as distant as Argentina (Mallmann's *cebollas enteras a las brasas*), Mexico (grilled cambray onions for *salsa tatemada*), India (whole onion over tandoor coals), and Turkey (*közlenmiş soğan*). The outer layers char and blacken; the inner layers steam in their own moisture and transform to a silky, jam-sweet texture. Grilled whole onions — especially with their skins left on as insulation — require 30–50 minutes of moderate grill heat. Halved onions, placed cut-side-down on the grill grates, develop a deeply caramelised cut face in 15–20 minutes. Pull temperature for a whole grilled onion (fully soft throughout): 88–95 °C (190–203 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted through the side, between outer layers, into the centre of the onion.

## Source recipes

### 1. Francis Mallmann — Cebollas Enteras a las Brasas (Argentine / South American tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann with Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 3 min · **Cook**: 40–50 min over embers · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large white or yellow onions (≈ 250 g each), unpeeled
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, to dress after cooking
- Coarse sea salt (sal gruesa)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) red-wine vinegar
- 15 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped

**Method**
1. Build a wood fire and let it burn to moderate-heat glowing embers. Place the whole unpeeled onions directly on the coals and hot embers — not on a grill grate, but in the coals.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the outer skin into the side of one onion, pushing through 2–3 outer layers to sit in the thickest section of the inner onion mass.
3. Cook 40–50 minutes, turning a quarter turn every 10–12 minutes, until the outer skin is completely blackened and ash-covered. Squeeze gently with tongs — the onion should feel completely yielding with no firm core.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Mallmann describes the internal result as "completely melted — soft sweet jam inside a charred shell."
5. Remove from embers with tongs; rest 10 minutes until cool enough to handle. Peel back and discard the charred skin; cut the sweet interior into rough quarters.
6. Dress with olive oil, vinegar, coarse sea salt and parsley. Final serving temperature: **warm to room temperature** (sweet, charred-smoky, tangy chimichurri-dressed).

### 2. Diana Kennedy — Cebollas Cambray Asadas (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g spring onions or small whole onions (cebollas de cambray), 2–3 cm diameter, trimmed but whole
- 2 tbsp corn or sunflower oil, for brushing
- Fine sea salt
- Lime wedges, to serve
- 1 tsp dried chilli flakes or arbol chilli powder, to dust (optional)
- Fresh coriander, to scatter

**Method**
1. Light a charcoal fire (Kennedy specifies: a wood-charcoal *parrilla*) and let the coals turn white-hot. Brush the onions lightly with oil.
2. Place the whole onions on the grill grate over direct heat.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of one of the largest onions into its centre.
4. Grill 20–25 minutes, turning every 5–6 minutes, until the outer layers are charred and the onions feel completely soft throughout.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Kennedy's grilled cambray onions are the classic accompaniment to Mexican grilled meats and are also blended charred to make *salsa tatemada*. The outer blackened layers are eaten as-is (the charring is integral to the flavour profile).
6. Season with salt; serve with lime wedges. Scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (charred, sweet, slightly smoky).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Charred Onions with Tahini (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium red onions (≈ 200 g each), halved through the equator with skins on
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- *Tahini sauce:* 60 g tahini; 3 tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove grated; 3–4 tbsp cold water; salt to thin
- 1 tsp sumac, to finish
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, to finish
- 30 g pomegranate seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat a charcoal or gas grill to medium-high direct heat (≈ 220–240 °C surface). Clean and oil grates.
2. Brush the cut faces of the onion halves with olive oil; season with salt. Place cut-side-down on the hot grates.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the side skin of one half into the thickest centre.
4. Grill cut-face-down 12–15 minutes, undisturbed, until the cut face is deeply charred and almost black with caramelisation. Turn; grill skin-side-down 10–12 more minutes.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** The onion halves should be completely soft, with a deeply charred cut face and yielding inner layers. Ottolenghi notes the char is "the whole point — it balances the sweetness of the onion."
6. Peel back the outer charred skin layers if desired (or leave on). Mix tahini sauce. Plate onions cut-face-up; drizzle with tahini; dust with sumac; scatter parsley and pomegranate seeds. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (charred-sweet, tahini-creamy).

### 4. Deborah Madison — Grilled Whole Onions with Herb Butter (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium yellow onions (≈ 200 g each), unpeeled
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- *Herb butter:* 40 g softened unsalted butter; 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves; 1 tbsp fresh chives, finely chopped; ½ tsp fine sea salt; 1 tsp lemon juice
- Flaked sea salt, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat a gas or charcoal grill to medium heat (≈ 190 °C grate temperature). Brush the onions with oil.
2. Place the unpeeled onions directly on the grates. Insert the MEATER probe through the skin into the side of one onion, tip in the interior.
3. Grill 25–35 minutes, turning every 8–10 minutes, until the skin is charred on all sides and the onion feels completely soft when pressed.
4. **Pull at 90–94 °C (194–201 °F).** Make a cross cut through the top of each grilled onion (like a baked potato). Push the sides to open up; insert a large knob of herb butter into the cavity while still hot.
5. Let the butter melt into the onion (2 minutes); sprinkle with flaked sea salt. Final serving temperature: **90–94 °C / 194–201 °F** (sweet, herb-butter–melting, smoky).
