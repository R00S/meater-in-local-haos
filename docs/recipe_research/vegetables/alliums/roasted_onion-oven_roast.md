## Cut profile
Roasting transforms the raw onion's sharp pungency into concentrated sweetness and
caramel depth. The sulphur compounds that make raw onions harsh break down during
prolonged oven heat, the sugars (which can be up to 10% of onion dry weight) caramelise
against the hot pan, and the cell structure collapses into a soft, yielding texture.
Internal temperature at optimal doneness — soft throughout with caramelised cut surfaces
and golden-brown exteriors — is around 88–93°C. Below 82°C the centres can still be
raw-tasting; above 99°C the onion becomes jammy and risks burning at the edges.

## Source recipes

### 1. Ottolenghi — Roasted Whole Onions (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Halve onions through root; brush cut face with oil; oven 200°C cut-face-up
  or cut-face-down. 35–45 min until deeply caramelised and soft throughout. Internal ~93°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**93°C** (completely soft and caramelised throughout)
- **Doneness cues**: Cut surface deep amber; outer papery layer charred; a knife passes
  through with no resistance; strong caramel aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Roasting whole halved onions at 200°C allows the time
  needed for full sulphur breakdown and caramelisation; the outer layer chars while
  the inner rings cook in their own steam, creating a multi-textured result.

### 2. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Red Onions (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-red-onions
- **Method**: Red onions; quarter or halve; toss with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, thyme.
  Oven 200°C for 35–45 min. Internal ~90°C; caramelised and glazed.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised glaze from balsamic; outer layers slightly charred;
  soft and yielding when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: Red onions caramelise more spectacularly than white or
  yellow onions due to their higher anthocyanin and sugar content; balsamic vinegar
  creates a lacquer glaze in the last 10 minutes of roasting.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Roasted Onions with Herbs (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/roasted-onions
- **Method**: Yellow onions; peel and halve; toss with oil, rosemary, garlic cloves.
  Oven 200°C for 35–45 min. Internal ~88–93°C; golden and yielding.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–93°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-amber on cut faces; soft; garlic soft and caramelised
  inside its skin; rosemary crispy.
- **Why this method works**: Whole garlic cloves roast at the same rate as onion halves;
  the rosemary's volatile oils infuse the onion's caramelised surface as it chars.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostade lökar (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-lokar
- **Method**: Gula lökar halverade; olja, salt, timjan. Ugn 200°C i 35–40 min.
  ~90°C inuti; gyllenbrun snittyta och mjuk.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbrun; mjuk och söt genomgripande.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend roasted onions as a side
  dish for grilled meats; the 200°C temperature is the standard Nordic home-oven
  approach for caramelised alliums.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Onions with Balsamic (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-onions-balsamic
- **Method**: Halved red onions; toss with oil and balsamic. Oven 200°C fan for 30–35 min.
  Internal ~88°C; caramelised glaze.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Balsamic glaze caramelised to sticky amber; outer leaves slightly
  charred; completely soft throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven at 200°C dries the balsamic faster than a static
  oven, concentrating the glaze in a shorter time; the result is a deeper colour with
  slightly less total cooking time.
