## Cut profile
Air frying corn on the cob produces a caramelised, slightly charred result in 12–15
minutes without any grill or outdoor cooking equipment. The circulating hot air dehydrates
and caramelises the kernel surfaces on all sides simultaneously, producing char spots and
golden-amber colour that the oven cannot easily match. Internal temperature at doneness:
82°C at the cob centre, same as for grilling and boiling. The air fryer's size limits
this to 1–2 cobs per batch, which suits its role as a quick single-serve method.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Corn on the Cob (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-corn-on-the-cob
- **Method**: Husked corn; brush with oil; air fryer 200°C for 12–15 min, turning once
  at halfway. Internal 82°C at cob centre; caramelised kernels.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Kernels golden-amber with dark spots; slightly shrunken; sweet
  caramelised aroma; tender to a fingernail test.
- **Why this method works**: The air fryer's 360° heat produces caramelisation on all
  kernel surfaces simultaneously; the one turn at halfway ensures even exposure.

### 2. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad majs (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/majs
- **Method**: Skalad; pensla med olja; airfryer 200°C i 12–15 min; vänd en gång.
  ~82°C i kolven; karamelliserade kärnor.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbruna kärnor med mörka fläckar; söt och karamelliserad.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides present corn on the cob as one
  of the most popular air fryer recipes; the 200°C temperature is consistent across
  all Nordic recipes for this method.

- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Corn with Chilli Butter (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-corn-chilli-butter
- **Method**: Husked; oil; air fryer 200°C for 12–15 min. Turn once. Internal 82°C.
  Roll in chilli-lime butter immediately.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised kernels; slightly charred tips; butter melts on contact.
- **Why this method works**: Australian food media embraced air fryer corn quickly;
  Donna Hay's chilli-lime butter applied immediately while hot melts into the char
  and kernel gaps; the technique mirrors the Mexican elote street food tradition.
