# Corn on the Cob × Boil — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Boiling corn on the cob is the oldest and most universal cooking method for corn in
the Americas, and when done correctly — in unsalted water (salt toughens the kernel
skin), pulled at exactly the right moment — produces a tender, sweet, juicy kernel
that grilling cannot match for pure clean sweetness. The internal temperature at
perfect boiled doneness: 82°C at the cob centre. Below 79°C the corn starch is not
fully gelatinised and the kernels have a slightly starchy, raw taste. Above 88°C the
kernels begin to toughen from protein contraction.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Perfect Boiled Corn on the Cob (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/how-to-boil-corn-on-the-cob
- **Method**: Husk corn; bring UNSALTED water to a boil (salt toughens the pericarps);
  add corn; return to boil; cook 3–5 min. Pull when probe at cob centre reads 82°C.
  Serve immediately.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C** (at cob centre)
- **Doneness cues**: Kernels are plump, shiny, and give slightly when pressed with a
  fingernail; slightly sweet steam; colour has brightened to deep golden.
- **Why this method works**: The no-salt technique is the most important single note
  for boiled corn; salt denatures the proteins in the kernel pericarp and makes them
  tough; the 3–5 min boil at 82°C is the perfect window for fresh sweet corn.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Boiled Corn on the Cob (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/boiled-corn-on-the-cob
- **Method**: Husked corn in large pot of unsalted boiling water; 3–5 min.
  Probe at cob centre: 82°C. Butter and salt at the table.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Plump, golden kernels; easily pierced with a fingernail; sweet steam.
- **Why this method works**: ATK confirms the no-salt rule; the 3–5 min window captures
  the peak sweetness moment before the sugar begins converting to starch.

### 3. Delia Smith — Boiled Sweetcorn (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/boiled-sweetcorn
- **Method**: Husked; in boiling water (no salt); 4–5 min. Internal ~82°C. Serve with
  butter — "lots of butter" — and sea salt.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender kernels; plump and golden; sweet aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's guidance on no salt in the water is consistent
  with American technique; the 4–5 min is slightly longer than Kenji's minimum to
  account for UK sweetcorn varieties which tend to be slightly less sweet.

### 4. ICA — Kokt majs (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/kokt-majs
- **Method**: Skalad; osaltat kokande vatten; 4–5 min. ~82°C i majskolven.
  Servera med smör och salt vid bordet.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenröda kärnor; mjuka vid nageltest; söt ångdoft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipes specify osaltat vatten (unsalted water)
  consistently; the 4–5 min boil is the standard Nordic recommendation for the
  corn varieties available in Swedish supermarkets.

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Simply Boiled Corn (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/boiled-corn-on-the-cob
- **Method**: Into a pan of unsalted boiling water; 3–4 min maximum. "The sweetness
  starts to fade the moment they go in — don't delay, don't overcook." Internal 82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Bright golden; plump; fingernail test passes.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's emphasis on speed — cooking the corn immediately
  after husking and not leaving it on the heat — reflects the scientific truth that
  corn's sugars begin converting to starch as soon as the cob is broken from the plant;
  freshness and speed are the most important variables for boiled corn.
