# Corn on the Cob × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilled corn on the cob is one of the most iconic summer preparations in North American
and Nordic outdoor cooking: the kernel sugars caramelise and char against the grill grates
(or the husks), the cob's moisture steams the interior while the exterior chars, and the
result — with its blend of sweet, smoky, and slightly bitter char notes — has no equivalent
in boiling or steaming. Internal temperature of the cob kernels at perfect grilled doneness:
82°C throughout (the last spot to reach temperature is the centre of the cob, near the core).
Below 79°C some kernels may still be slightly raw; above 88°C the kernels begin to dry out.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Corn on the Cob (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-corn-on-the-cob
- **Method**: Husked; brush with oil; grill over direct medium-high heat turning every
  3–4 min; 10–12 min total. Internal ~82°C at cob centre. Char marks on all sides.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C** (at cob centre, near core)
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks on all sides; some kernels showing dark char spots;
  steam wisping from the ends; the cob smells sweet and smoky.
- **Why this method works**: Direct medium-high heat for 10–12 min gives char marks
  on 4 sides without drying out the kernels; turning every 3–4 min ensures even
  heat distribution.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Corn on the Cob (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-corn-on-the-cob
- **Method**: Husked; grill over direct high heat; turn every 2 min; 8–10 min total
  for heavily charred version. Internal ~82°C. Serve with compound butter.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Kernels charred and slightly popped on all sides; the cob yields
  slightly when pressed; steam escaping from ends.
- **Why this method works**: High heat for shorter time produces dramatic char marks
  and slightly crunchy kernel tips without drying the interior; turning every 2 min
  (vs 3–4 min) maintains char without burning.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Corn (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-corn
- **Method**: Husked; BBQ over direct heat; turn every 3 min; 10–12 min. Internal ~82°C.
  Roll in flavoured butter immediately.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks on all sides; butter melts immediately on contact.
- **Why this method works**: Rolling in flavoured butter immediately while the cob is
  still hot means the butter melts into the char and the spaces between the kernels;
  doing this while cold would simply coat the surface.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad majs (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-majs
- **Method**: Skalad; grillning på direkt medelhög–hög värme; vänd var 3 min;
  10–12 min totalt. ~82°C nära majskolven. Servera med smör och salt.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillränder runt om; kärnorna börjar kola; smörigt och sött.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides present grilled corn as the
  summer BBQ standard; the 10–12 min direct heat recommendation is consistent with
  all Nordic grilling recipes.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet mais (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-mais
- **Method**: Avskallet; direkte varme, middels høy; snu hvert 3. minutt; 10–12 min.
  ~82°C i midten. Smør og flak salt.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper rundt; noen korn sviskne; søt og røykig aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian summer grilling of corn follows the same technique
  as the international standard; the smør og salt service is universal across all
  Nordic corn-on-the-cob recipes.
