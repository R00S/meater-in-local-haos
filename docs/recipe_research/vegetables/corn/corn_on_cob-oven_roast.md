# Corn on the Cob × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Oven-roasted corn on the cob — husked or in husk — produces a result between grilling
and boiling: more caramelisation than boiling, less char and smoke than grilling. The
in-husk method creates a steamed environment that keeps the kernels moist while the
dry heat of the oven penetrates slowly; the husked method allows caramelisation on
the exposed kernel surfaces. Internal temperature target: 82°C at the cob centre,
same as for boiling and grilling.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Oven-Roasted Corn on the Cob (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/oven-roasted-corn
- **Method**: In husk; place directly on oven rack at 220°C; roast 30 min. Probe through
  husk: 82°C at cob centre. Rest 5 min; peel husks back.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Husks are charred and papery; a probe through the husk reads 82°C;
  the corn inside is tender and sweet.
- **Why this method works**: The husk acts as a steam jacket — the moisture inside the
  husk creates a steam environment that keeps the kernels moist while the outside chars;
  the result is similar to husked boiling but with slight caramelisation from the steam heat.

### 2. Serious Eats — Oven-Roasted Corn (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/oven-roasted-corn
- **Method**: Husked corn on foil-lined tray; brush with oil; oven 220°C for 20–25 min,
  turning once. Internal 82°C; caramelised kernels.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Kernels golden-amber on at least one side; slightly shrunken;
  sweet and concentrated flavour.
- **Why this method works**: Husked corn at 220°C in the oven provides caramelisation
  on the kernel surfaces — something the husk method prevents; the oil coating
  promotes Maillard browning at the same temperature.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Oven-Roasted Corn on the Cob (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/oven-roasted-corn
- **Method**: Husked; rub with butter and herbs; wrap individually in foil; oven 200°C
  for 25–30 min. Internal 82°C. Unwrap to serve.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender and sweet; herbed butter has infused the kernels; steam
  when foil is opened.
- **Why this method works**: Wrapping individually in foil with butter creates the same
  steam jacket as the in-husk method but with the added flavour of herb butter
  infused directly onto the kernel surfaces from the start.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad majs (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-majs
- **Method**: Skalad; pensla med smör och örter; linda i folie; ugn 200°C i 25–30 min.
  ~82°C i kolven.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Mjuk och söt; örtersmöret infunderat i kärnorna; ånga vid öppning.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present foil-wrapped oven corn as an
  indoor alternative to grilling; the butter-and-herbs foil method is the standard
  Nordic approach for the summer season.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Oven-Roasted Corn (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/oven-roasted-corn
- **Method**: In husk; oven 220°C for 25–30 min. Probe through husk at centre: 82°C.
  Peel back husks; roll in chipotle butter.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Husks charred; corn tender inside; steam on opening.
- **Why this method works**: The in-husk method in the oven is the indoor equivalent
  of direct grill heat — the char on the husk gives some smoke-adjacent flavour;
  Jamie's chipotle butter finish adds a hot-smoky note that complements the steamed sweetness.
