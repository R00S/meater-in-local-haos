# Corn On Cob × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Corn on the cob (*Zea mays*) oven-roasted in its husk, or stripped and oven-roasted at high heat, produces a gentler result than live-fire grilling but still concentrates the natural sugars as moisture evaporates. At 200–220 °C, husked corn ears roast on a sheet pan in 25–30 minutes, the exposed kernels caramelising and taking on colour on the outer rows. Corn roasted in its husk achieves a steam-cooking effect — the kernels stay moist and cook evenly to 90–96 °C (194–205 °F) in 30–35 minutes. Pull temperatures: 88–92 °C (190–198 °F) for husked oven-roasted; 90–95 °C (194–203 °F) for in-husk. A MEATER probe is inserted through the kernel rows into the cob core at the mid-point, in any orientation.

## Source recipes

### 1. Diana Kennedy — Elotes Asados al Horno (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min (cooling for handling)

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks on and silks intact (for in-husk roasting)
- *Elote toppings:* 120 ml Mexican crema or soured cream; 60 g cotija cheese (finely grated); chilli powder; 1 lime

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Place the entire ears — husks on — directly on the oven rack.
2. Insert the MEATER probe through the husks and kernels into the cob core at the mid-point (use a firm push to pierce the husk layer; the probe can pass through).
3. Roast 30–35 minutes until the outer husks are dry and beginning to brown, and the probe reads the target temperature.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** The husks trap steam and cook the corn evenly.
5. Let the ears cool 5 minutes until handleable; peel back the husks (which can be used as a handle). Remove silks.
6. Apply crema, cotija, chilli and lime as for grilled elotes. Final serving temperature: **90–95 °C / 194–203 °F** (tender, sweet, creamy-spicy).

### 2. José Andrés — Maíz Asado con Mantequilla Compuesta (Spanish / American tradition)
**Source**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min (husked) · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks and silks removed
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- *Compound butter:* 60 g softened unsalted butter; 1 garlic clove grated; 1 tsp smoked paprika (pimentón de la Vera); 1 tsp flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped; ½ tsp lemon zest; pinch of salt

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Line a baking sheet with foil.
2. Rub the naked corn ears with olive oil; season with salt and pepper. Place on the sheet pan.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob core at the mid-section.
4. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once at 15 minutes, until kernels are golden and caramelised in spots.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** While hot, rub the compound butter over each ear; it will melt into the kernels. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (golden, butter-glazed, paprika-bright).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Tandoori Style Bhutta al Oven (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010); oven adaptation of street-food corn in a tandoor-style preparation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks removed
- 2 tbsp sunflower oil or neutral oil
- *Masala:* 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; ½ tsp ground cumin; ½ tsp amchur (dried mango powder); ½ tsp fine salt; ½ tsp garam masala
- 1 lime, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 210 °C (410 °F). Mix masala spices with oil into a paste. Brush generously over each ear of corn.
2. Place on a foil-lined baking sheet. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob centre.
3. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once at 15 minutes, until the kernels are golden and lightly charred at the tips.
4. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Squeeze lime over each ear. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (spiced, golden, tangy, an oven-friendly version of the Indian street-food classic).

### 4. Magnus Nilsson — Ugnsstekt Majs (Nordic / Swedish tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 ears of corn, husks removed
- 2 tbsp cold-pressed rapeseed oil
- 1 tsp flaked sea salt (Falksalt)
- Freshly ground white pepper
- 40 g cold unsalted butter, in thin slices, to serve
- 1 tbsp finely chopped chives, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Brush each corn ear lightly with rapeseed oil; season with flaked sea salt and white pepper.
2. Place on a foil-lined baking sheet. Insert the MEATER probe through the kernels into the cob centre.
3. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once, until the kernels are golden and concentrated in flavour.
4. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Nilsson's Nordic approach: simple ingredients showcasing the corn's natural sweetness. Lay cold butter slices on top while hot (they melt into the kernels); scatter chives. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (sweet, buttery, Nordic-minimal).
