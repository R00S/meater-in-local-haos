# Cabbage × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Braised cabbage — slow-cooked in fat, stock, or wine with aromatics until completely
tender and slightly caramelised — transforms the raw vegetable's harsh sulphurous notes
into a sweet, savoury depth that no quick-cook method can achieve. The internal temperature
at perfect braised doneness: around 82–88°C at the thickest stem piece, at which point
the entire cabbage has softened to a silky tenderness and the braising liquid has reduced
around it. Braising suits red cabbage (with apple and wine) and green cabbage (with pork
or caraway) in equal measure.

## Source recipes

### 1. Thomas Keller — Braised Red Cabbage (USA / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (red cabbage braise)
- **Method**: Thin-sliced red cabbage; lard or duck fat; brown in wide pan then add
  red wine and apple; cover; 220°C oven or medium stovetop 40–60 min until completely
  soft. Internal ~85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C** (completely soft and yielding)
- **Doneness cues**: All visible liquid absorbed or reduced; cabbage collapsed into
  a soft, shiny mass; deep purple-red colour; sweet-sour aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The fat (lard/duck fat) coats the cabbage strands from
  the start; the wine and apple provide acid and sugar for the long braise; the oven
  heat is even from all sides, reducing the risk of scorching.

### 2. Delia Smith — Braised Red Cabbage (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/braised-red-cabbage-with-apples
- **Method**: Shredded red cabbage with apple, onion, red wine vinegar, sugar, and spices;
  covered casserole in oven at 150°C for 2–2.5 h. Internal ~88°C; very soft and jammy.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely collapsed and tender; deep ruby red; sweet and slightly
  acidic braising liquid.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's very low temperature (150°C) over a very long time
  is the traditional British Christmas approach — the slow heat breaks down the cabbage
  fibres completely and concentrates the sweet-sour flavour without any risk of burning.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Christmas Braised Red Cabbage (UK / Nigella Christmas)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/braised-red-cabbage
- **Method**: Shredded; oil, red wine, sliced apples, star anise; casserole at 150°C
  for 2–2.5 h or stovetop medium-low 1.5 h. Internal ~88°C; soft and fragrant.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Star anise fragrant; cabbage completely soft; purple-red; slightly
  sticky from the reduced wine-apple liquid.
- **Why this method works**: Star anise's aniseed notes complement red cabbage's
  earthiness; Nigella's recipe is designed to be made ahead (it improves overnight),
  making the 88°C target appropriate for both the initial cook and a reheated version.

### 4. ICA — Brässerad rödkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/brasserad-rodkal
- **Method**: Strimlad rödkål med äpplen, rödvin, vinäger och socker. Gryta 150°C
  i 2–2.5 h. ~88°C inuti; silkeslen och sötsyrlig.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Silkeslen; djupt röd; sötsyrlig doft; köttsaften reducerad.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish rödkål is a Christmas tradition inseparable from
  the Swedish julbord; the 150°C slow braise is universal across all Nordic red cabbage
  recipes and the result is designed to be made days in advance.

### 5. Jacques Pépin — Choux Braisés (France / The Complete Pépin)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (braised cabbage, green or savoy)
- **Method**: Quartered green or savoy cabbage; blanch 5 min; drain. Braise in
  stock and butter at medium heat covered 20–30 min. Internal ~82°C; completely tender.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Quarters yield completely to paring knife; no crunch at all; pale
  golden exterior from the stock-butter braise.
- **Why this method works**: The blanch-then-braise is Pépin's classic technique for
  green cabbage; the blanch removes bitterness and starts the cooking; the braise
  finishes it in a rich stock-butter environment that flavours the cabbage throughout.
