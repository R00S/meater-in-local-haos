# Cabbage × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cabbage (*Brassica oleracea* var. *capitata*) braised is one of the most globally distributed vegetable preparations: German Rotkohl, French *chou braisé*, Polish *kapusta duszona*, Indian dum-cooked cabbage, and Chinese red-braised cabbage all transform the pungent, crunchy raw leaves through long, moist cooking into a tender, sweet, and deeply flavourful result. The technique involves long gentle simmering in a small amount of liquid (wine, vinegar, stock, or a combination) with a lid on, allowing the cabbage to stew in its own juices. Pull temperature for a fully braised cabbage wedge or shred: 88–95 °C (190–203 °F) — at this point the cell walls have fully collapsed, the harsh glucosinolates have converted to gentler compounds, and the texture is yielding and silky. A MEATER probe is inserted through the outer layers of a wedge into the thickest part of the core, or into the densest part of the shred pile.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nigel Slater — German-Style Red Cabbage with Apple and Cloves (German tradition)
**Source**: Nigel Slater, *The Kitchen Diaries, Vol. I* (4th Estate / HarperCollins, 2005); Slater explicitly acknowledges this as his interpretation of *Rotkohl*, the German braised red cabbage classic.
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 1 hr 15 min – 1 hr 30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 medium red cabbage (≈ 900 g), quartered, core removed, thinly shredded
- 2 large Bramley apples (≈ 400 g), peeled, cored, and roughly chopped
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 40 g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 60 ml (¼ cup) red wine
- 1 tbsp soft light brown sugar
- 5 whole cloves
- 2 bay leaves
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tsp fine sea salt

**Method**
1. Melt butter in a large, heavy lidded casserole over medium heat. Add onion; cook 5–6 minutes until softened.
2. Add the shredded red cabbage; toss to coat with the butter. Add apple, vinegar, red wine, sugar, cloves, bay, salt and pepper.
3. Stir well, cover, and reduce heat to low. Insert the MEATER probe into the densest mass of cabbage shreds near the centre of the pot.
4. Braise over very low heat 1 hour 15 minutes – 1 hour 30 minutes, stirring every 20 minutes. The cabbage should turn from bright purple to deep ruby-purple as the vinegar and heat work together.
5. **Pull at 90–96 °C (194–205 °F).** Slater notes: "The cabbage is ready when it has given up every trace of rawness and collapsed into a tangled, sweet-sour pile." Remove cloves and bay leaves.
6. Taste and adjust with more vinegar (for brightness) or sugar (for sweetness). Final serving temperature: **90–96 °C / 194–205 °F** warm as a side, or reheat from cold — it improves over two days.

### 2. Julia Child — Chou Braisé (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. II* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1970)
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ blanching) · **Cook**: 1 hr 30 min – 2 hr · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 medium green or Savoy cabbage (≈ 900 g), outer leaves removed, quartered
- 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 1 medium carrot, finely diced
- 1 bouquet garni (bay leaf, thyme, parsley stems)
- 250 ml (1 cup) beef or chicken stock
- 60 ml (¼ cup) dry white wine
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp freshly ground white pepper

**Method**
1. Blanch the cabbage quarters in a large pot of boiling salted water for 5 minutes; drain and press out excess moisture.
2. Melt butter in a heavy, lidded oval casserole over medium heat. Add onion and carrot; cook 5 min until softened. Place the blanched cabbage wedges on top.
3. Add stock, wine, bouquet garni, salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer.
4. Insert the MEATER probe through the outer leaves of a large wedge into the thickest part of the core.
5. Cover tightly and braise in a 165 °C (325 °F) oven (or over very low heat on the stovetop) for 1 hour 30 minutes – 2 hours until the cabbage is completely tender and the braising liquid has reduced.
6. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Child: "The cabbage should offer no resistance to a paring knife inserted into the core."
7. Discard bouquet garni; serve the wedges with their braising liquid spooned over. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (tender, sweet, wine-fragrant, French).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Bandh Gobhi Dum (Indian Slow-Braised Cabbage)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–35 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g green cabbage, outer leaves removed, core removed, shredded
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 2 medium tomatoes, roughly chopped
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide, lidded pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds (10 s); add onion (5–6 min golden); add garlic, ginger (1 min); add spices (30 s); add tomatoes and salt; cook 3 min.
2. Add shredded cabbage; toss well with the masala. Insert the MEATER probe into the densest part of the cabbage mass.
3. Cover tightly; reduce heat to low. Braise 20–25 minutes, stirring every 8 minutes, until the cabbage is completely soft and has absorbed the masala.
4. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Dust with garam masala; garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (spiced, soft, fragrant — serve with dal and rice).

### 4. Fuchsia Dunlop — Hóng Shāo Bǎitài (红烧白菜 — Chinese Red-Braised Cabbage)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Land of Fish and Rice: Recipes from the Culinary Heart of China* (W. W. Norton, 2016)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g napa cabbage (*Brassica rapa* ssp. *pekinensis*) or green cabbage, cut into large sections
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (老抽)
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 125 ml (½ cup) water or light stock
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish
- Optional: 1–2 tbsp Pixian doubanjiang for a spicy version

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wok or wide, lidded pan over high heat. Add garlic; stir-fry 20 seconds.
2. Add cabbage; toss and stir-fry over high heat 2 minutes. Add soy sauces, Shaoxing wine, sugar, water and optionally doubanjiang. Toss.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the densest cabbage section.
4. Cover; reduce heat to medium-low; braise 15–20 minutes until the cabbage is completely soft and the sauce has reduced to a glossy coating.
5. **Pull at 88–95 °C (190–203 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil. Final serving temperature: **88–95 °C / 190–203 °F** (silky, sweet-savoury, soy-glossed — an everyday Chinese home dish).
