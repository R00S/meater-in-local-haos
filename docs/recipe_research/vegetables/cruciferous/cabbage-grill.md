## Cut profile
Grilled cabbage wedges — charred on the grate, smoky, with a caramelised exterior
and a yielding, sweet interior — represent one of the most compelling street-food
preparations for this humble vegetable. The Mexican elote (corn on the cob) tradition
has its cabbage counterpart in the charred wedge served with miso butter, tahini, or
simply with lemon and chilli. Internal temperature at the stem of the thickest wedge:
around 79–82°C — enough to ensure the interior is fully tender while the exterior is
dramatically charred.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Cabbage Wedges (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-cabbage
- **Method**: Quarter cabbage through root; brush with oil; grill direct medium-high
  heat 5–7 min per flat face. Internal at stem: ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks on both flat faces; outer leaves lightly charred;
  stem tender to paring knife; sweet smoky aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Root-intact wedges hold together on the grill; medium-high
  heat creates char marks without burning the delicate leaf edges before the thick
  stem is cooked through.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Cabbage (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-cabbage
- **Method**: Wedges through root; oil; grill over hot coals or high gas flame 4–6 min
  per side. Internal stem ~82°C; char marks and smoky flavour.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks; outer layers charred; stem tender; smoke penetration.
- **Why this method works**: Hot coals give a slightly more intense char and smoky
  flavour than gas; the 4–6 min per side window is shorter than for gas grills
  due to higher ambient heat from the coals.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Cabbage (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-cabbage
- **Method**: Wedges; brush with oil; charcoal or gas grill 5–6 min per flat face.
  Internal ~79–82°C. Dress with miso butter.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Clear grill marks; outer layers wilted and slightly charred;
  stem yields to knife.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's miso butter dressing is applied immediately to
  the hot charred surface; the miso's umami and salt amplify the smoky char flavour;
  the butter melts into the porous leaf surfaces.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad kål i klyftor (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-kal
- **Method**: Kvartade kålklyftor med rotstocken kvar; olja och salt; grillen på
  medelhög–hög värme; 5–7 min per sida. ~79–82°C i stjälken.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillränder på båda sidor; mjuk inuti; söt och rökig smak.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides present charred cabbage as a
  summer BBQ side dish; the root-intact quarter technique is standard across all
  Nordic grilling recipes for cabbage.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet kål (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-kal
- **Method**: Kvartert kål gjennom roten; olje; direkte grilling sterk varme
  5–7 min per side. ~79–82°C i stilken.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper; ytre blader sviskne og kolgrillt; stilk myk.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian grilling of cabbage as a vegetable side for
  outdoor cooking is a growing trend; the technique matches the international standard
  for the root-intact wedge.
