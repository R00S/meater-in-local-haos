# Cabbage × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Cabbage (*Brassica oleracea* var. *capitata*) is a dense, layered head of overlapping leaves built around a thick stem. Sliced into wedges with the stem intact, the leaves stay attached and the wedge holds together through the cook; sliced into "steaks" across the stem axis, the inner ribs caramelise into deeply sweet, almost meat-like slabs. Oven roasting at 200–220 °C drives off surface water rapidly so that the outer leaves blister and brown via Maillard reactions while the dense interior softens through gentle steam from the leaves' own moisture. Carryover is moderate (3–5 °C) because the layered structure traps heat in the centre. Pulling slightly under target keeps the inner leaves silken rather than collapsed.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Cabbage Wedges (American tradition)
**Source**: J. Kenji López-Alt, *The Wok* (W. W. Norton, 2022) and seriouseats.com/easy-roasted-cabbage-wedges-recipe  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 medium green cabbage (≈ 1.0 kg), outer leaves removed, cut into 6–8 wedges with stem intact so wedges hold together
- 60 ml (¼ cup) extra-virgin olive oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) lemon juice, to finish
- 30 g grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, to finish
- 5 g chopped flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Line a heavy sheet pan with parchment.
2. Brush both flat faces of each cabbage wedge generously with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Arrange wedges flat-side-down on the sheet, well spaced.
4. Insert the MEATER probe into a centre wedge through one cut face, with the tip in the dense stem at the geometric centre of the wedge.
5. Roast 12–15 minutes on the first side until deeply browned on the underside.
6. Carefully turn wedges to the second flat face. Roast another 10–12 minutes until both faces are caramelised and the stem yields to a paring knife.
7. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F).** Kenji targets fully cooked but still cohesive wedges; the inner leaves should be silken, not collapsed.
8. Rest 3 minutes. Finish with lemon juice, Parmesan and parsley. Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (tender, deeply caramelised).

### 2. Magnus Nilsson — Pressed Roast Cabbage (Nordic tradition)
**Source**: Magnus Nilsson, *The Nordic Cookbook* (Phaidon, 2015)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 35–45 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 small white cabbage (≈ 800 g), cut into 4 thick wedges, root left intact
- 50 g cold unsalted butter, in small cubes
- 6 g (1¼ tsp) flaked sea salt (Falksalt or Maldon)
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 60 ml (¼ cup) light vegetable stock or water
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) cold-pressed rapeseed oil, to finish
- A squeeze of lemon

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Choose a heavy oven-proof pan (cast iron or copper) just large enough to hold the wedges flat.
2. Place the wedges flat-side-down in the cold pan. Scatter butter cubes around them. Tuck thyme sprigs between the wedges. Season with sea salt and white pepper.
3. Pour stock around (not over) the wedges. The liquid should barely coat the bottom of the pan.
4. Place over medium heat until the butter melts and the liquid begins to simmer (3–4 minutes).
5. Press a sheet of parchment directly onto the wedges, then place a heavy oven-proof plate on top to weigh them down — Nilsson's "pressed" technique drives the cut face into close pan contact for deep browning.
6. Insert the MEATER probe through the side of a wedge, into the central stem. Transfer the pan to the oven.
7. Roast 30–40 minutes. The first side should be deeply browned; turn the wedges and continue 8–10 minutes for the second face.
8. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** Nilsson pushes slightly higher than the American standard — Nordic preference is for thoroughly soft, butter-rich wedges that hold their shape but yield entirely to a fork.
9. Rest 5 minutes uncovered. Drizzle with rapeseed oil and a squeeze of lemon. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (silky, butter-poached, deeply browned faces).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Whole Roasted Cabbage with Brown Butter & Caraway (Middle Eastern–European fusion)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi & Helen Goh, *Sweet* concept extended in Ottolenghi's *Flavour* (Ebury Press, 2020); ottolenghi.co.uk  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 45–55 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 small Savoy or sweetheart cabbage (≈ 900 g), outer leaves trimmed, halved and cored, then cut into 4 wedges
- 80 g unsalted butter
- 5 g (1 tsp) caraway seeds, lightly crushed
- 1 small lemon, zest pared in strips
- 6 g (1¼ tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 2 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
- 15 g chopped fresh dill, to finish
- 30 g toasted hazelnuts, roughly chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Line a roasting tin with parchment.
2. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Continue to cook, swirling, 3–4 minutes until the milk solids turn deep amber and the butter smells nutty (*beurre noisette*). Off the heat add caraway, lemon zest and garlic. Steep 5 minutes.
3. Lay cabbage wedges flat-side-down in the tin. Brush both faces generously with the steeped brown butter; season with salt and pepper.
4. Insert the MEATER probe through one cut face into the central stem of one wedge, tip at the geometric centre.
5. Roast 25 minutes, then turn wedges and brush with the remaining brown butter. Roast another 15–20 minutes.
6. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Ottolenghi pushes well past the al-dente point — the goal is leaves that almost shred under a fork, with deeply caramelised edges.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Scatter dill and toasted hazelnuts. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (deeply tender, brown-butter glossed).

### 4. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — Wedge of Cabbage with Anchovy Butter (British / River Cottage tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, *River Cottage Veg Every Day!* (Bloomsbury, 2011)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 1 medium green or pointed cabbage (≈ 900 g), cut into 4 wedges with stem intact
- 40 g unsalted butter, softened
- 4 anchovy fillets in oil, drained and finely chopped
- 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) lemon juice, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Line a baking tray.
2. Mash anchovies, garlic and pepper into the softened butter with a fork. Reserve.
3. Brush both faces of each wedge with olive oil; season with sea salt.
4. Lay wedges flat on the tray. Insert the MEATER probe through one cut face into the central stem of the wedge nearest the centre of the tray.
5. Roast 18–20 minutes until the underside is well browned. Turn each wedge to the second flat face; dot with the anchovy butter. Roast another 10–12 minutes until the butter has melted into the leaves and both faces are caramelised.
6. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Fearnley-Whittingstall describes the doneness as "soft, sweet and savoury, with the wedge still cohesive when lifted with a fish slice."
7. Rest 3 minutes. Finish with lemon juice. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (tender, savoury, anchovy-butter glossed).
