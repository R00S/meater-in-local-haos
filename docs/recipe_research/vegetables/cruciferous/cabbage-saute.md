# Cabbage × Sauté — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sautéed cabbage — shredded or cut into wedges and cooked in fat over moderate-to-high
heat — produces a result completely different from braising: the edges char, the
centres remain slightly firm, and the whole preparation takes 8–12 minutes rather
than 2 hours. The internal temperature at the ideal crispy-edged, tender-centre doneness
is around 79–82°C. The technique divides between shredded cabbage (faster, more even)
and wedge cabbage (slower, with more textural contrast), both using the same basic
heat principle.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Charred Cabbage (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/charred-cabbage
- **Method**: Cabbage wedges (root-intact); cast-iron or heavy skillet over medium-high
  heat with oil; cut face down 5–7 min undisturbed until deeply charred. Flip;
  cook 3 min. Internal at stem: ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C** (charred and tender)
- **Doneness cues**: Cut face deeply charred; outer leaves slightly wilted; the core
  is tender but the outer layers are char-crisped.
- **Why this method works**: The undisturbed long contact with high heat creates true
  charring on the flat face — not just browning; this is the steakhouse technique
  for cabbage wedges.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Sautéed Sweetheart Cabbage (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Sweetheart (pointed) cabbage; quartered; sauté in oil and butter over
  medium-high 5 min per flat face; turn. Add garlic and chilli. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Flat faces caramelised and slightly charred; garlic golden; inner
  leaves wilted; outer edge slightly crispy.
- **Why this method works**: Sweetheart cabbage's thinner, more tender leaves caramelise
  faster than a round cabbage; the 5 min per face at medium-high heat gives deep
  caramelisation without burning the thin leaf edges.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Pan-Fried Savoy Cabbage (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/pan-fried-savoy-cabbage
- **Method**: Shredded savoy; butter over medium-high 5–8 min, stirring regularly.
  Season generously. Internal ~79°C; wilted with golden edges.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Wilted and tender; golden-brown on many edges; fragrant from butter.
- **Why this method works**: Shredded cabbage in butter gives even, rapid cooking with
  golden edges throughout; savoy cabbage's crinkled surface creates more char edges
  per gram than a smooth-leaf variety.

### 4. ICA — Stekt vitkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekt-vitkal
- **Method**: Strimlad vitkål; smör och olja på medelhög värme; 8–10 min med regelbunden
  rörelse. ~79°C inuti; gyllenbrun och mjuk.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun; mjuk och silkeslen; smörarom.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish stekt vitkål (pan-fried white cabbage) is a classic
  side dish for pork; the butter-oil combination adds flavour and prevents the
  cabbage from burning before it softens.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Charred Cabbage Wedges (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/charred-cabbage-wedges
- **Method**: Wedges; oil; large frying pan over medium-high 5–7 min per charred face.
  Internal ~79°C; dramatically charred exterior, tender core.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep char on contact faces; outer leaves crisped; core tender.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food presents the charred wedge as a restaurant
  technique for home cooks; the dramatic charring creates a bitter-sweet crust that
  contrasts with the sweeter tender interior.
