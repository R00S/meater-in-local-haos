## Cut profile
Roasted cauliflower is one of the great transformations of vegetable cooking: raw
cauliflower's sharp, sulphurous flavour becomes nutty, caramelised, and almost sweet
when the florets are pushed through a hot oven. The Maillard browning that creates
this transformation requires a high temperature (200–230°C) and a dry surface — wet
florets steam rather than caramelise. Internal temperature at perfect doneness: 85–90°C
at the thickest stem, at which point the floret surface is deeply golden and the
interior is completely tender.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Cauliflower (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-cauliflower
- **Method**: Cut into flat-faced florets (slice through the crown to expose maximum
  flat surface area). Toss with oil and salt; pre-heated baking sheet at 230°C.
  Roast 15–20 min without stirring. Internal ~88°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Contact faces are deeply golden; floret tips slightly charred;
  stem is completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: Flat-faced florets (cut through the crown rather than pulled
  apart) create maximum Maillard surface area; the pre-heated sheet causes immediate
  caramelisation on contact.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Cauliflower with Tahini (UK / Jerusalem)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Whole florets; toss with oil, cumin, turmeric; oven 220°C for 20–25 min.
  Internal ~88°C; deeply golden with spiced crust.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Spiced crust deeply golden; tips charred; completely tender inside.
- **Why this method works**: Spices (cumin, turmeric) create a flavoured crust that
  browns alongside the cauliflower's own Maillard reaction; the result is a spiced-
  caramelised surface that a plain roast cannot produce.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Cauliflower (UK / Nigella Christmas / Simply)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-cauliflower
- **Method**: Florets; oil, salt, cumin seeds; oven 200°C for 25–30 min. Internal ~85°C;
  golden and nutty.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden on surfaces; cumin seeds popped and fragrant; completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: Cumin seeds bloom and pop in the dry oven heat, releasing
  their essential oils directly onto the caramelising cauliflower surface; the 200°C
  temperature is Nigella's reliable home-oven approach.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad blomkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-blomkal
- **Method**: Bitar; olivolja, salt, currypulver. Ugn 220°C i 18–22 min.
  ~85–88°C inuti; gyllenbrun yta med krispiga kanter.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun och aromatisk; kanter lätt kolnade; mjuk inuti.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend curry powder or cumin
  for roasted cauliflower; the warm spices complement the nutty caramelisation that
  the high oven heat produces.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Cauliflower with Almonds (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-cauliflower-almonds
- **Method**: Florets + flaked almonds; oil and salt; oven 200°C fan for 18–22 min.
  Internal ~85°C; golden, almonds toasted.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden florets; almonds toasted; completely tender; nutty aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Almonds placed on top of the cauliflower from the start
  toast at the same rate as the floret tips; the nutty double-flavour (caramelised
  cauliflower + toasted almond) is a classic BBC Good Food combination.
