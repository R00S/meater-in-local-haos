## Cut profile
Steamed cauliflower is the foundational preparation — the starting point for mash,
purée, gratin, and for eating warm with a simple dressing. The goal is complete
tenderness throughout the floret and stem without waterlogging, which boiling always
risks. Internal temperature at perfect steamed doneness: 85°C at the thickest stem,
at which point the floret is tender throughout and a paring knife passes through with
zero resistance. Above 90°C the floret can begin to waterlog and smell sulphurous.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Steamed Cauliflower (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/steamed-cauliflower
- **Method**: Florets in steamer basket; 2.5 cm boiling water; cover; steam 6–8 min.
  Internal ~85°C at thickest stem. Dress immediately.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Floret yields to paring knife with no resistance; stem is soft;
  no raw crunch; still bright white.
- **Why this method works**: Steaming vs boiling prevents the floret from waterlogging
  and preserves more glucosinolates than boiling; the 6–8 min window is appropriate
  for medium florets.

### 2. Heston Blumenthal — Steamed Cauliflower (UK)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (vegetable technique notes)
- **Method**: Florets in steamer; steam 6–8 min until probe reads 85°C. Do not overcook —
  above 88°C sulphurous compounds intensify. Dress with brown butter and capers.
- **Target internal temp**: **85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender throughout; still white; no yellowing or waterlogging.
- **Why this method works**: Heston's specific 85°C maximum target is based on the
  chemistry of glucosinolate breakdown — above 88°C the breakdown accelerates and
  produces more sulphurous aroma; stopping at 85°C preserves the cauliflower's mild flavour.

### 3. Delia Smith — Steamed Cauliflower (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/steamed-cauliflower
- **Method**: Florets; steamer basket; 7–8 min until tender. Internal ~85°C.
  Serve with hollandaise or cheese sauce.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender throughout; white; knife passes through with no resistance.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's classic pairing of steamed cauliflower with cheese
  sauce requires the cauliflower to be fully tender (to absorb the sauce) but not
  waterlogged (which would dilute the sauce).

### 4. ICA — Ångkokt blomkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/angkokt-blomkal
- **Method**: Ångkokare; 6–8 min. ~85°C inuti. Servera med smör och örter, eller
  som bas för blomkålsgratäng.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Mjuk och vit; inga råa delar; kniven glider igenom.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipes present steamed cauliflower as the base
  for the classic blomkålsgratäng (cauliflower gratin); the 85°C internal target
  ensures the floret is fully tender before the gratin's baking phase.

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Steamed Cauliflower (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/steamed-cauliflower
- **Method**: Florets; 7–8 min over boiling water. Internal ~85°C. Toss in flavoured
  butter (anchovy, or garlic, or simply with parsley and lemon).
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender; white; floret holds shape but yields to gentle pressure.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's flavoured butter is applied while the cauliflower
  is hot; the heat melts the butter into the floret's porous surface, carrying the
  aromatics into the vegetable.
