## Cut profile
Roasting a whole cauliflower — head intact, leaves on — is the most dramatic of all
cauliflower preparations and produces a centrepiece-worthy result: the outer florets
char, the inner florets steam-cook in the oven's ambient heat, and the stem, which
is often discarded, becomes the most intensely flavoured part of the whole roast.
The internal temperature at the centre of the stem — the last point to be cooked —
is 85°C, at which point the entire cauliflower from charred outer surface to tender
inner core is cooked through. This is a 45–60 minute preparation at 200–220°C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Ottolenghi — Whole Roasted Cauliflower (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Blanch whole head 5 min; drain; brush with spiced oil; oven 200°C
  40–50 min. Probe at thick stem: 85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **85°C** (at thick stem centre)
- **Doneness cues**: Outer florets charred; inner florets golden; stem completely
  tender (probe inserted through the base).
- **Why this method works**: The blanch step is critical for whole roasting — it starts
  the cooking and ensures the inner core reaches temperature before the outer florets
  burn; without blanching, the outer florets char before the centre is cooked.

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Cauliflower with Butter and Almonds (UK / Ottolenghi Simple)
- **Source**: ottolenghi.co.uk/recipes/cauliflower-butter-almonds
- **Method**: Blanch 5 min; whole head in roasting dish; butter, garlic, lemon; roast
  200°C 40–45 min basting every 10 min. Probe stem 85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden outer florets; caramelised butter in dish; stem tender.
- **Why this method works**: Basting every 10 min keeps the outer florets moist and
  adds to the caramelisation; the butter browns and adds nutty flavour to the basting
  liquid with each cycle.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Whole Roasted Cauliflower (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/whole-roasted-cauliflower
- **Method**: Trim leaves; rub with oil and spices; no blanch; oven 200°C for 45–55 min.
  Probe stem 85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **85°C** (at stem)
- **Doneness cues**: Outer florets deeply golden; a skewer inserted through the base
  meets no resistance; strong roasted aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's no-blanch approach requires the additional time
  (45–55 min vs 40–45 min with blanch); starting a cold cauliflower directly in the
  oven gives a slower initial heating phase that can improve core-to-surface evenness.

### 4. ICA — Hel ugnsrostad blomkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/hel-ugnsrostad-blomkal
- **Method**: Trimma löv och ansa stilken; pensla med kryddig olja; ugn 200°C i 45–55 min.
  ~85°C i stjälken.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Yttre rosetter gyllenbruna; stjälken mjuk vid stickprov.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present whole roasted cauliflower as
  a vegetarian centrepiece; the 45–55 min no-blanch method is the standard Nordic
  approach, suited to the slightly smaller cauliflower sizes common in Swedish markets.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Whole Roasted Cauliflower (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/whole-roasted-cauliflower
- **Method**: No blanch; rub with olive oil, garlic, and paprika; oven 200°C fan for
  40–45 min. Probe at stem: 85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Outer florets deeply golden; stem tender to skewer; paprika
  has created a deeply coloured crust.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven at 200°C reduces the cooking time slightly
  compared to static; the paprika creates a deeply coloured, visually striking crust
  on the whole head that makes it an impressive table centrepiece.
