## Cut profile
Air-fried eggplant achieves the caramelised exterior that makes this vegetable special
without the large quantity of oil that oven roasting or pan frying requires — eggplant's
spongy tissue absorbs oil ravenously, and the air fryer's dry circulating heat produces
golden, caramelised surfaces with far less oil. Internal temperature at perfect doneness:
88°C throughout, at which point the interior is completely soft and the exterior surfaces
are golden-brown. This is the fastest path to a caramelised eggplant for a single serving.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Eggplant (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-eggplant
- **Method**: 2.5 cm cubes; toss with 1.5 tbsp oil and salt; air fryer 200°C for
  12–15 min, shaking once. Internal ~88°C; golden-brown on all surfaces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown all around; completely soft; significantly shrunken.
- **Why this method works**: ATK's testing found the air fryer uses significantly less
  oil than oven roasting for eggplant while achieving similar caramelisation; the
  360° air circulation prevents the steaming that would occur in a crowded oven tray.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Eggplant (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-eggplant
- **Method**: Cubed; 1 tbsp oil; air fryer 200°C for 12–14 min, shaking halfway.
  Internal ~88°C; golden, concentrated.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown; completely soft; concentrated flavour; no grey patches.
- **Why this method works**: The reduced oil requirement is the main advantage; 1 tbsp
  vs 2+ tbsp in oven roasting produces the same caramelised result via the hot dry air
  rather than oil-mediated heat transfer.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Aubergine (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-aubergine
- **Method**: 2 cm rounds or cubes; brush with oil; air fryer 200°C for 12–15 min.
  Internal ~85–88°C; golden and tender.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown; soft throughout; slightly caramelised edges.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food positions the air fryer as the most practical
  domestic method for aubergine; the oil efficiency and speed are the main advantages
  cited for this preparation.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad aubergine (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/aubergine
- **Method**: Kuber; olivolja och salt; airfryer 200°C i 12–15 min, skaka en gång.
  ~88°C inuti; gyllenbrun och mjuk.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun; mjuk; minskat i storlek; mediterran doft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides present aubergine as a Mediterranean
  vegetable well-suited to the air fryer's dry circulating heat; the oil-efficiency
  advantage is highlighted in all Nordic recipes for this method.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Eggplant with Miso (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-eggplant-miso
- **Method**: Thick wedges; brush with light coating of oil; air fryer 200°C for
  12–15 min. Brush with miso-butter in last 3 min. Internal ~88°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden; miso caramelised on surface; completely soft.
- **Why this method works**: Miso applied in the last 3 minutes caramelises without
  burning; the initial oil-only phase ensures the eggplant is golden before the miso
  coating is added.
