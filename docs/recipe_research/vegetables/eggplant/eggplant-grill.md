# Eggplant × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilled eggplant is the preparation that gave the world baba ganoush: the direct heat
chars the skin completely, the inside steams and collapses in the enclosed heat, and
the finished product — once the charred skin is removed — has a smoky, custardy,
almost silky interior with a flavour depth that no oven preparation can match. For
sliced grilled eggplant (not whole charred), the method delivers caramelised grill-
marked surfaces and a soft interior. Internal temperature at doneness for whole charred
eggplant: above 88°C throughout; for sliced grilled: 82–88°C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Charcoal-Grilled Eggplant for Baba Ganoush (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/baba-ganoush
- **Method**: Whole eggplant directly on charcoal grates; turn every 5 min; 20–25 min
  until completely charred on all sides and collapsed when pressed. Internal above 88°C.
  Rest in sealed bag 10 min; peel; season.
- **Target internal temp**: **>88°C** (collapsed interior, charred exterior)
- **Doneness cues**: Completely black on all sides; collapses when pressed with tongs;
  juices have been drawn out and are dripping from the base.
- **Why this method works**: Charcoal provides the most intense and smoke-flavoured
  heat for whole eggplant charring; the complete char and collapse indicates the
  interior has broken down completely.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Grilled Aubergine Slices (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Thick slices 1.5 cm; brush with oil; grill over direct high heat 4–5 min
  per side. Internal ~82–88°C; deep grill marks.
- **Target internal temp**: **82–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep char marks on both sides; flesh soft and silky throughout;
  the slice bends when lifted.
- **Why this method works**: High heat is essential for eggplant slices — medium heat
  causes the slice to steam before the grill marks develop; the thick cut (1.5 cm)
  ensures the interior has time to soften during the 4–5 min per side.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Aubergine (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-aubergine
- **Method**: Thick rounds or planks; brush with oil; grill over high heat 4–5 min
  per side. Internal ~82°C; deep char marks; soft.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Clear char marks; soft when pressed; slightly smoky from the grill.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's oil-brushing before grilling prevents sticking and
  ensures the eggplant surfaces make complete contact with the grill grates; the thick
  cut prevents through-charring before the interior softens.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad aubergine (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-aubergine
- **Method**: Tjocka skivor; pensla med olja; grillning på hög direkt värme 4–5 min per
  sida. ~82°C inuti; tydliga grillränder.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tydliga grillränder; mjuk och silkeslen; något rökig.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides present grilled aubergine as a
  Mediterranean-inspired BBQ vegetable; the high-heat technique is specified to prevent
  the grey-steamed result that lower heat produces.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet aubergine (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-aubergine
- **Method**: Tykke skiver; oljepensel; direkte høy varme; 4–5 min per side.
  ~82°C inni; grillstriper og myk kjøtt.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper; myk og silkemyk; litt røyksmak.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian grilling of aubergine follows the international
  thick-slice high-heat technique; the vegetable is presented as a versatile summer
  side dish in Norwegian BBQ recipes.
