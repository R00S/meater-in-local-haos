# Eggplant × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Roasting eggplant (aubergine) is the foundational preparation of Middle Eastern and
Mediterranean cuisine: the dense, bitter raw vegetable transforms under oven heat into
something soft, rich, and almost buttery. The internal temperature at perfect doneness
for cubes or wedges: 88–93°C, at which point the flesh is completely soft throughout
and the cut surfaces are caramelised golden-brown. Whole roasted eggplant for baba
ganoush requires a higher temperature and longer time, targeting charred skin and a
completely collapsed interior.

## Source recipes

### 1. Ottolenghi — Roasted Aubergine with Tahini (UK / Jerusalem)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column) + Jerusalem (Ebury, 2012)
- **Method**: Cubed or wedged; toss with olive oil, salt; single layer on baking sheet.
  Oven 230°C for 20–25 min; do not stir for first 15 min. Internal ~90°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut surfaces deep golden-brown; flesh completely soft and yielding;
  the eggplant has shrunk to about 50% of its original volume.
- **Why this method works**: 230°C is essential for eggplant — lower temperatures produce
  grey, steamed results; the no-stir approach allows maximum caramelisation on the
  contact faces; salting before roasting (rather than after) helps draw moisture from
  the flesh so it evaporates rather than steams in the oven.

### 2. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Eggplant (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-eggplant
- **Method**: Cubed 2–3 cm; toss with 2 tbsp oil; pre-heated baking sheet at 230°C.
  Roast 20–25 min without stirring. Internal ~90°C; deeply golden.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden-brown on contact face; completely soft; no raw white
  flesh remaining; caramelised aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Pre-heated sheet provides immediate caramelisation; the
  2 tbsp oil (more than for most vegetables) is essential because eggplant absorbs
  oil rapidly and will stick without adequate coating.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Oven-Roasted Aubergine (UK / Simply)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-aubergine
- **Method**: Thick slices or wedges; brush generously with oil; oven 200°C for 25–30 min,
  turning once. Internal ~88°C; golden-brown and soft.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown on both faces; completely soft; slightly collapsed.
- **Why this method works**: Brushing (vs tossing) ensures every surface gets a coating
  before the eggplant begins absorbing oil; the turn halfway provides browning on two faces.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad aubergine (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-aubergine
- **Method**: Kuber eller skivor; riklig olivolja; ugn 225°C i 20–25 min.
  ~88–90°C inuti; djupt gyllenbrun.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbrun; halverat i storlek; mjuk genomgripande.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides use aubergine as a Mediterranean
  ingredient and all Nordic recipes specify 220–230°C for roasting to prevent the
  steamed-grey result that lower temperatures produce.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Aubergine (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-aubergine
- **Method**: Cubed; oil, salt; oven 200°C fan for 20–25 min. Internal ~88°C; golden-brown.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown on all exposed surfaces; soft; concentrated flavour.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven at 200°C circulates heat to caramelise all exposed
  surfaces simultaneously; the eggplant's porous texture means the oil-coated surfaces
  brown faster than a denser vegetable would.
