# Eggplant × Sauté — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan-sautéed eggplant — sliced or cubed in a hot, well-oiled pan — is the foundational
cooking step for dozens of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern preparations: the base of
a pasta alla Norma, the start of an imam bayıldı, the key component of a caponata.
The challenge is the eggplant's voracious oil absorption, which means the pan must
be hot enough to sear the surface quickly before the eggplant draws in all the oil
and begins to steam itself. Internal temperature at perfect sautéed doneness: 85–88°C,
at which point the flesh is completely soft and the surfaces are golden-brown.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Melanzane Trifolate (Italy / Essentials)
- **Source**: Marcella Hazan "Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking" (Knopf, 1992)
- **Method**: Cubed; generous olive oil in wide pan over medium-high; single layer;
  cook 8–10 min turning occasionally. "Use more oil than you think. Less oil produces
  a grey, steamed result." Internal ~88°C; deeply golden.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden-brown on all sides; completely soft; no white raw
  patches; concentrated savoury aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Hazan's generous oil instruction is the single most
  important note for pan-sautéed eggplant; the oil provides both heat transfer and
  the frying medium that produces the caramelised surface.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Sautéed Aubergine (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Thick slices; heat oil until shimmering; single layer; cook 4–5 min
  per side over medium-high without moving. Internal ~85°C; golden on both faces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on both cut faces; soft throughout; slightly glossy.
- **Why this method works**: The undisturbed 4–5 min per side gives the most intense
  Maillard browning possible on each face; flipping only once rather than repeatedly
  maintains the full-contact caramelisation.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Pan-Fried Aubergine (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/pan-fried-aubergine
- **Method**: Thick rounds; olive oil; medium-high heat; 4–5 min per side undisturbed.
  Season after turning. Internal ~85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on both faces; soft and yielding; slightly glossy.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's instruction to season after turning prevents the
  salt from drawing moisture out of the surface before browning is achieved; seasoning
  after the first flip is the correct technique for pan-sautéed eggplant.

### 4. ICA — Stekt aubergine (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekt-aubergine
- **Method**: Tjocka skivor eller kuber; riklig olivolja på medelhög–hög värme;
  4–5 min utan rörelse, vänd en gång. ~85–88°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbrun på båda sidor; mjuk; blank yta.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides echo the international consensus:
  generous oil and undisturbed cooking are the non-negotiable requirements for
  pan-sautéed aubergine.

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Sautéed Aubergine for Pasta Sauce (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/sauteed-aubergine-pasta
- **Method**: Cubed; plenty of olive oil; high heat; cook stirring every 2–3 min
  until completely golden and soft, 10–12 min total. Internal ~88°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Uniformly golden on all sides; soft throughout; the aubergine
  has given up all its water and reabsorbed the oil.
- **Why this method works**: For a pasta sauce application, the fully-developed 10–12 min
  sauté is the correct approach; the eggplant needs to be soft enough to break down
  slightly when combined with the tomato sauce.
