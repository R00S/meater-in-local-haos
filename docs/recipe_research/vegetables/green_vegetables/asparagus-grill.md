# Asparagus × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilled asparagus is one of the most expressive vegetables on a grill: the direct
heat chars the tips while the body softens, the natural sugars caramelise against
the grate, and the slight smoke character from the grill adds a savoury dimension
that steam and pan methods cannot produce. The challenge is roll management — round
asparagus spears roll between grill grates unless placed perpendicular to the bars
or skewered. Internal temperature at perfect grilled doneness: around 79–82°C,
slightly higher than steamed because the char and caramelisation are part of the result.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Asparagus (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-asparagus
- **Method**: Toss with oil and salt; grill over direct medium-high heat 5–8 min
  (thick spears) or 3–5 min (thin), rolling 2–3 times. Internal ~79°C at base.
  Dress with lemon and parmesan.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks on all sides; tips slightly charred; bends easily
  when lifted with tongs.
- **Why this method works**: Weber's rolling technique ensures all sides get char marks;
  medium-high heat (not high) gives the cook control over the char level on thin spears
  that would burn over high heat before the base is tender.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Asparagus (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-asparagus
- **Method**: Toss with 1 tbsp oil; place perpendicular to grill grates (prevents
  rolling). Gas or charcoal direct heat; 3–5 min turning once. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks; tips dark and slightly crisped; bends without snapping.
- **Why this method works**: Perpendicular placement prevents rolling while still
  creating grill marks; a single flip rather than constant rolling gives more defined
  char bars and reduces the risk of dropping spears through the grates.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Asparagus with Hollandaise (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-asparagus
- **Method**: Oil and season; over hot gas BBQ grill 3–4 min per side. Internal ~82°C.
  Serve with a warm hollandaise alongside — the asparagus holds its shape and flavour
  better than when boiled.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Clear char marks; tips dark; bends when lifted; base soft.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie argues that grilled asparagus with hollandaise is
  superior to boiled with hollandaise because the char character adds a savoury
  note that contrasts with the buttery egg sauce; the slightly higher 82°C target
  reflects a slightly more done preference for UK audiences.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad sparris (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-sparris
- **Method**: Penslade med olivolja; grilla vinkelrät mot gallret på medelhög–hög
  värme 4–6 min. Vänd en gång. ~79°C inuti tjockaste delen.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillränder; topparna bruna; böjer sig mjukt.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides confirm the perpendicular placement
  technique; asparagus grilling is seasonal and treated as a fresh-produce event in
  Sweden where the season is brief.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet asparges (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-asparges
- **Method**: Olje og salt; over direkte grill middels høy varme 4–5 min.
  Vend en gang. ~79°C inuti; grillstriper og mørke topper.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper; toppene mørke og litt sprø; stengelen bøyer seg.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian recipes treat grilled asparagus as a premium
  spring side dish; the gas grill medium-high heat recommendation reflects the
  Norwegian preference for gas BBQs and the need to control the heat for thin
  Nordic asparagus varieties.
