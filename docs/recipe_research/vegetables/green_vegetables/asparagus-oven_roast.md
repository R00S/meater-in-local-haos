## Cut profile
Roasted asparagus is one of the best arguments for owning a sheet pan: in a hot oven,
the thin spears caramelise at their tips, develop a gentle char at their thinner ends,
and concentrate their grassy, slightly nutty flavour into something intensely green
and savoury. The internal temperature at perfect doneness is around 77–82°C — the
asparagus is fully tender with a little resistance at the thickest base, and the tips
are beginning to crisp. Thinner spears cook in 8–10 minutes at 220°C; thicker spears
need 12–15 minutes. The internal probe is inserted through the thickest part of the
thickest spear.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Asparagus (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-asparagus
- **Method**: Trim woody ends; toss with olive oil and salt. Single layer on baking sheet.
  Oven at 230°C / 450°F. Roast 8–12 min (thin 8 min, thick 12 min) until tips are
  slightly charred. Internal ~79°C at thickest base.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C** (at thickest base)
- **Doneness cues**: Tips deeply brown and slightly crispy; the spear bends but doesn't
  snap; the base yields to a paring knife with slight resistance.
- **Why this method works**: 230°C is essential for asparagus — lower temperatures
  steam the spears in their own moisture rather than roasting them; the high heat
  evaporates surface moisture before it can accumulate and cause steaming.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Asparagus with Almonds and Capers (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Toss with olive oil, salt; oven 220°C for 10–15 min until tips browned.
  Toss with toasted almonds, capers, lemon. Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tips dark brown; spear bends; slight resistance at very base.
- **Why this method works**: The caper-almond-lemon dressing is applied after roasting
  to avoid the capers burning and the lemon zest turning bitter; the dressing's salt,
  acid, and fat balance the asparagus's natural sweetness.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Asparagus (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-asparagus
- **Method**: Single layer on roasting tray; toss with olive oil and sea salt. Oven
  220°C for 10–12 min. Internal ~79°C; tips lightly charred.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tips golden-brown; spears bend without snapping; fragrant and nutty.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's minimal approach — oil, salt, hot oven — is
  the correct base technique; any additions should be post-roast to prevent burning
  in the high heat required for proper caramelisation.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad sparris (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-sparris
- **Method**: Trimma; blanda med olja och salt. Ugn 220°C i 10–12 min (tunna spjut
  kortare, tjocka längre). ~79°C inuti i tjockaste delen.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Topparna gyllenbrun och lätt krispy; stammen mjuk men med viss spänst.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish asparagus season (May–June) is brief and the
  roast method is one of the most popular for domestic cooking; ICA recipe guides
  calibrate the 10–12 min window for both white and green Nordic asparagus.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Roasted Asparagus with Prosciutto (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/roasted-asparagus
- **Method**: Wrap spears in prosciutto; lay flat on baking tray; drizzle with oil.
  Oven 200°C for 12–15 min. Internal ~82°C; prosciutto crispy, asparagus tender.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Prosciutto crispy and shrunken; asparagus tips dark; the wrapped
  spear bends easily when lifted from the tray.
- **Why this method works**: The prosciutto wrapping provides fat and salt from outside
  while protecting the asparagus from the direct dry heat; the net result is a more
  evenly cooked spear with the added benefit of crispy cured meat.
