## Cut profile
Steaming asparagus is the method that most purely preserves the vegetable's natural
flavour — grassy, slightly sweet, faintly mineral — without adding caramelisation or
reducing its moisture content. Steamed asparagus at 77°C internal is crisp-tender:
the tips are soft, the base has a little give, and the whole spear retains its vivid
green. Above 82°C the colour dulls and the texture softens into something limp rather
than bright and assertive. Steam is the method for white asparagus, which the French
and Germans treat very differently from green asparagus.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Steamed Asparagus (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/steamed-asparagus
- **Method**: 2.5 cm water in skillet; bring to boil. Add trimmed asparagus flat (not
  in a basket — water contact on the bottoms is fine for asparagus). Cover; steam 3–5 min.
  Pull at ~77°C; serve immediately.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; bends without snapping; base yields to fingernail pressure.
- **Why this method works**: Steaming in a wide skillet allows all the spears to lay
  flat in the steam, cooking evenly; the thin water layer does not boil up to cover
  the spears (which would begin to overcook them as boiling does).

### 2. Heston Blumenthal — Perfect Steamed Asparagus (UK)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (asparagus steaming technique)
- **Method**: Dedicated asparagus steamer or tall pot with rubber band bunching the
  spears tips-up; bottoms in boiling water, tips in steam. 4–6 min depending on
  thickness. Internal ~77°C. Dress immediately with hollandaise.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tips steam-cooked but still firm; base tender; vivid colour.
- **Why this method works**: The classic French/German method for white asparagus —
  stand upright so the tougher bottoms boil while the delicate tips steam; adapted
  for green asparagus the technique ensures differential cooking by thickness.

### 3. Jacques Pépin — Asperges à la vapeur (France)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (asperges vapeur)
- **Method**: Steamer basket; bring water to rolling boil. Steam 4–5 min for medium
  spears. Probe at thickest base; pull at 77°C. Serve with vinaigrette or hollandaise.
- **Target internal temp**: **77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; bends easily but tip is firm; a thumbnail pressed
  to the base leaves an impression without going through.
- **Why this method works**: French asparagus culture demands al dente — the vegetable
  should have life and structure, not collapse; 77°C is the Pépin target that achieves
  this across a range of asparagus thicknesses.

### 4. ICA — Ångad sparris (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/angad-sparris
- **Method**: Ångkokare eller vid kokning med lock; 4–5 min. ~77°C i tjockaste delen;
  servera omedelbart med smör och salt.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Klargrön; böjer sig utan att brista; mjuk men fast i toppen.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish asparagus season is celebrated and steaming is
  the traditional service method; Nordic culinary tradition serves new-season asparagus
  simply with butter, which is applied just before service to prevent steam-wilting.

### 5. Delia Smith — Steamed Asparagus with Lemon Butter (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/steamed-asparagus
- **Method**: Steamer basket over boiling water; 5–7 min (Delia cooks asparagus
  slightly longer than the French tradition). Pull at ~77°C internal; dress immediately
  with lemon butter sauce.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: The spear bends easily when lifted; vivid green; base yields to
  gentle pressure.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's slightly longer steam creates a more yielding
  texture that suits the British preference for less al dente vegetables; the lemon
  butter is prepared separately and added to prevent the direct steam from cooking off
  the butter's flavour compounds.
