# Broccoli × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air-fried broccoli combines the caramelisation of oven roasting with the speed of a
hot-air appliance, and the result is arguably the best broccoli the home cook can
achieve without a commercial oven: the tips become almost shatteringly crispy, the
stems stay tender, and the whole process takes 8–12 minutes from basket to table.
The smaller air fryer cavity concentrates heat more efficiently than a full oven,
which means the floret tips reach the Maillard browning temperature faster — and the
cooks must be watchful, because the window between "perfectly crisped tips" and
"acrid burnt tips" is narrow.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Broccoli (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-broccoli
- **Method**: Florets tossed with 1 tbsp oil, salt, garlic powder. Air fryer 200°C /
  400°F for 8–10 min, shaking once at 5 min. Internal ~85°C; tips dark golden.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tips deeply golden-brown, nearly charred; stems soft; florets crispy.
- **Why this method works**: ATK testing found the air fryer produces a crispier floret
  tip in half the time of an oven; the smaller cavity concentrates heat and creates
  a crisping effect closer to deep frying than conventional roasting.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Broccoli (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-broccoli
- **Method**: Flat-cut florets for maximum contact surface. 1 tbsp oil, pinch of red
  pepper flakes. Air fryer 200°C for 8–10 min, shaking once. Internal ~82–85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Contact face golden-brown; tips crispy; stems yielding.
- **Why this method works**: The flat-cut technique from oven roasting applies equally
  to the air fryer; the pepper flakes become crispy in the hot air and provide
  both heat and textural contrast.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Broccoli (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-broccoli
- **Method**: Florets in a single layer; oil, salt, optional garlic. Air fryer 200°C
  for 8–10 min, shaking halfway. Internal ~82°C; golden-crispy tips.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden on the tips; stems soft; no grey-green discolouration.
- **Why this method works**: UK food media has published extensively on air fryer
  broccoli as the definitive weeknight green; single layer is non-negotiable —
  overlapping florets steam instead of crisping.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad broccoli (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/broccoli
- **Method**: Broccolirosetter i ett lager; olivolja, salt. Airfryer 200°C i 8–10 min,
  skaka en gång. ~82–85°C inuti; knapriga topper.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Knapriga och gyllene toppar; stammen mjuk.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides consistently list broccoli as one
  of the top results; the 8–10 min window at 200°C produces the same result in any
  air fryer regardless of brand.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Broccoli with Lemon and Parmesan (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-broccoli-lemon-parmesan
- **Method**: Florets tossed with oil; air fryer 200°C for 8–10 min. Add lemon zest
  and parmesan in the last 2 min. Internal ~82–85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tips golden; parmesan melted and slightly crispy; lemon
  fragrant and slightly caramelised.
- **Why this method works**: Adding parmesan in the last 2 minutes prevents it from
  burning; the lemon zest's volatile oils are released and slightly caramelised in
  the dry circulating air.
