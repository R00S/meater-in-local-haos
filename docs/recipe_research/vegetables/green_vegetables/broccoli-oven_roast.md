## Cut profile
Roasting broccoli is a revelation to anyone who grew up only steaming or boiling it:
the florets caramelise and brown at their tips, the stems become slightly nutty, and
the cut surfaces develop a flavour depth — almost meaty in its savoriness — that no
other broccoli method can produce. The internal temperature at peak roasted doneness
is around 82–88°C — higher than for steamed broccoli because the extended high-heat
exposure transforms the texture and flavour in ways that 71–77°C cannot. Below 79°C
the stems are still firm-raw. Above 93°C the florets collapse.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Broccoli (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-broccoli
- **Method**: Cut into flat-faced florets (maximise Maillard contact). Toss with oil
  and salt. Pre-heated baking sheet at 230°C / 450°F. Roast 10–12 min until deeply
  brown on contact face; do NOT stir. Internal ~88°C at thickest stem.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Contact face is deeply brown with crispy, almost charred floret
  tips; stem is tender; the whole floret has shrunk and concentrated.
- **Why this method works**: Flat-cut floret faces create maximum Maillard surface area;
  not stirring allows full caramelisation to develop on the contact face; the pre-heated
  sheet causes immediate browning on contact.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Broccoli with Chilli and Garlic (UK / Plenty More)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Florets halved to expose flat face; toss with oil, garlic, chilli flakes.
  Oven 220°C for 15–20 min; the tips should be deeply browned and slightly charred.
  Internal ~88°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Dark brown floret tips; garlic chips golden; stems tender throughout.
- **Why this method works**: The chilli and garlic coating caramelises alongside the
  broccoli's own sugars; the 220°C temperature creates the intense browning that
  Ottolenghi considers non-negotiable for this preparation.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Oven-Roasted Broccoli (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/oven-roasted-broccoli
- **Method**: Toss broccoli florets in olive oil, garlic, and lemon zest. Oven 200°C
  for 15–18 min. Internal ~85–88°C; golden-brown floret tips.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tips brown; lemon zest slightly caramelised; stems completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: The lemon zest caramelises in the oven heat and fuses
  with the browned broccoli surfaces; the 200°C temperature is slightly more forgiving
  than Kenji's 230°C and gives a wider window for home-oven variability.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostade broccolibuketter (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-broccolibuketter
- **Method**: Broccolirosetter halverade; olivolja, salt, peppar. Ugn 225°C i 12–15 min.
  ~88°C inuti; topparna bruna och krispiga.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Bruna och knapriga topper; stammen mjuk.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend oven-roasted broccoli as
  a side dish upgrade from steaming; 225°C gives the same character as Kenji's 230°C
  and suits Nordic oven calibration standards.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Broccoli with Almonds (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-broccoli-almonds
- **Method**: Florets tossed in oil, salt, flaked almonds; oven 200°C fan for 12–15 min.
  Internal ~85°C; tips golden, almonds toasted.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden tips; almonds toasted alongside; tender throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Fan circulation at 200°C delivers even browning to all
  exposed surfaces simultaneously; the almonds toast at the same rate as the broccoli
  tips, making them a practical pairing in a single-tray roast.
