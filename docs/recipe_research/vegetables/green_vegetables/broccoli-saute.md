# Broccoli × Sauté — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sautéed broccoli — in a hot pan with oil, often with garlic and chilli — is the Italian
and Chinese kitchen's approach to this vegetable, and it produces a result very different
from steaming or roasting. The high-heat pan contact caramelises the cut surfaces while
the covered resting phase steams the interiors; the result is tender throughout with
charred, caramelised edges. Internal temperature at doneness is around 77–82°C —
softer than crisp-tender steam but firmer than the full collapse of very high-heat
roasting.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Stir-Fried Broccoli (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/stir-fried-broccoli
- **Method**: High heat in wok or large skillet; thin film of neutral oil. Add broccoli
  and stir-fry 1 min; add 3 tbsp water; cover 2 min. Uncover; season. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C** (tender-crunchy)
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green with golden contact patches; steam-tender interior;
  slight resistance at the very centre of thick stems.
- **Why this method works**: The steam-fry combination (blister then steam) is the
  Chinese restaurant technique for broccoli; it maintains the vibrant colour of
  steaming while delivering caramelised contact patches from the initial high heat.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Sautéed Broccoli Aglio e Olio (UK)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi / Guardian column)
- **Method**: Large flat-based pan; olive oil, sliced garlic, dried chilli. Medium-high
  heat; add broccoli florets and stems; sauté 5–7 min, turning once or twice. Cover
  2 min with a splash of water; season and serve.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown on flat faces; garlic golden; tender throughout.
- **Why this method works**: The Italian-origin aglio e olio technique creates a
  flavour transfer from the garlic and chilli oil directly onto the broccoli's cut
  surfaces during the sauté; the cover-and-steam phase finishes the interior.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Broccoli in Anchovy Oil (UK / How to Eat)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/broccoli-anchovy-oil
- **Method**: Large frying pan; olive oil and 4 anchovy fillets dissolved over medium
  heat. Add broccoli; sauté 5–7 min, turning; add splash of water; cover 2 min.
  Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green with golden contact patches; completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: The anchovy-infused oil creates a deeply umami base;
  the anchovy dissolves completely in the oil and becomes undetectable as a separate
  ingredient while contributing savoury depth to every piece of broccoli.

### 4. ICA — Stekt broccoli med vitlök (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekt-broccoli-vitlok
- **Method**: Stor stekpanna på hög värme; olja och krossad vitlök. Broccoli
  3–4 min utan omrörning; vattenstänk och lock 2 min. ~79°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun kontaktyta; mjuk men fast; klargrön.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present the sauté method as the
  everyday alternative to steaming; the garlic and oil combination is the most
  universal broccoli seasoning across all European traditions.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Flash-Fried Broccoli (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/flash-fried-broccoli
- **Method**: Wok or large frying pan on maximum heat; 1 tbsp oil; broccoli (including
  peeled stems). Fry 3 min; add garlic and chilli; fry 1 min more; add splash of water
  and cover 1 min. Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green with charred spots; tender throughout; garlic golden.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's "flash-fry" technique maximises the Maillard
  caramelisation on the floret surfaces in the shortest possible time; the brief
  covered steam finishes the interior without losing the precious char on the exterior.
