# Broccoli × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Broccoli (*Brassica oleracea* var. *italica*) sautéed in a hot pan — or stir-fried in a wok — produces golden-blistered florets with a caramelised stem and bright-green colour. The pan must be very hot; broccoli must be completely dry; it should not be crowded. Cooking time: 6–8 minutes. Pull temperature: 78–83 °C (172–181 °F) for tender-crisp, golden broccoli. MEATER probe through the stem of the largest floret.

## Styckesprofil

Broccoli (*Brassica oleracea* var. *italica*) sauterad i het panna — eller wokad — ger guldblåsiga buketter med karameliserad stjälk. Sautera i het olja i 6–8 minuter. Klar när lätt bränd i punkter och genomstekt men fortfarande krispig.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Qīng Chǎo Xīlánhuā (清炒西兰花 — Chinese Stir-Fried Broccoli)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets (3 cm); 3 tbsp neutral oil; 3 garlic cloves (thinly sliced); 1 tsp grated ginger; 2 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp oyster sauce; 1 tsp sugar; ½ tsp sesame oil

**Method**
1. Pat florets completely dry. Heat wok to maximum until smoking; add oil.
2. Add florets; insert MEATER probe through the largest stem. Stir-fry 4–5 min over max heat.
3. Add garlic and ginger (20 s); add soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar; toss 30 s.
4. **Pull at 80–83 °C (176–181 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil. Final serving: **80–83 °C / 176–181 °F** (vivid green, soy-glazed, Chinese).

---

### 2. Pailin Chongchitnant — Pad Broccoli with Oyster Sauce (Thai tradition)
**Source**: Pailin Chongchitnant, *Hot Thai Kitchen* (Appetite by Random House, 2016); stir-fried broccoli, a ubiquitous Thai home-cooking preparation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 5–7 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets; 2 tbsp neutral oil; 3 garlic cloves (chopped); 2 tbsp oyster sauce; 1 tbsp fish sauce; ½ tsp sugar; ½ tsp white pepper; 1–2 red chillies, sliced

**Method**
1. Heat wok to max; add oil; add garlic (15 s); add broccoli. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Stir-fry 4–5 min. Add oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, white pepper and chillies; toss 30 s.
3. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Final serving: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (vivid, savoury, Thai).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Broccoli Masala Sauté (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp cumin seeds; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp coriander; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp salt; lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Heat oil; add cumin (sizzle); add broccoli, turmeric, coriander, chilli. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Sauté 7–8 min. Season with salt; squeeze lemon; scatter coriander.
3. **Pull at 80–83 °C (176–181 °F).** Final serving: **80–83 °C / 176–181 °F** (spiced, golden, Indian).

---

### 4. Marcella Hazan — Broccoli Saltato con Burro e Limone (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min (+ 3 min blanching) · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g broccoli florets, blanched 3 min in salted water; 30 g butter; 1 tbsp olive oil; 3 garlic cloves sliced; ½ tsp salt; pepper; 1 tbsp lemon juice; 2 tbsp grated Parmesan

**Method**
1. Blanch and drain florets. Melt butter + oil in wide pan over high heat; add garlic (30 s).
2. Add florets; insert MEATER probe. Sauté 4–5 min, tossing, until golden. Season; add lemon.
3. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Scatter Parmesan. Final serving: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (golden, butter-bright, Italian).
