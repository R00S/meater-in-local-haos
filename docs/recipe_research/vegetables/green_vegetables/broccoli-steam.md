## Cut profile
Steaming broccoli is the method that best preserves what makes broccoli exceptional:
the glucosinolates that give it its distinctive flavour, the vitamin C that degrades
with prolonged water contact, and the brilliant emerald green of chlorophyll that
turns dull and army-green when overcooked. The internal temperature at the ideal
crisp-tender doneness — still with some resistance at the stem but yielding at the
floret — is around 71–77°C. Below 71°C the stem is still raw-crunchy. Above 82°C
the floret starts to turn soft and yellow-green, losing both colour and character.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Steamed Broccoli (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/steamed-broccoli
- **Method**: 2.5 cm of water in a saucepan; steam basket; bring to boil. Add broccoli
  florets cut to even size; cover; steam 5–7 min for crisp-tender. Internal ~77°C
  at thickest stem piece.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C** (crisp-tender)
- **Doneness cues**: Bright emerald green; the stem gives with pressure but has
  some resistance; the floret is tender with no raw crunch.
- **Why this method works**: Steam cooking above boiling water preserves more water-
  soluble nutrients than boiling because the broccoli does not contact the water;
  the confined steam space reaches temperature faster than a loose steamer basket.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Steamed Broccoli (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/steamed-broccoli
- **Method**: Steamer basket over 2.5 cm boiling water; medium florets. Cover and steam
  4–6 min. Pull at 71–77°C internal; immediately plunge in ice water to stop cooking
  if serving cold (or serve immediately).
- **Target internal temp**: **71–77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Bright green; the floret is tender; the stem yields to a paring knife
  but with a little resistance.
- **Why this method works**: ATK's immediate ice bath stop preserves the colour and
  crisp texture for salads; for warm service, pulling at 71°C with the residual steam
  heat brings it to 77°C by the time it reaches the table.

### 3. Delia Smith — Simple Steamed Broccoli (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/steamed-broccoli
- **Method**: Even-sized florets in a steamer over boiling water; steam covered 5–6 min.
  Internal ~77°C; still vivid green. Dress immediately with butter and lemon.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; stem is slightly firm but yielding; floret is tender.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's immediate butter dressing adds fat to the surface
  which protects the chlorophyll from further oxygen exposure, helping maintain the
  green colour during service.

### 4. ICA — Ångkokt broccoli (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/angkokt-broccoli
- **Method**: Ångkokare eller durkslag med lock; 4–6 min. ~77°C inuti; klargrön.
  Servera direkt med olivolja eller smör.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Klart grön; mjuk men med lite motstånd i stammen.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present steaming as the preferred
  broccoli method for nutrient retention; the 4–6 min window is consistent with
  Nordic vegetable size preferences.

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Steamed Broccoli with Anchovy Dressing (UK / How to Eat)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/steamed-broccoli-anchovy
- **Method**: Steam florets 5 min over boiling water; pull at vivid green and crisp-
  tender (internal ~77°C). Immediately dress with warm anchovy butter.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green, not olive or yellow-green; the floret yields to
  gentle pressure without collapsing.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's anchovy butter is served warm and applied
  immediately to preserve the bright colour; the umami of anchovy amplifies broccoli's
  natural sulphurous edge into something deeply savoury.
