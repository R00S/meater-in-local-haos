## Cut profile
The air fryer is the apex Brussels sprout method for small batches: the circulating
hot air penetrates the tight leaf layers simultaneously from all sides, creating crispy
outer leaves and a tender core without the steaming that can happen in a crowded oven
tray. The 10–12 minute cook time is less than half of oven roasting. The internal
temperature at perfect doneness is 82–88°C — same as oven roasting — but the textural
result is qualitatively different: more crispy leaf area per sprout, less caramelised
flat-face area (because the air fryer's heat comes from all directions).

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Brussels Sprouts (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halve; toss with 1.5 tbsp oil, salt. Air fryer 190°C / 375°F for 10–12 min,
  shaking basket halfway. Pull at ~85°C. They should be crispy on outside, tender inside.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Outer leaves crispy and dark; flat face golden; tender when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: ATK testing found that 190°C (not 200°C) prevents the
  outer leaves from burning before the centres cook through; the lower temperature
  gives a wider window for the cook to pull at the right moment.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Brussels Sprouts (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halved; 1 tbsp oil, salt, dash of fish sauce (secret ingredient). Air fryer
  200°C for 10–12 min, shaking once. Internal ~85–88°C; extremely crispy outer leaves.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Outer leaves shatteringly crispy; a few have separated and become
  chips; interior completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: The fish sauce adds umami and sugar that accelerates the
  Maillard reaction on the outer leaves; the air fryer's heat crisps these sugar-coated
  surfaces faster than oven heat would.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Brussels Sprouts (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halved; oil, salt, optional parmesan dusting. Air fryer 190°C for 10–12 min,
  shaking halfway. Internal ~82–85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Crispy outer leaves; flat face golden; parmesan melted and crispy.
- **Why this method works**: The parmesan dusting provides both flavour and a browning
  accelerant; the 190°C temperature prevents the parmesan from burning before the
  sprouts are cooked through.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad brysselkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/brysselkal
- **Method**: Halverade brysselkål; olja, salt, balsamvinäger. Airfryer 190°C i 10–12 min,
  skaka en gång. ~82–85°C inuti; knapriga ytterblad.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Ytterbladen knapriga och mörka; snittytan gyllenbrun; mjuk inuti.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides confirm Brussels sprouts as one of
  the top vegetable results; balsamic vinegar provides the acid balance that tames the
  sprout's natural bitterness in the dry circulating heat.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Brussels Sprouts with Chilli and Lemon (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halved sprouts; toss with oil, dried chilli flakes, lemon zest. Air fryer
  190°C for 10–12 min, shaking halfway. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Crispy; golden; lemon zest slightly caramelised; chilli fragrant.
- **Why this method works**: Australian food media has embraced air fryer Brussels
  sprouts; the chilli-lemon combination is Donna Hay's signature for this preparation,
  using the dry heat to concentrate the citrus and bloom the dried chilli.
