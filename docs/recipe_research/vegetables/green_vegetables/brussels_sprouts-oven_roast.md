# Brussels Sprouts × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Roasting transformed Brussels sprouts from the most reviled vegetable on the British
and American table into a modern restaurant staple. The halved-and-roasted sprout
develops a caramelised flat face, crispy outer leaves that char at their tips, and a
tender interior — three distinct textures in one small vegetable. The internal
temperature at perfect doneness is around 82–88°C: at 79°C the interior is still
tight and slightly raw; above 93°C the leaves collapse and the bitter glucosinolate
notes dominate.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Crispy Roasted Brussels Sprouts (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/crispy-roasted-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halve; toss with oil and salt. Pre-heated baking sheet (critical) at
  230°C / 450°F. Roast cut-face-down 15–20 min without stirring. Internal ~88°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut face is deeply caramelised; outer leaves are crispy and charred
  at tips; the sprout is completely tender when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: Pre-heated baking sheet causes immediate caramelisation
  on the flat face; not stirring allows the full Maillard crust to develop; the
  high temperature (230°C) creates the crispy outer leaves simultaneously.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Chestnuts (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Halved; toss with oil and salt. Oven 220°C for 20–25 min, tossing once.
  Toss with bacon lardons and chestnuts; roast further 10 min. Internal ~88°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Outer leaves dark and crispy; centre tender; some leaves
  have separated and crisped into individual chips.
- **Why this method works**: The two-stage roast (sprouts first, then add bacon and
  chestnuts) prevents the bacon from burning before the sprouts are cooked; the
  bacon fat releases and bastes the sprouts in the second stage.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Honey-Roasted Brussels Sprouts (UK / Nigella Christmas)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halved sprouts; toss with oil, honey, caraway seeds. Oven 200°C for
  20–25 min. Internal ~85–88°C; caramelised honey glaze.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Honey glaze is caramelised; outer leaves dark; interior tender.
- **Why this method works**: Honey's fructose content caramelises at a lower temperature
  than sucrose, creating a deep amber glaze at 200°C that complements the sprout's
  natural bitterness; the caraway adds an anise-adjacent flavour note.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostade brysselkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-brysselkal
- **Method**: Halverade; olja, salt, och balsamvinäger. Ugn 220°C i 18–22 min.
  ~88°C inuti; karamelliserade snittytor och knapriga yttre blad.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Snittytan djupt gyllenbrun; yttre blad knapriga; mjuk inuti.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides have fully adopted the oven-roasted
  Brussels sprout; balsamic vinegar addition provides the acidity that balances the
  sprout's bitterness and caramelises further during roasting.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-brussels-sprouts-bacon
- **Method**: Halved sprouts on a baking tray with bacon lardons and a drizzle of oil.
  Oven 200°C fan for 20–25 min. Internal ~85°C; deeply browned and crispy.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Bacon crispy; sprouts deeply browned; outer leaves slightly charred.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven circulation at 200°C creates even browning
  across the entire tray; cooking the bacon with the sprouts from the start bastes
  the sprouts in rendered bacon fat throughout the cooking time.
