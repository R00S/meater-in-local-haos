# Brussels Sprouts × Pan Fry — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Pan frying Brussels sprouts with slightly more oil and at medium heat (rather than
medium-high sauté) gives the cook more control over the caramelisation of the flat
faces and a more even browning all around the sprout. This is the method for the cook
who wants the French bistro version — golden, slightly crispy, served warm as a side
to duck or pork — rather than the high-char American roast version. Internal temperature
at doneness: 82°C at the centre, same as sauté, but the texture is slightly more
yielding because of the longer, gentler heat exposure.

## Source recipes

### 1. Bon Appétit — Pan-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Brown Butter (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/pan-fried-brussels-sprouts-brown-butter
- **Method**: Halve; heat 1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add sprouts
  cut-face-down; cook 6–8 min until deeply golden. Flip; cook 3 min more. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Flat face deep golden-amber; outer leaves golden; interior tender.
- **Why this method works**: Medium heat (vs. medium-high) gives a wider window for
  developing colour on the flat face; the butter browns during this time, creating
  a nutty beurre noisette coating on the cut surfaces.

### 2. Delia Smith — Pan-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Chestnuts (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/pan-fried-brussels-chestnuts
- **Method**: Blanch sprouts 3 min; dry well. Pan-fry in butter and oil over medium
  heat 4–6 min per side. Add chestnuts in last 2 min. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on cut face; chestnut warmed through; yielding interior.
- **Why this method works**: The pre-blanch is important for Delia's method because
  it ensures the centres are cooked before the pan-fry, allowing the pan phase to
  focus entirely on flavour development rather than cooking.

### 3. Cuisine Actuelle — Choux de Bruxelles à la poêle (France)
- **Source**: cuisineactuelle.fr/recettes/choux-bruxelles-poele
- **Method**: Blanchis 3 min; séchés. Beurre clarifié à feu moyen-vif; 4–5 min par
  face. Lardons ajoutés. ~82°C au centre.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C** (tendre)
- **Doneness cues**: Face coupée dorée profonde; feuilles extérieures légèrement croustillantes.
- **Why this method works**: French bistro technique uses blanched sprouts (as Delia
  does) because the pre-cooking ensures consistent results; the clarified butter
  provides a clean browning medium without the milk solids burning at medium-high heat.

### 4. ICA — Panstekta brysselkål (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/panstekta-brysselkal
- **Method**: Halverade; smör och olja på medelvärme. Snittytan ner 6–7 min.
  Vänd och stek 3 min till. ~82°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbrun snittyta; mjuk inuti; smörigt och aromatiskt.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipes distinguish pan-frying from sautéing
  in this context; medium heat and more butter gives a richer result than the quick
  sauté technique.

### 5. Donna Hay — Pan-Fried Brussels Sprouts with Pancetta (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/pan-fried-brussels-sprouts-pancetta
- **Method**: Halved; heat oil in large pan over medium heat; add pancetta, render 2 min;
  add sprouts cut-face-down; cook 5–6 min until golden; flip; cook 3 min more.
  Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Flat face caramelised; pancetta crispy; sprout tender when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: Rendering pancetta first provides fat that the sprouts
  cook in for the remainder of the time; the pancetta's salt and fat naturally season
  the sprouts without any additional work.
