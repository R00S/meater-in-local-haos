## Cut profile
Sautéed Brussels sprouts — halved or shredded, in a hot pan with fat — produce a
result that emphasises the savoury, slightly nutty character of the vegetable at
medium-high heat with a shorter cook time than oven roasting. For whole halved
sprouts, the technique mirrors pan-searing: cut face down, don't stir, until
caramelised. For shredded sprouts, the approach is more active: constant movement
over high heat produces wilted leaves with charred edges. Internal temperature at
doneness for halved sautéed sprouts: around 82°C at the centre.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Pan-Sautéed Brussels Sprouts (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/pan-sauteed-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halve; place cut-face-down in a cold pan with oil; bring to medium-high
  heat (starting cold gives better caramelisation). Cook 5–7 min undisturbed; shake
  to ensure even contact; cover 3 min. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut face deeply caramelised; outer leaves slightly softened;
  interior tender when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: Starting cold and bringing to heat gradually allows the
  cut face to make flat contact with the pan before the oil starts smoking; the
  result is more even caramelisation than placing cold sprouts into a hot pan.

### 2. Thomas Keller — Sautéed Brussels Sprouts (USA / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (Bouchon cookbook reference technique)
- **Method**: Blanch sprouts 2 min; shock in ice water; dry. Sauté in brown butter
  over medium-high heat 3–4 min per side. Internal ~82°C. Season with lemon juice.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden colour on all cut faces; outer leaves slightly charred;
  the brown butter aroma is nutty and caramelised.
- **Why this method works**: The pre-blanch ensures the centres are cooked before
  they hit the butter; the sauté then focuses entirely on developing the brown butter
  crust rather than simultaneously cooking the interior — this is the restaurant technique.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Quick Sautéed Brussels Sprouts (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/sauteed-brussels-sprouts
- **Method**: Halved; hot pan with butter; 4–5 min per side over medium-high. Toss;
  add garlic and caraway in last minute. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden flat face; garlic golden and fragrant; sprout yields gently.
- **Why this method works**: Butter provides the browning medium and flavour that
  olive oil cannot match for Brussels sprouts; the caraway addition was a Nigella
  signature that has become widely copied.

### 4. ICA — Stekta brysselkål i panna (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekta-brysselkal
- **Method**: Halverade; smör och olja på medelhög värme; snittytan ner 5–6 min
  utan omrörning. Tack på lock 2 min. ~82°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun snittyta; mjuk inuti; yttre blad lätt stekta.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend the cut-face-down method
  without stirring for pan-sautéed Brussels sprouts; the covered final step ensures
  the dense centres cook through without burning the caramelised face.

### 5. Yotam Ottolenghi — Shredded Brussels Sprouts Sauté (UK)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi / Guardian)
- **Method**: Thinly shred sprouts; wok or large frying pan over high heat; oil;
  stir-fry 3–4 min; add garlic and chilli; stir 1 min; toss with lemon juice.
  Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C** (shredded — lower threshold needed)
- **Doneness cues**: Leaves wilted with charred edges; garlic golden; bright green
  maintained at centre of thicker pieces.
- **Why this method works**: Shredding maximises the surface area for Maillard
  browning; the constant-movement stir-fry technique creates charred edges on many
  individual leaves in the same time it would take to caramelise one flat face.
