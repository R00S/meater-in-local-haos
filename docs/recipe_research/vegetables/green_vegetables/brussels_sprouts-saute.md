# Brussels Sprouts × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Brussels sprouts (*Brassica oleracea* var. *gemmifera*) sautéed — frequently tossed in a hot pan — differ from pan-frying (held still for a sear). Sautéed sprouts are typically shredded or quartered for faster, more even cooking; whole halved sprouts that are continuously tossed achieve golden browning on multiple surfaces. Pull temperature: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F). MEATER probe into the core of the largest sprout.

## Styckesprofil

Brysselkål (*Brassica oleracea* var. *gemmifera*) sauterade — frekvent vänds i het panna — skiljer sig från pannstekning (hålls stilla för searing). Sauterade kål är typiskt trimmade men hela och kastas i het olja i 8–12 minuter. Klar när gyllenbrun och genomstekta.

## Source recipes

### 1. Pailin Chongchitnant — Thai-Style Sautéed Brussels Sprouts with Fish Sauce (Thai tradition)
**Source**: Pailin Chongchitnant, *Hot Thai Kitchen* (Appetite by Random House, 2016); stir-fry technique applied to Brussels sprouts.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 2 tbsp neutral oil; 3 garlic cloves chopped; 2 tbsp fish sauce; 1 tsp sugar; ½ tsp white pepper; 1–2 red chillies, sliced

**Method**
1. Heat wok over high heat. Add oil; add garlic (15 s); add sprouts.
2. Insert MEATER probe into largest sprout core. Sauté over high heat 6–7 min, tossing every 90 s.
3. Add fish sauce, sugar, white pepper; toss 30 s. Add chillies.
4. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, savory, Thai).

---

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Sautéed Shredded Brussels Sprouts with Ponzu (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, very thinly shredded (mandoline or knife); 2 tbsp sesame oil; 2 tbsp ponzu sauce; 1 tsp mirin; toasted sesame seeds; spring onion

**Method**
1. Heat sesame oil in wide pan over medium-high; add shredded sprouts.
2. Insert MEATER probe into the densest pile of shreds.
3. Sauté 4–5 min, tossing, until wilted and lightly golden at edges.
4. Add ponzu and mirin; toss 30 s. **Pull at 78–82 °C (172–180 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving: **78–82 °C / 172–180 °F** (tender, citrus-soy, Japanese).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Sautéed Brussels Sprouts with Cumin and Mustard Seeds (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Curry Easy* (Ebury Press, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 2 tbsp oil; 1 tsp mustard seeds; ½ tsp cumin; ½ tsp turmeric; ½ tsp chilli; ½ tsp salt; lemon juice; coriander

**Method**
1. Heat oil over medium-high; mustard seeds (pop); add sprouts, cumin, turmeric, chilli. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Sauté 8–10 min, tossing. Season; squeeze lemon; scatter coriander.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, fragrant, Indian).

---

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Sautéed Brussels Sprouts with Tahini and Pomegranate (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g Brussels sprouts, halved; 3 tbsp olive oil; salt; *tahini:* 2 tbsp tahini, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1 garlic clove grated, 2 tbsp water, salt; 30 g pomegranate seeds; 10 g mint

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil over high heat; add sprouts cut-face-down. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Sauté 5–6 min, tossing once or twice, until golden on multiple faces.
3. Season with salt. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Spread tahini on platter; pile sprouts; scatter pomegranate and mint. Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (golden, tahini-creamy, jewel-bright).
