# Green Beans × Boil — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Boiling is the foundational green bean preparation — every tradition that uses this
vegetable has a boiling method — and when done with intention it produces results
that rival steaming. The internal temperature target depends entirely on the desired
final use: 71°C for al dente salad use (French salade niçoise); 77°C for tender-
crisp sides; 82°C for fully cooked dishes where the bean is a background element.
The key variable is the salt level and whether the cooked beans are shocked in ice water
(which fixes the vivid green colour) or finished directly in the cooking liquid.

## Source recipes

### 1. Jacques Pépin — Haricots Verts Bouillis (France / The Complete Pépin)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (boiled haricots verts technique)
- **Method**: Heavily salted boiling water (as salty as the sea — 12 g per litre).
  Plunge trimmed beans in bulk; boil 3–5 min until just al dente. Drain immediately;
  shock in ice water 2 min; drain. Internal 71–73°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **71–73°C** (al dente, for salads or reheating)
- **Doneness cues**: Snaps almost; vivid deep green (the ice shock locks in the colour);
  firm throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Heavy salting maintains turgor pressure in the cell walls,
  preserving texture; the ice shock stops enzyme activity immediately and locks in
  the Maillard-enhanced green pigment (the brief hot water phase activates enzymes
  that enhance green, then ice stops further breakdown).

### 2. Delia Smith — Boiled Green Beans (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/boiled-green-beans
- **Method**: Salted boiling water; cook 5–7 min until tender. Drain; serve with butter.
  No ice shock in Delia's approach — she serves them immediately from the pot.
- **Target internal temp**: **77–79°C** (tender, British-style)
- **Doneness cues**: Bends easily; vivid green (if served immediately); fully tender.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's no-shock approach is intended for immediate
  service; the 77–79°C target is softer than Pépin's because the British tradition
  prefers a fully tender bean rather than an al dente one.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Boiled Fine Green Beans (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/boiled-green-beans
- **Method**: Plenty of salted boiling water; thin French-style beans 3–4 min. Drain
  and toss immediately with best olive oil and sea salt. Internal ~73–77°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **73–77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; bends without snapping; olive oil coats immediately.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella tossing immediately with olive oil creates a
  quick coating that helps preserve the colour on the way to the table; the oil also
  carries the sea salt more effectively than dry salting.

### 4. ICA — Kokta haricots verts (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/kokta-haricots-verts
- **Method**: Saltat kokande vatten; 4–5 min. Häll av; isvatten om de ska serveras
  kalla eller sparas. ~73–77°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: **73–77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Levande grön; böjer sig utan att knäppas.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish boiled bean recipes typically specify ice shocking
  for cold salads and summer buffets (smörgåsbord), which is the most common serving
  occasion for green beans in the Nordic tradition.

### 5. Samin Nosrat — Salt Fat Acid Heat: Perfect Boiled Green Beans (USA)
- **Source**: saltfatacidheat.com (boiled green beans technique)
- **Method**: The most heavily salted water Nosrat recommends for any vegetable;
  boil 4–6 min until just tender. "Don't shock in ice water unless serving cold —
  the beans will cool on the plate." Internal ~77°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; tender but with structure; the salt level means
  they taste seasoned from within, not just on the surface.
- **Why this method works**: Nosrat's central thesis about salt applies directly here:
  beans boiled in properly salted water are seasoned throughout their flesh, not just
  on the surface; the result tastes more like green beans, not like green beans
  with salt added afterwards.
