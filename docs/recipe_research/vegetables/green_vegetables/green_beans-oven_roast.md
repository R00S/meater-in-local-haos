## Cut profile
Oven-roasted green beans are concentrated, slightly chewy at the edges, and deeply
savoury — a very different vegetable from the crisp-tender steamed or boiled version.
The roasting process dehydrates the beans while caramelising their surfaces, driving
the natural sugar concentration and creating Maillard flavour. Internal temperature
at perfect roasted doneness: around 82–88°C — higher than for steaming because the
intended final texture is more yielding, with some caramelised edges. This method
produces beans that are excellent warm but also serve well at room temperature in salads.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Roasted Green Beans (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-green-beans
- **Method**: Toss trimmed beans with oil and salt. Single layer on baking sheet. Oven
  at 230°C / 450°F for 10–15 min, not stirring. Internal ~88°C; slightly blistered and
  shrunken, some ends charred.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Blistered surfaces; slightly shrunken; ends charred; completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: 230°C maximises the caramelisation and dehydration that
  distinguish roasted from steamed beans; not stirring allows char to develop on the
  contact surfaces.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Green Beans with Almonds and Tarragon (UK / Plenty More)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Toss with olive oil, salt, sliced almonds. Oven 220°C for 12–15 min.
  Internal ~85–88°C; almonds toasted, beans slightly charred at tips.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Blistered skin; tips beginning to char; almonds golden.
- **Why this method works**: Almonds toasting alongside green beans at 220°C finish
  at approximately the same time; the toasted almond pieces coat the blistered bean
  surfaces during the final toss.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Crispy Roasted Green Beans (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-green-beans
- **Method**: Toss with oil, sea salt, and a small handful of parmesan. Oven 200°C fan
  for 12–15 min. Internal ~85°C; parmesan crisped, beans slightly charred.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Parmesan has crisped into savoury chips; bean surfaces blistered;
  completely tender with slightly chewy edges.
- **Why this method works**: Parmesan crisped onto roasted green beans creates a
  textural contrast (crispy parmesan + blistered bean) at the same baking time; 200°C
  fan is sufficient to crisp the parmesan without burning it.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostade gröna bönor (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-grona-bonor
- **Method**: Olivolja och salt; ugn 220°C i 12–14 min. ~85–88°C inuti; lite skrynklade
  och med karamelliserade kanter.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skrynklade; karamelliserade kanter; mjuka hela vägen igenom.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides have adopted oven-roasted green beans
  as a modern alternative to boiled; the 220°C temperature produces the right result
  for the thinner Nordic haricots vert variety.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Green Beans with Garlic (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-green-beans-garlic
- **Method**: Toss with oil, whole garlic cloves, and salt. Oven 200°C fan for 15 min.
  Internal ~85°C; garlic golden and sweet; beans slightly blistered.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden beans with charred tips; garlic soft and caramelised inside
  its skin; completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: Roasting whole garlic cloves alongside the beans (rather
  than sliced garlic that would burn) gives a sweet, deeply flavoured caramelised
  garlic that can be squeezed out of its skin and mixed with the beans.
