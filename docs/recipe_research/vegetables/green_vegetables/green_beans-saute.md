## Cut profile
Sautéed green beans in a hot pan develop a character unavailable to steamed beans:
the Maillard browning on the contact surfaces, the blistered skin, and the slightly
reduced moisture content concentrate the flavour. The Italian fagiolini in padella,
the French haricots verts sautés, and the Chinese dry-fried green bean are all
expressions of the same fundamental technique, targeting the same temperature range
(79–82°C internal) but arriving there with different oils, aromatics, and char levels.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Sautéed Green Beans (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sauteed-green-beans
- **Method**: Large skillet over high heat; 1 tbsp oil. Add trimmed beans in single
  layer; cook 2 min undisturbed; toss; cook 2–3 min more. Season; finish with
  garlic and red pepper flakes. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C** (blistered but tender)
- **Doneness cues**: Blistered surfaces with golden-brown patches; vivid green with
  some darkening; bends easily; slight tenderness throughout.
- **Why this method works**: The undisturbed first 2 minutes build blistered char
  patches on the side in contact with the pan; the toss then turns the beans to
  blister the other sides while the interior reaches temperature.

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Pan-Fried Green Beans with Feta (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Large pan over high heat; olive oil; beans in single layer 2–3 min per
  side. Add cherry tomatoes; cook 2 min. Top with crumbled feta. Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden patches on bean surfaces; cherry tomatoes slightly softened;
  feta beginning to melt into the pan juices.
- **Why this method works**: The cherry tomatoes release liquid when they burst, which
  creates a brief steaming effect in the pan that finishes the beans' interiors; the
  feta's salt and acid balance the beans' sweet-savoury character.

### 3. Jacques Pépin — Haricots Verts Sautés à l'Ail (France)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (haricots verts sautes)
- **Method**: Blanch beans 3 min; shock; dry well. Sauté in butter and garlic over
  medium-high heat 3–4 min. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden patches; garlic golden; beans yield without collapsing.
- **Why this method works**: The pre-blanch is Pépin's technique for restaurant-quality
  sautéed beans; the sauté then focuses on flavour development (butter, garlic) rather
  than cooking the interior, producing a more consistent result.

### 4. ICA — Stekta haricots verts med vitlök (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekta-haricots-verts
- **Method**: Stekpanna på hög värme; olivolja. Bönorna 3–4 min; vänd en gång.
  Vitlök i sista minuten. ~79°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllene kontaktfläckar; vitlöken gyllene; böjliga och mjuka.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present pan-sautéed haricots verts
  as a classic accompaniment to grilled fish and meat; the high-heat brief technique
  retains the vivid colour while delivering the flavour depth of caramelisation.

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Flash-Fried Green Beans with Sesame (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/flash-fried-green-beans
- **Method**: Wok or large pan over maximum heat; 1 tbsp oil. Beans 3–4 min,
  tossing frequently. Add sesame oil and seeds at the end. Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Blistered surfaces; bright vivid green; completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's sesame finish is added off the heat to prevent
  the sesame oil from burning; the high-heat flash-fry creates blistered surfaces
  that give the beans a charred intensity that contrasts with the nutty sesame finish.
