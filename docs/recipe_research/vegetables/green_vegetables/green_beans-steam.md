## Cut profile
Steamed green beans cooked to crisp-tender are the foundation of salads, side dishes,
and vegetable platters across multiple culinary traditions. The internal temperature
at crisp-tender is around 71–77°C: at 71°C the beans still snap and are vivid green;
at 77°C they bend easily and have a pleasant texture that yields but still has some
resistance. Above 82°C the beans begin to lose their colour and become soft; beyond
88°C they are well-done and olive-coloured. Steaming gives cleaner flavour than
boiling because the water-soluble sugars and vitamins are not diluted into the cooking water.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Steamed Green Beans (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/steamed-green-beans
- **Method**: 2.5 cm water in wide skillet; bring to boil. Add trimmed beans (flat in
  the liquid, tips above it). Cover; steam 3–5 min until crisp-tender. Internal 71–77°C.
  Dress immediately with butter or vinaigrette.
- **Target internal temp**: **71–77°C** (crisp-tender)
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; bends without snapping; slight resistance throughout.
- **Why this method works**: The wide skillet steam method gives even cooking across
  a flat layer of beans; dressing immediately prevents the beans from continuing to
  cook in their own residual heat.

### 2. Jacques Pépin — Haricots Verts à la Vapeur (France)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (haricots verts vapeur)
- **Method**: Fine French haricots verts; steamer basket 3–4 min maximum. Pull at
  71–73°C; shock in ice water; serve cold or warm with vinaigrette.
- **Target internal temp**: **71–73°C** (al dente — French preference)
- **Doneness cues**: Snaps almost; vivid intense green; firm throughout.
- **Why this method works**: French tradition demands haricots verts be slightly
  undercooked by UK/US standards — the snap test is used: the bean should bend
  but still have snap left at the centre. The ice shock stops cooking immediately.

### 3. Delia Smith — Steamed Green Beans (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/steamed-green-beans
- **Method**: Steamer basket over boiling water; 5–7 min (Delia allows more cooking
  time than the French tradition). Internal ~77°C; bends easily.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; bends without snapping; fully tender.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's slightly longer steam reflects the British
  preference for a less al dente result; 77°C is the point where the bean is
  both fully cooked and still recognisably a green bean in colour and structure.

### 4. ICA — Ångkokt haricots verts (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/angkokt-haricots-verts
- **Method**: Ångkokare 3–5 min. Iskallt vatten direkt för att stoppa koket.
  ~73–77°C inuti; levande grön färg.
- **Target internal temp**: **73–77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Levande grön; böjer sig utan att knäppas; fin spänst.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish cooking of fine beans follows the French model
  closely; the ice-water shock is standard in Swedish professional and home recipes
  because it locks in the vivid green colour for presentation.

### 5. Ottolenghi — Steamed Green Beans with Tahini (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Steamer basket; 4–5 min until just tender. Internal ~77°C. Immediately
  dress with tahini, lemon, garlic dressing while still warm.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**77°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Vivid green; bends; tender throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Warm beans absorb the tahini dressing more deeply than
  cold beans; Ottolenghi's timing (not ice-shocking these beans) is deliberate —
  the warm surface is essential for the dressing to cling.
