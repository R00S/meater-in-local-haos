# Spinach × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Steamed spinach is the gentlest preparation: no fat in the pan, no direct heat contact,
just the ambient hot steam wilting the leaves from the outside in. The result is
a cleaner flavour than sautéed spinach — less rich, more mineral, more purely green.
Internal temperature at doneness for steamed spinach is around 71–74°C, same as
sautéed, but the texture is slightly more watery because no moisture has evaporated
into a hot pan. The technique is valuable when serving spinach as a base for eggs
(florentine), fish, or as a neutral backdrop for a complex sauce.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Steamed Spinach (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/steamed-spinach
- **Method**: Place washed spinach (water still clinging) in a lidded saucepan over
  medium heat. Cover; steam in residual water 2–3 min until fully wilted.
  Drain in colander; press out excess moisture. Internal ~71–74°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**71–74°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Fully wilted; vivid dark green; no raw patches; easily
  compressible in colander.
- **Why this method works**: Steaming in the water left on washed leaves is the
  simplest method; ATK's technique of pressing out excess moisture in a colander
  after cooking prevents the waterlogged result that steaming without pressing produces.

### 2. Delia Smith — Steamed Spinach (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/steamed-spinach
- **Method**: Washed leaves in a large pan; cover; medium heat; steam 3–4 min in
  residual water. Drain; season with nutmeg, butter, salt.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**71–74°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Fully collapsed; deep green; excess water drains from the colander.
- **Why this method works**: Nutmeg is the classic spinach seasoning in the British
  and Italian traditions; added after draining, when the heat is residual, the
  nutmeg's volatile oils are not driven off by high cooking temperature.

### 3. ICA — Ångkokt spenat (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/angkokt-spenat
- **Method**: Tvättad spenat med kvarhängt vatten; stor gryta med lock på medelvärme.
  2–3 min. Häll av; krama ur extra vatten.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**71–74°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Fullständigt vikt; klargrön; inga råa fläckar.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present steamed spinach as the
  foundation for spinach-based dishes (stuvad spenat — creamed spinach in Swedish
  cuisine); the pressing step is essential before combining with cream sauce.

### 4. Nigella Lawson — Steamed Spinach with Lemon (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/steamed-spinach-lemon
- **Method**: Washed spinach in large covered pan; steam 2–3 min in residual water.
  Drain; dress with lemon juice and salt. Internal ~71–74°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**71–74°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Fully wilted; vivid green; lemon juice absorbed.
- **Why this method works**: Lemon juice applied immediately after draining replaces
  the rich oil or butter of sautéed spinach with a bright acidic note that is
  preferable when serving with fish or egg dishes.

### 5. Jacques Pépin — Épinards à la Vapeur (France)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (épinards vapeur)
- **Method**: Steamer basket over boiling water; 3–4 min for fresh leaves. Internal
  ~71°C. Express excess moisture by pressing in a clean towel; use immediately in
  composed dishes or dress simply with beurre noisette.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**71°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely wilted; vivid green when immediately served.
- **Why this method works**: Pépin's towel-press technique extracts more moisture
  than a colander alone and produces a drier result that is essential for uses in
  gratins, stuffings, and florentine dishes where excess moisture would make the dish
  watery.
