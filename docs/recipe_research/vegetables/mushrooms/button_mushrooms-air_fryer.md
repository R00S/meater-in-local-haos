## Cut profile
Air frying button mushrooms is one of the most effective applications of the air fryer
for vegetables: the circulating hot air causes rapid moisture evaporation from all
surfaces simultaneously, achieving the caramelised, browned exterior that a pan sauté
achieves only for the contact face. The result is a mushroom that is golden on all
sides with a concentrated flavour and slightly chewy texture. Internal temperature at
doneness: 79–82°C. The method works with whole small mushrooms or halved larger ones;
quartering makes them too small for the air fryer basket.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-mushrooms
- **Method**: Whole or halved; toss with 1 tbsp oil and salt (but NOT before air frying —
  toss just before placing in basket to minimise moisture release). Air fryer 200°C
  for 10–12 min, shaking once. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown on all surfaces; shrunken; no pooled moisture in basket.
- **Why this method works**: ATK's critical note about not salting too early applies to
  air fryer mushrooms as much as to pan sautéing; salt draws moisture out of mushrooms,
  which would steam the exterior rather than caramelise it.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-mushrooms
- **Method**: Halved mushrooms; toss with oil; air fryer 200°C for 10–12 min, shaking once.
  Internal ~82°C; golden all around, concentrated.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden on all sides; shrunken; concentrated meaty flavour.
- **Why this method works**: The air fryer produces a more evenly browned mushroom than
  a pan sauté (which only browns one or two faces) in the same amount of time.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-mushrooms
- **Method**: Whole or halved; 1 tbsp oil, salt, thyme leaves. Air fryer 200°C for
  10–12 min, shaking halfway. Internal ~79°C; golden all around.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Even golden colour; shrunken; thyme crisped; no wet patches.
- **Why this method works**: Thyme applied before air frying (rather than added at the
  end) dries and crisps in the hot air; the dry heat preserves and concentrates the
  thyme's essential oils into the mushroom surfaces.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade champinjoner (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/champinjoner
- **Method**: Hela eller halverade; olivolja och salt; airfryer 200°C i 10–12 min,
  skaka en gång. ~79–82°C inuti; gyllenbrun runt om.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun runt om; minskat; mustig smak; inga fuktiga partier.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides consistently recommend mushrooms
  as a top air fryer vegetable; the 360° heat is specifically valuable for the
  round shape of button mushrooms.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Mushrooms with Garlic Butter (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-mushrooms-garlic-butter
- **Method**: Whole mushrooms; oil; air fryer 200°C for 10 min. Toss in garlic butter
  immediately after basket. Internal ~79°C; golden; garlic fragrant.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden all around; garlic butter melting immediately; shrunken.
- **Why this method works**: Garlic butter added after (not before) cooking preserves
  the garlic from burning in the air fryer's high heat; the hot mushrooms immediately
  absorb the garlic butter, carrying the flavour throughout.
