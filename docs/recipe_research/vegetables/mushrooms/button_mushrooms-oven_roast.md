## Cut profile
Oven-roasting button mushrooms is the method for large batches — the sheet pan can hold
many more mushrooms than a skillet, and the oven heat from above and below ensures
even cooking even when the mushrooms are slightly crowded (something that would be
catastrophic in a pan sauté). The internal temperature at doneness is the same as
for sautéing (79–82°C) but the texture of oven-roasted mushrooms is slightly more
yielding and less crisply caramelised than pan-sautéed ones.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Roasted Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-mushrooms
- **Method**: Whole or halved; toss with 2 tbsp oil, salt; single layer on baking sheet.
  Oven 230°C for 15–20 min, not stirring. Internal ~82°C; golden-brown.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown on contact faces; shrunken; concentrated; no wet pooling.
- **Why this method works**: 230°C is the critical temperature for mushroom roasting —
  lower temperatures produce steamed rather than roasted mushrooms; the pre-heated
  baking sheet principle applies here as for other vegetables.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Mushrooms with Herbs (UK / Plenty More)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Whole mushrooms; toss with oil, thyme, garlic. Oven 220°C for 15–18 min.
  Internal ~82°C; golden and fragrant.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden colour; garlic cloves softened; thyme crisped;
  concentrated mushroom flavour.
- **Why this method works**: Garlic cloves roasted whole alongside mushrooms (not sliced)
  caramelise in their skins at the same rate as the mushrooms; the squeezed garlic
  paste from the skins is the dressing for the finished dish.

### 3. ICA — Ugnsrostade champinjoner (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-champinjoner
- **Method**: Hela champinjoner; olivolja, timjan, vitlök; ugn 220°C i 15–18 min.
  ~82°C inuti; gyllenbruna och aromatiska.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun; minskat; timjanen knaprig; mustig doft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides use oven-roasted mushrooms as a
  pizza and pasta topping preparation; the batch size of an oven tray suits the
  large quantities needed for these uses.

### 4. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Mushrooms on Toast (UK / How to Eat)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-mushrooms-on-toast
- **Method**: Whole button mushrooms; butter and oil; oven 200°C for 15–20 min.
  Internal ~82°C. Serve on thick toast with the pan juices.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden; shrunken; pan juices from the butter have accumulated;
  deeply savoury.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's butter in the roasting tray creates baste juices
  that caramelise around the mushrooms during roasting; these pan juices poured over
  toast are the point of the preparation — deeply flavoured mushroom butter.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Garlic Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-garlic-mushrooms
- **Method**: Whole mushrooms; toss with garlic oil, salt, parsley. Oven 200°C fan
  for 15 min. Internal ~79°C; golden, garlic-infused.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden with garlic oil coating; shrunken; parsley slightly wilted
  and fragrant from the oven heat.
- **Why this method works**: Garlic oil (infused rather than fresh) prevents burning
  in the oven heat while delivering consistent garlic flavour to all the mushrooms
  simultaneously.
