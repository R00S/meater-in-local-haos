## Cut profile
Sautéed button mushrooms are the most universally practised and most frequently
misunderstood preparation in vegetable cooking. The mistake — overcrowding the pan —
causes the mushrooms to steam in their own released water, producing grey, bland, slippery
results. The correct technique — a hot pan, single layer, no stirring for the first 2 minutes —
produces caramelised, golden-brown mushrooms with a concentrated, meaty flavour. Internal
temperature at perfect doneness: around 79–82°C, at which point the mushroom has
released and reabsorbed most of its water and the surfaces are deeply browned.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Sautéed Mushrooms (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sauteed-mushrooms
- **Method**: Dry pan over high heat; oil only after the pan is very hot. Add mushrooms
  cut-face-down in a SINGLE LAYER. Cook 2–3 min without moving; toss; cook 2 min more.
  Season only at the end. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C** (browned and cooked through)
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden-brown on contact faces; shrunken; savoury, meaty
  aroma; no wet mushroom smell.
- **Why this method works**: The dry pan maximises initial heat so the mushrooms begin
  caramelising before they release water; pre-seasoning draws water out before browning
  is possible. This is the single most important technique note for mushrooms.

### 2. Thomas Keller — Champignons Sautés (France/USA / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (mushroom sauté technique)
- **Method**: Clarified butter over high heat; mushrooms in single layer; cook undisturbed
  2 min; season; toss; cook 2–3 min more. Finish with fresh thyme and parsley. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden-brown; all moisture evaporated; mushroom has shrunk
  significantly; herb-butter coating.
- **Why this method works**: Keller's clarified butter has a higher smoke point than
  whole butter; it can withstand the high heat needed for mushroom caramelisation
  without burning before the browning is complete.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Buttery Sautéed Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/sauteed-mushrooms
- **Method**: Large pan; butter + splash of oil over medium-high; mushrooms in single
  layer; 3–4 min per side. Add garlic and thyme in last minute. Internal ~79°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-brown; shrunken; garlic fragrant; thyme crisped.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's butter-oil mix adds flavour and extends smoke
  point; the garlic-in-last-minute technique is essential — garlic added too early
  burns while waiting for the mushrooms to caramelise.

### 4. ICA — Stekta champinjoner (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekta-champinjoner
- **Method**: Stor het stekpanna; olja eller smör; champinjoner i ett lager 3–4 min
  utan rörelse. Vänd; krydda. ~79–82°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun; minskat i storlek; knaprig yta; mustig doft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides consistently emphasise single-layer
  cooking for mushrooms; the instruction not to stir for the first 3 minutes is
  present in all Nordic mushroom sauté recipes.

### 5. Gordon Ramsay — Perfect Sautéed Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: gordonramsay.com (mushroom technique video/recipe)
- **Method**: Very hot pan; olive oil; mushrooms flat-face-down; no touching for 2 min;
  toss; garlic, thyme; 2–3 min more; squeeze of lemon at end. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on one face; all moisture evaporated; concentrated
  meaty flavour; lemon juice finish brightens the pan juices.
- **Why this method works**: Ramsay's lemon juice addition at the end is a technique
  borrowed from protein cooking; the acid brightens the deep, almost heavy flavour
  of caramelised mushrooms without adding sweetness.
