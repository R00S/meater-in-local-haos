# Portobello Mushrooms × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying portobello mushrooms is a practical and effective method for quick weeknight
cooking: the circulating hot air concentrates the mushroom's flavour rapidly, caramelises
the gill surface, and softens the cap to 82°C in 10–12 minutes. The result lacks the
dramatic pooling of juices that gill-up oven roasting or grilling achieves, but compensates
with speed and an even golden-brown exterior that the convection-free oven cannot match.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Portobello Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Caps gill-side-up; brush with oil, season. Air fryer 200°C for 10–12 min.
  Internal 82°C; gills dark and glistening.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gills dark and glistening; cap completely soft; concentrated juices.
- **Why this method works**: Air fryer's 360° circulation reaches the gill side and the
  dome simultaneously; the faster heat penetration produces the same 82°C result in
  10 min vs 15–20 min in a conventional oven.

### 2. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Portobello Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Gill-side-up; oil and seasoning; air fryer 200°C for 10–12 min. Internal 82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gills dark; cap soft; slightly shrunken; concentrated aroma.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food confirms the air fryer as a viable portobello
  method; the time saving vs oven roasting makes this the recommended weeknight approach.

### 3. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade portobello-svampar (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/portobello
- **Method**: Hattar med lamellsidan upp; olja och salt; airfryer 200°C i 10–12 min.
  ~82°C i tjockaste hatten.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Lamellerna mörka; hatten mjuk; koncentrerat.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides present portobello as a practical
  air fryer option; the result is slightly less dramatic than oven roasting in terms
  of pooled juices but faster and equally tender.

### 4. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Portobello with Miso Butter (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-portobello-miso-butter
- **Method**: Caps gill-side-up; brush with miso-butter blend; air fryer 200°C for
  10–12 min. Internal 82°C; miso caramelised.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Miso caramelised and golden on gill surface; cap soft; intense
  umami aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Miso's sugar and protein content caramelise rapidly in the
  air fryer's hot circulating air; the combination of miso umami and mushroom umami
  creates a deeply savoury result.

### 5. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Portobello Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Caps gill-side-up; oil, garlic powder; air fryer 200°C for 10–12 min.
  Probe 82°C. Serve as burger substitute on toasted bun.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gills dark; cap yielding; concentrated; holds its shape.
- **Why this method works**: The air fryer's rapid heat penetration from 360° means the
  portobello cap softens more evenly than in an oven; the result holds its shape well
  on a burger bun, unlike an overcooked mushroom that collapses.
