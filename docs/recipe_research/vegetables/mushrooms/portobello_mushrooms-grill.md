# Portobello Mushrooms × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilling portobello mushrooms is the preparation that most fully realises the portobello's
extraordinary capacity for meaty, savoury depth — the grill marks, the smoke character,
and the concentrated juices that pool in the cap's gills produce a result that vegetarians
use as a direct steak substitute with good reason. The probe goes into the thickest part
of the cap, and the target temperature of 79–82°C coincides with the cap being completely
soft, the gills glistening with accumulated juices, and the grill having delivered its
char marks to the dome.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Portobello Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Remove stem; brush with oil; grill gill-side-up over direct medium-high
  heat 4–5 min; flip dome-side-down 3–4 min; flip back gill-up to finish. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grill marks on dome; gills glistening and dark; cap completely soft
  when pressed; juices pooling in gills.
- **Why this method works**: The gill-up start allows the mushroom to steam in its own
  juices while the dome gets grill marks; flipping to dome-down then back to gill-up
  for the finish maximises char on the dome while keeping the juices in the cap.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Portobello Burgers (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-portobello-mushroom-burgers
- **Method**: Marinate in oil, balsamic, garlic 30 min; grill dome-side-down first
  over direct medium heat 5 min; flip; cook 3–5 min. Internal ~82°C; gill juices
  concentrated.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep char marks; cap completely yielding; juices pooling; balsamic
  caramelised on dome surface.
- **Why this method works**: Balsamic marinade on the dome surface caramelises against
  the grill grates, creating a deep, lacquered crust; the 30-min marinade penetrates
  the cap's porous surface with garlic and acid flavour.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Portobello Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-portobello
- **Method**: Oil, salt, garlic rubbed on dome; grill dome-side-down 4–5 min; flip;
  fill gills with herb butter; grill 3–4 min more until butter melts. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grill marks on dome; herb butter melted into gills; cap soft.
- **Why this method works**: Herb butter filling the gills in the second half of cooking
  melts into the mushroom juices, creating a deeply flavoured pool of herb-butter
  mushroom jus in the cap; the grill's heat is sufficient to melt the butter without burning it.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad portobello (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-portobello
- **Method**: Pensla med olivolja och vitlök; grilla med toppen neråt 4–5 min;
  vänd; fyll med brie eller örtersmör; grilla 3 min till. ~82°C i hatten.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillränder på toppen; hatten mjuk; fyllningen smälter.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides present portobello with brie or
  herb butter filling as a standard summer BBQ option; the cheese melts into the
  mushroom's gill juices in the second grill phase.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet portobello (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-portobello
- **Method**: Olje, hvitløk, urter; grill topp-ned 4–5 min; snu; 3–4 min med urte-
  fyll. ~82°C inni hatten.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper; hatten myk; saftene samlet i gjellene.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian recipes present grilled portobello as a vegetarian
  main course; the standard preparation matches the international technique and the
  herb filling is typically Norwegian in its composition.
