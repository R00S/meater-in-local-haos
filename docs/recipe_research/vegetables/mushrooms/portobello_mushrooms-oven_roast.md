## Cut profile
Oven-roasting portobello mushrooms — whole caps, open gills exposed, with a simple
seasoning — transforms the raw, spongy mushroom into something dense, concentrated,
and deeply savoury. The key is roasting at high enough heat (200–220°C) that the
mushroom releases and then reabsorbs its own water before the external surfaces start
to caramelise. The internal temperature target is 82°C at the thickest part of the cap,
at which point the mushroom has fully softened, the gills are dark and glistening, and
the cap has lost about 40% of its original weight to evaporation.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Roasted Portobello Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Caps gill-side-up; drizzle with oil and season. Oven 220°C for 15–20 min.
  Probe at thickest part of cap: 82°C. The gills should be dark and glistening.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gills dark and glistening; cap soft and yielding; juices pooled
  in the gills; shrunken from original size.
- **Why this method works**: Gill-side-up roasting allows the juices to pool in the
  cap rather than running off; the concentrated mushroom liquor that pools in the
  gills is one of the most intensely flavoured things in the oven.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Portobello with Herbs (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Gill-side-up; fill gills with garlic, thyme, olive oil; oven 220°C for
  20 min. Internal 82°C; deeply roasted gills; garlic golden.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gills dark; garlic golden in the gills; cap yielding; strong herbal-
  mushroom aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The garlic and thyme placed in the gills cook in the
  pooling mushroom juices rather than dry-roasting; they become incredibly tender and
  flavourful, and their flavour infuses the mushroom from within.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Portobello Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Gill-side-up; butter, garlic, thyme in gills; oven 200°C for 15–18 min.
  Internal 82°C; butter melted into mushroom juices.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cap soft; butter and mushroom juices pooled in gills; thyme fragrant.
- **Why this method works**: Butter placed in the gills at the start of roasting melts
  into the mushroom's own juices as they pool, creating an extremely rich natural sauce
  that is simply spooned over the finished mushroom.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostade portobello (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostade-portobello
- **Method**: Hattar med lamellsidan upp; olja, timjan, vitlök; ugn 220°C i 15–20 min.
  ~82°C i tjockaste hatten.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Lamellerna mörka och glänsande; hatten mjuk; rik svampdoft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present roasted portobello as a
  vegetarian main; the gill-side-up technique is universal because the pooled juices
  are the most delicious part of the preparation.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-stuffed-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Fill gills with cream cheese, herbs, sun-dried tomatoes; oven 200°C fan
  for 15–20 min. Internal 82°C; filling set and golden at top.
- **Target internal temp**: **82°C** (mushroom cap)
- **Doneness cues**: Cap soft and yielding; filling set and slightly golden on top;
  cheese melted into mushroom juices at the gills.
- **Why this method works**: The cream cheese filling provides both flavour and moisture
  retention; the heat melts the cheese down into the gills, mixing with the pooling
  mushroom juices for a richer result.
