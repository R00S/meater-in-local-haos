# Portobello Mushrooms × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Portobello mushrooms (*Agaricus bisporus* mature) oven-roasted at 200–220 °C concentrate flavour dramatically as the high moisture content (≈ 90 %) evaporates. Placed gill-side-up on a sheet pan or in a dish, the caps act as natural cups that retain the concentrated cooking juices; gill-side-down produces a drier, more caramelised result. Whole caps take 20–25 minutes. The internal temperature at full doneness is 84–90 °C (183–194 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted through the convex cap skin into the thickest centre of the flesh, with the tip just above the gills; this avoids the hollow gill cavity which would read ambient air temperature.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Hóng Shāo Dà Mó Gū (烤大蘑菇 — Chinese Roasted Portobello with Doubanjiang)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012); oven-roasted preparation adapted from her stir-fried mushroom technique.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 22–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps (≈ 120 g each), stems removed, wiped clean
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tbsp Pixian doubanjiang (郫县豆瓣酱, chilli-bean paste) — or substitute: 2 tsp gochujang + ½ tsp salt
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (老抽)
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish
- 1 spring onion, thinly sliced, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Mix oil, doubanjiang, garlic, ginger, soy sauces, Shaoxing wine and sugar into a paste.
2. Spread this paste over the gill side of each cap. Place gill-side-up in a baking dish or on a foil-lined sheet pan.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the convex cap skin into the thickest centre of the flesh.
4. Roast 22–25 minutes until the caps are tender and the sauce has reduced to a glossy, concentrated glaze pooled in the gills.
5. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion. Serve over steamed rice. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (umami-rich, spicy, glossy, Chinese character).

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Oven-Roasted Portobello with Masala (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps, stems removed, wiped clean
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated ginger
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp garam masala
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 2 tsp tomato purée
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 15 g fresh coriander, to garnish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Combine oil, garlic, ginger, all spices, salt, tomato purée and lemon juice into a paste.
2. Spread the paste over the gill side of each cap. Place gill-side-up in a baking dish.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap top into the flesh centre.
4. Roast 22–25 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and the masala coating has caramelised slightly.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve with chapati. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (spiced, golden, Indian character).

### 3. Marcella Hazan — Grandi Funghi al Forno con Aglio e Prezzemolo (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1992)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 20–25 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps, wiped clean, stems removed
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, very finely sliced (NOT minced — Hazan specifies sliced so the pieces are visible and do not burn)
- 20 g (4 tbsp) flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) dry white wine
- Crusty Italian bread, to serve

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Brush a baking dish with 2 tbsp of the olive oil.
2. Place the mushroom caps gill-side-up in the dish. Drizzle remaining olive oil into the gills; season with salt and pepper. Pour the white wine into the bottom of the dish.
3. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the thickest flesh centre.
4. Roast 18–20 minutes. In the last 3–4 minutes, scatter the sliced garlic and parsley over the gills; return to oven briefly to warm and just slightly gold.
5. **Pull at 84–88 °C (183–190 °F).** Hazan: "The garlic must not darken." Serve with crusty bread to soak up the juices. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (garlic-parsley bright, wine-fragrant, Italian).

### 4. Deborah Madison — Oven-Roasted Portobello with Goat Cheese and Tapenade (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 22–25 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 large portobello caps (≈ 120–130 g each), stems removed
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 60 g black olive tapenade (jar or homemade)
- 100 g fresh goat cheese, crumbled
- 15 g fresh basil or flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Brush caps on both sides with olive oil; season with salt and pepper; scatter thyme.
2. Place gill-side-up in a baking dish. Insert the MEATER probe through the cap skin into the flesh centre.
3. Roast 15 minutes. Remove from oven; spread tapenade evenly over the gill side; scatter goat cheese. Return to oven for 7–10 more minutes until the cheese is slightly golden.
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Scatter fresh herbs. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (meaty, briny from tapenade, creamy from goat cheese).
