## Cut profile
Sliced or whole portobello mushrooms sautéed in a hot pan develop a concentrated,
almost steak-like savouriness that the more familiar smaller mushrooms cannot match.
The portobello's large cap releases more juice than a button mushroom, which means the
pan sauté must be done in stages at higher heat to evaporate the released water before
browning can begin. Internal temperature at perfect sautéed doneness: 82°C throughout
the thickest slice or cap piece — the same as for oven roasting or grilling.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Sautéed Portobello Mushrooms (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sauteed-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Sliced 1.5 cm; hot dry pan; oil added after the pan is hot. Single layer;
  cook undisturbed 3 min; toss; cook 3 min more. Salt only at end. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden-brown on sliced faces; shrunken significantly; no water
  pooling; intense meaty aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The dry-pan start technique is critical for portobello
  — their water content is higher than button mushrooms; starting dry and hot maximises
  evaporation before oil is added.

### 2. Thomas Keller — Sautéed Portobello (USA / Bouchon)
- **Source**: thomaskeller.com (mushroom sauté reference)
- **Method**: Caps whole or sliced; clarified butter at high heat; single layer; 3 min
  per side undisturbed; add garlic and thyme in last minute. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on contact face; shrunken; garlic golden; thyme fragrant.
- **Why this method works**: Keller's clarified butter high-heat approach for portobello
  is the same as for button mushrooms; the key difference is the size — thicker pieces
  need the full 3 min per side.

### 3. Ottolenghi — Pan-Fried Portobello (UK)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi)
- **Method**: Sliced portobello; olive oil; medium-high heat 4 min per side. Add
  garlic, lemon zest, parsley. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on sliced faces; garlic fragrant; lemon zest slightly
  caramelised.
- **Why this method works**: The lemon zest added with the garlic caramelises briefly
  in the hot pan before being pulled from the heat; this creates a slightly crisped
  lemon element that contrasts with the soft sautéed mushroom.

### 4. ICA — Stekta portobello-skivor (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/stekta-portobello
- **Method**: Skivade portobello; olja i het panna; 3–4 min per sida utan rörelse.
  Salt och vitlök i slutet. ~82°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbrun kontaktyta; minskat; mustig svampsmak.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present sliced portobello as a steak
  alternative in vegetarian cooking; the 3–4 min per side undisturbed approach
  is universally recommended across Nordic recipes.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Pan-Fried Portobello (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/pan-fried-portobello-mushrooms
- **Method**: Sliced; hot pan with oil + butter; 3 min per side. Add balsamic in last
  30 seconds. Internal ~82°C; caramelised glaze.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on both faces; balsamic glaze caramelised and sticky;
  completely tender.
- **Why this method works**: Balsamic added in the final 30 seconds caramelises against
  the already-hot mushroom surface, creating a sticky-sweet glaze that contrasts with
  the mushroom's savoury depth.
