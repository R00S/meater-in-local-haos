# Bell Peppers × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying bell peppers produces a result between oven roasting and sautéing: the
circulating hot air blisters the skin and caramelises the flesh faster than an oven,
but without the full-black charring of direct grill or broiler heat. The internal
temperature at doneness for halved or quartered air-fried peppers: 82–88°C — the
flesh is completely soft, the skin is blistered and beginning to char, and the
colour has deepened. The skin peels after a brief rest, though the skin is thinner
and less completely charred than from grilling.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Bell Peppers (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-bell-peppers
- **Method**: Halved and seeded; brush with oil; air fryer 200°C for 12–15 min,
  rotating once. Internal ~85°C; blistered skin, soft flesh.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin blistered with dark spots; flesh completely soft; sweet
  roasted aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Air fryer circulating heat blisters the skin from all
  angles simultaneously; the skin can be peeled after a brief rest for use as a
  classic roasted pepper preparation.

### 2. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Peppers (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-peppers
- **Method**: Strips or halves; oil; air fryer 200°C for 10–12 min, shaking halfway.
  Internal ~82–85°C; tender and beginning to blister.
- **Target internal temp**: **82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender; blistered surfaces; slightly collapsed; sweet and fragrant.
- **Why this method works**: Strips in the air fryer produce a sauté-like result faster
  than a pan; the 360° heat blisters all surfaces of each strip.

### 3. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade paprikor (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/paprika
- **Method**: Halverade; olja; airfryer 200°C i 12–15 min, vänd en gång.
  ~82–85°C inuti; blåst skinn, mjukt kött.
- **Target internal temp**: **82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Blåst skinn; köttet mjukt och sött; lätt att skala.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides present pepper roasting as one of
  the most practical air fryer uses; the result is usable for salads and toppings
  directly from the basket without the mess of gas-flame charring.

### 4. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Roasted Peppers (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-peppers
- **Method**: Halved peppers; brush with oil; air fryer 200°C for 12–15 min.
  Internal ~85°C; skin blistered; rest in bowl 10 min; peel.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin blistered; flesh silky; easily peeled after rest.
- **Why this method works**: Donna Hay presents the air fryer as the easiest method
  for roasted peppers in a domestic kitchen — no gas flame, no broiler smoke,
  and cleaner than oven roasting at very high temperatures.

### 5. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Peppers (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-peppers
- **Method**: Halved; 1 tbsp oil; air fryer 200°C for 15 min. Rest covered 5 min.
  Skin peels after rest. Internal ~85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin blistered; flesh soft; easily peeled; sweet smoky aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Serious Eats confirms the air fryer as a viable pepper-
  roasting method, particularly valuable for home cooks without gas hobs or broilers;
  the 15 min at 200°C produces enough blistering for easy skin removal.
