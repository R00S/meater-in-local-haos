## Cut profile
Grilling bell peppers over direct heat achieves the same charred skin and smoky sweetness
as oven roasting but with the added dimension of true smoke — from the char of the grill
grate, from burning fat and juices, and from wood or charcoal. The internal temperature
target is the same (88°C+ for fully charred and peeled) but the journey there is faster
and more aggressive. Whole peppers on a charcoal grill, turned until completely black on
all sides, are the definitive version of this preparation.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Charred Peppers (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-peppers
- **Method**: Whole peppers; grill over direct high heat, turning every 3–4 min until
  charred on all sides (15–20 min total). Internal 88–93°C. Rest in closed bag 10 min; peel.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–93°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely black on all sides; very soft when pressed; sweet smoke
  aroma; skin slips off after resting.
- **Why this method works**: Whole peppers on a hot grill develop the most complex smoky
  flavour because all surfaces char simultaneously; the bag rest creates intense steam
  that separates skin from flesh completely.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Peppers (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-peppers
- **Method**: Whole peppers over direct high heat; turn with tongs every 3 min; 12–15 min.
  Internal ~90°C. Rest in bowl with plastic wrap; peel after 10 min.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: All surfaces charred; flesh completely soft; strong smoke aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The 3-min turn intervals produce complete charring on 4–5 sides
  of each pepper; the bowl rest is as effective as a bag and easier to manage for multiple peppers.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Peppers (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-peppers
- **Method**: Halved peppers on BBQ grill cut-face-down over direct high heat 5–7 min.
  Flip; char skin side 3–4 min. Internal ~88°C. Rest covered.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Clear char marks; skin blistered; flesh soft; skin peels after rest.
- **Why this method works**: Halving and face-down grilling combines caramelisation of
  the cut surface with charring of the skin in one operation; the total time is shorter
  than grilling whole.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad paprika (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-paprika
- **Method**: Hela paprikor; direkt grillning på hög värme; vänd med tång var 3 min
  tills helt svarta; 12–18 min. ~88–90°C inuti. Vila i tätsluten skål 10 min; skala.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Hela ytan svart och blistrad; köttet mjukt; skalet lossnar.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides specify whole-pepper charring for
  roasted peppers intended for salads and antipasto; the same technique as Italian
  and American methods.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet paprika (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-paprika
- **Method**: Hele paprika; direkte varme; snu hvert 3. minutt til de er kulforskulte.
  ~88–90°C inni; hvile 10 min i lukket skål; skrell.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Kulforskulte; myk kjøtt; skallet løsner.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian summer grilling includes roasted peppers for
  sandwiches and salads; the technique is universal and the Norwegian recipes follow
  the same whole-charring approach as international guides.
