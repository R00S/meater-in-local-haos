# Bell Peppers × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Bell peppers (*Capsicum annuum*) oven-roasted at 220–230 °C follow the same skin-blistering trajectory as grilled peppers but with slower, more even heat penetration, and the option to roast slices or strips at lower temperatures for a tender-but-intact result. Whole peppers roasted at high heat until blackened are the classic preparation for peeling (identical to grilled, just slower); sliced peppers at 200–220 °C caramelise across their cut surfaces as their natural sugars concentrate. The internal temperature of a whole pepper when it is fully soft and the skin releases is 88–95 °C (190–203 °F); sliced peppers for eating as a dish (not for peeling) are done at 82–90 °C (180–194 °F). A MEATER probe is inserted through the shoulder of a whole pepper or into the thickest part of a slice.

## Styckesprofil

Paprika (*Capsicum annuum*) ugnsrostad vid 220–230 °C följer samma skin-blåsningsförlopp som grillad paprika men med långsammare, jämnare värmedistribution. 30–40 minuter hel. Klar när skinnet blåst och paprikapjäserna genommjuka. Skinn avlägsnas lätt efter ångning i förseglad påse.

## Source recipes

### 1. José Andrés — Pimientos Asados (Spanish / Catalan tradition)
**Source**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 40–45 min · **Rest**: 20 min (steaming)

**Ingredients**
- 4 large red bell peppers (pimientos rojos), left whole
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (Spanish, from Catalonia or Andalucia if possible)
- 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- Fine sea salt (flor de sal)
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 30 ml sherry vinegar (vinagre de Jerez), to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F). Place whole peppers in a heavy roasting tin or directly on the oven rack over a foil-lined tray to catch drips.
2. Insert the MEATER Plus probe through the shoulder of one pepper into the thickest part of the flesh wall, with the tip in the flesh (not penetrating to the seed cavity).
3. Roast 40–45 minutes, turning once at 20 minutes, until the skin is completely blistered and charred on all sides and the pepper has deflated noticeably.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Andrés notes the peppers should feel completely limp and soft when gently pressed — "they should give like a pillow."
5. Transfer to a bowl; cover tightly with cling film. Steam 20 minutes.
6. Peel, deseed, and tear into wide strips. Save all the juice in the bowl. Toss strips with their juice, olive oil, garlic, sherry vinegar and sea salt. Scatter parsley. Serve at room temperature or slightly warm. Final serving temperature: **room temperature** (sweet, silky, sherry-bright).

### 2. Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Peppers with Feta & Capers (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Ottolenghi: The Cookbook* (Ebury Press, 2008)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 35–40 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 red + 2 yellow bell peppers, halved, deseeded, left as halves
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Fine sea salt and black pepper
- 100 g (3½ oz) feta, crumbled
- 2 tbsp capers, rinsed
- 30 g black olives, pitted and halved
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 2 tsp lemon juice, to finish
- Fresh basil or flat-leaf parsley, to scatter

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Line a heavy sheet pan with baking parchment.
2. Place the pepper halves cut-side-up on the pan. Drizzle with olive oil; season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the flesh wall of one pepper half — slide it horizontally through the side wall so the tip is in the centre of the flesh.
4. Roast 25 minutes until the peppers are tender and the skin has blistered and darkened at the edges. Scatter feta, capers, olives and oregano into and around the pepper halves.
5. Return to oven for a further 10–12 minutes until the feta is golden at the edges.
6. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F).** The flesh should be fully tender and releasing juice; the feta should be golden. Squeeze lemon juice over; scatter herbs. Final serving temperature: **88–92 °C / 190–198 °F** (sweet, creamy-salty, herb-bright).

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Shimla Mirch Masala (Indian / North Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 3 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium red bell peppers (shimla mirch), halved lengthways and deseeded
- 3 tbsp sunflower oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 2 medium ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
- 1 tsp fine salt
- ½ tsp garam masala, to finish
- 15 g fresh coriander leaves, chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 200 °C (400 °F). Brush the pepper halves (inside and out) with 1 tbsp of the oil; season lightly with salt.
2. Place cut-side-up on a foil-lined sheet pan. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the flesh wall of one pepper half.
3. Roast 20–25 minutes until the peppers are tender and beginning to char at the edges.
4. Meanwhile, heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan; add cumin seeds and sizzle 10 s. Add onion; cook 5–6 min until golden. Add garlic, ginger; cook 1 min. Add coriander, turmeric, chilli powder; stir 30 s. Add tomatoes and salt; cook 5 min until saucy. Add garam masala; stir.
5. Spoon the masala generously into the roasted pepper halves in the last 5 minutes of roasting. Return to oven to warm through (5 min).
6. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Jaffrey's peppers should be tender and fragrant, the masala slightly bubbling in each half. Scatter coriander. Final serving temperature: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (warm-spiced, sweet-savoury).

### 4. Diana Kennedy — Chiles / Pimientos Asados (Mexican tradition)
**Source**: Diana Kennedy, *The Art of Mexican Cooking* (Bantam Books, 1989)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 15 min (sweating)

**Ingredients**
- 4 large red bell peppers (or poblano chillies — Kennedy uses whichever is sweetest and freshest)
- Neutral oil to brush
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp olive oil, to dress
- 1 tbsp lime juice
- Fine sea salt
- 15 g fresh epazote (or substitute flat-leaf parsley), roughly chopped

**Method**
1. Preheat oven to 230 °C (450 °F). Brush the whole peppers lightly with neutral oil.
2. Place on a foil-lined sheet pan directly under the upper heating element (or use the broil function). Insert the MEATER probe through the shoulder into the thickest flesh wall.
3. Roast 30–35 minutes, turning every 10 minutes, until the skin is blistered and blackened on all sides.
4. **Pull at 90–95 °C (194–203 °F).** Transfer to a plastic bag; seal and allow to sweat 15 minutes — Kennedy emphasises the bag method over the bowl-cling-film method as it produces more even skin separation.
5. Peel, deseed, and tear into strips. Dress with garlic, olive oil, lime juice and salt. Scatter epazote. Kennedy serves these at room temperature as a salsa or garnish.
6. Final serving temperature: **room temperature** (sweet-smoky, lime-bright, Mexican character).
