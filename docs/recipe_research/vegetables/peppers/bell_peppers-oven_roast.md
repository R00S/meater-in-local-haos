# Bell Peppers × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Roasting bell peppers — until the skin blisters and chars completely and the flesh
softens to a silky, sweet, smoky tenderness — is one of the transformations that defines
Mediterranean cooking. The internal temperature at perfect doneness is above 88°C
throughout, with the skin fully blistered and charred (the char on the skin is removed
after resting). The cook's probe is inserted into the thickest part of the wall; the
target is soft flesh, collapsed shape, and fully charred skin. The MEATER probe is
particularly useful here because it confirms doneness before the oven char goes too far.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted/Charred Bell Peppers (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/how-to-roast-peppers
- **Method**: Halve and seed, or roast whole. Halves cut-face-down at 230°C; whole
  peppers directly in 230°C oven or under the broiler. Roast until completely charred
  and blistered (15–20 min for halves). Internal 88–93°C. Rest in covered bowl to steam skin.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–93°C** (flesh soft and silky)
- **Doneness cues**: Skin completely black and blistered; flesh collapsed and very soft;
  steaming inside the bowl; skin peels easily after 10 min rest.
- **Why this method works**: The charred skin creates steam between itself and the flesh
  during the oven period; the bowl rest continues this steaming, loosening the skin
  to the point where it slips off with fingers.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Peppers with Capers (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Halved peppers; oven 220°C for 25–30 min until completely soft and charred.
  Internal ~88°C; peel when cool. Dress with capers, basil, anchovies.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin blistered and charred; flesh silky; strong sweet-smoky aroma.
- **Why this method works**: The caper-anchovy dressing applied to peeled roasted peppers
  at room temperature is the definitive Ottolenghi approach; the acidity from capers
  lifts the smoky sweetness of the roasted flesh.

### 3. Marcella Hazan — Peperoni Arrostiti (Italy / Essentials)
- **Source**: Marcella Hazan "Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking" (Knopf, 1992)
- **Method**: Whole peppers directly on gas flame or under broiler; char on all sides;
  rest in covered bowl; peel. Internal ~88°C throughout.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin fully black; flesh collapsed and very soft; sweet concentrated
  pepper aroma when the bowl is opened.
- **Why this method works**: Direct gas flame gives faster and more intense charring
  than an oven; the Italian tradition roasts whole peppers over the flame and the flesh
  is more concentrated in flavour than oven-halved variants.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsstekta paprikahalvor (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsstekta-paprikahalvor
- **Method**: Halverade; snittytan neråt; ugn 220°C i 20–25 min. ~88°C inuti;
  skalet blåst och kolnat; vila under lock 10 min.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skalet kolnat; köttet mjukt och silkeslen; skinnlösning lätt.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present oven-roasted peppers as a
  salad ingredient and pizza topping; the half-and-face-down method is the standard
  Nordic approach for home kitchen use.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Red Peppers (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-red-peppers
- **Method**: Halved and deseeded; oven 200°C fan 25 min until charred. Internal ~85–88°C.
  Rest covered 10 min; peel. Use in sauces, salads, or on bruschetta.
- **Target internal temp**: **85–88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Charred skin; silky flesh; sweet-smoky aroma; skin slips off after rest.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven at 200°C produces even blistering across the
  entire pepper half; the 25 min is slightly longer than for static oven due to
  the lower temperature.
