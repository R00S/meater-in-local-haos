# Bell Peppers × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Bell peppers (*Capsicum annuum*) sautéed in strips or rings are one of the most globally versatile vegetable preparations, appearing in Chinese stir-fries, Indian sabzis, Spanish pisto, French ratatouille, Korean bokkeum, and beyond. The sweet, watery flesh softens quickly in a hot, oiled pan; at 180–200 °C contact temperature, the cut surfaces caramelise while the interior remains tender-crisp to soft depending on cooking time. The target internal temperature for a tender-but-intact sauté is 80–88 °C (176–190 °F): at 80–82 °C the strips are tender-crisp (still with a slight bite); at 85–88 °C they are fully softened but hold their shape. A MEATER probe inserted horizontally into the thickest part of a strip (or the wall of a halved pepper) reads representative flesh temperature; carryover is negligible due to the high water content.

## Source recipes

### 1. Fuchsia Dunlop — Qīngjiāo Chǎo Ròusī (青椒炒肉丝 — Green Pepper and Pork Stir-Fry, vegetable adaptation)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice: Simple Chinese Home Cooking* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 5–6 min · **Rest**: 0 min (serve immediately)

**Ingredients**
- 500 g green bell peppers (or a mix of green, red and yellow), cut into long strips (julienned)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (peanut or sunflower)
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp finely grated fresh ginger
- 1 tsp light soy sauce (生抽)
- ½ tsp caster sugar
- ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, to finish
- Fine sea salt to taste

**Method**
1. Pat the pepper strips completely dry with paper towels. Wet vegetables steam instead of stir-frying.
2. Heat a wok or wide frying pan over maximum heat until it begins to smoke. Add the oil; when it shimmers instantly, add the pepper strips.
3. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through one of the larger strips near the centre of the wok, with the tip at the geometric centre of the strip.
4. Stir-fry on maximum heat, tossing constantly, for 2 minutes. The strips should colour and blister at the edges.
5. Add the garlic and ginger; stir-fry 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. Add soy sauce and sugar; toss for a further 30–60 seconds. The liquid should evaporate almost instantly in the wok heat.
7. **Pull at 80–82 °C (176–180 °F).** Dunlop stresses the wok-hay (镬气) result — strips should be slightly blistered at the edges, crisp-tender in texture, and intensely flavourful without being soggy.
8. Off the heat, drizzle sesame oil; toss once. Final serving temperature: **80–82 °C / 176–180 °F** — serve immediately with steamed rice.

### 2. Madhur Jaffrey — Shimla Mirch aur Aloo (Indian / North Indian sauté)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 12–15 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 400 g red and green bell peppers, cut into 2 cm squares
- 300 g waxy potatoes, boiled until just tender, peeled and cut into 2 cm cubes
- 3 tbsp sunflower or mustard oil
- 1 tsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion, sliced into half-moons
- 2 garlic cloves, finely minced
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- ½ tsp garam masala, to finish
- 2 tbsp chopped fresh coriander leaves, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat oil in a wide karahi or sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds; sizzle 10 seconds until they pop and darken.
2. Add onion; sauté 5–6 minutes until golden-brown.
3. Add garlic; cook 30 seconds. Add turmeric, ground coriander and chilli powder; stir 20 seconds.
4. Add the bell pepper squares; toss to coat with the spice mixture.
5. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest piece of bell pepper near the centre of the pan.
6. Sauté over medium-high heat 5–6 minutes, stirring frequently, until the peppers are tender and slightly blistered at the edges.
7. Add the par-boiled potato cubes; toss gently; cook 2–3 more minutes until everything is heated through and lightly crisped.
8. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Jaffrey targets peppers that are fully cooked but still holding their shape, infused with the warm-spiced masala.
9. Off the heat, sprinkle garam masala; garnish with coriander. Final serving temperature: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, golden, hearty).

### 3. José Andrés — Pisto Manchego (Spanish tradition)
**Source**: José Andrés & Richard Wolffe, *Vegetables Unleashed* (Clarkson Potter, 2019)
**Serves**: 4–6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 30–35 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 red + 2 green bell peppers, cut into 2 cm squares
- 2 medium courgettes, sliced into 1 cm half-moons
- 2 medium tomatoes, roughly chopped
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 60 ml (4 tbsp) extra-virgin olive oil (Spanish, preferably Picual or Hojiblanca)
- 1 tsp sweet smoked paprika (pimentón de la Vera)
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 4 eggs, to poach in the pisto (optional serving)
- 5 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, deep sauté pan over medium heat. Add onion; cook 8–10 minutes until soft and golden.
2. Add garlic; cook 1 minute. Add peppers and salt; toss to coat.
3. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest piece of bell pepper near the centre of the pan.
4. Sauté the peppers over medium heat 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and sweetly caramelised.
5. Add courgettes and paprika; cook 5–6 minutes. Add tomatoes; cook 8–10 minutes more until the pisto is thick and saucy and all vegetables are very tender.
6. **Pull at 85–88 °C (185–190 °F)** for the peppers — they should be melting-soft, flavour-concentrated.
7. Season; scatter parsley. If serving with poached eggs, make wells in the pisto and crack in eggs; cover and cook 3–4 minutes. Final serving temperature: **85–88 °C / 185–190 °F** (sweet, silky, paprika-scented Spanish stew).

### 4. Julia Child — Ratatouille (French / Provençal tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 6–8 · **Prep**: 20 min (+ salting) · **Cook**: 60–80 min total · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g red and green bell peppers, deseeded and sliced into 4 cm × 1 cm strips
- 500 g eggplant, unpeeled, cut into 1 cm rounds, then into strips
- 500 g courgettes, cut into 1 cm rounds
- 700 g ripe tomatoes, peeled, deseeded and roughly chopped
- 1 large onion, thinly sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, mashed to a paste
- 6 tbsp olive oil (divided)
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or ½ tsp dried thyme)
- 1 large bay leaf
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley
- 1 tsp fine sea salt and freshly ground pepper

**Method**
1. Salt the eggplant and courgettes; drain 30 minutes; press dry. Sauté the eggplant separately in 2 tbsp oil until lightly browned, 4–5 minutes; remove. Repeat with courgettes (3–4 min). Sauté onion in 2 tbsp oil until tender (8–10 min); add peppers, cook 5 min.
2. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest pepper strip near the centre of the pan.
3. In a heavy casserole, layer one-third of the tomatoes, then the sautéed onion and peppers, then the eggplant and courgette. Repeat layers. Top with remaining tomatoes. Add garlic, thyme, bay, and remaining oil.
4. Season. Cover and simmer over very low heat 10 minutes. Uncover; tilt pan to baste. Continue simmering 20–30 minutes more, uncovered, until most liquid has evaporated and the vegetables are meltingly tender.
5. **Pull at 88–92 °C (190–198 °F)** — Child's ratatouille is cooked long until "the vegetables are very tender, thoroughly blended in flavour, and the sauce is thick." Final serving temperature: **warm, or room temperature** — Child notes it improves if made a day ahead.
