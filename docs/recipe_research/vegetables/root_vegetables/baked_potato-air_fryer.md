# Baked Potato × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Whole baked potato (*Solanum tuberosum*) in the air fryer achieves a crispy, salty skin and fluffy interior in 35–45 minutes at 200 °C — significantly faster than the conventional oven (60–90 minutes) and with superior skin crispness due to the circulating hot air. A medium-to-large potato (200–250 g) takes 35–40 minutes; a very large potato (350+ g) takes 45–50 minutes. Pull temperature: 96–98 °C (205–208 °F) at the thickest centre. A MEATER probe is inserted along the long axis into the thickest central point of the potato before air frying begins. At 96–98 °C the starch granules are fully gelatinised and the interior is completely fluffy.

## Source recipes

### 1. Madhur Jaffrey — Masala Air-Fried Baked Potato (Indian tradition)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *At Home with Madhur Jaffrey* (Alfred A. Knopf, 2010); spiced baked potato adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium potatoes (≈ 200–220 g each), scrubbed clean; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp fine salt; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder; ½ tsp ground cumin; ½ tsp ground coriander; *filling:* 2 tbsp plain yoghurt; 1 tsp garam masala; fresh coriander; lemon juice; green chilli, sliced

**Method**
1. Pierce potatoes with a fork all over. Mix oil, salt, chilli, cumin and coriander; rub over potatoes.
2. Insert the MEATER probe along the long axis into the thickest centre.
3. Air fry at **200 °C for 38–42 minutes**, turning once at 20 minutes.
4. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split the hot potato; fill with yoghurt, garam masala, lemon juice, coriander and green chilli. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (fluffy, crispy-spiced skin, tangy filling).

### 2. Harumi Kurihara — Japanese Air-Fried Potato with Soy Butter (Japanese tradition)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007); Japanese butter-soy potato adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium potatoes (≈ 200 g each), scrubbed; 2 tbsp neutral oil; 1 tsp fine salt; *topping:* 20 g cold unsalted butter; 1 tbsp soy sauce; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds; spring onion

**Method**
1. Pierce potatoes; rub with oil and salt. Insert MEATER probe along long axis into the thickest centre.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 38–42 min**, turning once.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split and top with butter (it melts into the potato); drizzle soy sauce; scatter sesame seeds and spring onion. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (fluffy, butter-soy-fragrant, Japanese).

---

### 3. Deborah Madison — Classic Air-Fried Baked Potato with Soured Cream (American vegetarian tradition)
**Source**: Deborah Madison, *Vegetable Literacy* (Ten Speed Press, 2013); baked potato method adapted for air fryer.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 Russet potatoes (≈ 200–250 g each); 2 tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp flaked sea salt; *toppings:* 120 ml soured cream; 30 g chives; Cheddar, grated; freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Pierce potatoes; rub with oil and salt. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 40–45 min**, turning once.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; top with soured cream, chives, Cheddar, black pepper. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (fluffy, crispy skin, American classic).

---

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Air-Fried Potato with Za'atar Labneh (Middle Eastern / Israeli tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); spiced baked potato adaptation.
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 38–42 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 medium potatoes; 2 tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp salt; *topping:* 120 g labneh; 1 tbsp za'atar; 1 tbsp olive oil; 30 g pomegranate seeds; 10 g fresh mint

**Method**
1. Pierce potatoes; rub with oil and salt. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Air fry at **200 °C for 38–42 min**, turning once.
3. **Pull at 96–98 °C (205–208 °F).** Split; top with labneh, za'atar, olive oil, pomegranate seeds and mint. Final serving temperature: **96–98 °C / 205–208 °F** (fluffy, creamy labneh, za'atar-bright, jewel-beautiful).
