## Cut profile
The air fryer baked potato is not a compromise — it is a genuinely superior result for
the skin. The rapid circulating hot air desiccates the potato's exterior far more
efficiently than a conventional oven, producing a skin so dry and crispy it shatters
at the first cut. The interior still needs to reach 96–98°C for fully gelatinised starch,
and the smaller air-fryer cavity means a medium potato (200–250 g) achieves this in
35–45 minutes — significantly faster than oven baking. The probe reads the centre of
the longest axis, and the air fryer's reliability means the target is hit consistently.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Baked Potato (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-baked-potato
- **Method**: Pierce potato 8 times with a fork; toss in 1 tsp oil and coarse salt.
  Air fryer at 200°C / 400°F, 35–45 min flipping halfway. Pull at internal 98°C.
  Cut immediately; serve with preferred toppings.
- **Target internal temp**: 205°F / **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin is extremely crispy — noticeably crispier than oven-baked;
  interior steams visibly when cut open.
- **Why this method works**: ATK testing found the air fryer produces the best baked
  potato skin of any method; the rapid air circulation removes surface moisture
  that a conventional oven's static hot air takes twice as long to remove.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Baked Potato (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-baked-potato
- **Method**: Pat potato dry; brush with oil; season with flaky salt. Air fryer 200°C,
  40–50 min depending on size, flipping once at 20 min. Internal 96–98°C when done.
- **Target internal temp**: 210°F / **96–98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin crackles; the potato compresses slightly when squeezed
  through a towel; fork goes in with no resistance.
- **Why this method works**: The Serious Eats test confirms the skin advantage of
  air frying; larger potatoes (300 g+) benefit from the 40–50 min range; probe
  placement at the exact geometric centre gives the most reliable reading.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Jacket Potato (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-jacket-potato
- **Method**: Pierce potato all over; rub with oil and salt. Air fryer 200°C / 400°F
  for 35–40 min (medium potato). Check internal; aim for 98°C. Rest 5 min before
  cutting.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tap the skin — it should sound and feel hollow; the skin should
  be visibly dry and slightly wrinkled.
- **Why this method works**: UK food media has enthusiastically adopted the air fryer
  jacket potato; the BBC Good Food test confirms 35–40 min at 200°C is the right
  window for a standard UK baking potato (Maris Piper or King Edward).

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad bakad potatis (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/luftfriterad-bakad-potatis
- **Method**: Stick potatis med gaffel 8 gånger; pensla med olja och salt.
  Airfryer 200°C i 35–45 min, vänd halvvägs. Kärntemperatur 98°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skalet är torrt, krispy och ljust brunat; sticks med kniv utan
  motstånd.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides recommend the baked potato as
  the entry-level recipe for air fryer use; the 200°C setting and 35–45 min window
  are calibrated for standard Nordic yellow potatoes (similar in size and starch
  content to UK Maris Piper).

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Baked Potato (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-baked-potato
- **Method**: Season generously; pierce. Air fryer 200°C for 35–40 min. Internal
  temp 98°C; rest 5 min then split open and add desired toppings.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Squeeze gently through a cloth — the potato should give slightly,
  indicating a fully cooked interior; the skin should be dry to the touch.
- **Why this method works**: Australian food media has embraced the air fryer as a
  kitchen essential; Donna Hay's approach follows international consensus on the
  98°C target and highlights the skin texture advantage over the conventional oven.
