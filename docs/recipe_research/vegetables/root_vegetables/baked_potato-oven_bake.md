# Baked Potato × Oven Bake — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The baked potato is one of the most studied single ingredients in food science:
its transformation from raw, dense starch to fluffy interior with crackling skin
is a precise thermodynamic event. The potato's starch gelatinises progressively from
the outside in as the internal temperature climbs; at 96–99°C the centre is fully
cooked, the skin is dry enough to crisp, and the interior is light and steaming.
The MEATER probe inserted through the potato's longest axis at its centre gives the
most reliable reading — and for a dish this simple, the probe is the only way to
guarantee the interior is fully done without the guesswork of a fork test.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — The Best Baked Potatoes (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/the-best-baked-potatoes
- **Method**: Russet potatoes; rub with oil and coarse salt; pierce 6–8 times.
  Oven at 220°C / 425°F directly on the rack (not foil — foil traps steam and softens
  the skin). Bake 45–60 min until skin is crisp and internal temp reads 98°C.
- **Target internal temp**: 210°F / **98°C** (fully gelatinised starch)
- **Doneness cues**: Skin is dry, crispy, salty; a sharp knife meets no resistance;
  the potato feels hollow when squeezed through a towel.
- **Why this method works**: Direct rack contact dries the skin from below while the
  oven air dries it from above; skipping foil is the critical step — foil steams
  the skin into leathery toughness regardless of internal temperature.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Perfect Baked Potato (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/perfect-baked-potato
- **Method**: Brush with oil; dip in flaky sea salt. Direct oven rack at 200°C /
  400°F; bake 50–60 min. ATK specifies internal temp of 205°F / **96°C** as the
  target for "perfectly fluffy, not gummy" interior.
- **Target internal temp**: 205°F / **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Centre yields to gentle finger pressure; skin is dry and salty;
  insert a paring knife with zero resistance.
- **Why this method works**: The 96°C target gives a starch that is fully gelatinised
  without becoming gluey — ATK found that potatoes pulled at 96°C had a
  noticeably lighter, more airy texture than those pulled at 88°C (underdone starch).

### 3. Heston Blumenthal — Twice-Baked Potato / The Perfect Jacket Potato (UK)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/wordofmouth/2011/jan/13/heston-blumenthal-jacket-potato (Guardian)
- **Method**: Oven 180°C; bake until internal reads 99°C (approximately 1 h). Remove;
  cut open immediately to release steam; scoop, season, and return hollowed shell to
  200°C for 10–15 min for maximum crispiness. Internal 99°C ensures complete starch
  conversion.
- **Target internal temp**: 210°F / **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Steam should pour out when cut immediately after removal;
  interior should be dry and floury, never sticky.
- **Why this method works**: Heston's double-bake technique is designed around reaching
  99°C and then exploiting the steam release — allowing the interior to dry slightly
  before the second skin-crisping phase.

### 4. ICA — Bakad potatis i ugn (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/bakad-potatis
- **Method**: Storbakad potatis; pensla med olja och salt. Ugn 200°C i 50–60 min
  direkt på gallret. Kärntemperatur 98°C; skalet ska vara krispigt.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skalet är torrt och krispy; gaffeln möter inget motstånd.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish baked potato recipes position the 200°C temperature
  as a reliable home-oven setting that achieves both interior doneness and a dry
  crispy skin without requiring the higher temperature of some US recipes.

### 5. Nigella Lawson — Jacket Potato (UK / How to Eat)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/jacket-potato
- **Method**: Oven 220°C; pierce generously; rub with vegetable oil; sprinkle with
  salt; bake directly on rack 1 h until the skin feels dry and crisp and a skewer
  inserted at the centre meets no resistance. Internal temp: 98–99°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **98–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: The skewer test — zero resistance throughout is the indicator;
  the skin sounds hollow when flicked.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's approach emphasises the sound and feel of the
  potato over a thermometer; for MEATER-probe use, 98–99°C is the instrument-based
  translation of her "no resistance" and "hollow sound" tests.
