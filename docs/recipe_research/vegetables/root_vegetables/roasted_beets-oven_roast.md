# Roasted Beets × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Roasting a whole beet in foil is one of the few cooking methods where the vegetable
genuinely improves itself by enclosure: the steam generated inside the foil packet
cooks the interior to full softness (93–99°C internal) while the skin loosens to the
point where it slips off with a paper towel. The flavour of the roasted beet has no
equivalent in any other preparation — the natural sugar concentrates, the slightly
earthy geosmin compounds mellow, and the deep purple-red colour becomes almost
luminous. This is a vegetable that benefits from patience: a large beet needs 60–75
minutes at 200°C and the probe cannot be rushed.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Beets (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-beets
- **Method**: Wrap each beet individually in foil after rubbing with oil and salt.
  Oven at 200°C / 400°F; roast until probe at centre reads 93–99°C (45–75 min depending
  on size). Unwrap; let cool 10 min; rub off skin with paper towel.
- **Target internal temp**: **210°F / 99°C** (fully soft, no resistance)
- **Doneness cues**: Foil packet is fully puffed (full steam build-up). A skewer
  inserted through the foil meets zero resistance. Skin slips off when rubbed.
- **Why this method works**: The foil packet creates a steam environment that cooks
  the beet from all sides simultaneously, eliminating the hard centre that
  beets cooked without foil often have. Probe through the foil gives an accurate
  reading without opening the packet.

### 2. Samin Nosrat — Salt Fat Acid Heat Beets (USA / Salt Fat Acid Heat)
- **Source**: saltfatacidheat.com (roasted beet technique)
- **Method**: Foil wrap; oil, salt, thyme, orange peel. Oven 190°C until completely
  tender — "slide a paring knife in and it should meet no resistance whatsoever."
  Internal 93–99°C. Cool in foil.
- **Target internal temp**: **93–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Zero knife resistance; the beet is soft throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Samin's approach adds aromatics inside the foil packet
  which infuse the beet's porous surface during cooking; the lower temperature (190°C
  vs 200°C) extends the cooking time and intensifies the sweet flavour concentration.

### 3. Ottolenghi — Roasted Beets with Yoghurt (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column) + Plenty
- **Method**: Foil-wrapped; 200°C for 1–1.5 h depending on size. Internal 95–99°C.
  Peel after cooling; slice or wedge; serve with yoghurt, dill, walnuts.
- **Target internal temp**: **95–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely tender; skin tears away cleanly; interior colour is
  uniform deep purple with no pale undercooked centre.
- **Why this method works**: Ottolenghi's classic pairing of roasted beet and yoghurt
  requires the beet to be perfectly soft — any firm centre would be noticeable when
  cut into slices or wedges. The foil packet is non-negotiable for this purpose.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsstekta rödbetor (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsstekta-rodbetor
- **Method**: Inslagna i folie med olja och salt; ugn 200°C i 50–70 min beroende
  på storlek. Kärntemperatur 95–99°C. Skalet lossnar lätt efter vilotid.
- **Target internal temp**: **95–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Foliepaketet är puffat och varmt; gaffeln möter inget motstånd.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides treat foil-wrapped roasted beets as
  a standard technique; the 200°C temperature is consistent with international practice
  and the 50–70 min window suits Nordic beet sizes.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Foil-Roasted Beets (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/foil-roasted-beets
- **Method**: Wrap individually in foil with a drizzle of oil; oven 200°C for 45–60 min
  (medium beets). Probe through foil at centre; pull at 98°C. Rest in foil 10 min.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: A skewer passed through the foil finds no resistance; skin wrinkles
  and lifts when the foil is opened.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food positions the foil-roast method as the
  definitive beet preparation for British home cooks; the 45–60 min window suits
  the medium-sized red beet common in UK supermarkets.
