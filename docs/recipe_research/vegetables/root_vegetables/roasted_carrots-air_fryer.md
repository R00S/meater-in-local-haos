# Roasted Carrots × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying concentrates the carrot's natural sweetness faster than any oven method —
the rapid circulating hot air simultaneously dehydrates the cut surfaces and caramelises
the exposed sugars in a 15–20 minute window that takes 30–35 minutes in a conventional
oven. The smaller batch size of an air fryer basket (single layer is essential) means
this is the method for 2–4 servings rather than a side dish for eight, but within those
constraints it produces caramelised edges and tender centres that are genuinely superior
to any method that crowds the carrots.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Roasted Carrots (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-carrots
- **Method**: Cut carrots into batons or coins (1.5–2 cm thick). Toss with 1 tbsp oil
  and a pinch of salt. Air fryer 200°C / 400°F in single layer; cook 15–20 min,
  shaking basket every 7 min, until golden and tender. Internal ~90°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C** (tender throughout)
- **Doneness cues**: Golden edges; soft when pressed; slightly caramelised cut surfaces.
- **Why this method works**: Single-layer cooking in the air fryer is non-negotiable
  for carrots — overcrowding causes steaming instead of roasting, losing the
  caramelisation that is the entire point of this method.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Glazed Carrots (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-glazed-carrots
- **Method**: Carrot batons; toss with oil, brown sugar, pinch of cumin.
  Air fryer 190°C for 15–18 min, shaking twice. Glaze with honey in the last 3 min.
  Internal ~88–90°C; edges caramelised.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Sugars on cut surfaces are golden-amber; batons are flexible
  but hold their shape.
- **Why this method works**: The smaller air fryer cavity creates slightly more humid
  air than a full-size oven, which helps glaze the carrot surface before
  the final honey addition caramelises into a lacquer.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Roasted Carrots (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-carrots
- **Method**: Halved carrots; toss with oil, honey, thyme. Air fryer 200°C for
  15–18 min, tossing once. Internal ~90°C; golden and tender.
- **Target internal temp**: **~90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden, slightly caramelised surface; tender when poked.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food testing found the air fryer produces
  equivalent caramelisation to an oven in roughly half the time; halved carrots
  fit well in the air fryer basket and have more surface area for browning.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade morötter (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/luftfriterade-morotter
- **Method**: Morötter i batonger; blanda med olja, honung, salt. Airfryer 200°C i
  15–20 min, skaka en gång i mitten. ~90°C inuti; gyllene yta.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun yta; mjuka vid tryck; karamelliserade kanter.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer recipes recommend carrots as one of the
  most successful vegetable results; the smaller Nordic carrot varieties reach the
  90°C internal target faster than larger US or Australian varieties.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Honey-Glazed Carrots (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-honey-carrots
- **Method**: Halved baby carrots or carrot batons; toss with oil, honey, cumin.
  Air fryer 200°C for 12–15 min, tossing once. Internal ~88–90°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised glaze; tender but holding shape; edges slightly charred.
- **Why this method works**: Baby carrots in an air fryer at 200°C develop a
  deep caramelised surface much faster than in an oven because of their smaller
  diameter; Donna Hay's cumin addition is specific to the concentrated flavour
  intensity of air-fried vegetables.
