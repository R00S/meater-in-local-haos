## Cut profile
Roasting transforms a carrot from a crunchy raw vegetable into something almost
confectionery: the natural sugars caramelise against the hot pan, the cell walls
soften, and the flavour concentrates into an intense sweetness that raw carrots
only hint at. The internal temperature for fully roasted carrots — where the
flesh is tender throughout but not collapsed — is around 88–93°C. Below 82°C the
centre is still firm; above 98°C the carrot begins to lose its structural integrity.
The probe placed at the thickest point of the thickest carrot in the tray gives
the most conservative (safest) reading.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Carrots (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-carrots
- **Method**: Halve carrots lengthwise; toss with oil, salt, a pinch of sugar.
  Oven at 230°C / 450°F on a pre-heated baking sheet (for immediate caramelisation
  on contact). Roast 20–25 min until browned and tender.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C** (tender with slight resistance at centre)
- **Doneness cues**: Cut surfaces are deeply caramelised (dark amber patches);
  a paring knife passes through with no resistance but the carrot holds its shape.
- **Why this method works**: The pre-heated baking sheet causes immediate caramelisation
  on the cut face, concentrating sugars before the interior softens; the high
  temperature (230°C) balances browning against doneness time.

### 2. Ottolenghi / Yotam Ottolenghi — Roasted Carrots with Harissa (UK / Plenty)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2011 (Ottolenghi column) + Plenty (cookbook)
- **Method**: Carrots left whole or halved; toss with olive oil and spices. Oven 220°C
  for 30–40 min; flip once at 20 min. The carrots should colour deeply and be
  tender throughout.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88–93°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep amber colour on cut surfaces; slightly shrivelled at the
  thin ends; paring knife passes through with no resistance.
- **Why this method works**: Whole or halved carrots roast more slowly than chunks,
  giving a longer window for caramelisation to develop; the slightly lower peak
  temperature (220°C vs Kenji's 230°C) suits olive oil's lower smoke point.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Honey-Roasted Carrots (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/honey-roasted-carrots
- **Method**: Batons or halved; toss with olive oil, honey, thyme. Oven 200°C for
  35–40 min, stirring once. The honey caramelises into a glaze in the last 10 min.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–93°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Glossy caramelised glaze; tips are slightly charred; soft
  throughout when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: The honey addition lowers the effective caramelisation
  temperature of the surface, so a lower oven (200°C) produces similar browning
  to a plain carrot at 220°C; the flavour depth from honey caramelisation is
  qualitatively different from plain sugar caramelisation.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsstekta morötter (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsstekta-morotter
- **Method**: Dela morötter på längden; blanda med olivolja, salt och honung.
  Ugn 200°C i 25–30 min. Kärntemperatur ~90°C; ytan är gyllenbrun.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun skuren yta; mjuka hela vägen igenom.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend halved carrots at 200°C
  for a reliable everyday result; the 25–30 min window suits most Nordic root
  vegetable sizes.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Honey-Glazed Roasted Carrots (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/honey-glazed-roasted-carrots
- **Method**: Toss with oil, honey, wholegrain mustard. Oven 200°C / fan 180°C for
  30–35 min until tender and caramelised. Internal 88–90°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Glossy caramelised surface; tips browning; soft when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: Fan circulation at 180°C dries the surface more efficiently
  than a static 200°C oven, accelerating caramelisation without requiring a
  higher temperature that would risk burning the honey glaze.
