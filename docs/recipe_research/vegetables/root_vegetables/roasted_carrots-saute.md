# Roasted Carrots × Saute — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Carrots (*Daucus carota*) sautéed in a hot pan — whether as Chinese stir-fried matchsticks, Japanese *kimpira gobo*-style glazed rounds, Indian tempering with spices, or French *carottes glacées* — develop a sweet, lightly caramelised character while retaining their structural texture. Carrots should be cut thinly (coins, matchsticks, julienne) for sautéing — 3–5 mm thick — so the centre cooks through before the surface over-colours. Pull temperature: 82–88 °C (180–190 °F) for tender-crisp glazed carrots. MEATER probe inserted along the long axis of the thickest piece (or into the densest pile).

## Source recipes

### 1. Harumi Kurihara — Kimpira Ninjin (きんぴらにんじん — Japanese Glazed Carrot Matchsticks)
**Source**: Harumi Kurihara, *Harumi's Japanese Home Cooking* (DK, 2007)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 6–8 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g carrots, peeled and cut into 5 cm matchsticks (julienne); 2 tbsp sesame oil; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; 1 tbsp mirin; 1 tsp caster sugar; ½ tsp dried red chilli flakes; 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat sesame oil in a wide, heavy pan over medium-high. Add carrot matchsticks; stir-fry 3–4 minutes.
2. Insert MEATER probe into the densest pile. Add soy sauce, mirin, sugar and chilli; toss vigorously and reduce the heat to medium. Cook 2–3 more minutes, tossing, until the glaze coats the carrots.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Scatter sesame seeds. Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (tender-crisp, glossy, sweet-soy, Japanese kimpira character).

### 2. Fuchsia Dunlop — Chǎo Húluóbo Sī (炒胡萝卜丝 — Chinese Stir-Fried Carrot Shreds)
**Source**: Fuchsia Dunlop, *Every Grain of Rice* (W. W. Norton, 2012)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 4–5 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g carrots, peeled and cut into fine julienne; 2 tbsp neutral oil; 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced; 1 tsp grated ginger; 1 tbsp light soy sauce; ½ tsp caster sugar; ½ tsp toasted sesame oil; spring onion and sesame seeds to finish

**Method**
1. Heat wok to maximum until smoking. Add oil; add carrots. Insert MEATER probe into the densest pile.
2. Stir-fry over maximum heat 3–4 minutes until carrots soften and edges begin to caramelise. Add garlic and ginger (20 s); add soy sauce and sugar; toss 30 s.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Drizzle sesame oil; scatter spring onion and sesame seeds. Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (tender-crisp, soy-ginger-fragrant, vivid orange).

---

### 3. Madhur Jaffrey — Gajar ki Sabzi (Indian Sautéed Carrots with Mustard Seeds)
**Source**: Madhur Jaffrey, *Madhur Jaffrey's Indian Cookery* (BBC Books, 1982)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g carrots, peeled and cut into 4 mm coins; 2 tbsp sunflower oil; 1 tsp whole mustard seeds; 10 fresh curry leaves; ½ tsp turmeric; 1 tsp ground coriander; ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli; ½ tsp fine salt; 2 tbsp freshly grated coconut (optional); lemon juice; fresh coriander

**Method**
1. Heat oil over medium-high; mustard seeds (pop 10 s); curry leaves (sizzle 5 s); add carrots with turmeric and coriander. Insert MEATER probe.
2. Sauté 7–8 min, stirring every 2 min. Season with chilli, salt; cook 1 more minute.
3. **Pull at 82–86 °C (180–187 °F).** Scatter coconut if using; squeeze lemon; scatter coriander. Final serving: **82–86 °C / 180–187 °F** (spiced, fragrant, South Indian).

---

### 4. Julia Child — Carottes Glacées (French tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, Louisette Bertholle & Simone Beck, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Vol. I* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 18–22 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g carrots, peeled, cut into 4 cm batons or turned into ovals (*tournés*); 30 g (2 tbsp) unsalted butter; 1 tbsp caster sugar; ½ tsp fine salt; 125 ml (½ cup) water; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Combine carrots, butter, sugar, salt and water in a medium pan with a lid. Bring to a boil.
2. Insert MEATER probe into the thickest carrot baton.
3. Simmer partially covered over medium heat 12–15 minutes. Remove lid; continue cooking, shaking the pan, until the liquid reduces completely to a golden glaze (5–7 more minutes).
4. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Scatter parsley. Child: "The carrots should be tender and coated in a shiny, buttery glaze — no excess liquid should remain." Final serving: **86–90 °C / 187–194 °F** (glossy, buttery-sweet, classic French *carottes glacées*).
