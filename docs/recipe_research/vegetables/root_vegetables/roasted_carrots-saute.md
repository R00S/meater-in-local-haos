# Roasted Carrots × Sauté — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Sautéed carrots are the stovetop expression of everything roasting does with dry heat —
but in a pan, with fat, in direct contact with the Maillard-hot surface. The key is
patience: a heavy pan over medium-high heat, a single layer of carrot pieces, and the
discipline not to stir too often. The caramelisation happens at the pan-contact surface,
and each flip reveals a new golden face. Internal temperature of ~88°C marks when the
carrot is cooked through to tender — sautéed carrots at 82°C are still firm at the centre.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Sautéed Carrots with Butter and Herbs (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/sauteed-carrots-butter
- **Method**: Coin-cut or batons; large heavy skillet over medium-high heat.
  1 tbsp butter + 1 tsp oil; single layer; cook 3–4 min per side without stirring.
  Season; add herbs at the end. Internal ~88°C when done.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C** (tender, slight resistance at very centre)
- **Doneness cues**: Golden brown on contact face; bends without snapping; paring
  knife passes through with very slight resistance.
- **Why this method works**: Butter + oil combination raises the effective smoke point
  while adding butter's flavour notes; not stirring allows the contact face to
  develop a full Maillard crust rather than steaming.

### 2. Delia Smith — Glazed Carrots (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/glazed-carrots
- **Method**: Coin-cut carrots; pan with 25 g butter, 1 tsp sugar, pinch of salt;
  medium heat, covered, 10–12 min (steaming in their own moisture). Uncover; raise heat;
  cook 5 min to evaporate liquid and glaze.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–93°C** (soft throughout)
- **Doneness cues**: Shiny glaze coating; soft when pressed; liquid almost entirely gone.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's covered phase steams the carrot to doneness first,
  then the uncovered glaze phase caramelises the surface; the result is softer than
  pure pan-sear sautéing but with an intense concentrated glaze.

### 3. Jacques Pépin — Carottes Vichy (France)
- **Source**: jacquespepin.net (carottes vichy — classic French bistro method)
- **Method**: Coin-cut 5 mm; sauté in clarified butter over medium heat with a pinch
  of sugar; add 3 tbsp water; cover 5 min; uncover and evaporate to a glaze. Salt and
  parsley at the end.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely tender; surface glazed; water has evaporated entirely.
- **Why this method works**: The Carottes Vichy technique uses the carrot's own starch
  with a small amount of water to create a self-basting glaze; the coin cut maximises
  surface area for quick cooking in the covered phase.

### 4. ICA — Snabbstekta morötter (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/snabbstekta-morotter
- **Method**: Batonger; stekpanna med smör och olivolja på medelhög värme.
  Enkel lagning utan omrörning 3–4 min per sida. ~88°C inuti; gyllenbrun yta.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun kontaktyta; mjuka men håller formen.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish everyday recipe guides recommend the sauté method
  for carrots as a quick weeknight side; the 88°C target coincides with the visual
  doneness cues that most home cooks rely on.

### 5. Ottolenghi — Pan-Fried Carrots with Tahini (UK)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi Saturday column)
- **Method**: Halved lengthwise; heavy frying pan over medium-high with olive oil;
  cut-face down, 3–4 min until golden; flip and cook 3 min covered; uncover and cook
  1 min. Internal ~90°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on cut face; completely tender throughout; tips
  slightly caramelised.
- **Why this method works**: The half-and-half covered/uncovered technique gives
  the carrot a golden cut face (open) then steams it through (covered) without
  losing the crust; tahini dressing is added off the heat to preserve its fresh flavour.
