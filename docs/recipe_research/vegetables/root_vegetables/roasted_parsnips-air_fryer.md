# Roasted Parsnips × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying concentrates the parsnip's exceptional natural sweetness in a shorter
window than oven roasting — the circulating hot air caramelises the cut surfaces
rapidly while the interior softens to 88–90°C. The single-layer requirement of an
air fryer basket means this is not the method for a large gathering, but for 2–3
servings it produces a caramelised, slightly crispy parsnip that rivals the oven
result in a fraction of the time. The parsnip's high sugar content means the air
fryer temperature should not exceed 200°C or the tips will burn before the centres
are cooked through.

## Source recipes

### 1. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Parsnips (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-parsnips
- **Method**: Parsnip wedges or batons; toss with oil, honey, pinch of salt. Air fryer
  190°C for 15–20 min, shaking basket halfway. Internal ~88–90°C; golden and glazed.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden on cut surfaces; tips slightly caramelised; soft when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: 190°C (rather than the standard 200°C) prevents the parsnip's
  high sugar content from burning before the centres are cooked through; the slightly
  lower temperature adds 3–5 min cooking time but prevents bitter char.

### 2. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade palsternackor (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/palsternackor
- **Method**: Palsternacksklyftor med olja, honung och salt. Airfryer 190°C i 15–18 min,
  skaka en gång. ~88–90°C inuti; karamelliserad yta.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun och karamelliserad; mjuka hela vägen igenom.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides confirm 190°C as the right
  temperature for high-sugar root vegetables; palsternacka (parsnip) is widely eaten
  in Sweden and the air fryer result is preferred over oven by home cooks for speed.

### 3. Allrecipes — Air Fryer Honey Parsnips (USA / International)
- **Source**: allrecipes.com/recipe/air-fryer-honey-parsnips
- **Method**: Cut into batons; toss with 1 tbsp oil, 1 tsp honey, salt. Air fryer 195°C
  for 14–18 min, shaking halfway. Probe ~88°C; golden glaze on surface.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Pliable and soft; surface is caramelised; tips slightly darker.
- **Why this method works**: 195°C is a compromise between 190°C (slower caramelisation)
  and 200°C (risk of burning parsnip tips); the short time window requires one shake
  to prevent the honey-coated surfaces from sticking to the basket.

### 4. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Parsnips with Thyme (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-parsnips-thyme
- **Method**: Parsnip wedges with olive oil, fresh thyme, salt. Air fryer 190°C for
  15–18 min, tossing once. Internal ~90°C; golden and fragrant.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden surfaces; soft when pressed; thyme has crisped and is
  deeply aromatic.
- **Why this method works**: Thyme's volatile oils are released quickly in the air
  fryer's dry circulating heat, infusing the parsnip's porous surface during cooking;
  the technique is simpler than oven roasting and produces a very similar result.

### 5. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Root Vegetables (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-root-vegetables
- **Method**: Mixed root vegetable guide includes parsnips: 190°C, single layer, 15–18 min.
  Parsnips specifically noted to require 190°C not 200°C due to high sugar content.
  Internal ~88–90°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden; pliable; zero resistance to knife.
- **Why this method works**: ATK's systematic root vegetable air fryer tests confirmed
  that high-sugar vegetables (parsnips, sweet potatoes) need a lower temperature than
  potatoes; the 190°C recommendation is the practical outcome of comparing burnt-tip
  failures at 200°C.
