# Roasted Parsnips × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Parsnips are one of the most rewarding vegetables for oven roasting: the natural
sugars are significantly higher than in carrots (nearly 10% sucrose), and the
caramelisation that happens against a hot oven surface at 200–220°C turns a mild,
starchy root into something with nutty, almost honeyed sweetness. The internal
temperature at doneness — around 88–93°C — coincides with the visual signals most
recipes describe: a golden crust, tender flesh, and tips that are starting to char
from their higher sugar concentration. Parsnips roast best in honey, butter, or
alongside the drippings of roasting meat.

## Source recipes

### 1. Nigella Lawson — Honey-Roasted Parsnips (UK / Feast)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/honey-roasted-parsnips
- **Method**: Quarter large parsnips lengthwise; parboil 5 min; drain. Toss in
  oil and honey; oven 200°C for 30–35 min turning once until golden and tender.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden amber on flat faces; tips slightly charred; soft throughout
  but not collapsed.
- **Why this method works**: The brief parboil softens the dense centre before
  the oven caramelises the surface; honey's fructose content caramelises at a
  lower temperature than sucrose, creating a deep glaze at 200°C rather than 230°C.

### 2. Heston Blumenthal — Perfect Roast Parsnips (UK)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (roast parsnip guide)
- **Method**: Parboil 5 min; drain; rough surface by shaking. Parsnip fat (or goose
  fat) pre-heated in oven at 200°C. Roast 35–40 min turning every 15 min.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–93°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Crispy, craggy exterior on all faces; soft under the crust.
- **Why this method works**: Heston applies the same roughened-surface technique
  from his roast potatoes to parsnips; the rough surface creates more surface area
  for the hot fat to crisp — particularly important for parsnips' slightly sticky
  sugar content.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Sticky Honey and Rosemary Parsnips (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/sticky-parsnips
- **Method**: Peel and halve; toss in oil, honey, fresh rosemary; oven 190°C for
  35–40 min, turning once. Internal ~90°C; golden and sticky.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised glaze; tips golden-brown; a skewer goes through
  with one push.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's large batches suit a full oven at 190°C
  where the residual moisture in a crowded tray creates initial steam, then
  as the pan dries, the caramelisation accelerates in the last 10 minutes.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsstekta palsternackor (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsstekta-palsternackor
- **Method**: Klyftor; blanda med olja, honung, timjan. Ugn 200°C i 25–30 min,
  vänd en gång. ~90°C inuti; gyllenbrun yta.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun yta; mjuka vid gaffeltest; spetsarna karamelliserade.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish parsnips (palsternacka) are a traditional root
  vegetable well suited to 200°C oven roasting; ICA recipe guides present them as
  a holiday side dish alongside roast meat.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Glazed Roasted Parsnips (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/glazed-roasted-parsnips
- **Method**: Parsnip wedges; toss with oil, maple syrup or honey; oven 200°C fan
  for 25–30 min, turning once halfway. Internal ~88–90°C; deep golden glaze.
- **Target internal temp**: **88–90°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised glaze on all surfaces; tender throughout; a
  knife slips through without resistance.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven at 200°C drives caramelisation faster than
  static heat; the maple syrup glaze (vs honey) gives a slightly more complex
  caramel note that suits parsnip's herbal sweetness.
