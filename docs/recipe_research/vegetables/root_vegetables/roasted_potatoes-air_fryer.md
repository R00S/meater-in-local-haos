## Cut profile
Air-fried roasted potatoes achieve a crust quality that rivals or surpasses conventional
oven roasting in half the time. The rapid circulating hot air creates a Maillard reaction
on every exposed surface simultaneously, without requiring pre-heated fat or the oven's
slower heat-up cycle. The interior still needs to reach 96–99°C for fully cooked starch,
which typically happens by the time the crust is deeply golden. The key difference from
oven roasting: the air fryer's violent air movement means the potatoes dry out faster,
so slightly more oil is needed to prevent the surface from parching rather than crisping.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Roasted Potatoes (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-potatoes
- **Method**: Toss potato chunks (2.5–3 cm) in 2 tbsp oil, salt, pepper.
  Air fryer 200°C / 400°F for 20–25 min, shaking basket every 10 min.
  Pull when golden on all sides and probe reads 98°C.
- **Target internal temp**: 205°F / **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Uniformly golden on all surfaces; crispy when pressed with a
  spoon; interior steams when broken.
- **Why this method works**: ATK testing found air fryer roast potatoes superior to
  oven roast potatoes for weeknight cooking — same crust quality, 30–40% less time.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Crispy Roasted Potatoes (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-crispy-roasted-potatoes
- **Method**: Parboil potato chunks 5 min; drain and toss with 1.5 tbsp oil.
  Air fryer 200°C for 20–25 min, shaking every 7–8 min.
  Internal temp 96–98°C; deep golden crust.
- **Target internal temp**: **96–98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden crust on all exposed surfaces; the rough edges from the
  parboil have crisped completely.
- **Why this method works**: The brief parboil (even shorter than for oven roasting)
  gives the surfaces enough roughness for the air fryer to crisp dramatically; the
  shorter parboil is sufficient because the air fryer's heat is so efficient.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Roast Potatoes (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-roast-potatoes
- **Method**: Par-boil 5 min; drain and rough up. Toss with oil and salt. Air fryer
  200°C for 20–25 min, shaking twice. Internal temp 98°C when done.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden; crispy; no pale spots.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food has positioned the air fryer roast potato
  as the Sunday lunch shortcut; the parboil-and-shake step is retained because it
  produces a significantly better crust than skipping it.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade rostade potatisar (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/luftfriterade-potatisar
- **Method**: Koka potatisbitar 5 min; häll av och rista. Blanda med olja och salt.
  Airfryer 200°C i 20–25 min, skaka korgen varannan 7 minut.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun yta; krispy och fast vid beröring.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides recommend roasted potatoes as one
  of the most successful air fryer results; the 200°C and 20–25 min window is
  consistent across Nordic recipes for medium-sized potato chunks.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Roasted Potatoes (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-potatoes
- **Method**: Toss potato chunks with oil, salt, rosemary. Air fryer 200°C for
  20–25 min, tossing halfway. Internal 98°C; golden and crispy.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden crust; rosemary has crisped and is fragrant; potato
  breaks easily when pressed.
- **Why this method works**: Australian food media recommends air fryer roasted
  potatoes for everyday cooking; Donna Hay's addition of rosemary is specific to
  this method — the herb crisps beautifully in the dry circulating air.
