# Roasted Potatoes × Oven Roast — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Roasted potatoes are where internal temperature becomes almost a secondary concern:
the visual and textural target (deep golden crust, fluffy interior, crispy edges)
is more immediately legible than any probe reading. But a probe does tell the cook
something important — whether the interior has reached 96°C and is fully cooked
through, which guarantees the characteristic light, airy texture that separates great
roast potatoes from dense, gluey ones. The best roast potatoes in the world, from
Heston Blumenthal's parboil-and-roughen technique to the fat-soaked Sunday classics of
the French and British tradition, all converge on the same internal state.

## Source recipes

### 1. Heston Blumenthal — Perfect Roast Potatoes (UK / In Search of Perfection)
- **Source**: hestonfatduck.co.uk (roast potato technique from In Search of Perfection)
- **Method**: Parboil in heavily salted water until just starting to break apart at
  the edges (8–12 min). Drain; shake vigorously to roughen the surface. Heat duck fat
  or goose fat in roasting tray in 200°C oven until smoking. Add potatoes; roast
  45–55 min, turning every 15 min, until deep golden on all sides.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**99°C** (fully gelatinised)
- **Doneness cues**: Deep uniform golden crust on all sides; rough, craggy surface from
  the shake has crisped into a shattering shell; interior steams when broken open.
- **Why this method works**: The parboil starts gelatinisation and creates a rough
  surface; hot fat contact at 200°C creates the steam-then-fry cycle inside each
  potato: the surface moisture flashes to steam, puffs the interior, then crisps.

### 2. J. Kenji López-Alt — Extra-Crispy Roast Potatoes (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/extra-crispy-roasted-potatoes
- **Method**: Parboil in alkaline (baking soda) salted water 10 min. The baking soda
  breaks down the surface pectin, creating an ultra-rough surface. Roast at 230°C /
  450°F in pre-heated oil, turning every 20 min, 45–60 min total.
- **Target internal temp**: **98–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Surface should be uniformly golden with visible crispy nubs;
  broken open, the interior is dry and fluffy.
- **Why this method works**: The alkaline water accelerates the surface breakdown
  that Heston achieves by shaking; the result is an even more deeply craggy surface
  that maximises surface area for crisping in the hot oven fat.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roast Potatoes (UK / How to Eat)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roast-potatoes
- **Method**: Parboil 7 min; drain and shake. Oven 220°C with goose fat pre-heated.
  Roast 45–55 min turning once; the potatoes are done when they sound hollow when
  tapped and are a deep, even gold.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C** (implied by fully fluffy interior)
- **Doneness cues**: Hollow sound when tapped; deep golden crust; no soft or pale spots.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's simpler technique achieves a very good result
  with goose fat and the same parboil-and-shake principle; her "hollow sound" test is
  a reliable non-instrument indicator of 98°C internal temperature.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsstekta potatisar (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsstekta-potatisar
- **Method**: Dela potatis; koka 5 min. Häll av, rista. Olja och salt i ugnsform.
  Ugn 225°C i 35–45 min, vänd halvvägs. Gyllenbrun på alla sidor.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbrun yta; inget motstånd vid knivtest.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish everyday roast potatoes are a simpler version —
  parboil, shake, high-oven — that delivers very good results from the same
  thermodynamic principles; olive oil is standard in Sweden rather than animal fat.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Best Roast Potatoes (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/potato-recipes/the-best-roast-potatoes
- **Method**: Par-boil 15 min until slightly falling apart; drain and shake. Roast
  tray with 1 cm goose fat pre-heated to 210°C. Add potatoes; roast 45–55 min
  turning every 15–20 min. Finish with garlic, rosemary, and sea salt.
- **Target internal temp**: **98–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Shatteringly crispy exterior; the potato holds its shape but
  feels light and airy; the crust is at least 5 mm thick.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's approach uses a longer parboil to maximise
  surface roughness; more rough surface = more surface area = crispier result.
  The longer parboil makes 15 min total in the 210°C fat sufficient for colour.
