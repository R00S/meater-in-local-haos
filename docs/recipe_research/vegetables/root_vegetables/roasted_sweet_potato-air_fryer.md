# Roasted Sweet Potato × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying a sweet potato produces one of the most consistently impressive results of
any vegetable in this method: the high sugar content of the sweet potato caramelises
rapidly in the circulating hot air, the exterior becomes deeply golden before the
interior overcooks, and the whole process takes 30–40 minutes for a whole medium sweet
potato or 15–20 minutes for wedges. The caramelisation in an air fryer is qualitatively
different from oven roasting — the rapid air movement creates a crispier, more lacquered
surface that some cooks prefer for its textural contrast with the custardy interior.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Sweet Potato (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-sweet-potato
- **Method**: Pierce whole sweet potato 6–8 times; rub with oil. Air fryer 190°C /
  375°F for 35–45 min until probe at centre reads 99°C. Rest 5 min before cutting.
- **Target internal temp**: 210°F / **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin is dry and slightly crispy; the sweet potato yields to gentle
  pressure; sugar ooze at pierce points.
- **Why this method works**: ATK recommends 190°C (not 200°C) for whole sweet potatoes
  because the higher sugar content means the skin burns before the thick centre reaches
  99°C; the slightly lower temperature gives more time for interior cooking.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Sweet Potato Wedges (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-sweet-potato-wedges
- **Method**: Cut sweet potato into wedges; toss with oil, salt, paprika. Air fryer
  200°C for 18–22 min, flipping halfway. Internal at thickest wedge: 98–99°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **98–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised and golden on cut faces; interior is soft when probed.
- **Why this method works**: Wedge cut sweet potatoes at 200°C caramelise dramatically
  in the air fryer; the high sugar content creates a lacquered surface by the time
  the interior reaches temperature.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Sweet Potato (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-sweet-potato
- **Method**: Whole sweet potato pricked and rubbed with oil; air fryer 190°C for
  35–40 min. Pull at 99°C. Rest 5 min; split and season.
- **Target internal temp**: **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Wrinkled skin; yields to pressure; caramel ooze.
- **Why this method works**: UK recipes confirm 190°C for whole sweet potatoes in
  the air fryer; the BBC Good Food test found 200°C resulted in burnt skin before
  the interior reached temperature on a large sweet potato.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad sötpotatis (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/sotpotatis
- **Method**: Hel sötpotatis stuckna; olja; airfryer 190°C i 35–40 min (hel) eller
  klyftor 200°C i 18–20 min. Kärntemperatur 99°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Rynkigt skal (hel); karamelliserad yta (klyftor); mjuk inuti.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides distinguish between whole baked
  sweet potato (190°C, longer time) and wedges (200°C, shorter time) — a practical
  distinction based on the different surface-to-volume ratios of the two shapes.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Sweet Potato with Tahini (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-sweet-potato-tahini
- **Method**: Wedges with olive oil and salt; air fryer 200°C for 15–18 min, tossing
  halfway. Internal ~98°C; caramelised and golden. Serve with tahini and pomegranate.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Lacquered caramelised surface; soft centre; crispy edges.
- **Why this method works**: Australian food media has embraced sweet potato as a
  versatile vegetable; Donna Hay's pairing with tahini and pomegranate is built on
  the intense caramelised sweetness that only an air fryer or very high oven heat
  can achieve in this short timeframe.
