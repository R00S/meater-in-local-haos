## Cut profile
Baking a sweet potato whole — in its skin, like a jacket potato — is a different dish
from roasted wedges. The skin remains intact, the interior steams inside its own
jacket, and the sugars that would caramelise against a hot pan instead concentrate
inside the flesh, making the whole baked sweet potato one of the sweetest naturally
occurring foods in the vegetable kingdom. The internal temperature target of 99°C at
the absolute centre (the last point to reach temperature in a whole sweet potato)
ensures the entire interior is custardy rather than dense at the core.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Baked Sweet Potato (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/baked-sweet-potato
- **Method**: Pierce sweet potato 6–8 times; place on foil-lined baking sheet.
  Oven at 200°C / 400°F for 45–60 min (depending on size). Pull when probe reads 99°C.
  Split open and add butter, salt, pepper.
- **Target internal temp**: 210°F / **99°C** (fully custardy throughout)
- **Doneness cues**: Skin is wrinkled and slightly collapsed; the sweet potato is
  visibly soft when pressed through a towel; syrup may have oozed from the skin.
- **Why this method works**: ATK specifically identifies 99°C as the target for a whole
  baked sweet potato — the same starch gelatinisation endpoint as for white potatoes,
  but the sweet potato's higher sugar content means it also caramelises internally.

### 2. Serious Eats — Baked Sweet Potato in the Oven (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/baked-sweet-potato
- **Method**: Rub skin with oil and salt; place on wire rack in 200°C oven. Bake
  45–60 min. The sugars in the skin caramelise and ooze; pull at 99°C internal.
- **Target internal temp**: **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Sweet caramel ooze from the skin punctures; skin is wrinkled and
  slightly crispy; interior is soft throughout.
- **Why this method works**: The wire rack allows air circulation under the skin,
  helping it dry and crisp slightly rather than steaming on a flat pan surface.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Baked Sweet Potato (UK / Kitchen)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/baked-sweet-potato
- **Method**: Whole sweet potato, pricked; oven 200°C for 50–60 min. The skin should
  be wrinkled and the interior completely soft when squeezed.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: The sweet potato collapses slightly under gentle pressure;
  the skin is dry and slightly crispy; the interior is custardy when scooped.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's no-oil approach for the skin (unlike white
  baked potatoes) is valid for sweet potatoes because the high sugar content of
  the skin provides its own natural caramelisation without added fat.

### 4. ICA — Bakad sötpotatis (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/bakad-sotpotatis
- **Method**: Sticka hålet med gaffel; lägg på gallret i ugn 200°C i 45–55 min.
  Kärntemperatur 99°C; skalet är rynkigt och det sipprar socker.
- **Target internal temp**: **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Rynkigt skal; mjuk vid kläm; sockerläckage på plåten.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grocery-store recipe guides position the whole
  baked sweet potato as a quick weeknight meal; the 200°C temperature and 45–55 min
  window is calibrated for the standard Nordic sweet potato size.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Baked Sweet Potato Skins (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/baked-sweet-potato
- **Method**: Whole sweet potatoes, pricked; oven 200°C, 45–60 min until probe reads
  99°C. Cool slightly; scoop and fill the skins with seasoned butter and cheese.
- **Target internal temp**: **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin wrinkled and slightly charred; flesh scoops cleanly with a
  spoon; strong caramel aroma.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's twice-baked approach (scoop, season, return to oven)
  requires the sweet potato to be fully cooked at 99°C in the first bake so the
  filling can be seasoned warm before the skin-crisping second bake.
