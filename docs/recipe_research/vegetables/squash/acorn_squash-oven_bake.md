## Cut profile
Oven-baking acorn squash (as opposed to roasting at the higher temperatures that produce
deep caramelisation) produces a result focused on the interior's silky texture rather
than the crust's caramelised character. Baking at lower temperatures (175–190°C) gives
the squash time to cook through completely without the outer edges overcooking before
the thick centre is done. This is the method for stuffed acorn squash — where the filling
benefits from the gentler heat — and for a more yielding, less caramelised result.

## Source recipes

### 1. Bon Appétit — Stuffed Baked Acorn Squash (USA)
- **Source**: bonappetit.com/recipe/stuffed-baked-acorn-squash
- **Method**: Halved; filled with sausage-breadcrumb stuffing; oven 190°C for 40–50 min
  until probe in thickest flesh reads 96°C and filling is golden. Rest 5 min.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C** (squash flesh)
- **Doneness cues**: Squash flesh yields to fork; filling is golden and cooked through;
  the skin holds its shape as a bowl.
- **Why this method works**: The lower 190°C temperature is essential for stuffed squash —
  the filling needs time to cook through at the same rate as the squash flesh; higher
  heat would char the filling before the squash centre is done.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Baked Stuffed Acorn Squash (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/baked-stuffed-acorn-squash
- **Method**: Halved; parboiled 10 min first; then baked at 190°C with filling 25–30 min.
  Probe squash flesh: 96°C. Stuffing internal: 74°C (if meat-based).
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C** (squash), **74°C** (meat filling if applicable)
- **Doneness cues**: Squash flesh soft and silky; filling set and golden.
- **Why this method works**: The parboil pre-starts the squash cooking, ensuring the
  flesh and stuffing are done simultaneously; without parboiling, the squash often
  finishes before the filling is properly cooked.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Baked Acorn Squash with Maple Butter (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/baked-acorn-squash-maple-butter
- **Method**: Halved; cavity filled with maple butter (maple syrup + butter + pinch of
  cinnamon); oven 180°C for 40–45 min. Probe 96°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Flesh completely soft; maple butter has caramelised in the cavity;
  a fork passes through with no resistance.
- **Why this method works**: The lower temperature (180°C) for the maple butter variation
  prevents the maple syrup from burning in the cavity; the longer time allows full
  cooking of the thick flesh.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsbakad ekollon-squash (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsbakad-ekollon-squash
- **Method**: Halverad med lönnsirap och smör i hålan; ugn 180°C i 40–50 min.
  ~96°C inuti squashköttet.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Mjuk och silkeslen inuti; lönnsirap karamelliserat; gaffeltest OK.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipes use the lower-temperature bake for stuffed
  or glazed acorn squash; the 180°C temperature prevents over-browning of the glaze
  while ensuring full interior cooking.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Baked Acorn Squash (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/baked-acorn-squash
- **Method**: Halved; brush with oil and season; oven 190°C for 40 min. Probe 96°C.
  No stuffing — serve with soured cream and herbs.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Flesh yields to fork; skin slightly collapsed; fragrant and sweet.
- **Why this method works**: The unfilled bake at 190°C gives a more even, fully-cooked
  interior across the entire halved squash without the faster-cooking risks of
  higher temperatures; served with soured cream to balance the sweetness.
