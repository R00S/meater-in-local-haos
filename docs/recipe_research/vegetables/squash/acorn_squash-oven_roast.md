## Cut profile
Acorn squash — named for its deep-ridged acorn shape — is one of the best squash
varieties for oven roasting because its thick, slightly sweet orange flesh takes to
high oven heat with a concentration of caramel sweetness that no other method produces.
The cut-face-down method is standard: the flat cut surface contacts the hot oiled pan,
caramelises against it, and the skin side steams the interior from above. Internal
temperature at perfect doneness: 96–99°C at the thickest part of the flesh, where
the squash is silky and completely yielding.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Acorn Squash (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-acorn-squash
- **Method**: Halve; scoop seeds; brush cut face with butter or oil. Oven 200°C cut-face-
  down on a buttered baking sheet. Roast 30–40 min until deeply caramelised and probe
  reads 96°C at thickest flesh.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut face is deep amber-caramel; flesh yields completely to a knife;
  the skin has collapsed slightly inward.
- **Why this method works**: Cut-face-down means the caramelisation happens on the flat
  surface in contact with the hot pan; the interior steams in the enclosed cavity formed
  by the skin; no foil, no tent — the full contact creates maximum browning.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Roasted Acorn Squash (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/roasted-acorn-squash
- **Method**: Halved; brush with 1 tsp butter mixed with brown sugar. Oven 200°C
  cut-face-down 25 min; flip cut-face-up; drizzle with more butter-sugar mix; roast
  15 min more. Internal 96°C; deeply caramelised.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut face caramelised to amber; filling molten and fragrant; skin
  slightly collapsed.
- **Why this method works**: The two-stage method (face-down for caramelisation, then
  face-up for glazing) maximises both the Maillard browning of the cut surface and the
  brown butter glaze on the interior flesh.

### 3. Samin Nosrat — Roasted Acorn Squash (USA / Salt Fat Acid Heat)
- **Source**: saltfatacidheat.com (roasted squash guide)
- **Method**: Halved; season cavity with salt, pepper, butter. Cut-face-down on an
  unoiled baking sheet. 200°C for 35–40 min. Probe 96°C. Serve with flaky sea salt.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep caramel crust on cut face; completely silky interior; skin
  holds its shape as a bowl.
- **Why this method works**: Samin's no-oil approach for the baking sheet is deliberate —
  the cut surface sears against the dry hot metal, creating a more intense caramelisation
  than an oiled sheet; the butter inside the cavity bastes the flesh from within.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad ekollon-squash (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-ekollon-squash
- **Method**: Halverad; smör och socker i hålan; snittytan neråt; ugn 200°C i 30–35 min.
  ~96°C inuti; djup karamellisering.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun snityta; smältande söt smörarom; mjuk vid stickprov.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides follow the same cut-face-down method
  as international recipes; acorn squash is an autumn supermarket vegetable in Sweden
  and the butter-sugar filling is the standard Nordic preparation.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Acorn Squash (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-acorn-squash
- **Method**: Halved; drizzle of oil and honey in cavity; cut-face-down at 200°C fan
  for 30–35 min. Internal 96°C; honey caramelised.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Honey caramelised in the cavity; cut face deeply golden; fork
  passes through with no resistance.
- **Why this method works**: Honey's fructose content caramelises at a lower temperature
  than sucrose, concentrating the cavity's flavour and protecting the cut surface from
  drying out during the cut-face-down phase.
