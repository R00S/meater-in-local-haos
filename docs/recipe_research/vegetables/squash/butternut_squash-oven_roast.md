## Cut profile
Butternut squash roasted in the oven undergoes one of the most dramatic vegetable
transformations in cooking: the dense, raw, almost starchy flesh softens to a silky,
custardy interior while the cut surfaces caramelise into a deep amber from the squash's
very high natural sugar content. The internal temperature at perfect doneness — completely
soft throughout with deeply caramelised surfaces — is around 96–99°C at the thickest
point of the neck. Below 88°C the squash is still firm at the core; at 99°C it is
fully custardy and the caramelisation on the surfaces is at its peak.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Butternut Squash (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-butternut-squash
- **Method**: Cube 2–3 cm or halve; toss cubes with oil and salt. Pre-heated baking sheet
  at 230°C / 450°F. Roast 20–25 min until deeply caramelised on contact faces. Internal ~96°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cut surfaces are deep amber-brown; the flesh has noticeably softened
  and slightly collapsed; a paring knife passes through with zero resistance.
- **Why this method works**: Pre-heated baking sheet causes immediate caramelisation;
  230°C is essential — lower temperatures steam the squash in its own moisture before
  browning can occur.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Butternut Squash with Tahini (UK / Jerusalem)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi column)
- **Method**: Wedges or halves; brush with oil; oven 220°C for 30–40 min until
  completely caramelised and soft. Internal ~96–99°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **96–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep amber caramelisation on all surfaces; flesh so soft it
  yields to the lightest touch; the tahini dressing pooling into the cavities.
- **Why this method works**: Roasting in large pieces (wedges, halves) gives longer
  caramelisation time before the flesh becomes dangerously soft; the tahini dressing
  needs a hot, silky surface to emulsify against.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Roasted Butternut Squash (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/roasted-butternut-squash
- **Method**: Cubes or wedges; oil, salt, a pinch of nutmeg and chilli. Oven 200°C for
  30–35 min. Internal ~96°C; deeply golden.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden caramelisation; completely soft; nutmeg aroma
  intensified by the heat.
- **Why this method works**: Nutmeg is the traditional squash spice; it blooms in the
  oven heat and permeates the caramelising surfaces; chilli heat complements the squash's
  sweetness without masking it.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad butternutsquash (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-butternutsquash
- **Method**: Klyftor; olivolja och salt; ugn 200°C i 30–35 min. ~96°C inuti; djupt
  gyllenbrun karamellisering.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Djupt gyllenbruna ytor; fullständigt mjuk; skalet lossnar lätt.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides use butternut squash as an autumn
  staple; 200°C for 30–35 min produces reliable results for the standard Nordic
  supermarket butternut size.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Butternut Squash (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-butternut-squash
- **Method**: Wedges; oil, salt, sage leaves. Oven 200°C fan for 25–30 min.
  Internal ~96°C; caramelised and soft.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deeply golden; sage leaves crisped and fragrant; tender throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Sage is the classical Italian partner for butternut squash;
  the leaves crisp in the circulating fan air and infuse the caramelising squash
  surfaces with resinous herbal notes.
