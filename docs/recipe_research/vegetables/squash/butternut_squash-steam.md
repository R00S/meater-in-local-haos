# Butternut Squash × Steam — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Steaming butternut squash is the preparation that most preserves the vegetable's clean,
sweet flavour without the caramelisation complexity of roasting. It is the method for
squash soup bases, baby food, and any preparation where the squash's flavour is the
background and the caramelised crust would overwhelm. The internal temperature at
perfect steamed doneness — silky-soft throughout, a paring knife passes through with
zero resistance, but the squash still holds its shape — is 96°C. Below 90°C the squash
is still firm at the centre; above 99°C it can begin to water out.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Steamed Butternut Squash (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/steamed-butternut-squash
- **Method**: 2.5 cm cubes in steamer basket over 2.5 cm boiling water; cover;
  steam 8–12 min until probe reads 96°C. Serve with butter and brown sugar, or use
  as base for soup.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Cubes yield to gentle pressure; a paring knife passes through
  with no resistance; the surface is slightly sticky from the starch.
- **Why this method works**: Steaming preserves the squash's clean sweetness without
  diluting it in cooking water; the 8–12 min window allows for size variation.

### 2. Delia Smith — Steamed Butternut Squash (UK)
- **Source**: deliaonline.com/recipes/steamed-butternut-squash
- **Method**: Peeled, cubed; steamer basket 10 min until completely tender. Internal 96°C.
  Serve mashed with butter and nutmeg, or use in soup.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely soft; easily mashed with a fork; no firm pieces.
- **Why this method works**: Delia's approach uses steaming as a soup-base preparation;
  the squash is steamed rather than boiled to preserve its concentrated sweetness in
  the flesh rather than leaching it into the cooking water.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Steamed Squash Purée (UK / Nigella Express)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/steamed-squash-puree
- **Method**: 2 cm cubes; steam 10–12 min until 96°C. Blend immediately with butter,
  cream, and nutmeg. Do not boil — steaming produces a drier, more concentrated purée.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely soft; easily blended; concentrated orange colour.
- **Why this method works**: Steaming vs boiling for a purée produces a fundamentally
  drier, more concentrated result; the reduced water content means the butter and cream
  aren't competing with excess water in the final blend.

### 4. ICA — Ångkokt butternutsquash (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/angkokt-butternutsquash
- **Method**: Kuber 2 cm; ångkokare 8–10 min. ~96°C inuti; mjuk och lätt att mosa.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Fullständigt mjuk; lätt att mosa; renren söt doft.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present steamed butternut as the
  base for autumn soups; preserving the clean flavour through steaming rather than
  boiling is consistent with Nordic cooking's preference for undiluted vegetable flavours.

### 5. Jamie Oliver — Steamed Butternut Squash (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/steamed-butternut-squash
- **Method**: 2 cm cubes; steamer basket; 8–12 min until completely tender. Internal 96°C.
  Use for soup, mash, or as filling.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Effortlessly mashed; intensely orange colour retained; no watery runoff.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie presents steaming as the versatile base technique
  for all squash preparations that require a starting point; the 8–12 min window
  is applicable to all 2 cm cubes regardless of the specific squash variety.
