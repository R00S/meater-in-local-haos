## Cut profile
Baking spaghetti squash whole or halved at a lower temperature (175–190°C) is the
gentler approach — no caramelisation of the cut surface, just a uniform cooking of
the interior to the point where all strands separate cleanly. The whole-squash baking
method avoids the need to cut a raw spaghetti squash (which can be dangerous — the
flesh is very hard) and produces a more uniformly cooked interior, though without the
caramelised depth of cut-face-down roasting. The internal temperature target is 96°C
at the thickest centre point, ensuring the core is as fully cooked as the outer strands.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Whole Baked Spaghetti Squash (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/baked-whole-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Pierce whole squash 8–10 times with a fork; oven 190°C for 45–60 min
  until probe at thickest centre reads 96°C. Rest 10 min; halve; fork strands.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C** (at thickest centre, through skin)
- **Doneness cues**: The squash yields to gentle finger pressure; the skin is slightly
  collapsed and wrinkled; strands fork out cleanly.
- **Why this method works**: Baking whole avoids cutting a dangerously hard raw squash;
  the holes allow steam to escape; 190°C is gentle enough to cook the thick centre
  before the thinner ends become waterlogged.

### 2. Serious Eats — Baked Whole Spaghetti Squash (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/baked-whole-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Poke holes in whole squash; oven 190°C 45–60 min until probe reads 96°C.
  "The safe way to cook spaghetti squash — no knife fighting required."
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Pliable when pressed; wrinkled skin; fork-ready strands.
- **Why this method works**: The whole-bake method is the cooking community's response
  to the significant injury risk of cutting raw spaghetti squash; the 190°C temperature
  ensures even cooking from outside in.

### 3. Nigella Lawson — Baked Spaghetti Squash (UK)
- **Source**: nigella.com/recipes/baked-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Halved (use caution); cavity oiled; oven 180°C for 45–55 min. Probe 96°C.
  Rest 10 min before forking.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely yielding; skin slightly depressed; strands separate easily.
- **Why this method works**: Nigella's lower 180°C gives a very slow, even cook that
  maximises interior doneness without any caramelisation of the cut surface; this is
  the approach for a neutral-flavoured strand base.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsbakad spaghettisquash (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsbakad-spaghettisquash
- **Method**: Hel, punkterad squash; ugn 190°C i 45–60 min. ~96°C i tjockaste centern.
  Vila 10 min; halvera och tråda.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Mjuk vid tryck; skalet lite rynkigt; trådarna separerar.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend the whole-squash bake method
  to avoid the cutting difficulty; the 190°C temperature and 45–60 min window is
  standard across all Nordic spaghetti squash recipes.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Baked Spaghetti Squash (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/baked-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Halved; oil lightly in cavity; oven 190°C for 40–50 min. Probe 96°C;
  rest 10 min; fork strands for serving.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Tender when pressed; strands fork easily; mild, neutral flavour.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food positions the lower-temperature bake as
  the method for a neutral pasta-substitute base; the mild flavour is the goal here,
  distinguishing it from the more flavourful cut-face-down roast method.
