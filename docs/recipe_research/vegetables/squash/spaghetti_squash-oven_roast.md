## Cut profile
Spaghetti squash is the most functionally unusual member of the squash family: when
cooked, its flesh separates into long, spaghetti-like strands that serve as a pasta
substitute. The cooking method determines the texture of these strands — roasting
cut-face-down produces strands that are slightly caramelised, with a concentration
of flavour that boiling and steaming cannot achieve. Internal temperature at perfect
doneness: 96–99°C throughout, at which point a fork draws the strands easily from the
skin with clean, noodle-like definition.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt — Roasted Spaghetti Squash (Serious Eats / USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Halve crosswise (crosswise gives longer strands than lengthwise cut);
  brush cut face with oil; oven 200°C cut-face-down on baking sheet. Roast 30–45 min
  until probe reads 98°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **98°C** (strands separate easily)
- **Doneness cues**: A fork dragged across the cut surface pulls long, clean strands;
  the cut face is deeply caramelised; the squash yields completely.
- **Why this method works**: The crosswise cut produces the longest possible strands
  (running around the circumference); cut-face-down allows caramelisation against the
  hot pan while the interior steams in its own moisture.

### 2. America's Test Kitchen — Roasted Spaghetti Squash (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/roasted-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Halved lengthwise; poke holes in skin; oven 200°C cut-face-down 35–40 min.
  Probe 96–99°C. Turn and continue 5 min if needed. Rest 5 min before forking strands.
- **Target internal temp**: **96–99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skin slightly collapsed; cut face caramelised; strands fork out easily.
- **Why this method works**: Poking holes in the skin allows steam to escape and prevents
  the squash from building internal pressure that would cause the skin to split; the
  rest period after cooking allows the strands to firm slightly and fork more cleanly.

### 3. Samin Nosrat — Roasted Spaghetti Squash (USA)
- **Source**: saltfatacidheat.com (squash roasting guide)
- **Method**: Halved; brush with oil; season cut face; oven 200°C cut-face-down 35–45 min.
  Internal 99°C. Rest 10 min; fork strands into a warm bowl.
- **Target internal temp**: **99°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Completely yielding; caramelised face; strands separate cleanly.
- **Why this method works**: The 10-min rest is Samin's technique for spaghetti squash —
  allowing it to cool slightly makes the strands firmer and they fork out more cleanly
  than when done immediately.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad spaghettisquash (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-spaghettisquash
- **Method**: Halverad tvärs; olja; snittytan neråt; ugn 200°C i 35–40 min.
  ~98°C inuti; trådarna separerar lätt med en gaffel.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**98°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Snittytan karamelliserad; trådarna lossnar lätt; mjuk genomgripande.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides present spaghetti squash as a gluten-
  free pasta alternative; the crosswise cut for longer strands is specified in all
  Nordic recipes.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Spaghetti Squash (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-spaghetti-squash
- **Method**: Halved; oil and salt; oven 200°C fan cut-face-down 30–35 min.
  Internal 96°C; rest 10 min; fork strands.
- **Target internal temp**: **96°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Strands fork out cleanly; caramelised cut face; fully tender.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven at 200°C circulates heat evenly around the halved
  squash; 30–35 min is slightly shorter than static oven due to the improved
  circulation; the rest period is maintained for clean strand separation.
