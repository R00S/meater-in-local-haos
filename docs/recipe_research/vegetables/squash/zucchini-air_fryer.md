## Cut profile
Air-fried zucchini achieves a result that resolves the vegetable's fundamental challenge:
the rapid circulating hot air removes surface moisture faster than a conventional oven,
preventing the steaming effect that makes oven-roasted zucchini soggy when overcrowded.
The single-layer air fryer basket means every piece is exposed to moving hot air on all
sides simultaneously. The internal temperature at doneness: 82°C — the same as oven
roasting, but typically achieved 5–8 minutes faster.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Zucchini (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-zucchini
- **Method**: Thick rounds; 1 tbsp oil, salt. Air fryer 200°C for 8–10 min, shaking once.
  Internal ~82°C; caramelised on all surfaces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden on all visible surfaces; slightly shrunken; tender but holds shape.
- **Why this method works**: Air fryer removes surface moisture more aggressively than
  an oven, producing caramelisation in 8–10 min vs 12–15 min in a conventional oven.

### 2. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterad zucchini (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/zucchini
- **Method**: Tjocka skivor; olja och salt. Airfryer 200°C i 8–10 min, skaka en gång.
  ~82°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun yta; mjuk inuti; inga råa eller vattniga delar.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides list zucchini as a recommended
  summer vegetable for air frying; the 200°C / 8–10 min window applies across all
  Swedish and Nordic recipes for this vegetable.

### 3. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Zucchini Chips (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-zucchini-chips
- **Method**: Very thin rounds (3–4 mm); seasoned breadcrumbs and parmesan; air fryer
  200°C for 8–10 min until golden. Internal ~82°C; crispy coating.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Breadcrumb coating golden and crispy; zucchini tender underneath.
- **Why this method works**: The breadcrumb coating absorbs excess moisture from the
  zucchini surface during cooking, contributing to crispiness while the air fryer heat
  creates Maillard browning on the coating.

### 4. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Courgette (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-courgette
- **Method**: Thick rounds or wedges; oil and seasoning. Air fryer 190°C for 10–12 min,
  shaking halfway. Internal ~79–82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden; tender; no raw crunch; holds shape.
- **Why this method works**: BBC Good Food's 190°C recommendation is slightly lower
  to prevent the cut surfaces from browning unevenly; the slightly longer time compensates.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Zucchini with Feta (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-zucchini-feta
- **Method**: Thick wedges; oil, salt; air fryer 200°C for 10 min. Top with crumbled
  feta and fresh mint after cooking. Internal ~82°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden; tender; holds shape; feta softens slightly on residual heat.
- **Why this method works**: Feta and mint are added after cooking to prevent the feta
  from becoming rubbery in the air fryer; the contrast between the warm, slightly
  caramelised zucchini and the cool, salty feta is the point of this preparation.
