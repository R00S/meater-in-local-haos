# Zucchini × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Grilled zucchini (courgette) is a staple of Mediterranean summer cooking — the char
marks, the smoky notes from the grill, and the slight concentration of flavour from
the heat make it the most flavourful version of this vegetable. The key is cutting
thick enough to develop char marks without the interior drying out, and using high
enough heat that the surface chars before the interior overcooks. Planks (5–8 mm thick)
or wedges are the preferred cut for the grill. Internal temperature at doneness: 79–82°C —
grill marks set, interior tender, and the vegetable still holding its shape.

## Source recipes

### 1. Weber — Grilled Zucchini (USA)
- **Source**: weber.com/US/en/recipes/vegetables/grilled-zucchini
- **Method**: Planks 6–8 mm thick; brush with oil; grill over direct medium-high heat
  3–4 min per side. Internal ~79–82°C. Dress with balsamic and herbs.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Clear grill marks; slightly translucent at the centre; no raw
  crunch; holds its shape.
- **Why this method works**: Medium-high (not maximum) heat gives the 6–8 mm plank
  time to develop char marks without the interior drying out; the balsamic dressing
  adds acid that lifts the char flavour.

### 2. Serious Eats — Grilled Zucchini (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/grilled-zucchini
- **Method**: Diagonal bias cut 6 mm planks; toss with oil; grill on high direct heat
  2–3 min per side. Internal ~79°C; pronounced char marks.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Char marks on both sides; translucent interior; still holding shape.
- **Why this method works**: Diagonal bias cuts produce longer planks with more
  surface area for char marks; high heat for a shorter time prevents moisture loss
  while maximising char development.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Chargrilled Courgette (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/chargrilled-courgette
- **Method**: Planks; brush with olive oil and garlic; charcoal or gas grill over
  direct heat 3–4 min per side. Internal ~82°C; deep char marks.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep char marks; slightly blistered surface; tender throughout.
- **Why this method works**: Garlic brushed onto the cut surface creates micro-Maillard
  events where the garlic touches the grill grate — small caramelised patches of garlic
  embedded in the char marks.

### 4. Grillade.se — Grillad zucchini (Sweden)
- **Source**: grillade.se/grillad-zucchini
- **Method**: Skivor 6–8 mm; olivolja och salt; direkt grillning medelhög värme
  3–4 min per sida. ~79–82°C inuti.
- **Target internal temp**: **79–82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillränder; lätt genomskinlig mitt; håller formen.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish grilling guides present zucchini as an ideal
  grill vegetable for the summer season; the 3–4 min per side at medium-high heat
  is the universal Nordic recommendation.

### 5. Dagbladet Mat — Grillet zucchini (Norway)
- **Source**: mat.dagbladet.no/oppskrift/grillet-zucchini
- **Method**: Skivet 6 mm; olje; direkte grilling middels høy varme; 3 min per side.
  ~79°C inuti; grillstriper.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**79°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Grillstriper; gjennomskinnelig; myk og smidig.
- **Why this method works**: Norwegian grilled zucchini recipes are consistent with
  the international approach; the 3 min per side recommendation is calibrated for
  the slightly thinner zucchini varieties common in Nordic supermarkets.
