## Cut profile
Oven-roasted zucchini develops a concentrated, slightly chewy character that sautéing
cannot produce: the extended high-heat oven time dehydrates the flesh partially,
concentrating its flavour and creating caramelised surfaces on all exposed faces.
The internal temperature at perfect doneness is 82–88°C — beyond the point where the
zucchini still has the crisp bite of raw, but before it becomes a waterlogged collapse.
Slicing into thick planks or large chunks and roasting at high heat (220°C+) is the
most reliable way to achieve caramelisation before the natural water content causes steaming.

## Source recipes

### 1. Serious Eats — Roasted Zucchini (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/roasted-zucchini
- **Method**: Thick planks or wedges (1.5 cm thick); toss with oil and salt; single layer
  on baking sheet at 230°C. Roast 12–15 min without stirring. Internal ~85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Deep golden on contact face; slightly shrunken; tender throughout;
  edges beginning to char.
- **Why this method works**: 230°C is essential — lower temperatures cause steaming
  before browning; the pre-heated sheet (Kenji's technique) creates immediate
  caramelisation on contact.

### 2. Ottolenghi — Roasted Courgette with Lemon and Mint (UK / Plenty More)
- **Source**: theguardian.com/lifeandstyle (Ottolenghi)
- **Method**: Thick wedges; toss with olive oil; oven 220°C for 15–20 min. Dress
  with lemon, mint, and toasted pine nuts after roasting. Internal ~82–85°C.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden-amber on all roasted surfaces; slightly shrunken; the zucchini
  holds its shape but yields to gentle pressure.
- **Why this method works**: The mint-lemon dressing is applied after roasting to preserve
  the aromatics; the roasting concentrates the zucchini's flavour enough to stand
  up to the bright acid of the lemon.

### 3. Jamie Oliver — Oven-Roasted Courgette (UK)
- **Source**: jamieoliver.com/recipes/vegetables-recipes/roasted-courgette
- **Method**: Thick rounds or wedges; oil, salt, garlic powder. Oven 200°C for 15–20 min.
  Internal ~82°C; golden cut surfaces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Golden cut surfaces; slightly shrunken; tender.
- **Why this method works**: Jamie's approach at 200°C (slightly lower than Kenji's
  230°C) produces slightly less aggressive caramelisation but a more evenly cooked
  interior; suitable for larger zucchini slices.

### 4. ICA — Ugnsrostad zucchini (Sweden)
- **Source**: ica.se/recept/ugnsrostad-zucchini
- **Method**: Tjocka skivor eller klyftor; olivolja och salt; ugn 220°C i 12–15 min.
  ~82–85°C inuti; gyllenbrun yta.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82–85°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Gyllenbrun skuren yta; lätt skrynklig; mjuk.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish recipe guides recommend oven-roasted zucchini as
  a summer-season side dish; 220°C produces reliable caramelisation for the Italian-
  origin vegetable now widely grown in Nordic kitchen gardens.

### 5. BBC Good Food — Roasted Courgette with Parmesan (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/roasted-courgette-parmesan
- **Method**: Thick planks; scatter with parmesan; oven 200°C fan for 15 min.
  Internal ~82°C; parmesan golden, courgette caramelised.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**82°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Parmesan golden and crispy; zucchini caramelised at edges; tender.
- **Why this method works**: Fan oven ensures even heat circulation; parmesan applied
  before roasting (not after) bakes into a golden crust that adheres to the surface.
