# Roasted Tomatoes × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Air frying tomatoes is the fastest route to concentrated, caramelised roasted tomatoes:
the rapid circulating heat produces in 10–15 minutes what a conventional oven achieves
in 25–30. The result is a tomato with caramelised cut surfaces, concentrated flavour,
and slightly collapsed flesh — ideal as a quick sauce ingredient, bruschetta topping,
or side dish. Internal temperature at doneness: 88–90°C throughout, same target as
oven roasting. Cherry tomatoes work particularly well in the air fryer basket; larger
tomatoes should be halved to ensure complete cooking.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air Fryer Tomatoes (USA)
- **Source**: americastestkitchen.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-tomatoes
- **Method**: Halved tomatoes or whole cherry tomatoes; drizzle with oil; air fryer
  200°C for 10–12 min, not shaking. Internal ~88°C; caramelised and concentrated.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised cut surfaces; slightly collapsed; concentrated sweet-
  acid flavour; skin slightly wrinkled.
- **Why this method works**: Not shaking (allowing the tomatoes to stay stationary) is
  critical — this allows maximum caramelisation to develop on the cut surfaces without
  the tomatoes rolling and losing their position.

### 2. Serious Eats — Air Fryer Roasted Tomatoes (USA)
- **Source**: seriouseats.com/air-fryer-roasted-tomatoes
- **Method**: Cherry tomatoes whole or halved; 1 tbsp oil; air fryer 200°C for 10–12 min
  without shaking. Internal ~88°C; burst skins, concentrated juices.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skins slightly burst; juices concentrated and sticky; deeply sweet.
- **Why this method works**: Whole cherry tomatoes burst in the air fryer's heat,
  releasing their concentrated juices; the air fryer's rapid heat produces this burst
  effect faster and more completely than a conventional oven.

### 3. BBC Good Food — Air Fryer Roasted Tomatoes (UK)
- **Source**: bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/air-fryer-roasted-tomatoes
- **Method**: Halved tomatoes; oil, salt, dried oregano; air fryer 200°C for 10 min.
  Internal ~88°C; caramelised surfaces.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Caramelised cut surface; oregano fragrant and slightly dried;
  concentrated flavour.
- **Why this method works**: Oregano applied before air frying (not after) dries and
  concentrates in the hot air; the combination of caramelised tomato and dried herb
  is the foundation of Mediterranean flavour.

### 4. Airfryer.se — Luftfriterade tomater (Sweden)
- **Source**: airfryer.se/recept/tomater
- **Method**: Halverade tomater; olivolja och salt; airfryer 200°C i 10–12 min.
  ~88°C inuti; karamelliserade och koncentrerade.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Karamelliserade snittytor; lätt kollapsade; sött och koncentrerat.
- **Why this method works**: Swedish airfryer guides present roasted tomatoes as a
  quick weeknight ingredient; the 10–12 min air fryer time is used for bruschetta
  toppings, pasta sauce bases, and bruschetta in Nordic cooking.

### 5. Donna Hay — Air Fryer Burst Tomatoes (Australia)
- **Source**: donnahay.com/recipes/air-fryer-burst-tomatoes
- **Method**: Cherry tomatoes whole; 1 tbsp oil, salt, balsamic vinegar; air fryer
  200°C for 10 min without shaking. Internal ~88°C; burst and concentrated.
- **Target internal temp**: ~**88°C**
- **Doneness cues**: Skins burst; balsamic caramelised; deeply sweet-sour concentration.
- **Why this method works**: Balsamic vinegar applied to whole cherry tomatoes before
  air frying caramelises against the tomato skin as the tomatoes burst; the result is
  a balsamic-glazed roasted tomato in 10 minutes.
