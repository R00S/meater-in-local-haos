# Beef Burger × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
The beef burger patty cooked in an air fryer is a relatively recent home-kitchen variant of the same fundamental cut — coarsely ground beef of around 80/20 fat/lean ratio, formed into a patty 1.5–2.5 cm thick. The air fryer is essentially a small high-velocity convection oven; it concentrates radiant heat from a top element while a fan circulates 175–205 °C air across the patty surface. That circulation produces evenly browned faces without flipping in some models, although a single mid-cook flip improves uniformity. The trade-off versus pan-frying is no rendered-fat puddle to baste in, and versus grilling no smoke — but the convection allows surface browning at lower equipment temperatures than direct radiant heat would require. Carryover is small (2–3 °C) because the patty is thin and the cook short.

## Source recipes

### 1. America's Test Kitchen — Air-Fryer Diner-Style Burgers (American tradition)
**Source**: America's Test Kitchen, *Air Fryer Perfection* (America's Test Kitchen, 2019); americastestkitchen.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g freshly ground beef chuck (80/20)
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 4 slices American cheese (optional)
- 4 soft potato or brioche buns, split
- Pickles, lettuce, sliced tomato, ketchup and mustard, to serve

**Method**
1. Divide the beef into 4 equal portions (175 g each). Form each into a patty 1.5 cm thick and 12 cm wide. Press a shallow thumb-print into the centre of each so they don't dome.
2. Preheat the air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F).
3. Season patties on both sides with salt and pepper just before cooking. Lay them in a single layer in the basket — work in two batches if needed.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one patty, with the tip at the geometric centre, parallel to the basket floor.
5. Air-fry 4 minutes; flip; air-fry another 3–5 minutes for medium.
6. **Pull at 65–68 °C (149–154 °F).** ATK targets a pink centre — slightly above food-safety code but the testers' preferred eating temperature for ground beef cooked at home from fresh, single-source ground.
7. If using cheese, top in the last 60 seconds. Rest 2 minutes on the bun. Final serving temperature: **68–71 °C / 154–160 °F** (medium, juicy, well-browned).

### 2. Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall — River Cottage Burger in the Air Fryer (British tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall & Naomi Devlin, *River Cottage A to Z* (Bloomsbury, 2021), burger entry; rivercottage.net  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+ 30 min chill) · **Cook**: 10–12 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 400 g coarsely-ground 80/20 beef mince (River Cottage prefers chuck and short rib)
- 5 g (1 tsp) Worcestershire sauce
- 5 g (1 tsp) Dijon mustard
- 5 g (1 tsp) flaked sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 1 small shallot, very finely grated
- 2 ciabatta or sourdough rolls, split
- A handful watercress, slice of mature Cheddar, and tomato chutney to serve

**Method**
1. Mix mince, Worcestershire, mustard, sea salt, pepper and shallot lightly with cool hands. Do not over-work. Form 2 patties 2 cm thick. Refrigerate 30 minutes — Fearnley-Whittingstall stresses chilling so the patty holds shape under air-fryer convection.
2. Preheat the air fryer to 190 °C (375 °F).
3. Lay patties in the basket. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one, tip at geometric centre.
4. Air-fry 5 minutes; flip; air-fry 4–6 minutes more.
5. Add cheese slices on top; air-fry a final 60 seconds.
6. **Pull at 67–70 °C (153–158 °F).** Fearnley-Whittingstall targets a slightly more cooked-through finish for British shop-bought mince — between medium and medium-well.
7. Rest 2 minutes on the bun with chutney and watercress. Final serving temperature: **70–72 °C / 158–162 °F** (just-pink centre, medium).

### 3. Adam Liaw — Air Fryer "Smash" Burger (Australian / Asian-fusion tradition)
**Source**: Adam Liaw, *Tonight's Dinner* (Hardie Grant, 2022) and adamliaw.com  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 7–9 min · **Rest**: 1 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g coarsely-ground beef chuck (80/20)
- 5 g (1 tsp) fine sea salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground white pepper
- 30 g (2 tbsp) Kewpie mayonnaise
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) Worcestershire sauce
- 4 slices tasty cheese (Australian melting cheddar)
- 4 soft milk-bun-style burger rolls, split and lightly toasted
- Sliced cucumber pickle, tomato, butter lettuce to serve

**Method**
1. Mix mince with mayo and Worcestershire — Liaw uses Kewpie to lock in moisture during air-frying. Form 4 thin patties (150 g each, 1 cm thick, 13 cm wide). Press a shallow dimple in the centre.
2. Preheat the air fryer to 205 °C (400 °F).
3. Season the patties just before cooking. Lay them in the basket in a single layer.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally into the edge of one patty, tip at geometric centre.
5. Air-fry 3 minutes; flip; air-fry another 3–4 minutes.
6. Top with cheese; air-fry a final 60 seconds with the door briefly cracked to vent.
7. **Pull at 65–68 °C (149–154 °F).** Liaw aims for the same medium-juicy finish as a smash burger from a flat-top griddle.
8. Rest 1 minute on the toasted bun. Final serving temperature: **67–70 °C / 153–158 °F** (medium, melted cheese, juicy).

### 4. Joshua Weissman — Air Fryer Diner Cheeseburger (American food-media tradition)
**Source**: Joshua Weissman, *An Unapologetic Cookbook* (Alpha, 2021); joshuaweissman.com and his YouTube channel  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 450 g freshly ground beef chuck
- 5 g (1 tsp) kosher salt
- 2 g (½ tsp) coarsely ground black pepper
- 2 g (½ tsp) garlic powder
- 2 g (½ tsp) onion powder
- 2 slices yellow American cheese
- 2 brioche buns, split and toasted
- 30 g iceberg lettuce, shredded; 2 slices ripe tomato; 4 dill pickle chips; "burger sauce" (mayo, ketchup, relish, splash of pickle brine)

**Method**
1. Combine the dry spices. Form the beef into 2 equal patties, 1.5 cm thick, 13 cm wide. Press a thumb-print in the centre of each.
2. Preheat the air fryer to 200 °C (400 °F) for 5 minutes.
3. Season patties with the spice mix on both sides. Place in the basket without crowding.
4. Insert the MEATER probe horizontally through the edge of one patty, tip at geometric centre.
5. Air-fry 4 minutes; flip; air-fry another 3 minutes. For cheese, lay slices on patties for the last 60 seconds.
6. **Pull at 66–68 °C (151–154 °F).** Weissman's American diner standard — medium, but on the cooler side so the patty stays juicy when stacked.
7. Rest 2 minutes on the toasted bun with sauce, lettuce, tomato and pickles. Final serving temperature: **68–71 °C / 154–160 °F** (medium, glossy with melted cheese).
