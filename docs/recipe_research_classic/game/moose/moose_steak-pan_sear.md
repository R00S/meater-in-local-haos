## Cut profile

Pan-searing moose steak (loin, backstrap, or haunch slice) is the standard indoor cooking method across Scandinavia, Finland, and northern Canada. The lean meat is seared hot and fast in butter or clarified butter — 3–5 minutes per side — then pulled well before well-done. Some Scandinavian recipes finish in a 125–140 °C oven after searing for more controlled doneness.

Target internal temperature: **58–63 °C (136–145 °F)** for medium-rare to medium. Do not exceed 68 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Chefkoch.de — Elchsteaks (Canadian recipe, German source)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/46421015693274/Elchsteaks.html (fetched 2026-04-24). *Described as "Recipe from Canada."*
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 2 hours (slow oven)

*Translation: Moose steaks — Canadian style. Honey-glazed and slow-roasted in the oven after initial searing.*

**Ingredients**
- 4 moose steaks
- ¼ cup (60 ml) butter
- ¼ cup (60 ml) honey
- ½ cup (120 ml) sour cream
- ½ tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- ¼ cup (30 g) plain flour (for dusting)

**Method**
1. Melt butter in a heavy frying pan. Dust steaks with flour and brown in the butter on both sides until golden (3–4 min per side). Transfer to a baking sheet.
2. Preheat oven to 160 °C.
3. Mix honey with salt and pepper. Brush generously over both sides of the steaks.
4. Bake in the oven for approximately 2 hours (low and slow method) — this is a Canadian home-style technique.
5. Insert probe. Check **65–68 °C (149–154 °F)** for fully cooked but still moist.
6. Warm the pan drippings, add 2 tbsp flour and the sour cream to make a gravy. Serve with baked potato and salad.
*Final resting temperature: 67–70 °C (153–158 °F).*

### 2. Chefkoch.de — Reh oder Hirschsteaks (adapted for moose — German venison/deer steak with marinades)
**Source**: Chefkoch.de; https://www.chefkoch.de/rezepte/1520541257290915/Reh-oder-Hirschsteaks-mit-verschiedenen-Marinaden.html (fetched 2026-04-24; applies equally to moose loin)
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 12 hrs · **Cook**: 15 min

*Translation: Venison/deer steaks with various marinades — applicable to moose loin. Three marinade options.*

**Ingredients** *(Marinade 1 — herb oil)*
- 4 moose loin steaks (or venison), approx. 180 g each
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 sprig rosemary, needles stripped
- 1 sprig thyme
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Mix marinade ingredients. Coat steaks. Marinate overnight.
2. Bring to room temperature 30 minutes before cooking.
3. Heat a heavy iron pan over high heat. Sear steaks 3–4 minutes per side.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 5–7 minutes before serving.
*Final resting temperature: 61–64 °C (142–147 °F).*

### 3. Tasteline.com — Älgbiff i smör (Swedish butter-seared moose steak)
**Source**: Tasteline.com; https://www.tasteline.com/ingrediens/alg/ (fetched 2026-04-24)
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 10 min

*The classic Swedish home-kitchen approach: butter, heat, thyme.*

**Ingredients**
- 2 moose loin steaks, 160–200 g each
- 2 tbsp clarified butter (or regular butter)
- 1 sprig fresh thyme
- 1 garlic clove, crushed
- Salt and pepper

**Method**
1. Season steaks with salt and pepper. Heat clarified butter in a heavy pan until very hot.
2. Sear steaks 3 minutes first side, without moving. Add thyme and garlic to the pan.
3. Flip and cook 3 minutes. Baste continuously with the butter.
4. Insert probe. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F)** for medium-rare.
5. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with boiled potatoes, lingonberry jam, and pickled cucumber.
*Final resting temperature: 61–63 °C (142–145 °F).*
