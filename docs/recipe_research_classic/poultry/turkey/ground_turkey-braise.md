# Ground Turkey × Braise — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile
Ground turkey (minced turkey) is made by grinding dark meat (thigh and leg), white meat (breast), or a blend. A dark-meat-inclusive blend (typically 93/7 lean/fat) has noticeably better flavour and moisture retention than pure breast-meat grinds. Because turkey is poultry, the USDA minimum safe internal temperature is 74°C (165°F) with no reduction for resting. Ground turkey cooks faster than beef or pork at the same mass due to its lower fat content and should be monitored closely to avoid dryness. Carryover is 1–2°C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Marcella Hazan — Ragù di Tacchino (Italian tradition)
**Source**: Marcella Hazan, *Marcella Cucina* (HarperCollins, 1997) — turkey variant of her *ragù bianco* technique  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 75 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground turkey thigh
- 30 ml extra-virgin olive oil + 30 g unsalted butter
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 1 medium carrot, finely chopped
- 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
- 4 sage leaves
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 240 ml dry white wine
- 240 ml whole milk
- 240 ml chicken stock
- A grating of nutmeg
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil and butter in a heavy braising pan over medium heat. Add onion, carrot and celery; sauté 8 minutes until pale gold.
2. Add sage and rosemary; 30 seconds.
3. Add turkey in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
4. Brown 6–8 minutes, breaking apart with a wooden spoon.
5. Add wine; simmer until almost evaporated (5 minutes).
6. Add milk and nutmeg; simmer 8 minutes until milk is absorbed.
7. Add stock; bring to a bare simmer. Cover partially; reduce to lowest heat. Simmer 50 minutes.
8. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Hazan's *ragù bianco di tacchino* is done when the sauce is thick, glossy, and the turkey has turned nearly dissolving — well above the food-safety threshold and at the texture inflection point.
9. Rest 5 minutes off heat for fat to layer. Toss with fresh tagliatelle. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (creamy, herbal, wine-perfumed).

### 2. Hugh Acheson — Slow-Cooker Turkey Mince Bolognese (American Southern tradition)
**Source**: Hugh Acheson, *The Chef and the Slow Cooker* (Clarkson Potter, 2017); hughacheson.com  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 75 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g coarsely-ground turkey thigh
- 30 ml olive oil + 30 g unsalted butter
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 carrots, finely chopped
- 2 stalks celery, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 200 g pancetta, finely diced (optional, for depth)
- 30 ml tomato paste
- 240 ml dry white wine
- 1 tin (400 g) chopped tomatoes
- 480 ml chicken stock
- 240 ml whole milk
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme + 1 bay leaf
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt; 2 g (½ tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- A grating of nutmeg

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil and butter in a heavy braising pan over medium heat. Render pancetta until crisp; remove. Add onion, carrot and celery; sauté 10 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Push veg aside; add turkey in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Brown 6–8 minutes, breaking apart.
4. Stir in tomato paste; cook 2 minutes. Add wine; reduce by half (5 minutes). Add tomatoes, stock, milk, thyme, bay and nutmeg.
5. Bring to a bare simmer. Cover partially; reduce to lowest heat. Simmer 60 minutes.
6. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Acheson's test: the sauce is thick and the turkey nearly dissolved into it.
7. Stir in pancetta. Rest 10 minutes off heat. Season; toss with rigatoni or pappardelle. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (sauce-bound, savoury, glossy).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Spiced Turkey Mince with Tomato & Yoghurt (Levantine tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi, *Plenty More* (Ebury Press, 2014) technique extended; ottolenghi.co.uk recipes — turkey-mince adaptations  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 50 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 500 g coarsely-ground turkey thigh
- 30 ml olive oil
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin + 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander + 2 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon + 2 g (½ tsp) sweet smoked paprika
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt
- 200 g chopped tomatoes
- 240 ml chicken stock
- 30 g pine nuts, toasted, to finish
- 100 g thick Greek yoghurt, to serve
- 5 g chopped mint and parsley, to finish

**Method**
1. Heat olive oil in a heavy braising pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; cook 6 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Bloom cumin, coriander, cinnamon and paprika 30 seconds.
3. Add turkey in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
4. Brown 5 minutes, breaking apart.
5. Add tomatoes, stock and salt; bring to a simmer. Cover partially; reduce to low. Simmer 35–40 minutes.
6. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Ottolenghi targets sauce-bound, glossy mince that holds shape on a spoon.
7. Rest 5 minutes off heat. Top with pine nuts, mint, parsley and dollops of yoghurt. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (spice-rich, creamy-cool counterpoint).

### 4. Tina Nordström — Kalkonfärssås (Swedish ragù tradition)
**Source**: Tina Nordström, *Tina lagar svenskt* (Bonniers Förlag, 2009) — turkey variant of her *köttfärssås*; köket.se  
**Serves**: 6 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 70 min · **Rest**: 10 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g kalkonfärs (ground turkey thigh)
- 30 g smör (butter)
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- 1 medium morot (carrot), finely diced
- 1 stick selleri (celery), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 30 ml tomatpuré
- 240 ml dry white wine
- 400 g chopped tomatoes
- 240 ml chicken stock
- 5 ml Worcestershire-sås
- 2 bay leaves, 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 5 g (1 tsp) salt, 2 g (½ tsp) white pepper, 1 g (¼ tsp) ground kryddpeppar
- 100 ml grädde (cream), added at the end

**Method**
1. Melt butter in a heavy gryta over medium heat. Sauté onion, carrot and celery 8 minutes. Add garlic; 1 minute.
2. Push veg aside; add färs in clumps. Insert MEATER probe into the largest clump.
3. Brown 5–6 minutes, breaking apart. Mix with vegetables.
4. Stir in tomatpuré; cook 2 minutes. Add wine; reduce by half (4 minutes). Add tomatoes, stock, Worcestershire, herbs, salt, white pepper and allspice.
5. Cover partially; reduce to low. Simmer 55 minutes, stirring every 20 minutes.
6. Stir in cream; simmer 5 minutes more.
7. **Pull at 86–90 °C (187–194 °F).** Nordström targets a glossy, cream-rounded ragù.
8. Rest 10 minutes off heat. Toss with fresh tagliatelle or serve over potatismos. Final serving temperature: **84–88 °C / 183–190 °F** (creamy, herbal, savoury, allspice-perfumed).
