# Ny Strip × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

The New York strip on the grill benefits from the direct radiant and convective heat of a gas or charcoal grill to produce pronounced grill marks and a lightly smoky exterior while the moderate marbling keeps the interior moist. The strip's firm, single-muscle composition makes it more forgiving of higher direct heat than the ribeye — it can be cooked over screaming-hot coals without the fat flare-ups that a ribeye's heavier marbling creates. The fat cap, rendered against the grates, crisps and delivers flavour to the exterior. Indirect-heat finishing after initial direct searing allows thick strip steaks to reach medium-rare temperature evenly. Carryover from a very hot charcoal grill is 4–6 °C; from a gas grill somewhat less.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steven Raichlen — Grilled Strip Steaks with Cowboy Butter (American tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *Project Fire* (Workman Publishing, 2018)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 New York strip steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- 10 g (2 tsp) coarse sea salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- **Cowboy butter**: 100 g softened unsalted butter; 2 garlic cloves, minced; 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped; 5 g fresh chives, snipped; 5 ml (1 tsp) Dijon mustard; 5 ml (1 tsp) fresh lemon juice; pinch cayenne; fine salt

**Method**
1. Season steaks with salt and pepper 1 hour before grilling; refrigerate uncovered to dry-brine the surface.
2. Mix cowboy butter ingredients; refrigerate in a log.
3. Preheat grill to high (approximately 260 °C at the grate). Clean and oil grates.
4. Place steaks on the hottest part of the grill at a 45-degree angle to the grates.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
6. Grill 3–4 minutes without moving for clean grill marks.
7. Rotate 90 degrees; grill 1 minute for crosshatch. Flip. Grill second side 3–4 minutes.
8. Stand on fat cap; render 1–2 minutes.
9. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's target is medium-rare.
10. Rest 5 minutes. Top each steak with a disc of cowboy butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 2. Bobby Flay — Grilled NY Strip with Red Chimichurri (Argentine-American fusion tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+overnight marinade optional) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 strip steaks, 300–400 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- **Red chimichurri**: 40 g flat-leaf parsley; 4 garlic cloves; 1 small red jalapeño; 30 ml (2 tbsp) red wine vinegar; 5 ml (1 tsp) dried oregano; 80 ml (⅓ cup) olive oil; fine salt

**Method**
1. Blend chimichurri to a rough paste; season. Set aside.
2. Brush steaks with olive oil; season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Preheat grill to high. Clean and oil grates.
4. Place steaks on the grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side, flipping once.
6. Stand on fat cap; render 1 minute.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Flay's target is medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with red chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 3. Francis Mallmann — Strip Steak on the Grill (Argentine / South American tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–16 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 thick-cut strip loins (*lomo de tira* equivalent — in Mallmann's Argentina, the strip corresponds to the short loin end of the *bife ancho/angosto* range), 400–600 g each, 4–5 cm thick
- Coarse sea salt (applied generously just before placing on the grill — Mallmann's method, not in advance)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Chimichurri (for serving): flat-leaf parsley, oregano, garlic, red wine vinegar, olive oil, red chilli flakes, fine salt

**Method**
1. Build a hardwood or charcoal fire; allow to burn down to glowing embers with no flames. Mallmann cooks over embers, not flames — the radiant heat is more even.
2. Season steaks with coarse salt and pepper generously on both sides just before cooking.
3. Place steaks on the grill rack over the embers, close to the heat.
4. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Cook at a steady medium-high heat — Mallmann's fire is hotter than most gas grills but cooler than a blazing fire. Cook 6–8 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 50–52 °C (122–126 °F).** Mallmann's Argentine preference: a warm red-pink *jugoso* (juicy) interior. The Argentine term for medium-rare is *jugoso*; fully cooked is *bien cocido* — Mallmann never cooks to *bien cocido*.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri alongside. Final serving temperature: **53–56 °C / 127–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine standard).

### 4. Yotam Ottolenghi — Grilled Strip Steak with Herb Salsa (Israeli / Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi and Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012); adapted to strip steak  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 strip steaks, 300–350 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground cumin
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground coriander
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **Green herb salsa**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 20 g fresh coriander; 2 garlic cloves; 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil; 15 ml (1 tbsp) lemon juice; salt

**Method**
1. Mix olive oil, garlic, cumin, coriander, salt and pepper into a marinade. Coat steaks; marinate 1 hour.
2. Blend herb salsa; set aside.
3. Preheat grill to high. Oil grates.
4. Place steaks on the grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F).** Ottolenghi's preference for beef steaks is medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with herb salsa. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 5. Neil Perry — Grilled Strip Steak with Anchovy Butter (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min tempering) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Australian grain-fed strip steaks, 350–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Anchovy butter**: 60 g softened unsalted butter; 4 anchovy fillets, finely mashed; 10 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped; 5 ml (1 tsp) fresh lemon juice; black pepper

**Method**
1. Mix anchovy butter; refrigerate in a log.
2. Season steaks; brush with oil.
3. Preheat grill to high. Clean and oil grates.
4. Place steaks on grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side, flipping once.
6. Render fat cap 1 minute.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Perry's Australian standard is medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Top with anchovy butter. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
