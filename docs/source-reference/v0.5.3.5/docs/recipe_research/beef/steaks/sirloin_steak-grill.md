# Sirloin Steak × Grill — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Top sirloin on the grill is a very widely prepared cut across American BBQ, South American asado, and Korean bulgogi traditions. The lean, firm muscle structure sears well over direct high heat, producing charred grill marks on the surface while the moderate fat content prevents the excessive flare-ups associated with ribeye. For steaks under 3 cm thick, direct-heat-only grilling is standard; for thicker cuts, a two-zone approach (sear then indirect finish) produces better results. Sirloin's lean composition means it must not be cooked past medium without significant quality loss. Carryover from a hot grill is 4–6 °C.

## Source recipes

### 1. Steven Raichlen — Grilled Top Sirloin (American BBQ tradition)
**Source**: Steven Raichlen, *The Barbecue! Bible* (Workman Publishing, 1998)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min (+1 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 top sirloin steaks, 300–400 g each, 3 cm thick
- 60 ml (¼ cup) olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) Worcestershire sauce
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) soy sauce
- 5 g (1 tsp) freshly ground black pepper
- 5 g (1 tsp) coarse salt

**Method**
1. Whisk olive oil, garlic, Worcestershire, soy, pepper and salt into a marinade. Coat steaks; marinate 1 hour at room temperature.
2. Preheat grill to high. Clean and oil grates.
3. Remove steaks from marinade; pat surface dry.
4. Place on the hottest part of the grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side; rotate 90° after 2 minutes for crosshatch marks.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Raichlen's target for sirloin on the grill: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, American BBQ standard).

### 2. Francis Mallmann — Sirloin Asado (Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Francis Mallmann and Peter Kaminsky, *Seven Fires: Grilling the Argentine Way* (Artisan Books, 2009)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 12–16 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sirloin steaks (*bife de chorizo* equivalent — Argentine preferred term for the strip/sirloin region), 400–500 g each, 4 cm thick
- Coarse salt (*sal gruesa*)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Hardwood charcoal or quebracho wood
- Chimichurri: parsley, oregano, garlic, red wine vinegar, olive oil, chilli flakes, fine salt

**Method**
1. Build a hardwood fire; burn to white embers. Season steaks generously with coarse salt and pepper just before cooking.
2. Place steaks directly over the hot embers.
3. Insert the MEATER probe from the side of one steak into its centre.
4. Grill 6–8 minutes per side. Mallmann's Argentine method cooks at moderate-high heat from glowing coals, producing a deep, even crust.
5. **Pull at 49–51 °C (120–124 °F).** Mallmann's Argentine preference: *jugoso* (juicy, red interior). High-carryover charcoal fire.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (*jugoso*, Argentine standard).

### 3. Yotam Ottolenghi — Spiced Sirloin on the Grill (Middle Eastern tradition)
**Source**: Yotam Ottolenghi and Sami Tamimi, *Jerusalem* (Ebury Press, 2012)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+2 h marinade) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 sirloin steaks, 300 g each
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 5 g (1 tsp) ground allspice
- 3 g (½ tsp) ground cinnamon
- 3 g (½ tsp) ground cumin
- 3 g (½ tsp) ground coriander
- Fine salt and black pepper
- **Herb salsa**: 30 g flat-leaf parsley; 20 g fresh mint; 15 ml olive oil; 15 ml lemon juice; salt

**Method**
1. Mix marinade; coat steaks; marinate 2 hours refrigerated.
2. Blend herb salsa; set aside.
3. Preheat grill to high; oil grates.
4. Remove steaks from marinade; pat dry.
5. Place on grill. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
6. Grill 4 minutes per side.
7. **Pull at 52 °C (126 °F).** Ottolenghi's preference: medium-rare.
8. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with herb salsa. Final serving temperature: **54–57 °C / 130–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 4. Bobby Flay — Grilled Sirloin with Red Chimichurri (American-Argentine tradition)
**Source**: Bobby Flay, *Bobby Flay's Grill It!* (Clarkson Potter, 2008)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 4 top sirloin steaks, 300 g each, 2.5–3 cm thick
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- Sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper
- **Red chimichurri**: 40 g flat-leaf parsley; 4 garlic cloves; 1 red jalapeño; 30 ml red wine vinegar; 5 ml (1 tsp) dried oregano; 80 ml olive oil; salt

**Method**
1. Blend chimichurri; set aside.
2. Brush steaks with oil; season with salt and pepper.
3. Preheat grill to high. Oil grates.
4. Place steaks. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
5. Grill 4 minutes per side.
6. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Flay's standard: medium-rare.
7. Rest 5 minutes. Serve with red chimichurri. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare).

### 5. Neil Perry — Grilled Sirloin with Herb Salad (Australian tradition)
**Source**: Neil Perry, *The Food I Love* (Murdoch Books, 2005)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min (+30 min dry brine) · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 Australian top sirloin steaks, 350–400 g each
- Sea salt flakes and freshly cracked black pepper
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
- **Herb salad**: 20 g flat-leaf parsley leaves; 20 g fresh mint leaves; 15 ml (1 tbsp) extra virgin olive oil; 10 ml (2 tsp) red wine vinegar; salt

**Method**
1. Dry-brine steaks 30 minutes. Pat dry; brush with oil.
2. Preheat grill to high. Oil grates.
3. Place steaks. Insert the MEATER probe from the side into the centre.
4. Grill 4 minutes per side.
5. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Perry's Australian standard: medium-rare.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Dress herb salad; serve alongside. Final serving temperature: **55–57 °C / 131–135 °F** (medium-rare, Australian standard).
