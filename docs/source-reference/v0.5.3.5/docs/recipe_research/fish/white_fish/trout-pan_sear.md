# Trout × Pan Sear — Recipe Temperature Research

## Cut profile

Trout in this context refers primarily to pan-seared whole trout fillets or portions — most commonly rainbow trout (*Oncorhynchus mykiss*), brown trout (*Salmo trutta*), or steelhead (sea-run rainbow trout). Rainbow trout fillet is thin to medium-thick, with pink-orange flesh (due to carotenoid content from diet), moderate fat, and delicate, fine-grained muscle flakes. Pan searing is the definitive method for trout in French, American, and Central European cuisines — the skin crisps rapidly, the thin flesh cooks in minutes, and classic preparations like *truite meunière* are foundational to the culinary canon. Steelhead behaves more like salmon and can tolerate slightly higher temperatures before drying out. Carryover for a thin trout fillet is minimal — 2–3 °C — and the cook window is narrow.

## Source recipes

### 1. Julia Child — Truite Meunière (French classical tradition)
**Source**: Julia Child, *Mastering the Art of French Cooking, Volume 1* (Alfred A. Knopf, 1961)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whole rainbow trout, 300–400 g each (cleaned, gutted, heads on or off), or 4 rainbow trout fillets, 140–170 g each
- Fine salt and freshly ground white pepper
- 60 g plain flour
- 45 ml (3 tbsp) clarified butter or vegetable oil (for the pan)
- 80 g (5 tbsp) whole unsalted butter
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 2–3 thin lemon slices, to garnish

**Method**
1. If using whole trout: dry the inside and outside thoroughly. If using fillets: pat dry on both sides. Season with fine salt and white pepper immediately before cooking.
2. Dredge in flour, pressing lightly to coat all surfaces. Shake off all excess — a thin, even coat only.
3. Heat a wide, heavy sauté pan (*à bords évasés*) over medium-high heat. Add clarified butter; heat until foam subsides.
4. Place trout or fillets in the pan (whole trout first on one side, fillets flesh-side down first if skinless; skin-side down if skin-on).
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thickest point of the body (just behind the head for whole trout) into the flesh, clear of the spine.
6. Cook 3–4 minutes per side for fillets (7–8 per side for whole trout).
7. **Pull at 57–59 °C (135–138 °F).** Julia's target for *truite meunière* is fully cooked and flaking — she writes "the fish should fall away from the bone when the flesh is probed at the thickest point." For thin fillets, the probe is the definitive guide.
8. Transfer to warm plates. Pour off fat from the pan; wipe clean. Add whole butter; heat until golden-brown and nutty (*beurre noisette*). Add lemon juice (it will sizzle dramatically). Add parsley. Spoon immediately over the trout. Final serving temperature: **59–62 °C / 138–144 °F** (fully cooked, French classical standard).

### 2. René Redzepi — Pan-Seared Brown Trout with Hay Butter (New Nordic / Scandinavian tradition)
**Source**: René Redzepi, *Noma: Time and Place in Nordic Cuisine* (Phaidon Press, 2010)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 20 min · **Cook**: 6–7 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on brown trout or rainbow trout fillets, 160–180 g each
- Fine salt
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) cold-pressed rapeseed oil (*raps* — Scandinavian cold-pressed canola)
- **Hay butter**: 60 g unsalted butter; 10 g clean, dry aromatic hay (or substitute 1 tbsp dried thyme and 1 tbsp dried chamomile); 2 ml (½ tsp) fresh lemon juice; fine salt
- Fresh sorrel leaves or dill, to garnish

**Method**
1. Make hay butter: melt butter in a small pan; add hay (or dried herb blend); steep 15 minutes over the lowest heat. Strain through a fine sieve; season with lemon and salt. Keep warm.
2. Season trout fillets with fine salt on both sides.
3. Heat a carbon-steel or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add rapeseed oil.
4. Place fillets skin-side down. Press briefly with a spatula.
5. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the flesh.
6. Cook skin-side down 4–5 minutes until skin is crisp and golden.
7. Flip; cook flesh-side down 1–2 minutes.
8. **Pull at 55–57 °C (131–135 °F).** Redzepi's New Nordic standard for trout is barely cooked — the delicate wild trout flavour is at its best with a warm, slightly translucent centre.
9. Rest 2 minutes skin-side up. Drizzle hay butter over; garnish with sorrel or dill. Final serving temperature: **57–59 °C / 135–138 °F** (barely cooked, New Nordic standard, fragrant with hay and herbs).

### 3. Alice Waters — Panfried Trout with Garden Herbs (American / California tradition)
**Source**: Alice Waters, *Chez Panisse Menu Cookbook* (Random House, 1982); the trout preparation is documented in seasonal menus throughout the book  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whole rainbow trout (cleaned, 300–400 g each), or 4 skin-on fillets
- Fine sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 60 g plain flour
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 40 g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 1 small bunch fresh mixed herbs (thyme, chervil, flat-leaf parsley), left on the stems
- 1 lemon, halved

**Method**
1. Season trout inside and out (whole fish) or on both sides (fillets) with salt and pepper.
2. Dredge lightly in flour; shake off excess.
3. Heat a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add olive oil and half the butter.
4. When foam subsides, add trout. Insert the MEATER probe into the thickest part of the flesh, behind the head for whole fish, from the thick end for fillets.
5. Cook 4–5 minutes per side, turning once carefully.
6. Add remaining butter and herbs. Baste with the foaming herb butter for the final 2 minutes.
7. **Pull at 57–60 °C (135–140 °F).** Waters' California farm-to-table standard for trout: fully cooked and flaking, with the herb-butter baste perfuming the skin.
8. Rest 2 minutes. Squeeze lemon over; serve with pan juices. Final serving temperature: **60–62 °C / 140–144 °F** (fully cooked, fragrant with fresh herbs).

### 4. Fergus Henderson — Brown Trout with Brown Butter (British nose-to-tail tradition)
**Source**: Fergus Henderson, *The Complete Nose to Tail* (Bloomsbury Publishing, 2012)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 5 min · **Cook**: 8–10 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 whole brown or rainbow trout (cleaned, 300–400 g each), heads on
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) vegetable or sunflower oil
- 80 g (5 tbsp) unsalted butter
- 30 g capers, rinsed
- 15 ml (1 tbsp) red wine vinegar
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped

**Method**
1. Season trout inside and out with salt and pepper.
2. Heat a large heavy frying pan over medium-high heat. Add oil.
3. Add trout. Insert the MEATER probe behind the head into the thickest part of the body.
4. Cook without moving 4–5 minutes per side.
5. Transfer to warm plates. Wipe pan.
6. Add butter; heat until golden-brown and nutty.
7. **Pull at 58–60 °C (136–140 °F).** Henderson's standard for trout is fully cooked — the flesh should separate cleanly from the bones; "a trout that resists this is undercooked."
8. Add capers; cook 30 seconds. Add vinegar (it will spit). Add parsley. Pour over trout. Final serving temperature: **60–62 °C / 140–144 °F** (fully cooked, classic British beurre noisette-and-capers).

### 5. Rene Redzepi — Steelhead Trout with Smoked Cream (Nordic-American fusion tradition)
**Source**: Presented at MAD Symposium and documented in *A Work in Progress* (Phaidon, 2013)  
**Serves**: 2 · **Prep**: 15 min · **Cook**: 6–7 min · **Rest**: 2 min

**Ingredients**
- 2 skin-on steelhead trout fillets (sea-run rainbow trout), 180–200 g each
- Fine salt
- 20 ml (1½ tbsp) vegetable oil
- 20 g unsalted butter
- **Smoked cream**: 120 ml (½ cup) double cream (*heavy cream*); small piece cold-smoked wood chip (alder or birch) — Redzepi infuses cream with cold smoke using a portable smoker; substitute 5 ml (1 tsp) good smoked salt dissolved in the cream
- Fresh dill and thin cucumber slices, to garnish

**Method**
1. Lightly season cream with smoked salt (or infuse with cold smoke 15 minutes). Keep warm.
2. Season steelhead on both sides with fine salt.
3. Heat a heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add oil.
4. Place skin-side down. Insert the MEATER probe from the thick end into the centre of the flesh.
5. Cook skin-side down 4–5 minutes.
6. Flip; add butter. Baste 1–2 minutes.
7. **Pull at 52–54 °C (126–130 °F).** Steelhead behaves more like salmon than trout; Redzepi's preference is a medium-rare finish — warm, slightly translucent pink interior.
8. Rest 2 minutes. Pool smoked cream around; garnish with dill and cucumber. Final serving temperature: **54–56 °C / 130–133 °F** (medium-rare, salmon-like doneness).
