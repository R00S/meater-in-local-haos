# Pork Belly × Air Fryer — Recipe Temperature Research

> ✅ **STATUS: CONFORMS TO RECIPE_COLLECTION_TOR.md** — validated 2026-03-04

## Cut profile
Air-fried pork belly — scored, skin-on strips or a small slab — achieves crispy
crackling in 25–35 minutes by combining high convection heat (200–220°C) with
rapid air circulation that strips surface moisture from the rind faster than a
conventional oven at the same temperature. The method favours thin slices and small
slabs; large whole bellies benefit more from conventional oven roasting. Carryover
is minimal given the dry, high-velocity environment; pull promptly when the probe
target is reached, then rest briefly skin-side up to preserve rind crispness.

## Source recipes

### 1. J. Kenji López-Alt / Serious Eats — Air Fryer Crispy Pork Belly
**Source**: seriouseats.com  
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 15 min (+1 h drying) · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g pork belly, skin-on, cut into 5 cm wide strips approximately 1.5 cm thick
- 1 tbsp fine salt (for rind)
- ½ tsp white pepper
- ½ tsp five-spice powder (optional)
- 1 tsp rice wine vinegar or cider vinegar (for brushing rind before drying)

**Method**
1. Score the rind of each strip at 5–6 mm intervals, cutting just through the rind
   and 2–3 mm into the fat. Pat the rind completely dry with paper towels.
2. Brush rind lightly with vinegar. Pat dry again. Rub fine salt into the rind,
   pressing into the score lines. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack for at least 1
   hour (overnight preferred) to dry the rind surface.
3. Pat the rind completely dry once more before air frying — any surface moisture
   destroys the crackle.
4. Season the flesh and cut sides with white pepper and five-spice if using.
5. Preheat air fryer to 200°C/390°F. Place pork belly strips skin-side up in a
   single layer in the basket. Do not overlap.
6. Air fry at 200°C for 20–25 minutes. At the 15-minute mark, insert the MEATER probe
   from the short end of the thickest strip, probe tip in the flesh layer at the
   midpoint, clear of fat, rind, and basket floor.
7. **Pull at 72–74°C** (162–165°F). The rind should be puffed, golden, and crackly.
   Fat should be rendered — not white or waxy.
8. Rest 5 minutes on a wire rack, rind side up. Final serving temperature:
   **74°C / 165°F** (well done). Serve immediately; air-fryer crackling softens
   within 10 minutes.

### 2. Nigella Lawson — Chinese-Spiced Air Fryer Crispy Pork Belly
**Source**: nigella.com  
**Serves**: 2–4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+overnight marinade and drying) · **Cook**: 30 min · **Rest**: none

**Ingredients**
- 700 g pork belly, skin-on, whole slab or strips approximately 2 cm thick
- 1 tsp Chinese five-spice powder (*wǔ xiāng fěn*)
- 1 tsp flaky sea salt
- ½ tsp ground ginger
- ½ tsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp white rice vinegar (for brushing rind, to draw out moisture)

**Method**
1. Score rind at 5–8 mm intervals. Mix five-spice, salt, ground ginger, and sugar.
   Rub the spice mixture onto the flesh and cut sides only — keep the rind free of
   spice, as spice on the rind will burn before the crackling sets.
2. Brush the rind only with rice vinegar. Pat dry. The vinegar draws out surface
   moisture and helps the rind blister more reliably.
3. Place uncovered on a wire rack in the refrigerator overnight. Lawson's
   instruction: the rind must feel papery and bone-dry to the touch before air
   frying — if it still feels tacky, leave another few hours.
4. Preheat air fryer to 200°C/390°F. Pat the rind absolutely dry once more.
5. Place belly skin-side up in the basket. Air fry at 200°C for 25 minutes without
   opening the drawer.
6. At the 20-minute mark, insert the MEATER probe from the short end into the
   thickest part of the flesh, probe tip midway into the meat layer, clear of fat
   and rind.
7. After 25 minutes, flip and cook flesh-side up for a further 5 minutes.
8. **Pull at 70–73°C** (158–163°F). Lawson's primary cue is rind colour and
   texture — the probe confirms the flesh is fully cooked and no longer pink.
9. No rest required. Final serving temperature: **70–75°C / 158–167°F** (well done).
   Slice and serve immediately; crackling is at peak crispness straight from the fryer.

### 3. Marion Grasby — Asian Salt-Crusted Air Fryer Crispy Pork Belly
**Source**: Marion's Kitchen (marions-kitchen.com)  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 20 min (+1–2 h drying) · **Cook**: 30 min at 200°C + 5–10 min at 220°C · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 800 g pork belly, skin-on, whole slab or cut into 2–3 equal pieces
- **Salt crust** (for rind): 4 tbsp coarse sea salt
- **Flesh marinade**: 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine or dry sherry, 1 tsp garlic powder,
  ½ tsp five-spice powder, ½ tsp white pepper

**Method**
1. Blanch pork belly in boiling water for 5 minutes. This firms the surface and
   helps the skin separate slightly from the fat layer for more reliable crackling.
   Pat completely dry.
2. Mix the flesh marinade and rub it thoroughly onto the flesh side and cut sides
   only. Leave uncovered in the refrigerator for 1–2 hours to dry.
3. Pack the rind side with a thick, even layer of coarse salt (the salt crust).
   This crust reflects heat onto the rind and acts as a moisture barrier during
   the first phase of cooking.
4. Preheat air fryer to 200°C/390°F. Place belly skin-side up in the basket with
   the salt crust intact.
5. Air fry at 200°C for 30 minutes with the salt crust in place.
6. Remove from air fryer. Brush away the entire salt crust. Insert the MEATER probe
   from the side into the thickest flesh layer, probe tip clear of fat and rind.
7. Return to air fryer at 220°C/430°F for 5–10 minutes until the rind blisters
   uniformly all across the surface.
8. **Pull at 75–78°C** (167–172°F). The rind should be deeply and evenly blistered —
   Marion's standard is uniform crackle, not patchy crisping.
9. Rest 5 minutes rind side up. Final serving temperature: **75–80°C / 167–176°F**
   (well done). Slice and serve with steamed jasmine rice and sweet chilli dipping
   sauce.

### 4. ICA/Coop Sweden — Sidfläsk i luftfritösen (Air Fryer Pork Belly)
**Source**: ica.se/recept; consistent with coop.se/recept and Swedish air-fryer
guidance for *sidfläsk*  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 10 min · **Cook**: 25–30 min · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 600 g sidfläsk (*pork belly*, Swedish), skin-on, cut into 1.5–2 cm thick slices
- 1 tbsp coarse salt (*grovt salt*)
- ½ tsp white pepper (*vitpeppar*)
- ½ tsp dried thyme or ground caraway (*timjan eller kummin*) — optional

**Method**
1. Score the rind of each slice at 5 mm intervals with a sharp knife. Pat rind
   side completely dry.
2. Mix salt, pepper, and optional seasoning. Rub into the rind, pressing into the
   score lines. Leave 30 minutes at room temperature; this short dry-brine draws
   out surface moisture.
3. Pat rind dry again. Preheat air fryer to 200°C/390°F.
4. Place slices skin-side up in a single layer in the basket. Do not stack.
5. Air fry at 200°C for 25–30 minutes.
6. At the 20-minute mark, insert the MEATER probe from the short side of the
   thickest slice into the flesh layer at the midpoint, probe tip clear of fat
   and rind surface.
7. **Pull at 70–72°C** (158–162°F). Swedish pork standard: *70°C kärntemperatur*
   (core temperature). The rind should be puffed and golden — crackling quality is
   judged by texture (*svålens krispighet*), not temperature.
8. Rest 5 minutes rind side up on a wire rack. Final serving temperature:
   **70°C / 158°F** (well done, *genomstekt*). Serve with boiled potatoes (*potatis*)
   and lingonberry jam (*lingonsylt*).

### 5. The Woks of Life (Leung family) — Air Fryer Cantonese Crispy Pork Belly (脆皮燒肉)
**Source**: thewoksoflife.com (Bill, Judy, Sarah, and Kaitlin Leung); consistent
with Cantonese *siu yuk* (燒味 roast meat) tradition adapted for home air fryers  
**Serves**: 4 · **Prep**: 30 min (+overnight dry-cure) · **Cook**: 20–22 min at 200°C + 5–8 min at 220°C · **Rest**: 5 min

**Ingredients**
- 700 g pork belly (*wu hua rou*, 五花肉), skin-on, whole slab
- **Rind preparation**: 1 tbsp white rice vinegar + 1 tbsp coarse salt (applied
  to rind surface; brushed off fully before air frying)
- **Flesh marinade**: 1½ tsp fine salt, 1 tsp five-spice powder (*wǔ xiāng fěn*,
  五香粉), ½ tsp sugar, ½ tsp white pepper, 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine (*shaoxing
  jiǔ*, 紹興酒)

**Method**
1. Blanch pork belly skin-side down in boiling water 3–4 minutes to open the rind
   pores. Drain and pat completely dry.
2. Score the rind at 5–6 mm intervals. Mix the flesh marinade. Apply to the flesh
   side and cut sides only — **do not allow any marinade to contact the rind**, as
   it will prevent uniform crackling.
3. Brush the rind with rice vinegar. Pat dry. Sprinkle coarse salt evenly over the
   rind. Place skin-side up, uncovered, on a rack in the refrigerator overnight.
   The salt draws moisture from the rind; overnight drying is essential for
   Cantonese-style (*zha pi*, 炸皮) exploded crackle.
4. Next day: brush away all salt from the rind. Pat the rind absolutely bone-dry
   with paper towels. This step cannot be skipped.
5. Preheat air fryer to 200°C. Insert the MEATER probe from one short end of the
   belly into the thickest flesh section, probe tip in the meat layer, clear of fat,
   rind, and marinade-penetrated surface.
6. Air fry at 200°C for 20–22 minutes.
7. Increase air fryer to 220°C/430°F. Air fry for a further 5–8 minutes until the
   rind blisters uniformly across the surface — the Cantonese standard is a fully
   erupted, shatteringly crisp skin (*zha pi*), not partial crispness.
8. **Pull at 75–78°C** (167–172°F). The rind should be blistered and crackly
   throughout.
9. Rest 5 minutes skin-side up. Final serving temperature: **75–80°C / 167–176°F**
   (well done). Slice each piece to show the layer of crackling, fat, and meat.
   Serve with steamed white rice (*bái fàn*) and hoisin dipping sauce.

## Temperature consensus

| Tradition | Target flesh temp | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| USA (Serious Eats) | 74°C / 165°F | USDA pork standard; rind judged by texture |
| UK (Nigella Lawson) | 70–75°C / 158–167°F | "No pink" standard; rind by colour and texture |
| Australia/SE Asia (Marion Grasby) | 75–80°C / 167–176°F | Two-stage: 200°C then 220°C blast |
| Sweden (ICA/Coop) | 70°C / 158°F | Swedish 70°C *kärntemperatur* standard |
| Cantonese/Chinese (Woks of Life) | 75–80°C / 167–176°F | *Siu yuk* tradition; two-stage at higher temp |

**Full observed range: 70–80°C at the flesh.** All traditions target between 70°C
(Swedish minimum) and 80°C (Asian upper end). The variation reflects two distinct
objectives: the Swedish and UK approaches prioritise "safely cooked, no pink," which
requires only 70°C; the Cantonese and Australian approaches push to 75–80°C because
the slab geometry and two-stage cooking naturally drives higher flesh temperatures.
No tradition for air-fried pork belly targets below 70°C — the fat layers must
render for the method to succeed, and rendering in a belly slab begins above 70°C.
The full range maps to `well_done` in pork doneness terms.

## What makes this method special
The air fryer achieves pork belly crackling in 25–35 minutes through rapid,
high-velocity hot air that dries the scored rind surface faster than a conventional
oven can at the same temperature. It cannot fully replicate the shattering crackle
of a 6-hour oven roast — the fat layer is not as deeply rendered, and the rind lacks
the airy puffed structure of traditional crackling — but it comes close enough for
weeknight use with a fraction of the time and no complex setup. Overnight rind drying
and deep scoring matter far more than cook time adjustments; skipping either step
produces a chewy rind regardless of temperature.

## Summary
Air frying delivers crispy pork belly in under 35 minutes — a practical shortcut
versus the 2–8 hour oven roast. Overnight rind drying and deep scoring are
non-negotiable; both matter more than any cook time or temperature adjustment. The
crackle is lighter and less dramatic than a full oven blast, but genuine and
shatteringly crisp with proper preparation. It is the right method for speed and
small portions; not the right method when crackling quality is the primary goal.
